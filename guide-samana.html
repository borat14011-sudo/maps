Samaná offers an untouched, ultra-luxury escape where dramatic emerald cliffs meet calm, turquoise waters. For Houston finance couple Will and Kathryn, the upscale enclave of Las Terrenas provides the perfect balance of sophisticated French-Caribbean dining and pristine safety. Baby Mary Claire will thrive along the flat, stroller-friendly wooden deck of Pueblo de los Pescadores and the tranquil, shallow tide pools of Playa Bonita. With private beachfront villas and dedicated nanny services readily available, this peninsula promises a seamless, high-end tropical retreat.
🚕 Getting there: A 45-minute drive from Samaná El Catey International Airport (AZS) to the luxury resorts of Las Terrenas via a pre-booked private luxury SUV, complete with a rear-facing infant car seat and ample space for an UPPAbaby stroller.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Playa Bonita

Gentle, shallow waves make this pristine beach perfect for Mary Claire's first ocean dip. A wide, flat paved walking path runs parallel to the sand, making it exceptionally stroller-friendly.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Pueblo de los Pescadores

A charming beachfront strip with a smooth wooden boardwalk ideal for stroller walks. Enjoy the gentle ocean breeze while exploring upscale boutiques.

Midday 11am-1pm / 2h

Museo de la Ballena

An air-conditioned indoor respite showcasing Samaná's rich humpback whale history. The smooth, flat exhibition floors are perfectly suited for strollers.

Day trip 10am-2pm / 4h

Playa Rincón

A breathtaking white-sand crescent featuring a calm, cool freshwater river inlet (Caño Frío) on the west end, ideal for shaded, gentle baby splashing.

Morning excursion 9am-1pm / 4h

Cayo Levantado

Charter a private catamaran to this idyllic island. Relax on shaded luxury daybeds positioned right along the calm, crystal-clear wading waters.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Playa Cosón

Lined with towering coconut palms that offer abundant natural shade. Perfect for lay-down stroller naps while parents enjoy a quiet beach walk.

Morning 8-11am / 3h

Los Haitises National Park Private Boat Tour

Explore spectacular limestone caves and lush mangroves on a private, canopy-shaded speedboat, keeping Mary Claire safe from direct sun exposure.

Late afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Playa Las Ballenas

An exceptionally wide, calm beach with virtually no waves. This is one of the safest spots on the peninsula for shallow infant wading and sandcastle building.

Early morning 7-8:30am / 1.5h

Las Terrenas Beachfront Pedestrian Path

A beautifully paved, flat trail winding through tropical gardens along the shoreline, perfect for an early morning stroller jog before the midday heat.

🍽️ Where to eat

Porto Restaurant

Upscale beachfront dining at Balcones del Atlántico. Features convenient highchairs, a gentle sea breeze, and an exquisite signature seafood paella perfect for an early family dinner.

Mi Corazón

An elegant French-Dominican fusion establishment. Ask for a table on the quiet, breezy outdoor garden terrace where baby can sleep peacefully in her stroller.

El Lugar

Acclaimed international bistro featuring a quiet, open-air courtyard, exceptional beef tenderloin, and a highly accommodating staff who welcome infants warmly.

La Salsa

Located right on Playa Las Ballenas, offering delicious fresh lobster, a relaxed upscale vibe, and plenty of space to park a stroller table-side under the palms.

El Cabito

A dramatic cliffside restaurant in Las Galeras. Book an early 5:00 PM sunset slot to enjoy the fresh catch-of-the-day while Mary Claire enjoys the cooling trade winds.
