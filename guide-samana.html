Samaná

← back to travel board
Samaná is a lush, eco-diverse peninsula where coconut-fringed jungles tumble into crystal-clear turquoise waters. It offers a laid-back, authentic Dominican escape that balances adventurous nature outings with tranquil, shallow lagoons perfect for tiny toes. Families will love the slow pace, friendly locals, and endless opportunities to connect with wild nature.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi ride from El Catey International Airport (AZS) directly to beach hotels in Las Terrenas or Samaná town.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Rincón & Caño Frío

A breathtaking crescent of white sand where the cold, calm Caño Frío river meets the sea. The freshwater river is shallow, shaded, and completely current-free, making it an absolute paradise for toddlers to splash safely.

Morning / 3-4h

Whale Watching in Samaná Bay

Board a family-friendly catamaran to witness majestic humpback whales leaping in their natural winter breeding grounds. Toddlers will love the boat ride, but choose a larger, stable vessel for smooth sailing.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Bonita

True to its name, this beach features a paved pedestrian path lined with palm trees and calm, shallow shorelines. It is highly stroller-friendly and perfect for building sandcastles under the shade of coconut trees.

Morning / 4h

Los Haitises National Park Boat Tour

Explore ancient caves, dramatic rock formations, and dense mangroves teeming with frigatebirds on a covered boat ride. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the dark pirate-like caves, which are easy to walk through with a carrier.

Morning / 3h

Cascada El Limón via Mule

Journey through a lush tropical forest to reach this spectacular 130-foot waterfall cascading into a green pool. Rent a mule with a local guide to ride with your toddler, and bring a baby carrier for the final steps down.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Puente de Samaná

Walk along these iconic pedestrian bridges connecting the mainland to small, lush offshore cayes. The paved walkway is completely stroller-accessible, offering spectacular ocean views and cool breezes for a fussy toddler.

Midday / 3h

Playa El Valle

A rugged, dramatic beach tucked between two lush cliffs with a gentle freshwater river running right into the ocean. The river estuary provides a safe, wave-free paddling pool for toddlers while parents enjoy the wild scenery.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal de Samaná

An energetic, colorful local market filled with mounds of tropical fruits, sweet coconuts, and local spices. It is a fantastic sensory experience for kids; let your toddler point out and sample sweet, fresh mangoes.

Afternoon / 1h

Museo de las Ballenas

A small, engaging museum in Samaná town featuring a massive, real skeleton of a humpback whale. It is a quick, air-conditioned, and highly educational indoor escape that is perfect for toddlers obsessed with sea life.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita Beach Bar & Restaurant

Get the fresh-caught red snapper grilled with garlic, served right on the beach while your toddler plays in the sand.

Mi Corazón

Order the passion fruit glazed sea bass, and enjoy the beautiful garden terrace which has plenty of space for restless toddlers.

La Salsa

Order a wood-fired Margherita pizza and a fresh passion fruit juice to enjoy on the beachside deck.

El Lugar

Try the tender French-style beef tenderloin with pepper sauce in a relaxed, open-air tropical courtyard setting.

Tierra y Mar

Get the traditional Dominican coconut-braised fish while enjoying the panoramic harbor views.
