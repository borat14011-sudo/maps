Untamed natural beauty meets sophisticated French-Caribbean comfort

Samaná

← back to travel board
Samaná offers a refreshing alternative to the sterile, over-commercialized mega-resorts of Punta Cana, blending wild coconut forests with a distinct European expat influence. For a family traveling with a toddler, it provides the perfect mix of untouched nature and calm, shallow beaches that rival giant swimming pools. It is a place where you can comfortably navigate high-end dining in the sand while your baby naps in a carrier under the palms.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 minute private SUV transfer from Samaná El Catey International Airport (AZS), or ~2.5 hours via the modern Juan Pablo II Highway from Santo Domingo (SDQ).

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Playa Bonita Boardwalk

Walk along the flat, paved pedestrian path that runs directly parallel to the shore, lined with towering coconut palms and boutique hotels. It is the absolute best spot in the province for a smooth, stroller-friendly morning walk with ocean breezes.

Midday / 2-3h

Caño Frío at Playa Rincón

A crystal-clear, slow-moving freshwater river that winds through mangroves and meets the ocean, offering incredibly calm, shallow wading pools. Keep your toddler in a flotation vest for a gentle splash in the cool, shaded water away from the ocean waves.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Las Ballenas

A wide, tranquil beach with almost no current or waves, making it feel like a giant, warm saltwater pool for little ones. The dirt road running along the beach is quiet enough for an easy stroller push to find shade under the sea grape trees.

Morning / 3-4h

Whale Samana by Kim Beddall

Embark on a marine biologist-led humpback whale watching tour aboard a large, stable, double-deck vessel with plenty of shade. The spacious boat is safe and comfortable for holding a toddler in a carrier during the calm-water bay cruise.

Morning / 4h

Los Haitises National Park with Moto Marina Tours

Explore dramatic limestone caves and quiet mangrove canals on a covered speedboat charter that keeps the baby completely out of the direct sun. The bird life and calm waters of the bay will keep a toddler captivated without any strenuous walking.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Pueblo de los Pescadores

Stroll this vibrant, colorful strip of former fishermen's homes converted into beachfront restaurants with flat wooden decking. It is highly stroller-accessible and the perfect spot to grab an early fresh fruit smoothie while your toddler looks at the boats.

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Playita

A picture-perfect crescent cove that is protected from strong winds, offering shallow, turquoise water where a toddler can sit safely at the water's edge. You can rent a beach lounger with a thatched umbrella for reliable, all-day sun protection.

Morning / 3h

Parada La Manzana (El Limón Waterfall Trail)

Experience the lush Dominican jungle safely by renting a horse with a local guide, carrying your toddler securely in a front-facing carrier. This specific ranch provides a smoother, well-maintained trail and a comfortable resting area with clean facilities.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa El Valle

A rugged, dramatic beach surrounded by towering cliffs where a freshwater river meets the sea, creating safe, wave-free tidepools. It is perfect for sensory play on the soft, golden sand away from the stronger ocean breaks.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Terrasse

Order the fresh grilled red snapper with coconut sauce at this beachfront French-Caribbean gem. The open-air deck allows you to step directly onto the soft sand to soothe a fussy baby right by the water's edge.

El Lugar

Feast on premium imported steaks and fine wine in a cozy, intimate garden courtyard setting. The lush tropical greenery dampens ambient noise, making it highly accommodating for an early dinner with a sleeping toddler.

La Playita Restaurant (Chez Sandro)

Savor authentic seafood paella while sitting at a shaded table directly on the sand of a calm cove. Your toddler can play with beach toys right at your feet while you wait for your meal.

Mi Corazón

Indulge in upscale Swiss-Dominican fusion cuisine, such as the lobster tail with passion fruit reduction, in a refined yet welcoming atmosphere. They open early for dinner, allowing you to dine before toddler bedtime routines.

La Dolce Vita

Enjoy handmade pasta and wood-fired pizzas on an outdoor terrace overlooking the ocean. The casual, family-friendly Italian vibe and quick service are ideal for handling unpredictable toddler dining limits.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sublime Samana Hotel & Residences

Offers expansive multi-bedroom suites with full kitchens and private balconies, perfect for prepping baby food and milk. The property features a shallow, canal-style pool that runs the length of the resort, making water play incredibly accessible.

Bahia Principe Grand El Portillo

A premium all-inclusive resort featuring a dedicated children's water park and shallow splash pools perfect for toddlers. The calm, reef-protected beach has virtually no waves, and the resort provides complimentary wooden cribs upon request.

Hotel Alisei

Features spacious apartment-style beachfront rentals with fully equipped kitchens, separate living areas, and private terraces. The beachfront pool has a safe, shallow children's section, and the staff is highly attentive to families with infants.
