Puglia

Puglia offers a fairy-tale escape where whitewashed villages rise above groves of ancient olive trees and two shimmering seas. Children will marvel at the conical trulli houses of Alberobello and splash safely in the naturally shallow, warm coastal waters. It is a region that embraces children warmly, making every meal and piazza stroll feel like a shared celebration.
🚕 Getting there: ~50 min rental car drive from Bari Karol Wojtyła Airport to the family-friendly masserias of the Valle d'Itria

What to do

Early Morning / 2 hours

Wandering the Trulli of Alberobello

Explore the whimsical, cone-roofed houses of Rione Monti that look straight out of a storybook. Toddlers will love running down the car-free stone alleys, and it's highly stroller-accessible if you stick to the main paths.

Morning / 2 hours

Exploring the Grotte di Castellana

Descend into a magical underground world of massive stalactites and stalagmites that will spark older kids' imaginations. For toddlers, bring a baby carrier as the damp cave steps are not stroller-friendly.

Morning / 3-4 hours

Splashing at Spiaggia di Pescoluse

Nicknamed the Maldives of Salento, this beach offers crystal-clear, warm, and incredibly shallow waters perfect for tiny paddlers. Toddlers can safely splash for yards out while parents relax on the soft golden sand.

Morning to Afternoon / 3 hours

Animal Adventures at Zoosafari Fasano

Drive through one of Europe's largest drive-through safari parks to see lions, giraffes, and elephants right from your car window. Toddlers will be absolutely thrilled by the up-close animal encounters without the walking fatigue.

Late Afternoon / 2 hours

Lama Monachile Beach and Gelato Stroll

View the iconic cliffside beach of Polignano a Mare before heading into the historic center for a refreshing treat. The pebble beach is tough for strollers, so stick to the panoramic bridge view and the flat town square with a gelato in hand.

Afternoon / 1.5 hours

Olive Oil Tasting at Masseria Brancati

Visit ancient olive trees over a thousand years old in a beautiful country estate where kids can run freely in the secure courtyards. Toddlers will love meeting the farm dogs while parents sample the estate's liquid gold.

Morning / 1 hour

Cartapesta Demonstration in Lecce

Watch master artisans mold paper and glue into stunning sculptures in historic workshops. It's a mesmerizing craft for young eyes to watch, and the baroque plazas outside are perfect for toddlers to chase pigeons afterward.

Late Afternoon / 1.5 hours

Stroller Walk in Circular Locorotondo

Wander through one of Italy’s most beautiful circular white villages, filled with quiet, pedestrian-only alleys and colorful flower boxes. It's incredibly peaceful and flat enough for an easy stroller ride while toddlers nap.

Morning / 1 hour

Watching the Pasta Ladies of Bari Vecchia

Walk down Strada delle Orecchiette to watch local grandmothers rapidly hand-shape pasta on wooden tables right on the street. Toddlers will be captivated by the fast-moving hands, and the ladies often hand kids a piece of dough to play with.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cantina

Order the fresh, handmade orecchiette with tomato sauce and local burrata which is simple enough for picky toddlers but outstanding for adults.

Trattoria Terra Madre

Get the organic, farm-to-table vegetable antipasti platters sourced directly from the restaurant's backyard garden where kids can stretch their legs.

Osteria del Tempo Perso

Savor the pureed fava beans with wild chicory served inside a historic cave setting that will captivate the whole family.

Panificio Fiore

Grab a warm, thick slice of traditional Bari focaccia topped with cherry tomatoes and olives from this historic bakery near the cathedral.

Osteria Le Maciare

Feast on succulent grilled local meats and handmade pasta in a highly family-friendly tavern known for its warm hospitality.

👨‍👩‍👧 Puglia's historic centers are paved with uneven cobblestones, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier for younger toddlers. Keep in mind that many restaurants do not open for dinner until 8:00 PM, so plan for a late afternoon snack and enjoy the lively, kid-friendly evening stroll.
