Puglia

← back to travel board
Puglia offers a sublime blend of slow-paced luxury and rustic charm that keeps both high-achieving parents and active toddlers happy. You will bypass the frenetic, cliffside pace of the Amalfi Coast for wide-open olive groves, shallow turquoise beaches, and incredibly warm, family-centric Italian hospitality. The region's fortified masserias (historic farm estates) provide self-contained oasis environments where nap times never have to conflict with world-class dining.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min private Mercedes V-Class van transfer from Brindisi Airport (BDS) to the luxury masserias in Savelletri, pre-arranged with a premium rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Zoo Safari Fasanolandia

Drive through one of Europe's largest drive-through safari parks where your toddler can see lions, giraffes, and elephants from the safety of your air-conditioned rental car. It is completely self-contained and requires zero stroller lifting, making it a stress-free morning hit.

Morning / 2h

Rione Monti Trulli District

Explore the whimsical, cone-shaped stone houses that look straight out of a fairy tale. You must swap the stroller for a baby carrier here, as the steep stone-paved slopes and staircases are challenging for wheels.

Morning / 1-2h

Parco Naturale Regionale Dune Costiere

Stroll along the flat, wooden boardwalks winding through pristine coastal dunes, wetlands, and ancient olive trees. It is highly stroller-accessible and offers a serene, breezy environment perfect for inducing a morning stroller nap.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Spiaggia di Pescoluse

Known as the 'Maldives of Salento,' this beach features ultra-fine sand and crystal-clear, knee-deep water extending meters from the shore, ideal for a toddler's first swim. The private beach clubs here offer stroller-friendly wooden walkways right up to the umbrellas.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Giardini Pubblici Giuseppe Garibaldi

An elegant public park in the heart of Lecce featuring wide, shaded gravel paths, towering palm trees, and a dedicated toddler playground. It is the perfect, car-free oasis to let your toddler crawl or walk freely to burn off energy before dinner.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Porto Vecchio di Monopoli

Walk along the flat, fortified sea wall of this historic harbor to watch the bright blue-and-red 'gozzi' fishing boats bobbing in the water. The entire path is stroller-friendly and leads directly to flat, pedestrian-only piazzas.

Morning / 1.5h

Masseria Brancati Olive Grove Tour

Walk through flat, soft-dirt paths shaded by monumental, 3,000-year-old olive trees that look like living sculptures. Toddlers love the open space and tactile nature of the farm, while parents can enjoy a premium olive oil tasting.

Morning / 1h

Lama Monachile Viewpoint

Enjoy the postcard-perfect view of Polignano a Mare's famous cliff cove from the flat, paved bridge above. Avoid taking a stroller down the steep, wet Roman stone steps to the beach itself; the bridge viewpoint gives you the iconic photo safely.

Evening / 1h

Piazza del Duomo Lecce

A massive, enclosed baroque piazza with only one entrance, creating a safe, completely car-free limestone courtyard for your toddler to run around. Visit during the early evening 'passeggiata' to watch local families gather under the soft golden lights.

🍽️ Where to eat

Trattoria Terra Madre

Order the organic, farm-to-table vegetable antipasti platters grown directly in the backyard garden. The spacious, flat outdoor courtyard seating and early 7:30 PM opening time are perfect for matching a toddler's dinner schedule.

Caffè Alvino

Order the warm, custard-filled pasticciotto leccese alongside a cold caffe leccese (espresso with almond syrup over ice). The massive outdoor patio on the flat piazza provides endless visual stimulation to keep a baby occupied in her highchair.

Cala Masciola

Order the salt-crusted catch of the day at this upscale beachside restaurant. You can dine on a wooden deck right over the water, allowing your toddler to safely play in the soft sand next to your table while you enjoy a glass of local Fiano.

Osteria del Porto

Order the raw seafood platter and sea urchin pasta at this casual seaside spot. The outdoor tables sit directly on the flat harbor front, where the ambient sound of lapping waves easily drowns out any toddler fussiness.

Masseria Il Frantoio Restaurant

Indulge in a curated multi-course Puglian tasting menu served in a spacious, candle-lit courtyard. The quiet, garden-like setting makes it incredibly easy to park a sleeping stroller right next to your dining table.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Borgo Egnazia

An ultra-luxury castle-like resort featuring spacious multi-room townhouses, indoor and outdoor heated pools, and a world-class nursery/creche service equipped specifically for infants and toddlers.

Masseria Torre Coccaro

A five-star historic estate boasting a heated outdoor pool, an on-site playground, a petting farm, and remarkably accommodating chefs who will happily prepare custom, organic vegetable purees for your baby on demand.

Masseria Muzza

A serene white-stone oasis located close to Otranto, offering large family suites with private gardens, wide flat lawns for crawling, and exceptionally quiet surroundings for undisturbed naps.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a premium, structured baby carrier like an Ergobaby, as the steep staircases and uneven cobblestones of historic towns like Ostuni and Alberobello are virtually impassable with a stroller. Plan your days around the afternoon 'riposo' (typically 1:30 PM to 4:30 PM) when shops and restaurants close, making it the perfect time to head back to your masseria pool for nap time. For baby supplies, bypass small corner stores and head directly to a local 'Farmacia' (pharmacy), which stocks premium organic baby food pouches (omogeneizzati), specialized Italian diapers, and sensitive baby sunscreens.
