Lake Garda

← back to travel board
Where dramatic Alpine foothills meet the sparkling, sapphire waters of Italy's largest lake, Lake Garda offers a playground of pure magic for families. Toddlers can paddle in gentle, crystal-clear shallows, while parents soak in the charm of medieval lakeside towns lined with gelato shops and sun-drenched squares. It is the perfect blend of slow-paced Italian living and effortless, kid-centric outdoor exploration.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min drive by rental car or private taxi from Verona Villafranca Airport (VRN) to the southern lake hub of Sirmione.

What to do

Full Day / Best from 10:00 AM

Gardaland Resort

Italy's top theme park features Peppa Pig Land and Fantasy Kingdom, which are absolute paradise for toddlers. Older kids will love the gentle roller coasters, and there are plenty of shaded stroller paths.

Morning / 1.5h

Scaligero Castle

This fairy-tale moated castle looks straight out of a storybook and features flat harbor-side walks perfect for little legs. Toddlers will love watching the ducks and swans swim in the castle's ancient moat.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Parco Natura Viva

A fantastic drive-through safari and walkable zoo where kids can spot giraffes, lions, and cheetahs from the safety of the car. It is incredibly stroller-friendly with plenty of shady picnic spots to rest.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Thermal Park Villa dei Cedri

A natural outdoor spa with warm thermal lakes set amidst giant cedar trees, featuring dedicated shallow areas safe for toddlers to splash. The warm water is perfect for relaxing little muscles after a long day of sightseeing.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Riva del Garda Beach Walk

A beautifully paved, completely flat lakeside path perfect for stroller walks or toddlers on balance bikes. It leads to Spiaggia degli Ulivi, a pebble beach with a lovely playground and grassy areas for running wild.

Tuesday Morning / 2h

Desenzano Tuesday Market

A bustling, vibrant open-air market where families can sample fresh local cheeses, seasonal fruits, and bakery treats. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the colors and sounds, and it's easy to grab picnic supplies for the beach.

Morning / 2-3h

Funivia Malcesine-Monte Baldo

Take a rotating cable car up to the mountain peaks for breathtaking panoramic lake views and encounters with grazing mountain cows. The summit has wide, flat grassy paths that are perfectly safe for toddlers to toddle around on.

Morning / 3h

Parco Giardino Sigurtà

Rent a family-sized golf cart or hop on the miniature train to explore these award-winning, rolling green gardens. Toddlers will adore the petting farm, the boxwood maze, and rolling down the endless grassy hills.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Baia delle Sirene Park

A magical, olive-grove-shaded beach club with a dedicated playground, shallow waters, and mini lounge chairs for kids. It is widely considered the most beautiful and family-friendly cove on the entire lake.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ristorante Pizzeria La Roccia

Order the classic, wood-fired Margherita pizza made with fresh local mozzarella, served in a spacious, highly welcoming family environment.

Trattoria Clementina

Get the homemade pumpkin tortelloni or simple butter-and-sage pasta, which is always a massive hit with toddlers.

Ristorante Al Gondoliere

Enjoy the fresh lake trout fillet with lemon and olive oil while kids enjoy a customized portion of simple pasta on the lakeside terrace.

Gelateria Cristallo

Treat the family to a giant cup of creamy, house-made hazelnut and strawberry gelato right on the bustling harbor front.

Osteria Al Torcol

Try the traditional handmade potato gnocchi with tomato sauce, a soft and comforting dish perfect for little hands and hungry parents.

👨‍👩‍👧 Invest in a lightweight, sturdy stroller with good suspension to navigate the historic cobbled streets of lakeside towns. Always pack water shoes for the little ones, as almost all of Lake Garda's beautiful beaches are pebbly rather than sandy.
