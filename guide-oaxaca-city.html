Oaxaca City

← back to travel board
Oaxaca City welcomes families with its pedestrian-friendly cobblestone streets, vibrant markets, and rich indigenous traditions. Children will love the whimsical paper-mache puppets, bright street art, and sweet hot chocolate around every corner. It is a sensory feast that manages to feel incredibly safe, warm, and easy to navigate with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to the Centro Histórico hotels

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Santo Domingo Temple and Plaza

This majestic church features a wide, car-free plaza perfect for toddlers to run around while street vendors blow bubbles. Inside, the stunning gold-leaf interior will captivate older kids.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado 20 de Noviembre

A sensory explosion where families can sample fresh tlayudas and pan de yema dipped in hot chocolate. Toddlers will love the lively atmosphere, but keep them close in the busy aisles.

Morning / 2h

El Tule Tree

Visit the widest tree in the world in a quiet, manicured park just outside the city. Kids will love trying to spot animal shapes in the gnarled bark with the help of local guides.

Morning / 3-4h

Painting Alebrijes in San Martín Tilcajete

Visit a family workshop to see how these whimsical, brightly colored wooden animal figures are carved and painted. Toddlers can paint their own simple wooden animal to take home as a souvenir.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo Juárez El Llano Park

A spacious green park with playgrounds, fountains, and wide paths perfect for stroller walks. On weekends, it hosts a lively market with jumping castles and toy vendors.

Afternoon / 1h

Oaxaca Textile Museum

Housed in a beautifully restored colonial building, this museum features colorful textiles and a lovely, quiet courtyard. It is a peaceful, stroller-friendly spot to escape the afternoon heat.

Morning / 4-5h

Hierve el Agua

These stunning petrified waterfalls feature shallow, mineral-rich turquoise pools that are safe and incredibly fun for kids to splash in. Note that the drive is winding, so pack motion sickness remedies.

Morning / 2-3h

Monte Albán Archaeological Site

Explore the ancient Zapotec capital built on a flattened mountaintop with massive grassy plazas perfect for kids to run. Stick to the lower grassy areas with toddlers to avoid steep climbs.

Afternoon / 30m

Chocolatería Mayordomo

Watch raw cacao beans ground into sweet, warm chocolate paste right before your eyes. Toddlers will love the sweet aroma and tasting a cup of fresh, child-friendly hot chocolate.

🍽️ Where to eat

Criollo

Enjoy a relaxed, high-end tasting menu in a spacious, open-air courtyard where children can safely wander and look at the resident chickens.

Itanoní Flor de Maíz

Order the simple, delicious tetelas and quesadillas made from heirloom corn, which are absolute crowd-pleasers for picky toddlers.

Boulenc

Get the sourdough pastries, artisanal pizzas, and fresh fruit juices in a beautiful, plant-filled courtyard.

Las Quince Letras

Try the famous mole tasting platter for the adults, while kids feast on simple, high-quality chicken quesadillas in a welcoming family atmosphere.

Chepiche Café

Head to this beautiful garden café in the Barrio de Xochimilco for fluffy pancakes, fresh fruit bowls, and plenty of space for toddlers to move around.

👨‍👩‍👧 Choose a sturdy, compact stroller for the uneven cobblestone streets, but rely on a baby carrier for navigating busy markets and archaeological sites. Always carry cash in pesos for street snacks and public restrooms, which often require a small fee and do not provide toilet paper.
