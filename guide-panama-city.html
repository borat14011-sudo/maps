Panama City

← back to travel board
Panama City blends dazzling modern architecture with colonial charm and lush, jungle-fringed escapes right at its doorstep. Kids will marvel at the giant ships navigating the canal, while parents enjoy the vibrant, walkable historic quarters. It’s an accessible, tropical playground where urban convenience meets wild nature.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-30 minute taxi or Uber ride from Tocumen International Airport (PTY) to downtown or Casco Viejo hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Miraflores Visitor Center

Watch massive cargo ships pass through the Panama Canal locks from the observation decks. Toddlers will love the giant boats up close and the interactive exhibits inside.

Late Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Casco Viejo Walk

Stroll the vibrant cobblestone streets lined with colorful colonial buildings and plazas perfect for kids to run around. Toddlers can easily navigate the plazas, and there are plenty of spots to stop for refreshing shaved ice.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Amador Causeway

Ride bikes or walk along this scenic path connecting four islands with stunning views of the city skyline. There are fantastic playgrounds along the way, and the wide path is perfect for strollers.

Morning / 2 hours

Biomuseo

Designed by Frank Gehry, this multi-colored museum celebrates Panama's incredible biodiversity through eye-catching exhibits. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant multi-screen theater showing local wildlife.

Early Morning / 2 hours

Metropolitan Natural Park

A real tropical rainforest inside the city limits where you can spot sloths, monkeys, and colorful birds. The trails are short and manageable, though a baby carrier is recommended over a stroller.

Lunchtime / 1-2 hours

Mercado de Mariscos

Experience the bustling energy of the local fish market, where you can sample incredibly fresh ceviche. It can be loud and busy, so keep toddlers close or in a carrier.

Morning / 2 hours

Punta Culebra Nature Center

Run by the Smithsonian, this open-air museum offers a hands-on look at marine life with touch tanks, sea turtles, and wild sloths in the trees. It’s highly stroller-friendly and right on the water.

Morning / 3 hours

Summit Municipal Park

A sprawling botanical garden and zoo home to rescued native animals, including the magnificent Harpy Eagle. It features wide grassy areas for toddlers to toddle freely and a fun playground.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Mi Pueblito

Explore replica villages showing the traditional lifestyles of Panama's diverse cultures, complete with a mock church and schoolhouse. It's a colorful, low-key spot where toddlers can wander safely away from traffic.

🍽️ Where to eat

Maito

Order the sancocho (traditional chicken soup) and the creative modern takes on local seafood in a lush garden setting.

El Trapiche

Try the Fiesta Panameña platter to sample local staples like carimañolas, empanadas, and tamal de olla that kids will love.

Café Coca Cola

Grab a casual, budget-friendly breakfast of hojaldres (Panamanian fried bread) and eggs at the oldest operating cafe in Panama.

Sano Pecado

Indulge in delicious, kid-approved bubble waffles and healthy fruit smoothies in a colorful, modern cafe.

Dodó Bon Pan Café

Enjoy fresh pastries, savory quiches, and great coffee in a cozy, whimsical setting right next to Plaza Francia.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry US dollar bills (the local currency is pegged 1:1) since small street vendors and taxis don't accept cards. Stick to Uber for convenient, hassle-free transportation around the city with kids, as they are safer and cheaper than standard yellow cabs.
