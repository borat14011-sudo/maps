What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Cinta Costera

This stunning waterfront park features miles of wide, perfectly paved pathways ideal for a smooth stroller walk with Mary Claire. Will and Kathryn can enjoy panoramic views of the skyline and ocean under the shade of mature palms, ideally during the cooler early morning hours.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Miraflores Visitor Center

Watch massive cargo ships navigate the locks from an air-conditioned observation deck equipped with stroller-friendly elevator access. It is best to visit during the morning schedule to avoid the peak midday heat, and the indoor exhibition halls provide cool refuge for the baby.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Casco Viejo

The historic colonial quarter is filled with beautiful plazas, pastel-colored architecture, and ruins. While the cobblestones can be bumpy, a high-quality baby carrier allows you to easily explore the shaded Plaza de la Independencia and scenic Paseo de las Bóvedas.

Midday 11am-1pm / 2h

BioMuseo

Designed by Frank Gehry, this world-renowned museum is fully air-conditioned and features wide, stroller-accessible ramps. The vibrant, multi-colored exhibits are highly visual, making it an engaging sensory experience for Mary Claire while parents appreciate the architecture.

Late afternoon 4:30-6:30pm / 2h

Amador Causeway

A flat, breeze-swept paved causeway connecting four islands with spectacular views of the canal entrance. Perfect for a late afternoon stroller walk, it features plenty of benches and shady spots to rest and feed the baby while watching ships pass by.

Early morning 7:30-9:30am / 2h

Metropolitan Natural Park

A lush tropical rainforest right within the city limits, offering a paved, stroller-friendly loop trail (Sendero Los Momótides). It is incredibly shaded and quiet, allowing the family to spot sloths, monkeys, and colorful birds without disturbing Mary Claire's morning nap.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Parque Omar

Panama City's equivalent to Central Park is a massive green lung with smooth paved paths, lush lawns, and shaded playgrounds. It is a peaceful sanctuary where Will and Kathryn can enjoy a leisurely walk while Mary Claire watches local families play under giant banyan trees.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Punta Culebra Nature Center

A peaceful, open-air marine science center run by the Smithsonian, featuring shaded trails, sea turtle ponds, and a small frog exhibition. The paths are stroller-accessible and the calm ocean breezes from the surrounding bay keep the entire family cool.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Mi Pueblito

A charming, low-key cultural mock-village nestled at the foot of Ancon Hill. The paved village squares represent Afro-Antillean, Indigenous, and Colonial Panamanian history, offering a very quiet, crowd-free environment perfect for a slow-paced stroll.

🍽️ Where to eat

Fonda Lo Que Hay

A celebrated upscale-casual venue serving creative local fare like toasted concolón rice with shredded beef. The lively, high-energy atmosphere is perfect for hiding occasional baby noises, and their early 6 PM dinner seating is highly accommodating.

Santa Rita Bistro

An elegant yet welcoming Spanish-Panamanian fusion bistro famous for its grilled octopus and premium tapas. They offer sturdy highchairs, spacious indoor and outdoor tables, and exceptionally warm service that caters graciously to families with infants.

La Vespa Vista Mare

An upscale Italian restaurant featuring fresh seafood risotto and wood-fired pizzas on a spacious, breezy waterfront terrace. The ocean sounds are soothing for baby Mary Claire, and the spacious outdoor layout easily accommodates a stroller next to the table.

Maito

Ranked among Latin America's top restaurants, Maito serves innovative Panamanian fine dining like cassava-crusted pork. Will and Kathryn can book an early evening reservation in the gorgeous, lush garden patio which is quiet and spacious enough for a stroller.

Makoto Panama

A stunning, high-end Japanese rooftop restaurant offering exceptional sushi and Kobe beef dishes. The restaurant features a private elevator for easy stroller access, and the beautiful covered outdoor terrace provides a gentle breeze and highchairs for upscale family dining.
