Belize City

← back to travel board
Belize City serves as the vibrant gateway to ancient Maya history, lush tropical jungles, and the sparkling Caribbean Sea. While bustling, its close proximity to wildlife sanctuaries, interactive museums, and gentle coastal breezes makes it an exciting base for curious little explorers. Families will love the easy mix of wildlife encounters and colorful Creole culture.

What to do

Morning / 3h

The Belize Zoo

This unique zoo only houses rescued native animals in spacious natural habitats, allowing kids to see jaguars, tapirs, and toucans up close. Toddlers will love the stroller-friendly dirt paths and the chance to spot the playful spider monkeys.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Museum of Belize

Housed in a former colonial prison, this cool indoor museum showcases ancient Maya pottery, colorful stamps, and pirate history. It is a great air-conditioned escape for toddlers to gaze at shiny jade artifacts.

Morning / 3-4h

Altun Ha Maya Ruins

Located just north of the city, these ancient pyramids feature wide, grassy plazas perfect for kids to safely run around and burn energy. The main temple is easy to view from the ground, so there is no need for toddlers to climb the steep stone steps.

Morning / 1h

Michael Finnegan Market

Immerse your senses in this bustling local market filled with pyramids of colorful tropical fruits like sweet mangoes and bananas. Toddlers will be captivated by the sights, and you can grab cheap, fresh fruit snacks on the go.

Afternoon / 3h

Old Belize Marina and Cucumber Beach

A quirky cultural museum is paired here with a man-made beach featuring calm, shallow waters and a giant water slide. It is the safest spot right in the city limits for toddlers to splash in the Caribbean Sea without strong waves.

Morning / 3h

Crooked Tree Wildlife Sanctuary

A short drive away, this peaceful wetland is a paradise for birdwatching and scenic boat rides. Toddlers can easily spot massive jabiru storks and turtles sunbathing on logs from the safety of a flat-bottomed boat.

Late Afternoon / 30m

Belize Sign Monument

This vibrant, multi-colored sign right along the beachfront is the ultimate photo-op to capture your family memories. The grassy park surrounding it is ideal for a toddler to stretch their legs while watching local boats sail by.

Morning / 2-3h

Community Baboon Sanctuary

Run by local villages, this conservation area lets you walk through the jungle to see wild black howler monkeys in the trees. The guides are incredibly kid-friendly and often help toddlers listen to the monkeys' famous booming roars.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Fort George Pedestrian Walk

Take a breezy stroll along the harbor wall near the Baron Bliss Lighthouse to watch cruise ships and local fishermen. It is fully paved, stroller-friendly, and there are sweet local vendors selling refreshing coconut water with straws for the kids.

Where to eat

  • Nerie's Restaurant — A legendary local spot serving comforting, mild Belizean stew chicken with rice and beans that even picky toddlers will devour.
  • Wet Lizard — Located right on the Tourism Village waterfront, this lively spot offers crispy fish fingers, breezy outdoor seating, and great views of the harbor.
  • Celebrity Restaurant — A spacious, air-conditioned family favorite with a massive menu ranging from fresh seafood to simple burgers and milkshakes for the kids.
  • Ice Cream Shoppe Belize — The ultimate treat stop offering locally made, creative ice cream flavors in a clean, air-conditioned space with high chairs available.
👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry insect repellent with DEET for jungle excursions, and stick to bottled water for drinking. Pavements can be uneven, so a lightweight baby carrier is often more practical than a bulky stroller when exploring the city streets.
