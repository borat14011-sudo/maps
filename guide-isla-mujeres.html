Isla Mujeres

← back to travel board
Isla Mujeres is a Caribbean paradise where turquoise, wave-free waters meet laid-back island vibes. Perfect for families, this small island offers stress-free exploration via golf carts and endlessly shallow beaches. It is the ultimate gentle introduction to Mexico for travelers with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min private shuttle from Cancun Airport to Puerto Juarez, then a ~20 min ferry ride to the island.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Norte

This world-famous beach features waist-deep, crystal-clear water with virtually no waves, making it a giant natural swimming pool. Toddlers can safely splash for hours in the shallows while parents relax on rental loungers.

Morning / 1-2h

Punta Sur Sculpture Park

Explore the rugged southern cliffs, find the Mayan ruins of Ixchel, and spot giant wild iguanas roaming the paths. Stroller-friendly paved walkways make this cliffside park easy to navigate with toddlers.

Midday / 2-3h

Garrafon de Castilla

A quieter, budget-friendly alternative to the main eco-park, offering calm waters filled with colorful fish right off the shore. The shallow rocky entry has plenty of tiny tide pools for toddlers to examine.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Golf Cart Island Tour

Rent a golf cart to cruise the scenic loop of the island, letting the warm ocean breeze keep everyone cool. Toddlers will love the sensory excitement of the open-air ride—just bring a portable clip-on harness.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal

A bustling local market where you can experience authentic island life and grab cheap, fresh fruit. Toddlers will enjoy pointing at the colorful produce displays and eating sweet local bananas.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Kin Ha (Parque de los Sueños)

A fantastic oceanfront beach club featuring multiple swimming pools, water slides, and kayak rentals. There is a dedicated, shallow kids' pool perfect for toddlers to safely splash under umbrellas.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa Sol

Located right next to Playa Norte, this beach offers spectacular sunset views and equally calm, shallow waters. It is less crowded in the late afternoon, giving toddlers plenty of space to build sandcastles.

Morning / 1h

Capilla de Guadalupe

A stunning, modern chapel built on a cliffside with a glass wall behind the altar overlooking the crashing ocean. The quiet, grassy outdoor courtyard is a peaceful spot for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Midday / 2h

MIA Reef Tide Pools

The shallow lagoon surrounding the islet of MIA Reef hotel is open to the public and features knee-deep water flowing over soft sand. Toddlers can safely wade across the calm channel without facing any open-ocean waves.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mango Cafe

Get the legendary coconut French toast and a fresh mango smoothie for a sweet breakfast treat.

Lola Valentina

Order the local fish tacos with poblano cream sauce while kids swing on the indoor bar-stool swings.

Picus Cocina de Mar

Order the incredibly fresh ceviche and crispy fish fingers while sitting at a table directly in the sand.

Soggy Peso

Grab their famous seasoned beef burgers and a freshly squeezed margarita right on the bay docks.

Javi's Cantina

Order the grilled catch of the day with chimichurri sauce in a lively, beautifully lit garden courtyard.
