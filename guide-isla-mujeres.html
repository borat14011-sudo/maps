Isla Mujeres

← back to travel board
Avoid Cancun’s chaotic mega-resorts for this low-key, golf-cart-paced island offering the calmest wading waters in the Mexican Caribbean. With Playa Norte functioning as a giant, wave-free toddler pool, parents can sip cold drinks with their toes in the sand while keeping a close eye on their little one. The vibe is laid-back and highly walkable, meaning no car seat logistics or highway traffic once you land on the island.
🚕 Getting there: ~50 minutes total: a 30-minute private van from Cancun Airport (CUN) to Puerto Juárez, followed by a 20-minute Ultramar ferry to the island.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Norte

Walk out 100 yards in waist-deep, crystal-clear water with zero undertow or waves. Bring a pop-up tent for shade, as this beach is the ultimate safe harbor for a toddler's first ocean swim.

Early Morning / 1-2h

Punta Sur

This dramatic cliffside lookout features a sculpture trail and a historic shrine to the Mayan goddess Ixchel. The main paved loop is fully stroller-accessible, though you should skip the steep lower dirt paths with a toddler.

Morning / 1h

Isla Mujeres Malecón

A wide, paved pedestrian seawall path on the eastern side of the island that is perfect for a breezy stroller walk. Go early in the morning to catch the sunrise and enjoy the crashing waves before the heat peaks.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Kin Ha Beach Club

An oceanfront beach club featuring multiple swimming pools, shady lounge areas, and easy water access. The tiered pools are ideal for letting a toddler splash around safely away from the open ocean waves.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal

The local municipal market in Centro is the best spot to grab fresh tropical fruits, juice, and quick snacks. It can get tight for large strollers, so use a baby carrier to navigate the colorful local stalls.

Mid-day / 2-3h

Garrafon de Castilla

A relaxed, low-key beach club located right next to the main national park, offering calm waters and shallow snorkeling. It is much quieter and cheaper than the neighboring adventure park, perfect for a baby nap under a shady palapa.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Hacienda Mundaca

Explore the shaded jungle ruins of a historic 19th-century estate built by a pirate. The flat, packed-dirt pathways are shaded by massive trees, offering a cool and quiet respite from the midday sun.

Afternoon / 30m

Capilla de Guadalupe

A stunning, modern chapel built right on the rocky cliffs with a massive glass wall behind the altar overlooking the ocean. It is quiet, fully air-conditioned, stroller-friendly, and makes for a peaceful pitstop during an island drive.

Morning / 2-4h

Golf Cart Island Loop

Renting a golf cart is the classic way to get around the island and doubles as a great way to lull a toddler to sleep with the ocean breeze. Secure your baby carrier to the seat and bring a portable clip-on stroller fan to keep them cool.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mango Café

Order the famous stuffed French toast or the polo chipotle. The bright, multi-level space is loud enough to easily drown out baby squeals, and they open early at 8:00 AM.

Lola Valentina

Sip a fresh mezcal cocktail while sharing upscale coconut shrimp and tacos. Located on the main pedestrian street, it features spacious seating for strollers and highly accommodating staff.

Poc Chuc

Try the authentic Yucatecan Poc Chuc (marinated grilled pork) or the comforting lime soup. It is a highly casual, fast, and incredibly welcoming family-run spot where toddlers are treated like royalty.

Javi's Cantina

High-end grilled seafood and steaks served in a breezy, open-air garden courtyard. The live acoustic music provides great ambient noise, and they open early at 5:00 PM for dinner.

Sardinian Smile

Authentic Italian pasta and wood-fired pizzas served in a lush, quiet garden setting. It is the perfect choice when your toddler needs simple, familiar carbs in a calm and spacious environment.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Mia Reef Isla Mujeres

Set on a private islet at the tip of Playa Norte, this resort features a shallow beach cove with zero waves that acts as a natural pool for toddlers. They offer spacious suites, cribs, and the convenience of an all-inclusive setup.

Ixchel Beach Resort

Located directly on the sand of Playa Norte, this property offers spacious one- and two-bedroom suites with full kitchens, which are essential for storing milk and prepping baby food. The pool is just steps from the rooms.

Izla Beach Front Hotel

A quieter, luxury option on the south-central beach with stunning pools, a kids' pool area, and large, modern rooms. They gladly provide sturdy cribs and have elevators making stroller navigation seamless.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a golf cart for your entire stay to eliminate the hassle of folding strollers and installing car seats in local taxis. Stick strictly to bottled water for drinking and brushing your baby's teeth, which can be purchased in bulk at the Chedraui supermarket. Pack a lightweight, UV-protected pop-up beach tent because natural shade is limited on Playa Norte and midday canopy rentals can fill up quickly.
