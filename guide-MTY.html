Monterrey

← back to travel board
Framed by the majestic Sierra Madre Oriental, Monterrey blends cosmopolitan energy with breathtaking outdoor adventures suitable for all ages. Families will love discovering lush green ecoparks, interactive museums, and mouthwatering culinary traditions nestled beneath towering limestone peaks. It is a surprisingly stroller-friendly northern haven that seamlessly pairs rugged nature with modern convenience.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Parque Fundidora

This massive, stroller-friendly urban park built on an old steel foundry features paved paths, playgrounds, and boat rides. Toddlers will love chasing the ducks and watching the boats along the Santa Lucía riverwalk.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Paseo Santa Lucía Boat Ride

Take a relaxing, 2.5-kilometer artificial riverboat cruise connecting Fundidora Park to the Macroplaza. It is the perfect seated activity to keep toddlers entertained without tiring out little legs.

Full Day / 5-6h

Bioparque Estrella

Drive through an open safari to feed giraffes, zebras, and elephants right from your vehicle. It is an absolute thrill for toddlers, though the bumpy ride means bringing a secure carrier instead of a stroller.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Ecológico Chipinque

Hike scenic, shaded trails up the mountain to see butterflies, coatis, and breathtaking views of the city. The lower paved loops and insectary are ideal for families with young toddlers.

Afternoon / 2h

Horno 3 Steel Museum

Located inside a giant former blast furnace, this interactive science museum features hands-on exhibits and a dramatic fire show. The ground-floor sensory displays are highly engaging for curious toddlers.

Morning / 2h

The Macroplaza

Explore one of the world's largest public squares, home to grand fountains, monuments, and gardens. Toddlers can run freely on the wide pedestrian plazas, but keep an eye out for occasional steps.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Natural La Estanzuela

Walk along a crystal-clear mountain stream beneath a canopy of lush green trees just minutes from the city. The paved initial pathway is stroller-friendly and leads to shallow splash areas perfect for toddler sensory play.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Mercado Barrio Antiguo

A trendy, colorful indoor food hall offering everything from local tacos to artisanal ice cream in a relaxed setting. High chairs are available, making it an easy, low-stress spot to feed picky toddlers.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Museo de Historia Mexicana

Learn about Mexico's vibrant history in an air-conditioned, spacious museum with interactive displays right on the canal. The outdoor plaza and shallow steps are great for toddlers to safely practice walking.

Where to eat

  • Cabrito at El Rey del Cabrito — Monterrey's most iconic restaurant serves tender, roasted baby goat in a vibrant, kitschy dining room that kids will find fascinating.
  • Tacos de Trompo at Tacos El Pata — This family-friendly spot serves excellent spit-roasted pork tacos and features a small, dedicated kids' play area to keep toddlers busy.
  • Glorias de Linares — These sweet, chewy candies made of burnt goat's milk and pecans are a local delicacy that toddlers with a sweet tooth will love.
  • Empalmes at Restaurante El Tío — Taste these traditional northern corn tortilla sandwiches filled with refried beans and lard, offering a mild, comforting flavor perfect for young children.
👨‍👩‍👧 Monterrey can get extremely hot, so plan outdoor park visits for early morning and head to air-conditioned museums or malls by midday. While Fundidora is highly stroller-friendly, cobblestone streets in Barrio Antiguo are best navigated using a sturdy baby carrier.
