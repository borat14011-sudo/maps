Monterrey

← back to travel board
Ringed by the majestic Sierra Madre Oriental, Monterrey blends ultra-modern infrastructure with warm northern Mexican hospitality. Families can explore sprawling green parks, interactive museums, and breathtaking natural canyons, all while enjoying incredibly stroller-friendly spaces. It is a dynamic destination where dramatic peaks meet accessible, toddler-approved city adventures.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Parque Fundidora

This massive, stroller-friendly urban park built on an old steel foundry features expansive paved paths, playgrounds, and a scenic canal. Toddlers will love chasing birds and riding in the rented family quad-bikes.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo Santa Lucía

Take a relaxing, 2.5-kilometer boat ride along this artificial river connecting Fundidora Park to the Macroplaza. It is a fantastic, low-effort way for toddlers to see the city lights and bridges without getting tired.

Full Day / 5-6h

Bioparque Estrella

A thrilling safari park where kids can feed giraffes and zebras directly from a massive open-air truck. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the free-roaming animals and the gentle petting zoo area.

Morning / 3h

Chipinque Ecological Park

Located in the Sierra Madre, this pine-forested reserve offers fresh air, stunning mountain views, and butterfly sanctuaries. The lower trails are paved and perfect for sturdy strollers, plus there is a great playground at the plateau.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Horno 3 Science and Technology Museum

Housed inside a historic blast furnace, this interactive science museum features hands-on exhibits and a thrilling steel-pouring show. Toddlers will enjoy the sensory light displays and riding the massive outdoor elevator.

Afternoon / 1-2h

The Macroplaza

One of the world's largest plazas, this historic heart of the city features impressive fountains, monuments, and wide-open walking spaces. Toddlers can safely run around the traffic-free plazas while parents admire the towering Faro del Comercio.

Afternoon / 3-4h

KidZania Monterrey

A sprawling indoor edutainment center where kids roleplay real-world professions in a miniature city. There is a dedicated, padded 'RightZKeepers' zone designed specifically for toddlers under four to explore safely.

Evening / 1-2h

Mercado Barrio Antiguo

This trendy indoor food hall offers a relaxed atmosphere with diverse dining options from tacos to gelato under one roof. It is highly casual and perfect for families with toddlers who need quick, fussy-eater-friendly food options.

Morning / 3-4h

Grutas de García

Ride a scenic aerial cable car up a dramatic mountain face to reach these spectacular, illuminated ancient caves. Strollers aren't practical inside due to stairs, so bring a toddler carrier to explore the cool, subterranean chambers comfortably.

Where to eat

  • El Rey del Cabrito — Monterrey's most iconic restaurant serves traditional spit-roasted baby goat (cabrito) in a colorful, kitschy setting that kids find fascinating.
  • La Postrería 77 — Famous for its towering, decadent slices of chocolate cake and family-friendly café vibe, this is the ultimate sweet-tooth reward for well-behaved toddlers.
  • Tacos Orinoco — This legendary, retro-styled taco spot serves spectacular al pastor and costra tacos in a bustling, casual environment where kid noise blends right in.
  • Panem Bakery & Bistro — An excellent, family-friendly spot offering freshly baked artisanal pastries, child-friendly breakfast options, and spacious seating areas.
👨‍👩‍👧 Monterrey's summer heat can be intense, so plan outdoor park visits for early mornings or late afternoons and utilize air-conditioned spaces during midday. Taxis and Uber are highly affordable and reliable here, but bring your own lightweight travel car seat as local vehicles rarely provide them.
