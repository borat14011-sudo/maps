Ponta Delgada

← back to travel board
Ponta Delgada welcomes families with its vibrant black-and-white tiled streets, emerald volcanic lakes, and gentle marine breezes. Nestled on São Miguel Island, this safe and walkable haven blends European charm with wild, prehistoric nature. It is the perfect Atlantic playground for curious toddlers and relaxed parents alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi or rental car drive to downtown Ponta Delgada hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1h

Portas da Cidade

Walk through the iconic 18th-century basalt arches that mark the entrance to the historic city center. The surrounding plaza is wide, flat, and completely car-free, making it perfect for toddlers to toddle safely.

Afternoon / 2h

Jardim António Borges

This enchanting, historic botanical garden features towering banyan trees with massive roots and hidden stone caves. Toddlers will love exploring the shady paths, duck ponds, and the dedicated playground.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado da Graça

A bustling local farmers market packed with colorful pineapples, local cheeses, and fresh fish. Toddlers will be captivated by the vibrant sights and smells, especially the sweet, locally-grown Azorean pineapples.

Afternoon / 1h

Arruda Pineapple Plantation

See how Ponta Delgada's famous sweet pineapples are grown inside glass greenhouses at various stages of growth. It is free to enter, stroller-accessible, and finishes with delicious pineapple ice cream that toddlers will adore.

Evening / 1-2h

Farol de Santa Clara and Port

Stroll along the scenic harbor to see the red-capped lighthouse and watch the colorful boats bobbing in the water. The flat, paved marina path is ideal for stroller pushing and watching giant container ships.

Morning / 1.5h

Gruta do Carvão

Explore the ancient underground volcanic tunnel right within the city limits. While the deep cave tour is best for older kids, toddlers can safely marvel at the visitor center and the cave entrance area.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Pinhal da Paz

Located just a short drive north of the city center, this sprawling forest reserve offers manicured lawns, pine-scented air, and roaming peacocks. It is a dream spot for a family picnic with an excellent playground and animal enclosures.

Morning / 1-2h

Forte de São Brás

A historic 16th-century sea fort guarding the harbor that now houses a military museum. Toddlers will love looking at the old cannons lining the stone ramparts overlooking the ocean.

Morning / 3h

Whale and Dolphin Watching Boat Tour

Embark on a family-friendly boat trip from the marina to spot resident dolphins and sperm whales. Most tour operators offer larger, stable catamarans that easily accommodate families with young children.

🍽️ Where to eat

A Tasca

Try the famous local tuna steak sesame-crusted or the shared petiscos like local cheese with pineapple jam.

O Tasco

Get the incredibly tender slow-cooked beef ribs or the octopus rice in a family-friendly, welcoming rustic atmosphere.

Rotas da Ilha Verde

Order the delicious vegetarian sweet potato gnocchi and their famous homemade chocolate cake, which is a massive hit with kids.

Ramada o Galego

Order the classic Bife a Regional, a tender steak topped with local garlic and spicy red pepper, served alongside crispy fries that toddlers will happily steal.

Gelataria do Cais

Treat the family to artisanal gelato featuring local Azorean flavors like Dona Amelia cake or fresh island passion fruit.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a sturdy, compact stroller for the cobblestones, but bring a baby carrier for navigating the natural volcanic sights and garden steps. Always carry light layers and rain gear, as the island's unique microclimate can bring four seasons in one day.
