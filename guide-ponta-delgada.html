Ponta Delgada

← back to travel board
Ponta Delgada offers a low-stress, highly walkable base for a young family looking for an exotic escape without the exhausting jetlag. The city's waterfront and historic parks feature flat, paved paths perfect for stroller walks, while the surrounding microclimates offer incredibly mild, baby-friendly weather year-round. Azorean culture is deeply family-centric, meaning your toddler will be warmly welcomed with open arms at every local establishment.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 minute taxi or rental car drive from João Paulo II Airport (PDL) directly to downtown Ponta Delgada hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Jardim António Borges

A romantic 19th-century park featuring massive Australian banyan trees and dramatic lava stone caves. The wide, paved pathways are exceptionally stroller-friendly, and the dense canopy provides excellent shade to keep the baby cool.

Afternoon / 1h

Plantação de Ananases Augusto Arruda

A historic pineapple plantation where you can walk through active glass greenhouses to see how these unique fruits are cultivated. The outdoor dirt tracks are flat enough for a sturdy stroller, and the gift shop serves refreshing, fresh-squeezed pineapple juice.

Morning / 30m

Portas da Cidade

The iconic three-arched city gates set in a wide, beautifully paved pedestrian plaza. This completely car-free square gives your toddler plenty of safe space to toddle around while you snap photos of the classic architecture.

Morning / 2-3h

Reserva Florestal de Recreio do Pinhal da Paz

A massive, manicured forest reserve just north of the city center featuring grazing deer and a fantastic kids' playground. Its gravel paths are easy to navigate, and the lush lawns are perfect for spreading out a picnic blanket.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Terra Nostra

A world-class botanical garden located in nearby Furnas with flat, wide gravel paths winding through exotic flora. While the famous orange geothermal pool is too hot for infants, the shaded garden paths are incredibly peaceful for a stroller nap.

Morning / 1h

Mercado da Graça

The city's vibrant farmer's market, filled with the aromas of fresh local cheeses, pineapples, and freshly cut flowers. The main aisles are wide and flat enough for a stroller, offering a rich sensory experience that isn't overwhelmingly crowded on weekday mornings.

Morning / 1h

Miradouro da Vista do Rei

The most famous viewpoint on the island, overlooking the massive blue and green twin lagoons of Sete Cidades. The viewing platform is paved and barrier-free, allowing you to easily push the stroller right up to the edge for panoramic family photos.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Forte de São Brás

A historic 16th-century fortress situated right on the western edge of the city harbor. The flat, wide concrete ramparts are highly accessible for strollers and offer sweeping, breezy views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Evening / 1-2h

Portas do Mar

A modern, completely flat waterfront promenade lined with shops, cafes, and marina views. It is entirely car-free and paved, making it the premier spot in the city for an easy, breezy evening stroller walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

A Tasca

Order the famous grilled limpets (lapas) and the sesame-crusted tuna. Go right at their 6:30 PM opening to secure a table, as the lively, bustling atmosphere easily drowns out any toddler noise.

Cais da Sardinha

Order the fresh grilled catch of the day or the rich seafood rice. The restaurant boasts a massive outdoor deck overlooking the ocean, providing ample room to park a stroller directly at your table.

Restaurante Alcides

Order the legendary Bife à Alcides, a perfectly tender steak cooked with garlic and local red peppers. The historic dining room is spacious and the staff are incredibly warm and accommodating to families with young children.

Forneria São Dinis

Order the artisanal wood-fired pizzas made with local Azorean cheeses. It features a relaxed, modern aesthetic with wide gaps between tables, making it incredibly easy to navigate with a high chair or stroller.

Restaurante da Associação Agrícola de São Miguel

Order the absolute best steak on the island, the Bife à Associação. Located a short drive north of Ponta Delgada, this massive, airy venue is highly family-friendly with plenty of high chairs and extremely fast service.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Hotel Açores Atlântico

A luxury 5-star hotel right on the main marina featuring a heated indoor pool perfect for baby swim time. They offer premium wooden cribs, spacious family suites, and exceptionally wide elevators and hallways designed for stroller ease.

Octant Ponta Delgada

A chic, modern waterfront hotel featuring a spectacular rooftop pool and spacious, soundproofed family rooms. They provide robust baby-proofing amenities on request and a stellar breakfast buffet with fresh, organic fruit purees.

Senhora da Rosa, Tradition & Nature Hotel

A tranquil boutique hotel nestled on a historic pineapple farm just minutes from downtown Ponta Delgada. It features lush, quiet garden pathways for peaceful stroller walks, family-friendly outdoor pools, and spacious garden lodges.

👨‍👩‍👧 Book your rental car well in advance and request a mid-sized SUV to comfortably fit both your luggage and a premium stroller. While the waterfront is flat, the historic city center features traditional cobblestones; bring a stroller with excellent suspension or a high-quality baby carrier. Azorean weather can shift rapidly, so always pack a diaper bag stocked with light layers and a plastic stroller rain cover.
