What to do

Morning 9:30-10:30am / 1h

Portas da Cidade

Stroll through the iconic 18th-century city gates. The surrounding plaza is completely flat, pedestrian-only, and paved with smooth basalt tiles, making it incredibly easy for a sleek city stroller during quiet mid-morning hours.

Late morning 11am-12:30pm / 1.5h

Jardim António Borges

An enchanting 19th-century romantic garden filled with exotic trees and shaded grottos. The wide dirt pathways are highly stroller-accessible, and the dense canopy provides excellent sun protection for Mary Claire's sensitive skin.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Avenida Infante Dom Henrique

The city's premier oceanfront promenade. It features a broad, barrier-free boardwalk that is ideal for an breezy afternoon walk while Mary Claire naps soundly in her stroller to the sound of crashing waves.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Plantação de Ananases Augusto Arruda

A historic pineapple plantation featuring unique glass greenhouses. The outdoor grounds and pathways are level and stroller-friendly, offering a quiet, low-sensory environment with sweet, soothing aromas.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Jardim José do Canto

A meticulously manicured private botanical park. It is exceptionally peaceful and uncrowded, offering smooth grassy lawns under giant canopy trees that are perfect for laying down a luxury blanket for baby tummy time.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Forte de São Brás

A historic Renaissance fortress overlooking the harbor. While the interior has some steps, the surrounding exterior sea-wall paths are perfectly flat, offering refreshing Atlantic breezes and scenic harbor vistas.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Pinhal da Paz

A tranquil forest reserve just north of the city. It features beautifully manicured lawns, smooth dirt trails, and wild hydrangeas, offering a quiet natural escape with plenty of clean family restrooms and picnic areas.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Vista do Rei Viewpoint (Sete Cidades)

A breathtaking volcanic crater lake viewpoint. The main viewing platform is paved and fully accessible, allowing Will and Kathryn to take iconic family photos without navigating difficult terrain.

Day trip 10am-2pm / 4h

Parque Terra Nostra

A stunning thermal water park and botanical garden in nearby Furnas. While the hot spring pools are too warm for an infant, the endless shaded gravel pathways winding through ancient ferns provide a gorgeous, cool sensory walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

Anfiteatro Restaurant

Upscale contemporary Azorean cuisine overlooking the marina. The spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates a luxury stroller, the staff is highly welcoming to infants, and they provide wooden highchairs on request. Signature: Seared local tuna with sweet potato.

Cais da Sardinha

Ocean-facing seafood restaurant built directly on the water. It offers ramp access, a stunning wind-shielded outdoor terrace, and an early dinner service starting at 6:30 PM, perfect for beating Mary Claire's bedtime. Signature: Grilled fresh local catch with garlic butter.

Otaka

High-end Japanese-Azorean fusion fine dining. Book their earliest 7:00 PM seating in the spacious, quiet back corner of the restaurant, which provides a serene, low-lit environment where baby can sleep in her carrier. Signature: Azorean beef and truffle sashimi.

Restaurante Alcides

A legendary local institution famous for its traditional steaks. Highly welcoming to families, the friendly staff happily provides highchairs and warm water for preparing baby bottles. Signature: Bife à Alcides (garlic-infused local steak).

A Tasca

A lively, charming tavern serving gourmet Portuguese petiscos. Arrive right at the 6:30 PM opening to secure a spacious corner table before the bustling, louder dinner crowd arrives, which naturally masks any baby babble. Signature: Sesame-crusted tuna tataki.
