St. Maarten (Sint Maarten)

← back to travel board
This unique island blends vibrant Dutch spirit with chic French flair, offering calm turquoise waters and endless tropical warmth. Families will love the gentle, wave-free bays perfect for little splashers alongside thrilling, plane-spotting beaches. It is an accessible, double-sided paradise where every day promises a new, easygoing coastal escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min by pre-booked taxi or rental car from Princess Juliana International Airport to major family resorts in Simpson Bay or Little Bay.

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Maho Beach

Watch spectacular low-flying airplanes land right over the beach, a thrilling sight for older kids. Toddlers may find the jet engine noise loud, so bring earmuffs or watch from the safety of a beachfront restaurant.

Morning / 3-4h

Loterie Farm

A lush nature sanctuary at the foot of Pic Paradis featuring easy hiking trails and a stunning spring-fed pool area. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, shaded pools while surrounded by tropical trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Mullet Bay Beach

A gorgeous, wide stretch of fine white sand with calm, crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming. There is plenty of shade under the sea grape trees, making it an ideal spot for toddler naptime.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Sint Maarten Yacht Club

Grab a seat to watch the Simpson Bay drawbridge open as massive mega-yachts sail in and out of the lagoon. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the giant boats passing just feet away.

Morning / 2h

Seaside Nature Park

A peaceful 30-acre coastal park where kids can interact with rescued farm animals at the petting zoo. Toddlers can enjoy a gentle, guided pony ride along the scenic beachside trails.

Wednesday or Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Marigot Market

A colorful open-air market on the French side filled with fresh tropical fruits, sweet spices, and local crafts. It is stroller-friendly and a sensory delight for curious toddlers to explore.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Pinel Island

Take a quick, 5-minute ferry to this tiny, car-free offshore island with shallow, knee-deep waters. Toddlers can safely wade for hours in the giant, warm tide pool while parents relax on beach loungers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Grand Case Beach

A calm, wave-free beach on the French side lined with excellent eateries and soft sand. It is incredibly safe for toddlers to splash safely right at the water's edge.

Morning / 2-3h

Rainforest Adventures

Take a scenic chairlift up Sentry Hill for breathtaking 360-degree views of neighboring Caribbean islands. While the big ziplines are for older kids, toddlers will love the gentle canopy views and the Emilio Wilson Museum.

🍽️ Where to eat

Rosemary's Lolo

Order the smoky BBQ ribs, grilled chicken, and sweet, fluffy fried johnnycakes at this authentic, open-air beachside stall.

SXM Yacht Club Restaurant

Dine on kid-friendly burgers and fresh fish tacos right on the wooden deck while watching massive yachts navigate the drawbridge.

Zee Best Restaurant

Famous for its freshly baked French pastries, giant chocolate croissants, and fluffy custom omelets that kids will devour.

Chesterfields Restaurant

A casual, harbor-front spot offering great kids' meals and delicious coconut shrimp with scenic views of Great Bay Marina.

Gutside Restaurant

A cozy, family-run spot nestled in the hills serving authentic Caribbean creole fish, sweet plantains, and classic rice and peas.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat to easily hop between the Dutch and French sides, as taxis can quickly become expensive for a family. Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a lightweight stroller, as some historic walkways and sandy beach paths can be uneven.
