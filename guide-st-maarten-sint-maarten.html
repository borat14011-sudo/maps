St. Maarten (Sint Maarten)

← back to travel board
Where vibrant Dutch culture meets refined French culinary flair, St. Maarten offers a gorgeous playground of pristine beaches and gentle tropical waves. From watching giant jets soar over Maho Beach to snorkeling in shallow lagoons, this island blends Caribbean relaxation with unforgettable family memories.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 30 minutes via rental car or taxi from Princess Juliana International Airport to most major beach resorts and hotels across the island.

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Maho Beach Plane Spotting

Watch giant commercial airplanes fly incredibly low over the sand to land at the adjacent airport. Toddlers will love the roaring engines and dramatic views, but bring noise-canceling headphones for sensitive ears.

Morning / 3-4h

Loterie Farm Nature Reserve

A lush jungle sanctuary featuring hiking trails, a tree-top canopy tour, and a gorgeous spring-fed swimming pool. The shallow, cascading pool areas are perfect for splashing with toddlers under the shade of tropical trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Le Galion Beach (Baby Beach)

Protected by a coral reef, this calm, shallow bay has virtually no waves, making it the absolute safest spot for toddlers to wade and build sandcastles. Paddleboard rentals and shallow waters stretch out for yards.

Morning / 1-2h

Parotte Ville Bird Sanctuary

A delightful walk-in aviary where friendly, colorful parrots land right on your hands to feed on cups of sweet nectar. It is incredibly interactive, enclosed, and gentle enough for even the youngest toddlers to enjoy.

Wednesday or Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Marigot Open-Air Market

A bustling waterfront market on the French side filled with colorful stalls selling fresh tropical fruits, spices, and local crafts. Toddlers will enjoy the sensory sights, and it is highly stroller-friendly along the harbor.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Pinel Island Ferry & Beach

Take a quick 5-minute ferry ride to this tiny, uninhabited island paradise with shallow, warm waters and roaming iguanas. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the friendly iguanas that sun themselves near the beach chairs.

Morning / 2-3h

Rainforest Adventures Chairlift

Home to the world's steepest zip line, but families with toddlers can enjoy the gentle Soualiga Sky Explorer chairlift ride for breathtaking 360-degree island views. The estate grounds also feature a lovely museum and grassy paths.

Morning / 2-3h

Mullet Bay Beach

A picture-perfect beach with soft white sand, shady sea grape trees, and calm, crystal-clear waters. Toddlers can easily play in the soft sand near the shore while parents enjoy food from nearby local shacks.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Seaside Nature Park

A 30-acre nature park offering beachside horseback riding and a charming petting zoo with goats, ducks, and peacocks. Toddlers can safely pet the small farm animals and run around the seaside playground.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sky's the Limit Lolo

Order the famous barbecued ribs, grilled red snapper, and local side dishes like johnnycakes and mac and cheese at this classic beachside open-air eatery in Grand Case.

Zee Best Bakery & Restaurant

Indulge in warm, freshly baked French pastries, giant chocolate croissants, and fluffy crepes that toddlers will absolutely devour for breakfast.

Karakter Beach Bar

Dine right on the sand with your toes in the water; get the classic Dutch bitterballen or a fresh fish sandwich while kids play safely in the sand beside your table.

The Greenhouse Restaurant

Enjoy family-friendly American and Caribbean staples like coconut shrimp, burgers, and baby back ribs right on the lively Philipsburg boardwalk.

Emilio's

Set in a historic 17th-century sugar cane estate, order the upscale Caribbean-fusion dishes while kids enjoy the spacious, grassy outdoor historic courtyard.
