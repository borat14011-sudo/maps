Step into a real-life fairy tale where cobblestone streets wind past historic stone castles and beautiful river views. Quebec City blends old-world European charm with endless kid-friendly parks, interactive museums, and sweet maple treats. It is an easily walkable, safe, and magical playground that will captivate both toddlers and parents alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or rideshare from Jean Lesage International Airport (YQB) to Old Quebec historic district

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Château Frontenac & Dufferin Terrace

Stroll along the wide boardwalk overlooking the St. Lawrence River under the shadow of a grand castle. It is completely stroller-friendly with plenty of room for toddlers to safely run and watch street performers.

Afternoon / 2h

Quartier Petit Champlain & Funiculaire

Ride the glass funicular railway down the cliff into North America's oldest shopping district, filled with magical fairy lights. Toddlers will love the short cliffside ride, and parents can easily navigate the pedestrian-only historic streets.

Morning / 2-3h

Montmorency Falls (Parc de la Chute-Montmorency)

Marvel at a roaring waterfall that is higher than Niagara, easily viewed via a cable car ride or accessible boardwalks. The cable car is perfect for toddlers, and the mist from the falls provides great sensory fun on hot days.

Morning / 3h

Aquarium du Québec

Discover marine life including polar bears, walruses, and seals in a scenic park overlooking the river. Kids will love the outdoor splash pad, touch pools, and the multi-level indoor ocean discovery zone.

Afternoon / 2h

Plains of Abraham (Parc des Champs-de-Bataille)

Let the kids burn off energy in this massive, historic green park featuring grassy rolling hills and a fantastic wooden playground. Toddlers will enjoy chasing squirrels and watching the horse-drawn carriages pass by.

Morning / 2-3h

Musée de la Civilisation

Explore this highly interactive museum featuring hands-on exhibitions specifically designed for young children. The 'Once Upon a Time' costume and play area is a dream come true for imaginative toddlers.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Grand Marché de Québec

Visit this vibrant indoor food market to sample local cheeses, fresh berries, and maple treats. Toddlers will love the indoor family zone equipped with toys, and parents will appreciate the easy, high-quality lunch options.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Île d'Orléans Berry Farms

Take a short drive across the bridge to this idyllic agricultural island for seasonal berry picking and artisanal treats. Toddlers will adore eating giant ice cream cones dipped in real chocolate at the island's famous chocolaterie.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

La Promenade Samuel-De Champlain

Bike or stroll along this beautifully landscaped multi-use path right beside the St. Lawrence River. The modern park features gentle water fountains for splashing and expansive grassy lawns perfect for a family picnic.

🍽️ Where to eat

Aux Anciens Canadiens

Treat the family to authentic, hearty Quebecois meat pies (tourtière) in a historic, red-roofed 17th-century house.

Chez Ashton

Grab a quick, casual, and highly family-friendly meal of legendary Quebec poutine featuring squeaky cheese curds and hot gravy.

Cochon Dingue Champlain

Enjoy a relaxed breakfast of thick French toast, fresh fruit, and maple syrup in a cheerful, kid-friendly bistro setting.

Chic Shack

Savor gourmet, locally-sourced burgers and rich, artisanal milkshakes in a cozy spot right near the Chateau Frontenac.

Crêperie Petit-Château

Watch skilled chefs spin sweet and savory crêpes filled with everything from fresh strawberries to gooey cheese in a charming stone courtyard.
