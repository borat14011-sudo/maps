Stockholm

← back to travel board
Stockholm weaves sophisticated Scandinavian design with tranquil, water-wrapped landscapes that are incredibly welcoming to young families. The city's pristine air and exceptionally flat, stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks along Strandvägen make exploring with a baby effortless. You will find a culture that seamlessly integrates high-end luxury with child-friendly infrastructure, from state-of-the-art nursing rooms to stylish spots with stroller parking. It is a fairy-tale capital where royal parks and elite comforts merge for an unforgettable family getaway.
🚕 Getting there: Take the Arlanda Express train directly from Arlanda Airport to Stockholm Central Station in exactly 18 minutes, featuring level-entry boarding for strollers, or book a private Mercedes V-Class transfer (40 minutes) equipped with a premium rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Djurgården

This peaceful royal park offers wide, paved stroller paths along the canal with shady oak trees that provide natural sun protection for Mary Claire.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Vasa Museum

This world-famous maritime museum is highly stroller-accessible, offering spacious elevators and a cool, climate-controlled environment perfect for a midday nap.

Afternoon 2-5pm / 3h

Skansen Open-Air Museum

Paved, gentle pathways make pushing the stroller a breeze, while the quiet children's zoo offers delightful, low-noise sensory stimulation for Mary Claire.

Late morning 11:15am-12:30pm / 1.5h

Royal Palace (Kungliga Slottet)

Enjoy the dramatic changing of the guard from the outer courtyard; the flat cobblestones here are easy to navigate, though the dark indoor cellars should be skipped with a baby.

Lunchtime 12-2pm / 2h

Rosendals Trädgård

A tranquil orchard garden with greenhouse dining where Will and Kathryn can enjoy organic cider while Mary Claire crawls safely on the lush grass under the apple trees.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Stockholm Toy Museum (Bergrummet)

Located inside a cool, quiet underground cavern, this fully stroller-accessible museum features colorful, visual toy displays that will fascinate an infant.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Skeppsholmen Island

This quiet, museum-filled island features a peaceful waterfront loop with stunning views of Gamla Stan and wide, flat gravel paths perfect for rocking the baby to sleep.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Junibacken

A storybook theme park dedicated to Astrid Lindgren's books, featuring a dedicated indoor stroller parking area, spacious baby changing rooms, and gentle sensory playscapes.

Afternoon 2-4:30pm / 2.5h

Drottningholm Palace Gardens

The expansive formal baroque gardens of the royal family's residence are completely flat, stroller-friendly, and offer a quiet, scenic escape away from the city bustle.

Savor classic Swedish meatballs in a historic, elegant villa; they gladly provide premium highchairs and have a lovely, quiet outdoor garden terrace perfect for families.

Sturehof

Indulge in fresh seafood and pan-fried turbot; the spacious dining room accommodates strollers during early evening seatings, and the staff is remarkably accommodating to infants.

Operakällarens Bakficka
