What to do

Early morning 9-11am

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada

This mesmerizing underwater world is highly sensory for baby Mary Claire, featuring moving sidewalk tunnels under sharks. It is fully stroller-accessible, air-conditioned, and best visited early to avoid school crowds.

Morning 10am-12pm

Royal Ontario Museum

Marvel at dinosaur skeletons in wide, highly accessible galleries. The museum features clean family washrooms and quiet nursing areas, making infant care easy between exhibits.

Morning to afternoon 9:30am-1:30pm

Toronto Islands via Centre Island Ferry

Board the ferry for a breezy ride to a car-free island oasis. Paved paths are perfect for stroller walks amidst manicured gardens and stunning skyline views.

Afternoon 2-4pm

High Park

A massive green space offering beautiful walking trails and a small, quiet zoo to see llamas and peacocks. Plenty of mature oak canopy provides protective shade for baby.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Harbourfront Centre

Stroll along the clean, flat wooden boardwalks right on Lake Ontario. This scenic walk features gentle lake breezes, art installations, and plenty of shaded benches to pause for a feeding.

Mid-morning 10:30am-12:30pm

Art Gallery of Ontario

Walk through soaring glass galleries designed by Frank Gehry. The wide ramps make pushing a stroller effortless, and the gallery offers a peaceful family room for quiet downtime.

Mid-morning 10-11:30am

Allan Gardens Conservatory

A historic, glass-domed botanical conservatory filled with colorful orchids and lush tropical plants. It is a warm, peaceful, and quiet sensory experience perfect for a sleeping baby in a carrier.

Morning 9:30-11:30am

Riverdale Farm

A quiet, 7.5-acre historic farm located in the heart of Cabbagetown. Paved paths wind past ponds, wooded areas, and gentle farm animals, offering a peaceful, slow-paced morning walk.

Early afternoon 1-3pm

Distillery Historic District

A pedestrian-only village filled with Victorian industrial architecture. The cobblestones can be slightly bumpy, but a sturdy stroller handles it well as you browse art galleries and outdoor patios.

🍽️ Where to eat

Terroni Adelaide

Housed in a historic courthouse, this upscale Italian spot serves incredible thin-crust Margherita pizzas. The vibrant, bustling atmosphere easily masks any sudden baby babbles, and they gladly provide highchairs.

Cluny Bistro & Boulangerie

A gorgeous, spacious French bistro in the Distillery District serving signature duck confit. The restaurant is exceptionally spacious with plenty of room for strollers and highly accommodating staff.

El Catrin Destileria

Indulge in upscale Mexican fare and tableside guacamole on a massive, heated outdoor patio. The festive energy and ambient music level make it an ideal stress-free dining spot with an infant.

Mildred's Temple Kitchen

Famous for their ultra-fluffy wild blueberry pancakes, this modern Liberty Village restaurant is highly family-friendly. Book an early weekend brunch slot to secure spacious booth seating.

Piano Piano Harbord Street

A chic, whimsically designed Italian restaurant serving premium wood-fired pizzas and sweet potato agnolotti. It boasts a lively yet polished crowd, welcoming families with highchairs and warm hospitality.
