Toronto

Toronto welcomes families with its sparkling waterfront, sprawling green spaces, and a wonderfully diverse cultural mosaic. From soaring skyscrapers to ferry rides on Lake Ontario, the city offers a clean, safe, and exciting urban adventure. It is incredibly easy to navigate with little ones, making every neighborhood a new playground.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min UP Express train ride to Union Station or ~30-45 min taxi to downtown hotels

What to do

Morning / 1.5-2h

CN Tower

Soar 1,136 feet in a glass-walled elevator for panoramic views of the entire city and lake. Toddlers will love crawling on the reinforced Glass Floor section.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Toronto Island Park

Take a scenic 15-minute ferry ride to an car-free island oasis complete with beaches and a vintage amusement park. It is incredibly stroller-friendly with plenty of grassy spots for toddlers to run free.

Morning / 2h

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada

Walk beneath giant sharks and sea turtles in the immersive underwater tunnel. The dedicated toddler play area with water tables and a crawl-through pop-up tank is a major hit.

Afternoon / 2-3h

High Park & Jamie Bell Adventure Playground

Explore Toronto's largest park featuring a free domestic animal zoo and a massive, castle-themed wooden playground. Toddlers will love feeding the ducks at Grenadier Pond.

Morning / 1.5-2h

St. Lawrence Market

Browse the historic, bustling food stalls to sample local cheeses, fresh fruit, and famous peameal bacon sandwiches. Toddlers will enjoy the sensory experience and the tasty, soft bakery treats.

Morning / 1-2h

Riverdale Farm

Step back in time at this free, 7.5-acre historic working farm located right in the heart of the city. Toddlers can get up close to cows, sheep, goats, and chickens along the shaded pathways.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Royal Ontario Museum

Marvel at massive dinosaur skeletons and sparkling gems in Canada's largest museum. The hands-on discovery gallery offers tactile learning and costumes perfect for curious toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1.5-2h

Distillery Historic District

Stroll along pedestrian-only cobblestone streets lined with Victorian-era buildings, art installations, and sweet shops. It is completely safe from cars, making it stress-free for toddlers to wander.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Toronto Zoo

Discover over 5,000 animals spread across specialized geographic pavilions, including giant pandas and polar bears. Rent a pull-along wagon at the entrance to keep toddlers comfortable during this extensive walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mildred's Temple Kitchen

Order the world-famous, ultra-fluffy Mrs. Biederhof's Blueberry Pancakes served with warm maple syrup.

Pizzeria Libretto

Get the classic Margherita pizza cooked in a wood-fired oven, which arrives at the table lightning-fast for hungry kids.

Pai Northern Thai Kitchen

Savor the kid-approved Pad Thai with chicken, served in a lively, colorful space that naturally masks toddler noise.

Rudy

Grab a Rude Dude double cheeseburger and a side of crispy crinkle-cut fries for a quick, mess-friendly family meal.

The Senator Diner

Treat the family to classic organic beef burgers, thick milkshakes, and home-style mac and cheese in Toronto's historic jazz-era diner.
