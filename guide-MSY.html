New Orleans

← back to travel board
New Orleans welcomes families with a sensory explosion of jazz rhythms, sweet powdered sugar, and moss-draped oak trees. Beneath the lively festival spirit lies a surprisingly gentle destination packed with streetcars, shady parks, and interactive bayou adventures. It is a city where every corner sings, promising magic for toddlers and parents alike.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Louisiana Children's Museum in City Park

Set in beautiful City Park, this indoor-outdoor museum features sensory exhibits, a water music garden, and a mock Mississippi River. Toddlers will love the dedicated soft-play zones and the outdoor interpretive wetlands.

Afternoon / 1h

St. Charles Avenue Streetcar Ride

Climb aboard the world's oldest continuously operating streetcar for a breezy, nostalgic ride under a canopy of historic live oaks. Toddlers adore the gentle rumble and clatter, but be sure to fold your stroller before boarding.

Morning / 3h

Audubon Zoo

Located in Uptown, this historic zoo features a realistic swamp exhibit with white alligators and a wonderful splash park called Cool Zoo. Toddlers can safely wander the shaded, paved pathways to spot lazy jaguars and elephants.

Morning / 1-2h

The French Market

Wander through this bustling open-air market filled with colorful local crafts, music, and quick bites. It is highly sensory, so visit early in the day when crowds are thin and vendors are just opening.

Morning / 2h

Barataria Preserve

Walk along wooden boardwalks over the swamp to spot wild alligators, turtles, and herons safely. The flat, stroller-friendly paths make it incredibly easy for little legs to explore Louisiana's natural bayous.

Afternoon / 2h

Storyland at City Park

This fairytale-themed playground features larger-than-life sculptural play areas where toddlers can climb inside Captain Hook's ship or slide down the mouth of a giant dragon. It is fully fenced, allowing parents to relax under the shade of ancient oak trees.

Afternoon / 2h

Steamboat Natchez Harbor Cruise

Steam down the Mississippi River on an authentic paddlewheeler while listening to live calliope music. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant, churning red wheel, and the indoor decks offer great air-conditioned relief.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Jackson Square Street Performers

Watch brass bands, magicians, and puppeteers perform in the pedestrian-only plaza right in front of the cathedral. It is an energetic, car-free zone where toddlers can dance freely and clap along to the music.

Afternoon / 45m

Preservation Hall Matinee

Introduce your little ones to authentic acoustic jazz in an intimate, historic room. Opt for an afternoon matinee show, which is shorter and welcomes families with open arms and toe-tapping rhythms.

Where to eat

  • Cafe Du Monde Beignets — Treat the family to warm, pillowy fried dough buried in a mountain of powdered sugar, but grab an outdoor table to easily sweep up the toddler-induced sugar storm.
  • Parkway Bakery & Tavern Po'Boys — Enjoy legendary, overstuffed French bread sandwiches in a casual, family-friendly setting with a spacious outdoor patio perfect for messy eaters.
  • Hansen's Sno-Bliz — Cool down with historic, shaved-to-order ice drenched in homemade syrups, a soft and sweet treat that toddlers can easily eat with a spoon.
  • Central Grocery Muffulettas — Grab a classic, hearty sesame-bread sandwich layered with meats and olive salad, perfect for slicing up and sharing picnic-style at nearby Woldenberg Park.
👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a compact, easily foldable stroller for navigating narrow, bumpy French Quarter sidewalks and boarding the historic streetcars. Avoid Bourbon Street entirely with young children, focusing instead on the family-friendly daytime charms of the Garden District and City Park.
