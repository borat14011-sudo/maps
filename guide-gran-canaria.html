Gran Canaria

← back to travel board
Gran Canaria welcomes families with a mini-continent of diverse landscapes, ranging from Saharan-like sand dunes to lush, forested ravines. With year-round spring weather, sheltered tidal pools, and stroller-friendly coastal promenades, it is an effortless paradise for travelers of all ages. Here, lazy beach days blend seamlessly with mountain explorations and vibrant local food markets.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi to Las Palmas beach hotels, or ~30 min to the southern Maspalomas resorts.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Maspalomas Dunes

Walk along the edge of these giant golden sand dunes that look like a mini Sahara desert. Toddlers will love playing in the soft sand, but stick to the designated boardwalk paths to keep little legs from tiring out.

Morning / 2-3h

Poema del Mar Aquarium

This world-class modern aquarium in Las Palmas features a massive curved glass deep-sea exhibit that will leave children spellbound. It is completely stroller-accessible, making it an easy and mesmerizing indoor escape for toddlers.

Morning / 3-4h

Palmitos Park

Explore a lush botanical garden and zoo nestled in a valley, featuring exotic birds, dolphins, and a giant butterfly house. The paved paths are highly stroller-friendly, though there are some moderate inclines to navigate.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de Las Canteras

This spectacular golden-sand city beach is protected by a natural reef, keeping the water calm and shallow for splashing toddlers. The wide, car-free promenade is perfect for a stroller stroll lined with superb ice cream shops.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado del Puerto

Visit this vibrant iron-structured food market where parents can sample delicious local tapas while kids enjoy fresh fruit juices. The atmosphere is bustling and casual, making toddler noise go completely unnoticed.

Morning / 1-2h

Degollada de la Goleta Viewpoint

Marvel at the iconic Roque Nublo volcanic monolith from this easily accessible mountain viewpoint. It offers dramatic, out-of-this-world views without requiring a steep, challenging hike for toddlers.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Puerto de Mogán

Stroll through this picturesque coastal village filled with colorful houses, canal bridges, and blooming bougainvillea. It is completely pedestrianized and features a sheltered, shallow golden-sand beach that is perfect for toddler wading.

Morning / 1-2h

Hacienda La Rekompensa

Tour a beautiful working banana farm with over 7,000 plants and interact with friendly farm animals. Toddlers will love the wide open spaces and the chance to taste sweet, locally grown Canary bananas.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Los Azulejos de Veneguera

Gaze at these vibrant, multi-colored volcanic rock formations painted in shades of blue, green, and red by natural mineral deposits. It is a quick, dazzling roadside stop that requires no heavy walking for little feet.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante El Herreño

Order the traditional papas arrugadas con mojo (wrinkled potatoes with mild red and green sauces) and fresh local goat cheese.

La Marinera

Feast on fresh, locally caught sea bass baked in salt, served alongside a kid-friendly basket of bread and garlic aioli.

Restaurante Tagoror

Try the slow-roasted Canarian pork chops inside a spectacular, cool cave restaurant carved directly into the mountain rock.

Cofradía de Pescadores de Mogán

Enjoy the crispy fried calamari and grilled octopus right next to the harbor where the fishing boats come in.

Helamore

Treat the family to authentic, artisanal Italian gelato made with local tropical fruits like Canary banana and sweet mango.
