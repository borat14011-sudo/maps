What to do

Late afternoon 5-7pm

Maspalomas Dunes

These breathtaking Sahara-style dunes are a sight to behold. For an infant-friendly experience, avoid dragging a stroller through the sand and instead stroll along the paved pedestrian promenade of Meloneras at sunset, offering dramatic views and gentle ocean breezes.

Morning 10am-12pm

Catedral de Santa Ana & Museo Diocesano de Arte Sacro

Located in the historic Vegueta district of Las Palmas, this stunning cathedral features a smooth elevator that takes you up to the tower for panoramic views. The surrounding plaza is entirely pedestrian-friendly, ideal for a quiet afternoon stroll with a sleeping baby.

Morning 10:30am-12:30pm

Cueva Pintada Museum & Archaeological Park

Explore pre-Hispanic Canarian history at this beautifully preserved archaeological site in Gáldar. The museum is highly accessible with wide, stroller-friendly wooden ramps and climate-controlled indoor exhibition spaces that offer cool relief from the midday sun.

Morning 9-11:30am

Puerto de Mogán

Often called Little Venice, this peaceful fishing village features calm, shallow waters and flat, car-free streets adorned with colorful bougainvillea. It is highly stroller-accessible, with plenty of shaded seaside cafes perfect for feeding baby Mary Claire.

Morning 9-11am

Playa de Las Canteras

A spectacular golden-sand beach protected by a natural reef, making the waters incredibly calm and safe for dipping baby's toes. The parallel paved promenade stretches for miles, offering a completely flat, stroller-friendly walk lined with excellent cafes.

Early morning 9-11am

Jardín Botánico Canario Viera y Clavijo

Spain's largest botanical garden showcases exotic Macaronesian flora. While the lower ravine trails can be rugged, the upper entrance features flat, paved pathways winding through spectacular cactus gardens and shady palm groves ideal for a peaceful morning walk.

Morning 10am-1pm

Palmitos Park

A lush botanical oasis and aviary nestled in a scenic valley. The park features paved pathways throughout, plenty of shaded resting benches, and private nursing areas, making it a highly comfortable and visually engaging day out for parents and baby.

Morning 9:30-10:30am

Roque Nublo Viewpoint from La Goleta

While the steep hike to the volcanic monolith itself is too strenuous for a stroller, you can enjoy majestic views of Roque Nublo from the paved paths at the La Goleta parking lot and viewpoint. Use a premium baby carrier for a short, scenic walk in the fresh pine air.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm

Roque Bentayga Visitor Centre

This ancient sacred volcanic peak features an incredibly informative and fully accessible visitor center. Learn about pre-Hispanic worship through interactive displays in a quiet, air-conditioned environment that is gentle and engaging for families.

🍽️ Where to eat

Papi's Grill

Located near the beachfront of Playa del Inglés, this stylish dining venue surprises with its sophisticated decor and premium grilled meats. The spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates large strollers, and the staff is exceptionally welcoming to families during the early dinner hours.

La Marinera

A legendary local seafood institution situated at the northern end of Playa de Las Canteras. Request a table on the wide oceanfront terrace to enjoy fresh, salt-baked sea bass and authentic Canarian wrinkly potatoes while baby relaxes in a highchair provided by the attentive staff.

Restaurante Casa Montesdeoca

Set within a romantic, historic 14th-century courtyard in Las Palmas, this upscale restaurant serves delicate Spanish-Mediterranean cuisine. The peaceful open-air courtyard provides a quiet, low-noise sanctuary that is perfect for a relaxed family dinner with an infant.

Restaurante Samsara

Located near the Maspalomas dunes, this high-end Asian-fusion restaurant boasts a beautifully curated tropical garden terrace. Securing an early 7:30pm reservation allows you to dine in a serene atmosphere before the late-night crowds arrive, with spacious seating suitable for baby.

Restaurante El Herreño

A traditional, spacious restaurant in the heart of the historic Vegueta neighborhood. Its airy, open-concept dining room is perfect for maneuvering a stroller, and the kitchen serves comforting local Canarian dishes like goat cheese with honey in a casual yet refined setting.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Gloria Palace Royal Hotel & Spa

$220-350/night

Overlooking the tranquil waters of Amadores Beach, this luxurious 4-star superior hotel offers spectacular stone architecture and spacious family suites. It features heated infinity pools, complimentary high-quality cribs on request, and easy elevator access to the beach promenade below.

Lopesan Baobab Resort

$280-400/night

A spectacular 5-star African-themed luxury resort in Meloneras. This property boasts massive family suites, a lazy river, multiple tropical swimming pools, and dedicated baby amenities including premium cribs, making it an absolute paradise for parents looking for upscale convenience.

Radisson Blu Resort, Gran Canaria

$250-380/night

Located in quiet Arguineguín, this luxury resort offers large family suites featuring fully equipped kitchens and deep blackout curtains perfect for Mary Claire's nap schedule. Parents will love the private balconies, heated saltwater pools, and direct, stroller-friendly beach access.
