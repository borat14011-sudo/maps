Savannah

Savannah wraps visitors in a timeless embrace of draped Spanish moss, brick-paved lanes, and majestic antebellum architecture. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the historic district offers a slow-paced sensory playground of manicured squares and gas-lit avenues. The wide, flat, stroller-friendly paved pathways of Forsyth Park provide the perfect shaded sanctuary for morning walks past the iconic cast-iron fountain. Here, coastal Georgia's gentle breezes meet high-end hospitality, ensuring a seamless, upscale getaway for the modern young family.
🚕 Getting there: Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport (SAV) to the Historic District is a seamless 20-minute drive; book a private luxury SUV transfer via Savannah Car Service equipped with a pre-installed, rear-facing Nuna Rava car seat.

What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Forsyth Park

The expansive 30-acre green space features wide, paved, stroller-ready walking paths ideal for Mary Claire's morning stroller nap under the shade of massive live oaks.

Late morning 10:30-11:30am / 1h

Chippewa Square

A peaceful historic square with ample benches under Spanish moss, perfect for a calm bottle feeding session away from the bustling River Street crowds.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Owens-Thomas House & Slave Quarters

A stunning English Regency mansion; while the upper floors are stairs-only, the serene parterre garden and museum courtyard offer a quiet, stroller-accessible sensory experience.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Savannah Botanical Gardens

Escape the city center for a tranquil stroll along the paved paths and wooden boardwalks, featuring beautiful rose gardens and a peaceful pond that baby will love watching.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Bonaventure Cemetery

Stroll along the wide, flat dirt pathways lined with scenic, moss-draped historic tombs; it offers a hushed, park-like environment ideal for a long, quiet afternoon stroller nap.

Midday 1-2:30pm / 1.5h

Telfair Academy

America's oldest public art museum offers a fully climate-controlled interior with spacious galleries and elevator access, providing a cool sanctuary during the midday Savannah heat.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Skidaway Island State Park

The Sandpiper Trail loop features a flat, barrier-free boardwalk over scenic salt marshes, offering a gentle nature walk with zero stroller bumps and serene coastal views.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Juliette Gordon Low Birthplace

A meticulously restored Victorian mansion that features elevator access for strollers and a beautiful, quiet courtyard garden perfect for a soothing feeding break.

Morning 10-11am / 1h

Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Baptist

Take in the breathtaking French Gothic architecture in a cool, quiet, and reverent space; the soothing acoustics are perfect for calming a fussy baby, and ramp access is available.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Grey

Savor elevated Southern classics like crab rice in a beautifully restored 1930s Greyhound terminal; book an early 5:30 PM reservation in the spacious, high-ceilinged Main Hall where ambient noise easily masks baby babbles.

The Olde Pink House

Indulge in legendary crispy scored flounder in an elegant 18th-century mansion; request a spacious ground-floor table during the early 5:00 PM seating, where staff warmly welcome families and provide highchairs.

Elizabeth on 37th

Enjoy fine coastal dining featuring spicy Savannah red rice inside a grand Southern mansion; their early seating offers a quiet atmosphere with spacious tables that easily accommodate a highchair or luxury travel crib.

Collins Quarter

Famous for its signature spiced lavender mocha and avocado smash, this stylish cafe offers a vibrant outdoor terrace on Bull Street, perfect for family watching without worrying about baby noise levels.

Vic’s on the River

Feast on rich crawfish beignets and shrimp and grits with scenic river views; the restaurant features a stroller-friendly elevator entrance and a spacious dining room that keeps family dining refined yet comfortable.
