Savannah

← back to travel board
Savannah wraps families in a warm embrace of Spanish-moss-draped plazas, historic charm, and coastal sea breezes. Cobblestone streets lead to playground-filled squares, while sweet treats and friendly trolley drivers make exploring effortless. It is a slow-paced wonderland where history feels like a storybook come to life for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or rideshare from Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport (SAV) to the historic downtown district.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Forsyth Park

Let kids run wild on the two shaded playgrounds and marvel at the famous ornate fountain. It's stroller-friendly with wide paved paths, perfect for toddlers to toddle safely under the giant oaks.

Morning / 2h

Old Town Trolley Tour

Hop on and off this open-air trolley to see the sights without tiring little legs. Toddlers will love the breezy ride and the friendly, animated tour guides.

Morning / 2h

Savannah Children's Museum

This unique, entirely outdoor museum is built in the ruins of an old railroad yard. Toddlers will love the sensory garden, giant maze, and hands-on building blocks.

Morning / 2-3h

Oatland Island Wildlife Center

Walk the stroller-friendly 1.5-mile maritime forest trail to spot wolves, cougars, and bison in natural habitats. Toddlers will especially love visiting the barnyard animals at the trail's end.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Tybee Island Light Station and Museum

Drive out to this historic lighthouse where kids can play in the sand and explore the coastal museum. Toddlers will love splashing in the calm, shallow waves of nearby North Beach.

Afternoon / 1h

Leopold's Ice Cream

Treat the family to world-famous, scratch-made ice cream in a retro 1919 parlor setting. The classic music and sweet aromas will keep toddlers mesmerized while waiting in the queue.

Afternoon / 2h

Georgia State Railroad Museum

Explore historic steam locomotives and take a real train ride on select days. The open-spaced railyard gives toddlers plenty of room to roam safely.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

River Street and Savannah’s Waving Girl Statue

Watch giant container ships glide down the Savannah River and watch candy-making through the glass at River Street Sweets. Keep an eye on toddlers here, as the historic cobblestones can be uneven for strollers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Savannah City Market

A four-block open-air pedestrian promenade filled with local art, music, and sweet treats. Toddlers will enjoy the lively, car-free space to stretch their legs and listen to street musicians.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Pirate's House

Order the honey pecan fried chicken for the adults and classic mac and cheese for the kids in this legendary, pirate-themed historic tavern.

Little Duck Diner

Get the signature 'Waffle On a Stick' or gourmet grilled cheese in this chic, vintage-inspired diner that toddlers adore for its rubber duck theme.

Vic's on the River

Order the famous southern shrimp and grits while enjoying a table with a window view of the passing riverboats.

Clary's Cafe

Grab a hearty breakfast of blueberry pancakes and corned beef hash at this iconic, colorful, and family-friendly neighborhood diner.

Treylor Park

Try the unique PB&J chicken wings or the family-friendly waffle fries nachos in their casual, stroller-friendly outdoor backyard patio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Savannah’s squares are perfect for quick toddler resets, but the historic cobblestones along River Street can be very bumpy, so opt for a sturdy stroller with large wheels. Keep some wet wipes handy for sticky fingers after visiting the city's famous praline and ice cream shops.
