Valencia

← back to travel board
Valencia wraps families in coastal warmth, blending futuristic architecture with historic charm and lush green spaces. Children can play on a giant climbable Gulliver, splash in shallow sea waves, and taste sweet local horchata. It is an effortlessly walkable, stroller-friendly paradise where Mediterranean life slows down to a child's pace.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or direct Metrovalencia Line 3 or 5 ride to the city center, or ~25 min taxi to the El Cabanyal beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

City of Arts and Sciences

Marvel at the spectacular futuristic buildings designed by Santiago Calatrava. Toddlers will love strolling along the wide, car-free pathways and watching the glass-bottomed kayak boats glide across the shallow outdoor pools.

Morning / 3h

Oceanogràfic

Explore Europe's largest aquarium, home to belugas, sea lions, and penguins. The massive, immersive glass tunnels offer toddlers an exciting, safe way to watch sharks and rays swim right overhead.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Gulliver Park

Climb a giant scale model of Lemuel Gulliver tied down by the Lilliputians, transformed into an incredible playground. While older kids tackle the steep slides, toddlers love exploring the giant's boots and running along the soft-surface ground.

Afternoon / 2h

Turia Gardens

Spend an afternoon in this lush green park built in a diverted riverbed that snakes through the entire city. It is a car-free haven where families can rent quad-cycles or let toddlers run wild on several dedicated playgrounds.

Morning / 1-2h

Central Market (Mercado Central)

Introduce little ones to a feast of sights and smells inside one of Europe's largest covered markets. It is highly toddler-friendly for picking up cheap, healthy snacks like fresh pre-cut fruit cups and local cheeses.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Malvarrosa Beach

Take a break on this expansive, soft-sand beach right in the city. The calm, shallow waves make it an exceptionally safe and easy spot for toddlers to splash around and build sandcastles.

Morning / 3-4h

Bioparc Valencia

Walk through an immersive, barrier-free zoo designed to replicate African habitats. Toddlers can get incredibly close to lemurs, giraffes, and elephants along paved, highly stroller-friendly pathways.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Cabecera Park (Parque de Cabecera)

Relax by the beautiful lake located at the western end of the Turia Gardens. Toddlers will love watching the ducks swim up to the banks and seeing the giant swan-shaped pedal boats pass by.

Evening / 1h

Plaza de la Virgen

Soak up the historic atmosphere in this fully pedestrianized central square. It is a fantastic safe space to let your toddler chase pigeons while you enjoy a quiet outdoor coffee next to the beautiful Turia Fountain.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Pepica

Order the legendary, kid-friendly traditional chicken and vegetable paella at this historic beachfront institution.

Horchatería Santa Catalina

Treat the family to cool, creamy tiger-nut milk called horchata alongside warm, sugar-dusted fartons for dipping.

La Más Bonita Patacona

Enjoy giant slices of homemade cake and fresh fruit juices in a beautifully styled, toddler-friendly beachfront backyard patio.

San Tommaso

Grab a table at this cozy Italian gem in the old town for hand-tossed Margherita pizzas and fresh pasta that toddlers love.

Mercatbar by Quique Dacosta

Sample creative but highly approachable tapas like Iberian ham croquetas and patatas bravas in a lively, welcoming atmosphere.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a sturdy but lightweight stroller because the vast Turia Gardens and old town plazas are highly walkable, though some historic streets have uneven cobblestones. Keep in mind that Spanish dining hours trend late, so carry plenty of toddler snacks to bridge the gap before restaurants reopen for dinner around 8:00 PM.
