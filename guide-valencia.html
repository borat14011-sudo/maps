Valencia

← back to travel board
Valencia blends futuristic architecture with centuries-old charm, offering a vibrant yet relaxed Mediterranean escape. The Turia Park, a sprawling stroller-friendly promenade carved from a former riverbed, is perfect for morning walks with baby Mary Claire. For luxury, the beachfront Malvarrosa provides calm lagoon-like waters and soft sand, ideal for infant paddling. The City of Arts and Sciences dazzles with its sweeping white structures, and many hotels offer blackout curtains and cribs for seamless sleep.
🚕 Getting there: Valencia Airport to hotel strip: 15-20 minutes by private transfer with pre-booked infant car seat and stroller (e.g., Welcome Pickups or Suntransfers).

What to do

Morning 10am-12pm

Oceanogràfic

Europe's largest aquarium—stroller-accessible, cool indoor spaces, and calm dolphin shows that fascinate infants. Avoid feeding times if noise-sensitive.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Turia Park

A 9km green corridor with smooth paths, shaded playgrounds, and abundant benches. Perfect for a leisurely stroll or picnic with baby.

Morning 9-11am

Malvarrosa Beach

Sandy, shallow waters and lifeguards; rent a cabana for shade. Quiet weekday mornings are best for infant play in calm waves.

Early 9-11am

Bioparc Valencia

Immersive zoo where animals roam without visible barriers—stroller paths, baby-changing facilities, and shaded viewing areas. Low noise levels.

2-hour session, midday

Hemisfèric

IMAX dome and planetarium; choose the gentle 'Ocean' film which mesmerizes infants. Stroller parking available, earplugs for sensitive ears.

2-3 hours, afternoon

Museu de les Ciències Príncipe Felipe

Interactive science museum with hands-on zones; spacious, stroller-friendly, and a calm 'baby area' with soft play.

Late morning 11am-12pm

Plaza de la Virgen

Historic square with fountains and outdoor cafés—shaded edges for baby, occasional street musicians (not too loud). Good for a short sit.

Late afternoon 5-7pm

Albufera Natural Park

Boat ride on a calm lagoon; book a private boat with sun canopy. See rice fields and birdlife. Quiet, gentle, ideal for infant naps.

30 minutes, morning

La Lonja de la Seda

UNESCO Gothic silk exchange—cobblestone courtyard, but interior is cool and stroller-friendly. Short visit to admire architecture.

🍽️ Where to eat

Canalla Bistro

Ricard Camarena's casual spot—signature 'canalla rice' (valencian paella). Highchairs, outdoor terrace, early seating at 7pm, moderate noise.

La Salita

Bib Gourmand restaurant with a tasting menu—infant-friendly (announce in booking), highchair, quiet ambience, garden terrace.

Central Market

Bustling food market—stroller navigable in aisles, buy fresh fruit and jamón. Avoid peak hours (12-2pm) to reduce noise. No highchairs but many stools.

Ca' Sento

Michelin-starred fine dining—highchairs upon request, quiet elegance, early seating (7:30pm). Signature: saffron-infused suckling pig.

Restaurante El Poblet

Quique Dacosta's tasting menu—book private dining room for family, highchair, blackout curtains, and baby-friendly service. No age policy.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pharmacies (farmacias) are abundant in Valencia; stock up on formula and infant paracetamol at Farmacia Cruz Verde near City of Arts. Stroller terrain: flat boardwalk at Malvarrosa and Turia Park, but old town cobblestones can be bumpy—use a robust stroller or baby carrier. For medical care, Hospital Quirónsalud Valencia has a pediatric ER and English-speaking staff. Summer heat is intense (30°C+); use a UV sunshade on the stroller and schedule outdoor activities for early morning or late afternoon.
