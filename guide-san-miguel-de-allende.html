San Miguel de Allende

← back to travel board
Nestled in Mexico's central highlands, San Miguel de Allende enchants families with its rose-colored spires, colonial plazas, and artistic soul. Little ones will love chasing bubbles in the sun-drenched gardens, while parents soak in the rich culture and striking architecture. It is a slow-paced, visually stunning playground that feels straight out of a fairy tale.
🚕 Getting there: ~1.5 hour private shuttle or taxi from Del Bajío International Airport (BJX) directly to your colonial-zone hotel

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parroquia de San Miguel Arcángel

Marvel at the towering, pink neo-Gothic church that looks like a giant sandcastle. Toddlers will love running around the open, pedestrian-only plaza (El Jardín) directly in front of it.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Juárez

This lush, green oasis features paved pathways, towering French-style gardens, and a fantastic gated playground area. It is the perfect spot for toddlers to burn off energy on the swings while parents enjoy the local art vendors.

Morning / 2h

El Charco del Ingenio

A massive botanical garden and nature reserve filled with hundreds of species of cacti and a conservatory. Toddler note: Stick to the main flat dirt paths and use a baby carrier rather than a stroller.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Museo la Esquina (Mexican Toy Museum)

This colorful museum houses thousands of handmade, traditional Mexican toys spread across multiple vibrant rooms. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the whimsical wooden ferris wheels, puppets, and miniature trains.

Midday / 2h

Fábrica La Aurora

A former textile mill beautifully converted into art galleries, design shops, and cafes. The spacious, wide hallways are highly stroller-friendly, and the outdoor duck ponds are a huge hit with little kids.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de Artesanías

A vibrant, narrow market alleyway bursting with colorful textiles, painted tin mirrors, and wooden toys. Toddlers will love the bright colors and hanging decorations, but keep them close as some pathways are tight.

Morning / 2-3h

Rancho Vía Orgánica

An eco-farm and educational center located just outside of town where kids can interact with farm animals and walk through organic crops. Toddlers will love feeding the chickens and seeing the friendly goats.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Churros at San Agustín

Indulge in a classic sweet treat at this famous cafe owned by a famous Mexican actress. Toddlers will love dipping the warm, sugar-dusted churros into mild, creamy Spanish hot chocolate.

Afternoon / 1h

Biblioteca Pública courtyard

The second-largest bilingual library in Mexico features a stunning, peaceful courtyard with a fountain. There is a dedicated children's book room where toddlers can quietly look through colorful picture books.

🍽️ Where to eat

Hecho en México

Order the crowd-pleasing chicken fajitas and fresh guacamole in their spacious, casual courtyard that is highly welcoming to noisy toddlers.

Café de la Parroquia

Get the traditional chilaquiles for breakfast while the kids feast on fluffy, fruit-topped pancakes in a sunny, garden-like setting.

La Grotta

Order the delicious, thin-crust wood-fired Margherita pizza which is an absolute favorite for picky toddler palates.

Inside Café

Get the famous, thick-cut fluffy French toast topped with fresh berries and paired with an expertly crafted espresso for parents.

Chamonix

Treat the family to fresh homemade pasta or grilled chicken breast in a quiet, intimate, and shaded outdoor patio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller for a sturdy baby carrier, as the historic cobblestones and narrow sidewalks are highly uneven and tough to navigate on wheels. Always carry cash in pesos for small market treats, and plan your outdoor activities for the cooler morning hours to beat the high-altitude midday sun.
