What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Miami Beach Boardwalk

This flat, wooden and paved oceanfront pathway offers miles of scenic strolling. It is entirely stroller-accessible, cooled by ocean breezes, and perfect for an early morning walk with Mary Claire before the sun gets too intense.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden

A massive, lush sanctuary featuring paved paths that wind through rare tropical plants. It offers abundant shade, a dedicated butterfly conservatory, and a quiet, tranquil environment ideal for stroller naps.

Mid-day 12-2pm / 2h

Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science

This world-class facility is fully air-conditioned and features a stunning, multi-level Gulf Stream aquarium. The mesmerizing, glowing marine displays are highly engaging for an infant's sensory development, and the entire facility is stroller-friendly with wide elevators.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Crandon Park Beach

Located on Key Biscayne, this beach is famous for its offshore sandbar which creates incredibly calm, shallow, wave-free waters. It is the ultimate spot in Miami for a baby's first gentle dip in the ocean, framed by plenty of shaded palm trees.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Pérez Art Museum Miami (PAMM)

This contemporary art museum features wide, smooth, stroller-friendly galleries and a stunning outdoor shaded plaza with hanging gardens overlooking Biscayne Bay. It is quiet, sophisticated, and perfect for design-loving parents with a baby in tow.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Vizcaya Museum and Gardens

An Italian Renaissance-style villa with expansive, manicured gardens. While the main house has stairs, the outdoor paved garden paths are highly scenic and offer many shaded, quiet nooks next to Biscayne Bay for peaceful resting.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Matheson Hammock Park

Featuring a historic, man-made atoll pool flushed naturally by the tides of Biscayne Bay, this park offers warm, current-free, dead-calm saltwater. It is incredibly safe for an infant to splash around safely under the shade of mature live oaks.

Late afternoon 5-7pm / 2h

South Pointe Park

Situated at the southern tip of Miami Beach, this park offers panoramic cruise ship views, wide paved walking paths, and a lush grassy hill. There is a gentle, interactive water splash pad where Mary Claire can cool off away from the sandy beach.

Early morning 11am-12:30pm / 1.5h

Wynwood Walls

An open-air museum dedicated to world-class street art. Visiting right at opening allows you to stroll the paved, flat outdoor galleries comfortably with a stroller, enjoying the vibrant colors before the midday heat and crowds arrive.

🍽️ Where to eat

Call Me Gaby

This upscale South of Fifth Italian gem features a gorgeous, breezy outdoor garden terrace. Highly welcoming to families during early dinner seatings, they offer gourmet pinsas and fresh pastas in a sophisticated yet low-noise environment.

Mandolin Aegean Bistro

Set in a stunning, blue-and-white 1940s home in the Design District, this hotspot offers world-class Greek-Turkish cuisine. The outdoor courtyard is bustling and white-noise friendly, making it easy to dine elegantly with Mary Claire in her stroller.

Joe's Stone Crab

A legendary Miami institution. Opt for an early lunch in the main dining room or head to Joe's Take Away next door for their signature stone crab claws and key lime pie to enjoy as a luxury picnic at nearby South Pointe Park.

The Lido Bayside Grill

Located at The Standard, this bayside eatery offers sweeping waterfront views and healthy Mediterranean-inspired dishes. The gentle, lapping water and open-air sea breeze provide natural white noise, keeping baby relaxed during your meal.

Le Zoo

A chic French brasserie located inside the luxury Bal Harbour Shops. The lush outdoor terrace is highly stroller-accessible and perfect for an upscale, quiet lunch of steak frites and oysters after some light shopping.
