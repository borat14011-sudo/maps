What to do

Morning / 3h

Miami Children's Museum

Located on Watson Island, this interactive museum features two floors of hands-on exhibits including a giant sandcastle and a cruise ship play area. It is perfect for toddlers who can safely explore the dedicated sensory rooms and soft play zones.

Morning / 3-4h

Crandon Park Beach

This tranquil beach on Key Biscayne features shallow, calm waters protected by an offshore sandbar, making it incredibly safe for little ones. Toddlers will love splashing in the gentle surf and exploring the nearby playground shaded by palm trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden

Wander through a lush, 83-acre paradise filled with exotic plants, giant water lilies, and a butterfly conservatory. Toddlers can run safely along the paved paths and marvel at the colorful wings in the Wings of the Tropics exhibit.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Wynwood Walls

An outdoor museum showcasing massive, colorful murals by world-renowned street artists that will captivate eyes of all ages. Bring a stroller for easy navigation along the flat, pedestrian-only concrete paths.

Morning / 4h

Zoo Miami

As the only tropical zoo in the continental US, this open-air park groups animals by territory with no-cage moated exhibits. Rent a shaded safari cycle with a built-in toddler seat to explore the massive pathways comfortably.

Afternoon / 3h

Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science

This state-of-the-art facility features a 500,000-gallon Gulf Stream aquarium spanning three floors and a planetarium. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the hammerhead sharks swimming directly overhead in the massive deep ocean lens.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

Located at the tip of Key Biscayne, this scenic park offers quiet beaches and a historic 1825 lighthouse. Toddlers will enjoy strolling the paved seawall and watching boats pass by in the gentle sea breeze.

Afternoon / 2h

Little Havana's Calle Ocho

Immerse your family in Cuban culture with live music, vibrant street art, and the famous Domino Park. Stop by a ventanita for a sweet treat; toddlers will love watching the colorful rooster sculptures along the sidewalk.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Lincoln Road Mall

A lively, pedestrian-only outdoor mall lined with shops, cafes, and beautiful fountains. It is completely car-free, giving toddlers plenty of safe space to walk and stretch their legs while you grab an outdoor bite.

🍽️ Where to eat

Versailles Restaurant

Order the classic Cuban sandwich and sweet, crispy ham croquetas, then grab warm guava pastries from the walk-up window.

The Salty Donut

Indulge in artisanal craft donuts like the traditional Guava and Cheese or Maple Bacon, which are sweet hits for both kids and parents.

Joe's Stone Crab

Feast on world-famous sweet stone crab claws served with mustard sauce, and grab some crispy fried chicken for the kids in a casual, highly accommodating setting.

Glass & Vine

Enjoy fresh Florida snapper and hand-cut fries in a lush outdoor garden setting right next to a shaded playground in Coconut Grove.

El Palacio de los Jugos

Try the fresh-squeezed mango or passionfruit juice alongside a plate of tender, family-friendly roast pork and sweet plantains.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of high-SPF sunscreen, light linen layers, and a sturdy stroller cover to handle Miami's sudden, brief tropical rain showers. Many top family attractions offer shaded areas, but visiting early in the morning helps beat the midday heat and humidity.
