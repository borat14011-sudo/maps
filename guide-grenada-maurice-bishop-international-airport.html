Grenada (Maurice Bishop International Airport)

← back to travel board
Grenada welcomes families with the warm scent of nutmeg and some of the Caribbean’s calmest, most pristine beaches. From exploring lush rainforests with friendly mona monkeys to building sandcastles on Grand Anse, this island is a playground of natural wonder. It is a safe, laid-back paradise where kids can discover the joy of island life at their own pace.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 to 15 minutes by pre-arranged taxi to the main family resort areas along Grand Anse Beach and Lance aux Épines

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Grand Anse Beach

Two miles of powdery white sand and exceptionally calm, shallow waters perfect for little swimmers. Toddlers will love splashing in the gentle shoreline protected by shady sea grape trees.

Afternoon / 1h

House of Chocolate

A mini-museum and sweet cafe in the heart of St. George's showcasing Grenada's cocoa history. Toddlers can enjoy local dairy-free chocolate ice cream while parents learn about the chocolate-making process.

Morning / 2h

Grand Etang National Park

A lush rainforest sanctuary where you can spot exotic flora and the island's famous mona monkeys. Keep toddlers in a carrier to watch the friendly monkeys descend from the trees near the visitor center.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

St. George's Market Square

A vibrant, sensory-rich market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local spices, and friendly vendors. It is best navigated with a baby carrier so your toddler can safely marvel at the bright sights and sweet aromas.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Fort George

An iconic historical fort offering breathtaking panoramic views of the harbor and colorful capital. Toddlers can watch the ships crawl across the bay, but stick to a carrier as the ramparts have steep steps.

Morning / 1-2h

Concord Falls

A beautiful waterfall that requires absolutely no hiking to see, making it incredibly accessible for families. Toddlers can safely dipping their toes in the cool, shallow edge of the stream right by the viewing platform.

Afternoon / 2-3h

La Sagesse Nature Center

A quiet, secluded beach and nature area with a highly protected bay that creates wave-free, pool-like conditions. It is an ideal, uncrowded spot for toddlers to safely wade and play in the sand.

Morning / 3h

Belmont Estate

A historic 17th-century working plantation featuring lush gardens, chocolate production, and an organic farm. Toddlers will adore meeting the goats, sheep, and tortoises at the onsite petting farm.

Midday / 1-2h

Spice Land Mall Play Area

An easy-going indoor shopping center with a designated air-conditioned play area and toy store. It is the perfect spot to beat the midday heat, let your toddler burn off energy, and stock up on familiar snacks.

🍽️ Where to eat

Esther's Bar

Grab fresh fruit smoothies and local fish tacos while sitting at casual picnic tables right on the sand of Grand Anse.

The Beach Club at Calabash

Order the delicious, kid-friendly pasta or fresh catch of the day in a relaxed, upscale beachside setting with plenty of space for toddlers to play nearby.

Container Park

Choose from multiple open-air food stalls serving up everything from quick burgers to local rotis in a fun, casual courtyard.

Sails Restaurant & Bar

Enjoy the savory coconut shrimp and watch the colorful wooden boats glide across the calm waters of the Carenage.

Coconut Beach Restaurant

Dine on French-Creole chicken and local rice dishes with your toes tucked into the soft sand of Grand Anse Beach.
