Spice island luxury met with calm Caribbean shores

Grenada (Maurice Bishop International Airport)

← back to travel board
Grenada offers a sophisticated yet incredibly laid-back escape for families looking to bypass the hectic mega-resort crowds of busier Caribbean islands. Its exceptionally calm, warm waters at Grand Anse are ideal for an infant's first ocean dip, while the lush, accessible spice gardens provide sensory-rich environments without strenuous trekking. With the international airport located just minutes from the premier luxury resorts, you can transition from your flight to a beachside lounger with zero transit friction.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 minute private taxi from Maurice Bishop International Airport (GND) directly to the premier family resorts along Grand Anse Beach.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Grand Anse Beach

The quintessential Caribbean stretch with ultra-calm, shallow waters perfect for holding a wading toddler. Seek shade under the mature sea grape trees to keep baby out of the midday sun.

Afternoon / 1h

House of Chocolate

An air-conditioned museum and cafe sanctuary in the capital, ideal for cooling off while sampling local organic cocoa treats. The flat interior layout is completely stroller-friendly for an easy pitstop.

Morning / 1-2h

Annandale Waterfall & Forest Oasis

Unlike other rugged Grenadian falls, this features a paved, flat 2-minute pathway from the entrance directly to the viewing pool. It is perfect for carrying an infant to see the rushing water without a strenuous hike.

Morning / 3-4h

Belmont Estate

An active 300-year-old plantation where toddlers can see farm animals like goats and tortoises at the petting farm. The estate's restaurant offers high chairs and a spacious, open-air layout.

Morning / 1-2h

Laura Herb & Spice Garden

A gentle, paved walking path lined with nutmeg, cocoa, and cinnamon trees that stimulates baby's senses. The paths are wide enough for a sturdy travel stroller and shaded by a dense, cool canopy.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Fort Frederick

Offers dramatic 360-degree views of the harbor with much easier stroller access and fewer steep steps than neighboring Fort George. The wide, paved ramparts make it safe to stretch your legs under close supervision.

Morning / 2-3h

La Sagesse Beach

A highly secluded, crescent-shaped cove where the water is exceptionally shallow and wave-free, making it the safest natural wading pool on the island for babies. The nearby nature center provides handy shade and changing facilities.

Morning / 1-2h

St. George's Market Square

A vibrant sensory experience of colors and scents where you can pick up fresh tropical fruits for baby's snack time. Go early on Saturday to beat the crowds and heat, carrying your child in a front pack.

Morning / 1-2h

Grand Etang National Park Visitor Centre

A high-altitude rainforest stop where baby can spot the friendly Mona monkeys right from the paved viewing area. The visitor centre is flat and easily accessible for a quick, cool-breeze photo op.

🍽️ Where to eat

Umbrellas Beach Bar

Order the local catch of the day or a classic burger at this vibrant beachside joint. Its open-air, high-energy vibe easily drowns out toddler babble, and you can step right onto Grand Anse beach if baby gets restless.

The Beach Club at Calabash

Savor the West Indian Crabback in a luxurious, shaded beachfront setting. The gentle grassy lawn leading to the sand allows one parent to stroll with the baby while waiting for the upscale mains to arrive.

Savvy's at Mount Cinnamon

Try the fresh, farm-to-table Mahi Mahi served in a relaxed, open-air pavilion. They open early for dinner at 6:00 PM, aligning perfectly with early toddler bedtimes, and offer plenty of space between tables.

Dodgy Dock

Enjoy the popular street food nights and order fresh fish tacos right on the water. The spacious wooden deck is highly casual, extremely welcoming to families, and features live music that keeps kids entertained.

BB's Crabback

Feast on the iconic 'BB's Crabback' while sitting on the open-air deck overlooking the yachts in the harbor. The staff is famously warm to young children, and the constant harbor breeze keeps the dining area cool.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Spice Island Beach Resort

An luxury all-inclusive directly on Grand Anse Beach offering pristine, spacious suites and complimentary top-tier wooden cribs. The calm beachfront location means you can easily baby-monitor the room from your beach lounger.

Mount Cinnamon Resort

Features multi-bedroom villas with full kitchens, critical for prepping formula and baby meals, alongside in-villa washer/dryers. The resort offers a gentle beach club, dedicated kids' activities, and highly responsive babysitting services.

Radisson Grenada Beach Resort

Boasts a massive, 300-foot freeform oasis pool with entry-level shallows perfect for water-testing a toddler. The property is completely flat, making stroller navigation to your beachfront room incredibly smooth.
