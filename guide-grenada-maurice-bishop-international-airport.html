Grenada greets you with cinnamon-scented breezes and the calm turquoise waters of Grand Anse Beach, a two-mile stretch of powdery sand perfect for stroller walks. The island's lush interior offers shaded trails and waterfalls where baby Mary Claire can splash in shallow pools. Your private villa at Spice Island Beach Resort includes a crib, blackout curtains, and direct beach access for early morning sand play. From the airport, it's a 15-minute drive through fragrant nutmeg groves to your resort.
🚕 Getting there: 15-minute private transfer from Maurice Bishop International Airport to the Grand Anse hotel strip; use Spice Island Tours for a luxury SUV with infant car seat and stroller storage.

What to do

Morning 8-10am

Grand Anse Beach

Calm, shallow waters and soft sand ideal for baby's first beach day; shaded spots under palm trees, stroller-accessible boardwalk nearby.

2 hours

Belmont Estate

Working cocoa farm with stroller-friendly paths, shaded verandas, and baby-friendly cocoa tea tasting; gentle tour avoids loud machinery.

1-1.5 hours

Fort George

Historic fort with paved walkways and panoramic views of St. George's harbor; stroller accessible, cool stone walls provide shade.

45 minutes

Annandale Falls

Easy 5-minute walk from parking to a 40-foot waterfall; shallow pool at base for light splashing, stroller can get close.

1 hour

Grenada National Museum

Air-conditioned galleries with maritime artifacts and sugar plantation history; baby-friendly climate, stroller space, and quiet corners.

2 hours

Molinere Underwater Sculpture Park

Boat tour to see submerged sculptures; clear water and calm seas for baby-friendly glass-bottom boat option (no diving needed).

1.5 hours

Grand Etang National Park

Crater lake with paved viewing platform stroller-accessible; cool mountain air, monkeys, and easy loop trail with shade.

Half-day

Levera Beach

Secluded, calm lagoon with gentle waves and turtle sanctuary; bring a beach tent for shade, soft sand suitable for baby.

Morning 8-10am

St. George's Market

Covered market with fresh fruit vendors, spices, and local crafts; stroller-friendly aisles, avoid peak noon heat, sample nutmeg ice cream.

🍽️ Where to eat

Dodgy Dock Restaurant

Seafood platter and coconut shrimp on a waterfront deck; highchairs available, early bird seating at 5:30pm, family-friendly vibe.

The Beach House Restaurant

Fresh lobster and tropical cocktails with toes in the sand; shaded outdoor terrace, highchair, and quiet evenings after sunset.

Rhodes Restaurant

Fine dining with a tasting menu featuring local spices; dim lighting and plush seating, but no infants policy — call ahead for private room.

Aquarium Restaurant

Casual beachfront spot serving grilled fish and rum punch; early dinner 5pm, stroller parking, and live steel drum music (not too loud).

BB's Crabback

Family-run shack specializing in crab backs and garlic shrimp; outdoor picnic tables, highchair by request, best for lunch.
