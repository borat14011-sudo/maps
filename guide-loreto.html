Loreto

← back to travel board
Loreto offers a dramatic contrast of Sierra de la Giganta mountains and the calm, crystal-clear waters of the Bahia de Loreto National Park, far removed from the mega-resort crowds of Cabo. It is exceptionally safe, highly walkable, and the gentle, wave-free waters of the Gulf of California act like a giant, warm wading pool for a toddler. For a busy finance dad, the direct flights and quick airport transfers make for a low-friction, high-value getaway where you can easily push a stroller down the peaceful historic malecon.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 minute private shuttle or airport taxi to downtown Loreto hotels, or ~35 minutes south to the resorts at Ensenada Blanca.

What to do

Morning / 1h

Misión de Nuestra Señora de Loreto Conchó

The oldest mission in the Californias, located in a quiet pedestrian-only plaza perfect for pushing a stroller. Visit in the early morning for cool temperatures and beautiful morning light reflecting off the stone facade.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Malecon de Loreto

A wide, paved, and completely flat boardwalk running along the waterfront with stunning views of the islands. It features a great kid-friendly playground on the north end and is perfect for an easy stroller walk at sunset.

Morning / 2h

Playa La Darsena

The main town beach located right next to the marina, featuring exceptionally calm, shallow water with almost no waves. It is perfect for a toddler to safely splash and play in the wet sand while parents relax under a beach umbrella.

Morning / 3-4h

Isla Coronado Boat Tour

Hire a private panga boat from the local cooperative to visit this pristine volcanic island with a white-sand crescent beach. The water is as calm and clear as a swimming pool, making it ideal for a toddler to wade in while you spot wild sea lions and dolphins on the way.

Morning / 3-4h

Misión de San Francisco Javier de Viggé-Biaundó

An incredible drive through the dramatic Sierra de la Giganta mountains leads to a fully restored 18th-century stone mission. The surrounding oasis features ancient olive orchards with flat pathways that are easy to navigate with a baby carrier.

Afternoon / 1h

Plaza Principal

The shaded central town square filled with massive laurel trees, benches, and a peaceful fountain. It is entirely vehicle-free, allowing your toddler to safely stretch her legs and crawl or walk around in the shade.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Ensenada Blanca

A stunning, quiet bay south of town framed by dramatic headlands with incredibly shallow tide pools at low tide. Your toddler can inspect tiny hermit crabs and small fish in ankle-deep, warm water.

Afternoon / 30m

Paleteria La Michoacana

A clean, bright local shop serving handmade Mexican ice cream and fresh fruit paletas (popsicles). It is a perfect, air-conditioned pitstop to cool down, and the coconut or mango paletas are a messy but delicious hit for toddlers.

Early Morning / 1h

Darsena Marina Loreto

Take a flat, easy morning stroll along the harbor docks to watch the local fishermen bring in their daily catch. Your toddler will love watching the brown pelicans and sea lions that linger around the boats waiting for scraps.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mi Loreto

Order the cochinita pibil or the arrachera steak served in a beautiful, colorful courtyard under a thatched palapa canopy. The open-air setting easily absorbs toddler noise, and the staff is incredibly warm and accommodating to young children.

Orlando's Cocina

The ultimate breakfast spot to order classic chilaquiles or stuffed poblano peppers in a highly casual, open-air street-side setting. They open early, feature fast service, and have high chairs readily available for breakfast with a wakeful toddler.

Domingo's Place

Famous for its steak and traditional Loreto-style clams (almejas tatemadas) served in a lush, walled garden. The spacious tables provide plenty of room for a high chair, and the early dinner hours fit a toddler's sleep schedule perfectly.

Panaderia El Trigo

A beloved local bakery perfect for grabbing fresh, warm pan dulce (Mexican sweet bread) and empanadas at dawn. It is an ideal stop for early-rising toddlers who need a quick, delicious bite before sit-down restaurants open.

La Picazón

Located right on the beach north of town, this casual open-air restaurant offers world-class fresh fish tacos and chocolate clams. Your toddler can play directly in the clean sand right at the edge of your table while you enjoy ocean views.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Villa del Palmar Beach Resort & Spa at Islands of Loreto

A luxury resort featuring massive, shallow, multi-tiered pools shaped like a turtle, ideal for toddlers to splash safely. The spacious multi-bedroom suites come with fully equipped kitchens, perfect for preparing baby formula and warming milk.

Hotel Oasis

Located directly on the quiet, sandy beach at the southern end of the malecon, this historic hotel offers a peaceful palm-shaded pool area. The beachfront ground-floor rooms make it incredibly easy to step out onto the sand, and the town center is a short, stroller-friendly walk away.

La Misión Loreto

Positioned right on the historic malecon, this hotel combines classic hacienda style with modern luxury, featuring a heated outdoor pool and spacious rooms with cribs available upon request. It offers the ultimate convenience of being steps away from both the beach and downtown restaurants.

👨‍👩‍👧 Book a private panga boat for your island excursions rather than a group tour so you can set your own schedule and return to the mainland immediately if your toddler gets fussy or needs a nap. While the historical center of Loreto is highly pedestrianized and paved with stroller-friendly flat concrete, a lightweight travel stroller works best, but keep a baby carrier handy for the cobblestones near San Javier. Standard baby supplies like diapers and wipes are easily purchased at the local El Pescador supermarket, but plan to buy large jugs of bottled water (garrafones) for mixing formula as tap water is not drinkable.
