Loreto captures the pristine, slow-paced essence of old Baja, where the majestic Sierra de la Giganta mountains meet the calm, indigo waters of the Sea of Cortez. This tranquil escape offers a sophisticated alternative to Cabo, ideal for Houston finance couple Will and Kathryn to unwind without the crowds. For baby Mary Claire, the flat, stroller-friendly Malecón de Loreto provides the perfect scenic route for morning walks beside gentle, wave-free shores. From shaded private boat charters to quiet colonial courtyards, the destination balances untouched natural beauty with seamless luxury comforts.
🚕 Getting there: Loreto International Airport (LTO) to the downtown luxury strip or Nopoló is a seamless 10 to 15-minute drive, while Villa del Palmar in Danzante Bay is 40 minutes. We recommend arranging a private suburban transfer through your resort, equipped with a pre-installed, rear-facing Clek infant car seat to ensure Mary Claire's safety and comfort.

What to do

Early morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM

Malecón de Loreto

A morning stroll on a wide, completely flat, paved boardwalk with stunning Sea of Cortez views and refreshing ocean breezes. Ideal for the stroller during Mary Claire’s early morning awake window.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Misión de Nuestra Señora de Loreto Concho

Baja's oldest mission features a beautifully preserved, quiet stone courtyard. The surrounding pedestrian-only plaza is stroller-friendly, shaded by historic arches, and perfect for a peaceful cultural morning.

Morning to Afternoon 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Isla Coronado Private Charter

A private, shaded panga boat charter to a volcanic island with a shallow, wave-free turquoise lagoon. Mary Claire can safely splash in the warm tide pools while Will and Kathryn enjoy artisanal beach picnics.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM

Playa La Dársena

A quiet, protected beach right next to the town harbor, completely free of strong surf. The ultra-calm, shallow water is ideal for dipping Mary Claire’s feet, with easy access to nearby cafes for Kathryn and Will.

Mid-day 12:00 PM - 1:30 PM

Plaza Cívica de Loreto

The historic heart of Loreto, shaded by massive laurel trees. It is peaceful, car-free, and ideal for letting Mary Claire practice crawling on the clean, flat stone plazas during the quiet mid-day hours.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Marina Puerto Escondido

A modern, highly secure, and paved marina south of town. Take a leisurely afternoon stroll past luxury yachts, featuring wide docks and a delightful harbor-front cafe with baby-friendly outdoor seating.

Morning 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Misión San Francisco Javier de Viggé-Biaundó

A scenic, paved mountain drive leads to this stunning stone mission. The grounds are serene and spacious, though a carrier is recommended over a stroller for the cobblestone paths surrounding the olive orchards.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Loreto Bay National Marine Park Glass-Bottom Boat Tour

Observe colorful tropical fish and docile marine life through a glass-bottom viewing panel. This keeps Mary Claire completely dry, shaded, and safe from the elements while exploring the Aquarium of the World.

Late Afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:30 PM

Playa Nopoló

A remarkably tranquil beach set within the upscale Loreto Bay residential area. It offers soft sand, virtually no waves, and beautiful views of Isla Carmen, providing a serene, uncrowded setting for family relaxation.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mi Loreto

High-end traditional Mexican dining in a lush, romantic garden courtyard. Their signature cochinita pibil is outstanding, and the early 5:00 PM opening slot is perfect for avoiding Mary Claire’s bedtime fussiness.

Orlando's Restaurante

Famous for vibrant colors and exceptional seafood molcajetes. The spacious, fully shaded outdoor terrace easily accommodates large strollers, and the staff is legendary for doting on babies.

Danzante Restaurant

The premier fine-dining steakhouse at Villa del Palmar. It features an elegant outdoor terrace overlooking the sea, high-end highchairs, and an upscale, quiet ambiance that accepts early family seatings.

Domingo's Place

A beloved local institution serving exquisite lobster and steaks in a leafy, palapa-covered garden. The soft ambient noise level naturally masks infant babble, and highchairs are readily available.

Pepegina's Loreto

Upscale wood-fired artisanal pizzas served on a relaxed, ocean-breeze terrace near the marina. Its casual yet sophisticated vibe is perfect for a stress-free family dinner with speedy service.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

$350-400/night

The pinnacle of local luxury, set on a secluded, wave-free bay. Offers spacious multi-bedroom suites, complimentary wooden cribs, a zero-entry family pool, and reliable private babysitting services.

Hotel Posada de las Flores Loreto

$200-280/night

A stunning colonial boutique hotel on the historic plaza. Its central location allows easy stroller access to town, and the courtyard suites offer absolute privacy, high-quality wooden cribs, and a unique glass-bottom rooftop pool.

Loreto Bay Golf Resort & Spa at Baja Cove

$220-300/night

Nestled along the calm, shallow waters of Nopoló Bay. Features large, residential-style family suites with excellent blackout curtains, a beautiful beachfront pool, and gentle wave-free beach access ideal for infants.
