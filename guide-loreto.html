Loreto

← back to travel board
Tucked between the dramatic Sierra de la Giganta mountains and the crystal-clear Sea of Cortez, Loreto is a peaceful, historic oasis perfect for families. This sleepy pueblo mágico offers calm, shallow waters teeming with marine life and a slow pace of life that feels worlds away from crowded resort towns. It is an incredibly safe, walkable, and welcoming destination where toddlers can easily explore historic plazas and splash in gentle tides.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 to 15 minutes by taxi or rental car from Loreto International Airport (LTO) to the historic downtown and beachside hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Misión de Nuestra Señora de Loreto

Explore the oldest mission in the Californias, featuring beautiful stone architecture and a spacious, car-free pedestrian plaza. It is a peaceful spot where toddlers can safely toddle around the sunny square while parents admire the history.

Morning / 4-5h

Coronado Island (Isla Coronado) Boat Tour

Take a short, scenic boat ride to a volcanic island featuring sugar-white sand beaches and crystal-clear turquoise waters. The wave-free, shallow shoreline is essentially a giant natural wading pool, making it paradise for toddlers to splash safely.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Malecon de Loreto

Stroll along this scenic, paved beachfront boardwalk that offers sweeping views of the islands and passing boats. The wide, flat pathway is perfect for strollers, and there is a great beachside playground where little ones can burn off energy.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Nopoló

Visit this serene, quiet bay just south of town which offers incredibly calm, gentle waters and a stony shoreline perfect for beachcombing. Toddlers will love collecting smooth pebbles and watching small fish dart around the shallow water.

Morning / 1h

Loreto Mercado Municipal

Immerse yourself in local culture at this small, indoor municipal market packed with colorful fruits and daily staples. Toddlers will love tasting sweet, fresh-cut local mangoes or papayas, which make for an easy and healthy snack.

Morning / 3-4h

Misión San Francisco Javier de Viggé-Biaundó

Take a scenic drive into the mountains to visit this stunning, well-preserved stone mission surrounded by ancient olive orchards. Toddlers will enjoy wandering the shady gardens and meeting the friendly farm animals kept by nearby villagers.

Morning / 2h

Glass Bottom Boat Tour

Float over the protected waters of the Loreto National Marine Park to view colorful fish and sea stars without getting wet. It is the perfect snorkeling alternative for toddlers who are too young for gear but love marine life.

Morning / 5-6h

Magdalena Bay Whale Watching

Embark on a seasonal day trip to pet friendly gray mother whales and their calves in these protected, shallow calving lagoons. The large, stable panga boats and the inquisitive nature of the whales make this a magical, safe experience for older toddlers.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza Principal (Main Square)

Relax in the shaded central plaza surrounded by historic buildings, massive fig trees, and local families. Toddlers can safely chase bubbles and run on the wide pedestrian pathways while parents enjoy a quiet moment on a bench.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mi Loreto

Sit in the colorful, open-air courtyard and order the tender pork cochinita pibil served with fresh, warm handmade flour tortillas.

Orlando's Restaurante

Get the fluffy banana pancakes and fresh-squeezed orange juice at this vibrant, ultra-welcoming family spot.

Super Tacos de Lalo

Grab a casual plastic table and feast on their famous, crispy beer-battered fish tacos topped with mild crema.

Domingo's Place

Enjoy succulent coconut shrimp in a lush, jungle-like garden patio that has plenty of space and distractions for restless toddlers.

La Palapa

Dine right on the sand and order the fresh chocolate clams for the adults and simple, melty cheese quesadillas for the kids.
