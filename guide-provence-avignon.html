What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Palais des Papes

Explore Europe's largest Gothic palace. While stairs require a baby carrier inside, the surrounding gardens at Rocher des Doms offer smooth stroller paths, panoramic river views, and quiet shady spots for a bottle feeding.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Cours Mirabeau

Stroll along this iconic, tree-canopied boulevard in Aix-en-Provence. It is entirely flat, stroller-friendly, and lined with gentle mossy fountains that provide calming ambient white noise for Mary Claire's morning nap.

Afternoon 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM / 1.5h

Carrières des Lumières

An immersive, multi-sensory digital art show projected inside a massive limestone quarry. The cave stays cool and refreshing even in summer, is entirely stroller-accessible, and the soothing music and colorful lights are highly engaging for infants.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Pont du Gard

Marvel at the ancient Roman aqueduct. The site features wide, flat paved paths leading to the riverbank, making it easy to roll a stroller right up to shady grassy spots perfect for spreading out a picnic blanket.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM / 2h

Jardin du Pharo

Overlooking the Old Port of Marseille, these sweeping palace gardens offer cool sea breezes, paved coastal walking paths, and expansive lawns for crawl and tummy time away from the city traffic.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Abbey of Sénanque

Admire the striking contrast of lavender fields against a 12th-century abbey. The outdoor viewing paths are flat and gravel-paved, allowing for a smooth stroller ride while capturing classic family portraits.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM / 1.5h

Le Sentier des Ocres

Walk among the stunning rust-colored clay cliffs of Roussillon. The shorter loop takes only 30 minutes; swap the stroller for a baby carrier here due to a few natural sand steps, allowing Mary Claire to marvel at the vibrant colors.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM / 2h

Musée Granet

An air-conditioned sanctuary of fine art in Aix-en-Provence. Fully accessible with ramps and elevators, this quiet museum is perfect for a calm sensory escape during the warmest part of the day.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM / 2h

Parc Borély

A vast, classical French park in Marseille featuring a large lake with ducks, rose gardens, and wide stroller-friendly paths. Rent a canopy-shaded pedal car or enjoy a relaxed walk under the mature plane trees.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cour d'Honneur

Exceptional French cuisine served in a quiet, spacious outdoor courtyard garden. The ample space between tables easily accommodates a luxury stroller, and they offer early dinner seatings at 7:00 PM.

Le Grand Café de France

Savor a classic Niçoise salad on the wide, bustling outdoor terrace of Cours Mirabeau. The lively open-air environment means baby sounds blend right in, and highchairs are readily available.

Chez Fonfon

Enjoy world-class, authentic bouillabaisse in the picturesque cove of Vallon des Auffes. Request a harbor-view window table for lunch when the restaurant is bright, welcoming to families, and filled with soothing maritime views.

L'Agape

Modern, Bib Gourmand-rated French dishes in a warm, welcoming bistro setting. The staff is famously accommodating to families, providing highchairs and early evening service in their lovely, quiet indoor dining room.

Côté Cour

High-end dining by chef Ronan Kernen in a gorgeous, glass-roofed garden patio. The spacious layout fits strollers easily, and the soft, natural acoustics keep the atmosphere calm and baby-friendly.
