Provence (Avignon / Marseille area)

← back to travel board
Provence wraps families in a warm embrace of cicada songs, golden stone villages, and sparkling azure waters. From wandering the historic streets of Avignon to splashing in the calm coves near Marseille, this region offers a gentle, sensory-rich playground. It is a place where slow-paced European charm meets endless outdoor discoveries for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or train from Marseille Provence Airport (MRS) to Marseille center, or ~50 min rental car drive to Avignon

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Palais des Papes & Pont d'Avignon

Explore the massive medieval fortress with interactive Histopad tablets that older kids love, while toddlers can safely crawl and play in the stroller-friendly hilltop gardens of Rocher des Doms nearby.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Carrières des Lumières

An immersive, walk-through art and music projection show inside a cool, dark limestone quarry that mesmerizes toddlers with moving lights and offers a welcome escape from the summer heat.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Le Bois des Lutins

A spectacular eco-active woodland park featuring treehouses, giant slides, and net courses suspended in the trees, with a dedicated safe discovery area designed specifically for toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

Marseille Vieux-Port & Carousel

Watch the yachts, buy fresh fish at the lively morning market docks, and let toddlers ride the beautiful, classic double-decker vintage carousel right by the water's edge.

Morning / 3-4h

Pont du Gard

Marvel at the giant ancient Roman aqueduct and let toddlers splash safely in the shallow, calm pebble beaches of the Gardon River directly beneath the historic arches.

Morning / 1-2h

Les Halles d'Avignon

A vibrant indoor food market covered in a massive vertical green wall, perfect for picking up fresh local berries, cheese, and pastries for an easy toddler-friendly picnic.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parc National des Calanques Boat Tour

Board a family-friendly catamaran cruise from Marseille to see dramatic limestone cliffs and turquoise water, providing a breezy, relaxing ride perfect for stroller-bound nap times.

Morning / 1-2h

Sénanque Abbey Lavender Fields

See the iconic purple fields surrounding a 12th-century abbey, offering a beautiful, sensory, and highly fragrant outdoor walk that toddlers will find magical.

Morning / 2-3h

Château d'If Ferry & Fortress

Take an exciting, short boat ride from Marseille's harbor to this historic island fortress, where toddlers can enjoy the sea breeze and walk along the secure, walled pathways.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cour d'Honneur (Avignon)

Dine on classic, seasonal French bistro fare in a beautiful, spacious enclosed courtyard where toddlers can safely stretch their legs away from traffic.

Le Glacier du Roi (Marseille)

Treat the family to artisanal ice cream featuring local flavors like navette (orange blossom cookie) and lavender, located in the stroller-friendly Le Panier district.

Chez Fonfon (Marseille)

Indulge in authentic, world-famous Bouillabaisse in the picturesque harbor of Vallon des Auffes, with a highly welcoming staff and simple fresh fish options for kids.

Chez Serge (Carpentras)

A legendary family-friendly bistro famous for its truffle dishes, offering high chairs, a kid-friendly attitude, and a lovely outdoor terrace perfect for messy eaters.

La Table de Ventabren (Ventabren)

Enjoy Michelin-starred dining on a relaxed, panoramic hilltop terrace where the patient, warm staff goes out of their way to accommodate families with young kids.

👨‍👩‍👧 Paved cobblestone streets in hilltop villages like Gordes are highly unfriendly to stroller wheels, so pack a sturdy baby carrier for exploring. Always carry a light blanket and reusable containers to assemble impromptu picnics from local markets, which are often much easier and more relaxed than sit-down dining with restless toddlers.
