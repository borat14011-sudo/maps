Querétaro

← back to travel board
Santiago de Querétaro offers the sophisticated history of San Miguel de Allende but with flatter, exceptionally clean pedestrian streets and a safer, more relaxed atmosphere. It is a wealthy, modernizing city where historic haciendas double as spacious retreats perfect for families managing nap schedules. The vibrant, tree-shaded plazas and high-end dining enclaves provide a seamless, upscale international getaway with a toddler.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-minute authorized airport taxi (purchased inside the terminal) from Querétaro Intercontinental Airport (QRO) directly to the Centro Histórico.

What to do

Morning / 1h

Mirador de los Arcos

Take in the panoramic view of the iconic 18th-century pink stone aqueduct from this clean, paved plaza. The viewing area is entirely flat and stroller-accessible, making it perfect for a breezy morning photo opportunity.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Alameda Hidalgo

A massive, walled green sanctuary in the city center featuring paved, shaded walking paths and a classic carousel. It is ideal for an early morning stroller walk beneath giant ash trees to let the baby nap.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de la Cruz

An authentic, bustling public market filled with colorful fruit stalls, local cheeses, and sensory experiences. Keep your toddler in a baby carrier to easily navigate the vibrant, narrow aisles and grab fresh bananas or papayas.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas

The pedestrian-only heart of the historic center, shaded by manicured Indian laurel trees and centered around a stone fountain. The completely flat, car-free plaza allows your toddler to crawl or toddle safely while you enjoy an outdoor coffee.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Nacional El Cimatario

A protected volcanic national park just south of the city featuring easy, flat dirt trails. Strap on a hiking baby carrier to explore the semi-desert flora and spot white-tailed deer in the animal rescue area.

Midday / 1-2h

Museo de Arte de Querétaro

Housed in a magnificent 18th-century baroque monastery with one of the most beautiful courtyards in Latin America. The wide, flat stone corridors are highly stroller-friendly, quiet, and offer a cool, shaded escape from the midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Plazuela Mariano de las Casas

A wide-open stone plaza fronting the spectacular Templo de Santa Rosa de Viterbo, featuring dancing water fountains. It is a fantastic spot for toddlers to watch the water play while parents admire the church's dramatic flying buttresses.

Evening / 1h

Jardín Zenea

The city's historic main square, featuring an ornate 19th-century metal bandstand where local musicians often play. It is a lively, sensory-rich spot where your baby can listen to music while you relax on a shaded bench.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Viñedos Azteca

A beautiful, family-friendly ranch-style vineyard located about 45 minutes outside the city near Bernal. It features sprawling lawns for crawling, real horses to look at, and private lakeside spots for a relaxed family afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Hacienda Los Laureles

Order the traditional enchiladas queretanas or the premium arrachera steak. This upscale restaurant is set in a sprawling historic estate with massive, manicured lawns where your toddler can run or crawl safely while you dine.

La Mariposa

Order the signature 'Nieve de Mantecado' (butter-vanilla ice cream) and classic club sandwiches. This legendary, bustling diner is casual, exceptionally fast, and loud enough to easily drown out any baby fussiness.

Chucho El Roto

Order the classic chilaquiles or the savory chicken mole. Located right on the car-free Plaza de Armas, its spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates strollers and lets you step out instantly if the baby needs a walk.

Nicos Querétaro

Order the slow-cooked lechón (suckling pig) or artisanal breakfast tamales. This refined yet welcoming venue offers spacious seating, clean high chairs, and attentive staff who cater perfectly to families.

Restaurante 1810

Order the ribeye steak or the traditional sopa azteca. Situated on the main historic plaza, this classic establishment has a sheltered courtyard that is very stroller-accessible and offers early dining hours.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hacienda Jurica by Brisas

A beautifully restored 16th-century estate featuring expansive lawns, a large outdoor heated pool, and quiet, spacious rooms. It offers a peaceful oasis outside the busy center, complete with cribs and kid-friendly grounds.

Casa de la Marquesa

A stunning baroque palace in the pedestrian zone of the Centro Histórico. The soaring ceilings and large suites provide plenty of room for a travel crib, and the central location makes popping back for afternoon naps effortless.

DoubleTree by Hilton Queretaro

Offers reliable, premium comforts including an indoor heated pool, spacious double rooms, and excellent crib options. Its modern layout is highly elevator-accessible and provides quick highway access for regional day trips.

👨‍👩‍👧 Querétaro’s Centro Histórico is incredibly stroller-friendly compared to other Mexican colonial cities, but stick to a sturdy stroller with large wheels to navigate the occasional cobblestone. Uber is highly reliable, safe, and inexpensive here, though you should bring your own lightweight car seat for rides. To align with local schedules and avoid crowds, plan your restaurant meals for 1:30 PM for lunch or 7:00 PM for dinner when establishments are quieter and very accommodating to families.
