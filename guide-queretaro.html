Querétaro

← back to travel board
Nestled in central Mexico, Querétaro charms families with its exceptionally clean pedestrian streets, pastel-hued Spanish colonial architecture, and lush plazas. Kids will love chasing bubbles in the car-free squares while parents admire the soaring pink sandstone aqueduct. It is one of Mexico's safest, most walkable, and most vibrant cultural treasures for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min taxi or private shuttle from Querétaro International Airport (QRO) to the historic downtown hotel district.

What to do

Morning / 1h

Aqueduct of Querétaro (Los Arcos)

Marvel at the 74 towering pink stone arches that dominate the city's eastern skyline. Toddlers will love pointing at the massive structures from the safe, wide pedestrian sidewalk below.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque Alameda Hidalgo

A giant, lush green park right in the city center with paved paths, fountains, and towering shade trees. There is a great playground area, and it is perfect for toddlers to run around safely away from traffic.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de la Cruz

Dive into local life at this bustling indoor market filled with colorful piñatas, fresh fruit, and delicious street food. Grab some fresh bananas or sweet bread, which make perfect mess-free snacks for toddlers.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas

The beating heart of the historic center, surrounded by beautiful colonial buildings, outdoor cafes, and a central fountain. Toddlers will love chasing pigeons and watching local street performers in this completely pedestrianized zone.

Morning / 2h

El Cerrito Archaeological Zone

Explore an ancient Toltec pyramid located just 15 minutes outside the city center. The flat, stroller-friendly paths surrounding the ruin make it easy to navigate with a toddler.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Querétaro Regional Museum

Housed in a beautiful former convent, this museum features quiet courtyards and fascinating historical exhibits. The spacious, open-air cloisters are great for keeping toddlers calm and engaged in a peaceful setting.

Morning / 1h

Trolebús Tourist Bus

Take a classic open-air trolley ride around the historic center to see all the major sights without tiring out little legs. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the breezy, rumbling ride through the colorful streets.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Parque Bicentenario

A massive family park on the outskirts of town featuring a lake, small boat rides, water play areas, and a dedicated kids' playground. The gentle toddler splash zone is a lifesaver on hot sunny afternoons.

Morning / 1.5h

Cerro de las Campanas

A historic hilltop park with sprawling lawns, a giant statue of Benito Juárez, and a playground. It is incredibly peaceful and offers plenty of soft grass for toddlers to practice walking or crawling.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Mariposa

Order their legendary mantecado ice cream or a comforting plate of sweet enchiladas queretanas.

Chucho el Roto

Sit outside on the plaza and enjoy their famous chilaquiles or cecina while the kids watch the square's activities.

Nicos Querétaro

Try the slow-cooked barbacoa or the family-style sharing platters of carnitas in a welcoming, spacious dining room.

Tikua Sur Este

Try the cochinita pibil tacos or panuchos, which are flavorful but mild enough for young kids to enjoy.

Amadeus Restaurant Galería

Get their savory crepes or a slice of fresh apple strudel that toddlers will love sharing on the gorgeous patio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy baby carrier over a heavy stroller, as some historic side streets still feature bumpy, uneven cobblestones. Take advantage of the clean, pedestrian-only zones around Plaza de Armas for safe, stress-free evening walks with your toddler.
