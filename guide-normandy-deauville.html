Normandy (Deauville/Trouville & Bayeux)

← back to travel board
Normandy's Côte Fleurie blends aristocratic coastal prestige with the pastoral charm of apple orchards and historic half-timbered towns. In Deauville, the iconic Les Planches boardwalk offers a wide, stroller-friendly pathway running parallel to golden sands and colorful beach umbrellas. Bayeux provides a quiet, medieval contrast where cobblestone streets lead to soaring gothic spires and tranquil riverside walks. For families traveling with baby Mary Claire, private chauffeured Mercedes vans and top-tier infant amenities at legendary hotels ensure a seamless, ultra-refined French getaway.
🚕 Getting there: From Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) to Deauville, the drive is approximately 2 hours and 30 minutes. The ultimate luxury family transport is a pre-arranged private transfer in a Mercedes-Benz V-Class equipped with an approved rear-facing infant car seat, offering ample space for a stroller and multiple suitcases.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Les Planches de Deauville

Walk along the iconic 1923 wooden boardwalk, lined with glamorous art-deco beach cabins named after famous Hollywood stars. The wide, flat hardwood surface is exceptionally smooth, making it perfect for a seamless stroller ride with baby Mary Claire while enjoying the brisk, refreshing sea breeze.

Early afternoon 1:30 PM - 2:30 PM / 1h

Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Bayeux

Marvel at this soaring masterpiece of Norman-Gothic architecture located in the heart of medieval Bayeux. The wide, flat stone aisles allow for easy stroller navigation, and the dim, serene interior offers a wonderfully quiet environment that is perfect for an afternoon baby nap.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Musée de la Tapisserie de Bayeux

View the world-renowned 11th-century tapestry depicting the Norman Conquest of England. The climate-controlled exhibit hall is kept dimly lit and quiet, making it an ideal, soothing environment to carry Mary Claire in a wrap or carrier while listening to the engaging audio tour.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM / 2h

Plage de Trouville

Spend a relaxing morning on this broad, sandy beach known for its beautiful 19th-century villas. It is far less crowded than Deauville, offering clean, calm waters and plenty of space to set up a luxury baby-sized UV sun tent for Mary Claire to play safely.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Jardin Public de Bayeux

Stroll through this beautiful, landscaped 19th-century public park featuring a spectacular, monumental weeping beech tree. The wide gravel pathways are easy to navigate with a sturdy stroller, offering a peaceful, shaded oasis for a quiet family picnic under the trees.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Marché de Deauville

Explore the colorful open-air market set beneath gorgeous, traditional half-timbered market halls at the Place du Marché. The vibrant sights and sweet aromas of fresh apples and cheeses are highly engaging, though it is best explored using a baby carrier during busy peak hours.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM / 1.5h

Villa Strassburger

Admire the stunning Belle Époque architecture of this historical landmark built for Baron Henri de Rothschild. The beautifully manicured lawns and spacious wrap-around gravel pathways provide a highly scenic, tranquil setting where you can stroll with the baby away from the beach crowds.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Arromanches-les-Bains Promenade

Take in the historic views of the remaining World War II Mulberry Harbours from the flat, paved pedestrian seawall. This stroller-accessible coastal path allows Kathryn and Will to reflect on Normandy's history while enjoying a calm, scenic walk right beside the gently lapping waves.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM / 1.5h

Parc des Graves

Discover this tranquil, cliffside nature park located in nearby Villerville, offering spectacular panoramic views of the Seine Bay. The park features stroller-friendly paved loops and lush grassy clearings, making it a wonderful spot for Mary Claire to enjoy supervised crawling time on the grass.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Ciro's

Indulge in classic French seafood dishes like fresh Normandy lobster and oysters right on the Deauville boardwalk. The spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates luxury strollers, and the staff is highly accommodating with early dinner seatings and highchairs on request.

Les Vapeurs

Enjoy a bustling lunch of moules marinières at this legendary, neon-lit brasserie in Trouville-sur-Mer. The lively atmosphere provides excellent natural white noise that easily drowns out any baby fussiness, and the outdoor sidewalk terrace is exceptionally family-friendly.

La Rapière

Savor refined Norman cuisine, including tender roasted veal and apple tarts, tucked away in a charming 16th-century Bayeux stone alleyway. The staff is warm and accommodating to families, offering early lunch seatings and spacious corner tables that easily fit a highchair.

L'Angle Saint Laurent

Delight in high-end, creative local dishes at this upscale yet incredibly welcoming restaurant in Bayeux. The dining room features comfortable, spaced-out seating that provides privacy for nursing mothers and ample room for a sleeping baby in a stroller next to the table.

Le 1912

Experience Michelin-starred gastronomy featuring locally sourced coastal ingredients inside the elegant Hotel Royal. While highly sophisticated, booking the earliest evening seating ensures a quiet dining room where the staff gracefully welcomes well-behaved infants with impeccable service.
