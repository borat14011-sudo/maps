Tampa

← back to travel board
Tampa blends historic Cuban roots with vibrant waterfront energy, making it a dream for active families. From gentle Gulf breezes along the world's longest continuous sidewalk to encounters with wild manatees, this Gulf Coast gem keeps toddlers enchanted and parents relaxed. Sunny days here are packed with easy splash parks, interactive museums, and breezy tram rides.

What to do

Morning / 3h

The Florida Aquarium

Touch real stingrays and walk through a massive wetlands dome teeming with local birds. Toddlers will love the outdoor Splash Pad, so pack a swimsuit and towel.

Morning / 2-3h

Glazer Children's Museum

An absolute wonderland of hands-on exhibits where kids can pilot a cruise ship, shop in a mini grocery store, and splash in water tables. There is a dedicated soft-play zone specifically for crawlers and toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Tampa Riverwalk & Curtis Hixon Waterfront Park

Stroll along this scenic, traffic-free paved path directly on the water, featuring two great playgrounds and interactive fountains. It is perfect for stroller naps and letting toddlers run off energy by the river.

Morning / 3-4h

ZooTampa at Lowry Park

Get up close with Florida manatees and feed giant giraffes in a lush, shade-filled park. Toddlers adore the Wallaroo Station splash area and the gentle family coaster.

Midday / 1-2h

Tampa Electric's Manatee Viewing Center

Watch wild manatees gather in the warm water discharges of a power station from safe, elevated boardwalks. The flat, easy-to-walk paths are completely stroller-friendly and free to access.

Afternoon / 2h

Ybor City & the TECO Line Streetcar

Ride the historic, free streetcar into Ybor City to see the famous wild chickens roaming the brick-paved streets. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vintage tram cars and the colorful, clucking birds.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Armature Works

This trendy, restored historic building features a massive food hall with options for every picky eater and a spacious outdoor lawn. It is a fantastic spot to grab lunch while kids play on the grass by the river.

Morning / 2h

Lettuce Lake Park

Walk along the accessible, 3,500-foot boardwalk winding through a cypress swamp filled with wild turtles, herons, and alligators. The wide, flat boardwalk makes it incredibly easy to push a stroller deep into nature.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Ballast Point Park

Enjoy stunning views of the downtown skyline from this waterfront park featuring a massive shaded playground, a splash pad, and a stroller-friendly fishing pier. It is a peaceful, breezy spot to watch boats pass by.

Where to eat

  • Columbia Restaurant — Florida's oldest restaurant serves legendary Cuban sandwiches and kid-friendly empanadas in a gorgeous, tile-filled palace that captivates young eyes.
  • Goody Goody Burger — This retro Hyde Park diner offers classic butterscotch pies, fluffy pancakes, and mini sliders in a highly welcoming, family-friendly booth setting.
  • Sparkman Wharf — An open-air waterfront food court made from shipping containers with a massive artificial grass lawn where toddlers can run while parents eat.
  • Bo's Ice Cream — An iconic, walk-up soft-serve stand serving legendary upside-down banana splits and dipped cones since 1954.
👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense Florida humidity and afternoon thunderstorms by planning outdoor parks and zoo visits for early morning, saving air-conditioned museums for the midday heat. Keep a change of clothes, swim diapers, and towels in your diaper bag at all times, as Tampa's best parks feature unexpected, highly tempting splash pads.
