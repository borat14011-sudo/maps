Munich

← back to travel board
Munich welcomes families with its storybook architecture, sprawling green gardens, and a wonderfully relaxed, stroller-friendly pace. Children will love the glockenspiel's dancing figurines and the endless spaces to run, while parents enjoy the seamless public transit and welcoming beer gardens. It is the ultimate Bavarian playground where culture meets outdoor freedom for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-40 min S-Bahn train (S1 or S8) to Marienplatz, or a ~40 min taxi to central Munich hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Deutsches Museum (Kinderreich)

The world's largest science and technology museum features a dedicated Kids' Kingdom filled with interactive water play, giant building blocks, and a real fire engine. Toddlers will love pressing buttons and splashing safely in the water play zone.

Afternoon / 2-3h

English Garden (Englischer Garten)

This massive urban park is perfect for a leisurely stroll, watching the river surfers at Eisbachwelle, and letting kids feed the ducks. Toddlers can safely paddle in the shallow, gentle waters of the Schwabinger Bach stream.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Viktualienmarkt

Munich’s bustling open-air food market is a sensory delight with colorful fruit stalls, fresh pretzels, and a central shaded beer garden. It's highly stroller-friendly and great for a casual lunch where toddlers can snack on fresh berries.

Full Day / 4-5h

Hellabrunn Zoo

Set in a beautiful nature reserve along the Isar River, this geo-zoo groups animals by continent in spacious, natural enclosures. Toddlers will love the petting zoo area and the massive adventure playgrounds scattered throughout.

11:00 AM or 12:00 PM / 30 mins

Marienplatz Glockenspiel

Gather in Munich's central square to watch the historic clock tower put on a show with motorized copper figurines dancing and jousting. Hold toddlers on your shoulders so they can get a clear view of the colorful puppets spinning high above.

Afternoon / 2h

Nymphenburg Palace Gardens

This grand baroque palace features sprawling, flat lawns and canals where majestic swans glide by. It's an ideal spot for a stroller walk, and toddlers will be delighted by feeding the ducks or exploring the hidden woodland paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Munich Toy Museum

Located inside the old town hall tower, this cozy museum showcases a whimsical collection of historic European and American toys. While it is tight for large strollers, toddlers will be captivated by the vintage trains, dolls, and teddy bears.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Olympiapark

Built for the 1972 Olympics, this futuristic park offers rolling green hills, a large lake with pedal boats, and a Sea Life Aquarium. Toddlers will enjoy chasing bubbles on the grassy slopes or watching the colorful fish indoors.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Königlicher Hirschgarten

Munich's largest beer garden features an adjacent deer park where children can feed free-roaming fallow deer through the fence. Parents can enjoy a cold Bavarian brew while toddlers play safely in the nearby shaded playground.

🍽️ Where to eat

Hofbräukeller at Wiener Platz

Order the classic giant Bavarian pretzel and roast pork with potato dumplings while the kids enjoy the dedicated indoor play area.

Chinesischer Turm Beer Garden

Grab some traditional Steckerlfisch (grilled fish on a stick) and a side of obatzda cheese with pretzels while kids enjoy the historic wooden carousel next to the tower.

Wirtshaus in der Au

Feast on their famous oversized, fluffy potato dumplings served in a warm, welcoming Bavarian tavern setting that is highly accommodating to families.

Café Frischhut

Indulge in warm, freshly fried Schmalznudeln (traditional Bavarian donuts) sprinkled with sugar, which are an absolute favorite for kids and parents alike.

Ratskeller München

Try the kid-friendly potato pancakes with applesauce or the classic Wiener Schnitzel in this atmospheric, historic cellar restaurant right beneath Marienplatz.

👨‍👩‍👧 Purchase a Group Day Ticket (Gruppen-Tageskarte) for the transit network, which covers up to five adults and is highly economical for families navigating the stroller-accessible U-Bahn system. Note that most shops and supermarkets are strictly closed on Sundays, so plan ahead by stocking up on toddler snacks and diapers on Saturday.
