Split

Split enchants families with its colossal Diocletian's Palace, where kids can explore car-free stone alleys that feel like a living museum. Beyond the ruins, gentle pebble beaches and the lush pine forests of Marjan Hill offer the perfect natural playground. It's an effortlessly walkable coastal escape where history and relaxed Mediterranean living seamlessly blend.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi or private transfer from Split Airport (SPU) directly to the pedestrian-zone borders or beachside hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Diocletian's Palace Basement Halls

Step into the cool, cavernous substructures of a real Roman palace where kids can imagine ancient gladiators and dragons. It is stroller-accessible via the main southern ramps, offering a shaded break from the midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Marjan Hill Forest Park

Hike or ride the little tourist track up this green oasis for panoramic Adriatic views and a fantastic fenced playground at the top. Toddlers will love spotting local birds and running along the paved, car-free paths.

Morning / 2-3h

Bačvice Beach

Split's most famous sandy beach features exceptionally shallow, calm waters perfect for toddlers to splash safely. Parents can relax on rented loungers while watching locals play the traditional ball game of picigin in the shallows.

Morning / 1h

Pazar (Split Green Market)

A vibrant open-air market bursting with colorful fresh fruits, local cheeses, and sweet pastries just outside the palace walls. Toddlers will enjoy tasting sweet fresh figs or strawberries while soaking in the lively local atmosphere.

Evening / 1-2h

The Riva Harbor Promenade

This wide, completely pedestrianized palm-fringed harbor walk is ideal for letting toddlers toddle safely without traffic. Grab an ice cream, sit on the benches, and watch the massive ferry boats bobbing in the harbor.

Morning / 2h

Split Science Museum and Zoo on Marjan

A small, modest animal park and science center perfect for young children, featuring domestic animals and beautiful botanical garden surroundings. It's a low-key, manageable walk that won't overwhelm little legs.

Afternoon / 30m

Gregory of Nin Statue

Rub the shiny golden big toe of this colossal bishop statue for good luck and a fun family photo opportunity. Toddlers will be awed by its massive scale, and the surrounding Giardin Park is great for a quick run-around.

Midday / 3-4h

Bene Beach & Recreational Area

Tucked inside the Marjan forest, this pine-shaded beach offers natural midday shade, concrete and pebble areas, plus an onsite playground. It is easily reachable via bus 12, making it a stress-free day trip with a stroller.

Afternoon / 1h

Froggyland

A delightfully quirky museum featuring over 500 stuffed frogs meticulously arranged in humorous, human-like scenarios. Toddlers are mesmerized by the intricate, toy-like displays, making it a perfect quick indoor distraction.

🍽️ Where to eat

Villa Spiza

Get the daily changing handmade pasta or fresh seafood risotto, which is simple, delicious, and highly kid-approved.

Buffet Fife

Order the hearty, comforting pašticada beef stew with gnocchi at this bustling, ultra-casual, family-friendly harbor-side staple.

Konoba Varoš

Savor the slow-cooked peka meat under a iron bell in their spacious, welcoming courtyard where kids are treated like family.

Luka Ice Cream & Cakes

Treat the family to unique, all-natural artisan gelato flavors like rosemary-lemon or classic chocolate, served in a quiet alley.

Bokamorra

Enjoy upscale yet family-friendly wood-fired Neapolitan pizzas with creative toppings in a vibrant, stylish atmosphere.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to a baby carrier rather than a heavy stroller, as Diocletian's Palace features many steep stone steps, narrow alleys, and uneven cobblestones. To avoid the intense summer heat and crowds, schedule beach visits and outdoor palace explorations for early morning, reserving the afternoon for shaded Marjan Hill or indoor naps.
