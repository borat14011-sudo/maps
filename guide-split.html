What to do

Early morning 9am-11am

Diocletian's Palace

Explore the ancient Roman palace, with stroller-accessible paths through the Peristyle and underground cellars (cool and shaded). Avoid afternoon crowds for a peaceful morning.

Late morning 11am-12pm

Saint Domnius Cathedral

Climb the bell tower for stunning views (carrier recommended for baby), but the church itself is quiet and stroller-friendly.

Late afternoon 4pm-6pm

Riva Waterfront

A wide, flat promenade lined with cafes and palm trees, perfect for stroller walks. Gentle sea breezes and ice cream stands keep everyone happy.

Morning 9:30am-12pm

Marjan Hill

Stroller-friendly paths up the hill with shaded pine forests and scenic viewpoints. Pack a picnic and enjoy the children's playground at the base.

Mid-morning to early afternoon 10am-2pm

Bacvice Beach

Croatia's most famous urban beach with shallow, calm waters ideal for infant paddling. Rent sunbeds and umbrellas for a comfortable day.

Late morning 10am-12pm

Mestrovic Gallery

A stunning villa-museum with gardens and sculptures, stroller accessible. The quiet indoor spaces and serene garden paths are ideal for a relaxed visit.

Late afternoon 5pm-7pm

Old Town Streets

Wander the narrow cobblestone alleys of Diocletian's Palace – stroller may be bumpy, but many streets are manageable. Stop for gelato and people-watch.

Morning 9am-11am

Salona Archaeological Park

Ancient Roman ruins just outside Split, with grassy fields and open spaces perfect for a baby to crawl. Shaded paths and minimal crowds.

Late morning 10am-1pm

Kasjuni Beach

A pebbly beach with crystal clear water, less crowded than Bacvice. Shade from pines, and a small café. Bring a stroller with larger wheels for the path.

🍽️ Where to eat

Konoba Matejuška

Family-run seafood taverna on the waterfront, serving grilled fish and black risotto. Highchair available, outdoor terrace, early dinner seating works well with infants.

Zora

Modern Dalmatian cuisine with a focus on local ingredients. Quiet indoor atmosphere, highchairs, and a kids' menu. Signature dish: lamb under the bell.

Bokeria Restaurant

Trendy spot in the old town with Mediterranean tapas and an outdoor courtyard. Lively but not too loud, highchairs provided. Try the octopus salad.

Villa Spiza

Intimate bistro serving daily fresh seafood and pasta. No highchairs but staff accommodating; early seating recommended (opens 6pm). Their homemade gnocchi is a hit.

Kantun Paulina

Famed for its slow-cooked lamb and peka dishes, in a rustic setting. Family-friendly with a large garden terrace. Highchairs available. Reserve ahead.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Park Split

$200-400/night

Five-star boutique hotel in a quiet area, 10-min walk to the Riva. Offers family suites with separate living area, crib on request, heated outdoor pool, and a serene garden. Blackout curtains in suites.

Hotel Vestibul Palace

$250-400/night

Luxury boutique hotel within Diocletian's Palace walls, with a private courtyard. Family suite includes a king bed and sofa bed, crib available. Rooftop pool with city views. Stroller-friendly but cobblestones outside.

Le Méridien Lav Split

$200-350/night

Resort-style hotel 20 minutes from center, with multiple pools (including a kids' pool), private beach access, and spacious family rooms. Crib and baby essentials provided upon request. Great for longer stays.

👨‍👩‍👧 Split's old town cobblestones can be tough for strollers; use a carrier for tight alleys and the Riva's smooth promenade for walks. Pharmacies (e.g., Ljekarna Split) are well-stocked with baby formula, diapers, and sunscreen. For heat management, plan indoor activities between 11am-4pm and use shaded courtyards or the hotel pool. The pediatric unit at KBC Split (University Hospital) offers emergency care if needed.
