Tokyo

← back to travel board
Tokyo is a dazzling wonderland where futuristic bullet trains zip past centuries-old wooden shrines draped in cherry blossoms. It is remarkably clean, safe, and welcoming for little explorers, offering a seamless blend of high-energy play zones and serene, stroller-friendly parks. From sensory-rich digital art museums to hidden playground gems, the city turns every daily outing into an unforgettable family adventure.

What to do

Morning / 2h

teamLab Planets Tokyo

An immersive digital art museum where kids can wade through warm, glowing water filled with projected koi fish. Toddlers will love the sensory lights, but pack a change of clothes just in case.

Afternoon / 2h

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

A massive green oasis featuring sprawling lawns perfect for toddlers to run free and a greenhouse with tropical plants. It is highly stroller-friendly and ideal for a relaxing family picnic under the trees.

Early Morning / 1-2h

Tsukiji Outer Market

A bustling street market packed with sweet tamagoyaki (rolled omelets) on wooden skewers and fresh strawberries. Go early to avoid heavy crowds, which can be tough to navigate with a stroller.

Morning / 3h

Tokyo Toy Museum

Housed in an old schoolhouse, this multi-story wonderland is filled with beautiful, handcrafted wooden toys and interactive playrooms. There is a dedicated, safe playroom designed specifically for babies and toddlers under two.

Morning / 1-2h

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest and most iconic temple features a giant red lantern and a lively approach street lined with sweet-smelling traditional snacks. Toddlers will be charmed by the colorful five-story pagoda and the gentle smoke from the incense burner.

Afternoon / 3h

Ueno Zoo and Park

Japan's oldest zoo is home to giant pandas, elephants, and a lovely children's petting area. The surrounding park features wide paths and a large playground that is fantastic for active toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Sumida Aquarium

Located inside Tokyo Skytree Town, this modern indoor aquarium features a massive open-pool penguin enclosure and glowing jellyfish tunnels. It is completely flat and stroller-friendly, making it a stress-free sensory escape.

Morning / 2h

Meiji Jingu Shrine and Yoyogi Park

Walk down a wide, shady forested path to this majestic Shinto shrine, then head next door to Yoyogi Park to let toddlers chase pigeons on the lawns. The gravel paths can be bumpy, so a sturdy stroller or baby carrier is recommended.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Sunshine City Alpa and Pokémon Center Mega Tokyo

A massive indoor shopping mall in Ikebukuro featuring a giant Pokémon Center and a rooftop sky garden. It is an easy, air-conditioned spot to walk around on a rainy day with excellent nursing rooms.

Where to eat

  • Harajuku Gyozaro — Famous for its simple, delicious pan-fried and steamed dumplings that even picky toddlers will devour in a casual, welcoming setting.
  • Kura Sushi — A fun, high-tech conveyor belt sushi spot where kids can order via tablet, watch plates zoom to their table, and win small toys by recycling empty plates.
  • Tokyo Station Ramen Street — A convenient underground collection of top-tier ramen shops, many of which offer high chairs, child-sized bowls, and quick service.
  • Mister Donut — This ubiquitous Japanese chain serves adorable, incredibly soft Pon de Ring donuts, including cute character-themed treats that toddlers will love.
