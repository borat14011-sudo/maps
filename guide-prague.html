Prague

← back to travel board
Prague wraps families in a real-life storybook world where gothic spires tower over sprawling, green parks and winding pedestrian streets. Children will be captivated by mechanical clocks and whimsical puppet shops, while parents enjoy the highly walkable, historic charm. It is an enchanting European escape that feels both magically ancient and effortlessly welcoming for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi or family-friendly private transfer to Old Town hotels

What to do

Early Morning / 1-2h

Charles Bridge Morning Walk

Cross this historic pedestrian bridge early in the day to enjoy the street musicians and stunning river views without the crowds. Toddlers will love watching the boats glide underneath the stone arches.

Morning / 2-3h

Prague Castle and Golden Lane

Explore the fairytale courtyards of the world's largest ancient castle complex and watch the colorful changing of the guard. Toddlers will feel like royalty wandering the tiny, brightly painted houses of Golden Lane.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Petřín Hill Funicular and Mirror Maze

Ride the historic funicular railway up the hill to get lost in a whimsical, toddler-friendly hall of mirrors. The lush surrounding park is perfect for a relaxed family picnic and letting little ones run free.

Morning or Afternoon / 1h

Old Town Square Astronomical Clock

Gather in the historic square to watch the magical, 600-year-old mechanical show of moving figures and a tolling skeleton. It is short enough to keep a toddler's attention span completely captivated.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Prague Zoo and Kids' Reserve

Visit one of the world's top-rated zoos, featuring a chairlift, a children's tram, and a massive petting zoo. It is incredibly stroller-accessible with plenty of shaded play areas for toddlers to decompress.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Letná Park Playgrounds and Views

Let the kids run wild at the excellent wooden playgrounds while you soak in the most iconic panoramic views of Prague's bridges. Parents can grab a refreshing Czech beer at the casual outdoor beer garden nearby.

Afternoon or Evening / 2h

Manifesto Market Anděl

This vibrant, open-air food market offers a diverse range of global cuisines in a highly chic, family-friendly container village. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow water pool or playing in the sand while parents dine.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Slovanský Island Paddle Boats

Rent a whimsical swan-shaped pedal boat to cruise around the calm waters of the Vltava River. The island also features an excellent fenced-in playground and a miniature train ride perfect for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Hamleys Prague Experience

Far more than just a store, this massive toy paradise features a giant indoor slide, a real Venetian carousel, and an immersive butterfly house. It is the ultimate rainy-day sanctuary that toddlers will never want to leave.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café Louvre

Order the traditional Czech beef sirloin in cream sauce (svíčková) with bread dumplings, followed by their legendary hot chocolate.

Výtopna Railway Restaurant

Get your burgers, fries, and cold drinks delivered directly to your table by a buzzing network of intricate model trains.

Lokál Dlouhááá

Try the ultra-fresh Pilsner Urquell straight from the tanks alongside crispy, gooey fried cheese (smažený sýr) that kids love.

Angelato

Grab a scoop of their award-winning, all-natural pistachio or mango gelato near the base of Petřín Hill.

Cukrář Skála

Treat the family to a modern take on the traditional Czech cream roll (kremrole) while watching pastry chefs work through a giant glass wall.

👨‍👩‍👧 Prague's historic core is heavily cobblestoned, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier rather than a flimsy umbrella stroller. Most tram lines are highly modern and low-floor, making them incredibly easy to board with a stroller to save tired little legs.
