What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Castillo San Felipe del Morro

Explore a massive 16th-century fort with sweeping ocean views and sprawling grassy lawns. It is a fantastic spot for toddlers to run and fly kites, though parents should keep a close eye on steep drops and uneven steps.

Morning / 3h

Balneario de Carolina

A calm, shaded beach just outside the city center featuring gentle waves, golden sand, and great amenities. Toddlers will love wading in the shallow waters while parents appreciate the ample shade under the palm trees.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo de la Princesa

A scenic, tree-lined pedestrian promenade that wraps along the historic city walls. It is completely stroller-friendly and features street vendors selling sweet shaved ice (piraguas) to keep toddlers happy.

Afternoon / 1h

Plaza de Armas

The main historic square of Old San Juan, home to a beautiful four-seasons fountain and plenty of space to rest. Toddlers will be delighted watching and chasing the friendly local pigeons while parents relax on shaded benches.

Morning / 3-4h

Museo del Niño de Carolina

A massive interactive children's museum located just minutes from San Juan, complete with an outdoor mini-zoo and go-karts. It features a dedicated indoor soft play area that is perfect for toddlers to safely burn off energy.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza del Mercado de Río Piedras

A bustling, authentic local indoor market filled with colorful mounds of tropical fruits and vegetables. It is a sensory delight for little ones who can sample sweet, fresh bananas and passion fruit.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Save a Gato Cat Sanctuary

Stroll along the historic Paseo del Morro to spot the famous community cats cared for by this local non-profit. Toddlers will love counting the colorful cats lounging on the rocks, though they should be reminded to look but not touch.

Afternoon / 30m

Parque de las Palomas

An iconic gated park perched on the city walls where hundreds of pigeons gather. You can buy cheap bags of corn to feed them, which is a thrilling experience for brave toddlers who want the birds to land on their hands.

Morning / 3h

La Marquesa Forest Park

A lush nature reserve just south of the city in Guaynabo featuring aviaries, a butterfly garden, and a funicular ride. The cable car ride is a thrilling, easy way for toddlers to experience the rainforest canopy without a long hike.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chocobar Cortés

Order the famous chocolate-infused grilled cheese sandwich paired with a rich, kid-friendly hot chocolate.

Café el Punto

Try the mild, sweet chicken mofongo and a glass of fresh passion fruit juice.

Lote 23

Grab crispy fried chicken sliders at Hen House within this open-air food truck park that has space for toddlers to wander.

Señor Paleta

Cool off with an artisanal strawberry-cheesecake or passion fruit gelato ice pop.

La Mallorquina

Enjoy sweet and savory mallorca breakfast sandwiches dusted with powdered sugar at Puerto Rico's oldest restaurant.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a sturdy, lightweight umbrella stroller with good wheels, as the historic blue cobblestones of Old San Juan can make for a very bumpy ride. Always pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and rash guards to protect sensitive skin from the intense Caribbean sun.
