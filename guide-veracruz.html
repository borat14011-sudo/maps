Veracruz

← back to travel board
Veracruz beckons families with its rhythmic marimba beats, warm sea breezes, and a captivating blend of Spanish colonial history and Gulf Coast culture. Children will love the bustling boardwalks and massive aquariums, while parents savor the world-famous coffee and rich culinary heritage. It is an authentic Mexican escape where history feels alive and the ocean is always within reach.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to the main beach hotels in Boca del Río or downtown Veracruz

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Aquarium de Veracruz

Explore Mexico's largest aquarium to see majestic manatees, playful penguins, and walking shark tunnels. Toddlers will love the interactive touch tanks and the stroller-friendly flat ramps throughout the facility.

Morning / 2h

San Juan de Ulúa

Step back in time at this historic island fortress featuring imposing stone walls, drawbridges, and old cannons. Because of the uneven historic pathways, we recommend using a baby carrier instead of a stroller here.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

El Malecón

Take a breezy stroll along the wide, paved boardwalk to watch massive cargo ships slip into the harbor. It is completely flat and stroller-friendly, making it the perfect spot for an easy sunset walk with snacks.

Evening / 1-2h

Zócalo de Veracruz

Relax in the vibrant main square as marimba bands play and locals dance the traditional danzón. Toddlers will love the open, car-free plaza space to run around and groove to the music.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

Immerse your senses in this bustling public market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local toys, and fragrant spices. Keep toddlers close in a carrier as the narrow aisles can get quite crowded and busy.

Mid-day / 2-3h

Mandinga Lagoon

Take a gentle, scenic boat ride through peaceful mangrove tunnels to spot nesting herons and local fishermen. Local boat captains provide small life jackets, making this a safe and calming nature escape for young kids.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Cívica Boca del Río

Visit this charming, family-friendly town square in the Boca area that features a small playground and nearby ice cream parlors. It is a wonderfully shaded spot for parents to rest while toddlers burn off some energy.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo Naval México

This highly interactive naval museum features scale model ships, flight simulators, and hands-on navigation exhibits. The spacious outdoor central courtyard has miniature wooden ships perfect for toddler sensory play.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Villa del Mar

Splash in the calm, shallow waters of this popular city beach lined with casual seafood palapas. The waves are very gentle, making it an ideal spot for toddlers to build sandcastles safely under the shade of an umbrella.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gran Café de la Parroquia

Order the iconic 'café con leche' poured dramatically from copper kettles and pair it with sweet, freshly baked 'concha' pastries for the kids.

Villa Rica Mocambo

Savor the signature 'Pescado a la Veracruzana' (red snapper in tomato, olive, and caper sauce) right on the beach while the kids play safely in the sand.

La Estancia de Boca

Try the savory 'empanadas de minilla' stuffed with spiced shredded fish alongside fresh, toddler-friendly tropical fruit juices.

Nevería Güero Güero

Treat the family to traditional hand-churned exotic fruit sorbets, with the creamy coconut and sweet mamey flavors being absolute crowd-pleasers.

Mariscos Villa Rica Centro

Feast on 'arroz a la tumbada,' a rich and comforting brothy seafood rice dish that is mild enough for kids to share and enjoy.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of lightweight, breathable clothing and high-SPF sunscreen to handle the Gulf Coast's intense humidity and heat. Opt for a sturdy baby carrier over a heavy stroller when exploring historic cobblestone sites like San Juan de Ulúa.
