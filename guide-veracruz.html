Veracruz

← back to travel board
Veracruz beckons discerning families with its intoxicating blend of Spanish colonial architecture, vibrant marimba rhythms, and rich Afro-Caribbean heritage. The gentle Gulf breeze keeps the palm-fringed avenues pleasant, while the wide, stroller-friendly Malecón boardwalk offers seamless coastal walks with baby Mary Claire. Parents can retreat to the upscale enclave of Boca del Río, where calm, shallow waters and premium oceanfront suites provide an effortless sanctuary. It is an authentic, under-the-radar Mexican escape that balances historical exploration with top-tier modern comforts.
🚕 Getting there: From Veracruz International Airport (VER) to the luxury hotel strip in Boca del Río, it is a seamless 20-minute drive. We recommend booking a private luxury SUV transfer equipped with a pre-installed, rear-facing infant car seat for Mary Claire and ample space for your stroller.

What to do

9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

San Juan de Ulúa

Explore this historic fortress via private tour; early mornings are best for cool breezes. Stroller-accessible on main paths, but bring a carrier for the ramparts.

10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Acuario de Veracruz

One of Latin America's largest aquariums is fully air-conditioned and highly stroller-friendly, ideal for Mary Claire to watch the sharks and manatees in cool comfort.

5:30 PM - 7:00 PM

The Malecón

Take a sunset stroll along this wide, flat, and paved promenade to enjoy the sea breeze while Mary Claire safely naps in her stroller.

6:00 PM - 7:30 PM

Plaza de Armas (Zócalo)

Watch traditional Danzón dancers under the shade of palm trees. Arrive early evening before the crowds and noise peak, enjoying a fresh coconut water.

11:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Museo Naval México

A beautifully restored colonial building featuring air-conditioned exhibition rooms and an expansive, flat courtyard perfect for strollers.

1:00 PM - 3:00 PM

Mandinga Lagoon

Take a private, shaded boat ride through calm mangrove tunnels. The gentle rocking motion is highly soothing for an infant's afternoon nap.

3:30 PM - 5:30 PM

Boca del Río Beaches

Relax under a private shaded palapa on the calmest beaches in the area, offering shallow, gentle Gulf waves ideal for dipping Mary Claire's toes.

9:00 AM - 2:00 PM

Coatepec Coffee Town Day Trip

A short, scenic drive into the cool highlands. Wander the flat, lush gardens of a boutique coffee estate while wearing Mary Claire in a carrier.

10:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Xalapa Museum of Anthropology (MAX)

Stroll through world-class indoor galleries and serene, shaded outdoor gardens showcasing massive Olmec heads; exceptionally quiet and stroller-friendly.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gran Café de la Parroquia

Savor their legendary 'Lechero' coffee and fresh conchas. Opt for an early morning 7:30 AM outdoor table to avoid crowds, offering plenty of space for strollers.

Villa Rica Mocambo

Indulge in high-end seafood like Huachinango a la Veracruzana (red snapper) in an elegant, air-conditioned dining room right by the beach, complete with highchairs.

Mardel

Upmarket contemporary dining with stunning ocean views. Their spacious, ocean-facing terrace easily accommodates strollers, making a 6:00 PM reservation ideal.

El Celler

An upscale international and Spanish dining spot in Boca del Río. Very quiet and relaxed, perfect for an early evening dinner with a sleeping baby.

Mariscos Villa Rica (Boca del Río)

Delight in fresh crab claws and octopus under a breezy, upscale beachside palapa. The spacious outdoor setting minimizes noise concerns if Mary Claire gets vocal.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Fiesta Americana Veracruz

$200-400/night (Premium Suites)

For a true five-star beachfront experience, book a Governor or Presidential Suite ($200-400/night for premium family suites; standard rooms run under $150/night). Perfect for baby Mary Claire, the resort offers complimentary cribs, a massive outdoor lagoon pool, direct beach access to calm shores, and excellent blackout curtains for daytime naps.

Hotel Emporio Veracruz

$200-400/night (Premium Suites)

Located right on the vibrant harbor, booking a Master Suite with ocean views ($200-400/night; standard rooms are under $150) grants premium space and club lounge access. Excellent indoor and outdoor pools, robust crib availability, and stroller-friendly access to the historic center make it ideal.

Camino Real Veracruz

$200-400/night (Premium Suites)

Featuring a striking boomerang-shaped architectural design in Boca del Río, a luxury Master Suite here ($200-400/night for premium tiers; standards under $150) offers sweeping sea views and private balconies. Features include a beautiful outdoor family pool, cribs on request, and immediate access to safe, shallow beach coves.
