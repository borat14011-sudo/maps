Cape Verde (Sal Island)

Sal Island greets affluent families with its mesmerizing turquoise waters and the infectious, relaxed rhythm of its legendary 'No Stress' philosophy. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the sprawling, stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks of Santa Maria Beach offer seamless transitions from beachfront lounging to chic seaside dining. High-end resorts here cater effortlessly to infants with dedicated shaded pools, personalized nanny services, and calm, wind-protected lagoons ideal for gentle dips. It is an exotic, sun-soaked sanctuary where European sophistication meets vibrant West African warmth.
🚕 Getting there: Amílcar Cabral International Airport (SID) to Santa Maria hotel strip: 20-minute drive via private air-conditioned Mercedes V-Class transfer, pre-arranged with a premium infant car seat and ample space for a luxury travel stroller.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Santa Maria Beach

Boasting miles of powder-soft golden sand and calm shallow waters, this iconic beach features wide wooden boardwalks perfect for strolling with a premium stroller. Will and Kathryn can relax under upscale cabanas while Mary Claire dips her toes in the gentle tide.

Early morning 8:30 AM - 9:30 AM / 1h

Pontão de Santa Maria

The colorful wooden pier is the beating heart of Sal, where local fishermen bring in their daily catch of massive tuna. It is fully stroller-accessible, though visiting in the early morning ensures you skip the midday heat and the largest crowds.

Late afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM / 1.5h

Viveiro, Botanical Garden & Zoo di Terra

A lush desert oasis featuring beautifully manicured paths, vibrant bougainvillea, and gentle farm animals. This is a quiet, shaded retreat perfect for Mary Claire to see rabbits and goats; the gravel paths are easily managed with an all-terrain stroller.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Pedra de Lume Salinas

An extraordinary salt crater inside an extinct volcano. While the high-density salt water is too harsh for infant skin, parents can take turns floating in the therapeutic waters while the other stays with Mary Claire in the shaded cafe area.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM / 1.5h

Shark Bay

An incredible shallow bay where baby lemon sharks swim around your ankles. Will and Kathryn can take turns wading out with water shoes, keeping Mary Claire safe and dry in an ergonomic carrier on the sandy shoreline under a UV umbrella.

Sunset 5:00 PM - 6:30 PM / 1.5h

Project Biodiversity Turtle Hatchery

A dedicated sea turtle conservation site located on the dunes. Visiting during the late afternoon allows you to learn about nesting loggerhead turtles in a quiet environment, with the chance to witness a magical sunset hatchling release.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM / 1.5h

Buracona (The Blue Eye)

A breathtaking natural pool carved into volcanic rock where the sun illuminates a striking turquoise 'eye'. The viewing platform is safe, though a baby carrier is highly recommended over a stroller due to the rocky terrain.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM / 2h

Kite Beach

The thrilling hub of the island's kitesurfing scene. The colorful kites in the sky will mesmerize Mary Claire, and the modern, open-air beach club provides excellent windbreaks and shaded daybeds to keep her comfortable.

Late afternoon 4:30 PM - 5:30 PM / 1h

Monte Curral

The high peak rising above the capital city of Espargos. A private vehicle can drive you directly to the summit, requiring zero strenuous walking while offering panoramic, breezy views of the entire desert landscape.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Pastis

An intimate, upscale restaurant tucked into a romantic courtyard, famous for its prime Brazilian beef tenderloin and lobster. Booking the earliest seating is recommended; they easily accommodate families with highchairs in the cozy outdoor space.

LobStar

A refined, modern seafood bistro in Santa Maria specializing in fresh-caught lobster and octopus. The spacious outdoor street-side terrace is stroller-friendly, and the atmosphere is lively enough to drown out any baby squeals.

Barracuda

A premier beachfront institution offering elegant local Creole and Portuguese dishes. It features direct sand access and a spacious ocean-facing deck where a gentle sea breeze keeps baby Mary Claire cool during an early sunset dinner.

Américo's Restaurant

An elegant, long-running dining room known for its impeccably prepared seafood cataplana and top-tier service. They offer early dinner seatings, spacious table layouts, and highchairs, making it a stress-free luxury experience with an infant.

Cape Fruit

A bohemian-chic café decorated with lush plants and colorful swings, serving organic juices, healthy wraps, and fresh fruit purees perfect for Mary Claire. Fully shaded and incredibly relaxed, it is the ideal spot to escape the midday heat.

The pinnacle of luxury on the island, boasting a massive zero-entry pool, lush manicured gardens, and direct beach access. They provide complimentary high-quality wooden cribs, blackout curtains, spacious ocean-view suites, and a dedicated team to arrange private in-room nanny services.

Hotel Morabeza

$200-350/night

A legendary, family-run boutique hotel directly on Santa Maria Beach. It offers elegant, multi-room family suites, quiet garden-facing pools away from the wind, and paved, stroller-friendly pathways connecting the rooms to the sea-facing terraces.

Odjo d'Água Hotel

$200-300/night

A charming, upscale boutique hotel built on a rocky point overlooking a sheltered sandy cove. Featuring spacious executive suites, a quiet beachfront infinity pool, and a highly-regarded restaurant, it offers a tranquil, intimate retreat with a private beach feel.
