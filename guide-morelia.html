Morelia

🚕 Getting there: ~35 min taxi or private shuttle from General Francisco Mujica International Airport (MLM) to the historic downtown center

What to do

Late Afternoon / Saturday Evening

Catedral de Morelia

This majestic pink stone cathedral dominates the skyline and hosts a spectacular, family-friendly light and music show on Saturday nights. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles in the spacious Plaza de Armas right outside.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Acueducto de Morelia

Walk alongside the 253 impressive stone arches of this 18th-century aqueduct, which is beautifully lit at night. The adjacent paved pathway is completely stroller-friendly for an easy afternoon stroll.

Morning / 3-4h

Parque Zoológico Benito Juárez

One of the largest zoos in Mexico, featuring a beautiful lake with pedal boats and a massive variety of animals. Toddlers will love the petting zoo area and the miniature train ride.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Mercado de Dulces y Artesanías Valentín Gómez Farías

A colorful, sensory-rich market filled with traditional Mexican candies like ate, cajeta, and colorful sugar skulls. Toddlers will be wide-eyed at the bright colors, and you can easily grab a soft, sweet treat for them.

Morning / 1h

Orquidario de Morelia

Located inside the Centro de Convenciones park, this lush greenhouse showcases hundreds of vibrant orchid species in a calm, misty environment. It is a quiet, low-sensory escape that is easy to navigate with a toddler.

Morning or Late Afternoon / 2h

Bosque Cuauhtémoc

Morelia's largest central park features shaded walking paths, a dedicated children's playground, and a small skate park. It’s the perfect spot for toddlers to burn off energy under the shade of giant trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Casa de las Artesanías de Michoacán

Housed in a former monastery, this vibrant center displays and sells incredible handmade toys, textiles, and pottery from around Michoacán. Kids will be fascinated by the colorful wooden toys and painted masks.

Morning / 30 mins

Callejón del Romance

This narrow, pedestrian-only alleyway is adorned with pink stone walls, climbing bougainvillea, and poetic inscriptions. It's safe from traffic, making it a great spot for toddlers to walk freely while you snap family photos.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Planetario de Morelia Felipe Rivera

Located in the same park as the orchid house, this planetarium offers exciting dome shows about the stars and universe. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glowing projections on the giant ceiling.

🍽️ Where to eat

Lu Cocina Michoacana

Order the traditional Uchepos (sweet corn tamales) and Corundas, which are mild and perfectly soft for toddler palates.

Tata Mezcalería + Cocina de Autor

Try the slow-cooked pork carnitas or the artisanal trout dishes while sitting in their beautiful, spacious open-air courtyard.

La Conspiración de 1809

Get the traditional Sopa Tarasca, a creamy bean and tomato soup topped with tortilla strips and avocado that kids love.

Cenaduría La Inmaculada

Get the crispy buñuelos drenched in sweet syrup or the classic local quesadillas in a lively, casual courtyard setting.

Café del Museo del Dulce Calle Real

Treat the family to authentic hot chocolate served in clay mugs alongside fresh, sweet Conchas and artisanal Ate fruit jellies.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to the historic center's pedestrian-friendly streets, but bring a sturdy, compact stroller to handle the occasional uneven cobblestones. Keep a stash of small change handy for public restrooms and the irresistible street vendors selling fresh fruit cups and sweet gazpacho.
