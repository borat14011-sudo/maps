St. John

← back to travel board
With over sixty percent of its lush landscape protected as a national park, St. John offers a serene refuge of calm turquoise waters and soft white sands. Perfect for slow-paced exploration, families can wander shaded sugar mill ruins or splash alongside friendly sea turtles in shallow, wave-free bays. It is an effortlessly beautiful destination where the rhythms of nature dictate your perfect beach day.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi from STT airport to Red Hook ferry, 20 min ferry to Cruz Bay, then 10-15 min open-air safari taxi to north shore beaches

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Trunk Bay

Widely considered one of the world's most beautiful beaches, it features an underwater snorkeling trail and incredibly soft, powdery sand. The calm, lifeguarded shoreline makes it incredibly safe for toddlers to splash in the shallows.

Morning / 2-3h

Maho Bay Beach

This shallow, calm bay is famous for the wild sea turtles feeding on the seagrass close to shore. Toddlers will love wading in the knee-deep, wave-free water, while parents can grab shaved ice and street tacos from the local vendors right across the road.

Morning or Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Annaberg Sugar Plantation Ruins

An accessible historical site offering flat, stroller-friendly paths winding through dramatic stone ruins and massive historic windmills. Shaded by large trees, it is an easy walk where kids can spot roaming friendly goats and iguanas.

Morning / 1h

Cinnamon Bay Nature Trail

A short, level loop trail that takes you through lush tropical forest, historic ruins, and over an elevated wooden boardwalk. It is the perfect length for toddlers to stretch their legs under a dense canopy of shade.

Morning / 2-3h

Hansen Bay Beach

Located on the quieter East End, this secluded beach offers exceptionally calm, warm waters and plenty of beach toys provided by the welcoming hosts. It is the ultimate crowd-free paradise for families looking for a relaxed, shallow swim.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Cruz Bay Beachfront Playground

A lovely beachfront play park located right in the heart of Cruz Bay with modern climbing structures, slides, and swings. It is the perfect spot for kids to burn off energy while parents enjoy the ocean breeze and watch the ferry boats come and go.

Midday / 1h

Virgin Islands National Park Visitor Center

A colorful, air-conditioned educational hub offering interactive exhibits on marine life and island history. Toddlers will love the taxidermy sea creatures and touch tables, and kids can earn a Junior Ranger badge here.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Our Market Smoothies

A vibrant local stand in Cruz Bay serving up freshly blended tropical fruit smoothies made with local passionfruit, mango, and soursop. It is a delicious, cooling treat that toddlers love after a long morning in the sun.

Morning / 1-2h

Leinster Bay Trail

An easy, flat dirt path running along the water's edge with stunning views of Tortola across the channel. The trail is completely stroller-accessible and leads to a quiet, shallow cove perfect for beachcombing with young kids.
