Malta

← back to travel board
Malta packs thousands of years of history, shimmering turquoise lagoons, and honey-hued walled cities into a compact, sun-drenched archipelago. With its pedestrian-friendly piazzas, gentle beaches, and welcoming Mediterranean culture, it is an effortless playground for families traveling with little ones. From castle-topped towns to boat rides in hidden coves, every day feels like an accessible storybook adventure.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi to Valletta, or ~35 min private shuttle to the northern beach resorts in Mellieħa

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Popeye Village

Explore the whimsical wooden houses of this original 1980 film set, featuring splash pools, puppet shows, and boat rides. Toddlers will love the colorful characters and the easy-to-navigate boardwalks around the bay.

Afternoon / 3h

Esplora Interactive Science Centre

Housed in a historic villa overlooking the harbor, this hands-on science center features an excellent outdoor adventure park and a dedicated sensory play area. It's the perfect indoor escape on a hot afternoon with plenty of interactive exhibits for little hands.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Mdina Silent City

Wander the traffic-free, golden-stone alleys of this ancient walled city where kids can safely run around without car worries. Toddlers will be enchanted by the colorful doors and horse-drawn carriages clip-clopping by.

Morning / 2h

Malta National Aquarium

Walk through the impressive underwater tunnel to see sharks, rays, and colorful Mediterranean fish up close. Toddlers will enjoy the touch pools and the fantastic sea-themed outdoor playground right outside the exit.

Morning at 11:30 AM / 1-2h

Upper Barrakka Gardens

Watch the daily noon cannon firing at the Saluting Battery and enjoy panoramic views of the Grand Harbour from this shady, flower-filled garden. The wide, flat pathways are perfect for strollers, and there are resident cats to spot.

Morning or Late Afternoon / 3h

Golden Bay Beach

This wide, sandy beach offers gentle, shallow waters perfect for toddlers to splash in safely. Facilities are excellent, with sun loungers, cafes, and easy parking nearby making it stress-free for families.

Sunday Morning / 2h

Marsaxlokk Fish Market

Stroll along the harbor to see the iconic, brightly painted 'luzzu' fishing boats with the traditional Eye of Osiris. The bustling market is a sensory delight, and the flat promenade is very stroller-friendly.

Afternoon / 2h

Buskett Gardens

Malta’s only woodland area offers shady pathways, orange groves, and quiet picnic spots away from the beach crowds. It is a peaceful nature retreat where toddlers can safely collect pinecones and watch for local lizards.

Evening / 2h

Valletta Waterfront

Walk along this beautiful, pedestrianized promenade lined with historic 18th-century warehouses painted in vibrant colors. It’s an ideal spot for an easy evening stroll with plenty of space for toddlers to run alongside the giant harbor ships.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ta' Doni

Grab a traditional Maltese Ftira, a delicious sourdough flatbread sandwich stuffed with tuna, capers, and olives, served in a relaxed, family-friendly setting.

Guzé Bistro

Try the slow-cooked traditional Maltese rabbit stew (Fenek) or homemade pasta in a cozy, welcoming 16th-century building that happily accommodates families.

Crystal Palace

Pick up cheap, warm, flaky pastizzi (savory pastries filled with ricotta cheese or mushy peas) that toddlers love to nibble on for a quick snack.

One80 Kitchen & Lounge

Enjoy fresh Mediterranean seafood platters on the spacious, family-friendly terrace overlooking the beautiful Mellieħa bay.

Sotto Pizzeria

Order the classic, ultra-crispy Roman-style Pinsa pizza, which is always a guaranteed crowd-pleaser for picky young eaters.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a lightweight, sturdy umbrella stroller or a baby carrier, as the historic cobblestones and steep hills in Valletta can be challenging for heavy travel systems. Always carry plenty of water and sun hats for the little ones, as shade can be scarce when exploring the ancient open-air fortresses.
