La Paz

← back to travel board
Nestled along the Sea of Cortez, La Paz is a peaceful coastal haven boasting shallow, wave-free bays perfect for tiny splashers. Families can easily stroll the lively, flat malecón or embark on low-stress boat trips to meet curious sea lions. It is the ultimate laid-back Mexican getaway where toddler-friendly nature meets rich local culture.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle from La Paz Airport (LAP) to downtown hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Balandra

A stunning, crystal-clear lagoon where the water stays ankle-deep for hundreds of yards, making it incredibly safe for toddlers to splash. Toddler note: Bring a pop-up tent and snacks, as there are no shops or facilities on this protected beach.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

El Malecon Boardwalk

This wide, paved sea-side boardwalk is completely stroller-friendly and features whimsical bronze marine sculptures and several public playgrounds. Toddler note: Visit at sunset when the air cools down and local families come out to play.

Morning / 5-6h

Isla Espiritu Santo Boat Tour

A scenic boat ride to a protected island where you can snorkel with friendly, playful sea lion pups in shallow waters. Toddler note: Book a private charter for maximum schedule flexibility, and make sure they provide toddler-sized life jackets.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Madero

A bustling local market filled with vibrant fruit stalls, freshly squeezed juices, and casual food counters. Toddler note: It is a great, low-key spot to pick up easy toddler snacks like bananas, mangoes, and mild flour quesadillas.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa El Coromuel

A popular local beach close to town featuring calm waters, shady palapas, clean public restrooms, and a fun water slide. Toddler note: It features a long paved ramp making it the most wheelchair and stroller-accessible beach in the area.

Mid-day / 1-2h

Museo de la Ballena

An engaging, air-conditioned museum dedicated to the biology and conservation of whales and other marine mammals. Toddler note: The massive whale skeletons hanging from the ceiling are visually mesmerizing for young children.

Morning / 1-2h

Serpentario de La Paz

An outdoor animal rescue center where kids can observe turtles, lizards, birds, and regional desert reptiles up close. Toddler note: There is a hands-on domestic animal feeding area that is highly engaging for toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Jardín Velasco

This historic central plaza features a beautiful iron kiosk, towering shade trees, and a peaceful fountain. Toddler note: Grab an ice cream nearby and let your toddler run around safely in this car-free, open plaza.

Mid-day / 3-4h

Playa Pichilingue

A sheltered white-sand bay with very gentle waves and beachfront restaurants where you can dine with your toes in the sand. Toddler note: You can rent shaded lounge chairs right at the water's edge so toddlers can play in the sand while you eat.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bismark-Gito

Order the legendary battered fish tacos and fresh coconut shrimp while enjoying beach views and a dedicated kid-friendly outdoor play area.

Clara y Patria

Get the artisanal sourdough toasts, fresh pastries, and excellent cold brew coffee in their spacious, tranquil courtyard.

Mc-Fisher

Try the smoked marlin empanadas and stuffed shrimp tacos at this highly casual, open-air spot beloved by local families.

Harker Board CO.

Grab a wood-fired artisanal pizza and craft beer on the rooftop terrace while the kids enjoy simple cheese quesadillas.

La Fuente Ice Cream

Indulge in traditional Mexican ice cream flavors like pitaya (cactus fruit) or creamy vanilla from this iconic, colorful parlor on the malecon.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack water shoes for the whole family to protect against buried stingrays at shallow beaches like Balandra, remembering to always do the 'stingray shuffle' walk. Renting a car directly from the airport is highly recommended to easily haul strollers, beach gear, and toys to the spectacular bays located outside of town.
