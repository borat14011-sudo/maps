La Paz offers a sophisticated, low-key alternative to Cabo, where the desert dramatically meets the rich, turquoise waters of the Sea of Cortez. This eco-luxury paradise thrives on a slower pace, making it an incredibly restorative sanctuary for a young family with a baby in tow. The crown jewel for infant travelers is the five-kilometer paved Malecón boardwalk, perfect for sunset stroller walks while gentle ocean breezes keep baby Mary Claire perfectly cool. Here, luxury is defined by private beach clubs, world-class marine life, and tranquil, wave-free coastal lagoons.
🚕 Getting there: San José del Cabo International Airport (SJD) to the La Paz hotel strip takes exactly 2 hours and 15 minutes via a scenic, fully paved highway. The safest and most comfortable option is a pre-arranged private luxury SUV transfer, such as a Chevrolet Suburban with a pre-installed, sanitized rear-facing Britax infant car seat, offering plenty of luggage space for your stroller and baby gear.

What to do

Early morning 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Playa Balandra

This world-famous shallow lagoon features crystal-clear, knee-deep water with virtually no waves, creating a massive natural baby pool where Mary Claire can safely splash. Arrive early to secure a shaded palapa, and bring a premium pop-up beach tent for extra UV protection.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Malecón de La Paz

A beautifully paved five-kilometer seaside promenade lined with whimsical bronze sculptures and stunning ocean vistas. It is completely stroller-friendly, flat, and ideal for a breezy morning walk before the midday heat sets in.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Playa El Caimancito

Known for its incredibly calm, emerald waters and soft white sand, this upscale beach offers a peaceful, uncrowded atmosphere. The gentle slope and lack of strong undertow make it ideal for dipping baby's toes in the Sea of Cortez.

Afternoon 1:00 PM - 2:30 PM

Marina CostaBaja

A pristine, world-class marina perfect for a scenic stroller walk to admire luxury yachts. The paved docks are wide and flat, lined with high-end boutiques and quiet cafes where you can stop for an iced latte in the shade.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Museo de Arte de Baja California Sur (MUABCS)

Step into this beautifully restored historic building to enjoy contemporary Mexican art in a quiet, air-conditioned sanctuary. The spacious galleries are fully stroller-accessible, offering a peaceful sensory break from the Baja sun.

Full morning 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM

Private Catamaran Charter to Isla Espiritu Santo

Charter a luxury private catamaran through Baja Expeditions, featuring an air-conditioned cabin where Mary Claire can comfortably nap. Cruise past dramatic red cliffs and enjoy calm anchorages where you can swim in crystal-clear water while baby rests safely.

Evening 5:30 PM - 6:30 PM

Jardín Velasco

A charming, historic plaza in the heart of downtown La Paz shaded by towering, mature trees. It is a peaceful spot to sit on a bench with an artisanal gelato while baby enjoys watching local families under the gentle canopy.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Playa El Coromuel

A popular blue-flag beach featuring excellent amenities, including paved wheelchair/stroller ramps directly onto the sand. The beach is sheltered from strong winds, offering calm swimming conditions and plenty of shaded palapas for rent.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 3:45 PM

Santuario de la Virgen de Guadalupe

A striking, architecturally unique sanctuary that offers a deeply peaceful, cool indoor atmosphere. It serves as an excellent, quiet stop during an afternoon stroll to escape the heat and admire the elegant stained glass windows.

🍽️ Where to eat

Nim

Chef Cristina Kiewek's acclaimed restaurant features a stunning, high-ceilinged covered courtyard that is naturally cool and wonderfully spacious. High chairs are readily available, and the vibrant, worldly menu includes ocean-fresh ceviche for the parents.

Sorstis

This upscale Mediterranean oasis is set in a romantic, lush open-air courtyard garden with soft, ambient music that easily masks baby noises. The staff is exceptionally welcoming to families, offering comfortable high chairs and exquisite wood-fired pizzas.

Nemi

Offering highly refined, contemporary Mexican cuisine in a stylish, minimalist setting with a quiet terrace. Booking an early seating ensures a peaceful dining experience where attentive staff cater to your spacing needs with a stroller.

Bismark-Gito

A legendary waterfront seafood institution on the Malecón, featuring a spacious, breezy second-floor terrace with spectacular sunset views. The bustling, energetic atmosphere is highly forgiving of a chatty baby, and they provide sturdy high chairs.

Oliva Al Mare

Located within the exclusive Puerta Cortés resort, this high-end Italian restaurant offers spectacular beachfront dining on a wide, flat terrace. The gentle lapping waves provide natural white noise for Mary Claire while parents enjoy handmade pastas.
