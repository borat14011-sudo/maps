Eleuthera

← back to travel board
Eleuthera captivates families with its dramatic cliffs, pristine pink-sand beaches, and a slow-paced rhythm that instantly melts away stress. Children can safely splash in waist-deep, crystal-clear shallows while parents soak in the untouched, natural beauty of this Bahamian gem. It is a playground of quiet coves, friendly locals, and endless sunshine.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-40 min drive by rental SUV from Governor's Harbour Airport (GHB) or North Eleuthera Airport (ELH) to nearby family villas

What to do

Morning / 1h

Glass Window Bridge

Witness the breathtaking contrast where the deep blue Atlantic Ocean meets the calm, turquoise Bight of Eleuthera. Toddler note: Keep little ones securely in a carrier as there are no safety railings near the cliff edges.

Friday Evening / 2-3h

Governor's Harbour Friday Night Fish Fry

Experience vibrant local culture with music, dancing, and freshly fried fish right on the harbor. Toddler note: Go early around 6:00 PM before the crowds peak and loud music starts.

Morning / 2-3h

French Leave Beach

A stunning stretch of soft, pink-hued sand with gentle waves perfect for building sandcastles. Toddler note: Bring a pop-up sun tent as shade is very limited on this expansive beach.

Low Tide Morning / 1-2h

Queen's Bath

Natural rock pools carved by the ocean that warm up in the sun like natural hot tubs. Toddler note: Only visit at low tide and wear sturdy water shoes to protect small feet from sharp limestone.

Morning / 2h

Leon Levy Native Plant Preserve

A lush national park featuring paved boardwalks through native coppice and a colorful sensory garden. Toddler note: The flat wooden walkways are completely stroller-friendly and great for energetic toddlers to walk safely.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Ten Bay Beach

A shallow, calm cove protected from wind and waves, making it feel like a giant, warm wading pool. Toddler note: You can walk out dozens of yards in knee-deep water, making it the safest beach on the island for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1h

Ocean Hole in Rock Sound

A natural limestone sinkhole filled with ocean water and teeming with friendly, hungry tropical fish. Toddler note: Bring some bread or fish food so toddlers can watch the fish swarm right at the water's edge.

Midday / 1h

Preacher's Cave

A massive, historic cave where shipwrecked Puritans found shelter in the 17th century. Toddler note: The cave floor is flat, sandy, and completely shaded, offering a cool, sun-free spot for little ones to explore.

Morning / 1h

Island Farms

A vibrant roadside nursery and market offering fresh local produce, homemade jams, and freshly baked bread. Toddler note: Kids will love looking at the colorful flowers and tasting the sweet, freshly sliced local Eleuthera pineapples.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tippy's Restaurant

Order the fresh coconut shrimp and wood-fired pizzas while sitting on the open-air deck overlooking the ocean.

The Buccaneer Club

Get the authentic Bahamian cracked conch or the family-friendly chicken fingers on the colorful, shaded outdoor patio.

Da Perk Cafe

Try the iced lattes, fresh pastries, and grilled panini sandwiches right across from the harbor.

Pascal's Oceanfront Restaurant

Enjoy a fresh grouper burger while the kids splash in the resort's swim-up pool which is open to diners.

Frigate's Bar & Grill

Order the local lobster tail or jerk chicken wings and dine right on the water's edge in Rock Sound.
