Eleuthera

← back to travel board
Eleuthera offers a slow-paced, crowd-free alternative to Nassau, defined by pristine pink-sand beaches and friendly, low-key settlements. It is a perfect escape for a young family, where you can rent a 4x4, pack a diaper bag, and find an entire cove to yourselves without competing for beach chairs. The island's gentle Caribbean-side waters act as giant natural wading pools for toddlers, while the quiet, unpretentious resorts ensure stress-free logistics.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 minute drive in a rented 4x4 SUV from Governor's Harbour Airport (GHB) directly to central hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

French Leave Beach

A stunning stretch of soft, pink sand with calm barrier-reef-protected waters perfect for a toddler's first dip. Park close to the sand and bring an all-terrain stroller or baby carrier to walk the shoreline easily.

Morning / 1-2h

Leon Levy Native Plant Preserve

A lush, national park featuring safe, well-maintained wooden boardwalks that are highly stroller-friendly. Your toddler will love looking for native birds and turtles in the freshwater wetlands while you walk under shaded canopies.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Ten Bay Beach

This Caribbean-side beach is incredibly shallow for dozens of yards out, creating a warm, wave-free wading pool ideal for infants. There is plenty of natural shade from the casuarina trees to keep baby out of the direct sun.

Morning / 1-2h

Edwin's Turtle Lake Marine Reserve

A quiet, brackish lake where you can rent a safe double-kayak to float with your toddler and spot green sea turtles swimming right next to you. The water is completely flat and glassy, ensuring zero motion sickness for the little one.

Morning / 30m

Island Farms

A fantastic roadside nursery and farm stand where you can stock up on fresh local bananas, baby-friendly avocado, and homemade pestos. The shaded garden pathways are easy to navigate while carrying a baby.

Midday / 1h

Preacher's Cave

A massive, historic open-air cave that provides deep shade and cool breezes on a hot day. The flat, sandy ground inside is perfect for a toddler to practice walking without the hazard of steep drops.

Morning / 30m

Glass Window Bridge

The narrowest point of the island where you can see the stark contrast between the deep blue Atlantic and calm turquoise Bight of Eleuthera. Park safely in the pull-off and view this dramatic sight right from the safety of your vehicle or carrier.

Morning / 1h

Queen's Bath

Natural tide pools carved into the rock that fill with warm, sun-heated ocean water during low tide. While the walk down is rocky, you can safely hold your toddler in a carrier to peer down at the tiny fish swimming in these natural warm tubs.

Evening / 1-2h

Governor's Harbour Fish Fry

A vibrant, outdoor Friday-night tradition right on the waterfront. Arrive early at 6:00 PM to beat the crowds, grab some fresh grilled fish, and let your toddler dance to the steel drum music before bedtime.

🍽️ Where to eat

1648 An Island Restaurant

Order the stone-fired pizzas and fresh grouper while dining on a spacious, shaded deck overlooking the harbor. The calm waterside setting is perfect for keeping a toddler entertained, and they open early at 7:00 AM for early-rising families.

Tippy's Restaurant

Famous for its open-air beach gazebo, go early at 5:00 PM for coconut shrimp and local lobster flatbread. The constant, noisy rush of the Atlantic surf easily drowns out any toddler fussing.

Buccaneer Club Restaurant

A highly casual Bahamian-Chinese spot with a large, fenced-in grassy outdoor seating area. Perfect for letting your toddler crawl or toddle around while you enjoy cracked conch and a cold Sands beer.

Pascals Oceanfront Seafood Restaurant

Located at Sky Beach Club, this spot features a swim-up pool bar next to the dining tables. You can enjoy a casual lunch of mahi-mahi tacos while your wife dips with the baby in the adjacent pool.

The Cove Restaurant

An upscale, serene dining room offering fresh sushi and hand-crafted cocktails. The staff is incredibly accommodating to families with high chairs and early dinner bookings starting at 5:30 PM.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

French Leave Resort

Spacious, luxury villas featuring fully equipped kitchens which are ideal for storing milk and prepping baby food. The resort provides high-end cribs, and the calm, harbor-front pool deck is incredibly peaceful for family naps.

The Cove Eleuthera

Tucked between two private white-sand coves with virtually no waves, making beach play entirely stress-free. They offer complimentary cribs, spacious suites, and a gorgeous zero-entry pool perfect for dipping an infant.

Cape Eleuthera Resort & Marina

Two-bedroom villas with full kitchens and private washers and dryers, which is a massive lifesaver for baby laundry. The marina village is quiet, paved, and flat—ideal for morning stroller walks.
