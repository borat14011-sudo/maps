Montreal wraps families in a cozy embrace of cobblestone streets, lush parks, and sweet maple treats. It is a bilingual playground where historic European flair blends seamlessly with cutting-edge, hands-on science and nature spots. From its sprawling botanical gardens to bustling food markets, the city offers an effortless, stroller-friendly escape for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Uber ride to Downtown hotels, or the direct 747 Express bus.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Montreal Science Centre

This interactive museum in the Old Port features hands-on science exhibits and an IMAX theater. Toddlers will love 'Mini Mondo', an indoor interactive playground designed specifically for early learners.

Morning / 2-3h

The Biodome

Walk through five replica ecosystems of the Americas under one massive roof, complete with free-roaming animals. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the playful penguins and the tropical rainforest monkeys.

Afternoon / 2h

Mount Royal Park

Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted, this sprawling green oasis offers gorgeous city views and paved stroller paths. Feed the ducks at Beaver Lake, where toddlers can safely run and play on the grass.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Jean-Talon Market

One of North America's largest open-air markets, filled with vibrant fruit stalls, bakeries, and cheese shops. The wide, flat lanes are easy to navigate with a stroller while snacking on fresh berries.

Sunset / 1h

La Grande Roue de Montréal

Take a ride on Canada's tallest observation wheel for breathtaking, panoramic views of the St. Lawrence River. The climate-controlled, enclosed cabins are safe, spacious, and perfect for toddlers.

Afternoon / 3h

Montreal Botanical Garden

Explore beautifully themed outdoor gardens and walk among free-flying butterflies in the insectarium. The vast, shady lawns are ideal for quiet strolls and midday toddler naps in the stroller.

Morning / 2h

Pointe-à-Callière Museum

An incredible archaeology museum built over real ruins with a dedicated interactive exhibition for kids. Toddlers will love playing on the simulated pirate ship in the 'Come Aboard! Pirates or Privateers?' exhibit.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Voiles en Voiles

An outdoor adventure park in the Old Port designed like giant pirate ships with aerial ropes courses. For the littlest adventurers, there are ground-level inflatable castles and safe toddler play zones.

Morning / 2h

Atwater Market and Lachine Canal

Pick up fresh pastries and local cheese from this beautiful art-deco market, then picnic along the historic canal. The flat, grassy banks are perfect for letting toddlers stretch their legs while watching passing boats.

🍽️ Where to eat

Beauty's Luncheonette

Order the famous Beauty's Special bagel with smoked salmon, cream cheese, tomato, and onion in a retro, kid-friendly 1940s diner.

La Banquise

Try the classic, kid-approved traditional poutine with squeaky cheese curds and warm gravy at this legendary, ultra-welcoming 24-hour spot.

Schwartz's Deli

Grab a world-famous, melt-in-your-mouth medium smoked meat sandwich on rye with a side of cherry soda to share with the family.

Kem Coba

Treat the family to their signature, colorful soft-serve twist made with fresh, rotating seasonal flavors like passionfruit and coconut.

St-Viateur Bagel

Buy a dozen hot, wood-fired sesame bagels straight from the oven for a quick, delicious, and toddler-approved hand-held snack.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a sturdy stroller with good suspension to easily navigate the historic cobblestones of Old Montreal. Because many metro stations lack elevators, using a baby carrier for public transit or opting for short taxi rides is highly recommended.
