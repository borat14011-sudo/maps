Lake Garda (Sirmione)

← back to travel board
Sirmione offers a breathtaking blend of Roman history, thermal waters, and sparkling lake views that feel surprisingly manageable with an infant. The pedestrianized old town features smooth paved paths perfect for a high-end stroller, while the gentle lake breezes provide natural cooling. Families will love the flat, shaded lakefront promenades and calm, shallow waters that are incredibly safe for an infant's first splash. With abundant pharmacies stocking premium European formula and easily accessible pediatric care, it is a stress-free Italian escape.
🚕 Getting there: Fly into Verona Villafranca Airport (VRN), which is a 35-minute drive (approx. 35 km) to Sirmione. The best option is a pre-arranged private Mercedes V-Class transfer with a certified infant car seat booked via InAUTO Garda, ensuring plenty of room for your stroller and luggage without the hassle of rental car lines.

What to do

Early morning at 8:30 AM to beat the heat / 2h

Grotte di Catullo

Explore the ruins of a massive Roman villa; the main pathways are wide and stroller-accessible, with beautiful olive groves providing ample shade for Mary Claire's nap.

9:30 AM before the midday sun / 2h

Lido delle Bionde

A beautiful, clean beach with calm, shallow waters perfect for dipping baby's toes, featuring a paved access path and sun lounger rentals with umbrellas for shade.

Late afternoon around 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Parco San Vito

A serene public park located just outside the castle walls, offering flat paved walking paths, grassy shaded areas under mature trees, and a quiet escape from the crowds.

Mid-morning / 2h roundtrip

Navigazione Laghi Ferry Ride

Take a smooth, low-vibration ferry ride to nearby Lazise; the boats are stroller-accessible with indoor air-conditioned cabins to keep Mary Claire cool.

Sunset stroll around 6:00 PM / 1h

Lungolago di Sirmione

A completely flat, paved, and pedestrian-only lakeside promenade running from Colombare to the castle, offering stunning water views and zero stairs for your stroller.

Friday morning at 8:00 AM / 1.5h

Mercato Settimanale del Venerdi

Stroll through this lively local market in Colombare featuring flat, paved streets; perfect for picking up fresh local fruit, cheeses, and soft cotton baby clothes.

Early afternoon for quiet nap time / 1h

Parco Maria Callas

A beautifully manicured, quiet park dedicated to the opera singer, featuring flat asphalt paths, lush green lawns, and massive shade trees ideal for a peaceful stroller nap.

Late afternoon at 3:30 PM / 2h

Spiaggia Brema

A spacious, flat grassy beach in Colombare with plenty of natural shade from weeping willows and incredibly calm, shallow water ideal for a relaxed family afternoon.

11:00 AM for an early lunch or gelato / 1h

Piazza Carducci

Sirmione's vibrant, flat main square facing the lake, perfect for strolling with the stroller, grabbing a shaded outdoor table, and letting Mary Claire watch the boats.
