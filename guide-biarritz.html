Biarritz

← back to travel board
Biarritz blends rugged Basque coast with Belle Époque elegance, offering a serene escape for young families. The stroller-friendly Promenade de la Plage arcs past the iconic Rocher de la Vierge, where calm tide pools invite gentle splashing. Hotel du Palais provides direct beach access and a heated seawater pool perfect for infant floats. The gentle waves and sandy shores of Côte des Basques create a natural paddling zone for baby Mary Claire.
🚕 Getting there: From Biarritz Pays Basque Airport, a private chauffeur transfer takes 10 minutes; request an infant seat and stroller-friendly vehicle (e.g., a Mercedes V-Class).

What to do

Late morning 10-11am / 1h

Rocher de la Vierge

See the iconic rock statue and sea arch; stroller-accessible boardwalk with shade from the casino building. Ear protection recommended if windy.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Grande Plage

Wide sandy beach with calm shallow areas; rent a beach umbrella and let baby crawl on soft sand. Lifeguarded in season.

Late afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Musée de la Mer

Aquarium with touch pools and seal feeding; stroller-accessible, quiet corners for nursing. Skip the 3D theater if baby is sensitive to loud sounds.

Early evening 5-6pm / 1h

Côte des Basques

Famous surf beach with gentler waves at the southern end; perfect for baby’s first sandy toes. Walk the promenade above for shade and sea breeze.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Phare de Biarritz

Climb the lighthouse for panoramic views (skip if baby naps) or enjoy the grassy park at its base – great for a picnic blanket. Stroller-accessible paths.

Midday 11:30am-12pm / 30min

Chapelle Impériale

Intricate neo-Byzantine chapel with quiet, cool interior; stroller-friendly entrance. Short visit (20 min) before baby fusses.

Late afternoon 4-5pm / 1h

Villa Belza

Historic cliffside castle with sea views; outdoor terrace for coffee while baby watches waves. No interior access, but the walkway is stroller-ready.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Port des Pêcheurs

Charming fishing port with colourful boats; flat cobblestone (stroller okay with good wheels), fish market smells may be strong – keep baby upwind.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Plage Miramar

Sheltered cove near the Sofitel hotel; calm, shallow water ideal for infant splashing. Access via stairs (stroller can be parked above).

🍽️ Where to eat

Café de Paris

Grande Plage terrace with ocean views; serves local Basque pintxos and fresh seafood. Highchairs available, early seating 6:30pm is quiet.

Le Surfing

Laid-back spot on Côte des Basques with terrace; specializes in grilled fish and salads. Baby-friendly, highchairs, and calm early dinners.

L'Impertinent

Michelin-recommended Basque cuisine in the city center; offers a children’s menu and accepts babies (call ahead for highchair). Quiet ambiance after 8pm.

Chez Albert

Rustic bistro near the port known for pipérade and duck confit. Outdoor tables in a pedestrian street; stroller park next to table.

Le Bistro du Royalty

Elegant brasserie inside Hôtel du Palais; terrace with sea view. Perfect for afternoon tea; they provide a baby changing table in the restroom.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hôtel du Palais

$200-400/night

5-star former imperial palace on Grande Plage. Heated seawater pool, family suites with blackout curtains, cribs for under 2s, and direct beach access.

Sofitel Biarritz Le Miramar

$200-400/night

Contemporary luxury on the cliffside with indoor/outdoor pools and a kid’s club (0-3 years). Family suites with kitchenettes, cribs, and blackout blinds.

Château de Brindos

$200-400/night

Relais & Châteaux property on a private lake just outside Biarritz. Heated outdoor pool, spacious suites, and complimentary cribs; serene gardens for baby’s nap.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pharmacies (e.g., Pharmacie du Palais) carry formula, baby sunscreen and nappies – stock up near the market. The Promenade de la Plage and boardwalks are smooth for strollers, but the old town (rue du Port Vieux) has cobblestones; use a jogger or baby carrier. For medical care, Centre Hospitalier de la Côte Basque (Anglet) has a pediatric ER; manage heat with a portable fan and shade between 12-4pm.
