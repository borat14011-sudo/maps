What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Rocher de la Vierge

Walk across the historic metal footbridge designed by Gustave Eiffel to reach this iconic rock formation. The paved path is completely stroller-friendly, and the morning sea breeze provides a refreshing environment for Mary Claire's morning nap.

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Plage du Port Vieux

This horseshoe-shaped cove is sheltered from the Atlantic's strong waves and winds, making it the safest beach in Biarritz for baby Mary Claire to dip her toes in the calm water. It is highly accessible via a gentle ramp.

Morning 8-10am / 2h

Les Halles de Biarritz

Navigate the vibrant covered market early in the morning before the crowds arrive. The wide aisles easily accommodate a stroller, allowing Will and Kathryn to sample local Basque cheeses and gateau Basque while Mary Claire is entertained by the colorful displays.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Aquarium de Biarritz

This Art Deco aquarium features a spectacular seal habitat and creative fish displays. The indoor, darkened galleries are fully wheelchair and stroller-accessible, offering a cool, quiet sensory experience that is perfect for escaping the afternoon heat with an infant.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Phare de Biarritz

While the climb up the 248 steps of the lighthouse is not suitable with an infant, the surrounding manicured grassy park is completely flat and stroller-accessible, making it an ideal spot for Will and Kathryn to enjoy panoramic coastal views while Mary Claire crawls safely on the grass.

Sunset 6-7:30pm / 1.5h

Grande Plage Promenade

The wide, paved seaside boardwalk of the main beach is entirely barrier-free and perfect for a smooth stroll. It offers incredible views of the ocean and the historic Hôtel du Palais, ideal for an easy walk while Mary Claire relaxes in her stroller.

Mid-day 12-2pm / 2h

Lac de Marion

Escape the coastal winds at this serene freshwater lake located just inland. The flat, shaded dirt path winding around the lake is perfect for a peaceful stroller walk under the oak trees, offering a quiet environment for Mary Claire's afternoon sleep.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Cité de l'Océan

This striking modern museum designed by Steven Holl features spacious, climate-controlled interiors that are easy to navigate with a stroller. The vast outdoor lawns surrounding the building offer a safe, clean open space for Mary Claire to play and crawl.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Plage de la Milady

Located on the southern edge of Biarritz, this beach features a flat coastal promenade and a well-maintained children's playground right by the sand. It is a fantastic, less-crowded spot to watch local surfers while Mary Claire enjoys the baby-friendly swings.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Gamaritz

This upscale bistronomy spot serves creative Basque tapas and main dishes near Port Vieux. Their outdoor terrace is lively and welcoming to families, making it easy to enjoy a meal at the early 7:00 PM seating without worrying about occasional baby noises.

Chez Albert

Set in the picturesque, pedestrian-only Port des Pêcheurs, this legendary seafood restaurant specializes in wild-caught local fish grilled to perfection. The spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates strollers and offers highchairs on request.

Le Miremont

A historic tearoom dating back to 1872, offering exquisite French pastries and light lunch options overlooking the ocean. They provide highchairs, and the stroller-accessible lower level allows you to dine elegantly with Mary Claire by your side.

L'Impertinent

This highly acclaimed restaurant offers a sophisticated but warmly welcoming Basque-influenced dining experience. By booking the earliest dinner slot, Will and Kathryn can enjoy world-class cuisine while Mary Claire sleeps soundly in her stroller at a quiet corner table.

Café de la Poste

A classic and lively local brasserie with a spacious, sun-drenched outdoor terrace on Rue Helianthe. The casual, high-energy atmosphere is perfect for a relaxed family lunch where baby laughter fits right in, and highchairs are readily available.
