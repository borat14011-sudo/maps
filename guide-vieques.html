Vieques

← back to travel board
Vieques is a pristine island sanctuary where wild horses roam free along turquoise shores and golden sands. Unhurried and naturally beautiful, it offers families a safe, laid-back escape far from the mega-resorts. It is a place where toddlers can splash in gentle, shallow waters while parents soak in the Caribbean sun.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or rental Jeep drive from Antonio Rivera Rodríguez Airport (VQS) to the main towns of Isabel Segunda or Esperanza

What to do

Evening / 2h

Mosquito Bay Bioluminescent Bay

Experience the brightest bioluminescent bay in the world on a family-friendly electric catamaran tour. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glowing water that lights up like magic at the touch of a hand.

Morning / 3-4h

Sun Bay Beach

This sweeping, crescent-shaped beach features calm waters, plenty of shade under palm trees, and public restrooms. It is the most accessible beach on the island, making it incredibly easy for families with strollers and gear.

Morning / 2h

Playa Negra (Black Sand Beach)

Take a short, flat 15-minute nature walk through a wooded trail to discover a stunning beach with dramatic black volcanic sand. Toddlers will love playing in the dark, magnetic sand and spotting tiny hermit crabs along the path.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Fuerte de Vieques (Count Mirasol Fort)

Explore the last Spanish fort built in the Americas, offering manicured grassy lawns for kids to run around and beautiful ocean views. The small indoor museum has cool, air-conditioned historical exhibits that are quick and easy for short attention spans.

Morning / 3h

Playa Caracas (Red Beach)

Located in the National Wildlife Refuge, this beach offers bright turquoise water, soft white sand, and handy picnic gazebos. The gentle, rolling waves are perfect for toddlers to splash safely under parent supervision.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Esperanza Malecon

Stroll down the flat, paved beachside boardwalk filled with ocean breezes, friendly local dogs, and passing wild horses. It is the perfect spot for an easy stroller walk, followed by an afternoon ice cream cone.

Morning / 3h

Playa La Chiva (Blue Beach)

A long stretch of beach with shallow, crystal-clear water and reef areas close to the shore. The ultra-calm tide pools near the first parking spots are ideal for toddlers to sit and play safely in the water.

Afternoon / 1h

Vieques Conservation and Historical Trust

This small, engaging museum in Esperanza features a touch tank with live sea stars and crabs, plus educational displays about the bio bay. It is a fantastic, air-conditioned educational detour that toddlers will find highly tactile.

Morning / 1h

Plaza Muñoz Rivera

The central town square of Isabel Segunda is a great place to buy fresh fruit from local street vendors and watch the fountains. Toddlers will enjoy chasing pigeons while parents admire the colorful, historic colonial architecture.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Quenepo

Get the fresh local mofongo or the pan-seared snapper while enjoying an upscale yet incredibly welcoming open-air beachside atmosphere.

Duffy's Esperanza

Get the famous mahi-mahi fish tacos and a side of sweet plantains at this ultra-casual, kid-friendly open-air spot right on the boardwalk.

Bananas

Get the coconut shrimp and a fresh fruit smoothie for the kids at this lively beachside bar and grill.

Coqui Fire Cafe

Get the delicious chicken quesadillas for the kids and try their famous house-made hot sauces on your own tacos.

Rising Roost

Get the fluffy coconut French toast and fresh squeezed juices at this charming, relaxed breakfast patio in Isabel II.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a 4x4 Jeep well in advance, as many of the best beaches in the wildlife refuge require navigating unpaved, bumpy dirt roads. Pack plenty of kid-friendly, reef-safe sunscreen and heavy-duty bug spray, as sand flies (no-see-ums) can become active at dusk.
