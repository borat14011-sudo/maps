Nice

Nice captures the timeless glamour of the French Riviera while remaining remarkably welcoming to upscale families traveling with infants. The iconic Promenade des Anglais offers miles of completely flat, stroller-ready paved pathways directly overlooking the sparkling Baie des Anges. Parents can sip chilled local rosé under the shade of premium beach club umbrellas while baby Mary Claire naps to the gentle sounds of the Mediterranean. It is a destination where high-end French living seamlessly accommodates the schedules and needs of a young family.
🚕 Getting there: A 15-minute private Mercedes V-Class executive van transfer from Nice Côte d'Azur Airport to the Promenade hotel strip, pre-arranged with a premium rear-facing infant car seat and maximum trunk space for a luxury stroller.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Promenade des Anglais

This legendary seaside boulevard is completely flat, wide, and perfectly paved for a smooth stroller ride while Will and Kathryn enjoy the sea breeze. Mary Claire can doze in the shade of the stroller canopy during a morning walk.

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Castle Hill (Colline du Château)

Skip the stairs and take the spacious Art Deco elevator (Ascenseur du Château) to the top for panoramic views of the bay. The shady park at the summit features cool mist from the Henri Janssen waterfall and quiet paths for a peaceful nap.

Early morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Cours Saleya Flower Market

A sensory delight filled with vibrant blooms, fresh lavender, and local produce. Visited early in the morning, the flat market lanes are spacious enough for a stroller before the heavy crowds arrive.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Jardin Albert Ier

One of Nice's oldest public parks, offering lush lawns, shady pine trees, and a beautiful traditional carousel. It provides a peaceful, grassy oasis just steps from the beach for Mary Claire to enjoy some supervised tummy time.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Musée Matisse

Housed in a stunning 17th-century villa in the quiet Cimiez district, this museum is fully stroller-accessible via elevators and ramps. The quiet, air-conditioned galleries offer a serene art-viewing experience perfect for a sleeping baby.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Monastère de Cimiez Gardens

Located next to the ancient monastery, this serene Italianate garden features manicured rose beds, ancient olive groves, and quiet shaded benches. It is the ultimate tranquil escape from the bustling city center for an afternoon rest.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Port Lympia

Stroll past the colorful Venetian-style buildings and luxury yachts bobbing in Nice's historic harbor. The wide docks are flat and stroller-friendly, allowing for a relaxing walk with plenty of maritime visual stimulation for baby.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Parc Phoenix

A massive botanical garden and zoo near the western end of the city featuring flat paths and a giant tropical greenhouse. The gentle ambient sounds of exotic birds and flowing water make for an incredibly calming environment for infants.

Mid-day 11am-3pm / 4h

Castel Plage Private Beach

Rent a luxurious double daybed with an oversized umbrella at this upscale private beach club. Mary Claire can stay cool and nap under the heavy shade while parents enjoy premium beachside service and calm water access.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Rotonde

Located inside the iconic Le Negresco, this whimsical carousel-themed brassiere offers 5-star service in a vibrant, upscale setting. The cheerful decor is visually stimulating for babies, and the staff is exceptionally welcoming to families with early evening seatings.

Le Safari

An upscale institution right on Cours Saleya featuring a spacious outdoor terrace. The lively atmosphere easily absorbs any baby noise, and the staff gladly accommodates strollers at the table for an early lunch of Niçoise specialties.

Le Galet

A chic beachfront restaurant on the Promenade des Anglais where you can dine with your feet almost in the pebbles. The outdoor deck is highly stroller-accessible, and the soothing sound of the waves makes for a relaxing dining experience for parents and baby.

La Petite Maison

Famous for its high-end Niçoise cuisine and upscale clientele, this spot features a breezy outdoor patio. The bustling, musical environment is perfect for hiding occasional infant squeals while enjoying signature truffle pasta.

Chez Acchiardo

A beloved, family-run traditional restaurant in Vieux Nice that welcomes children like royalty. Opening early for dinner at 7:00 PM, it is ideal for getting baby fed and settled while parents savor rich, authentic Daube Provençale.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Boscolo Nice Hotel & Spa

$200-400/night

A grand 5-star Autograph Collection hotel with a serene, modern white-marble design. Offers spacious family suites, complimentary high-quality wooden cribs on request, a heated rooftop swimming pool, and excellent blackout curtains for daytime naps.

Hotel La Pérouse Nice

$200-400/night

A secluded boutique luxury hotel carved into the cliffs of Castle Hill, offering a quieter escape from the city. Features a heated outdoor pool surrounded by lemon trees, spacious family rooms, and direct elevator access to the sea level.

Hotel West End Promenade

$200-400/night

A historic 4-star boutique luxury hotel situated directly on the stroller-friendly Promenade des Anglais. It offers classic French elegance, spacious junior suites, prompt crib setup, and unbeatable proximity to private beach clubs with infant amenities.
