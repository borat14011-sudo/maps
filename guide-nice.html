What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Promenade des Anglais

Walk, cycle, or scooter along this world-famous seaside avenue with stunning views of the Baie des Anges. It is entirely flat and paved, making it perfect for smooth stroller rides.

Morning / 2h

Castle Hill (Colline du Château)

Climb up for panoramic views of the orange tiled roofs, a rushing artificial waterfall, and a massive shaded playground. Skip the steps and take the free historic elevator to keep toddler meltdowns at bay.

Morning / 1h

Cours Saleya Flower Market

Immerse your senses in a sea of colorful blooms, fresh local berries, and spices in the heart of the Old Town. Toddlers will love snacking on fresh strawberries while exploring the bustling pedestrian stalls.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Promenade du Paillon (Le Miroir d'Eau)

This lush urban park features a massive water mirror splash pad with dancing fountains and incredible wooden animal climbing structures. Bring a change of dry clothes as toddlers will immediately want to run through the water.

Morning / 2-3h

Parc Phoenix

A massive botanical garden and zoo featuring exotic birds, wallabies, lemurs, and a giant tropical greenhouse. The flat, paved paths make it incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller.

Morning / 1-2h

Plage Publique de l'Opéra

Experience Nice's iconic pebble beaches by throwing smooth stones into the crystal-clear turquoise sea. Be sure to pack thick water shoes so tiny toddler feet can walk comfortably on the rocks.

Midday / 45m

Le Petit Train de Nice

Hop aboard this small electric tourist train for a effortless, breezy ride through the Old Town up to Castle Hill. It is an ideal, low-energy way for toddlers to see the sights when little legs are tired.

Afternoon / 1h

Villa Masséna Gardens

Relax in the peaceful, palm-shaded public gardens surrounding this majestic Belle Époque villa. It is a quiet oasis away from the crowds, perfect for a peaceful stroller nap.

Afternoon / 1h

Confiserie Florian

Take a short, sweet-scented tour of this historic confectionery to see how candied fruits and chocolates are made. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant copper cauldrons and love tasting the sweet jams.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez René Socca

Order a plate of hot, crispy chickpea pancakes (socca) directly from the outdoor counter for a quick, kid-friendly finger food snack.

Fenocchio

Savor a scoop of artisanal lavender, orange blossom, or simple chocolate gelato at this legendary ice cream parlor in the bustling Place Rossetti.

Le Safari

Sit outdoor in the lively Cours Saleya and enjoy an authentic, fresh Salade Niçoise alongside family-friendly, wood-fired pizzas.

Chez Pipo

Try their famous, traditional pan bagnat sandwich in a cozy, welcoming neighborhood spot just off the port.

La Voglia

Feast on exceptionally generous, family-sized portions of fresh Italian pasta and thin-crust pizzas right near the flower market.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a sturdy stroller with good suspension to comfortably navigate the historic, uneven cobblestones of Vieux Nice. Since many traditional restaurants do not open for dinner until 7:00 PM or 7:30 PM, keep toddler schedules on track by packing afternoon snacks or opting for casual outdoor pizzerias.
