St. Kitts (Basseterre)

← back to travel board
St. Kitts blends rich Caribbean history with lush, volcanic landscapes perfect for family exploration. From the gentle chug of the sugar train to the calm, turquoise waters of south-peninsula beaches, this island offers a relaxed pace that keeps both parents and little ones enchanted. Basseterre’s colorful streets and welcoming culture make it an effortless tropical escape for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Robert L. Bradshaw International Airport (SKB) to Basseterre, or ~25 min to Frigate Bay resort area.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Brimstone Hill Fortress National Park

Explore this massive UNESCO-listed fort featuring panoramic ocean views and grand stone walls. Toddlers will need close supervision on the steep ramparts, but the wide-open grassy lawns are perfect for running around.

Afternoon / 3h

St. Kitts Scenic Railway

Ride the historic double-decker train through lush sugarcane fields and over tall canyon bridges. It is a highly entertaining, sit-down journey that is perfect for toddlers who need a break from walking.

Morning / 3-4h

South Friar's Bay (Carambola Beach)

A calm, sheltered beach with golden sand and crystal-clear water ideal for safe swimming. Toddlers can play safely in the gentle shallows while parents relax on shaded loungers.

Afternoon / 1h

The Independence Square

A vibrant, historic public square in the center of Basseterre featuring a beautiful central fountain and shady paths. It is an easy, stroller-friendly spot to let toddlers stretch their legs while admiring the colonial architecture.

Morning / 1-2h

Clay Villa Plantation House & Gardens

Walk through beautifully landscaped historic gardens filled with colorful tropical plants and rescue animals. Toddlers will love seeing the resident monkeys and birds in this peaceful, shady sanctuary.

Morning / 1h

Basseterre Public Market

Immerse your family in local culture at this bustling market packed with bright tropical fruits and friendly local vendors. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vibrant sights, especially the sweet, fresh bananas.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Cockleshell Bay

Located at the southern tip of the island, this beach offers stunning views of neighboring Nevis and incredibly calm, shallow waters. Toddlers can spot wild monkeys and goats wandering near the beach shacks.

Morning / 1-2h

Romney Manor and Caribelle Batik

Wander through the gorgeous botanical gardens of a 17th-century estate centered around a massive, 350-year-old Saman tree. Toddlers will love the wide-open lawns, and parents can watch live demonstrations of vibrant batik wax-dyeing.

Morning / 30m

The Circus and Berkeley Memorial

Stand at the heart of Basseterre to see the iconic green, ornate clock tower modeled after London's Piccadilly Circus. It's a quick, central landmark stop where toddlers can point at the clock and watch the gentle flow of town life.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Fredo's Restaurant

Order the traditional mutton stew or local saltfish served with seasoned rice and sweet plantains.

Sprat Net Bar & Grill

Get the freshly caught grilled lobster or BBQ ribs served on rustic picnic tables right by the water.

The Shipwreck Beach Bar

Try the famous conch fritters while the kids watch the wild green monkeys and mongooses that visit the tables.

Carambola Beach Club

Enjoy upscale beachfront dining with a menu of fresh sushi rolls and Caribbean-style mahi-mahi.

Arthur's Restaurant & Bar

Feast on crispy coconut shrimp and fish tacos at this breezy, kid-friendly spot right on the black sands of Dieppe Bay.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat for maximum flexibility, as many island taxis do not have them readily available. Keep plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and bug spray on hand, especially when exploring shady plantation gardens and breezy beaches.
