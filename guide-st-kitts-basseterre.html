St. Kitts (Basseterre)

← back to travel board
Welcome to an island where lush emerald peaks meet the azure Caribbean Sea, offering a playground of historic wonders and soft-sand beaches. St. Kitts blends rich cultural heritage with laid-back island vibes, making it an effortless tropical escape for families. From the chugging scenic railway to calm, shallow bays, every corner holds a new adventure for parents and little explorers alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Robert L. Bradshaw International Airport (SKB) to Basseterre and Frigate Bay resorts

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Brimstone Hill Fortress National Park

Explore this massive UNESCO World Heritage fortress with panoramic ocean views and sprawling grassy lawns where kids can spot wild green monkeys. Toddler note: Bring a baby carrier as the stone ramparts are steep and not stroller-friendly.

Afternoon / 3h

St. Kitts Scenic Railway

Ride the historic open-air double-decker train through lush sugarcane fields and over tall bridges with island music playing along the way. Toddler note: The rhythmic movement and cooling breeze are great for a midday nap.

Morning / 3-4h

South Friars Bay Beach

A sheltered beach with exceptionally calm, warm waters and soft sand that is perfect for sandcastle building and safe splashing. Toddler note: Look for gentle green monkeys playing in the trees near the beach bars.

Morning / 1-2h

Clay Villa Plantation House & Gardens

Wander through beautiful, historic ecological gardens filled with colorful tropical flowers, ancient trees, and rescued animals like tortoises. Toddler note: The flat, shaded paths are highly manageable for tiny, wandering feet.

Morning / 1h

The Circus and Berkeley Memorial

Stroll through the heart of Basseterre to admire the Victorian-style green clock tower and lively round-about. Toddler note: Grab a sweet treat from a nearby bakery to enjoy while sitting on the steps watching the colorful city bustle.

Morning / 1h

Basseterre Public Market

Immerse your senses in this bustling, vibrant market filled with piles of colorful mangoes, bananas, and local root vegetables. Toddler note: Go early to beat the heat, and let your toddler pick out a sweet, familiar fruit like a banana to snack on.

Morning / 1-2h

Romney Manor and Wingfield Estate ruins

Discover stunning botanical gardens, a massive 350-year-old Saman tree, and the crumbling stone ruins of an old sugar estate. Toddler note: The wide grassy lawns are perfect for letting little ones run free under the shade of giant trees.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Frigate Bay Beach Strip

A lively stretch of beach lined with casual open-air shacks, offering shallow waters on the Caribbean side and plenty of space to play. Toddler note: Early evening is ideal for a gentle beach walk and an early casual dinner with your toes in the sand.

Afternoon / 1h

Black Rocks

Witness the dramatic, black volcanic rock formations shaped by the ocean waves on the island's northern coast. Toddler note: Keep toddlers securely in a carrier or hold hands tightly, as the viewing paths are near breezy cliffs.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Fredo's Restaurant

Order the classic steamed fish with fungee or the mutton stew for a comforting, authentic taste of Kittitian home cooking.

The Shipwreck Tavern

Try the famous mahi-mahi tacos while the kids watch the wild green monkeys and mongooses that visit the surrounding trees.

Spice Mill Restaurant

Feast on the fresh lobster pizza or passionfruit glazed pork chops right on the tranquil shores of Cockleshell Bay.

Carambola Beach Club

Savor the fresh sushi rolls and coconut shrimp while relaxing on premium lounge chairs on a calm beach.

Sprat Net

Grab a rustic wooden picnic table and dig into freshly caught grilled snapper or lobster served straight from the open coals.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a high-quality baby carrier as many historic sites and natural attractions feature uneven cobblestones, steep stairs, or grassy terrain unsuitable for strollers. Always carry cash, as US dollars are widely accepted alongside Eastern Caribbean dollars for quick purchases at local fruit markets and beachside shacks.
