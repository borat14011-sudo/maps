Corfu blends Venetian elegance with Ionian serenity, offering a refined escape for young families. Stroll the Liston promenade with your stroller beneath arcades, then let baby Mary Claire splash in the calm, shallow waters at Agios Gordios beach. The island's lush hills and olive groves provide a tranquil backdrop, while luxury resorts offer private pools and cribs for fuss-free naps. With its gentle pace and child-friendly amenities, Corfu is a Mediterranean haven for your first family vacation.
🚕 Getting there: Corfu airport to main luxury hotels is a swift 15-40 minute private transfer. Book a car with infant seat and ample trunk for a stroller — services like Corfu Transfers or your hotel’s chauffeur offer air-conditioned comfort for a stress-free arrival.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am

Achilleion Palace

Opulent palace built for Empress Elisabeth with stunning gardens. Stroller-accessible paths and shaded terraces make it ideal for a leisurely morning. The peaceful statues and sea views captivate adults, while babies enjoy the open lawns.

Late morning 10am-12:30pm

Corfu Old Town & Spianada

Explore the UNESCO-listed Old Town with its wide Spianada square — perfect for stroller walks. Little ones love watching cricket on the green or feeding pigeons. The Liston arcades offer shade and cafes for a break.

Morning 9-11:30am

Paleokastritsa Monastery & Beach

Perched on a cliff, the monastery offers panoramic views and a serene courtyard (stroller-friendly). Below, the calm, clear bay with shallow edges is ideal for baby's first paddle. Arrive early for parking and shade.

Early morning 8:30-10:30am

Canal d'Amour (Sidari)

Famous sandstone channels with shallow, warm lagoons — safe for gentle splashing. Arrive early to avoid crowds. The beach is sandy, and there are caves to explore (skip if too loud later). Bring a pop-up tent for shade.

Midday 11am-12:30pm

Corfu Aquarium

Compact indoor aquarium with touch tanks and colorful fish — a hit for babies fascinated by movement. Stroller-friendly, air-conditioned, and quiet in the morning. Perfect break from the sun.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm

Vlacherna Monastery & Mouse Island

Iconic monastery on a tiny island, reached by a short causeway (stroller-accessible). The views over Corfu town and sea are breathtaking. Babies enjoy the gentle breeze and boat-spotting. Best at sunset but busy; morning is serene.

Early morning 9-10:30am

Kassiopi Castle

Ruined Byzantine castle with easy uphill path (stroller possible but bumpy). The vantage point offers sweeping coastline views. Shady trees at base for picnic. Skip if too hot; early morning is cool.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Mount Pantokrator (drive-up)

Drive to the highest point of Corfu for panoramic views. The summit has a small chapel and café with seating (stroller-friendly). Fresh mountain air is a relief from coastal heat. Baby can nap in car.

Afternoon 2-4pm

Corfu Donkey Rescue

A delightful, ethical attraction where children can pet and learn about rescued donkeys. Shaded, calm, and stroller-accessible. The gentle animals and rural setting are soothing for babies. Great for a low-key afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Rex Restaurant

Iconic Corfu restaurant serving traditional dishes like pastitsada. Offers highchairs and an outdoor terrace with gentle breeze. Early seating at 6:30pm avoids noise; staff are welcoming to infants.

Avli Taverna

Family-run taverna in a leafy courtyard near the Old Port. Known for grilled octopus and moussaka. Highchairs available, and the open space allows for stroller parking. Baby-friendly atmosphere with reasonable noise levels.

Venetian Well

Elegant dining under a historic Venetian well — romantic for parents. They offer a children's menu and highchairs. The courtyard is quiet and shaded. Best for early dinner (7pm) to avoid late crowds.

To Katoi

Sophisticated restaurant in a vaulted stone cellar with modern Greek cuisine. While intimate, they accommodate infants with advance notice (request a quiet corner). Signature dish: lamb with honey. Outdoor seating available in summer.

The Merchant's House

Boutique restaurant in a restored 19th-century mansion, serving contemporary Greek dishes. They have a highchair and a calm garden terrace. Menu includes fresh fish and organic produce. Perfect for a relaxed lunch.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grecotel Corfu Imperial

$250–400/night

Five-star resort with private beach, lagoon-style pools, and a kids' club. Cribs and blackout curtains upon request. Family suites have separate living areas. The shallow, sandy beach cove is ideal for baby paddling.

Angsana Corfu Resort & Spa

$280–400/night

Luxury boutique resort with spacious suites and private pools. Offers cribs, baby toiletries, and a dedicated infant menu. The main pool has a shallow section, and the property is flat for stroller cruising.

Domes of Corfu, Autograph Collection

$200–350/night

Modern beachfront resort with sprawling pools, kids' club, and family suites. Cribs and bottle warmers provided. The beach is pebbly but the infinity pool has a baby-friendly ledge. Blackout blinds ensure good naps.
