What to do

Morning / 2-3h

La Aurora Zoo

Ranked among the best zoos in Latin America, this beautifully landscaped park features spacious habitats for lions, elephants, and penguins. Toddlers will love the petting zoo and the wide, stroller-friendly paved paths beneath shady trees.

Afternoon / 2h

Museo de los Niños (Children's Museum)

Located right next to the zoo, this interactive museum features hands-on exhibits covering science, geography, and music. There is a dedicated, safe soft-play area designed specifically for toddlers to explore securely.

Morning / 1h

Mapa en Relieve (Relief Map)

This massive, open-air 3D relief map of Guatemala was built in 1905 and offers a fascinating bird's-eye view of the country's volcanoes and valleys. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the scale, but be sure to hold their hands tightly on the viewing towers.

Morning / 1h

Plaza de la Constitución

The city's historic heart is a grand plaza flanked by the National Palace and the Metropolitan Cathedral. It's a fantastic spot for toddlers to stretch their legs and chase the hundreds of friendly pigeons gathering by the central fountain.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

An underground labyrinth of colorful stalls filled with fresh tropical fruits, wooden toys, and woven textiles. It is an sensory wonderland, but narrow aisles make it best navigated with a baby carrier rather than a stroller.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Paseo Cayalá

A sprawling, pedestrian-only white city within the city featuring beautiful plazas, fountains, and grassy lawns. It is exceptionally clean, safe, and stroller-friendly, making it the perfect spot for a relaxed family walk and gelato.

Morning / 2h

Parque Ecológico Deportivo Cayalá

This lush nature reserve inside the city offers easy, flat walking trails through a subtropical forest. Toddlers can easily spot colorful butterflies, local birds, and giant clay sculptures along the paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo Ixchel del Traje Indígena

A stunning museum showcasing the vibrant, rainbow-colored traditional textiles of Guatemala's Mayan communities. The bright patterns are visually stimulating for toddlers, though parents should keep active hands close to avoid touching the displays.

Sunday Morning / 2h

Pasos y Pedales

Every Sunday, major avenues like Avenida Las Américas are closed to car traffic, transforming into a vibrant public park. Families can stroll safely, rent small tricycles, and watch street performers and dogs playing.

🍽️ Where to eat

Kacao

Order the traditional Subanik (a rich, spiced ceremonial meat stew) served in a beautiful, thatched-roof garden setting that feels like a jungle oasis.

Saúl Bistro Zona 14

Get the sweet Nutella crepes and wood-fired pizzas in a whimsical, colorful outdoor patio featuring eccentric decor and kid-friendly lawn spaces.

San Martín (Paseo Cayalá)

Order fresh, crispy champurradas (traditional Guatemalan sweet biscuits) to dip in hot chocolate while sitting on their spacious, family-friendly outdoor patio.

Arrin Cuan

Try the iconic Pepián de Pollo (chicken in a rich, spiced seed-and-nut sauce) accompanied by festive, live marimba music that will have toddlers dancing.

Nais Aquarium

Order classic, kid-approved burgers and milkshakes while dining right next to giant, mesmerizing indoor saltwater aquariums filled with colorful fish.

👨‍👩‍👧 For maximum safety and convenience, base your family in Zone 10 or Zone 14 and use registered Uber rides for easy door-to-door transport. Pack a lightweight stroller for pedestrian havens like Paseo Cayalá, but bring a sturdy baby carrier for navigating the historic center's uneven sidewalks.
