Guatemala City

Guatemala City surprises families with its lush green spaces, rich Mayan heritage, and vibrant modern districts. From feeding giraffes with a volcanic backdrop to exploring interactive museums, this high-altitude capital offers an energetic yet welcoming introduction to Central America. Colorful pedestrian zones and family-friendly eateries make navigating with little ones remarkably easy.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 30 min Uber or pre-arranged hotel shuttle to Zone 10 (Zona Viva) hotels depending on traffic

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

La Aurora Zoo (Zoológico La Aurora)

Consistently rated one of the best zoos in Latin America, this lush park features spacious animal habitats and an incredible dinosaur-themed playground. Toddlers will love the wide, stroller-friendly paved pathways and the chance to safely feed the giraffes from a raised platform.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Children's Museum (Museo de los Niños)

Located right next to the zoo, this interactive museum features hands-on exhibits focusing on science, geology, and a fun mini-supermarket. Toddlers have a dedicated, soft-play sensory room to explore safely away from older kids.

Morning / 1-2h

Mapa en Relieve (Relief Map)

This massive, open-air 3D relief map of Guatemala was built in 1905 and offers a giant's-eye view of the country's complex geography. Toddlers will enjoy pointing at the 'tiny' toy-like volcanoes and oceans from the safe, family-friendly observation towers.

Morning / 2h

Plaza de la Constitución & Central Market

Experience the bustling heart of Zone 1 where kids can feed the hundreds of friendly pigeons in the grand plaza before heading underground to browse colorful market stalls. Toddlers will love the bright textiles, but navigate with a baby carrier here as market aisles are too narrow for strollers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Museo Popol Vuh

This beautifully curated museum houses an extraordinary collection of pre-Columbian Mayan pottery, burial urns, and stone carvings. Toddlers will be visually captivated by the colorful ceremonial animal masks displayed at kid-eye level.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo de la Sexta

A vibrant, pedestrian-only historic street filled with street performers, shops, and gorgeous architectural facades. It is completely flat and stroller-accessible, making it the perfect safe space for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Ecológico Deportivo Cayalá

A sprawling nature reserve right in the city center featuring lush forest trails, wooden suspension bridges, and grassy play fields. It is a fantastic, tranquil escape where toddlers can spot local birds and butterflies safely off the leash.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Ciudad Cayalá

A massive, white-walled, pedestrian-only village-within-a-city packed with fountains, open plazas, and playgrounds. It's incredibly stroller-friendly, safe, and offers plenty of spots for toddlers to run around while parents grab a coffee.

Morning / 2h

Museo Miraflores

Set atop an ancient Mayan archaeological site, this modern museum features beautiful grassy outdoor gardens, koi fish ponds, and a glass floor showcasing ancient tombs. Toddlers will love exploring the sprawling outdoor lawns and feeding the fish.

🍽️ Where to eat

Kacao

Dine under a traditional thatched roof in Zone 10 and order the mild, savory chicken Pepían, Guatemala's national stew.

Arrin Cuan

Enjoy live marimba music in a colorful courtyard while sharing a platter of sweet fried plantains and kid-friendly chuchitos (local tamales).

Saúl Bistro

Head to their whimsical, beautifully decorated Cayalá location to enjoy wood-fired pizzas and sweet chocolate crepes that kids will love.

Siete Caldos

Try the comforting Kaq'ik (traditional turkey soup) in a very spacious, family-friendly setting that offers plenty of high chairs.

San Martín

Grab a table at this beloved bakery for fresh-out-of-the-oven pan dulce, cheese empanadas, and excellent coffee for the parents.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to upscale, walkable areas like Zone 10 (Zona Viva), Zone 14, and Ciudad Cayalá, using Uber for all point-to-point family transport. Bring a lightweight stroller for the smooth walkways of Cayalá and the zoo, but rely on a baby carrier for the historic, bumpy sidewalks of Zone 1.
