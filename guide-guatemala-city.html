Guatemala City blends vibrant Mayan heritage with cosmopolitan luxury, set against a dramatic backdrop of three towering volcanoes. In upscale Zona 10, also known as the Zona Viva, you will find leafy streets, high-end boutiques, and highly secure, stroller-friendly enclaves. Will and Kathryn can comfortably navigate the paved pathways of Paseo Cayalá, a sprawling white-stucco lifestyle district perfect for morning walks with baby Mary Claire. This highland capital offers a sophisticated, spring-like climate year-round, ideal for avoiding extreme heat with an infant.
🚕 Getting there: The journey from La Aurora International Airport (GUA) to the Zona 10 hotel strip takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes. The premier luxury option is a pre-arranged private SUV or Mercedes-Benz V-Class transfer booked directly through your hotel concierge, which includes a pre-installed rear-facing infant car seat and ample trunk space for your stroller.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Paseo Cayalá

A pristine, secure, and entirely pedestrianized lifestyle district featuring striking white Mediterranean architecture. The wide, paved streets are exceptionally stroller-friendly, and the open-air plazas offer tranquil fountains and quiet coffee shops for nursing or resting.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

La Aurora Zoo

Ranked among the finest zoos in Central America, this lush park features beautifully landscaped enclosures. Wide, paved paths and a dense canopy of shade trees keep baby Mary Claire cool, while quiet resting benches offer easy spots for feeding.

Late Morning 10:30 AM - 12:00 PM / 1.5h

Museo Ixchel del Traje Indígena

This stunning, modern museum showcases the vibrant textile artistry of Guatemala's indigenous Mayan communities. Located on a peaceful university campus, the fully air-conditioned and stroller-accessible galleries offer a quiet, low-sensory environment perfect for a morning stroll.

Midday 12:00 PM - 1:30 PM / 1.5h

Museo Popol Vuh

Situated directly adjacent to Museo Ixchel, this museum houses an extraordinary collection of pre-Columbian Mayan ceramic and stone art. The quiet, cool interior is highly accessible, making it easy to admire historic treasures while baby Mary Claire naps in her stroller.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM / 1.5h

Jardín Botánico de la Universidad de San Carlos

The oldest botanical garden in Central America serves as a peaceful, emerald sanctuary in the middle of the city. Shaded by towering exotic trees, the flat dirt paths are ideal for a gentle carrier walk away from urban sounds.

Afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM / 1.5h

Parque Fontabella

An upscale, European-inspired shopping plaza in the heart of Zona 10. Completely stroller-friendly with spotless family restrooms, it features quiet courtyard seating surrounded by beautiful vertical gardens and soothing water features.

Early Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Mapa en Relieve

A massive, open-air relief map of Guatemala built in 1905, offering a fascinating 3D perspective of the country's volcanic topography. The paved paths surrounding the map are stroller-accessible, though parents should use a baby carrier to ascend the viewing towers.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM / 1.5h

Kaminaljuyu Archaeological Park

An ancient Mayan city located within modern city limits, featuring grassy mounds and quiet, pine-shaded archaeological zones. It is a peaceful, uncrowded park perfect for a morning walk using a baby carrier on the grassy terrain.

Early Morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM / 1.5h

Plaza de la Constitución

The historic heart of the city, flanked by the majestic National Palace and the Metropolitan Cathedral. Visit early in the morning to enjoy the open plaza and architecture before the crowds arrive, though a baby carrier is recommended over a stroller due to uneven historic plazas.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tamarindo

An upscale Asian-fusion restaurant in Zona 10 set in a spectacular architectural space with a lush garden terrace. The serene outdoor seating is perfect for an early 6:00 PM reservation, providing plenty of space for a stroller and a peaceful, ambient noise level.

Jake's

Set in a beautifully restored historic farmhouse, this legendary establishment serves exceptional steaks and international fare. The charming, leafy courtyard provides a tranquil, upscale setting where a stroller fits comfortably beside your table.

Sublime

An award-winning fine dining restaurant that takes guests on a sensory journey through Guatemalan history. Book the earliest dinner seating to enjoy their elegant, quiet courtyard space where the attentive staff warmly welcomes luxury-traveling families.

Saúl Bistro Zona 14

Known for its high-design interiors, outstanding crepes, and vibrant local coffee, this bistro features a gorgeous, plant-filled outdoor patio. The atmosphere is upscale yet wonderfully family-friendly, offering highchairs and a lively, forgiving background buzz.

Gracia Cocina de Autor

An elegant, high-end Spanish restaurant in Zona 10 helmed by a master chef. The spacious outdoor terrace is highly accommodating for families, offering a relaxed yet sophisticated dining experience perfect for a long lunch.
