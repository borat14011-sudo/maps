Palma mixes Mediterranean charm with family-friendly infrastructure, offering wide, shaded promenades and calm, shallow coves perfect for wading with a baby. The city's old town is surprisingly flat for strollers, and many restaurants welcome infants with high chairs. Note that strong midday sun means you'll want shade or AC by 11 AM, and most beaches have accessible wooden walkways.
__AFFORD__
🚕 Getting there: From Palma de Mallorca Airport (PMI) to Playa de Palma or city center hotels is a 15-20 minute drive. Best transport: pre-book a car seat taxi or private transfer with child seat (e.g., ShuttleDirect or TaxiPalma) – they'll help with stroller and luggage.

What to do

Early morning (9 AM opening) to avoid heat

Catedral de Mallorca (La Seu)

Gothic cathedral with massive stained-glass windows, cool stone interior, and a floor-level ramp perfect for strollers. Shaded outdoor square for stroller parking. Avoid the audio-guided tour if baby is fussy – just enjoy the peaceful nave.

Late afternoon (5 PM) when sun is lower

Parc de la Mar

Sprawling park right below the cathedral with wide paved paths, shade trees, and a large reflecting pool. Plenty of benches for nursing. Flat and stroller-friendly. Baby can splash toes in the shallow edge of the pool (supervised).

9 AM before crowds build

Playa de Palma (section near Balneario 6)

Long, calm beach with gentle waves and fine sand. Wooden walkways right to the shore for stroller access. Plenty of umbrellas and loungers for rent (seek shade). Shallow entry perfect for wading with baby.

Midday (hottest hours) – AC break

Palma Aquarium

Indoor, air-conditioned marine park with easy stroller navigation. The big shark tank mesmerizes babies. There's a soft-play area for toddlers, and changing tables in restrooms. Skip the outdoor touch pools if it's too hot.

Open 10 AM; visit by 11 AM

Fundació Pilar i Joan Miró

Modern art museum set in Miró's former studios with ramps and shady gardens. Stroller-friendly paths connect the buildings. Quiet, uncrowded – baby can nap in the cool gallery while you enjoy the art.

Morning (opens 10 AM) before it gets hot

Castell de Bellver

Circular castle on a hill with a gentle slope leading up – strollerable but you'll need a bit of push. The inner courtyard is shaded and flat. Panoramic views of Palma. No loud exhibits; baby can enjoy the open space.

Late morning (10-11 AM) before lunch rush

Mercat de l'Olivar

Covered market with wide aisles, excellent for a stroller. Bustling but not deafening – you can buy fresh fruit, pastries, and even prepared food. Shaded and airy. Good spot for people-watching while baby snacks.

9 AM or late afternoon (5 PM)

Cala Estància

Small, calm sandy cove with a manicured beach and a wooden boardwalk. Very shallow water, minimal waves, and some shade from pine trees. Less crowded than Playa de Palma. Stroller-friendly access via ramp.

Morning (open 9:30 AM) – cooler and quieter

Jardí Botànic de Sóller

Botanical garden about 30 min from Palma but worth the drive. Shaded paths, flat terrain, and a small cafe with high chairs. Baby can see colorful flowers and birds. The train ride from Palma to Sóller is also stroller-friendly but skip if short on time.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ca'n Toni

Local family-run spot serving authentic paella and grilled seafood. High chairs available, outdoor terrace with shade. The arroz brut (saffron rice with meat) is a hit – order it with a side of patatas bravas. Noise level is moderate, okay for babies.

Mercado 6 de Enero

Food hall style market with multiple stalls – stroller weaves through easily. Great for lunch: grab a freshly made empanada from La Pascualeta and fresh fruit juice. No loud music, plenty of seats. Baby can try new flavors.

La Bodeguilla

Casual tapas bar with high chairs and a small outdoor patio. The patatas bravas and croquetas are excellent. Waitstaff patient with families – they'll bring a bowl of plain pasta for baby. Noise is low buzz.

Celler de la Rosa

Traditional Mallorcan restaurant with a large, shaded courtyard. High chairs, room for stroller, and early dinner slots (7 PM opening). Try the tumbet (vegetable casserole) and suckling pig. Very baby-tolerant atmosphere.

Pizzeria da Nino

Family-friendly Italian spot with high chairs and a kids' menu. Air-conditioned indoors. The pizza napoletana is perfect for sharing – super thin crust. Quiet enough for a sleeping baby. Open for lunch and early dinner.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Meliá Palma Bay

Beachfront hotel with family rooms, cribs on request (pack 'n play style), and a shallow children's pool. Blackout curtains included. Direct beach access via ramp. Stroller-friendly lobby and elevator. Close to Playa de Palma.

Hotel Costa Azul

Boutique hotel near the cathedral with spacious suites (some with kitchenette). Crib provided, high chair in room, blackout blinds. Small rooftop pool (0.4m deep for babies). Quiet street, easy stroller walks to Parc de la Mar.

Iberostar Selection Llaut Palma

All-inclusive resort with a dedicated baby zone: bottle warmers, cribs with mattress pads, high chairs in restaurants. Large shaded pool with shallow area. Beach with calm waters. Room size generous for a pack 'n play. Blackout curtains standard.

👨‍👩‍👧 Formula is widely available in pharmacies (Farmacia) and supermarkets (Mercadona, Carrefour) – bring a portable bottle sterilizer for easy cleaning. Most sidewalks in Palma old town are smooth and wide enough for a stroller, but cobblestone streets near the cathedral require a rugged wheels. For pediatric care, head to Hospital Universitari Son Espases (public) or Clínica Juaneda (private) – both have English-speaking staff and 24/7 emergency rooms.
