Mallorca

← back to travel board
Mallorca blends turquoise Mediterranean waters and dramatic limestone peaks with welcoming Spanish warmth. It is a playground where historic narrow-gauge trains wind through orange groves and shallow, wave-free coves double as giant toddler wading pools. Here, easy logistics and rich Balearic culture combine to create an effortless, unforgettable family escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or rental car drive to Palma hotels, ~45-60 min to northern beach resorts like Port de Pollença.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Ferrocarril de Sóller (Wooden Train)

Climb aboard this vintage wooden train as it chugs from Palma through mountain tunnels and fragrant citrus orchards to the valley of Sóller. Toddlers will love the open-air breeze and the rhythmic clacking of the historic wooden carriages.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de Alcúdia (Alcúdia Beach)

This sprawling, white-sand beach offers exceptionally shallow, calm turquoise waters that stretch out for yards, making it incredibly safe for little splashers. Toddlers can safely wade and build sandcastles while parents relax under straw umbrellas.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Palma Cathedral (La Seu)

Marvel at this colossal Gothic cathedral towering over the sea, featuring a mesmerizing rose window designed by Antoni Gaudí. The wide, vehicle-free plaza outside is perfect for toddlers to chase pigeons and burn off energy before stepping inside.

Morning / 2h

Cuevas del Drach (Caves of Drach)

Journey deep underground to witness stunning stalactite formations and a magical, classical music concert played on boats on an illuminated subterranean lake. It is cool and shaded, though you should babywear toddlers as stroller access is limited by stairs.

Afternoon / 3h

Palma Aquarium

Escape the midday sun at this world-class aquarium featuring one of Europe's deepest shark tanks and interactive touch pools. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the colorful soft play area and the pirate ship play zone outdoors.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercat de l'Olivar

Immerse your senses in this bustling, vibrant indoor food market packed with fresh colorful fruits, local cheeses, and pastries. Toddlers will love tasting fresh strawberries or choosing a sweet ensaimada pastry from the baker's stall.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Castell de Bellver (Bellver Castle)

Explore a rare circular 14th-century castle perched high on a hill, offering panoramic views of Palma's harbor. The surrounding pine forest features a fantastic public playground that toddlers will love after a walk through the castle moat.

Morning / 3-4h

Sa Dragonera Natural Park Boat Trip

Board a glass-bottom boat from Sant Elm to this uninhabited dragon-shaped island, watching for wild wall lizards that scurry along the paths. The gentle boat ride is short enough to keep toddlers highly entertained without sea-sickness.

Morning / 2h

Jardines de Alfabia (Alfabia Gardens)

Wander through these historic Moorish gardens filled with lush palms, pergolas, and playful trick fountains that spray water at the push of a button. Toddlers will be delighted by the farm animals on-site and the refreshing mist of the water gardens.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ca'n Joan de S'Aigo (Palma)

Order the traditional fluffy ensaimada pastry dusted with powdered sugar alongside a cup of thick hot chocolate or artisanal almond ice cream.

S'Hort de Cartoixa (Valldemossa)

Sip freshly squeezed local orange juice and snack on potato coca (coca de patata) in their lovely shaded garden courtyard.

Restaurante El Cruce (Vilafranca de Bonany)

Feast on massive, ultra-authentic plates of local paella, snails, and grilled meats at this bustling, high-energy, kid-friendly roadside institution.

Es Repòs (Port de Pollença)

Enjoy beachside dining with fresh seafood paella and tapas while your kids play directly in the soft sand just steps from your table.

Patiki Beach (Port de Sóller)

Savor farm-to-table Mediterranean small plates and fresh fish right on the sand with a laid-back atmosphere perfect for families.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a compact car with pre-booked car seats to easily navigate the narrow, winding mountain roads and reach secluded family-friendly coves. Pack a lightweight, sturdy travel stroller for Palma's cobblestones, but switch to a baby carrier for the rocky steps leading down to many beaches.
