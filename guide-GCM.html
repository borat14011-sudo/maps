Turquoise waters and gentle wildlife for all ages

Grand Cayman

← back to travel board
Grand Cayman welcomes families with its powdery white sands, crystal-clear shallow waters, and remarkably friendly marine life. From splashing in gentle tide pools to meeting giant, gentle sea turtles, this island paradise blends effortless Caribbean relaxation with thrilling discoveries for little explorers. It is a safe, easy-to-navigate haven where toddler-friendly memories are waiting around every palm tree.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Stingray City

Take a boat to this shallow sandbar where friendly stingrays glide through waist-deep, crystal-clear water. Toddlers can safely watch and touch the rays from the security of a parent's arms while standing on the sandy bottom.

Morning / 3-4h

Cayman Turtle Centre

Get up close with green sea turtles of all sizes, swim in a safe lagoon, and touch baby turtles in touch tanks. The wading pools and aviary are stroller-accessible and perfect for keeping toddlers entertained.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Seven Mile Beach at Governor's Beach

Play on legendary, soft coral sands with incredibly calm, warm waters that feel like a giant bathtub. Shade is sparse, so bring a beach pop-up tent, but the zero-entry shoreline is absolutely perfect for tiny splashers.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Starfish Point

Discover large, brightly colored red starfish resting in the shallow, tranquil waters of this quiet peninsula. The water here is extremely calm and wave-free, allowing toddlers to easily peer at the starfish without needing to swim.

Morning / 2h

Queen Elizabeth II Royal Botanic Park

Wander through lush tropical gardens, look for the endangered blue iguanas, and explore the wooden boardwalks. Strollers roll easily along the paved paths, and the colorful butterflies will keep little eyes wide with wonder.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Camana Bay Harbour & Splash Pad

A vibrant waterfront town center featuring a fantastic interactive fountain where kids can cool off. Pack a change of clothes so your toddler can run wild through the sprouting water jets before you grab gelato.

Morning / 1-2h

The Hamlin Stephenson Market at The Cricket Grounds

Experience local Caymanian culture by browsing stalls of fresh tropical fruits, sweet coconut water, and local crafts. Toddlers will love tasting sweet local bananas and mangoes while enjoying the lively, outdoor atmosphere.

Afternoon / 2h

Pedro St. James National Historic Site

Step back in time at this grand 18th-century plantation home featuring expansive, manicured lawns overlooking the ocean. The wide-open, grassy grounds are perfect for toddlers to run around and burn off energy safely away from traffic.

Morning to Afternoon / 4h

Rum Point Club

Relax under shady casuarina trees on colorful hammocks while kids play in the shallow, waveless harbor waters. The ultra-shallow, wave-free shoreline makes this one of the safest beaches for toddlers to sit and dig in the sand.

Where to eat

  • Cimboco — A colorful, casual Caribbean cafe with a highly welcoming vibe, offering delicious banana pancakes and toddler-approved pizzas.
  • Gelato & Co. at Camana Bay — Indulge in authentic, freshly made Italian gelato with plenty of kid-friendly flavors right next to the interactive splash fountains.
  • Macabuca Bar & Grill — An open-air, oceanfront spot where parents can enjoy fish tacos while toddlers watch colorful divers and boats enter the water.
  • Chicken! Chicken! — A budget-friendly, casual spot serving up savory, tender Caribbean wood-roasted chicken with easy-to-eat sides like sweet potato and cornbread.
👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a car seat to easily explore the island at your own pace, as public buses are not practical for folding strollers. Always pack reef-safe sunscreen and a UV rash guard for your little ones, as the tropical sun reflection off the white sand is incredibly intense.
