Great Exuma

← back to travel board
Dive into a world of impossibly blue water, shallow sandbars, and friendly wildlife perfect for little explorers. Great Exuma offers a laid-back island pace where families can slow down, beach-hop, and create unforgettable memories together. It is an uncrowded tropical paradise tailor-made for stress-free family bonding.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 25 min taxi or rental car from Exuma International Airport (GGT) to George Town and nearby beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 4-5h

Swimming with Pigs at Big Major Cay

Take a boat charter to feed the famous swimming pigs in shallow water. Toddlers will love watching them splash from the safety of the boat or shallow shoreline.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Stocking Island and Chat 'N' Chill

Take a short water taxi to this iconic beach spot where kids can watch wild stingrays glide through the shallows. Toddlers can safely splash in the calm, knee-deep water.

Morning / 2-3h

Tropic of Cancer Beach

Relax on a crescent of pristine white sand with incredibly calm, shallow waters. Bring a pop-up tent for toddler shade as there are few natural trees.

Low Tide / 2h

Shelling at Coco Plum Beach

Famous for its extreme low tide sandbars and abundance of sand dollars. Toddlers can walk hundreds of yards out in water only ankle-deep.

Morning / 1h

George Town Straw Market

Stroll through this colorful local market to pick up handmade woven toys and souvenirs. It's a shaded, easy walk that's perfect for a quick stroller break.

Morning / 3h

Moriah Harbour Cay National Park

Explore a protected playground of mangroves, sandbanks, and sea turtles via a gentle kayak or boat tour. Toddlers can easily spot starfish in the crystal-clear shallows.

Morning / 1-2h

Sea Turtle Watching at Hoopers Bay

Walk down the public beach access to see wild green sea turtles swimming right near the shore. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching them surface from the safety of the shallow sand.

Late Morning / 2h

Starfish Spotting at Starfish Beach

Search for giant, colorful red cushion starfish resting on the sandy ocean floor. Toddlers can gently touch them in the ultra-calm waters under close supervision.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Nurse Shark Watching at Compass Cay

Stand on the docks to watch docile nurse sharks swim in the marina waters. Toddlers can safely observe these gentle giants from the dry wooden platforms above.
