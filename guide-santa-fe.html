Santa Fe

← back to travel board
Nestled in the foothills of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains, Santa Fe enchants all ages with its rich cultural heritage and vibrant terracotta-hued streets. Families will discover a whimsical blend of hands-on art, scenic mountain parks, and flavorful southwestern cuisine. It is a walkable, sensory-rich playground where historic charm meets kid-friendly wonder.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or rideshare from Santa Fe Regional Airport, or ~60 min shuttle from Albuquerque International Sunport to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Meow Wolf (House of Eternal Return)

An immersive, mind-bending art installation that feels like walking through a giant, interactive fantasy world. Toddlers will love the secret passages and glowing, musical rooms, though keep them close as it can get busy.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Santa Fe Plaza & Palace of the Governors

The historic heart of the city where kids can watch Native American artisans sell beautiful handmade jewelry under the portal. It is a wide-open, car-free plaza perfect for toddlers to safely stretch their legs.

Mid-day / 1-2h

Harrell House Bug Museum

Located inside the Santa Fe Place Mall, this quirky spot features thousands of mounted insects and live creepy crawlies you can touch. Toddlers are absolutely fascinated by the friendly giant tortoises and colorful frogs.

Morning / 1-2h

Santa Fe Railyard Park

A beautifully designed urban green space featuring a fantastic natural playground with wooden climbing structures and sand areas. Toddlers will love playing in the dirt and watching the commuter trains pass right by the park.

Saturday Morning / 2h

Santa Fe Farmers Market

A bustling market filled with local crafts, fresh pastries, and the sweet smell of roasting green chiles. Toddlers will enjoy the live acoustic music and picking out fresh, colorful berries to snack on.

Morning / 2h

Randall Davey Audubon Center & Sanctuary

A peaceful nature escape featuring easy, flat hiking trails winding through lush gardens and pine forests. It is perfect for a stroller-friendly nature walk where toddlers can spot local birds and butterflies.

Afternoon / 2h

Museum of International Folk Art

Home to the world's largest collection of folk art, filled with colorful toys, dioramas, and miniatures from around the globe. The dedicated, hands-on family play area is a dream come true for active toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Canyon Road Art Galleries

A historic half-mile stretch lined with beautiful adobe galleries, many featuring lush, outdoor sculpture gardens. Toddlers will love interacting with the kinetic wind sculptures that spin colorfully in the mountain breeze.

Morning / 1-2h

Dale Ball Trails

An accessible trail network offering stunning views of the high desert landscape and mountains. The short, flat sections near the trailhead are ideal for little legs to scramble over rocks and explore juniper trees.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Shed

Get the cheese enchiladas with blue corn tortillas, smothered in their famous, vibrant red chile sauce.

Tomasita's

Order the honey-drizzled sopapillas and a plate of chicken flautas, which are always a hit with kids.

Kakawa Chocolate House

Treat the family to a cup of rich, historic Mesoamerican chocolate elixirs and homemade artisan truffles.

Plaza Cafe Downtown

Try the classic green chile cheeseburger or their famous coconut cream pie in Santa Fe's oldest dining spot.

Cowgirl BBQ

Feast on the hickory-smoked baby back ribs while the kids enjoy the dedicated play area in the outdoor patio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep everyone hydrated and apply plenty of sunscreen, as Santa Fe sits at a high altitude of 7,000 feet where the sun is deceptively strong. Pack a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a child carrier backpack to navigate the occasionally uneven adobe sidewalks and gravel nature paths.
