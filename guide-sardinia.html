Cagliari wraps coastal beauty, rich Mediterranean history, and family-friendly warmth into a vibrant Sardinian capital. From the white sands of Poetto Beach to the historic ramparts of Castello, it's a playground of gentle waves and sensory discoveries. Parents and kids alike will fall in love with the easygoing pace, rich pasta, and wild flamingos nesting right in the city's backyard.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or 10 min train ride from Cagliari Elmas Airport to the city center, or ~20 mins by taxi to the Poetto beach resorts.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Poetto Beach

This sprawling white-sand beach features calm, shallow waters perfect for little splashers and a paved promenade ideal for stroller walks. Toddlers will love building sandcastles while parents enjoy beachside cafes.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Molentargius - Saline Regional Park

Watch thousands of bright pink wild flamingos nesting in the salt pans right next to the city. Rent a bike with a child seat to easily navigate the flat, stroller-friendly dirt paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Bastione di Saint Remy

Climb or take the public elevator up to this majestic limestone terrace for panoramic views of the city and sea. The wide open plaza at the top is completely car-free, giving toddlers plenty of space to run around safely.

Morning / 1.5h

Mercato di San Benedetto

Immerse your senses in Europe’s largest indoor fish market, bustling with colorful fruit stalls and friendly vendors offering cheese samples. It is sensory-rich but stroller-friendly, and kids will be mesmerized by the giant seafood displays on the lower floor.

Afternoon / 2h

Parco di Monte Urpinu

A lush hilltop park filled with pine trees, free-roaming peacocks, and duck ponds that toddlers will adore feeding. It offers great shaded playground areas to escape the midday Mediterranean sun.

Morning / 1h

Cagliari Cathedral

Explore this stunning 13th-century cathedral hidden within the medieval Castello district, featuring intricate marble and cool interiors. The quiet, winding streets outside are car-free, though bumpy for umbrella strollers.

Morning / 2h

Orto Botanico dell'Università di Cagliari

Wander through lush botanical gardens featuring massive centuries-old ficus trees with hanging roots, succulent greenhouses, and Roman cisterns. The shaded gravel paths are great for a quiet, sensory stroller nap.

Morning / 30 mins

Roman Amphitheatre of Cagliari

Peer into this ancient 2nd-century amphitheater carved directly into the rocky hillside of the city. While you can't walk inside the ruins, viewing it from the path is a quick, impressive history lesson that won't test a toddler's patience.

Evening / 1-2h

Passeggiata at Marina District

Enjoy an early evening family stroll along the bustling harbor-front streets, lined with palm trees and vibrant shops. Grab a cup of gelato and watch the ferries glide into the port as the sun sets.

🍽️ Where to eat

Luigi Pomata

Try their legendary tuna pasta or fresh local seafood dishes in an upscale yet welcoming atmosphere.

Antica Cagliari

Order the local culurgiones potato-and-mint pasta or the seafood fregola, which is always a hit with kids.

Framento

Get their award-winning sourdough pizzas topped with fresh local cheeses and cured meats, perfect for a casual family dinner.

Ristorante Martinelli's

Indulge in delicate local seafood appetizers and fresh homemade pasta, served by incredibly welcoming staff who treat children like royalty.

Gelateria Peter Pan

Try their creamy, natural gelato made with local Sardinian milk, featuring unique flavors like wild berry and classic pistachio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy stroller with good suspension or a baby carrier to navigate Castello's steep, cobblestoned alleys and historic steps. Most restaurants open for dinner at 8:00 PM, so pack extra snacks for toddlers or plan for a larger late-afternoon snack to bridge the gap.
