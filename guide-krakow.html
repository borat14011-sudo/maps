Krakow's historic heart beats with a regal, fairytale energy, where medieval townhomes wrap around one of Europe's largest town squares. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the city offers an intimate, walkable escape steeped in deep cultural luxury. Strolling along the Planty Park's lush green belt provides a perfectly paved, shaded canopy ideal for Mary Claire’s mid-day stroller naps. From private horse-drawn carriage rides to serene Vistula River views, this Polish jewel balances sophisticated dining with an incredibly family-welcoming atmosphere.
🚕 Getting there: Kraków-Balice International Airport (KRK) is just a 25-minute drive from the historic Old Town. The most seamless option for a family with a stroller is a pre-arranged private Mercedes V-Class transfer booked through your hotel, which includes a pre-installed, premium rear-facing infant car seat and ample trunk space for travel gear.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am

Wawel Royal Castle

Explore the stunning Renaissance courtyard of Poland's historic seat of kings. The outdoor grounds and castle courtyards are fully stroller-accessible and provide a majestic, breezy setting for a gentle morning walk with Mary Claire. Quiet, uncrowded pathways allow for a peaceful pace.

Mid-day 12-2pm

Planty Park

This unique green ring encircles the entire Old Town, featuring perfectly flat, paved pathways shaded by towering chestnut trees. It is the ultimate luxury for parents, offering a quiet, sensory-rich environment for Mary Claire to nap while you stroll past historic fortifications.

Late afternoon 3-4:30pm

Rynek Główny (Main Market Square)

Indulge in a private, blankets-provided horse-drawn carriage ride around Europe's grandest medieval square. It is a classic Krakow experience that keeps Mary Claire safely elevated from the cobblestones while providing gentle, sleep-inducing motion.

Morning 10am-12pm

Princes Czartoryski Museum

Home to Leonardo da Vinci's 'Lady with an Ermine,' this world-class, quiet museum is fully climate-controlled and equipped with modern elevators. It offers a sophisticated, peaceful cultural escape that is easy to navigate with a high-end travel stroller.

Sunset 5-6:30pm

Vistula River Boulevards

Enjoy a sunset stroll along the pristine, paved riverside paths beneath Wawel Castle. The wide, flat promenade is entirely car-free, offering calm waters, fresh river breezes, and plenty of space to roll along without any cobblestone bumps.

Morning 10-11:30am

Cloth Hall (Sukiennice)

Peruse the elegant, historic market stalls of the Cloth Hall. The wide, ground-floor central arcade is completely flat and stroller-friendly, allowing you to browse premium amber jewelry and Polish crafts in a sheltered, indoor environment.

Afternoon 3-5pm

Jordan Park (Park Jordana)

A beautifully manicured historic park just outside the Old Town, featuring vast grassy lawns, quiet ponds, and wide paved paths. It offers a pristine environment for sensory baby play on the grass, away from the city crowds.

Sunset 6-7pm

Kraków Eye

Take a private flight on the modern Ferris wheel located on the Vistula River bank. The fully enclosed, climate-controlled luxury gondolas provide a smooth, gentle ride with stunning panoramic views of the city, perfectly safe and calm for an infant.

Morning 10am-12pm

Stanisław Lem Garden of Experiences

An expansive, green outdoor sensory park that combines lush parkland with interactive installations. The wide pathways are completely stroller-friendly, providing a serene, visual-rich walk with plenty of shaded benches for nursing.

🍽️ Where to eat

Szara Gęś w Kuchni

Located right on the Main Square, this upscale establishment serves modern Polish cuisine. Their signature 'Szara Gęś' (grey goose egg dessert) is world-famous. Book a table on the elegant outdoor terrace for early seating to easily accommodate a stroller and enjoy the square's vibrant atmosphere.

Farina Restaurant

An elegant, Michelin-recommended seafood restaurant housed in a historic building. The atmosphere is quiet, refined, and highly accommodating to families, featuring spacious seating layouts, highchairs upon request, and incredibly fresh, flour-free culinary creations.

Fiorentina Restaurant

Acclaimed for its exquisite Polish-Italian fusion and beautiful historic interiors. Request a table in their peaceful, secluded garden courtyard for an early dinner, providing a quiet, low-noise environment where Mary Claire can relax.

Copernicus Restaurant

A highly sophisticated dining room set beneath a grand glass atrium. Opt for an early lunch seating to experience their masterfully crafted tasting menus in a calm, naturally lit setting that is spacious enough for a luxury stroller.

Pod Aniołami

A legendary upscale restaurant specializing in traditional Polish meats marinated in wild herbs. While the cellars are historic, their quiet, heated ground-floor courtyard patio is perfect for families, offering a cozy and welcoming vibe with excellent highchair availability.
