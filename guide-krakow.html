Krakow

← back to travel board
Krakow strikes the perfect balance for an active family, offering world-class history and high-end dining without the crushing crowds of Paris or Rome. The city's legendary Planty Park forms a continuous green ring around the pedestrianized Old Town, allowing you to wheel a stroller from a Michelin-rated spot straight into a peaceful shaded playground. It is clean, remarkably safe, and highly walkable, ensuring your daughter's nap schedule remains entirely intact while you enjoy Polish hospitality.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-minute private Mercedes van transfer via Krakow Direct from John Paul II International Airport Balice straight to your Old Town hotel, avoiding the hassle of the train with heavy luggage and a stroller.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Wawel Royal Castle

Explore the grand Italianate courtyard of Poland's historic heart where your toddler can toddle safely away from vehicle traffic. While the outdoor castle grounds are highly stroller-friendly, you will need to use the complimentary cloakroom for your stroller if you enter the private royal apartments.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Planty Park

This lush, 4-kilometer loop of gardens completely encircles the Old Town, offering a shaded, paved haven for long stroller walks. It features several modern, fenced play areas like the Wild Promenada playground near the Archaeological Museum.

Morning / 3h

Krakow Zoo

Nestled in the middle of the dense Wolski Forest, this compact zoo is incredibly peaceful and offers wide, paved paths shaded by massive oak trees. Your daughter will love the mini-zoo section where she can feed dwarf goats and see alpacas up close.

Morning / 1-2h

Main Market Square

Watch your toddler chase the famous Krakow pigeons in Europe’s largest medieval square while you enjoy a flat white from an outdoor café. Stick to the paved outer perimeter for smooth stroller rolling, as the central medieval cobblestones can be quite bumpy.

Morning / 1h

Stary Kleparz

Krakow’s oldest active food market is a vibrant, covered sensory experience where you can grab fresh berries, regional sheep's cheese, and warm pastries. The wide, flat aisles are easily navigable with a compact stroller during the quieter mid-morning hours.

Afternoon / 2h

Jordan Park

This massive park just west of the city center boasts spectacular, modern playground zones segregated by age, including safe sensory swings for infants. It is the perfect spot to let her play on real grass while you grab a premium coffee from the park's central pavilion.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Vistula River Boulevards

These wide, paved, completely car-free river paths right beneath Wawel Castle are ideal for an uninterrupted sunset stroller run or walk. There are plenty of grassy banks and benches where you can sit and watch the riverboats and swans pass by.

Morning / 2h

Museum of Municipal Engineering

Located in a historic tram depot in Kazimierz, this spacious museum has a fantastic interactive zone called 'Kreatywna Strefa' designed for youngest explorers to play. It is fully stroller-accessible, climate-controlled, and highly engaging for visual toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Krakus Mound

An ancient prehistoric mound offering the best panoramic view of Krakow without the Old Town crowds. The grassy slopes at the base are perfect for a family picnic, though you will want a sturdy stroller to navigate the dirt path leading up.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pod Nosem

Order the exquisite wild mushroom pierogi or the slow-cooked wild boar; the refined, quiet atmosphere on Kanonicza Street is highly welcoming to families during early dinner service. There is ample room for a stroller at the outdoor tables nestled beneath the castle walls.

Dynia Resto Bar

Try the signature pumpkin gnocchi in this modern, airy restaurant that features a quiet, closed interior garden away from street noise. It is highly popular with local parents due to its spacious layout, high chairs, and dedicated kids' play corner.

Gaska

Order the slow-roasted goose leg with potato dumplings; the cozy, casual atmosphere here means baby noises are easily absorbed. They are exceptionally family-friendly, offering sturdy high chairs, a changing table, and toys to keep toddlers occupied.

Charlotte (Plac Szczepanski)

Feast on fresh-baked croissants with their signature white chocolate lemon cream for breakfast. This location on Plac Szczepanski has wide, flat outdoor seating with plenty of room to park a stroller while watching the square's beautiful fountains.

Bunkier Cafe

Grab an excellent espresso and a slice of traditional Polish cheesecake in this glass-walled pavilion nestled directly inside Planty Park. Its casual vibe, spacious semi-outdoor seating, and immediate access to the park's walking paths make it a dream for parents with sleeping babies.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Copernicus

Located on Krakow's most historic and quiet street, this luxury 5-star hotel offers spacious suites, a gorgeous indoor swimming pool in a vaulted medieval cellar for family swim time, and top-tier crib setups.

Radisson Blu Hotel, Krakow

Situated directly opposite the green Planty Park ring, this hotel offers unbeatable stroller access to the Old Town. It features large family rooms, robust baby amenities, and a highly accommodating staff used to international family travelers.

Sheraton Grand Krakow

Positioned right on the Vistula River bend below Wawel Castle, this hotel boasts large rooms with beautiful river views, an indoor heated pool, and seamless access to the car-free riverfront paths for early-morning baby walks.

👨‍👩‍👧 While Krakow's Old Town is highly walkable, the historic cobblestones can be brutal on small wheels, so bring a stroller with sturdy suspension rather than a lightweight travel stroller. Most high-end restaurants in Krakow are incredibly welcoming to young children, but it is best to book early dinner slots around 5:30 PM when dining rooms are quieter and service is fast. Poland uses the 'Zabka' convenience store chain everywhere; they are perfect for grabbing quick, reliable baby essentials like wipes, bottled water, or organic fruit pouches on the go.
