Rome is a vibrant, living museum where cobblestone streets lead to ancient wonders and sweet treats around every corner. For families, the city's slow-paced dining, sprawling parks, and grand fountains offer an engaging, sensory-rich playground. It is a magical destination where history comes alive and toddler tantrums are easily cured with a fresh scoop of artisanal gelato.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi or the Leonardo Express train to Termini Station

What to do

Morning / 2h

The Colosseum

Walk the path of gladiators in this iconic ancient amphitheater that will leave older kids awestruck. Toddlers will love pointing at the pigeons and exploring the wide, stroller-accessible outer ring.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Villa Borghese Gardens

Rome's central green oasis features playgrounds, swan-filled ponds, and Surrey bike rentals. It is the perfect place for toddlers to run free on the grass and escape the city traffic.

Early Morning / 30m

Trevi Fountain

Toss a coin into the sparkling blue waters to ensure your family's return to Rome. Keep toddlers close to avoid the crowds, and visit early to secure a spot right by the water.

Afternoon / 2h

Explora Museum

A hands-on, interactive museum designed specifically for children with water play, wind tunnels, and a toddler-only sensory zone. It offers a much-needed break from historical sightseeing.

Midday / 1-2h

Testaccio Market

A bustling covered food market where kids can sample fresh fruits and parents can enjoy gourmet street food. It is stroller-friendly with plenty of open space for little ones to wander.

Weekend Morning / 1h

Gianicolo Puppet Theater

Watch the traditional Pulcinella hand-puppet show on Janiculum Hill, which has delighted Roman children for decades. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the slapstick action while parents enjoy panoramic views of Rome.

Evening / 1h

Piazza Navona

A pedestrian-only square filled with street performers, artists, and beautiful fountains that captivate young eyes. Toddlers can safely toddle around the wide open space without worrying about cars.

Morning / 3h

Bioparco di Roma

Located inside Villa Borghese, this historic zoo features over 1,000 animals in lush, green environments. It’s an easy, low-stress day out with wide paths perfect for strollers.

Morning / 2h

Castel Sant'Angelo

Explore a real castle complete with ramparts, secret passages, and excellent views of the Tiber River. Toddlers will enjoy running around the surrounding park and playground before heading inside.

🍽️ Where to eat

Da Enzo al 29

Try the legendary, creamy Cacio e Pepe pasta served in a cozy, authentic Trastevere atmosphere.

Giolitti

Treat the kids to a towering cone of Rome's most famous gelato, featuring classic flavors like pistachio topped with fresh whipped cream.

Pizzeria da Remo

Get the ultra-thin, crispy Roman-style Margherita pizza baked in a traditional wood-fired oven.

Frigidarium

Order any artisanal gelati flavor and ask to have it dipped in their signature dark or white chocolate shell.

Forno Campo de' Fiori

Grab some warm, freshly baked Pizza Bianca sliced open and stuffed with mortadella for an easy family picnic.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the bulky stroller for a lightweight umbrella stroller or carrier, as Rome's ancient cobblestones and historic steps can be challenging to navigate. Always carry cash for quick gelato stops and public restrooms, which often require a small coin entry fee.
