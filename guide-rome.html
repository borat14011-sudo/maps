What to do

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Villa Borghese Gardens

Enjoy wide, paved, stroller-friendly pathways shaded by centuries-old stone pines. It is exceptionally quiet, making it the perfect spot for Mary Claire to nap in her stroller while you admire the lake and classical temples.

Early morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

The Colosseum Arena Floor

Book a private, skip-the-line tour specifically highlighting elevator access to the flat arena floor. This avoids the steep steps of the main tiers, keeping the stroller ride smooth and avoiding heavy midday crowds.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Piazza Navona

This grand, flat, entirely pedestrianized square is perfect for pushchairs. The gentle, white-noise splash of Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers is soothing for infants, and there are no cars to worry about.

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

St. Peter's Basilica

Utilize the dedicated, crowd-free side entrance elevator reserved for families with strollers. Inside, the massive, quiet acoustics and cool marble floors offer a calm, temperature-controlled sanctuary for a sleeping baby.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Bioparco di Roma

Located inside Villa Borghese, this historic zoo features lush, shady botanical pathways and gentle walking loops. It is incredibly quiet compared to the ruins and offers sensory stimulation for a developing baby.

Late afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Orto Botanico di Roma

Nestled in Trastevere, these botanical gardens are an oasis of calm. The paved slopes wind through bamboo groves and Japanese gardens, offering a sensory-rich environment with minimal tourists.

Sunset 5:30-6:30pm / 1h

Gianicolo Hill Terrace

Stroll up the paved paths of Janiculum Hill for sweeping, panoramic views of Rome. The terrace is wide and stroller-accessible, offering a refreshing breeze away from the valley heat.

Early morning 7:30-8:15am / 45m

Trevi Fountain (Early Hours)

Visit at dawn before the crowds arrive. The flat viewing area is easily accessible with a stroller, allowing you to toss a coin with Mary Claire in quiet peace before the ambient noise peaks.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Castel Sant'Angelo Outer Ramparts

The wide, flat ramp leading up to the castle is highly accessible. Take the stroller-friendly elevator to the lower terrace to enjoy panoramic Tiber River views in a spacious, uncrowded setting.

🍽️ Where to eat

Da Enzo al 29

Famous for authentic Cacio e Pepe. Arrive 15 minutes before their early 12:15 PM lunch opening to secure an outdoor terrace table, which easily accommodates a compact stroller and offers a lively, forgiving noise level.

Emma Pizza Romana

An upscale, spacious pizzeria serving outstanding thin-crust Roman pies. They provide highchairs, have a highly professional staff accustomed to families, and offer early dinner seatings starting at 7:00 PM.

Ristorante Piperno

Located in a quiet, secluded piazza in the historic Roman Ghetto. The outdoor tables are perfect for parking a stroller, and the staff warmly welcomes infants while serving legendary carciofi alla giudia.

Hostaria Da Pietro

An elegant, family-run institution near the Spanish Steps. The incredibly warm staff love babies, happily provide highchairs, and serve classic Roman pastas in a cozy, welcoming atmosphere.

Giolitti

Rome's most famous gelateria features a spacious, stroller-accessible indoor seating salon. Sit down for a table-service gelato Coppa Olimpia, allowing you to avoid the chaotic counter lines with Mary Claire.
