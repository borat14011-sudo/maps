Heraklion

← back to travel board
Steeped in legendary Minoan history, Heraklion welcomes families with its walkable Venetian harbor, vibrant open-air squares, and gentle Cretan seas. Transitioning from your Athens flight, you will find a relaxed island pace perfect for wandering toddlers and curious kids alike. It is a captivating blend of ancient labyrinths, fresh seaside air, and warm Greek hospitality.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 minute taxi or private transfer from Heraklion International Airport (HER) to the city center, or ~20-25 minutes to the resort beaches of Ammoudara.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Palace of Knossos

Explore the legendary labyrinth of King Minos and the Minotaur, featuring wide paths and vibrant reconstructed frescoes. Strollers are tough on the dirt paths, so bring a toddler carrier.

Afternoon / 1.5-2h

Cretaquarium (Thalassokosmos)

Dive into the marine world of the Mediterranean with massive tanks hosting sharks, sea turtles, and colorful jellyfish. It is fully stroller-accessible with low-viewing windows perfect for tiny eyes.

Morning / 2h

Heraklion Archaeological Museum

Witness original Minoan treasures, including the famous Phaistos Disc and colorful frescoes, in a cool, air-conditioned space. Brightly lit rooms and fascinating animal figurines keep little ones engaged.

Late Afternoon / 1-1.5h

Koules Fortress

Stroll along the scenic harbor mole to this sturdy 16th-century Venetian fortress overlooking the Cretan Sea. The long, pedestrian-only harbor wall is ideal for letting toddlers toddle safely away from traffic.

Morning / 2h

Natural History Museum of Crete

Let kids experience an earthquake simulator, explore the discovery room, and marvel at life-size models of regional animals. The dedicated Stavros Niarchos discovery area is designed specifically for younger children.

Morning / 1h

Central Market (1866 Street)

Savor the sights, smells, and sounds of Heraklion’s historic market street, packed with local cheeses, olives, and herbs. Grab a fresh, warm cheese-filled pastry to keep little hands busy while you browse.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Georgiadis Park

Relax under the shade of mature trees in this central green oasis featuring a lovely playground and a cozy eco-cafe. It is a fully gated, car-free space where toddlers can run free and mingle with local kids.

Morning / 3h

Ammoudara Beach

Splash in the shallow, calm waters of this long sandy beach located just west of the city. Rent a sunbed with an umbrella for crucial shade, and enjoy the soft sand that is perfect for building sandcastles.

Evening / 1h

Morosini Fountain at Lions Square

Wander past the elegant Venetian Loggia to the bustling heart of the city to watch water sprout from stone lion mouths. This pedestrianized square is great for chasing pigeons and enjoying a quick scoop of gelato.

🍽️ Where to eat

Peskesi

Order the slow-cooked pork with honey and thyme or the hand-rolled Cretan pasta called skioufichta.

Kastella

Try the fresh grilled sea bream or the crispy baby calamari, perfect for little fingers to dip in tzatziki.

Kirkor

Grab a table right on Lions Square for their legendary warm, custard-filled bougatsa pastry dusted with powdered sugar and cinnamon.

Merastri

Enjoy slow-roasted lamb and fresh baked bread in a beautiful, plant-filled rustic courtyard with plenty of space for family dining.

Amalia's Kitchen

Opt for kid-friendly gyro platters, fresh Greek salads, or familiar burgers in a highly accommodating, casual setting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a sturdy baby carrier for ancient ruins like Knossos, as cobblestones and dirt paths make strollers highly impractical. Greek dinner times skew late, so look for tavernas opening by 6:00 PM or plan for heavy afternoon snacks to keep toddlers happy.
