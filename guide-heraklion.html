Heraklion

← back to travel board
Heraklion serves as the perfect, low-stress Cretan gateway for active families transitioning from a busy Athens flight. Combining walkable Venetian fortifications, rich Minoan history, and shaded plazas, the city offers a manageable pace for a stroller-bound toddler. You'll find high-quality seaside resorts just outside the center, allowing you to balance morning ruins with afternoon pool naps. It's an authentic Greek hub where Cretan hospitality means locals genuinely welcome your baby at every taverna.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 min taxi from Heraklion Airport to city center hotels, or ~20 min to seaside resorts.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Palace of Knossos

Explore the cradle of Minoan civilization with its vibrant reconstructed frescoes and sprawling courtyard. The terrain is highly uneven with dirt paths and ancient stones, so leave the stroller behind and use a sturdy baby carrier.

Afternoon / 2h

Heraklion Archaeological Museum

This world-class, air-conditioned museum houses the finest collection of Minoan artifacts in the world. It is highly stroller-friendly with wide ramps, modern elevators, and spacious galleries to navigate during a midday nap.

Morning / 2h

Natural History Museum of Crete

A interactive museum featuring realistic dioramas of Mediterranean ecosystems and a dedicated kids' discovery zone. The multi-level facility is fully stroller-accessible, offering a cool, sensory-rich escape for a curious toddler.

Morning / 2-3h

Cretaquarium (Thalassokosmos)

Located just a 15-minute drive east of the city, this massive aquarium showcases diverse Aegean marine life from sharks to sea turtles. It is fully paved, flat, and air-conditioned, making it exceptionally easy to navigate with a stroller.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Koules Fortress (Castello a Mare)

Walk the wide, paved harbor breakwater leading up to this impressive 16th-century Venetian fortress. While the interior has steps, the exterior path is flat, breezy, and perfect for an easy stroller stroll while watching the fishing boats.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Georgiadis Park

A lush, shaded green lung in the center of Heraklion featuring a well-maintained children's playground and a relaxed outdoor café. It is a favorite local spot where your toddler can safely touch grass and watch local families play.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Historical Museum of Crete

Offering a peaceful look at Crete’s modern history and folk art, this museum is far quieter than the main archaeological site. The elevators and gentle ramps make it stress-free to wheel a stroller through the galleries.

Morning / 1h

Central Market on 1866 Street

A vibrant, sensory-rich pedestrian street filled with stalls selling Cretan herbs, local cheeses, and fresh fruits. Go early in the morning before the crowds pack the narrow paths so you can easily maneuver a stroller.

Afternoon / 3h

Archanes Village

A beautifully restored traditional village just 20 minutes south of Heraklion, surrounded by olive groves. The colorful, pedestrianized central square is safe and perfect for letting a toddler walk around while you enjoy an outdoor lunch.

🍽️ Where to eat

Peskesi

Savor authentic Cretan farm-to-table cuisine like slow-cooked pork with honey and thyme in a beautiful, open-air garden courtyard. It opens early at 13:00, allowing you to beat the late-night Greek dinner rush with a toddler.

Kirkor

Located right on Lion Square, this legendary café is the spot to try traditional sweet Bougatsa pastry filled with warm custard. The ample outdoor seating lets you park the stroller right at your table while watching the fountain.

Amalia's Kitchen

A highly welcoming, spacious eatery near the harbor offering a mix of Greek favorites and family-friendly American classics. They provide clean high chairs and have a wide layout that easily accommodates stroller parking.

Kastella

Enjoy fresh grilled sea bass on a breezy, open-air harbor terrace overlooking the sea. The ambient sound of the waves serves as a natural white noise machine, making it easy for a baby to nap in their stroller while you dine.

Merastri

A gorgeous restaurant with a spacious, leafy outdoor garden serving elevated Cretan classics like lamb cooked in clay. The unhurried pace and large, well-spaced tables offer plenty of room for baby gear and diaper bags.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Amirandes Grecotel Boutique Resort

A luxury resort east of the city featuring massive lagoon pools, private beach coves, and excellent baby-friendly amenities including cribs, bottle warmers, and high-end baby food preparation services.

Legacy Gastro Suites

An upscale boutique hotel in the city center offering massive, highly styled suites with separate living spaces and kitchenettes, making bottle prep and post-bedtime relaxation for parents incredibly easy.

GDM Megaron Historical Monument Hotel

A luxury harbor-front hotel featuring a rooftop pool, spacious family rooms, and top-tier soundproofing to ensure the baby sleeps soundly despite the central, walkable location.
