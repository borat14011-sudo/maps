What to do

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Prince's Island Park

A pristine urban oasis featuring paved, stroller-friendly pathways, plenty of shade from mature trees, and calm lagoons perfect for a peaceful midday walk with Mary Claire.

Morning 9:30am-12:30pm / 3h

Heritage Park Historical Village

Step back in time at this sprawling outdoor living history museum, featuring wide, stroller-accessible dirt paths, vintage steam train rides, and a quiet, sensory-friendly environment.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Studio Bell, home of the National Music Centre

Explore five floors of Canadian music history in a stunning architectural marvel; it is fully stroller-accessible via spacious elevators with plenty of quiet corners to soothe the baby.

Early morning 9-10am / 1h

Calgary Tower

Ascend 191 meters for panoramic views of the city and the Rocky Mountains; visit during the early morning hours to skip the crowds and enjoy a quiet, mesmerizing experience for Mary Claire.

Afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Contemporary Calgary

Housed in a striking former planetarium, this spacious gallery offers wide, barrier-free exhibition halls that make maneuvering a luxury stroller a breeze while you take in modern art.

Late afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Esker Foundation

A quiet, light-filled contemporary art gallery in the historic Inglewood neighborhood, perfect for a peaceful stroll while Mary Claire naps in her carrier.

Morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Wilder Institute/Calgary Zoo

Wander through world-class exhibits on well-paved, flat paths with plenty of nursing nooks, botanical gardens for quiet downtime, and stroller rental options.

Morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Bow River Pathway

Take a scenic, active walk along this beautifully paved, flat pathway network winding along the river, offering spectacular skyline views and cool river breezes.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

TELUS Spark Science Centre

Visit the Creative Kids Museum inside, which features safe, tactile sensory spaces specifically designed for babies and toddlers to explore under soft lighting.
