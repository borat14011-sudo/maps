Puerto Vallarta

← back to travel board
Nestled between the Sierra Madre mountains and the sparkling Banderas Bay, Puerto Vallarta wraps families in warm Mexican hospitality and vibrant coastal charm. From strolling the bustling boardwalk to encountering majestic marine life, this tropical playground offers gentle tides and rich cultural sights perfect for little feet.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2h

El Malecón Boardwalk

This wide, pedestrian-only strip features whimsical sculptures, street performers, and stunning ocean views. It is completely stroller-friendly, though it gets busiest and loudest after sunset.

Morning / 3-4h

Vallarta Botanical Garden

Wander through lush jungle trails, orchid houses, and splashing streams where kids can cool off. The main paths are manageable with a rugged stroller, and the koi ponds are a huge hit with toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de Los Muertos

A lively beach perfect for building sandcastles under a shady palapa while watching the iconic sail-shaped pier. The southern end has gentler waves suitable for toddlers splashing under close supervision.

Full Day / 6-7h

Las Caletas Day Trip

Accessible only by boat, this private cove offers a secluded paradise with gentle waters, parrot encounters, and a dedicated kids' adventure park. Toddlers will love the calm, shallow shoreline and shady hammocks.

Morning / 1-2h

Municipal Market of Rio Cuale

A colorful, bustling indoor market filled with local crafts, wooden toys, and bright Mexican folk art. The sensory experience is exciting for toddlers, but keep them close in the narrower aisles.

Morning / 2h

Estero El Salado

A protected mangrove reserve right in the city where families can take a guided boat tour to spot crocodiles, iguanas, and exotic birds. The calm boat ride is highly engaging and safe for seated toddlers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Caracol Kid-Zone

An air-conditioned shopping mall featuring a vibrant indoor soft-play area and coin-operated rides. It is the perfect escape from the midday humidity or a sudden tropical shower for energetic toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Parish of Our Lady of Guadalupe

This iconic brick cathedral with its crown-topped spire is the heart of the historic center. The quiet interior provides a peaceful, cool sensory break, and the plaza outside is great for feeding pigeons.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Cuale River Island

A shaded, pedestrian-only island oasis in the middle of the river, home to resident iguanas hanging in the trees. It features a small playground and quiet pathways that are ideal for a relaxed stroller walk.

Where to eat

  • Pancho's Takos — Famous for their melt-in-your-mouth al pastor tacos, this bustling spot has a lively atmosphere where toddlers can munch on mild cheese quesadillas.
  • Fredy's Tucan — A beloved, garden-courtyard breakfast spot serving fluffy pancakes, fresh fruit plates, and traditional chilaquiles that the whole family will love.
  • Pescado Embarazado — Sold by beachside vendors, this local specialty of grilled fish or shrimp marinated in lime and spices is an easy, mess-free finger food for kids.
  • Joe Jack's Fish Shack — A casual, highly family-friendly eatery known for its excellent fish and chips, rooftop terrace, and welcoming staff who happily accommodate high chairs.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to the flat, paved Malecón for stroller walks, as the cobblestone streets of the Romantic Zone can be incredibly bumpy; a sturdy baby carrier is highly recommended here. Always carry cash in pesos for beach vendors, and keep bottled water on hand to ensure everyone stays hydrated in the tropical heat.
