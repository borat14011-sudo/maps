Stroll past pastel antebellum mansions along the historic battery as the gentle coastal breeze keeps the Lowcountry air fresh and inviting. Charleston radiates a timeless Southern charm, seamlessly blending cobblestone romance with modern, family-friendly sophistication. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the city offers incredibly stroller-ready paved promenades like the iconic Waterfront Park, ensuring seamless luxury walks. What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Waterfront Park

Stroll along the paved, stroller-friendly harbor promenade to see the famous Pineapple Fountain; mature oak trees provide excellent shade, and the gentle breeze keeps baby Mary Claire cool during her morning nap.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

South Carolina Aquarium

A fully stroller-accessible indoor haven featuring a massive two-story ocean exhibit; the calm blue lighting and mesmerizing fish offer a highly soothing sensory experience for a young baby.

Late morning 11am-1pm / 2h

Historic Charleston City Market

Explore this historic, open-air covered market featuring flat concrete walkways that are easy for strollers, allowing you to browse local sweetgrass baskets while baby is shielded from direct sunlight.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

White Point Garden

Located at the tip of the peninsula, this scenic park offers wide, flat dirt paths lined with historic cannons and massive, shaded live oak trees that shield infants from the afternoon sun.

Midday 11:30am-1pm / 1.5h

Charleston Harbor Tours

Board the Carolina Belle for a gentle, scenic harbor cruise; the boat features a spacious, covered, and climate-controlled main deck perfect for a calm ride with an infant.

Morning 9am-12pm / 3h

Magnolia Plantation and Gardens

Meander along the flat boardwalks and smooth dirt paths of America's oldest public gardens, offering a lush, quiet landscape that is highly conducive to stroller naps.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Children's Museum of the Lowcountry

An air-conditioned indoor oasis featuring a dedicated, soft-play infant area where Mary Claire can safely crawl and play away from the afternoon humidity.

Late afternoon 4-5pm / 1h

Rainbow Row

Take a gentle walk along the flat, paved sidewalk opposite these iconic, pastel-colored historic homes, offering a picture-perfect backdrop for family portraits without heavy crowds.

Early afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Gibbes Museum of Art

A quiet, spacious museum with wide galleries, smooth floors, and stroller-accessible elevators, providing a peaceful, air-conditioned environment for a midday family retreat.
