Charleston

← back to travel board
Charleston blends Southern refinement with a highly walkable, coastal pace that takes the stress out of traveling with a toddler. The historic district's shade-draped alleys and harbor breezes keep little ones cool, while the world-class culinary scene welcomes early-dining families with open arms. For a finance dad seeking top-tier comfort without pretension, the city delivers luxury accommodations and easy, paved routes perfect for high-end strollers.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 minute drive from Charleston International Airport (CHS) to downtown hotels via private black car service or Uber Car Seat.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Waterfront Park and Pineapple Fountain

Dip your toddler's toes in the shallow splash pool of the iconic Pineapple Fountain while watching container ships glide past the harbor. The wide, paved boardwalk is perfectly flat for strollers and offers plenty of shaded benches under live oaks.

Midday / 2h

South Carolina Aquarium

Escape the midday heat to see the two-story Great Ocean Tank and touch shallow-water invertebrates at the toddler-height touch tank. It is fully ADA-accessible with wide ramps, making stroller navigation a breeze.

Morning / 1-2h

Charleston City Market

Stroll the historic covered sheds to watch local sweetgrass basket weavers at work and pick up a unique keepsake for your daughter. Stick to the Great Hall section for air-conditioned comfort and smoother concrete floors under your stroller wheels.

Late Afternoon / 1h

White Point Garden

Let your toddler toddle safely under the canopy of ancient live oaks while you admire the historic antebellum mansions and harbor views. The crushed-shell paths are wide, and the cool sea breeze provides a perfect natural wind-down before afternoon naptime.

Morning / 2-3h

Charles Towne Landing State Historic Site

Walk the fully paved, shaded loop trails to see native wildlife like otters and black bears in the Animal Forest. The replica 17th-century sailing ship provides a great backdrop, and there is ample open grass for a toddler to burn off energy.

Morning / 3h

Magnolia Plantation and Gardens

Board the covered tram tour for an easy, stroller-free ride through some of America's oldest romantic gardens. If walking, the main garden paths are mostly flat dirt, but skip the narrow swamp boardwalks with a stroller and stick to the open meadow areas.

Morning / 2h

Children's Museum of the Lowcountry

Perfect for a rainy day, this museum features a dedicated soft-play 'Baby and Toddler' garden and an interactive water table shaped like the local Cooper River. It is fully stroller-friendly with easy parking and clean family restrooms equipped with changing tables.

Morning / 3h

Folly Beach County Park

Rent a beach chair and umbrella at this county-run park which features outdoor showers, clean restrooms, and a stroller-accessible boardwalk right to the sand. It is much quieter and easier to manage with an infant than the main commercial strip of Folly Beach.

Evening / 1-2h

Shem Creek Park Boardwalk

Walk the wide, flat wooden boardwalk over the marsh to watch local shrimp boats roll in and spot wild bottlenose dolphins swimming in the creek. The entire length is railed and completely stroller-safe, ending at a covered pavilion with cool breezes.

🍽️ Where to eat

Leon's Fine Poultry & Oyster Shop

Order the famous fried chicken and chargrilled oysters in this airy, converted auto body shop. The energetic ambient noise easily drowns out toddler babble, and they open early at 11 AM for an easy lunch before afternoon naptime.

Husk

Experience legendary Southern dining by ordering the slow-smoked heritage pork during their relaxed lunch service. Book the earliest reservation slot; the staff is incredibly accommodating, and the historic space has plenty of room for an tucked-away stroller.

The Daily

Grab a Stumptown espresso and the avocado toast with soft scrambled eggs at this trendy, spacious cafe. It is a highly casual, counter-service spot with wide pathways, making it effortless to navigate with a stroller for an early morning bite.

Cru Cafe

Savor the four-cheese macaroni and cheese and the pan-roasted duck breast in this charming historic home. Request a table on the covered outdoor porch where you have more breathing room for a high chair and a quieter environment for a sleeping baby.

Home Team BBQ

Try the smoked chicken wings with Alabama white sauce and a 'Gamechanger' frozen cocktail for dad. The spacious indoor/outdoor layout, loud music, and highly accommodating staff make it the ultimate stress-free dinner spot for families.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Charleston Place

This ultra-luxury downtown icon features a gorgeous rooftop saltwater pool with a retractable glass roof, perfect for baby swim time in any weather. They provide high-end wooden cribs, bottle warmers upon request, and sit right in the center of the walkable historic district.

The Dewberry Charleston

Located right next to Marion Square, this luxurious hotel offers spacious suites and custom wooden cribs for a perfect nursery setup. The adjacent park is ideal for early morning stroller walks while mom and dad enjoy world-class room service coffee.

Wild Dunes Resort (Sweetgrass Inn)

Located on Isle of Palms, this luxury coastal resort features multi-bedroom condos with full kitchens, a zero-entry family pool, and direct stroller-friendly boardwalks to the beach. It offers the perfect balance of resort amenities and home-like convenience for preparing baby meals.
