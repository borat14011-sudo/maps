Charleston

← back to travel board
Charleston welcomes families with its cobblestone streets, moss-draped oaks, and warm, salty breezes. From splashing in waterfront fountains to exploring historic forts, this coastal gem offers a slow-paced, enchanting escape for all generations. It is a place where history comes alive alongside sandy shores and sweet Southern hospitality.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or rideshare to historic downtown hotels, or ~35 min to Isle of Palms beach rentals.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Waterfront Park & Pineapple Fountain

Splash in the iconic Pineapple Fountain and watch sailboats drift by on the harbor. It is a perfect toll-free energy burner where toddlers can safely wade in the shallow, chlorinated water.

Afternoon / 2-3h

South Carolina Aquarium

Marvel at the two-story Great Ocean Tank and get hands-on at the touch tanks filled with hermit crabs and stingrays. The indoor, air-conditioned spaces offer a welcome respite from the midday Southern heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Charleston City Market

Stroll through this historic four-block market to see local artisans weaving sweetgrass baskets. Toddlers will love the bustling, open-air atmosphere, colorful toy vendors, and sweet treats along the way.

Morning / 3-4h

Folly Beach County Park

Build sandcastles and splash in the gentle waves at this family-friendly beach complete with restrooms and outdoor showers. The wide sandy shoreline is highly stroller-accessible and perfect for toddler naps under a rented umbrella.

Morning / 3h

Magnolia Plantation and Gardens

Walk along romantic boardwalks draped in Spanish moss and visit the petting zoo to feed friendly goats and sheep. Toddlers will love the miniature tram ride that loops through the swamp gardens.

Afternoon / 2-3h

The Children's Museum of the Lowcountry

Let kids run wild in safe, interactive play zones, including a giant pirate ship and a dedicated toddler room for soft play. It is an ideal rainy-day or high-heat escape designed entirely for early childhood play.

Morning / 2-3h

Charles Towne Landing State Historic Site

Explore the birthplace of the Carolinas, featuring shaded paved paths perfect for strollers and an Animal Forest zoo with native species like bears and otters. Toddlers will love boarding the Adventure, a replica 17th-century sailing ship.

Morning / 2h

Cypress Gardens

Glide through a swamp of towering cypress trees on a peaceful, flat-bottomed boat ride where you can spot sunbathing turtles. The butterfly house and swamp-nature center are huge hits for curious little eyes.

Morning / 3h

Patriots Point Naval & Maritime Museum

Board the massive USS Yorktown aircraft carrier to explore real military airplanes parked right on the flight deck. While some steep stairs are tricky, toddlers will love the sheer scale of the ships and the breezy harbor views.

🍽️ Where to eat

Husk

Order the famous Husk cheeseburger or the wood-fired oysters for a gourmet taste of local Southern ingredients in a historic, family-friendly carriage house setting.

Cru Cafe

Dig into their legendary four-cheese macaroni and cheese or the fried green tomato sandwich on the breezy, casual porch.

Leon's Fine Poultry & Oyster Shop

Feast on crispy fried chicken, hushpuppies, and fresh oysters in a spacious, lively garage-turned-eatery that naturally masks noisy toddlers.

Fleet Landing Restaurant

Enjoy classic shrimp and grits while sitting on the waterfront deck watching boats pass by, offering plenty of visual distraction for restless little ones.

Jeni's Splendid Ice Creams

Cool down on King Street with a scoop of kid-favorite Brambleberry Crisp or gooey Buttercake ice cream.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller to navigate Charleston's bumpy, historic cobblestone streets and uneven brick sidewalks. Always pack sunscreen, bug spray, and hats, as the Southern sun and coastal humidity can be intense even during the shoulder seasons.
