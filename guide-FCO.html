Rome

← back to travel board
Rome is a living storybook where cobblestone streets lead to ancient wonders and bustling piazzas. Kids can chase pigeons by grand fountains, while parents marvel at towering monuments from antiquity. It is a sensory feast of warm pizza, cool marble, and endless Italian sunshine.

What to do

Early Morning / 2h

The Colosseum

Step into history at this legendary amphitheater where gladiator tales come to life. Toddlers will find the stone ruins fascinating, but use a baby carrier as strollers are tough to push on the ancient, uneven ground.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Villa Borghese Gardens

Rome's ultimate green escape offers wide paved paths, a toy train, and swan-filled ponds. Rent a family-sized pedal-kart to easily zip around the shaded lawns with tired toddlers.

Early Morning / 1h

Trevi Fountain

Toss a coin over your shoulder to guarantee your return to Rome, a magical ritual kids absolutely adore. Go early in the morning to beat the massive crowds and keep a tight hold of little hands.

Morning / 2h

Explora Children's Museum

A hands-on indoor play space featuring water play features, wind tunnels, and a mini-supermarket for role-playing. There is a dedicated, padded sensory area designed specifically for toddlers under three.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Testaccio Market

An authentic covered neighborhood market where you can sample local street food like suppli and fresh pasta. It is completely stroller-friendly and features a central open seating area perfect for messy toddler lunches.

Morning / 1h

The Pantheon

Look up at the giant open oculus in the dome and watch the sunlight move across the colorful ancient marble floor. The flat, pedestrianized piazza outside is ideal for toddlers to stretch their legs safely before heading inside.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

The Orange Garden

A quiet, fragrant hilltop park filled with bitter orange trees and stunning panoramic views of Rome. The walled garden is safe for running, and toddlers will love peeking through the famous keyhole gate nearby.

Afternoon / 1h

Largo di Torre Argentina Cat Sanctuary

See ancient Roman ruins that now double as a protected sanctuary for dozens of roaming rescue cats. Toddlers will love spotting the colorful felines napping on broken marble columns from the secure walkways above.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Piazza Navona

This grand, oval-shaped piazza is alive with street performers, toy vendors, and three magnificent fountains. The entire square is car-free, making it the perfect spot for kids to run around while you enjoy an espresso.

Where to eat

  • Frigidarium — Indulge in artisanal gelato dipped in a signature shell of dark or white chocolate shell that kids will love cracking open.
  • Emma — This spacious, welcoming pizzeria serves thin-crust Roman pizzas and crispy suppli that satisfy both picky toddlers and foodie parents.
  • Forno Campo de' Fiori — Grab a slice of classic 'pizza bianca'—warm, salted flatbread freshly baked and perfect for an easy, on-the-go toddler snack.
  • Giolitti — Rome’s oldest gelato parlor offers a classic, old-world vibe with dozens of colorful, kid-friendly flavors to choose from.
👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a lightweight, sturdy stroller to tackle Rome's bumpy cobblestones, but always pack a baby carrier for navigating ancient archaeological sites.
