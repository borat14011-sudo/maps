What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Côte Sauvage (Wild Coast)

Explore the rugged, windswept cliffs and watch dramatic waves crash against the rocks. Toddlers should be kept safely in a carrier here due to the open paths and strong winds.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Grande Plage de Quiberon

Relax on this wide, south-facing sandy beach right in the heart of town. The gentle shoreline and soft sand are perfect for toddlers building their first sandcastles.

Afternoon / 50 mins

Le Petit Train de Quiberon

Hop aboard this tourist train for a scenic loop around the town and the Wild Coast. It's a lifesaver for tired toddler legs and offers a fun, breezy ride.

Morning / 1-2h

Pointe du Conguel

Walk to the very tip of the peninsula to enjoy panoramic views of the islands. The flat, stroller-friendly gravel paths make this an effortless nature walk with young children.

Morning / 30 mins

Château Turpault

Admire this stunning 19th-century Anglo-Medieval castle standing guard over the ocean. While the interior is private, toddlers will love watching the ocean spray dramatically near its base.

Morning / 1-2h

Quiberon Weekly Market

Wander through the bustling stalls at Place du Varquez to pick up local cheeses, fresh berries, and warm pastries. Toddlers will enjoy the lively sights, sounds, and sweet bakery smells.

Afternoon / 1h

Port Maria

Stroll along the busy fishing harbor and watch the colorful boats unload their daily catch. It's a great spot for toddlers to watch maritime activity and wave at the passing ferries.

Afternoon / 1h

La Maison d'Armorine

Visit this legendary sweet shop to see how traditional Breton caramels and Niniches lollipops are made. Toddlers will be spellbound by the sugary aromas and colorful candy displays.

Morning / 2-3h

Plage du Conguel

This sheltered beach on the eastern side of the peninsula is protected from heavy waves. It's the ultimate spot for toddlers to safely splash in the calm, shallow waters.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Moulerie de la Baie

Order the classic moules marinière cooked in white wine and shallots, served with a mountain of toddler-approved crispy fries.

Crêperie du Vieux Port

Try the savory buckwheat galette complete with ham, cheese, and egg, followed by a sweet salted butter caramel crêpe for the little ones.

Le Fisher

Get the fresh, locally caught sea bass or their excellent, kid-friendly cod fish and chips.

La Mare aux Canards

Savor their traditional galettes or the tender duck breast, served in a cozy, exceptionally family-welcoming atmosphere.

Glacier L'Igloo

Treat the family to homemade artisanal ice cream, with the salted butter caramel being an absolute must-try.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a windbreaker and layers for everyone, as the coastal breeze can change from warm to chilly in minutes. Rent a beach wagon or a sturdy all-terrain stroller, as some sandy paths near the coves can be tough to navigate with standard wheels.
