🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi or rental car drive from Marseille Provence Airport (MRS) to Aix-en-Provence hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Pont du Gard

Marvel at this massive Roman aqueduct where kids can paddle in the shallow, gentle Gardon River. It is highly stroller-friendly with plenty of shade and wide-open park spaces for toddlers to safely roam.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Carrières des Lumières

Walk through a cool, subterranean limestone quarry filled with giant, moving art projections set to music. It is sensory heaven for toddlers, who can dance in the colorful lights while escaping the midday heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Sénanque Abbey

See the iconic 12th-century stone abbey nestled in vibrant purple lavender fields. While the interior requires quiet, the outdoor paths are perfect for a sensory stroll sniffing the fragrant blooms.

Morning / 2-3h

Palais des Papes & Pont d'Avignon

Explore Europe's largest Gothic palace and sing the famous nursery rhyme on the historic half-bridge. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles in the massive, car-free palace square right outside.

Morning / 1-2h

Le Sentier des Ocres

Walk through striking orange and red clay cliffs on an easy, kid-friendly loop trail that feels like a natural playground. Toddlers will love the vibrant colors, but wear old shoes as the ochre dust stains easily.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue Market

Wander along sparkling canals filled with mossy, spinning waterwheels and vibrant food stalls. Toddlers will enjoy feeding the local ducks and sampling fresh strawberries and soft artisanal cheeses.

Morning / 2h

Château des Baux-de-Provence

Explore the dramatic ruins of a medieval fortress perched high on a rock with spectacular valley views. Toddlers can run safely along the grassy plateaus and gaze at the giant medieval siege engines.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Musée du Bonbon Haribo

Discover the sweet history of the world's most famous gummy candies in a colorful, highly interactive museum. Toddlers will love the giant candy replicas, sensory guessing games, and the sweet treats at the end.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Valensole Plateau Lavender Fields

Drive through endless fields of purple lavender and bright yellow sunflowers that stretch to the horizon. It is a fantastic spot for iconic family photos and a gentle, fragrant wander through nature's colors.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cantine de l'Alchimiste

Grab the seasonal, market-fresh plat du jour and a fresh fruit juice while your little ones relax in the casual, welcoming outdoor courtyard in Aix-en-Provence.

Le Bistro du Paradou

Enjoy their legendary, family-style single menu featuring classic roasted chicken or lamb, followed by an epic, kid-friendly selection of local cheeses.

La Maison de Celou

Order the slow-cooked beef daube or fresh pasta while the kids play safely in the beautiful, spacious garden setting of this family-friendly gem.

Chez Serge

Try the freshly baked artisanal pizzas or the decadent fresh truffle pasta, both of which are massive crowd-pleasers for adults and toddlers alike.

Le Jardin du Quai

Feast on a surprise daily market-fresh menu in a lush, hidden garden where kids can stretch their legs and explore between delicious courses.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a compact car with a pre-booked car seat, as navigating narrow village streets and finding parking is much easier with a smaller vehicle. Always carry a lightweight, sturdy stroller with all-terrain wheels for the bumpy cobblestones, alongside a baby carrier for steep castle ruins.
