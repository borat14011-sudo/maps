Safe, colorful colonial magic for the whole family

Mérida

← back to travel board
Mérida charms families with its pastel-hued colonial streets, lush plazas, and rich Mayan heritage. As one of Mexico's safest cities, it offers gentle adventures from horse-drawn carriage rides to shady, shallow cenotes. Here, local life naturally spills into the warm evening air, making every day feel like a relaxed, toddler-friendly celebration.

What to do

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Plaza Grande & Sunday Mérida en Domingo

This vibrant main square comes alive on Sundays with music, folk dancing, and local food stalls. Toddlers will love chasing pigeons under the shade of giant laurel trees while parents enjoy the festive, car-free atmosphere.

Afternoon / 2h

Gran Museo del Mundo Maya

A world-class museum dedicated to Mayan culture, featuring striking modern architecture and interactive exhibits. The spacious, air-conditioned halls provide a perfect, cool escape for stroller pushing when the afternoon heat peaks.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Horse-Drawn Carriage Ride on Paseo de Montejo

Clip-clop down Mérida’s grandest boulevard in a traditional 'calesina' carriage lined with beautiful 19th-century mansions. It is a breezy, low-effort sightseeing option that toddlers will find absolutely magical.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Zoológico Centenario

A beloved local park featuring a free zoo, leafy playgrounds, and a nostalgic miniature train. Keep toddlers happily entertained with a ride on 'El Trenecito' for just a few pesos while enjoying the deep shade of the trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Lucas de Gálvez

A bustling, sensory-rich traditional market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and fragrant food stalls. Walk the wider outer aisles with a toddler carrier to easily navigate around the vibrant crowds.

Morning / 3-4h

Cenote San Ignacio

A shallow, enclosed cenote located just outside the city with clear, calm waters and excellent facilities. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow entry areas or play in the on-site swimming pool and manicured garden.

Saturday Night / 1h

Pok-ta-Pok Mayan Ball Game Reenactment

Watch a free, dramatic historical reenactment of the ancient Mayan ball game right in front of the main cathedral. The live drums and fiery ball will captivate older toddlers, though it is best to sit near the back for an easy exit if the noise gets too loud.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Parque de las Américas

A massive, beautifully designed park spanning four blocks with Mayan-style fountains, a dedicated enclosed playground, and kids' rental cars. In the evening, parents can snack on marquesitas while toddlers burn off energy on the wide plazas.

Morning / 1-2h

Quinta Montes Molina

A stunningly preserved Porfirian-era mansion that offers a glimpse into Mérida's golden age. The lush, tropical gardens surrounding the villa are peaceful and offer a quiet, shaded space for a slow-paced toddler stroll.

Where to eat

  • Sopa de Lima (Lime Soup) — This shredded turkey and crispy tortilla soup in a bright, citrusy broth is comforting, mild, and highly appealing to toddlers.
  • La Chaya Maya — A legendary, welcoming restaurant serving authentic Yucatecan dishes where kids can watch traditional Mayan ladies hand-press corn tortillas.
  • Marquesitas from a Street Cart — These thin, crispy rolled crepes filled with sweet cajeta and savory Edam cheese make the perfect sweet-and-salty handheld snack for little travelers.
  • El Colón Dulcería y Sorbetería — A historic parlor on Paseo de Montejo serving fresh, dairy-free tropical fruit sorbets like coconut and mamey that offer a sweet way to cool down.
👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy baby carrier over a heavy stroller, as Mérida’s historic colonial sidewalks are often narrow, uneven, and high off the road. Schedule your outdoor sightseeing for early morning or late afternoon to avoid the intense midday heat, taking advantage of the city's lively, cool evening culture instead.
