Antigua

← back to travel board
Antigua welcomes families with 365 powdery white-sand beaches, calm turquoise waters, and a laid-back Caribbean rhythm. From historic harbors to gentle wildlife encounters, this island paradise offers a stress-free tropical escape. Children will love splashing in the warm shallows while parents soak in the breathtaking views.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 to 30 min taxi to major beach resorts in Dickenson Bay or Jolly Harbour

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Stingray City Antigua

Feed and swim with gentle stingrays in their natural shallow-water habitat. Toddlers can safely watch and touch from the secure floating platform.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Dickenson Bay

This is the ultimate family-friendly beach, offering calm waters and plenty of shade. Toddlers will love splashing in the virtually wave-free shallows.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Shirley Heights Lookout

Enjoy panoramic harbor views and lively steel pan music during the famous sunset gathering. Go early in the afternoon before the evening crowds to keep it toddler-friendly.

Morning / 2h

Nelson's Dockyard

Explore the historic harbor filled with beautiful yachts and restored 18th-century stone buildings. The wide, paved pathways are highly stroller-friendly.

Morning / 2-3h

Galleon Beach

A tranquil beach known for frequent sea turtle sightings close to the shore. Bring a pop-up beach tent as natural shade is limited here.

Morning / 1h

Devil's Bridge National Park

Marvel at the dramatic natural limestone arch carved by crashing Atlantic waves. Keep toddlers securely in a carrier as there are no safety railings near the cliffs.

Morning / 1-2h

St. John's Public Market

A bustling, colorful sensory experience packed with tropical fruits, spices, and local crafts. Toddlers will love pointing out the bright pineapples, but watch out for crowds.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Ffryes Beach

A picture-perfect beach with powdery soft sand and clear, shallow turquoise water. It is a fantastic, quiet spot for toddlers to build sandcastles without the crowds.

Midday / 1h

Museum of Antigua and Barbuda

Learn about the island's history inside a cool, air-conditioned historical courthouse. The interactive shell displays are a big hit with curious toddlers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shirley Heights Lookout Restaurant

Get the traditional Caribbean jerk chicken during their family-friendly Sunday sunset BBQ.

Ana's on the Beach

Enjoy fresh grilled lobster and wood-fired pizzas right on the soft sand of Dickenson Bay.

Dennis Cocktail Bar & Restaurant

Order the local goat curry or coconut shrimp with a stunning view over Ffryes Beach.

Catherine's Cafe

Savor French bistro-style mahi-mahi in a shady, chic beachside garden perfect for families.

Papa Zouk

Try the legendary pan-fried snapper with creole sauce in a vibrant, casual rum-shack setting.
