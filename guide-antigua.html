Antigua

← back to travel board
Antigua Guatemala blends majestic volcanic backdrops with colonial ruins that double as safe, enclosed grassy spaces for toddlers to explore. The city's slow-paced, pedestrian-friendly walking streets and vibrant culinary scene offer a rich, stress-free escape for parents craving culture without long-haul flights from Texas. From lush gardens hidden behind heavy wooden doors to warm, welcoming locals who adore children, it is an incredibly manageable slice of Central America.
🚕 Getting there: ~1 hour to 1.5 hours private shuttle transfer from La Aurora International Airport (GUA) in Guatemala City to your hotel in Antigua.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Hill of the Cross (Cerro de la Cruz)

Take a paved, stroller-friendly path up to this iconic viewpoint for a sweeping look at the city layout and Agua Volcano. Go early in the morning to beat the midday heat and catch the clearest skies for family photos.

Morning / 1-2h

Ruins of San Francisco El Grande

The expansive grounds feature massive, walled grassy lawns where your toddler can safely run free among dramatic colonial stone arches. It is much quieter than other ruins, offering a peaceful sanctuary for an outdoor diaper change or a shaded rest.

Afternoon / 1h

ChocoMuseo Antigua

Enjoy a hands-on, 45-minute mini chocolate workshop that is sensory-friendly for toddlers and engaging for parents. The colonial courtyard has plenty of space to sit and sip cacao tea while the little one plays.

Midday / 2-3h

Caoba Farms Restaurant and Market

This organic farm features a massive, baby-friendly outdoor garden, a small farm animal sanctuary, and a shaded playground. It is a wonderful, open-air escape from the tight cobblestone streets where parents can eat while kids explore.

Morning / 2h

Valhalla Macadamia Farm

Located just outside town, this peaceful farm offers free tours, macadamia oil skin massages, and pancake breakfasts in an open garden. The flat, shaded dirt paths are very easy to navigate with a carrier or heavy-duty stroller.

Afternoon / 1h

Parque Central

The leafy heart of Antigua is perfect for people-watching, buying fresh fruit, and letting your toddler watch the central fountain. Local families gather here, creating a warm atmosphere with street vendors selling colorful balloons that will keep your little one captivated.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de Artesanías at El Carmen Ruins

Browse through colorful textiles, wooden toys, and local crafts set against the dramatic, earthquake-damaged facade of El Carmen Church. The stalls are closely packed, so leave the stroller behind and use a baby carrier for this sensory-rich walk.

Morning / 1-2h

Convent of Las Capuchinas

This unique circular convent features beautiful subterranean chambers with incredible acoustics and a massive central courtyard garden. The wide, flat stone paths are easy for toddlers to toddle along safely away from street traffic.

Morning / 3-4h

Hobbitenango

A whimsical eco-park styled like a hobbit village nestled high in the mountains with incredible volcano views and green lawns. While the shuttle ride up is bumpy, the magical houses and wide-open play areas make it a fantastic half-day family adventure.

🍽️ Where to eat

Hector's Bistro

Famous for its upscale French-Guatemalan fusion, this restaurant features a spacious, shaded garden courtyard that absorbs toddler noise perfectly. Order the coq au vin and let your child enjoy the secure, garden-like atmosphere.

Saberico

Step through a narrow doorway into a massive, magical garden oasis with paths and hammocks that are great for an active toddler. The signature pepian (a rich Guatemalan chicken stew) is outstanding, and the early opening hours are a lifesaver.

Fridas

A vibrant, festive Mexican-themed spot right by the Santa Catalina Arch that welcomes families with open arms and colorful decor. Their tacos are stellar, the atmosphere is loud enough to drown out any toddler cries, and they provide sturdy high chairs.

Mesón Panza Verde

Perfect for a high-end date-night feel with the baby in tow during their early dinner hours, featuring a gorgeous, tranquil courtyard with a fountain. Parents can enjoy excellent steak and wine while the soothing sound of trickling water keeps the baby calm.

Fernando's Kaffee

A beloved local café serving some of the best coffee and house-roasted chocolate in town within a peaceful, cat-friendly courtyard. The vibe is incredibly relaxed, making it a stress-free spot for an early morning breakfast of huevos divorciados with a toddler.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Casa Santo Domingo

A luxury museum-hotel built inside a historic convent with expansive, flat cobblestone grounds, beautiful gardens with macaws, and a heated pool. They provide high-quality cribs, and the spacious layout means you can easily push a stroller directly to your room.

Porta Hotel Antigua

Extremely family-friendly, featuring a dedicated kids' club, a playground, two pools (including a shallow toddler area), and large grassy lawns. The rooms are spacious enough for a pack-and-play, and the staff are incredibly attentive to young families.

El Convento Boutique Hotel

An upscale, quiet oasis featuring large suites with private fireplaces and outdoor patios perfect for putting down a sleeping infant. Located on a quieter street, it offers high-end cribs and a peaceful central courtyard to decompress after a day of exploring.
