What to do

Early morning 9–11am / 2h

Shirley Heights Lookout

Breathtaking panoramic views of English Harbour and the Caribbean Sea. Stroller-accessible paved path; go early morning (9-11am) for cooler temps and fewer crowds. Skip the Sunday BBQ party – too loud for baby.

Mid-morning 10am–12pm / 2h

Nelson's Dockyard National Park

Historic Georgian naval dockyard with stroller-friendly walkways, shaded verandas, and a charming museum. Calm, flat terrain perfect for a leisurely family stroll. Baby can explore the grassy parade ground.

Late morning 10am–1pm / 3h

Half Moon Bay

A crescent of soft white sand and gentle waves – one of Antigua's most protected swimming coves. Shallow, clear water ideal for baby's first dip. Bring a pop-up tent for shade. Limited facilities; pack snacks.

Morning tour 9am–12pm / 3h

Stingray City Antigua

Interactive sandbar encounter with friendly southern stingrays in waist-deep water. Calm, supervised environment – staff hold rays for gentle touches. Suitable for babies in a carrier; avoid if your infant is startled by splashing. Life vests provided.

Late afternoon 4–5:30pm / 1.5h

Devil's Bridge National Park

Natural limestone arch carved by Atlantic waves. Dramatic blowholes and coastal views. The stroller path is gravel; better to wear baby in a carrier. Skip on windy days – salt spray can be intense for little ones.

Morning 9am–12pm / 3h

Valley Church Beach

Secluded stretch of calm, shallow water backed by palm trees. Gentle slope entry, minimal waves – perfect for baby's first beach play. A few beach bars offer shade and highchairs. Arrive early for parking.

Flexible / 1–2h for trail

Antigua Rainforest Canopy Tour

Zipline course through tropical canopy. Skip for baby – but the adjacent nature trail is stroller-friendly with birdwatching and shaded benches. Ideal for a short family walk while one parent does the zipline.

Afternoon 2–5pm / 3h

Fort James Beach

Historic fort ruins with a wide, flat beach and calm water near the cruise port. Stroller-friendly boardwalk along the sand. Shallow swimming area, lifeguards on duty. Great for a low-key afternoon.

Morning 9–11am / 2h

Betty's Hope Sugar Plantation

Restored 17th-century sugar mill and museum. Open grounds, grassy paths suitable for strollers. Interpretive signs and windmill ruins. Limited shade – go early and bring sun hats. Baby can crawl on lawn.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sheer Rocks

Perched on cliffs at Cocos Hotel, offering contemporary Caribbean tapas and sunset views. Highchair available, outdoor terrace with sea breeze. Calm atmosphere early evening; no minimum age. Signature lobster tacos.

The Coconut Grove

Beachfront at Dickenson Bay with a laid-back vibe. Kids' menu, highchairs, shaded sand area. Grilled snapper and rum punch for parents. Early seating (5:30pm) recommended to avoid loud music later.

Papa Zouk

Creole and French Caribbean dishes in a garden setting. Highchairs, stroller-friendly ramp, and a separate children's play corner. Signature bouillabaisse and coconut shrimp. Reservations essential.

Catherine's Cafe

Chic French cafe at English Harbour with waterfront terrace. Highchairs, relaxed vibe, and excellent pain au chocolat. Perfect for a light lunch; baby can nap in stroller under shade. Open 8am–4pm.

Le Bistrot de la Mer

French–Caribbean bistro in Jolly Harbour with air-conditioned indoor seating and a quiet outdoor courtyard. Highchairs, changing table in restroom. Escargot and grilled mahi-mahi. Early dinner (6pm) avoids crowds.
