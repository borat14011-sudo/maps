What to do

Morning 8:30-10:30am

Cable Beach

This world-famous stretch offers incredibly calm, shallow turquoise waters and soft sand, making it perfect for dipping baby Mary Claire's toes. Abundant shade from beachfront palms and rentable umbrellas keeps infants safe from the midday sun.

Late morning 11am-12:30pm

The Current: Baha Mar Gallery and Art Center

A quiet, air-conditioned sanctuary showcasing spectacular Bahamian art. The spacious, flat gallery layout is highly stroller-accessible, offering a peaceful environment perfect for a morning stroller nap while parents browse.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Marina Village

A colorful, pedestrian-only outdoor market featuring paved, level walkways that are incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller. Stroll past luxury yachts and enjoy live, gentle music in a vibrant yet family-friendly atmosphere.

Morning 9-11am

Ardastra Gardens & Wildlife Conservation Centre

This tranquil boutique zoo features shaded pathways winding through lush tropical foliage. The quiet atmosphere and signature marching flamingos provide gentle sensory entertainment that will not overwhelm a baby.

Morning 9-11:30am

Clifton Heritage National Park

A peaceful coastal park offering historical ruins and quiet walking paths. The shaded coppice forest trails are excellent for a scenic carrier walk, ending at a calm beach area with gentle surf.

Early morning 8-9:30am

Nassau Straw Market

This historic downtown market is best visited early in the morning before the crowds arrive. To navigate the bustling aisles easily, use a baby carrier instead of a bulky stroller to shop for hand-woven souvenirs.

Morning 9-10am

Queen's Staircase

While the 66 steps themselves are not stroller-friendly, the shady, limestone-carved canyon leading to the base of the staircase is incredibly cool, breezy, and perfect for a carrier-bound contact nap.

Day trip 9am-3pm

Blue Lagoon Island

Take a smooth, scenic ferry ride to this private island paradise. The island features highly sheltered, shallow lagoons with virtually zero waves, offering a safe, tranquil environment for infant water play under the shade of mature palms.

Morning 9-10:30am

Junkanoo Beach

Located close to downtown, this lively beach features a flat, paved boardwalk running alongside it. It is ideal for an easy, breezy stroller walk with ocean views without having to push wheels through deep sand.

🍽️ Where to eat

Carmine's

Located in Marina Village, this bustling Italian restaurant serves family-style portions like their signature Penne Alla Vodka. The cheerful, lively atmosphere easily masks any infant noise, and they happily provide sturdy highchairs.

Yellowbell

A sophisticated poolside dining option at The Island House, offering a refined Yellowbell Lobster Roll. The sun-soaked, breezy outdoor patio is highly accommodating for families, featuring comfortable seating and a relaxed daytime pace.

The Poop Deck

An iconic waterfront dining institution famous for fresh Bahamian cracked conch. The open-air, harbor-view deck is breezy and casual, making early dinner seatings stress-free with a baby.

Graycliff Restaurant

While the indoor dining rooms are formal, their historic, lush garden terrace offers a peaceful outdoor setting. Book an early lunch reservation to enjoy their premium Chateaubriand while baby sleeps in a stroller nearby.

Cafe Martinique

A sophisticated French-Chic dining destination in Marina Village serving local lobster dishes. Request an early outdoor patio table to enjoy a luxury dining experience in a quieter, highly accommodating open-air setting.
