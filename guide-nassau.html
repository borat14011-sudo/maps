Nassau

← back to travel board
Nassau welcomes families with its vibrant candy-colored architecture, swaying palms, and some of the calmest turquoise waters in the Caribbean. From the gentle rhythm of steel drum bands to the historic fort ruins, this tropical paradise offers an effortless blend of adventure and relaxation. It is a sunny playground where both energetic older kids and toddlers can find magic around every corner.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 minutes by taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to Cable Beach and Paradise Island hotels

What to do

Morning / 2h

Ardastra Gardens & Wildlife Conservation Centre

Get up close with marching pink flamingos and hand-feed colorful rainbow lorikeets in these lush tropical gardens. It is highly stroller-accessible and perfect for toddlers to safely wander and see exotic animals up close.

Full Day / 5-6h

Aquaventure at Atlantis Paradise Island

Experience this world-renowned water park featuring thrilling slides, a mile-long lazy river, and spectacular marine habitats. For toddlers, the Splashers water playground offers gentle slides, water cannons, and shallow wading pools.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Pirates of Nassau Museum

Walk through a replica pirate ship and learn about the real golden age of piracy in this interactive indoor museum. Toddlers will love the vibrant wax figures, though some dark, atmospheric corners might require holding them close.

Half Day / 4h

Blue Lagoon Island

Take a scenic boat ride to a private island to swim in a calm, wave-free lagoon and meet friendly dolphins. The gentle, shallow sandy shore is an absolute dream for toddlers to safely wade and play.

Morning / 1h

The Queen's Staircase & Fort Fincastle

Climb the historic 66 steps carved out of solid limestone by slaves in the late 18th century, leading up to a small hilltop fort. Skip the stroller and use a baby carrier here, as the stairs and fort pathways are uneven.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Arawak Cay (The Fish Fry)

Immerse yourselves in Bahamian culture at this vibrant strip of colorful seaside restaurants serving fresh seafood. Toddlers will enjoy the upbeat music and outdoor seating, making it easy to manage any table-side squirming.

Morning / 2-3h

Junkanoo Beach

A lively, easily accessible public beach close to downtown with calm, shallow waters and plenty of shade. Toddlers can safely splash in the gentle surf while parents enjoy fresh coconut water from nearby beach shacks.

Morning / 2-3h

Clifton Heritage National Park

Explore winding nature trails, historic ruins, and beautiful secluded beaches. While snorkeling is great for older kids, toddlers will love spotting hermit crabs and lizards along the shady woodland paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Nassau Straw Market

Browse a bustling covered market filled with hand-woven straw baskets, wooden toys, and local crafts. Toddlers will be fascinated by the vibrant sights, but keep them close as the narrow aisles can get crowded.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Poop Deck (at Sandyport)

Order the freshly caught grilled snapper or cracked conch right on the beach where kids can play in the sand while you wait.

Athena Cafe & Bar

Head upstairs to this lively Greek spot in downtown Nassau for delicious chicken souvlaki and honey-drizzled loukoumades that toddlers adore.

Graycliff Restaurant

Indulge in an upscale family lunch in a historic mansion, followed by a visit to the on-site chocolatier for delicious hand-spun treats.

McKenzie's Fresh Conch Salad

Try the legendary, freshly made-to-order conch salad or mild fried fish sticks at this casual, open-air stand beneath the Paradise Island bridge.

Wild Thyme

Dine in a beautiful, breezy garden courtyard on local favorites like Bahamian lobster mac and cheese, featuring plenty of space for restless toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep a lightweight baby carrier handy for historic sites like the Queen's Staircase where strollers cannot navigate the steps. Always pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a high-SPF sun hat for your toddler, as the Bahamian sun is exceptionally strong even on breezy days.
