Buenos Aires captivates with its grand European architecture, leafy boulevards, and a passionate, late-night culture that surprisingly embraces children with open arms. Strolling through the elegant parks of Palermo and Recoleta reveals a city that moves to a sophisticated yet leisurely rhythm. For families traveling with an infant, the wide, stroller-friendly paved pathways of the Parque Tres de Febrero and the modern docks of Puerto Madero offer seamless navigation. From private tango salons to quiet shaded plazas, the city blends high-end finance-district sophistication with incredibly warm, baby-friendly hospitality.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Parque Tres de Febrero

Stroll along the wide, paved paths of this expansive park, admiring the rose garden and tranquil lakes. It is entirely stroller-friendly, offering plenty of shade under grand jacaranda trees for an outdoor bottle feeding.

Late Morning 10:30 AM - 12:30 PM / 2h

Jardín Japonés

A beautifully manicured, calm sanctuary featuring gentle streams, koi ponds, and smooth wooden bridges. It offers an incredibly quiet atmosphere ideal for a morning stroller nap, with clean, accessible family restrooms.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM / 1.5h

El Ateneo Grand Splendid

Visit one of the world's most beautiful bookstores, housed inside a magnificent historic theater. The wide carpeted ramp access and elevators make navigating with an infant stroller easy, and the quiet acoustics of the book-lined walls are remarkably soothing.

Morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM / 1.5h

Recoleta Cemetery

Explore the fascinating maze of ornate marble mausoleums. While some pathways are slightly uneven, the main thoroughfares are wide enough for a sturdy stroller, and the quiet, reverent atmosphere is perfect for keeping baby calm.

Early Afternoon 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM / 2h

Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes

An air-conditioned haven of fine arts featuring works by Argentine and European masters. Elevators and wide corridors offer seamless stroller access, and the cool, spacious galleries provide a perfect respite from the afternoon heat.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:30 PM / 2h

Puerto Madero Waterfront Promenade

Walk along the wide, pedestrian-only docks flanking the canal. The flat, brick-paved promenade is perfect for a smooth stroller ride, offering refreshing river breezes and beautiful views of the Puente de la Mujer.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Plaza Francia

Relax on the grassy lawns of this elegant Recoleta square. It is a fantastic spot to spread out a luxury travel blanket for Mary Claire to have some tummy time while enjoying the shade of the historic Gomero de la Recoleta rubber tree.

Sunday Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Feria de San Telmo

Go early to avoid the massive crowds and experience the vibrant antique market. While cobblestones require a sturdy stroller, arriving right at opening allows you to enjoy the street tango dancers and live music before the streets fill up.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Usina del Arte

A beautifully restored electric power plant turned arts center that features 'iUpiii', the city's first cultural space specifically designed for babies aged 0-3. It offers soft play areas, sensory stimulation, and private nursing rooms in a gorgeous, modern setting.

🍽️ Where to eat

Don Julio

Argentina's world-famous parrilla. Book the very first lunch seating at 12:00 PM to beat the crowds, where the staff warmly welcomes families with highchairs and offers succulent, tender cuts of grass-fed beef that can easily be shared.

La Cabrera

An upscale Palermo steakhouse known for its exceptionally generous portions and warm family atmosphere. They provide highchairs, toy bags for young children, and a lively, buzzy environment where a slightly fussy baby will go unnoticed.

Fervor

An elegant, classic Recoleta dining room specializing in dry-aged beef and fresh grilled seafood. Request a spacious booth table on the ground floor for easy infant seat placement, and enjoy their early 7:30 PM dinner service.

Croque Madame (Palacio Errázuriz)

Dine on the stunning outdoor gravel terrace of the National Museum of Decorative Arts. The beautiful garden setting is perfect for stroller parking, allowing Mary Claire to sleep soundly under the trees while you enjoy gourmet pastries and espresso.

Rapanui (Recoleta)

Indulge in legendary Argentine gelato and chocolates in a spacious, air-conditioned parlor. The Recoleta location is highly accessible, features highchairs, and offers a sweet, sensory-rich stop for parents and baby alike.
