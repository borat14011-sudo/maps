Buenos Aires

← back to travel board
With its wide, leafy avenues, colorful street art, and world-class parks, Buenos Aires welcomes families with open arms and a relaxed tempo. Children are celebrated in every neighborhood, making it easy to wander past historic plazas and tango-filled streets. Parents will love the late-afternoon cafe culture, while little ones stay captivated by the city's whimsical energy.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min private transfer or taxicab from Ministro Pistarini International Airport (Ezeiza) to Palermo hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Mayo & Casa Rosada

The historic heart of the city features the famous pink government house and a spacious plaza perfect for chasing pigeons. Toddlers will love the wide-open pedestrian spaces and the colorful ceremonial guards.

Afternoon / 2h

Jardín Japonés (Japanese Gardens)

A serene oasis in Palermo with red bridges, koi ponds, and lush paths. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by feeding the massive, colorful fish from the wooden bridges.

Morning / 1-2h

Caminito in La Boca

A bright, open-air street museum filled with pastel-painted buildings, street tango dancers, and giant puppets. It is highly visual for toddlers, though best visited in the morning before the heavy crowds arrive.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Museo de los Niños (Abasto Shopping)

An incredible indoor interactive museum designed as a mini-city where kids can play doctor, baker, or bus driver. It is an absolute paradise for toddlers on a rainy or hot day.

Late Morning / 2h

San Telmo Market

A bustling historic indoor market filled with antiques, fresh produce, and delicious food stalls. Toddlers will enjoy the sensory sights and grabbing a fresh empanada or pastry.

Morning / 2-3h

Bosques de Palermo (Palermo Woods)

A massive green space featuring paddle boats, a beautiful rose garden, and endless paved paths. Toddlers can run freely on the grass or watch ducks splash in the large lakes.

Afternoon / 2h

Centro Cultural Recoleta

A vibrant cultural space with rotating kid-friendly art installations, a dedicated playroom, and colorful courtyards. Toddlers will love the dedicated sensory play areas and interactive exhibits.

Late Morning / 1h

El Ateneo Grand Splendid

A breathtaking bookstore housed inside a grand historic theater, complete with a stage café. The dedicated children's section in the basement has plenty of space for toddlers to browse colorful Spanish picture books.

Morning / 2-3h

Costanera Sur Ecological Reserve

A massive riverside nature reserve right next to Puerto Madero, perfect for spotting wild birds and lizards. Rent a family surrey bike with a toddler seat to breeze through the shady paths easily.

🍽️ Where to eat

Don Julio

Get the legendary, ultra-tender grass-fed bife de lomo (tenderloin) alongside crispy french fries that kids will devour.

La Mezzetta

Order a thick slice of their famous, cheese-stuffed fugazzetta pizza, which is gooey, mild, and a massive hit with toddlers.

El Sanjuanino

Get the traditional baked beef empanadas and sweet corn humitas, perfect hand-held bites for little hands.

Rapanui

Indulge in the Franui (chocolate-covered frozen raspberries) and a scoop of their ultra-creamy dulce de leche ice cream.

Café Tortoni

Order the classic churros with hot dipping chocolate inside this historic, beautifully preserved 19th-century cafe.

👨‍👩‍👧 Porteños love children, so expect warm welcomes at restaurants even late at night, though dining early around 8 PM helps beat the local dinner rush. Cobblestone streets in historic neighborhoods can be bumpy, so bring a lightweight, sturdy stroller or a baby carrier for easier navigating.
