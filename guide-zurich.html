Zürich

← back to travel board
Zürich blends high-end Swiss efficiency with serene lakeside living, making it an incredibly low-stress destination for parents managing nap schedules and strollers. The city's pristine public transit, impeccably clean streets, and abundant green spaces ensure a seamless experience from morning coffee to early bedtime. From gentle lakeside strolls to safe, hyper-clean play areas, you get European charm without any of the logistical headaches found in older, more chaotic European capitals.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 minute taxi or a direct 12-minute train ride on the SBB line from Zürich Airport to the city center.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Zoologischer Garten Zürich

This world-class zoo features the massive, indoor Masoala Rainforest dome, which is perfect for keeping a toddler warm and entertained on a cool Swiss day. The paved, stroller-friendly paths and excellent nursing stations make it incredibly stress-free for parents.

Morning / 2h

Uetliberg Mountain

Take the S10 train up Zürich’s home mountain for panoramic views of the lake and Alps without the exhausting hike. The path from the station to the Uto Kulm hotel is fully stroller-accessible, though there is a slight incline.

Afternoon / 1.5-2h

Zürichhorn Park

This sprawling lakeside park offers wide paved paths for stroller walks and a fantastic playground right by the water. There is plenty of flat, manicured grass for your toddler to crawl or practice walking safely away from traffic.

Afternoon / 2h

Lindt Home of Chocolate

Located in Kilchberg, this interactive museum features a massive 9-meter chocolate fountain that will mesmerize your little one. The entire facility is highly modern, completely step-free, and offers spacious elevator access for strollers.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Lake Zürich Boat Cruise (ZSG)

Board a classic steamboat or modern motor vessel from Bürkliplatz for a gentle, breezy ride across the glassy lake. The indoor cabins are spacious enough to park your stroller, providing a quiet, climate-controlled space if your toddler needs to nap.

Morning / 2h

Landesmuseum Zürich

This castle-like museum houses Switzerland's cultural history and features an incredibly spacious, stroller-friendly layout with ramps and elevators throughout. The inner courtyard is a peaceful, car-free zone where your toddler can stretch their legs securely.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Rieterpark

Zürich's largest landscaped park surrounds the Rietberg Museum, offering mature beech trees, winding stroller-friendly paths, and quiet shaded spots perfect for an outdoor picnic. It is far less crowded than the lakeside parks, ensuring a tranquil atmosphere for an afternoon nap in the stroller.

Morning / 2h

Gemeinschaftszentrum Wipkingen (GZ Wipkingen)

This vibrant community center features a lovely petting farm with goats, sheep, and chickens that will delight a curious toddler. It boasts a safe, enclosed playground and an on-site café where parents can grab a flat white while supervising.

Morning / 1-2h

Bürkliplatz Market

Held on Tuesdays and Fridays, this vibrant lakeside market is filled with colorful flowers, fresh Swiss cheeses, and local fruits. It is highly stroller-accessible and allows you to experience local Swiss life at a relaxed, slow pace.

🍽️ Where to eat

Zeughauskeller

Order the classic Kalbsbratwurst or the slow-cooked marinated beef in this historic, cavernous former armory. The bustling, noisy atmosphere easily drowns out any toddler fussiness, and the staff are highly accommodating with highchairs.

Haus Hiltl

As the world's oldest vegetarian restaurant, its extensive buffet is perfect for picking small, healthy finger foods for a toddler. It is incredibly spacious, highly family-friendly, and offers easy elevator access to all levels.

Conditorei Péclard im Schober

Indulge in their famous thick hot chocolate and handmade cakes in a beautifully ornate parlor. Go early in the morning when it is quiet to secure a plush corner table with ample space to park a stroller.

Frau Gerolds Garten

This trendy outdoor urban garden container village in Zürich-West offers a relaxed, casual vibe where you can grab gourmet sausages or fresh salads. The open-air, car-free layout is ideal for a toddler to wiggle around without bothering indoor diners.

Sternen Grill

Grab their legendary St. Galler Bratwurst with a gold-crusted Bürli roll to eat upstairs in the restaurant or take away to the lake. The upstairs seating area is clean, fast-paced, and accustomed to families with young children needing quick service.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Dolder Grand

Nestled in the forest above the city, this legendary resort offers ultra-spacious suites, indoor pools, and top-tier babysitting services. They proactively provide high-end cribs, bottle warmers, and baby toiletries to ensure a seamless stay.

Baur au Lac

Situated in its own private park right by Lake Zürich, this luxury hotel offers immediate lakeside strolls without crossing busy roads. The exceptionally attentive concierge team can easily arrange baby-proofing items, premium cribs, and private transport.

Widder Hotel

Located in the historic Old Town, this design-forward hotel blends historical architecture with modern, spacious lofts that give families room to spread out. It is steps away from quiet pedestrian streets, making early morning stroller walks a breeze.

👨‍👩‍👧 Purchase the Zürich Card for unlimited second-class travel on trams, buses, trains, and boats, as the entire transit system is exceptionally clean, punctual, and mostly low-floor for easy stroller boarding. Tap water in Zürich is some of the cleanest in the world; you can safely fill your baby's bottles directly from any of the city's 1,200+ public drinking fountains. Keep in mind that Swiss shops and supermarkets (except those at the main train station and airport) are strictly closed on Sundays, so stock up on diapers and baby food beforehand.
