Anchorage

← back to travel board
Experience the rugged beauty of Alaska without sacrificing the comforts of home in family-friendly Anchorage. Nestled between towering peaks and the Cook Inlet, this city offers paved, stroller-accessible coastal paths, world-class wildlife viewing, and a thriving culinary scene. It’s the perfect playground for an active Houston dad looking to introduce his toddler to the great outdoors while keeping travel logistics simple and stress-free.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 minute drive via rental car from Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport (ANC) straight to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Alaska Zoo

Wander the shaded, gravel paths of this unique zoo dedicated entirely to sub-Arctic and Arctic wildlife. It’s highly stroller-accessible and the perfect size for a morning walk to see polar bears, wolves, and Siberian tigers up close.

Morning / 2-3h

Anchorage Museum

Head straight to the Discovery Center on the first floor, which features hands-on science exhibits and a dedicated sensory play space for crawlers and toddlers. The entire world-class facility is exceptionally stroller-friendly with wide elevators and clean nursing rooms.

Morning / 1-2h

Potter Marsh Wildlife Viewing Boardwalk

Walk along the 1,500-foot wooden boardwalk over a scenic marsh to spot spawning salmon, nesting bald eagles, and grazing moose. The flat, stroller-friendly boardwalk features safety railings and offers breathtaking, panoramic views of the Chugach Mountains.

Afternoon / 2h

Tony Knowles Coastal Trail at Westchester Lagoon

Stroll along this fully paved coastal trail starting at the lagoon, where your toddler can feed the ducks and play at the modern playground. The wide path is perfect for jogging strollers, offering stunning views of Fire Island and occasionally Mount Denali on clear days.

Morning / 1-2h

Alaska Aviation Museum

Located right on the shores of Lake Hood, the world's busiest seaplane base, this museum lets kids get up close to vintage aircraft. Toddlers will love sitting on the outdoor lawn watching real floatplanes splash down and take off every few minutes.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Glen Alps Overlook

Enjoy majestic, sweeping views of Anchorage and the Cook Inlet without a grueling hike via a short, fully paved stroller-accessible loop. It’s the easiest way to get crisp alpine mountain air and dramatic photos with your baby without leaving the city limits.

Afternoon / 2-3h

H2Oasis Indoor Waterpark

Escape any rainy weather at this massive, warm indoor waterpark featuring a dedicated Pirate Ship kiddie pool with shallow water and small slides. It's an energetic, safe environment where your toddler can splash around and burn off energy.

Morning / 1-2h

Alaska Railroad Anchorage Depot

Visit this historic downtown depot to watch the iconic blue-and-yellow locomotives roll in, or book a short, scenic day trip. The boarding areas are flat and spacious, and the train ride itself is incredibly smooth and mesmerizing for young children.

Morning / 1-2h

Campbell Creek Science Center

Explore gentle, flat dirt trails winding through lush spruce forests where you can easily push an all-terrain stroller. The center itself offers interactive nature displays and clean restrooms, making it a peaceful, low-stress escape into nature.

🍽️ Where to eat

Moose's Tooth Pub & Pizzeria

Order the legendary Avalanche pizza alongside a local craft beer for the parents. The bustling, energetic atmosphere is loud enough to easily drown out any toddler tantrums, and they provide high chairs and exceptionally quick service.

Snow City Cafe

Fuel up with the Ship Creek Benedict or giant stuffed French toast at this vibrant, highly popular breakfast spot. Since they open at 7:00 AM, it is perfect for early-rising toddlers, and the staff is incredibly welcoming to families with strollers.

Bear Tooth Grill

Enjoy fresh wild Alaskan cod tacos in a spacious, modern dining room with plenty of booth seating. It offers a slightly more relaxed but equally family-friendly vibe than its sister restaurant, Moose's Tooth, with plenty of room for diaper bags.

Glacier Brewhouse

Savor spit-roasted meats and fresh alder-grilled salmon in a warm, rustic lodge-like setting. The dining room is large and lively, meaning you won't have to worry about a fussy baby disturbing other diners during an early dinner.

Rustic Goat

Try the famous pecan-crusted halibut cheeks or elk sliders at this stylish, two-story neighborhood eatery. The open, industrial layout provides ample space to maneuver a stroller, and the noise level is consistently family-friendly.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Captain Cook

Anchorage’s premier historic luxury hotel offers spacious multi-room suites ideal for setting up a pack-and-play away from your sleeping area. Families will love the large indoor pool and central downtown location, making it easy to walk to shops and parks.

The Lakefront Anchorage

Situated directly on Lake Hood, this lodge-style hotel allows you to watch floatplanes taxi right past your window. They offer spacious rooms, cribs upon request, and a large lawn area where toddlers can toddle safely under the Alaskan sun.

Sheraton Anchorage Hotel & Spa

This reliable, modern hotel offers quiet, comfortable rooms with excellent blackout curtains to help baby sleep during the midnight sun. It features great crib options, an on-site restaurant, and is located just blocks from the Anchorage Museum.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack high-quality travel blackout shades, as the Alaskan midnight sun can severely disrupt an infant's sleep schedule during the summer months. Rent an all-terrain stroller with large rubber tires rather than a lightweight umbrella stroller to navigate gravel park trails and occasional uneven pavement easily. Always dress your toddler in layers, including a windproof fleece and a warm hat, as coastal winds can cause temperatures to drop rapidly even on sunny days.
