Anchorage

← back to travel board
Nestled between towering peaks and the sparkling Cook Inlet, Anchorage blends rugged Alaskan wilderness with convenient city comforts. It is a magical playground where toddlers can spot moose from paved trails and families can explore ancient glaciers. From vibrant outdoor markets to cozy, kid-friendly eateries, this northern gem offers an unforgettable frontier escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or rideshare to downtown Anchorage hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Alaska Zoo

Walk the shaded, gravel pathways to see orphaned and rehabilitated Arctic animals like polar bears, wolves, and Dall sheep. The wide, flat trails are ideal for strollers, and there is a fantastic playground for restless toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Anchorage Museum & Imaginarium

This world-class museum features hands-on science exhibits, a marine touch tank, and dedicated soft-play areas for toddlers to explore safely. It is the ultimate indoor refuge to spark young imaginations on a rainy day.

Morning / 1-2h

Tony Knowles Coastal Trail

Walk or bike along this breathtaking, fully paved coastal path offering views of Fire Island and Mount Denali. It is completely stroller-friendly with plenty of flat spots for toddlers to safely stretch their legs.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Alaska Wildlife Conservation Center

Drive or walk through this 200-acre sanctuary to see massive wood bison, bears, and moose up close. Toddlers will love the spacious boardwalks and the chance to spot baby animals in the nursery.

Weekend Midday / 1-2h

Anchorage Market

Alaska’s largest open-air market features local crafts, live music, and delicious street food. Toddlers will be entertained by the lively atmosphere and the yummy, kid-friendly snacks on offer.

Morning / 1h

Potter Marsh Bird Sanctuary

Stroll along the 1,500-foot wooden boardwalk over a scenic marsh teeming with migratory birds and spawning salmon. The boardwalk has high protective railings, making it exceptionally safe for curious toddlers.

Afternoon / 3h

H2Oasis Indoor Waterpark

Warm up in this massive indoor water park featuring a lazy river, water slides, and a pirate ship. Toddlers have their own shallow wading pool with gentle mini-slides to splash safely.

Afternoon / 2h

Kincaid Park Outdoor Play

Explore dense forests and rolling hills where you are highly likely to spot a resident moose from a safe distance. The park features a fantastic playground and wide, paved paths suitable for stroller walks.

Morning / 1h

Glen Alps Overlook Loop

Enjoy sweeping views of the city and Chugach Mountains without the strenuous climb by walking this short, paved interpretive loop. It is completely stroller-accessible and offers toddlers a safe taste of alpine tundra.

🍽️ Where to eat

Moose's Tooth Pub & Pizzeria

Order the famous Avalanche pizza and a local root beer in this lively, highly family-friendly joint that easily masks noisy toddlers.

Snow City Cafe

Treat the family to fluffy Sourdough Pancakes or reindeer sausage scrambles at this vibrant, award-winning breakfast spot with a great kids' menu.

Gwennie's Old Alaska Restaurant

Dig into a massive plate of traditional sourdough pancakes surrounded by historic Alaskan gold rush memorabilia that toddlers will find fascinating.

Wild Scoops Ice Cream

Grab a scoop of handmade 'Baked Alaska' ice cream topped with toasted marshmallow fluff, a sweet treat that toddlers will absolutely adore.

Bear Tooth Grill

Enjoy fresh halibut tacos and kid-friendly quesadillas in a spacious, casual dining room designed for families.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack versatile layers and high-quality rain gear, as Anchorage weather can shift rapidly even during the height of summer. Be sure to rent a sturdy, all-terrain stroller to easily navigate gravel nature trails and any unexpected muddy patches.
