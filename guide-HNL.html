Honolulu

← back to travel board
Honolulu cradles families in warmth, blending vibrant Polynesian culture with soft-sand beaches and lush volcanic peaks. From the calm, shallow waters of Waikiki to interactive animal encounters, this island paradise offers an effortless pace for multi-generational travelers. Pack your sunscreen and embrace the spirit of aloha at a toddler's leisurely pace.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Kuhio Beach (Waikiki)

The concrete breakwater creates a giant, tranquil saltwater pool perfect for little splashers. Toddlers can safely wade in the calm, wave-free waters while parents enjoy the classic Diamond Head view.

Morning / 2-3 hours

Honolulu Zoo

Nestled in Queen Kapiʻolani Park, this lush zoo is highly walkable with plenty of shade and a great Keiki Children's Zoo. Toddlers will love the playground and getting close to gentle farm animals.

Early Morning / 3 hours

Hanauma Bay Nature Preserve

This pristine volcanic crater offers shallow reef snorkeling filled with colorful fish right near the shore. A tram is available to transport tired toddlers down and up the steep hill.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Bishop Museum

Hawaii's premier natural and cultural history museum features an interactive science center and a fantastic indoor volcanic eruption model. The spacious manicured lawns are perfect for toddlers to run off energy.

Morning / 2 hours

Lyon Arboretum

Located in the misty Manoa Valley, this botanical garden offers easy, stroller-accessible paved paths through a lush rainforest. Toddlers will be enchanted by the giant tropical leaves and colorful fallen flowers.

Saturday Morning / 1-2 hours

KCC Farmers' Market

This lively Saturday market features fresh local fruits, sweet pastries, and live Hawaiian music. Toddlers will enjoy munching on fresh mango slices while riding in a stroller through the wide aisles.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Hawaii Children's Discovery Center

A world-class indoor play museum where kids can roleplay in a mock Hawaiian town, airplane, and grocery store. It is the ultimate air-conditioned escape for toddlers on a rainy or overly hot day.

Late Afternoon / 2 hours

Ala Moana Beach Park

Offering a calmer, much less crowded alternative to Waikiki, this park features a protective outer reef and a massive grassy park. It is highly stroller-friendly and sits right across from a world-class outdoor shopping mall.

Morning / 2 hours

Hoʻomaluhia Botanical Garden

This breathtaking, dramatic mountain-backed garden features a peaceful lake where kids can feed the fish. The paved paths are highly stroller-accessible, making it an easy nature walk for tiny feet.

Where to eat

  • Helena's Hawaiian Food — This legendary, casual spot serves child-friendly, melt-in-your-mouth kalua pig and sweet poi that kids love to eat with their fingers.
  • Leonard's Bakery — Grab a box of warm, sugar-dusted malasadas that are soft enough for toddlers to easily chew and enjoy.
  • Rainbow Drive-In — A local institution offering classic, kid-approved plate lunches like gravy-smothered Loco Moco and macaroni salad in an easy outdoor seating area.
  • Waiola Shave Ice — Taste the fluffiest shave ice in Honolulu with toddler-approved flavors like cotton candy and strawberry, served in easy-to-hold cups.
