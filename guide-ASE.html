Aspen

← back to travel board
Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Aspen shines as a year-round playground where pristine nature meets vibrant mountain culture. From wildflower-filled meadows to cozy alpine strolls, this historic town offers families a perfect blend of outdoor exploration and gentle, stroller-friendly adventures. Whether riding the gondola or splashing in mountain streams, memories are waiting at every elevation.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Maroon Bells Scenic Area

Marvel at the most photographed peaks in North America, featuring flat, easy walking paths and stunning reflective lakes. Toddler note: The paved trail leading to the lake is stroller-accessible, making it perfect for little legs.

Late Morning / 2h

Silver Queen Gondola

Ride to the top of Aspen Mountain for breathtaking panoramic views, a great playground, and open grassy areas to run around. Toddler note: The smooth, enclosed cabin ride is exciting for toddlers and completely safe.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Aspen Saturday Market

Stroll through this lively market featuring Colorado-grown fruits, artisan crafts, live music, and delicious local snacks. Toddler note: Grab a fresh Palisade peach for a sweet, messy treat that will keep your toddler happy in the stroller.

Afternoon / 1h

John Denver Sanctuary

An oasis of quiet wetlands, engraved rock sculptures, and beautiful perennial gardens next to the Roaring Fork River. Toddler note: Kids love splashing in the shallow, gentle streams and running across the flat grassy lawns.

Morning / 1-2h

Aspen Center for Environmental Studies at Hallam Lake

Discover a 25-acre nature preserve right in downtown, featuring resident birds of prey, indoor exhibits, and easy loop trails. Toddler note: Toddlers will love meeting the resident golden eagle and watching the trout in the indoor pond.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Snowmass Ice Age Discovery Center

Learn about the incredible mastodon and mammoth fossils found nearby through interactive displays and half-scale replicas. Toddler note: There are great hands-on digging stations and coloring activities perfect for curious little hands.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Herron Park

A beautiful neighborhood park bordering the river with an excellent playground, climbing structures, and shallow water entry. Toddler note: The toddler-safe swings and the gentle wading area of the creek are perfect for cooling off on warm days.

Morning / 1-2h

Ashcroft Ghost Town

Step back in time at this preserved silver-mining ghost town featuring easy, flat dirt paths through historic wooden cabins. Toddler note: The wide, flat paths are easy for toddlers to wander, and the old buildings spark great imagination.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Aspen Recreation Center

An amazing indoor facility boasting a lazy river, water slides, a zero-entry pool, and a climbing wall. Toddler note: The dedicated zero-entry toddler pool with interactive water features is perfect for safe, warm indoor splashing.

Where to eat

  • Hickory House Ribs — A casual, family-friendly log cabin restaurant serving up award-winning barbecue, giant portions, and a dedicated kids' menu.
  • Paradise Bakery & Cafe — A beloved Aspen institution perfect for grabbing freshly baked cookies, muffins, and homemade gelato to enjoy on the outdoor patio.
  • CP Burger — An upbeat, casual spot serving delicious burgers, spiked milkshakes for parents, and classic fries next to a mini-golf course in summer or ice rink in winter.
  • Meat & Cheese Restaurant and Farm Shop — An airy eatery offering delicious, high-quality charcuterie boards, artisan sandwiches, and plenty of shareable bites that please both foodies and picky toddlers.
