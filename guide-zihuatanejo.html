Zihuatanejo

← back to travel board
Nestled along a protected Pacific bay, Zihuatanejo pairs traditional Mexican fishing village charm with calm, toddler-friendly waters. Families can wander cobblestone streets shaded by palms and watch fishermen bring in the daily catch. It is the ultimate slow-paced coastal escape where young children can safely splash and play.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min pre-arranged private shuttle or official airport taxi to the main bay hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa La Ropa

This is the premier family beach in the area, boasting soft sand and incredibly gentle, warm waves. Toddlers can safely splash at the shoreline while parents relax under shaded palapas.

Morning / 2h

El Refugio de Potosi

A wonderful ecological reserve and wildlife sanctuary where kids can see coatis, spider monkeys, and colorful native birds. The flat, well-shaded paths are highly accessible for families carrying toddlers.

Morning / 4h

Playa Las Gatas Boat Trip

Take a quick and scenic water taxi from the town pier to this reef-protected beach with virtually no waves. Toddlers will love the brief boat ride, and the shallow tide pools are perfect for spotting tiny fish.

Sunset / 1h

Paseo del Pescador

This charming, pedestrian-only waterfront boardwalk is ideal for a scenic stroll to watch pelicans dive. It is completely stroller-friendly and free of vehicle traffic, making it a stress-free spot for toddlers to walk.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal

A vibrant indoor municipal market bursting with local color, fresh tropical fruits, and hand-painted crafts. Stop by a fruit stall to grab some freshly sliced sweet mango or bananas for an easy toddler snack.

Morning / 3h

Barra de Potosi Lagoon Tour

Board a small, safe panga boat to explore the calm mangrove lagoon filled with herons, egrets, and baby fish. The water here is glassy and still, preventing any motion sickness for young kids.

Early Morning / 1h

Playa Principal Fish Market

Every morning, local fishermen pull their colorful wooden boats right onto the beach sand to sell their fresh catch. Toddlers will be absolutely fascinated by the dozens of friendly pelicans waiting nearby for scraps.

Morning / 4h

Playa Coral on Isla Ixtapa

Take a short, fun ferry ride to this tranquil island beach known for its crystal-clear shallow waters. Before boarding the boat at Playa Linda, toddlers can safely view wild deer roaming in the nearby protected reserve.

Evening / 1-2h

The Zocalo (Plaza Principal)

The town's lively central plaza features a basketball court, space to run, and frequent evening cultural performances. It is a safe, community-focused gathering space where local and visiting kids play together under the lights.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Perla

Sit at a table directly in the sand and order the fresh fish tacos while your toddler safely plays with sand toys at your feet.

Bandido's

Get the sizzling chicken fajitas and cheese quesadillas at this vibrant, festive spot with a highly welcoming family atmosphere.

Carmelitas Cafe

Order the traditional chilaquiles and fluffy sweet concha breads for a cozy, authentic Mexican family breakfast.

Angustina Mezcal & Cocina

Try the gourmet pork belly tacos or fresh grilled octopus, featuring a surprisingly kid-friendly staff and easy plain quesadilla options.

Rufo's Grill

Feast on the giant, charcoal-grilled t-bone steak or half-chicken served in a casual, open-air alleyway that kids find exciting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always stick to bottled water for drinking and brushing toddler teeth, and pack high-SPF reef-safe sunscreen. Taxis are cheap and easily flagged down, but bring a lightweight carrier since the historic cobblestone streets can make strollers difficult to push.
