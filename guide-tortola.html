Tortola

← back to travel board
Tortola welcomes families with its dramatic emerald peaks, calm sapphire waters, and laid-back Caribbean charm. From exploring hidden coves to wandering colorful harbor markets, it is an easygoing paradise for all ages. Children and parents alike will fall in love with the soft trade winds and shallow tide pools.
🚕 Getting there: ~40 min taxi from Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport (EIS) to the West End beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Cane Garden Bay Beach

This lively, crescent-shaped beach offers calm, shallow waters protected by a reef, making it ideal for swimming. Toddlers will love splashing in the gentle shoreline waves while parents relax under rented umbrellas.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Smuggler's Cove

A quiet, pristine beach reached by a bumpy dirt road, offering peaceful shade under almond and coconut trees. It is perfect for toddlers to nap under the canopy and search for seashells in the calm tide.

Morning / 2h

Sage Mountain National Park

Walk through the lush, cool remnants of a primeval rain forest with well-marked trails that stay shaded under the dense canopy. The gravel paths near the entrance are easy enough for steady toddlers to wander safely.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Soper's Hole Wharf

A charming marina village filled with brightly colored pastel buildings, boutiques, and boardwalks. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching the luxury yachts and sailboats dock while enjoying a cold treat.

Morning / 1-2h

J.R. O'Neal Botanic Gardens

This peaceful four-acre sanctuary in Road Town features lily paths, a small waterfall, and a walk-through aviary. It is completely stroller-friendly and serves as a quiet escape from the midday sun.

Morning / 1h

Crafts Alive Market

An outdoor market in Road Town housed in colorful, traditional West Indian-style booths. Toddlers will love pointing at the bright hand-painted crafts, straw hats, and soft island toys.

Morning / 2-3h

Josiah's Bay Beach

A dramatic, wide stretch of sand known for beautiful scenery and a shallow, grassy stream running right into the ocean. Toddlers can safely splash in the calm stream water away from the main ocean break.

Afternoon / 3h

Brewers Bay Beach

A tranquil beach lined with ruins of old sugar distilleries and towering palm trees. The shallow reef close to shore is great for older kids to snorkel, while toddlers can play safely in the gentle shore break.

Afternoon / 1h

Mount Healthy National Park

The ruins of an 18th-century stone sugar mill windmill set inside a grassy, quiet park. It is a fantastic open space for toddlers to run around safely while parents admire the historic architecture.

🍽️ Where to eat

Myett's Restaurant & Grille

Order the fresh grilled mahi-mahi or the kid-approved chicken tenders right on the sands of Cane Garden Bay.

D'CoalPot Bar & Restaurant

Feast on their famous slow-cooked BBQ ribs and Caribbean roti in a family-friendly waterfront setting.

Bananakeet Cafe

Enjoy crispy coconut shrimp and panoramic views of the neighboring islands during a breathtaking sunset dinner.

Pusser's Road Town Pub

Try the classic, family-friendly British fish and chips alongside a famous local Painkiller cocktail for the parents.

The Sugar Mill Restaurant

Indulge in upscale butter-poached local lobster tail inside a historic, beautifully lit 400-year-old stone mill.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and high-quality bug spray, as both are expensive to purchase on-island and vital for sensitive skin. Rent a 4WD vehicle if you plan to explore independently, as Tortola's mountain roads are incredibly steep with sharp hairpin turns.
