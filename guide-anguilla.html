Anguilla

← back to travel board
Anguilla offers pristine white sands and turquoise waters so calm they feel like a private lagoon. The island's laid-back luxury means you can push a stroller along Meads Bay's gentle surf before the sun gets high. At Frangipani Beach Resort, cribs are delivered to your suite and the beachfront pool is steps from your door. Your baby will nap to the rhythm of the Caribbean while you sip rum punch under a palapa.
🚕 Getting there: AXA airport to Meads Bay hotel strip is a 15-minute drive. Book a private car service (e.g., Anguilla Private Transfers) with a pre-installed infant car seat and a trunk that fits your stroller — they'll even have a cool bottle of water waiting.

What to do

Late morning 10am-12pm

Shoal Bay East

Powdery sand and calm, shallow water perfect for baby's first paddle. Stroller rolls easily on the packed sand near the entrance; shade from sea grape trees.

Midday 11am-1pm

Little Bay

A secluded cove accessible by boat or a short climb — skip the climb with a baby; instead hire a water taxi from Crocus Bay for a 5-minute ride. Crystal-clear water, shade cliffs, and no crowds.

Full day 9am-3pm

Sandy Island

A tiny sandbar reachable by 20-minute ferry from Sandy Ground. Calm, shallow lagoons perfect for splashing. Bring a baby tent for shade and a cooler of snacks.

Early morning 7-9am

Rendezvous Bay

Miles of soft sand and gentle waves; the western end near Dune Preserve has shade and calm water. Stroller-friendly boardwalk at the Zemi Beach House side.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Meads Bay

Home to several luxury resorts with beach chairs and umbrellas. The flat, hard-packed sand near the waterline is ideal for a stroller at low tide.

2 hours morning

The Valley

Anguilla's capital with the Old Valley historic district. Stroller-friendly sidewalks on the main street; stop at the Heritage Museum (air-conditioned, quiet, baby carrier friendly).

1 hour

Heritage Museum

Small museum in a restored plantation house with exhibits on Arawak history and island life. Quiet, air-conditioned, and stroller-accessible; perfect for a mid-heat break.

Late afternoon 3-5pm

Crocus Bay

A sheltered cove with calm, clear water and a beach bar (Dolphin Discovery). Very shallow entry; bring a baby float. Shade from cliffs on the north end.

30-45 min

Big Spring National Park

A small archaeological site with petroglyphs and a shaded nature trail. Stroller-friendly path (paved) and a cool breeze from the spring. Skip if baby is napping – it's a short visit.

🍽️ Where to eat

Blanchard's

Upscale Caribbean cuisine with a signature coconut-crusted snapper. Highchairs, outdoor terrace with sea breeze, and early seating (5:30pm) for families. Noise level moderate; no minimum age.

Veya

Creative tapas with a Jamaican twist – try the jerk chicken tacos. Outdoor patio with fans and shade, highchairs available, and a relaxed vibe that welcomes babies. Early dinner at 6pm.

Straw Hat

Beachfront on Shoal Bay East; order the grilled lobster. Sand floor (stroller park at entrance), highchairs, shaded terrace. Early seating 12pm lunch or 5:30pm dinner.

Hibernia

French-Caribbean fusion in a garden setting. Known for the foie gras and conch. Highchairs, quiet garden with shade, and a kids' menu available. Reserve early for 6pm.

Tasty's

Local favorite for grilled fish and Johnny cakes. Casual outdoor picnic tables, no highchair but a booster seat works; early bird special at 5pm. Low noise, family-friendly.
