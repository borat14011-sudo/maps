Anguilla

← back to travel board
Anguilla welcomes families with its unmatched powdery-white beaches, calm azure waters, and an incredibly laid-back Caribbean soul. It is a peaceful paradise where toddlers can safely splash in gentle shallows while parents unwind under the shade of rustling palms. From colorful beach shacks to hidden coves, this island offers a slow-paced, stress-free escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min rental car or taxi to the West End beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Shoal Bay East

Relax on one of the world's best beaches with calm, shallow waters perfect for building sandcastles. Toddler note: The water here is incredibly still and clear near the shore, making it safe for tiny splashers.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Meads Bay Pond

Take a gentle walk around this salt pond to spot local birdlife like egrets and herons. Toddler note: Keep a lookout for crabs along the muddy edges, which toddlers find fascinating.

Morning / 2-3h

Little Bay

Take a short boat cove trip to this secluded beach surrounded by dramatic cliffs. Toddler note: The boat ride from Crocus Bay is very short, but bring a life jacket and a pop-up tent for shade.

Afternoon / 2h

Sandy Ground Beach

Watch the colorful sailboats and enjoy the lively, family-friendly harbor atmosphere. Toddler note: The sand is flat and wide, perfect for running around, and there are plenty of casual spots nearby for a quick snack.

Morning / 2h

Anguilla Watersports at Cove Bay

Let older kids tackle the floating obstacle course while toddlers play in the gentle, shallow waves of Cove Bay. Toddler note: The shoreline is exceptionally wide and shallow, ideal for wading.

Morning / 1h

The Valley Street Market

Explore the small local market in The Valley to pick up fresh tropical fruits like mangoes and bananas. Toddler note: It is a quick, sensory-rich stop that won't overwhelm short attention spans.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Rendezvous Bay

Stroll along this stunning, crescent-shaped bay with views of neighboring St. Martin. Toddler note: Dune Preserve at the end of the beach offers a fun, quirky wooden structure for toddlers to look at.

Morning / 1h

Heritage Collection Museum

Discover Anguilla's history through artifacts, old photographs, and island relics. Toddler note: The museum is small and air-conditioned, offering a quick, cool respite from the midday sun.

Lunchtime / 3h

Scilly Cay

Hop on a free 2-minute boat ride from Island Harbour to this tiny coral island for lunch. Toddler note: Toddlers will love the brief boat ride and searching for seashells on the tiny beach.

🍽️ Where to eat

Blanchards Beach Shack

Grab delicious mahi-mahi tacos and kid-friendly chicken tenders right on the sands of Meads Bay.

Sunshine Shack

Savor tender, slow-cooked BBQ ribs and chicken while sitting at colorful picnic tables on Rendezvous Bay.

Picante

Enjoy fresh, vibrant Caribbean-Mexican dishes like fish tacos and cheesy quesadillas in a colorful garden setting.

Veya

Indulge in upscale Caribbean fusion like Moroccan-spiced shrimp in a magical, treehouse-like atmosphere.

Falcon Nest Bar & Grill

Try the freshly caught grilled snapper or lobster right on the beach at this super casual local spot.
