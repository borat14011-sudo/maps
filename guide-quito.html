Perched high in the Andes, Quito captivates with its dramatic volcanic peaks and beautifully preserved Spanish colonial architecture. The city blends rich history with modern luxury, offering a sophisticated base for discerning travelers. For families with an infant like Mary Claire, the sprawling, flat paved paths of Parque La Carolina provide a perfect, stroller-friendly oasis amidst the bustling city. After exploring, parents can retreat to exclusive neighborhoods like La Carolina or Floresta, where upscale amenities cater to every family need.
🚕 Getting there: 45 minutes from Quito Mariscal Sucre International Airport (UIO) to the northern luxury hotel strip via a pre-arranged private Mercedes-Benz SUV transfer, complete with a pre-installed rear-facing infant car seat and ample luggage space for your premium stroller.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM (2h)

Jardín Botánico de Quito

Stroll through the lush orchid houses and native Andean gardens. The paved pathways are fully stroller-accessible, and the tranquil atmosphere provides a quiet escape for Mary Claire’s morning nap.

Late Morning 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM (2h)

Parque La Carolina

A massive green space in the heart of the modern business district. It features wide paved walkways, a scenic paddleboat lagoon, and plenty of shaded lawns perfect for blanket playtime.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM (2.5h)

Telefériqo Quito

Ascend the slopes of Pichincha Volcano for breathtaking panoramic views of the city. While the cable car ride is smooth and exciting, ensure you bring warm layers for the baby and a soft carrier, as the terrain at the top is uneven and rocky.

Early Morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM (1.5h)

Plaza Grande

The vibrant heart of the UNESCO-listed historic center, surrounded by grand colonial buildings. Arrive early in the morning to enjoy the lively atmosphere with a stroller before the midday crowds set in.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM (2h)

Museo de la Ciudad

Housed in a beautiful 10,000-square-meter former hospital, this museum offers spacious, quiet courtyards and ramp access. It is a peaceful, educational spot to wander while your baby sleeps soundly in their stroller.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM (2h)

Yaku Parque Museo del Agua

An interactive water museum located on the slopes of El Placer. It offers sensory water displays that will fascinate an older infant, along with stroller-friendly ramped walkways and spectacular city views.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM (2.5h)

Parque Metropolitano Guanguiltagua

One of the largest urban parks in South America, offering crisp mountain air and stunning views of the Cotopaxi volcano. The main dirt and paved trails are wide and suitable for an all-terrain stroller, providing a peaceful nature escape.

Morning 10:30 AM - 11:30 AM (1h)

Iglesia de la Compañía de Jesús

Quito's most ornate church, famous for its breathtaking gold-leaf interior. Because the aisles are narrow and the atmosphere is deeply quiet and reverent, we highly recommend using a baby carrier instead of a stroller here.

Afternoon 1:00 PM - 4:00 PM (3h)

Ciudad Mitad del Mundo

Stand on the equator just north of Quito in a dedicated monument park. The entire plaza is flat, fully paved, and highly stroller-accessible, offering fun photo opportunities and plenty of open space for a relaxed family afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Zazu

This Relais & Châteaux restaurant serves world-class contemporary Ecuadorian cuisine like grilled octopus and sea bass. Book an early 6:30 PM seating on their elegant, spacious main floor, which easily accommodates a luxury stroller and offers a quiet, relaxed atmosphere.

The Restaurant at Casa Gangotena

Indulge in sophisticated 'Cocina Mestiza' in a glass-domed colonial atrium. The serene, high-ceilinged space is perfect for an upscale lunch with Mary Claire, offering highchairs upon request and a peaceful noise level.

Somos Ecuador

Chef Alejandra Espinoza crafts vibrant, modern Ecuadorian sharing plates in a brightly lit, spacious dining room. The welcoming, energetic atmosphere is highly forgiving of minor infant noise, and they happily provide highchairs.

La Gloria

A legendary upscale dining institution in the trendy La Floresta neighborhood, known for its superb Spanish-Mediterranean dishes. The beautiful garden terrace is ideal for an early dinner, offering plenty of space for a stroller beside your table.

Lua

Chef Alexander Lau serves exceptional Peruvian-Asian fusion in a chic, converted house. Request a table in the spacious, covered outdoor patio area, which is incredibly accommodating for families with infants and provides a gentle, ambient breeze.
