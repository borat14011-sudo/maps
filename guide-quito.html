Quito

← back to travel board
Perched high in the Andes, Quito blends cobblestone colonial history with dramatic volcanic peaks and lush green parks. Families can step across the equator line, ride a soaring cable car, and wander through vibrant markets rich with tropical fruits. It's a high-altitude playground where old-world charm meets thrilling outdoor adventures.
🚕 Getting there: ~45-60 min taxi or private shuttle from Mariscal Sucre International Airport (UIO) to the historic center or northern hotel districts.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

TelefériQo Cable Car

Ride one of the world's highest cable cars up the side of Pichincha Volcano for breathtaking panoramic views of the city. Toddlers will love the soaring cabin ride, but bundle up as it gets very windy and chilly at the top.

Morning / 3-4h

Mitad del Mundo

Stand in both the Northern and Southern hemispheres at once and enjoy interactive physics experiments. Toddlers will love running along the bright yellow equatorial line and seeing the miniature scale model of colonial Quito.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque La Carolina

A massive urban park featuring a paddleboat lagoon, multiple playgrounds, and a retired airplane kids can look at. It is perfect for letting toddlers run free on the modern play structures or watching locals play traditional Ecuavolley.

Morning / 1-2h

Quito Botanical Garden

Explore lush greenhouses filled with thousands of orchids, carnivorous plants, and a magical butterfly house. It's a peaceful, stroller-friendly oasis where toddlers can watch colorful butterflies land on tropical flowers.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza Grande

The historic heart of Quito, surrounded by majestic palaces and the grand cathedral. Toddlers will be delighted chasing the friendly pigeons in the central plaza while parents admire the stunning colonial architecture.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

A vibrant, bustling market packed with colorful exotic fruits, fresh juices, and traditional food stalls. Toddlers will love tasting sweet granadillas and watching the lively vendors arrange towers of fresh berries.

Morning / 2-3h

Yaku Water Museum

An interactive museum perched on a hillside dedicated to the history and conservation of water. It features wonderful hands-on water play areas and bubble-making stations designed specifically for young, curious minds.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Calle La Ronda

A beautifully restored historic pedestrian street filled with artisan shops, traditional toy makers, and sweet treats. Walk here in the early afternoon so toddlers can play with traditional wooden spinning tops and eat hot chocolate.

Afternoon / 2h

Guápulo Park

A tranquil, terraced hillside park with walking paths, a small lake with ducks, and a fantastic playground. It's a great spot for a relaxed family picnic with panoramic views of the valley below.

🍽️ Where to eat

Hasta la Vuelta, Señor

Order the traditional giant cheese-filled empanadas de viento dusted with sugar and served in a beautiful colonial courtyard.

Café Plaza Grande

Try the famous hot chocolate served with local cheese cubes to melt inside, accompanied by a theatrical show by the ice cream servers.

Pims Panecillo

Enjoy kid-friendly Ecuadorian dishes like potato soup with cheese alongside dramatic panoramic views of the city from the top of El Panecillo hill.

The Secret Garden Quito Rooftop

Head to this rooftop spot for a relaxed, family-friendly vibe and excellent wood-fired pizzas with views of the historic center's domes.

Heladería San Agustín

Satisfy everyone's sweet tooth with legendary artisanal helados de paila, which is fruit sorbet hand-spun in copper bowls.

👨‍👩‍👧 Acclimate to the high altitude of 9,350 feet by keeping activities low-key for the first 48 hours and keeping everyone well-hydrated. Use a sturdy baby carrier rather than a stroller to navigate the steep hills, steps, and uneven cobblestones of the Old Town.
