What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Plaza Grande

The historic heart of Quito, perfect for a relaxed morning walk. The paved, flat pathways are highly stroller-accessible, and the lush central garden offers plenty of shaded benches for feeding Mary Claire under the palms.

Late Morning 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Jardín Botánico de Quito

A peaceful green sanctuary located inside Parque La Carolina. Its wide, paved paths are ideal for strollers, showcasing stunning orchids and native Andean flora in a quiet setting far from the city noise.

Early Morning 8:30 AM - 10:30 AM

TelefériQo

Ascend the slopes of Pichincha Volcano for breathtaking panoramic views of the Andes. To ensure Mary Claire stays comfortable with the rapid altitude change, bring a pacifier or bottle for her to suck on during the smooth, enclosed cable car ride.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM

Capilla del Hombre

This monumental art museum dedicated to Oswaldo Guayasamín features spacious, high-ceilinged galleries that absorb sound well, making it a soothing environment for a sleeping baby. The outdoor plazas are flat and stroller-friendly.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Iglesia de la Compañía de Jesús

Marvel at the breathtaking gold-leaf interior of Ecuador's most ornate church. Because the interior is highly hushed and lacks stroller ramps, we recommend using a premium baby carrier to admire the dazzling baroque architecture comfortably.

Morning 9:30 AM - 12:30 PM

Mitad del Mundo

Stand on the equator at this famous monument complex north of the city. The vast park features wide, flat pedestrian pathways that are fully stroller-accessible, plus plenty of outdoor cafes for a quick baby care break.

Afternoon 3:30 PM - 4:45 PM

Casa Museo Guayasamín

The beautiful former home of the artist features serene, manicured lawns and peaceful courtyards. It provides a quiet, crowd-free escape where you can gently push the stroller while appreciating local art and stunning valley views.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Parque La Carolina

A massive, modern city park that is highly popular with local families. It features completely paved boardwalks, smooth running tracks, and wide-open green spaces perfect for spreading a blanket out for Mary Claire's tummy time.

Early Morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM

Mercado Central de Quito

Immerse yourselves in local Ecuadorian culture amidst vibrant stalls of fresh flowers and tropical fruits. To navigate the bustling aisles easily and keep Mary Claire safe from crowds, we highly recommend using a baby carrier during an early morning visit.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Gangotena Restaurant

Sophisticated Andean cuisine served in an elegant, sun-drenched atrium. The spacious layout easily accommodates strollers, and the staff are incredibly attentive, offering a quiet corner table and early dinner seating.

Zazu

Relais & Châteaux fine dining featuring modern Ecuadorian seafood and a world-class wine cellar. They offer exceptional service, welcoming families for early dinner seatings with comfortable, plush booth seating.

Chez Jérôme

High-end French-Ecuadorian fusion set in a beautiful residential villa in La Floresta. The restaurant boasts a peaceful, spacious outdoor garden terrace that is perfect for dining with an infant in a stroller.

Urko Cocina Local

An innovative culinary journey highlighting Ecuador's diverse ecosystems. Reserve an early seating at 6:30 PM, where the highly accommodating staff will secure a quiet, spacious table with plenty of room for baby gear.

Octava de Corpus

A legendary Old Town restaurant known for its exquisite art collection and refined atmosphere. Request one of their private, intimate dining alcoves, which allows you to enjoy a quiet meal while Mary Claire sleeps soundly in her stroller.
