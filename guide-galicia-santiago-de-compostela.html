What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Santiago de Compostela Cathedral and Praza do Obradoiro

Behold the magnificent culmination of the Camino de Santiago in the grand main square, which features wide, flat granite paving that is highly accessible for luxury strollers. Arrive in the early morning to enjoy the quiet, awe-inspiring atmosphere before the daily arrival of pilgrim groups, and use the ramped side entrance to access the cathedral's spacious interior.

Late morning 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Parque de la Alameda

Stroll along the elegant, shaded avenues of this 19th-century park, which offers postcard-perfect views of the cathedral skyline. The wide, paved paths are ideal for a smooth stroller ride, while the mature oak and eucalyptus trees provide ample canopy cover to keep baby Mary Claire cool and protected from the sun during her morning nap.

Mid-morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Mercado de Abastos de Santiago

Explore Galicia's premier food market, housed in beautiful historic granite halls where local vendors display fresh seafood, artisan cheeses, and vibrant produce. The smooth indoor stone floors make stroller navigation seamless, and visiting during the quieter mid-morning window ensures a sensory-rich experience without the bustling lunchtime crowds.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM

Parque de San Domingos de Bonaval

Relax in this beautifully designed modern park created by Portuguese architect Alvaro Siza on the grounds of a former convent. The park features sprawling, manicured grassy lawns perfect for tummy time, gentle stone ramps for easy stroller access, and a soothing trickling water feature that creates a calming white-noise effect for an infant.

Early afternoon 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM

Centro Galego de Arte Contemporánea (CGAC)

Experience contemporary culture in an ultra-quiet, air-conditioned museum designed with wide, minimalist corridors and smooth ramps perfect for stroller wheels. This architectural gem offers a peaceful, climate-controlled sanctuary where parents can admire cutting-edge art while baby Mary Claire enjoys a quiet, undisturbed sleep.

Morning trip 10:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Pazo de Oca Gardens

Take a short private day trip to the 'Versailles of Galicia' to wander through breathtaking, centuries-old gardens filled with blooming camellias and peaceful stone canals. The flat gravel pathways are highly manageable with a sturdy multi-terrain stroller, and the soothing sound of flowing water makes it an idyllic, tranquil setting for a family walk.

Day trip 11:00 AM - 3:00 PM

Praia de Silgar (Sanxenxo)

Drive to the nearby Rias Baixas coastline to experience this pristine, sheltered beach known for its exceptionally calm waters and fine golden sand. The wide, flat paved beachside promenade is tailor-made for high-end strollers, and the quiet beach alcoves provide a serene spot to dip baby's feet into the gentle, shallow Atlantic waters.

Afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Colexiata de Santa María a Real do Sar

Visit this stunning 12th-century Romanesque church famous for its curiously leaning interior columns and peaceful, cloistered courtyard. The quiet historical site sees far fewer tourists than the main cathedral, providing a serene and whisper-quiet environment that is exceptionally comfortable for families with young infants.

Late afternoon 5:30 PM - 7:00 PM

Zona Vella (Old Town Santiago Walking Tour)

Meander through the car-free historical heart of Santiago, where the majestic granite architecture creates a cool, breezy microclimate. While some narrow lanes have historic cobblestones that require a gentle pace, the main arteries are smoothly paved, allowing you to admire the beautiful arcades, local boutiques, and quiet stone plazas at your own leisure.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Filigrana

Located inside A Quinta da Auga, this refined restaurant serves exquisite, modern Galician cuisine such as wild sea bass in a peaceful, estate garden setting. The spacious, elegant dining room easily accommodates luxury strollers, and the beautiful outdoor garden terrace is ideal for an early evening dinner with a relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere.

O Curro da Parra

An upscale, creative tavern tucked into the historic quarter offering market-fresh Galician specialties like slow-cooked Iberian pork. Request a table on the spacious ground floor during their first lunch seating to enjoy a quiet, unhurried dining experience with ample room to place an infant carrier next to your table.

Asador Gonzaba

A legendary Galician steakhouse renowned for its premium, dry-aged Galician blond beef cooked over open embers. The dining room is spacious, professional, and highly welcoming to families, offering sturdy wooden highchairs and a lively, warm ambiance where minor baby noises easily blend into the background.

Abastos 2.0

A chic, contemporary dining spot located right next to the historic market, celebrating hyper-local seafood dishes such as steamed razor clams and fresh hake. Opt for an outdoor high-table seating on the pedestrianized street, which allows you to keep baby Mary Claire comfortable in her stroller right by your side.

Café Altamira

A stylish, bright restaurant presenting a modern take on traditional Galician comfort food, situated in a quiet plaza opposite the Belvís Convent. The staff is exceptionally accommodating to families, the layout is stroller-friendly, and the early evening service allows for a relaxed dinner before baby's bedtime.
