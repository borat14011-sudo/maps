Discover a land of Celtic myths, misty stone streets, and lush green parks perfect for little feet. Santiago de Compostela blends historic wonder with relaxed Spanish charm, offering giant cathedrals alongside playgrounds hidden in oak groves. It is a slow-paced wonderland where family memories are made over warm empanadas.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or 20 min shuttle bus from Santiago de Compostela Airport (SCQ) to the historic old town hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Parque de la Alameda

A beautiful park with shaded pathways, quirky statues, and stunning views of the cathedral. Toddlers will love the playground and chasing ducks near the pond.

Morning / 1-2h

Santiago de Compostela Cathedral

The dramatic end-point of the famous Camino trail, featuring towering stone walls and wide plazas. Toddlers will love watching the triumphant pilgrims celebrate and running around the car-free Praza do Obradoiro.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de Abastos de Santiago

A bustling historic food market filled with fresh cheeses, sea creatures, and vibrant fruit stalls. Toddlers will be fascinated by the live lobsters and can snack on fresh local strawberries.

Afternoon / 1h

Santiago Tourist Train

A bright red hop-on train that winds through the historic streets and climbs up to panoramic viewpoints. It is the perfect way to sightsee when toddler legs are tired of walking.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque de Belvís

A massive, terraced green park featuring a fantastic playground, rolling grass hills, and historic watermills. Toddlers can run freely here while parents enjoy views of the convent.

Afternoon / 2h

San Domingos de Bonaval Park

A stunning park built on a former convent's grounds with secret pathways, lush lawns, and a modern art museum next door. Toddlers can safely explore the stone ramparts and grassy terraces.

Full Day / 4-5h

Praia de Area Maior in Louro

A breathtaking beach framed by a lagoon and a dramatic mountain, just an hour's drive away. It is ideal for toddlers to splash in the calm, shallow lagoon waters away from the ocean waves.

Morning / 1h

Colexiata de Santa María a Real do Sar

A beautiful 12th-century church famous for its strangely leaning stone pillars. Toddlers will enjoy the peaceful surrounding gardens and the nearby bubbling river walk.

Morning / 2h

Sarela River Walk

A magical, shady walking trail along the river lined with old stone mills and fairy-like mossy trees. It is a flat, easy dirt path perfect for a toddler-led nature walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

Abastos 2.0

Grab an outdoor seat and order the fresh, toddler-friendly empanada gallega of the day.

O Gato Negro

Try the traditional, incredibly tender octopus served on wooden plates, which kids love for its soft texture.

Café Casino

Head to this beautiful historic cafe for a slice of traditional Tarta de Santiago almond cake and hot chocolate.

A Moa

Enjoy a modern Galician meal in their beautiful backyard garden terrace, which has plenty of space for toddlers to wiggle.

La Crecha

Stop by this cozy tavern for delicious, easy-to-eat Galician cheeses and artisanal ice creams.

👨‍👩‍👧 Santiago's historic center is paved with uneven cobblestones, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier. Keep in mind that Galicia is notoriously rainy, so pack lightweight rain suits and stroller covers for uninterrupted outdoor fun.
