Galicia (Santiago de Compostela)

← back to travel board
Santiago de Compostela enchants families with its rain-kissed granite streets, emerald-green parks, and the mythical energy of the Camino's end. Kids can run free in spacious medieval plazas while parents soak in the historic charm and world-class Atlantic seafood. It is a fairy-tale Galician escape where ancient history feels wonderfully alive at every corner.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Santiago-Rosalía de Castro Airport directly to historic center hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Santiago de Compostela Cathedral & Praza do Obradoiro

Marvel at the soaring towers of this world-famous pilgrimage site and its spectacular interior. Toddlers will love chasing pigeons in the massive, car-free stone plaza outside while you admire the facade.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la Alameda

This lush, historic park features shaded oak avenues, colorful statues, and stunning views of the cathedral. There is a fantastic playground where toddlers can slide and swing under the canopy of ancient trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de Abastos

A sensory feast of five granite halls filled with fresh fish, local cheeses, giant octopus, and colorful fruits. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the live lobsters and friendly vendors offering samples of traditional Tetilla cheese.

Morning / 45 min

The Tourist Train (Tren Turístico)

A bright red trackless train that chugs around the city's historic streets and surrounding parks. It is the perfect way to rest tired toddler legs while still enjoying an exciting, bumpy sightseeing ride.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de Bonaval

A beautifully designed, peaceful park built on the grounds of a former convent cemetery, featuring wide grassy lawns and stone pathways. Toddlers will love rolling down the safe, grassy slopes while parents enjoy the quiet, artistic atmosphere.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo de Historia Natural

An engaging university-run museum featuring a vast collection of taxidermy, marine life, and sparkling minerals. Toddlers will be wide-eyed standing next to life-sized animals and colorful Galician wildlife displays.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Praza da Quintana

A grand, enclosed stone square behind the cathedral with wide granite steps that double as a local hangout. It is a brilliant, enclosed space for toddlers to practice walking and running safely while you grab a coffee to go.

Morning / 1h

Colexiata de Santa María de Sar

A beautiful 12th-century church famous for its highly unusual, dramatically leaning interior stone columns. Toddlers will find the 'crooked' walls intriguing, and the quiet cloister garden offers a peaceful escape.

Morning / 2h

Paseo del Río Sarela

An easy, shaded nature trail that follows a babbling river past old stone tanneries, mossy bridges, and small waterfalls. Bring a carrier for this dirt path, where toddlers will love tossing leaves and twigs into the running water.

🍽️ Where to eat

Abastos 2.0

Grab an outdoor table next to the market and order the incredibly fresh, simple steamed mussels or grilled razor clams.

O Gato Negro

Squeeze into this legendary, rustic tavern for a slice of traditional empanada gallega (savory pie) and a portion of mild Tetilla cheese.

Churrería El Metate

Treat the family to thick, dark hot chocolate and fresh, crispy churros perfect for dipping at this classic local institution.

O Sampedro

Enjoy a family-friendly, spacious dining room and order the ultra-tender octopus (pulpo a la gallega) and crispy ham croquetas.

A Moa

Dine in their gorgeous, walled-in backyard garden and try the delicious grilled Galician beef (croca) or fresh codfish.

👨‍👩‍👧 Santiago's historic center is fully pedestrianized but paved with bumpy cobblestones, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier. Keep a compact umbrella or rain covers handy at all times, as Galicia's famously lush green landscape is thanks to sudden, brief showers.
