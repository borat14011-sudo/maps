Sayulita

← back to travel board
Sayulita wraps families in a warm, bohemian embrace where golf carts zip down cobblestone streets lined with colorful Papel Picado. Between the gentle waves of the sheltered cove and the lush jungle canopy, this lively surf town offers a relaxed Mexican escape that is both adventurous and deeply comforting. It is a place where toddlers can dig in the golden sand while parents sip fresh coconut water under a palapa.
🚕 Getting there: ~50-60 min private shuttle or taxi from Puerto Vallarta International Airport (PVR) directly to your Sayulita hotel or rental.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Sayulita Main Beach (Playa Sayulita)

The pulsing heart of the town, perfect for watching surfers, building sandcastles, and renting a shaded lounge chair. Toddler note: The south end near the cliffs has much calmer waves and shallower water ideal for tiny splashers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Golf Cart Exploration

Rent an electric golf cart to navigate the town's steep, cobblestone hills and access hidden neighborhoods with ease. Toddler note: Toddlers absolutely love the breezy, open-air ride, which turns a simple commute into an exciting amusement park ride.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de los Muertos

A beautiful, quieter beach reached by a short, colorful walk past a decorated cemetery. Toddler note: The protective rock barriers keep the water tranquil and safe for toddler wading compared to the main surf beach.

Evening / 1h

Campamento Tortuguero Sayulita

A volunteer-run turtle sanctuary on the beach where you can participate in releasing newborn sea turtle hatchlings into the ocean. Toddler note: Toddlers will be completely mesmerized watching the tiny turtles make their sunset march to the waves.

Evening / 1-2h

Sayulita Plaza (El Centro)

The vibrant town square filled with street performers, balloon vendors, and towering palm trees. Toddler note: It is mostly pedestrian-only, making it a safe space for toddlers to run around while you enjoy an ice cream on a bench.

Morning / 1-2h

Easy Boogie Boarding at Chicama Surf Shop

Rent a giant foam board or book a gentle private family surf lesson in the whitewater. Toddler note: Let toddlers sit on a secure foam board in the wet sand to experience the gentle wash of the incoming tide.

Morning / 2h

Rancho Mi Chaparrita Horseback Riding

Take a guided, gentle horseback ride through quiet jungle trails that open up onto secluded, pristine beaches. Toddler note: Young children can safely ride double on a saddle with an adult on highly trained, calm horses.

Morning (Friday only) / 2h

Sayulita Mercado del Pueblo

A vibrant Friday market offering organic local food, live music, handmade toys, and beautiful Huichol bead art. Toddler note: It is a sensory delight where toddlers can snack on fresh, organic mango slices while listening to acoustic guitars.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Shopping & Gelato Stroll

Wander the colorful streets surrounding the plaza to shop for kid-friendly Mexican handicrafts and hand-woven pom-poms. Toddler note: Keep little ones motivated with stops at the local artisan toy displays and frequent sweet treat breaks.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Rústica

Get the wood-fired artisan pizzas, especially the shrimp-topped 'Sayulita' pizza, served in a beautiful, family-friendly open-air patio.

Chocobanana

Get the famous frozen bananas dipped in rich chocolate and rolled in sprinkles or coconut, an iconic local toddler favorite since 1991.

Mary's Traditional Mexican Cocina

Get the mouthwatering, mild shredded beef or fish tacos wrapped in warm, thick, hand-pressed corn tortillas.

Organi-K

Get a refreshing, beautifully presented acai bowl packed with fresh tropical fruits and berries that toddlers love to scoop up.

Tacos y Mariscos El Ivan

Get the legendary al pastor tacos sliced thin straight from the spit and topped with a juicy slice of fresh pineapple.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a golf cart equipped with a child safety seat ahead of time to easily conquer the town's steep, cobblestone hills without carrying tired kids. Always keep pesos on hand, as many beach vendors, street taco stands, and small boutiques do not accept credit cards.
