Edinburgh

← back to travel board
Edinburgh weaves a spell of gothic magic, volcanic hills, and secret gardens that will enchant travelers of all ages. From the towering castle to wide-open parks, this compact capital is exceptionally welcoming to little legs and stroller-pushing parents. Pack your layers and prepare for a fairy-tale adventure where history feels very much alive.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Uber directly to city center hotels, or a smooth 30-minute ride on the Edinburgh Tram which is highly stroller-accessible.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Edinburgh Castle

Explore the ancient fortress atop a dormant volcano, offering stunning views and historic cannons that kids love. Toddlers will enjoy marching along the cobblestone esplanade, though a carrier is better than a stroller here.

Afternoon / 2h

Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh

A lush, 70-acre oasis featuring majestic trees, a giant redwood grove, and glasshouses. Toddlers can run freely along the wide, paved paths and spot friendly squirrels.

Morning / 3h

National Museum of Scotland

This massive free museum features interactive galleries, giant animal skeletons, and a kinetic clock. The dedicated 'Imagine' gallery is specifically designed for toddlers to play and learn.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Princes Street Gardens

Nestled in the shadow of the castle, this gorgeous park features manicured lawns, a famous floral clock, and a fantastic play park. Toddlers will love the West Princes Street Gardens wooden castle playground.

Morning / 2h

Camera Obscura and World of Illusions

Five floors of mind-bending optical illusions, mirror mazes, and hands-on vortex tunnels. While older kids love the tricks, toddlers will be mesmerized by the colored lights and interactive projections.

Sunday Morning / 1-2h

Stockbridge Market

A vibrant Sunday market filled with artisan crafts, sweet treats, and savory street food. Toddlers will enjoy snacking on fresh pastries while sitting by the nearby Water of Leith.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

The Meadows Playground

A massive, grassy park south of the city center boasting one of Edinburgh's best and largest outdoor play areas. Toddlers can safely play in the enclosed toddler zone featuring swings, slides, and sand pits.

Morning / 3-4h

Edinburgh Zoo

Home to incredible animal encounters and the famous penguin walk. It is quite steep, so bring a sturdy stroller or use the hilltop safari bus to save toddler energy.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Portobello Beach

A beautiful sandy beach just a short bus ride from the city center, perfect for shell collecting and sandcastles. The flat, pedestrianized promenade is ideal for pushing a stroller while enjoying an ice cream.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mimi's Bakehouse

Treat the family to their award-winning cupcakes, classic afternoon tea, and fluffy scones with clotted cream.

The Scran and Scallie

Enjoy elevated Scottish gastropub classics like fish and chips or steak pie in a rustic, family-friendly setting with a dedicated kids' playroom.

Civerinos Slice

Grab massive, delicious New York-style pizza slices that are a guaranteed hit for kids and parents alike in a vibrant, casual atmosphere.

Bertie's Proper Fish & Chips

Feast on traditional battered haddock, twice-cooked chips, and mushy peas inside a spacious, beautifully restored former church that welcomes noisy toddlers.

The Sheep Heid Inn

Relax at Scotland's oldest pub for a traditional Sunday roast, followed by a game on their historic, hand-carved wooden skittle alley that kids find fascinating.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy, lightweight stroller with large wheels to navigate the steep cobblestone streets, but always pack a baby carrier for historic sites like the castle. To keep little ones happy, plan your day around Edinburgh’s plentiful green spaces and free museums, which offer excellent baby-changing facilities and quiet feeding areas.
