This destination seamlessly marries rich heritage with modern, upscale conveniences to ensure a seamless Scottish getaway.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Princes Street Gardens

This pristine public park offers beautifully manicured lawns and paved pathways that are ideal for a smooth stroller ride while taking in dramatic views of the castle. The gentle ambient noise of the nearby fountain is highly soothing for a morning infant nap.

Late morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh

Explore majestic glasshouses and sweeping botanical landscapes featuring wide, paved walkways that accommodate luxury strollers effortlessly. The serene atmosphere is perfect for a quiet morning, and the outdoor terrace cafe offers comfortable seating for a quick feeding break.

Afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

National Museum of Scotland

The grand, light-filled Victorian atrium is fully accessible via modern elevators, allowing you to easily navigate the exhibits with a stroller. It is incredibly spacious, meaning you can easily escape any crowds to find a quiet corner if Mary Claire needs a peaceful bottle feeding.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Palace of Holyroodhouse

Walk through the elegant royal apartments and flat, grassy courtyard ruins where the wide pathways are easy to navigate with a baby carrier. The historic gardens provide a tranquil, shaded space where you can relax on the grass under the Scottish sun.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Dean Village

A fairytale-like oasis hidden in the heart of the city, offering a quiet, paved path along the Water of Leith that is perfect for a soothing stroller walk. The gentle sound of the flowing river creates the ultimate natural white noise machine to help your baby drift off to sleep.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Portobello Beach Promenade

Enjoy a refreshing coastal stroll along this wide, completely flat, paved boardwalk that overlooks the golden sands of the Firth of Forth. The fresh sea breeze is invigorating, and the path is lined with charming, family-friendly cafes where you can stop for a gourmet coffee.

Late afternoon 5-6pm / 1h

Calton Hill

Accessible via a paved road on the southern side, this iconic peak offers panoramic views of the city skyline without the intense hike of Arthur's Seat. It is a scenic spot to enjoy a relaxed family sunset photo session while Mary Claire is snuggled in a carrier.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Inverleith Park

Located right next to the botanical gardens, this sprawling green park features a beautiful pond filled with swans and ducks to capture your baby's attention. The wide tarmac paths are incredibly smooth for strollers, and the castle views are absolutely spectacular.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

The Meadows

This vast, tree-lined park south of the Old Town is highly popular with local families and features wide, flat paved paths that are perfect for an afternoon stroll. It is the ultimate spot to spread out a luxury wool blanket for some outdoor tummy time in the fresh air.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Scran & Scallie

Savor upscale Scottish gastropub classics like their signature steak and ale pie in a relaxed, rustic-chic dining room. They are exceptionally welcoming to families with highchairs readily available, a dedicated kids' menu, and an early evening dining window.

The Dome

Dine under a magnificent Georgian dome while enjoying a sophisticated afternoon tea with delicate sandwiches and warm scones. The spacious layout easily accommodates strollers at the table, and the bustling, elegant atmosphere naturally masks any sudden baby noises.

Dishoom Edinburgh

Indulge in an upscale Bombay-style breakfast of luxury bacon naan rolls and house chai in a beautifully designed, multi-level restaurant. The staff are incredibly accommodating to young families, offering highchairs, coloring sheets, and spacious booths.

Howies Waterloo Place

Enjoy fresh, locally sourced Scottish seafood and game in a bright, airy Georgian hall with high ceilings and a relaxed vibe. They offer plenty of space between tables for strollers, highchairs upon request, and an early dinner seating that aligns perfectly with baby bedtimes.

Contini George Street

Feast on exquisite, authentic Italian pasta and antipasti served inside a spectacular, high-ceilinged former banking hall. This venue is wonderfully family-centric, offering highchairs, extremely attentive service, and an early dining slot that is perfect for families.
