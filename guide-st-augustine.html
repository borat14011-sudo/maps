Where historic pirate adventures meet sunny Florida shores

St. Augustine

← back to travel board
Step back in time in America's oldest city, where cobblestone streets lead to ancient forts and sun-kissed Atlantic beaches. Perfect for young families, this charming coastal gem blends historic mystery with easygoing Florida sunshine. Parents and toddlers alike will love the combination of walkable history, close-up wildlife encounters, and playful trolley rides.
🚕 Getting there: ~60 min drive via rental car or pre-booked airport shuttle from Jacksonville International Airport (JAX) to St. Augustine's historic district

What to do

Morning / 2h

Castillo de San Marcos National Monument

Explore the oldest masonry fort in the continental US, featuring massive coquina walls, cannon firings, and sprawling grassy areas. Toddlers will love running along the wide-open grassy moat area, though you should hold hands on the upper decks due to low ledges.

Morning / 3h

St. Augustine Alligator Farm Zoological Park

A thrilling wildlife park where families can walk wooden boardwalks over hundreds of native alligators and colorful wading birds. The paved, stroller-friendly paths and shaded resting spots make this an exceptionally easy and exciting adventure for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2h

St. Augustine Lighthouse & Maritime Museum

Climb the 219 steps of this iconic striped lighthouse for breathtaking coastal views, or let younger kids explore the shipwreck artifacts below. Toddlers can burn off energy at the maritime-themed play shipyard and wander the shaded, stroller-friendly nature trails.

Morning / 1.5h

Old Town Trolley Tours

A hop-on, hop-off narrated tour that makes navigating the historic city center completely stress-free for families with strollers. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the open-air ride, and it is a great way to sightsee during the hot afternoon hours.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Anastasia State Park

Discover miles of pristine white-sand beaches, gentle tidal pools, and scenic nature trails perfect for shell hunting. The calm, shallow waters of the Coquina Quarry area are ideal for toddlers to safely splash and play in the sand.

Afternoon / 1.5h

St. Augustine Pirate & Treasure Museum

An interactive, hands-on museum featuring real pirate artifacts, a simulated ship deck, and a treasure hunt map with keys. It is highly engaging for older kids, while toddlers will enjoy the bright, sensory-rich displays and digging for gold doubloons.

Saturday Morning / 2h

St. Augustine Amphitheatre Farmers Market

Experience local culture at this vibrant Saturday market featuring live music, fresh local produce, artisan crafts, and tasty baked goods. There is plenty of open, grassy space under the shade of mature oak trees for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Project Swing Playground

A massive, community-built wooden playground featuring castles, slides, swings, and a dedicated, fenced-in area designed specifically for toddlers. Located right next to the historic downtown parking garage, it is the perfect spot to let kids run free.

Morning / 2h

Marineland Dolphin Adventure

Interact with playful bottlenose dolphins at this historic oceanfront aquarium located just south of the city. Toddlers can safely view the dolphins splashing through massive underwater windows without needing to participate in the in-water programs.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Floridian

Order the southern-style grit cakes or the kid-friendly fried chicken tenders in this highly welcoming, casual eatery featuring local ingredients.

Harry's Seafood Bar & Grille

Enjoy flavorful jambalaya or blackened shrimp in their gorgeous, lively courtyard featuring twinkling lights and live acoustic music that keeps toddlers entertained.

The Hyppo Gourmet Ice Pops

Cool off with an all-natural gourmet ice pop in unique child-friendly flavors like strawberry lemonade or mango.

O'Steen's Restaurant

Feast on legendary, lightly battered St. Augustine-style fried shrimp at this cash-only, family-friendly local institution.

Cap's on the Water

Savor fresh seafood and stunning sunset views over the intracoastal waterway while the kids play in the sandy outdoor dining area.
