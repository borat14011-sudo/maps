Sicily

← back to travel board
Sicily welcomes families with open arms, blending ancient wonders and warm Mediterranean waters with a deeply kid-friendly culture. From exploring gentle volcanic slopes to savoring world-class street food, this island turns every corner into a playful history lesson. It is a place where stroller-friendly piazzas meet sandy shores perfect for little feet.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min private transfer from Catania Airport to Taormina, or ~35 min taxi from Palermo Airport to downtown.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Teatro Antico di Taormina

Let kids marvel at this ancient Greek amphitheater with sweeping views of Mt. Etna; the wide stone pathways are great for wandering toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Silvestri Craters on Mount Etna

Walk around these extinct, easy-to-reach lower craters where kids can collect black volcanic pebbles. Toddlers will love the sensory feel of the dark volcanic sand.

Afternoon / 3h

Ortigia Island Beaches & Streets

Wander the pedestrian-only alleys of Syracuse's historic heart and splash in the shallow, calm waters of Spiaggetta di Aretusa.

Morning / 3h

Spiaggia di Cefalù

A golden sand beach with calm, shallow waters perfect for toddlers to splash safely while parents enjoy the scenic backdrop of the historic town.

Afternoon / 1h

Teatro dei Pupi puppet show

Experience a traditional Sicilian marionette show filled with colorful knights and dragons; the vibrant movements keep toddlers mesmerized.

Morning / 1.5h

Mercato del Capo

Introduce little ones to a sensory explosion of colorful fruits, singing vendors, and tasty panelle (chickpea fritters); use a carrier here as it gets crowded.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Valley of the Temples

Stroll past majestic, well-preserved Greek temples along flat, paved pathways that are highly stroller-friendly. Toddlers will love pointing out the giant fallen statue of Atlas.

Afternoon / 1h

Villa Comunale Gardens

A lush public park in Taormina filled with quirky Victorian pavilions, shady paths, and a small play area perfect for toddlers to burn off energy.

Afternoon / 2h

Marzamemi Fishing Village

Explore this picture-perfect, pedestrian-only fishing hamlet with a wide, open piazza where toddlers can run safely while you sip espresso.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bam Bar

Get the legendary almond and peach granita topped with fresh whipped cream, served with a warm brioche bun for dipping.

Pizzeria Frida

Order the Vulcanotti thick-crust pizzas with folded, cheese-stuffed edges that are a massive hit with kids.

Caseificio Borderi

Grab a monster, custom-made panino loaded with fresh mozzarella and local meats, easily shareable for the whole family.

Ke Palle

Try the kid-friendly arancine (fried rice balls) stuffed with classic meat ragù or simple butter and cheese.

Gelateria Adika

Treat the kids to artisanal, all-natural gelato flavours like wild strawberry or sweet Sicilian pistachio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a sturdy, compact stroller for paved city centers, but switch to a baby carrier for ancient ruins and volcanic gravel paths. Sicilians absolutely adore children, so don't hesitate to ask restaurants to simplify dishes or warm up baby bottles.
