What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Duomo di Milano Rooftop via Elevator

Take the dedicated fast-track elevator to the terraces to admire the marble spires; the wide stone pathways are stroller-friendly, though a few steps require carrying, making it a breathtaking, breezy morning escape for the family.

Late morning 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Parco Sempione

This sprawling English-style park is entirely stroller-accessible with wide paved paths, offering mature shade trees, peaceful duck ponds, and quiet grassy spots perfect for Mary Claire's tummy time.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM

Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

Walk through the monumental glass-vaulted arcade which features smooth, flat mosaic floors that are perfect for strollers, offering a sheltered, climate-controlled space to admire luxury storefronts.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Pinacoteca di Brera

This world-class art museum features wide, quiet hallways and full elevator access, creating a soothing, climate-controlled environment that is ideal for a peaceful stroller nap while you admire masterpieces.

Afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM

Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli

A historic public garden featuring winding shaded pathways, beautiful fountains, and quiet play areas where you can relax under a canopy of historic trees away from city traffic.

Late morning 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Orto Botanico di Brera

A hidden, romantic botanical garden tucked behind the Brera palace that offers flat dirt paths, complete silence, and lush sensory plants that will captivate a calm infant.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM

Villa Necchi Campiglio Gardens

Explore the exquisite, peaceful gardens of this famous 1930s villa, which feature quiet, shaded benches and a calm poolside cafe area where families can relax away from the crowds.

Morning 10:30 AM - 12:00 PM

Triennale di Milano

A highly spacious modern design museum located inside Parco Sempione featuring wide ramps, large galleries, elevator access, and a very quiet, family-friendly cafe overlooking the park.

Late afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Piazza Gae Aulenti

A futuristic, completely pedestrianized circular piazza raised above street level, offering flat, smooth surfaces for strollers and captivating stepping-stone water fountains that baby Mary Claire will love watching.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ratanà

Enjoy their legendary saffron risotto in a restored historic building situated next to a modern park; they offer spacious outdoor terrace seating, highchairs, and a highly welcoming staff for early dinner seatings.

Biffi Caffè

A historic institution inside the Galleria offering elegant, outdoor corridor seating with plenty of room for a stroller, excellent Milanese veal cutlets, and a lively but gentle atmosphere.

Paper Moon

An upscale, chic dining spot that welcomes families warmly during their early 7:30 PM dinner service; request a table in the beautiful, quiet garden courtyard which easily accommodates strollers.

Giacomo Caffè

Located inside the majestic Palazzo Reale, this sophisticated cafe offers a quiet, elegant setting with wide-spaced tables, making it incredibly easy to park a stroller for a luxurious lunch.

Cantina della Vetra

A beloved local tavern near the ancient columns of San Lorenzo, offering incredibly welcoming service, highchairs, a relaxed early evening atmosphere, and delicious handmade pasta.
