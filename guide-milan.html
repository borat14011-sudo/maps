Milan

← back to travel board
Milan welcomes families with a vibrant blend of futuristic skylines, historic piazzas, and quiet, leafy escapes. Beyond the high fashion lies a playground of hands-on museums, sprawling parks, and delicious street eats that satisfy even the pickiest eaters. It is a city where ancient tram rides become thrilling adventures and every square holds the promise of a perfect gelato.
🚕 Getting there: ~50 min taxi or a 40-minute Malpensa Express train ride to Milano Centrale.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Duomo di Milano Terraces

Climb or take the elevator to the cathedral roof for a magical walk among spires with breathtaking city views. Toddler note: Strollers aren't allowed on the terraces, so bring a baby carrier for little ones.

Afternoon / 2h

Sempione Park (Parco Sempione)

Milan's green heart features duck ponds, wide paved paths perfect for strollers, and a great playground. Toddler note: There is a vintage trackless train ride and plenty of grass to run around safely.

Morning / 1.5h

Sforza Castle (Castello Sforzesco)

This majestic fortress features massive courtyards where kids can run freely and explore ancient stone walls. Toddler note: The spacious, car-free inner courts are perfect for toddlers to burn off energy safely.

Morning / 2h

MUBA - Museo dei Bambini Milano

Located in a historic church complex, this children's museum offers sensory exhibits and creative, open-ended play installations. Toddler note: They have dedicated sensory workshops specifically designed for toddlers and infants.

Afternoon / 2.5h

Leonardo da Vinci National Museum of Science and Technology

A massive museum showcasing real steam trains, submarines, and historical flying machines. Toddler note: The giant indoor train pavilion is a massive hit for toddlers obsessed with locomotives.

Morning (Tuesday/Saturday) / 1.5h

Mercato di Via Fauche

A vibrant, bustling local street market filled with fresh colorful fruits, cheeses, and warm bakery items. Toddler note: Keep toddlers in a stroller here as the narrow market lanes can get very crowded with shoppers.

Afternoon / 2h

Indro Montanelli Public Gardens

Home to shady pathways, beautiful fountains, and play areas, this park is ideal for a relaxed family afternoon. Toddler note: Little ones will love the old-fashioned carousel and the small amusement rides scattered throughout.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Navigli District Canal Walk

Stroll along the historic canals, which are wonderfully traffic-free during the daytime and lined with art shops. Toddler note: Enjoy an early afternoon walk before the evening aperitivo crowds arrive, making it easy to navigate with a stroller.

Morning / 1h

Civic Aquarium of Milan (Acquario Civico)

Set in a beautiful Art Nouveau building, this small but charming aquarium showcases Mediterranean marine life in intimate, easy-to-view tanks. Toddler note: The compact size is perfect for short toddler attention spans and tired little legs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Luini

Grab their famous, warm Panzerotti—pillowy fried dough pockets stuffed with melted mozzarella and tomato sauce.

Pizzeria Spontini

Treat the kids to thick, fluffy, slice-of-heaven Milanese-style pizza dripping with stringy mozzarella.

Gelateria della Musica

Try the incredibly creamy pistachio or banana gelato, made fresh with high-quality natural ingredients.

Ratanà

Order the child-friendly classic Risotto alla Milanese, a velvety saffron rice dish served in a welcoming, modern osteria.

Pasticceria Marchesi 1824

Enjoy a decadent hot chocolate and delicate pastries in a stunning, fairytale-like green pastel dining room.

👨‍👩‍👧 Many of Milan's historic streets and tram boarding steps are not stroller-friendly, so a lightweight, easily foldable stroller or a baby carrier is highly recommended. Italians typically dine late, so look for pizzerias that open by 7:00 PM or rely on local bakeries (panifici) for early evening toddler snacks.
