Terceira Island

Terceira combines dramatic volcanic landscapes with the fairytale charm of Angra do Heroísmo's rainbow-colored streets. Little ones will love splashing in calm natural lava pools and running through lush, exotic gardens. It is a slow-paced Azorean paradise where family adventures are safe, highly accessible, and endlessly magical.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min drive by rental car or taxi from Lajes Airport (TER) to Angra do Heroísmo hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Algar do Carvão

Descend directly into the mossy chimney of an extinct volcano, an awe-inspiring cavern with a crystal-clear underground lake. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the dripping water and green ferns, but use a baby carrier as there are many wet steps.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Duke of Terceira Garden

This terraced botanical wonderland in the heart of Angra features exotic plants, paved pathways, and cheeky resident ducks. It is a brilliant spot for toddlers to stretch their legs safely, leading up to a fantastic panoramic city viewpoint.

Morning / 2-3h

Biscoitos Natural Pools

Swim in incredible black basalt lava pools cooled by the Atlantic Ocean, featuring built-in concrete steps and sunbathing platforms. There are shallow, protected wading areas that are absolutely perfect for toddlers to splash safely under supervision.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Monte Brasil Reserve

Drive up this extinct volcanic peninsula to find lush forests, sweeping ocean vistas, and a fenced animal park housing gentle deer. Kids will love feeding the deer and burning off energy at the large, scenic wooden playground.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Angra do Heroísmo

A lively covered market bursting with sweet local pineapples, fresh cheeses, and fragrant flowers. It is completely stroller-accessible and the vendors love offering sweet fruit slices to passing toddlers.

Afternoon / 1h

Gruta do Natal

Explore a fascinating underground lava tube cave system with flat, well-lit wooden walkways. Strollers won't fit here, but you can strap your toddler into a front carrier and borrow a tiny safety helmet at the entrance.

Morning / 2-3h

Praia da Vitória Sandy Beach

Enjoy a rare, wide sandy beach in the Azores with incredibly calm, shallow harbor waters ideal for young swimmers. The flat paved promenade is perfect for stroller strolls and has ice cream shops steps from the sand.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Queijaria Vaquinha

Visit the island's oldest cheese dairy to taste mild, creamy local cheeses while watching cows graze in the adjacent fields. The casual patio has plenty of space for toddlers to roam while you enjoy a local craft beer and cheese board.

Morning / 3h

Whale Watching from Angra Harbour

Embark on a marine safari to spot wild dolphins, sperm whales, and sea turtles playing in the Atlantic. Choose a large catamaran tour option as they offer a much smoother, safer, and wind-sheltered ride for toddlers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Beira Mar

Order the fresh grilled limpets (lapas) sizzling in garlic butter and the catch-of-the-day fish filet, which is easy for toddlers to chew.

O Carvão

Get the traditional beef Alcatra, a melt-in-the-mouth pot roast slow-cooked in a red wine clay pot that kids will find tender and delicious.

Quinta do Martelo

Order the organic, farm-to-table historic buffet featuring sweet bread and slow-roasted beef inside a beautifully recreated 19th-century hamlet.

O Terminal

Get the famous regional steak cooked to perfection on a hot stone, served alongside crispy golden fries that toddlers will happily share.

Birou Bar

Get the prego no bolo do caco, a delicious steak sandwich served inside warm, sweet local sweet potato bread that is perfect for a quick family lunch.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a high-quality baby carrier instead of a bulky stroller, as the historic cobblestones of Angra and volcanic trails can be highly uneven. Weather changes quickly in the Azores, so always dress your little ones in layers and pack windproof jackets even in summer.
