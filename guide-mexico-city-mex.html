Mexico City (MEX)

← back to travel board
Mexico City welcomes families with its colorful neighborhoods, sprawling urban forests, and rich cultural tapestry. From floating gardens to interactive museums, this high-altitude capital offers a sensory playground for all ages. It is a city where ancient ruins and modern parks blend seamlessly, making every outing an exciting discovery.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-45 minutes via authorized airport taxi (such as Porto Taxi or Nueva Imagen) to central neighborhoods like Condesa, Roma, or Polanco.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Bosque de Chapultepec

This massive green park features lakes with pedal boats, shaded paths, and a free zoo. It's perfect for letting toddlers run free on the grass away from city traffic.

Afternoon / 3h

Papalote Museo del Niño

A world-class interactive children's museum with hands-on science, art, and sensory exhibits. Toddlers will love the dedicated 'Pequeños' zone designed just for early childhood play.

Morning / 3h

Xochimilco Canals

Ride on colorful wooden trajinera boats along ancient Aztec canals while listening to mariachi music. Kids will love watching the passing puppet boats, and you can easily step off to stretch little legs.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Coyoacán and Parque Centenario

Explore this bohemian, cobblestone neighborhood filled with ice cream vendors, street performers, and a central fountain. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles in the pedestrian plazas.

Midday / 1-2h

Mercado de Coyoacán

A vibrant, bustling market filled with colorful toys, fresh fruits, and delicious tostadas. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the hanging piñatas and sweet-smelling fruit stalls.

Morning / 2h

Acuario Inbursa

Mexico's largest underground aquarium showcases sharks, penguins, and touch tanks filled with marine life. It is completely stroller-friendly and a great air-conditioned escape on a rainy afternoon.

Morning / 4-5h

Teotihuacan Pyramids

Walk the ancient Avenue of the Dead and marvel at the colossal Pyramids of the Sun and Moon. Bring a sturdy carrier for toddlers as the archaeological site is wide open and stroller-unfriendly.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque La Mexicana

A stunning, ultra-modern park with high-tech playgrounds, a small lake, and designated areas for kids. Toddlers will love the soft-turf play equipment and watching the skyscrapers from the grassy hills.

Morning / 1-2h

Biblioteca Vasconcelos

A jaw-dropping mega-library with hovering bookshelves and a massive whale skeleton hanging from the ceiling. There is a cozy, quiet children's section perfect for a sensory break with picture books.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Cardenal

Order the freshly baked conchas with clotted cream and the hot chocolate frothed tableside.

Churrería El Moro

Treat the family to classic, crispy churros dusted in sugar and dipped in rich, warm chocolate.

Lalo!

Sit at the long communal table under vibrant murals to enjoy their famous French toast and kid-friendly artisanal pizzas.

Azul Histórico

Dine under the open-air trees of a historic courtyard while enjoying mild, delicious chicken tamales and handmade quesadillas.

Tacos El Huequito

Grab quick, legendary tacos al pastor shaved fresh off the spit, which are small enough and mild enough for toddlers to enjoy.
