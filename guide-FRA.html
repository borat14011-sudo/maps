Frankfurt

← back to travel board
Frankfurt surprises families with its dazzling skyline framing historic, cobblestone plazas and lush green oases. The Main River flows gently through the city, offering wide paths perfect for strollers and energetic kids. It’s an easy-to-navigate destination where modern science museums, quiet parks, and hearty, kid-friendly treats await.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Römerberg

This historic square features colorful, half-timbered houses and a car-free space perfect for running around. Toddlers will love watching the street performers and chasing pigeons near the Fountain of Justice.

Morning / 2-3h

Senckenberg Natural History Museum

One of Germany's largest natural history museums, it boasts massive dinosaur skeletons and a taxidermy gallery. Toddlers will be wide-eyed at the life-sized T-Rex statue right at the entrance.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Palmengarten

A massive botanical paradise with a historic greenhouse, giant water lilies, and a lovely playground. Toddlers will adore riding the Palmen-Express miniature steam train that chugs through the park.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Main River Promenade

This scenic, pedestrian-only path along the river is ideal for scooter rides, stroller walks, and picnics under the trees. Toddlers can safely watch the passing boats and play in the grassy patches.

Midday / 1h

Kleinmarkthalle

A bustling, sensory-rich indoor market filled with colorful fruit stalls, fresh pastries, and friendly vendors. Toddlers will enjoy picking out a soft pretzel or a fresh strawberry cup.

Morning / 3h

Frankfurt Zoo

An urban zoo featuring over 4,500 animals, an outstanding nocturnal house, and multiple play areas. Its flat, compact layout makes it incredibly easy for little toddler legs to explore without getting overtired.

Afternoon / 2h

Günthersburgpark

A beloved local park featuring massive green lawns, a giant adventure playground, and refreshing water fountains. In the summer, toddlers can splash safely in the dedicated wet-play areas.

Morning / 1h

Main Tower

Ride the high-speed elevator 200 meters up to the open-air observation deck for a breathtaking view of the skyscrapers. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the toy-like cars and tiny trains moving below.

Morning / 2-3h

Experiminta ScienceCenter

A hands-on science center where kids are encouraged to touch, build, and experiment. Toddlers can play with giant soap bubbles, mirrors, and soft building blocks in the dedicated early-years zones.

Where to eat

  • Apfelwein Wagner — A traditional, lively tavern where parents can try local apple wine while kids feast on giant, freshly baked soft pretzels and mild frankfurters.
  • Schreiber-Heyne at Kleinmarkthalle — Head to the famous Fleischwurst stall inside the market for a warm, savory sausage slice that is a legendary local snack and perfect for picky toddlers.
  • Café Liebfrauenberg — A classic, spacious café with outdoor seating near a fountain, offering delicious German cakes and easy finger foods for little ones.
  • Pizzeria Montana — A hip, family-friendly pizzeria serving authentic wood-fired Neapolitan pizzas with a cartoon mascot that kids will love.
