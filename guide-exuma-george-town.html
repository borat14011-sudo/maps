Sapphire waters and swimming pigs for family adventures

Exuma (George Town)

← back to travel board
With its blindingly blue waters, powdery sandbars, and friendly marine life, Great Exuma is a tropical playground that feels like a real-world fantasy. Families can wade in waist-deep shallows, meet famous swimming pigs, and discover a slow-paced island paradise where stress melts away. It is the ultimate laid-back escape for parents and little adventurers alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or rental car from Exuma International Airport (GGT) to George Town, or ~30 min to Great Exuma beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 4-5h

Pig Beach Excursion

Take a boat charter to swim with the famous, friendly pigs in the crystal-clear shallows of Big Major Cay. Toddlers will love watching them splash from the safety of the boat or the shallow shore.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Chat 'N' Chill on Stocking Island

Take a short water taxi to this iconic beach hangout where kids can play in the calm, shallow water and see wild stingrays gliding by. Toddlers can safely splash in the footprint-free sand while parents relax with a cold drink.

Morning (during low tide) / 2-3h

Coco Plum Beach

Famous for its vast, shallow sandbars at low tide, this beach is perfect for collecting sand dollars. Toddlers can safely wade hundreds of yards out in water that barely reaches their ankles.

Morning / 1h

George Town Straw Market

Browse local handmade straw baskets, hats, and colorful toys in the heart of George Town. It is a sensory-friendly, quick walk that lets kids pick out a unique, lightweight souvenir.

Morning / 2-3h

Tropic of Cancer Beach

Cross the one-lane bridge to Little Exuma to visit this stunning, crescent-shaped beach with powdery white sand. The gentle shore is great for sandcastle building, though shade is limited so bring a beach umbrella.

Morning / 1-2h

Feeding Iguanas at Bitter Guana Cay

Take a boat ride to see the prehistoric-looking Northern Bahamian Rock Iguanas that run down to the beach to greet visitors. Keep toddlers in carriers or close by as the iguanas can move surprisingly fast for fruit.

Morning / 3h

Kayaking at Moriah Harbour Cay National Park

Explore the calm, protected mangrove channels and pristine sandbanks teeming with baby sharks and sea turtles. Single or double kayaks make it easy to paddle with a toddler sitting comfortably in your lap.

Afternoon / 2h

Starfish Beach

Search for massive, colorful red starfish resting in the calm, knee-deep waters of this secluded beach area. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant sea stars, but remind them to keep them underwater.

Morning / 4h

Elizabeth Harbour Pontoon Boat Rental

Rent your own easy-to-drive pontoon boat to cruise the ultra-calm harbor at your family's own pace. The high railings and flat deck keep toddlers completely safe while you find your own private beach.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shirley's Fish Fry

Get the incredibly fresh cracked conch or grilled snapper at this beloved, colorful shack at the George Town Fish Fry.

Santanna's Bar and Grill

Located in Little Exuma, this oceanside spot serves up legendary deep-fried lobster tails that even picky kids will love.

Rusty Anchor

Enjoy a relaxed family dinner of coconut shrimp and stone crab claws at this harbor-view restaurant inside February Point.

Splash Beach Bar & Grill

Located at Hideaways Exuma, this ultra-casual spot features kid favorites like burgers and pizza right next to a family-friendly pool.

Mom's Bakery

Stop by this legendary Little Exuma roadside shack for warm, sweet coconut bread that makes a perfect toddler-approved snack.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat to easily explore Great and Little Exuma, as taxis can be expensive and lack child restraints. Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen, swim diapers, and snacks, as local grocery stores have limited inventory and high island prices.
