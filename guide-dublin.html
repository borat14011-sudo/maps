From spotting wild deer in the heart of the city to exploring ancient castle grounds, the Irish capital blends rich heritage with easy, kid-friendly charm. It is a city where every corner holds a story, easily navigated with little ones in tow.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Phoenix Park & Dublin Zoo

Roam one of Europe's largest enclosed city parks to spot wild fallow deer before visiting the world-class Dublin Zoo. Toddlers will love the dedicated play areas and the city farm section.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Dublin Castle & Dubh Linn Gardens

Explore the colorful courtyard of this historic castle and let the kids run free on the wide circular lawns of the Dubh Linn Gardens behind it. The flat grassy area is perfect for toddlers to safely stretch their legs.

Morning / 1-2h

St. Stephen's Green

Feed the resident ducks and swans at the Victorian lake before heading to the excellent, fully-fenced playground. Toddlers will delight in the sensory garden and the abundance of grassy picnic spots.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Temple Bar Food Market

Sample delicious local cheeses, fresh baked pastries, and hot crepes in this bustling outdoor market. It's a sensory delight for kids, but visit early in the morning to beat the crowded lunchtime rush with a stroller.

Morning / 2-3h

Imaginosity, Dublin Children's Museum

A hands-on, creative museum specifically designed for children under nine, featuring a supermarket, a climbing tree, and a construction zone. There is a dedicated, safe soft-play area specifically for babies and toddlers.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Malahide Castle and Gardens

Just a short train ride away, this medieval castle features a magical fairy trail in the woodlands and a massive state-of-the-art playground. Toddlers will love hunting for fairy houses hidden in the trees.

Afternoon / 2h

EPIC The Irish Emigration Museum

This highly interactive museum brings history to life with digital stamp passports, touch screens, and vibrant displays that keep older kids engaged. The spacious, ramped vaults are exceptionally stroller-friendly for those with toddlers.

Morning / 2h

National Botanic Gardens

Stroll through spectacular glasshouses and lush lawns filled with squirrels and exotic plants. The wide, paved paths are perfect for strollers, and the resident squirrels are a guaranteed hit with toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Howth Harbor and Cliff Walk

Take the DART train north to this charming fishing village to watch harbor seals swim right up to the pier. Stick to the flat pier walk with a stroller, which offers beautiful sea views without the steep cliff climbs.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Woollen Mills

Order the traditional Irish beef stew or a hearty sausage roll while enjoying views of the River Liffey from their family-friendly, spacious dining room.

Milano (Dawson Street)

Get the famous Dough Balls and a customized kid's pizza from their excellent, highly accommodating children's menu.

Queen of Tarts

Treat the family to their legendary warm blackberry and apple crumble or fluffy scones with clotted cream in a cozy, welcoming cafe setting.

Sano Pizza (Temple Bar)

Enjoy delicious, fast-fired Neapolitan margherita pizzas that arrive quickly enough to keep even the most impatient toddlers happy.

Brother Hubbard (North)

Feast on a vibrant Middle Eastern-inspired brunch like their famous baked eggs, featuring plenty of high chairs and a spacious, stroller-friendly courtyard.

👨‍👩‍👧 Invest in a lightweight, sturdy stroller as Dublin's historic cobblestones and public transit are best navigated with compact gear. Always carry a light rain jacket for the family, as the Irish weather can quickly shift from sunny skies to sudden, brief showers.
