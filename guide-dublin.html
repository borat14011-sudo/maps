Dublin

← back to travel board
Dublin blends rich literary history and compact walkability with an incredibly welcoming, family-first Irish culture. For a Houston dad, it offers a refreshing, temperate escape where grand Georgian parks seamlessly transition into premium dining and historic lanes. Navigating the city is highly efficient, with an abundance of manicured green spaces that keep your toddler active and happy. It is a sophisticated yet deeply relaxed destination where a pint of stout and a baby bottle can happily share the same table.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-30 minute taxi ride from Dublin Airport to city center hotels, easily booked via the FreeNow app or taken from the licensed taxi rank outside the terminals.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

St Stephen's Green

Feed the resident ducks at the Victorian lake and let your toddler stretch her legs on the expansive, well-kept lawns. The paved paths are perfectly smooth for strollers, and the playground features a dedicated, secure toddler section.

Morning / 3h

Dublin Zoo in Phoenix Park

Located in the massive Phoenix Park, this world-class zoo features wide, flat paths and fantastic animal viewing heights perfect for a toddler. The African Plains section offers plenty of space to run around and excellent baby changing facilities.

Afternoon / 2h

National Botanic Gardens

Wander through stunning, restored Victorian glasshouses and serene, wide-paved garden paths that are exceptionally stroller-friendly. The resident squirrels and large open lawns will keep your toddler enchanted while you enjoy a premium coffee from the Garden Tearoom.

Morning / 2h

EPIC The Irish Emigration Museum

Located in the historic CHQ building, this fully accessible, single-level museum features interactive touch screens that captivate toddlers while adults absorb Ireland's emigration history. It is completely indoor, step-free, and offers plenty of room to navigate a wide stroller.

Morning / 3h

Airfield Estate

Take a short Luas tram ride to this 38-acre working urban farm where your little one can get up close with milk cows, hens, and pigs. The estate features extremely wide paths, a dedicated soft-play area, and a fantastic farm-to-fork cafe.

Morning / 1h

The Little Museum of Dublin

This quirky, intimate museum overlooking St. Stephen's Green offers a rapid-fire 30-minute history of Dublin that is engaging without testing an infant's patience. Note that you must leave your stroller in their secure ground-floor hallway and use a baby carrier for the stairs.

Afternoon / 2h

Dun Laoghaire East Pier Walk

Ride the DART train south to this iconic seaside pier for a breezy, perfectly flat walk with stunning views of the Irish Sea. Treat the family to Teddy's Ice Cream at the end, a legendary local spot with easy stroller access.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Temple Bar Food Market

Visit on a Saturday morning to experience an open-air food market under a massive retractable canopy that keeps the rain off your stroller. Grab a fresh artisanal pastry and find a quiet bench where your toddler can people-watch in a vibrant, safe setting.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Merrion Square Park

Famous for its colorful Georgian doors and the Oscar Wilde statue, this park features a fantastic giant-themed wooden playground perfect for toddlers. It is much quieter than St Stephen's Green, offering a peaceful sanctuary with smooth gravel paths for stroller naps.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Woollen Mills

Order the Irish beef ribeye or their famous Irish coddle while enjoying their exceptionally spacious booths that easily accommodate high chairs. They open early, making it perfect for jet-lagged toddler wake-ups right by the River Liffey.

Fallon & Byrne

Head to the spacious basement cellar or the main floor restaurant for incredible artisan charcuterie, fresh pasta, and wine. It is upscale yet bustling and noisy enough to drown out any sudden toddler outbursts.

Brother Hubbard (North)

Feast on their signature Turkish Eggs Menemen in their massive, bright, and highly stroller-accessible courtyard. The staff are famously welcoming to families, providing high chairs and babyccinos without hesitation.

FX Buckley Crow Street

Enjoy a premium dry-aged Irish ribeye in a warm, plush steakhouse environment that welcomes families during early evening slots. The booths are deep and comfortable for holding an infant, and the staff are incredibly accommodating.

Avoca Cafe Suffolk Street

Head to the top-floor cafe of this beautiful department store for legendary seafood chowder and home-baked scones. It features a spacious elevator, plenty of high chairs, and a lively daytime atmosphere where baby noises blend right in.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Merrion Hotel

This ultra-luxury Georgian hotel offers spacious family suites, gorgeous private gardens for toddler walks, and a stunning indoor pool. They provide high-end wooden cribs, baby-proofing kits, and can easily arrange certified babysitting services through the concierge.

Anantara The Marker Dublin Hotel

Located in the clean, modern docklands area, this hotel features wide hallways, large family rooms, and an exceptional infinity pool. It is situated right next to a flat, pedestrianized plaza, making evening stroller walks effortless and peaceful.

The Westbury

Steps from Grafton Street, this iconic hotel combines five-star luxury with genuine family warmth, offering spacious rooms and top-tier cribs. Their superb concierge can source any baby supplies you need, and the lobby afternoon tea is a relaxed treat even with an infant.
