San Pedro

← back to travel board
San Pedro offers a laid-back, golf-cart-only pace that perfectly matches the rhythm of traveling with an infant. The barrier reef keeps the eastern beaches virtually wave-free, creating safe, shallow tide pools for tiny feet to splash. With highly accessible upscale condo resorts and exceptionally warm, family-oriented locals, you get a seamless, stress-free Caribbean getaway without the typical logistical headaches.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-minute scenic flight from BZE to San Pedro (SPR) via Tropic Air, followed by a 10-to-15-minute open-air golf cart taxi ride directly to your resort.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Secret Beach

This leeward-side beach features crystal-clear, waist-deep water with absolutely no waves, making it a giant, warm wading pool for your toddler. Leave the stroller at your golf cart, as the sandy shoreline is best navigated on foot or with a baby carrier.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

The Truck Stop

An upbeat shipping-container food park featuring a shallow swimming pool, outdoor games, and a wooden boardwalk over the lagoon. It is entirely flat and stroller-friendly, making it easy to grab a craft beer and local eats while your toddler watches the sunset.

Afternoon / 1h

Belize Chocolate Company

A delicious, air-conditioned haven in the heart of town specializing in bean-to-bar Belizean chocolates. The shop is small but easily accommodates a quick stroller roll-in for a cool-down chocolate milkshake and cookie.

Morning / 1-2h

San Pedro Central Park

A lively beachside park in town featuring a colorful playground where your toddler can swing and slide alongside local kids. The concrete plaza is perfect for strollers, and parents can grab fresh coconut water from nearby street vendors.

Morning / 4h

Private Snorkel & Sandbar Charter with Seaduced by Belize

A customized private boat charter that takes you to shallow sandbars where your baby can safely splash in inches of calm ocean water. The spacious catamarans provide plenty of shaded deck space for stroller naps while parents take turns snorkeling the reef.

Morning / 1h

Caye Coffee Roasting Co.

Stop by this local roastery to pick up freshly bagged Belizean coffee beans and enjoy a smooth, cold brew on their shaded patio. The flat, gravel-free entrance makes it easy to park your stroller while you chat with the friendly roasters.

Afternoon / 1h

The Gallery of San Pedro

Located inside Fido's Courtyard, this massive indoor art gallery features thousands of vibrant Belizean paintings and wood carvings. The wide, flat indoor layout offers a cool, air-conditioned respite to push a stroller while admiring local art.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Sunset Catamaran Cruise with El Gato Charters

A gentle, slow-paced sail along the barrier reef during the golden hour, offering a cool sea breeze and gorgeous views. The smooth motion of the catamaran is highly soothing and almost guarantees a peaceful nap for an infant in a baby carrier.

Evening / 2h

Hermit Crab Races at Crazy Canucks Beach Bar

A lighthearted, highly popular local tradition where kids and parents cheer on numbered hermit crabs on a giant table. It starts early in the evening and is incredibly family-friendly, though you'll want to use a carrier to navigate the sandy beach-bar floor.

🍽️ Where to eat

Elvi's Kitchen

Order the Mayan Fish cooked in a banana leaf while your toddler plays happily in the unique, clean sand floor underneath your table.

Blue Water Grill

Savor the wood-fired snapper pizza on the spacious beachside deck, which offers plenty of room for high chairs and a grassy lawn for restless toddlers.

Estel's Dine By the Sea

Feast on world-famous breakfast fry jacks and ranchero eggs right on the beach; their early 6:00 AM opening time is a lifesaver for early-rising babies.

El Fogon

Enjoy slow-cooked Belizean split-pea soup and stewed chicken under a spacious, breezy open-air palapa that provides natural white noise for fussy infants.

Rain Rooftop Restaurant

Indulge in a premium seafood paella with stunning sunset reef views, made highly accessible for strollers via the resort's modern elevator.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Phoenix Resort

Located right in downtown San Pedro, this luxury resort offers massive multi-bedroom suites, a dedicated kids' club, and complimentary baby gear including cribs and high chairs.

Grand Caribe Belize

Situated on a quiet stretch north of the bridge, this resort features spacious, high-end condos, multiple shallow-entry pools perfect for toddlers, and a convenient on-site grocery store.

Victoria House Resort & Spa

A luxurious, peaceful retreat south of town featuring manicured grassy lawns for baby crawling, pristine private pools, and extremely attentive staff who gladly arrange cribs and private babysitting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a 4-seater gas golf cart from a reputable dealer like Carts Belize, and bring a portable travel harness or clip-on seat since carts are the sole transport here. Trade your bulky stroller for a rugged baby carrier, as the sandy beach paths and bumpy cobblestone town streets are highly unforgiving to small wheels. Stock up on high-quality diapers and organic baby snacks at Super Buy South or Lino's Supermarket in town, as resort boutiques carry very limited, expensive baby supplies.
