Color, history, and playgrounds: Mexico City awaits your family.

Mexico City

← back to travel board
Mexico City unfolds as a sensory wonderland where ancient history meets lush, sprawling parks. Families will find endless joy wandering through vibrant markets, floating down ancient canals, and discovering world-class museums designed for curious minds. It is a warm, stroller-friendly metropolis where children are welcomed with open arms at every corner.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Chapultepec Park & Zoo

A massive green oasis with pedal boats, shaded paths, and a free zoo. Toddlers will love seeing the giant pandas and feeding the ducks near the lake.

Midday / 2-3h

Xochimilco Canal Cruise

Float down historic canals on colorful wooden boats while listening to lively marimba music. It's an easy, relaxing ride for toddlers, and you can buy snacks directly from passing boats.

Afternoon / 3h

Papalote Museo del Niño

An incredible interactive children's museum featuring giant bubble-making stations and hands-on science exhibits. The dedicated toddler zone is perfect for safe, sensory-focused play.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Coyoacán and Parque Centenario

Stroll through this bohemian neighborhood to see the famous coyote fountain and lively street performers. Toddlers can safely run around the pedestrian-only plazas while parents enjoy fresh street food.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado de Coyoacán

A bustling indoor market filled with colorful toys, fresh fruits, and amazing tostadas. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the hanging pinatas and bright displays.

Early Morning / 4-5h

Teotihuacan Pyramids

Marvel at the towering Pyramids of the Sun and Moon in this ancient archaeological site. The wide, flat pathways are great for sturdy strollers, though toddlers will need carrier rides for the steps.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Lincoln

This upscale park features a dedicated, gated playground and ponds where hobbyists sail model boats. It is a peaceful, ultra-clean spot for toddlers to burn off energy safely.

Morning / 2-3h

National Museum of Anthropology

Explore Mexico's rich history through massive artifacts, including the famous Aztec Sun Stone. The beautiful outdoor courtyards offer plenty of space for toddlers to toddle freely without disturbing museum-goers.

Morning / 2h

Acuario Inbursa

A world-class underground aquarium filled with sharks, penguins, and interactive touch pools. It’s a perfect rainy-day escape that keeps toddlers completely captivated by the glowing marine life.

Where to eat

  • El Moro Churreria — Indulge in warm, crispy churros dipped in rich chocolate, a sweet treat that kids and parents will absolutely adore.
  • Lalo! — A lively, family-friendly brunch spot featuring a massive colorful mural, incredible French toast, and a welcoming communal table.
  • Tacos El Huequito — Try their famous, mild tacos al pastor rolled up tightly, making them delicious and easy for little hands to hold.
  • Mercado Roma — A trendy gourmet food hall where parents can choose from diverse food stalls while kids enjoy artisanal popsicles in the open-air seating.
