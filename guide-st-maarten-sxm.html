St. Maarten (SXM)

← back to travel board
St. Maarten offers a unique dual-nation charm, blending sophisticated French gastronomy with relaxed Dutch convenience that makes traveling with a toddler seamless. Renting a car allows you to easily hop between ultra-calm, wave-free bays like Le Galion and world-class dining spots where baby noise is easily masked by the ocean breeze. It is an upscale yet incredibly accessible escape for a young family seeking both luxury and practicality.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 minute rental car drive from Princess Juliana International Airport (SXM) straight to the family-friendly resorts of Simpson Bay or Grand Case.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Parrot Ville Bird Sanctuary

An interactive aviary where your toddler can see colorful tropical birds up close and even feed them from a cup of seed. The pathways are flat and easy to navigate with a compact travel stroller.

Morning / 2-3h

Le Galion Beach (Baby Beach)

Protected by a coral reef, this shallow, wave-free bay is widely considered the safest swimming spot on the island for infants and toddlers. Bring a beach tent for shade, as natural cover is limited near the water's edge.

Afternoon / 3h

Friar's Bay Beach

A scenic, sheltered beach with calm waters and two excellent beach cafes offering lounge chair rentals and shade. The sand is soft and perfect for a toddler's first beach sensory play experience.

Morning / 2h

Seaside Nature Park

A 30-acre coastal nature park featuring a delightful petting zoo where little ones can feed ducks, goats, and miniature pigs. The dirt paths are bumpy, so opt for a baby carrier over a stroller here.

Morning / 3h

Loterie Farm

A lush tropical reserve at the foot of Pic Paradis featuring beautiful spring-fed pools and peaceful jungle canopies. While the zip-lines are for older kids, families can rent a private cabana at the L'eau Lounge pool area for a relaxing morning.

Morning / 1-2h

Marigot Market

An open-air Wednesday and Saturday market filled with vibrant colors, fresh tropical fruits, and local crafts. The paved waterfront promenade makes for an incredibly easy and scenic stroller walk.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Emilio Wilson Park

A historic estate turned public park featuring wide-open green lawns under massive, shady trees that are perfect for a family picnic. The paved pathways are highly stroller-accessible, offering a peaceful break from the beach.

Midday / 1-2h

Maho Beach Plane Spotting from Sunset Bar

Watch the world-famous low-flying jet arrivals from the safety and comfort of an outdoor restaurant patio rather than the crowded beach. Keep baby's ears protected with noise-canceling headphones due to the loud jet blasts.

Evening / 1-2h

Grand Case Boulevard Sunset Stroll

An evening walk along the main strip of the culinary capital of the Caribbean, which is highly pedestrian-friendly. Time it during the golden hour to catch a stunning sunset before grabbing an early family dinner.

🍽️ Where to eat

Rainbow Café

Order the fresh tuna tartare or the Shakshuka while your baby relaxes on a shaded daybed right on the beach. The chic, open-air deck has plenty of space for strollers and a lively daytime vibe that easily masks any toddler fussiness.

Karakter Beach Bar

Savor their famous Dutch-style bitterballen and fresh mahi-mahi wraps while sitting at a table directly in the sand. It's incredibly relaxed, allowing your toddler to play with sand toys right at your feet during lunch.

Talk of the Town

A legendary open-air 'Lolo' serving up smoky, slow-cooked ribs and Johnny cakes. The casual, bustling picnic-table setup is highly forgiving of messy toddlers, and the food is served almost instantly.

Pineapple Pete

Try the herb-crusted rack of lamb or the classic lobster thermidor at this spacious, bustling island institution. With live music, a high-energy atmosphere, and an extensive kids' menu, it is a stress-free spot for an early family dinner.

La Villa Restaurant

Indulge in classic French cuisine like duck breast with raspberry sauce in an upscale yet incredibly warm and family-welcoming environment. They open early at 6:00 PM, making it easy to enjoy a fine-dining experience before toddler bedtime.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Case Beach Club

Features fully equipped kitchens in every suite for easy baby food prep, two quiet private beaches with exceptionally calm water, and complimentary cribs. Its proximity to Grand Case Boulevard means you can walk to world-class dining with a stroller.

La Samanna, A Belmond Hotel

An ultra-luxury retreat offering spacious multi-bedroom villas, professional on-site babysitting services, and a stunning infinity pool. The calm, uncrowded waters of Baie Longue provide a serene setting for a luxury family getaway.

Simpson Bay Resort, Marina & Spa

Offers spacious villa-style accommodations with kitchens, laundry facilities, and a dedicated, shallow kids' pool. Its central location on the Dutch side means quick access to pharmacies and supermarkets for baby supplies.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car at the airport; having your own vehicle is crucial for quick escapes when a toddler meltdown strikes and allows you to easily store baby gear. Stick to French-side beaches like Le Galion for swimming, as the water is consistently calmer and safer for infants compared to the rougher Atlantic-facing shores. Stock up on diapers, organic baby food, and fresh milk at the Carrefour Market in Cole Bay, which offers the largest selection of American and European import goods.
