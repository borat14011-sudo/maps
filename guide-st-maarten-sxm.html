Two nations, endless sun, and perfect family beach days

St. Maarten (SXM)

← back to travel board
St. Maarten blends Dutch playfulness and French culinary flair into one effortlessly tropical, family-friendly Caribbean escape. Gentle turquoise waves lap against soft white sands while colorful planes soar overhead, sparking wonder in travelers of all ages. It is a dual-nation paradise where lush rainforest hills meet calm, shallow bays perfect for tiny splashers.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 30 min rental car or taxi to most major family resorts like Simpson Bay or Dawn Beach

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Maho Beach Plane Watching

Experience the thrill of watching massive jets land right over the beach. Toddlers will love the roaring engines and dramatic views, but bring noise-canceling headphones for sensitive ears.

Morning / 3-4h

Loterie Farm Jungle Pool

A lush nature sanctuary featuring a spring-fed pool, cascading waterfalls, and beautiful tropical gardens. It is a serene oasis for a gentle shaded walk, and toddlers will love paddling in the shallow spring water.

Morning / 2-3h

Mullet Bay Beach

This postcard-perfect beach offers calm, shallow waters and soft white sand that is ideal for building sandcastles. The shade of the sea grape trees provides a perfect spot for toddler naptime on the beach.

Morning / 1-2h

SXM Parrot Ville Bird Sanctuary

An open-air aviary where children can feed colorful, friendly parrots right from their hands. It is incredibly interactive, and toddlers will giggle as gentle birds perch on their shoulders.

Wednesday or Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Marigot open-air Market

Wander through this bustling French-side market filled with bright spices, local crafts, and fresh tropical fruits. Toddlers will enjoy the lively sights and sweet scents of fresh coconut and pastries.

Morning / 2-3h

Le Galion Beach (Baby Beach)

Protected by a coral reef, this extremely calm, shallow bay is practically wave-free, making it the safest beach on the island for toddlers to splash. Parents can relax easily while little ones wade out safely with no current.

Morning / 2-3h

Seaside Nature Park

A scenic coastal park where children can meet rescued farm animals at the petting zoo and go for gentle pony rides. It is a fantastic rustic escape with plenty of open space for little legs to run wild.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Grand Case Beach Stroll

Stroll along this iconic, colorful street lined with French bakeries and beachfront boutiques. Pop into a patisserie for a buttery croissant that will delight even the pickiest toddler eaters.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Philipsburg Boardwalk & Great Bay Beach

A wide, stroller-friendly paved boardwalk perfect for an easy walk alongside the sparkling blue Great Bay. Rent a beach chair, grab an ice cream, and let toddlers play in the gentle surf steps from the shops.

🍽️ Where to eat

Talk of the Town Lolo

Grab a plate of exceptionally flavorful, kid-friendly grilled ribs, BBQ chicken, and sweet local johnnycakes in a casual open-air beachfront setting.

Sint Maarten Yacht Club Bar & Restaurant

Order the delicious fish tacos or kids' chicken tenders while watching the mega-yachts pass through the drawbridge just feet away.

Rainbow Café

Enjoy a beachfront lunch of fresh poke bowls, tapas, and fluffy pancakes with your toes directly in the soft sand.

Karibuni Restaurant (Pinel Island)

Take a short ferry to Pinel Island and enjoy fresh grilled lobster or wood-fired pizza while toddlers splash in the shallow, crystal-clear lagoon.

Zee Best Bakery

Indulge in warm, freshly baked chocolate croissants and giant French crepes that make for a perfect family breakfast.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat to easily navigate between the French and Dutch sides, as taxis can quickly become expensive. Always pack reef-safe sunscreen and high-quality swim diapers, as natural shade can be limited on some of the more secluded beaches.
