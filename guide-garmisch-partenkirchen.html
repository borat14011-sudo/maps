Garmisch-Partenkirchen

← back to travel board
Garmisch-Partenkirchen delivers dramatic alpine crags and crystalline lakes without the rugged, inaccessible stress of remote peaks. The town's flat, beautifully paved valley floor allows you to push a high-end stroller effortlessly between frescoed Bavarian storefronts and mountain-view cafes. For a finance dad, it offers the perfect ROI: world-class infrastructure, efficient cable cars that whisk toddlers safely to panoramic peaks, and hearty, comforting cuisine that keeps everyone happy. It's the ultimate low-friction, high-reward European mountain escape for a young family.
🚕 Getting there: ~75 to 90 minutes via private luxury Mercedes V-Class transfer with a pre-booked toddler car seat from Munich Airport (MUC) directly down the A95 autobahn.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Zugspitze Glacier and Cable Car

Ride the ultra-modern cable car to Germany's highest peak where the glacier plateau has a flat, stroller-friendly walking path and family-friendly restaurant. Bring a warm baby carrier for the very top viewing deck, as steps can be icy.

Morning / 2h

Partnachklamm (Partnach Gorge)

Walk through a jaw-dropping limestone gorge with rushing water and dripping walls, easily accessed via a flat paved road from the Olympic Ski Stadium. You must use a front baby carrier here as strollers cannot fit through the narrow, wet tunnels.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Eibsee Lake Loop (Eibsee-Rundweg)

A pristine, turquoise alpine lake at the foot of the Zugspitze with a wide, flat, gravel path perfect for robust strollers. Stop at the lakeside playground or skip stones with your toddler along the shallow shorelines.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Michael-Ende-Kurpark

A peaceful, manicured park in the center of Garmisch dedicated to the 'Neverending Story' author, featuring flat paved paths, a sensory barefoot walk, and a premium children's playground. Toddlers will love watching the giant goldfish in the lily ponds while you enjoy the mountain views.

Morning / 2-3h

Wank Mountain (Wankbahn cable car)

Take the gentle, classic 4-person gondola up the Wank mountain for stunning panoramic views of the Zugspitze range without the high-altitude chill. The summit plateau is exceptionally flat and grassy, making it safe for toddlers to toddle around while you watch paragliders launch.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Ludwigstrasse in Partenkirchen

Stroll down this historic, cobblestone-free sidewalk street lined with gorgeous 'Lüftlmalerei' (traditional painted Bavarian frescoes) on the building facades. It's highly stroller-accessible with plenty of bakeries to grab a soft pretzel for the toddler.

Morning / 2h

AlpspiX Viewing Platform (Alpspitzbahn)

Hover over a 1,000-meter drop on two steel arms; the cable car ride up is stroller-accessible, and the immediate viewing area is safe and gated. The mountain station cafe has high chairs and offers spectacular, low-effort views.

Friday Morning / 1-2h

Garmisch Farmer's Market (Wochenmarkt Garmisch)

Held every Friday morning at Richard-Strauss-Platz, this vibrant market is packed with local cheeses, fresh soft pretzels, and seasonal berries. It's a flat, lively outdoor space where a stroller fits easily and toddlers can snack on fresh local treats.

Midday / 3h

Eckbauerbahn Gondola and Forsthaus Graseck

Ride the historic, vintage two-seater retro gondolas (infant in a carrier) up to the Eckbauer peak, then take an easy, paved downhill stroll to the mountain hotel Forsthaus Graseck for lunch. It offers an incredibly peaceful mountain experience far from the larger tourist crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Brauhaus Garmisch

Order the classic Bavarian pork roast ('Schweinebraten') with potato dumplings and local draft beer. The tavern is cavernous and lively, meaning any toddler fussiness is easily drowned out by the ambient chatter.

Gasthof Zum Rössle

Savor the homemade Käsespätzle (Bavarian mac-and-cheese) which is a guaranteed hit for toddlers. They open early for dinner (5:00 PM), have plenty of high chairs, and offer a cozy, welcoming family atmosphere.

Lupo Pizzeria

Order the wood-fired Parma pizza for the parents and a simple Margherita to share with the toddler. It's a casual, high-energy spot with fast service, which is crucial when dining with an impatient infant.

Konditorei Cafe Am Markt

Try their famous Agnes-Bernauer-Torte or a warm apple strudel with vanilla sauce. It has a spacious outdoor terrace right on the pedestrian square where you can park a stroller and enjoy a slow morning coffee while your child naps.

Eibsee Pavillon

Order the fresh pan-fried mountain trout ('Forelle Müllerin') right on the lake's edge. The restaurant features a dedicated kids' play corner inside and expansive outdoor seating where stroller-using families are warmly welcomed.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Werdenfelserei

A stunning luxury boutique hotel featuring spacious family suites with natural wood finishes, a rooftop pool (with designated family swim hours), and high-end cribs provided upon request.

Das Graseck - my mountain hideaway

Tucked away on a mountain plateau accessible via its own private cable car, this wellness hotel offers incredibly peaceful family suites, crib setups, and a beautiful outdoor heated pool perfect for a soothing swim with your baby.

Hotel Zugspitze

Located right in the heart of Garmisch, this traditional upscale hotel offers large family apartments, indoor pool access, and is steps away from the pedestrian zone, making stroller outings incredibly simple.
