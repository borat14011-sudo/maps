What to do

Morning / 4-5h

Zugspitze Peak Adventure

Ride the high-tech cable car to Germany's highest peak for year-round snow play and sweeping mountain views. Toddlers will love the high-altitude snow park and the family-friendly glacier restaurant.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Eibsee Lake Loop

Walk the flat, stroller-friendly trail around this breathtaking, turquoise alpine lake. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow crystal-clear shores while you enjoy the dramatic mountain backdrop.

Morning / 2h

Partnach Gorge

Walk through a dramatic limestone gorge filled with roaring waterfalls and dripping stone walls. Strollers aren't allowed due to narrow paths and steps, so use a baby carrier to safely marvel at the rushing waters.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Michael-Ende-Kurpark

Unwind in this peaceful public park dedicated to the author of The Neverending Story, featuring a barefoot sensory path and a maze. Toddlers will love climbing the playground structures and watching the pond ducks.

Morning / 3h

Wank Mountain Gondola

Ride the vintage-style yellow gondola up Wank Mountain, known locally as the 'friendly mountain' for its flat terrain. Toddlers can easily run through the high meadows and watch paragliders take off.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Ludwigstrasse Historic Walk

Stroll down this historic street lined with gorgeous 'Lüftlmalerei' (traditional painted murals) on pastel chalets. It is completely flat, making it perfect for strollers and quick bakery stops.

Morning / 2-3h

AlpspiX Viewing Platform

Stand on two steel arms hovering 1,000 meters above the valley floor for a thrilling alpine panorama. The platform is fully enclosed, letting toddlers safely peer through the grated floor at the tiny world below.

Friday Morning / 1-2h

Garmisch Farmer's Market

Visit the bustling weekly market to sample local mountain cheeses, fresh berries, and soft pretzels. Toddlers will love the sensory sights, sounds, and delicious, kid-approved finger foods.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Eckbauerbahn Gondola

Ride this cozy, historic two-seater gondola up to the Eckbauer peak for an easy walk through alpine pastures. It's a quieter, slower-paced ride that is perfect for toddlers who might get overwhelmed by larger cable cars.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gasthof Werdenfelser Hof

Order the traditional Bavarian roast pork with potato dumplings in their cozy, kid-friendly dining room.

Lupo Pizzeria

Grab a giant, family-sized stone-baked Margherita pizza in a relaxed, bustling atmosphere that welcomes wiggly kids.

Gasthaus Zur Schranne

Try the fluffy, shredded sweet pancake known as Kaiserschmarrn, a guaranteed hit for toddlers and parents alike.

Chocolaterie Amelie

Treat the family to premium, handmade hot chocolate and organic alpine-milk chocolate bars in this colorful shop.

Bräustüberl Garmisch

Pair a cold local draft beer for parents with warm Bavarian soft pretzels for the kids in their lively beer garden.

👨‍👩‍👧 Invest in a sturdy all-terrain stroller or a high-quality carrier, as many scenic alpine trails are gravel-based or feature steps. Always pack layers and rain gear for the little ones, as mountain weather can change dramatically in minutes.
