Munich

← back to travel board
Munich blends Bavarian charm with incredibly lush parks, pedestrian-friendly plazas, and a deeply welcoming atmosphere for little travelers. From watching surfers ride an urban river wave to feeding ducks in royal gardens, the city offers a relaxed pace where parents can sip local brews while toddlers play safely. It is a clean, flat, and endlessly stroller-accessible European destination.

What to do

Afternoon / 2-3h

English Garden & Eisbachwelle

Watch skilled surfers ride a permanent standing wave before letting your toddler splash in the shallow, gentle streams nearby. The vast lawns are perfect for burning off energy.

Morning / 3h

Deutsches Museum Kinderreich

This massive science museum features a dedicated Kids' Kingdom filled with giant building blocks, water play tables, and a real fire engine to climb on. It is an interactive indoor wonderland for active toddlers.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Viktualienmarkt

A bustling open-air market filled with colorful fruit stalls, giant pretzels, and a central beer garden. There is plenty of open space for toddlers to snack on fresh berries in the pedestrian zone.

11:00 AM / 30m

Marienplatz Glockenspiel

Gather in the central square to watch the historic mechanical figures dance and joust high up on the town hall tower. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the chiming bells, but keep them close in the crowds.

Morning / 3-4h

Hellabrunn Zoo

This spacious geo-zoo features natural-style enclosures along winding canals and massive wooden playgrounds. Stroller rentals are available at the entrance to keep little legs rested.

Morning / 2h

Nymphenburg Palace Park

Wander through the grand, flat palace grounds where toddlers can feed resident swans and watch ducks paddle down the canals. The gravel paths are highly stroller-friendly and surrounded by shaded woods.

Afternoon / 1h

Munich Toy Museum

Located inside the old town hall tower, this whimsical museum showcases centuries of historic dolls, model trains, and teddy bears. While it is more visual than hands-on, the colorful displays will charm curious toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Olympiapark

Explore the rolling green hills, climb the futuristic stadium paths, or take a peaceful boat ride on the lake. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles and feeding geese along the grassy banks.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Königlicher Hirschgarten

Munich’s largest beer garden features a shaded playground right next to the tables and an adjacent deer park where toddlers can feed gentle fallow deer. Parents can relax with a cold stein while keeping a close eye on play.

Where to eat

  • Hofbräuhaus am Platzl — The lively, loud atmosphere easily drowns out toddler tantrums, and kids will love tearing into massive, soft Bavarian pretzels.
  • Chocolaterie Beluga — This cozy spot near Viktualienmarkt lets you stir a solid chocolate block on a wooden spoon into hot milk for a customizable, kid-friendly treat.
  • Café Reitschule — An elegant cafe sitting right next to an indoor riding arena, allowing toddlers to watch horses train through large glass windows while you enjoy brunch.
  • Café Frischhut — Treat the family to warm, freshly fried Bavarian donuts called Schmalznudeln, which are soft and easy for little fingers to pull apart.
👨‍👩‍👧 Munich's U-Bahn stations are highly stroller-accessible with elevators, but buy a Group Day Ticket for the most cost-effective family travel. Keep small coins handy for public restrooms, and note that almost all shops and supermarkets are strictly closed on Sundays.
