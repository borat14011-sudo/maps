Rio de Janeiro

← back to travel board
Where emerald-draped mountains plunge into a glittering blue ocean, Rio de Janeiro welcomes families with its infectious, rhythmic warmth. From cable cars soaring above the canopy to gentle bays perfect for sandcastles, this coastal playground offers an unforgettable tropical escape. Children will be captivated by wild monkeys, colossal statues, and the joyful beat of carioca life.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Sugarloaf Mountain Cable Car

Ride the glass-walled cable cars up to breathtaking panoramic views of the bay where kids can spot tiny yachts. Toddlers will love the smooth ride and the shaded, paved woodland paths at the first stop on Urca Hill.

Early Morning / 2-3h

Christ the Redeemer via Corcovado Train

Ascend through the lush Tijuca rainforest on a bright red cog train to stand beneath the iconic giant statue. Toddlers will be enchanted by the train ride itself, but bring a carrier as the summit escalators and platforms get very crowded.

Morning / 2h

Jardim Botânico

Wander beneath towering imperial palms and discover sensory greenhouses, giant water lilies, and turtles splashing in ponds. It is incredibly stroller-friendly and features a fantastic, gated playground designed specifically for little ones.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Copacabana Beach at Posto 6

Enjoy the golden sands of Rio's most famous beach near Posto 6, where the calm, sheltered waters are safest for swimming. It is the perfect spot for toddlers to splash gently and dig in the sand while you sip fresh coconut water.

Midday / 2h

AquaRio Aquarium

Walk through South America's largest aquarium, featuring a spectacular underwater tunnel filled with sharks, rays, and colorful fish. It is fully air-conditioned and stroller-accessible, making it the perfect escape from the midday tropical heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Lage

Explore a public park featuring a stunning mansion with a courtyard pool, mysterious artificial stone caves, and walking trails. Toddlers will love pointing out the tiny capuchin monkeys in the surrounding trees and exploring the easy gravel paths.

Afternoon / 2h

Feira de São Cristóvão

Experience a lively indoor market filled with vibrant music, crafts, and the delicious aromas of Northeastern Brazilian food. While noisy, toddlers will be mesmerized by the colorful hanging decorations and the occasional daytime puppet show.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas

Rent a giant swan-shaped pedal boat on this tranquil saltwater lagoon or rent a family quad-cycle to cruise the flat, paved perimeter path. There are several excellent lakeside playgrounds with soft grass, perfect for active toddlers.

Morning / 3h

BioParque do Rio

Visit a modernized, conservation-focused zoo where kids can get close to jaguars, colorful macaws, and elephants in open-concept habitats. Toddlers will especially enjoy the petting zoo farm and the spacious, stroller-friendly walkways.

Where to eat

  • Confeitaria Colombo — Step into a fairytale-like 19th-century grand cafe with towering stained-glass mirrors to feast on delicious coxinha croquettes and sweet chocolate brigadeiros.
  • Churrascaria Palace — Enjoy a classic Brazilian barbecue experience where kids eat discounted, and waiters bring endless succulent cuts of roasted meat right to your table.
  • Tacacá do Norte — Stop by this casual, local favorite to introduce the family to refreshing, authentic Amazonian açaí bowls topped with fresh bananas and sweet granola.
  • Bibi Sucos — A vibrant, ultra-family-friendly juice bar chain serving freshly squeezed tropical juices, grilled cheese tapioca crepes, and kid-approved burgers.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to licensed yellow taxis or Uber for hassle-free transport, as navigating the hilly streets with a stroller can be physically challenging. Always pack high-SPF sunscreen and a clip-on stroller fan to keep your little ones cool and protected during the peak midday heat.
