Cannon Beach

← back to travel board
Where the misty Oregon rainforest meets the wild Pacific, Cannon Beach enchants families with its iconic Haystack Rock and endless tide pools. Cozy beach bonfires, lush forest trails, and a walkable village make this the ultimate stress-free coastal escape. Children will love searching for tufted puffins and building sandcastles under the watch of majestic sea stacks.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min drive by rental car from Portland International Airport (PDX) to Cannon Beach hotels.

What to do

Low Tide / 2 hours

Haystack Rock Tide Pools

Explore the magical tide pools teeming with colorful starfish and anemones at the base of this massive 235-foot sea stack. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow pools, but be sure to check the tide chart for low tide.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Ecola State Park Viewpoints

Drive up for breathtaking, panoramic views of the coastline, historic lighthouses, and lush Sitka spruce forests. The paved viewpoints are stroller-friendly, making it easy to enjoy the sights with little ones.

Afternoon / 2 hours

Tolovana Beach State Recreation Site

This wide-open stretch of sand features a fantastic playground just steps from the beach, perfect for burning off energy. Toddlers can safely play on the swings and slides before heading to the shoreline to build sandcastles.

Low Tide / 2 hours

Hug Point State Recreation Site

Walk along the sandy beach to discover seasonal waterfalls, carved stagecoach trails, and shallow caves to explore. It's an easy, flat walk from the parking lot, though toddler carriers are recommended for navigating wet rocks.

Tuesday Morning / 1-2 hours

Cannon Beach Farmers Market

Browse stalls filled with fresh Oregon berries, artisan cheeses, and baked goods while enjoying live music. It's a vibrant, stroller-friendly spot where toddlers can snack on fresh fruit and see colorful flowers.

Late Morning / 45 minutes

Icefire Glassworks

Watch master glassblowers shape molten glass into vibrant, glowing masterpieces right before your eyes. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glowing colors, and the viewing area is safely separated from the heat.

Afternoon / 30 minutes

Bruce's Candy Kitchen

Step into this pink-and-white-striped paradise that has been pulling salt water taffy since 1963. Toddlers can watch the antique taffy-pulling machine through the window while you pick out sweet treats.

Late Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Arcadia Beach State Recreation Site

A quieter alternative to the main beach, offering beautiful rock formations and peaceful sandy stretches. The close-in parking makes it incredibly easy to carry beach gear and tired toddlers to and from the sand.

Rainy Afternoon / 1 hour

Cannon Beach Library

A cozy, volunteer-run library featuring a dedicated children's nook packed with books and toys. It's the perfect, quiet refuge for a rainy coastal afternoon with a toddler.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pelican Brewing Company

Grab a table at this spacious, family-friendly pub and order the giant fish and chips paired with a local craft beer.

Mo's Restaurant

Enjoy a steaming bowl of their world-famous, creamy clam chowder served in a casual, kid-friendly dining room overlooking the ocean.

Sea Level Bakery + Coffee

Fuel up on expertly crafted espresso and artisanal, fresh-baked morning buns that toddlers will happily devour.

Ecola Seafoods Restaurant & Market

Order ultra-fresh wild salmon baskets and crispy halibut fish and chips from this no-frills, family-run market.

Fultano's Pizza

Wind down with a family-sized gourmet pizza and garlic knots in a relaxed, bustling atmosphere that welcomes noisy toddlers.
