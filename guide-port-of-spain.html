Port of Spain

← back to travel board
Port of Spain is a sensory explosion of steelpan rhythms, vibrant colonial architecture, and lush tropical landscapes tucked between mountains and the Caribbean Sea. Families will love the warmth of Trinidadian culture, where bustling open-air markets, exotic bird sanctuaries, and gentle green parks make for an unforgettable, authentic island escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-45 min taxi ride from Piarco International Airport (POS) to downtown Port of Spain hotels, depending on traffic.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2h

Queen's Park Savannah

This sprawling green park is perfect for kids to run off energy while parents admire the Magnificent Seven colonial mansions and sip fresh coconut water. Toddlers will love the wide-open grassy spaces and watching the occasional horse trot by.

Morning / 2h

Emperor Valley Zoo

Located right by the botanical gardens, this cozy zoo offers encounters with local wildlife like scarlet ibises, red-howler monkeys, and giraffes. It is stroller-friendly with plenty of shaded pathways for tired toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

Royal Botanic Gardens

Walk among some of the oldest established botanic gardens in the Western Hemisphere, filled with towering trees and blooming tropical flowers. Its paved, flat paths are excellent for stroller walks and quiet toddler naps under the canopy.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Caroni Bird Sanctuary

Take a gentle, flat-bottomed boat tour through the mangrove swamps to witness thousands of bright red Scarlet Ibis birds fly home at sunset. The calm, slow-moving ride is mesmerizing for toddlers, but be sure to pack insect repellent.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Maracas Beach

A scenic mountain drive leads to this iconic white-sand beach, famous for its calm waters and delicious food stalls. Toddlers can safely splash in the gentle shoreline shallows while parents enjoy the dramatic coastal views.

Midday / 1-2h

National Museum and Art Gallery

Discover Trinidad's rich carnival history, indigenous heritage, and local art in a cool, air-conditioned space. It is a fantastic sensory experience for kids to see miniature carnival costumes, and the quiet galleries offer a peaceful retreat from the midday heat.

Saturday Morning / 2-3h

Green Market Santa Cruz

This vibrant, family-friendly outdoor market features organic local produce, handmade crafts, and traditional Trinidadian treats. Toddlers will love the lively acoustic music, sweet tropical fruits, and dedicated play areas under the trees.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Chaguaramas Boardwalk

Located just west of the city, this paved seaside boardwalk features dedicated play areas, beautiful ocean breezes, and pedal boat rentals. It is completely stroller-accessible and perfect for a relaxing family sunset stroll.

Morning / 2h

Yerette - Home of the Hummingbird

Visit this enchanting private garden sanctuary where hundreds of iridescent hummingbirds of multiple species buzz right before your eyes. Toddlers will be absolutely spellbound by the tiny, colorful birds feeding mere inches away.

🍽️ Where to eat

Richard's Bake & Shark

Grab the legendary Trinidadian crispy fried flatbread stuffed with seasoned shark and loaded with garlic sauce, chadow beni, and pineapple.

Chaud Cafe & Bar

Indulge in high-quality international-meets-Caribbean bistro fare like gourmet burgers, artisanal flatbreads, and decadent desserts in a relaxed, family-friendly setting.

The Hot Shoppe

Order the soft, flavorful dhalpuri roti wrapped around curried chicken or channa (chickpeas) for a classic, messy, and delicious Trinidadian comfort meal.

Trotters

This bustling, highly family-friendly sports bar offers a massive menu of baby back ribs, pastas, and burgers that will easily satisfy picky young eaters.

Jaffa at the Oval

Enjoy an upscale yet welcoming buffet or à la carte lunch overlooking the historic cricket grounds, featuring excellent local curries and fresh seafood.
