Las Vegas

← back to travel board
Beyond the glittering casino floors lies a vibrant desert playground bursting with unexpected family-friendly wonders. From sensory-rich neon gardens and massive indoor play spaces to striking red-rock canyons, Las Vegas offers a captivating sensory feast for kids of all ages. Experience a whimsical side of Sin City where toddler smiles and giant fountains steal the spotlight.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Gardens

Marvel at thousands of fresh flowers arranged into towering, whimsical fantasy scenes that change with the seasons. Toddlers will love the bright colors, giant characters, and the nearby dancing fountains outside.

Late Afternoon / 1h

High Roller Observation Wheel

Soar 550 feet above the Strip in a spacious, air-conditioned glass cabin that offers 360-degree views without any walking. It is fully stroller-accessible, allowing toddlers to wander safely inside the pod while you enjoy the views.

Morning / 2h

Shark Reef Aquarium at Mandalay Bay

Walk through a spectacular underwater tunnel surrounded by sharks, giant rays, and sea turtles. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the touch pool where they can safely interact with gentle invertebrates.

Morning / 2-3h

Red Rock Canyon Scenic Drive

Escape the neon for a stunning 13-mile scenic drive past towering crimson cliffs and desert bighorn sheep. The visitor center features interactive outdoor exhibits and paved, stroller-friendly paths perfect for little legs.

Morning / 3h

Springs Preserve

This 180-acre cultural oasis features botanical gardens, a butterfly habitat, and interactive museums. Toddlers can burn off energy at the dynamic desert-themed playground and ride the trackless train.

Afternoon / 2-3h

DISCOVERY Children's Museum

Three floors of hands-on interactive galleries designed to spark curiosity, including a massive indoor climbing structure. Toddlers have their own dedicated sensory zone called Toddler Town to play safely away from bigger kids.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Downtown Container Park

An open-air shopping and dining hub built from shipping containers, guarded by a giant fire-breathing praying mantis. The center courtyard features an incredible treehouse playground where toddlers can slide and build with giant foam blocks.

Evening / 1h

The Neon Museum

Walk through an outdoor "boneyard" of retired, giant neon signs that tell the colorful history of the city. Strollers are welcome, and the early evening tours offer a magical, glowing sensory experience for little ones.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Illuminarium at Area15

Step into a fully immersive digital world with 360-degree projections that transport you to the African safari or outer space. Toddlers will be delighted by the interactive floors that react to their footsteps.

Where to eat

  • Black Tap Craft Burgers & Beer — Famous for their over-the-top, candy-topped CrazyShakes that are a visual and sugary feast perfect for sharing with toddlers.
  • Mon Ami Gabi — Sit on the outdoor patio for prime views of the Bellagio Fountains while enjoying a French-bistro breakfast with kid-friendly brioche French toast.
  • Fergusons Downtown — A revitalized motel courtyard featuring local food stalls, grassy areas for toddlers to toddle, and great coffee at Mothership Coffee Roasters.
  • Carlo's Bakery at The Venetian — Grab giant, colorful lobster tails and cupcakes from the famous Buddy Valastro, then enjoy them while watching the indoor gondolas float by.
👨‍👩‍👧 To avoid sensory overload and heavy crowds, stick to morning outings and utilize the free tram systems connecting major Strip resorts. Always pack a lightweight stroller and plenty of water, as navigating the sprawling casino properties requires surprisingly long walks.
