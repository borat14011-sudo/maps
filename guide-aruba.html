Aruba

← back to travel board
Aruba welcomes sophisticated families with its signature powder-white sands, gentle trade winds, and an effortlessly upscale Caribbean charm. The pristine shores of Palm Beach offer a calm, shallow lagoon perfect for introducing baby Mary Claire to the warm ocean water. A paved, stroller-ready beachfront promenade connects top-tier resorts, allowing seamless morning walks under the shade of native divi-divi trees. With world-class private dining and highly accessible luxury amenities, the island provides a stress-free tropical sanctuary for discerning parents.
🚕 Getting there: A 20-minute private luxury SUV transfer from Queen Beatrix International Airport (AUA) straight to the high-rise hotel canopy, pre-arranged with a premium rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Early morning 8:30-11am / 2.5h

Baby Beach

A protected, man-made lagoon on the southern tip of the island featuring incredibly calm, shallow waters. It is ideal for wading safely with an infant, though you should bring a pop-up beach tent as natural shade is limited.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

The Butterfly Farm

A lush, shaded tropical garden filled with hundreds of exotic butterflies. The paved pathways are perfectly stroller-accessible, and the quiet, soothing classical music provides a calming sensory experience for a baby.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Eagle Beach

Widely rated as one of the best beaches in the world, offering soft white sand and the island's iconic fofoti trees. These unique trees provide excellent natural shade pockets for baby's morning nap while parents relax.

Morning 9am-1pm / 4h

Iguana Beach on Renaissance Island

A private island oasis accessible via a smooth water taxi. While the famous Flamingo Beach is adults-only, Iguana Beach welcomes families with gentle waters, luxury beach bungalows, and quiet shaded areas under palm trees.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Donkey Sanctuary Aruba

A peaceful, non-profit sanctuary housing friendly rescued donkeys. The visitor center is fully stroller-friendly, highly shaded, and offers a quiet, slow-paced morning environment where baby can watch the gentle animals.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Palm Beach Plaza

An upscale, open-air shopping mall with wide, paved stroller-friendly walkways. Visited during the morning, it is exceptionally quiet and offers clean, air-conditioned family restrooms and plenty of shade.

Morning 9-10:15am / 1.25h

Arikok National Park Visitor Center

While the rugged park interior requires a 4x4, the modern Visitor Center is fully air-conditioned and stroller-friendly. It features educational exhibits about local wildlife and flora, offering a cool educational escape.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Philip's Animal Garden

A clean, interactive animal rehabilitation center with wide, flat pathways. It is highly engaging for young eyes, and the staff are incredibly accommodating to families maneuvering strollers through the shaded exhibits.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Mangel Halto

A secluded coastal gem famous for its calm, shallow mangrove-bordered waters. A sturdy wooden sun deck provides an elevated, stroller-safe viewing platform to enjoy the multi-hued turquoise water without stepping into the deep sand.

🍽️ Where to eat

The West Deck

An upscale yet casual oceanfront eatery serving signature Dutch Caribbean fish bites. The spacious outdoor wooden deck accommodates strollers easily, and the ambient ocean breeze nicely masks occasional baby babbles.

Papiamento Restaurant

Set in a historic 126-year-old Aruban manor, this venue serves signature Clay Pot Seafood in a lush garden. Booking an early 6pm seating ensures a peaceful dinner under sparkling fairy lights before baby's bedtime.

Barefoot Restaurant

An elegant dining concept where you eat with your feet in the sand. Ordering the signature red snapper while baby dines in an provided highchair on the spacious deck offers a picture-perfect Caribbean evening.

Ike's Bistro

An open-air Mediterranean-Caribbean fusion restaurant located at Manchebo Resort. The staff are exceptionally welcoming to infants, providing highchairs and accommodating stroller placement next to your table.

Eduardo's Beach Shack

The island's premier spot for fresh, organic acai bowls and pitaya smoothies. Situated right on the Palm Beach boardwalk, it is the perfect stroller walk-up destination for a healthy, upscale breakfast.
