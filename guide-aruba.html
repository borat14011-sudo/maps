Aruba

← back to travel board
Aruba offers a predictable, high-infrastructure escape where the constant trade winds temper the tropical heat. For a toddler, the shallow, wave-free shores of Palm and Eagle Beaches act like giant, natural wading pools. The island is highly westernized with paved walkways and modern supermarkets, meaning you won't struggle with stroller access or finding organic baby essentials. It is a frictionless Caribbean getaway that lets a busy dad decompress while keeping the little one perfectly safe and comfortable.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-minute taxi ride from Queen Beatrix International Airport (AUA) to the Palm Beach high-rise resort area, costing a flat regulated rate of approximately $31.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Philip's Animal Garden

A fantastic rescue sanctuary where toddlers can feed friendly goats, pigs, and camels from pre-packed cups of food. The dirt paths are wide enough for a sturdy jogging stroller, though a baby carrier is best for getting up close to the enclosures.

Morning / 3-4h

Baby Beach

A man-made lagoon with incredibly shallow, warm, and wave-free water where your toddler can safely splash for hours. Shade is scarce, so rent a tent cabana early, and note that the wet sand is packed hard enough for easy stroller-pushing right up to the water's edge.

Morning / 1h

The Butterfly Farm

Walk through a lush tropical garden filled with thousands of colorful butterflies that might land on your bright clothing. The paved, flat pathways are completely stroller-friendly, and the morning hours offer the most active butterfly flights.

Afternoon / 1h

Donkey Sanctuary Aruba

A peaceful, non-profit sanctuary where you can feed over a hundred rescued friendly donkeys from a safe, elevated viewing porch. The main visitor area and porch are stroller-accessible, making it easy to keep your toddler secure while interacting with the animals.

Morning / 1-2h

Arikok National Park Visitor Centre

While the rugged park interior requires a 4x4, the modern Visitor Centre features paved, educational walkways and cool, air-conditioned exhibits to introduce kids to local wildlife. It is highly stroller-accessible and serves as a comfortable, educational indoor pitstop.

Afternoon / 1h

Super Food Plaza

This massive, modern Dutch supermarket is a lifesaver for families needing premium European baby foods, fresh berries, and diapers. The wide aisles, high-quality shopping carts with infant seats, and onsite bakery make it a highly practical family stop.

Morning / 45 mins

Aruba Aloe Factory Museum and Store

Take a quick, self-guided or guided tour through the oldest operating aloe factory in the world to see how these soothing plants are harvested. The entire facility is flat, paved, air-conditioned, and highly stroller-accessible, offering a quick indoor escape from the sun.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Wilhelmina Park

A quiet, scenic waterfront park in town featuring manicured lawns and a chance to spot wild iguanas sunning themselves on the rocks. It has fully paved walking paths perfect for an easy stroller push while catching a nice ocean breeze.

Morning / 2h

Mangel Halto

A secluded beach surrounded by calm mangroves, offering shallow, crystal-clear tide pools that are incredibly safe for toddlers to sit in. While the wooden deck and stairs aren't stroller-friendly, a baby carrier makes it easy to access the quiet, shady sandy spots.

🍽️ Where to eat

Eduardo's Beach Shack

Grab a fresh dragon fruit acai bowl or a dairy-free smoothie at this beachside kiosk on the boardwalk. It's outdoor-only with plenty of open sand for a toddler to play on while you wait, making it a perfect, low-stress breakfast spot.

Zeerovers

This ultra-casual, overwater dock serves fresh fried local snapper and shrimp baskets by the pound. The open-air, bustling environment naturally drowns out toddler noise, and the picnic-table seating is highly accommodating for families.

Barefoot Restaurant

Enjoy high-end dining with your toes in the sand, ordering the signature snapper with Creole sauce while your toddler plays safely at your feet. The early seating at 5:30 PM is filled with families looking to catch the sunset before bedtime.

Papiamento Restaurant

Set in a historic 126-year-old Aruban manor house, this restaurant offers tables around a lush, sparkling pool garden. Book an early table for their famous clay-pot seafood dishes; the spacious outdoor garden layout gives you plenty of room to park a stroller.

The West Deck

A casual, open-air wooden deck right on the water serving excellent Aruban half-chicken and local fish bites. It is highly spacious, very loud with ocean breezes and soft music, and sits adjacent to a small beach where kids can burn off energy.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hyatt Regency Aruba Resort Spa and Casino

Features an incredible multi-tiered pool complex with a dedicated, zero-entry family pool area that is perfect for toddlers. The resort provides high-quality pack-and-plays, spacious rooms, and sits on a calm, wide stretch of Palm Beach.

The Ritz-Carlton, Aruba

Offers top-tier luxury with exceptionally spacious suites, complimentary cribs, and a highly attentive beach service that will set up umbrellas for baby shade. Its location at the end of Palm Beach means less foot traffic and quieter nap times.

Aruba Marriott Resort & Stellaris Casino

Boasts some of the largest standard guest rooms on the island, leaving plenty of floor space for a crib and play area. The sprawling lagoon-style pool has plenty of shallow ledges, and the resort's pathways are exceptionally stroller-friendly.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a high-UPF pop-up beach tent because natural shade on public beaches is heavily regulated and often claimed by 7:00 AM. Rent a car with a pre-installed car seat from a reliable local agency like Tropic Car Rental to easily navigate between Baby Beach and the high-rise district. The constant, strong Aruban trade winds can easily mask the sun's intensity, so apply mineral sunscreen twice as often as you think necessary and pack a lightweight, wind-resistant stroller canopy.
