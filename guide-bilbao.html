For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the city provides a sophisticated yet incredibly relaxed Basque escape where fine dining meets family comfort. What to do

9:30 AM - 11:30 AM / 2h

Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

The ultimate icon of Bilbao features wide, stroller-accessible ramps and spacious galleries perfect for a quiet morning wander. The outdoor plaza features Jeff Koons' 'Puppy' and Louise Bourgeois' giant spider, which provide fascinating visual stimulation for baby Mary Claire without any indoor echo.

3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Doña Casilda Iturrizar Park

This sprawling English-style garden features paved, flat pathways ideal for stroller naps under the shade of ancient trees. Will and Kathryn can enjoy a peaceful walk past the duck pond and classical fountains, ending near the modern playground where baby Mary Claire can enjoy some supervised grass time.

10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Funicular de Artxanda

A historic funicular railway that is fully stroller-accessible and takes you to the top of Mount Artxanda in just three minutes. The summit offers sweeping, quiet panoramic views of the city and a massive green park area perfect for a peaceful family picnic away from the city hubbub.

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM / 2h

Itsasmuseum Bilbao

Located on the banks of the river, this maritime museum is exceptionally spacious, flat, and quiet, making it incredibly easy to navigate with a large stroller. The outdoor dock area features historic ships and the iconic red Carola crane, offering a breezy, visually engaging walk for parents and baby.

12:00 PM - 1:30 PM / 1.5h

Plaza Nueva

A magnificent neoclassical enclosed square in the heart of the Old Town. Because it is completely pedestrianized and flat, it is the perfect spot to push the stroller around while admiring the architecture and popping into upscale pintxo bars that offer outdoor terrace seating.

8:30 AM - 10:00 AM / 1.5h

Paseo de Abandoibarra

A pristine, wide, and completely flat riverside promenade that stretches from the Guggenheim to the Euskalduna Palace. Lined with modern sculptures, green lawns, and shady spots, it is the premier spot in the city for an uninterrupted morning stroller run or walk.

4:30 PM - 5:30 PM / 1h

Azkuna Zentroa

A stunning cultural center redesigned by Philippe Starck, featuring 43 unique columns that baby will love looking at. The wide elevators make it simple to access the upper floors, where you can look up to see the glass-bottomed rooftop swimming pool from the main atrium.

10:00 AM - 12:30 PM / 2.5h

Ereaga Beach

Located in upscale Getxo, just a short drive from central Bilbao, this beach features a long, paved, sea-breeze-filled boardwalk. It is perfect for a coastal stroller walk on flat ground, offering calm waters and clean sand for baby Mary Claire to dip her toes in.

6:00 PM - 6:30 PM / 0.5h

Zubizuri Bridge

Santiago Calatrava's famous tied-arch footbridge features gentle ramp access on both sides of the river, making it highly accessible for strollers. It offers beautiful, unobstructed views of the modern skyline and a gentle, breezy walk for a quick photo opportunity.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bistró Guggenheim Bilbao

Savor their signature slow-cooked cod in pil-pil sauce. The restaurant offers early lunch seating at 1:00 PM, highly accommodating staff who readily provide highchairs, and a peaceful outdoor terrace looking out at the river.

Café Iruña

Famous for its stunning mudéjar decor and signature 'pintxos morunos' (spiced lamb skewers). The spacious, grand dining room easily accommodates stroller parking, and the bustling but friendly atmosphere naturally masks any baby babble.

Gure Toki

Known for their signature cod tempura and creative upscale pintxos. Located in Plaza Nueva, their extensive outdoor terrace seating allows Will and Kathryn to indulge in world-class Basque bites while baby Mary Claire naps comfortably in her stroller.

Victor Montes

An upscale, historic restaurant in Plaza Nueva offering signature hand-carved Jamón Ibérico and local Txakoli wine. Booking an early lunch ensures a quiet, spacious table indoors or on the elegant terrace where staff are incredibly welcoming to families.

Restaurante Kate Zaharra

Savor high-end traditional Basque grilled turbot in a rustic stone house high above the city. The spacious, quiet dining rooms, spectacular panoramic views, and beautiful outdoor lawn make it a wonderfully relaxed, luxury family dining experience.
