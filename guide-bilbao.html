Bilbao

← back to travel board
Bilbao pairs striking modern architecture with flat, stroller-friendly riverfront promenades that make navigating with a toddler seamless. The city's famous culinary scene is incredibly accessible, featuring wide pedestrian plazas where parents can savor world-class pintxos while kids safely wander. It is a compact, highly walkable destination where you can easily transition from cultural landmarks to lush green parks without logistical stress.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-minute taxi ride from Bilbao Airport (BIO) directly to central hotels, costing approximately €25-€30 and easily hailed outside arrivals.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

Marvel at Frank Gehry's titanium masterpiece where outdoor installations like Jeff Koons' giant floral 'Puppy' will instantly captivate your toddler. The museum interior is exceptionally stroller-accessible with wide elevators, smooth ramps, and spacious galleries.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Doña Casilda Iturrizar

Bilbao’s premier green space features a large duck pond, a classic vintage carousel, and multiple modern playground areas with soft rubber surfacing. It is the perfect shady spot for a stroller nap or letting a toddler burn off energy on the swings.

Morning / 1.5h

Funicular de Artxanda

Ride this historic cable railway up to Mount Artxanda for breathtaking panoramic views of the city. The summit features flat walking paths, a green park, and plenty of space for toddlers to run around safely away from traffic.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado de la Ribera

Explore Europe's largest indoor food market, located right on the edge of the old town. The ground-floor gastro-plaza is spacious and loud enough to easily drown out toddler tantrums while parents sample local delicacies.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Paseo de Abandoibarra

This completely pedestrianized, ultra-flat riverside promenade runs past the Guggenheim and features dedicated play areas and water fountains. It is the absolute best route in the city for a scenic, uninterrupted stroller walk.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza Nueva

This monumental neoclassical square in the heart of the Old Town is fully enclosed and car-free. Parents can sit at outdoor tables enjoying drinks and pintxos while keeping a direct eye on their toddler playing in the open plaza.

Morning / 2h

Itsasmuseum Bilbao

The maritime museum features indoor exhibits on shipbuilding and an outdoor dock home to the iconic red Carola crane. Kids love the interactive boat models, and the entire indoor layout is highly stroller-friendly.

Afternoon / 1h

Azkuna Zentroa

This former wine warehouse was redesigned by Philippe Starck into a vibrant cultural center featuring 43 unique structural columns. Toddlers will be fascinated looking up at the glass-bottomed rooftop swimming pool visible from the main atrium floor.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Playa de Plentzia

Take a clean, direct 45-minute ride on the Bilbao Metro to this beautiful, sheltered bay with calm waters. The beach features a flat boardwalk and gentle waves, making it the safest spot nearby for a toddler's first dip in the Cantabrian Sea.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café Iruña

Order the famous 'pinchos morunos' (spiced pork skewers cooked over charcoal) in this historic, mudéjar-style café. The grand, bustling dining room is loud and spacious, making it very welcoming for families with strollers.

Bitoque Albia

Savor award-winning, creative pintxos like their mini kobe beef burgers. The outdoor terrace seating faces the quiet Albia Gardens, providing a peaceful setup to dine with a sleeping baby in a stroller.

Gure Toki

Located inside Plaza Nueva, this spot is famous for its modern Basque bites like cod tempura and beef ribs. Grab an outdoor table in the plaza early in the evening so your toddler has room to move while you eat.

La Viña del Ensanche

A legendary tavern serving outstanding acorn-fed jamón ibérico on warm crusty bread. Go right when they open for evening service around 7:30 PM to beat the standing-only crowds and secure a comfortable table.

Bistró Guggenheim Bilbao

For a refined lunch, this museum-adjacent bistro offers high-end local dishes like roasted hake with Basque green sauce. They are exceptionally accommodating to families, providing high chairs and ample space between tables.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Gran Hotel Domine Bilbao

Located directly across from the Guggenheim, this luxury hotel offers incredibly spacious family suites, top-tier wooden cribs upon request, and a rooftop terrace perfect for parents to enjoy a glass of Rioja while the baby sleeps downstairs.

Radisson Collection Hotel, Gran Vía Bilbao

This centrally located hotel features large, ultra-soundproofed rooms that guarantee uninterrupted toddler naps. The helpful concierge can easily arrange baby-friendly amenities and recommend reliable local babysitting services.

Hotel Carlton

An elegant, historic landmark on Plaza Moyúa featuring massive rooms with high ceilings that never feel cramped with a crib and stroller. Its central location next to a metro station makes getting around the city effortless for a young family.

👨‍👩‍👧 Basque dining hours run late, so aim to eat pintxos during the early evening crawl around 6:30 PM when the bars are lively but not yet shoulder-to-shoulder crowded. The cobblestones in the historic Casco Viejo can be bumpy, so a stroller with decent suspension is highly recommended. Look out for the city's covered public playgrounds, which are excellent for keeping toddlers dry and active during Bilbao's frequent light rain showers.
