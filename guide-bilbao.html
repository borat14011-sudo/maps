Bilbao

← back to travel board
Nestled between green mountains and the Bay of Biscay, Bilbao blends industrial-chic design with a warm, pedestrian-friendly soul. Families will love the flat, stroller-friendly riverside paths, whimsical public art, and culinary scene that welcomes children at every turn. It is a vibrant Basque playground where cutting-edge culture meets relaxed family life.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or 20 min Bizkaibus A3247 to the city center hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

This iconic titanium masterpiece features the giant floral 'Puppy' sculpture outside, which kids absolutely adore. Toddlers will love running around the wide, car-free plaza and watching the outdoor water fountains shoot up from the ground.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de Doña Casilda Iturrizar

Bilbao's central park is a leafy paradise featuring a large duck pond, classical fountains, and multiple modern playground areas. It is the perfect shady spot for toddlers to stretch their legs and watch the resident peacocks stroll by.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Artxanda Funicular

Ride a vintage 1915 cable railway up Mount Artxanda for breathtaking, panoramic views of the entire city. The short, exciting three-minute ride is a massive hit with toddlers, and there is a grassy park at the top to run around.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado de la Ribera

Explore Europe's largest indoor food market, situated right on the edge of the river. It's a feast of colors and sounds where you can pick up fresh, easy-to-eat fruits for toddlers while sampling local delicacies.

Morning / 2h

Itsasmuseum Maritime Museum

This family-oriented museum showcases Bilbao's seafaring heritage with indoor boat models and interactive pirate-themed play zones. Outside, kids can marvel at the massive, bright red Carola crane rising over the shipyard docks.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Nueva

A magnificent, neoclassical enclosed square in the heart of the Old Town (Casco Viejo). Parents can sit at outdoor tables to enjoy pintxos while toddlers play safely in the wide, traffic-free pedestrian courtyard.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Abandoibarra Riverside Walk & Playground

A scenic, stroller-friendly promenade winding along the Nervión river with dedicated play zones. The highlight for toddlers is the beautifully designed play area featuring soft-ground swings and sensory climbing structures.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Bilbao Estuary Boat Tour

A gentle, scenic river cruise aboard the Bilboats vessels offers a unique perspective of the city's architecture. It is a relaxing, sit-down adventure that keeps toddlers entertained without wearing out their little legs.

Evening / 1-2h

Etxebarria Park

Perched on a hill overlooking the Old Town, this expansive park features a landmark industrial brick chimney and vast lawns. It is a fantastic spot for a late-afternoon family picnic, kite flying, and letting toddlers toddle freely.

🍽️ Where to eat

Cafe Iruña

Order the famous 'pintxos morunos' (spiced lamb skewers grilled on-site) while the kids enjoy fresh-baked pastries in this stunningly tiled, historic 1903 cafe.

Gure Toki

Savor high-end miniature Basque cuisine like their legendary cod croquettes and mini beef burgers, which are perfectly sized and flavored for young children.

La Viña del Ensanche

Get a plate of their world-class, melt-in-your-mouth acorn-fed Iberian ham served with crispy glass bread, a simple and savory crowd-pleaser for toddlers.

El Globo

Try the famous hot gratinéed crab pintxo (txangurro) for adults, and grab a plate of simple, mild cheese and ham skewers for the little ones.

Pastelería Don Manuel

Indulge in a slice of traditional 'Pastel Vasco', a rich, buttery Basque cake filled with smooth vanilla pastry cream that toddlers and parents will both love.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bilbao's public transport is incredibly stroller-friendly, with spacious elevators at almost every Metro Bilbao station making travel effortless. When dining out, embrace the local custom of 'pintxo crawling' early in the evening around 7 PM, when plazas are lively with local families and toddlers can run safely in pedestrianized zones.
