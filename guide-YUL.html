European charm meets playground fun in Montreal.

Montreal

← back to travel board
With its cobblestone streets, lush green parks, and sensory-rich food markets, Montreal offers an enchanting European getaway without the jet lag. Toddlers will love the whimsical interactive museums and stroller-friendly pathways, while parents indulge in world-class pastries. It is a vibrant, bilingual playground where every corner promises a new family adventure.

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parc La Fontaine

A sprawling park with two beautiful ponds, willow trees, and excellent playgrounds. Toddlers will love watching the ducks and playing in the dedicated paddling pool during the summer months.

Morning / 2-3h

Montreal Biodome

Walk through five indoor ecosystems ranging from a tropical rainforest to the sub-polar regions. It is fully stroller-friendly, and the sensory changes in temperature and sounds keep toddlers completely captivated.

Morning / 1-2h

Jean-Talon Market

A bustling open-air market filled with vibrant fruit displays, maple treats, and fresh pastries. Little ones can sample fresh berries while riding in a stroller down the wide, pedestrian-only walkways.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Old Port of Montreal Boardwalk

Stroll along the scenic St. Lawrence River on wide wooden boardwalks perfect for strollers. Toddlers can enjoy watching the giant boats and riding the gentle, classic carousel nearby.

Morning / 2-3h

Montreal Botanical Garden

An oasis of themed gardens, including a magical Chinese Garden and a colorful caterpillar-shaped topiary. The path network is highly stroller-friendly, and the adjacent Insectarium is a hit for curious toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Mount Royal Park

This lush mountain designed by Frederick Law Olmsted offers panoramic city views and gentle gravel paths. Head to Beaver Lake where toddlers can watch model boats in summer or slide down gentle snowy hills in winter.

Morning / 2-3h

Montreal Science Centre

Located in the Old Port, this interactive museum has a dedicated 'Clic!' zone designed specifically for children aged 4 and under to build and play. It is the perfect rainy-day escape for hands-on learning.

Afternoon / 1h

Grande Bibliothèque (Espace Jeunes)

The kids' section in the basement of the main library is a vibrant, quiet haven filled with toys, cushions, and French/English picture books. It is a fantastic, free indoor spot for a toddler to decompress and play quietly.

Midday / 1-2h

Atwater Market & Lachine Canal

This market features a beautiful outdoor play area and plenty of space to run. Grab some fresh cheese and bakery treats, then picnic by the canal while watching the boats pass by.

Where to eat

  • Fairmount Bagel — Grab warm, wood-fired sesame bagels straight from the oven, which are easy for toddlers to hold and chew on the go.
  • La Banquise — This iconic, colorful spot serves classic Quebec poutine with squeaky cheese curds that kids love, plus they offer high chairs and family-friendly service.
  • Kem Coba — Line up for their legendary, colorful soft-serve ice cream twists made with fresh, natural ingredients that will delight any child.
  • Juliette & Chocolat — A chocolate lover's paradise offering decadent crepes, waffles, and kid-sized hot chocolates in a very welcoming, bustling atmosphere.
