What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Edison and Ford Winter Estates

Stroll through the lush, shaded botanical gardens and historic homes along the Caloosahatchee River. The wide, paved pathways are highly stroller-accessible, and the massive historic banyan trees provide excellent canopy shade to keep baby Mary Claire cool.

Morning 8:30 AM - 10:30 AM

Lakes Regional Park

Enjoy a peaceful morning walk along paved trails wrapping around scenic lakes. The park features beautiful sensory gardens, shaded pavilions, and a miniature train ride that provides a delightful, low-speed visual treat for an infant.

Early Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Six Mile Cypress Slough Preserve

Discover Florida's native wetlands along a fully elevated, 1.2-mile boardwalk trail. The dense canopy offers cool shade, making it a quiet, sensory-rich walk perfect for a sleeping baby in a stroller.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Fort Myers River District

Explore the beautifully restored historic waterfront district. The wide, flat sidewalks are perfect for an easy afternoon walk, offering plenty of upscale boutiques, galleries, and quiet cafes to duck into for a quick feeding.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Manatee Park

A non-captive refuge where you can view native Florida manatees up close in the warm water canal. Paved, flat walkways and shaded viewing platforms make it incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Bunche Beach

A pristine, undeveloped beach known for its exceptionally shallow, warm, and wave-free waters. It is the perfect spot to let Mary Claire dip her toes in the Gulf of Mexico without worrying about heavy surf or crowds.

Afternoon 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM

Bell Tower Shops

An open-air luxury lifestyle center featuring beautiful fountains and landscaped walkways. This is a very stroller-friendly spot to enjoy high-end shopping and upscale dining in a calm, unhurried atmosphere.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Bowditch Point Park

Located at the quiet northern tip of Fort Myers Beach, this park offers a peaceful boardwalk system through barrier island habitats. It provides accessible beach entry and shaded picnic areas away from the busier resort zones.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:30 PM

IMAG History & Science Center

Escape the midday heat in this fully air-conditioned center. It features touch tanks and visually engaging exhibits that are highly stimulating for older infants, all within a spacious, stroller-friendly layout.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Veranda

Indulge in classic Southern fine dining inside a pair of historic early-20th-century homes. Book an early outdoor terrace seating in the romantic garden courtyard, where the gentle ambient noise of fountains comfortably masks any baby giggles.

Doc Ford's Rum Bar & Grille

Savor gourmet Caribbean-inspired seafood, like their signature Yucatan Shrimp, in a spacious and lively waterfront setting. The accommodating staff gladly provides sturdy highchairs, and the breezy patio keeps the atmosphere cool and casual.

Osteria Celli

Enjoy sophisticated, upscale Italian cuisine featuring house-made pastas like Tagliolini with fresh seafood. Opt for an early 5:00 PM seating in their elegant dining room for a quiet, spacious table perfect for accommodating a luxury stroller.

Tarpon House Restaurant

Located water-side, this elegant dining spot offers fresh, locally caught seafood and prime steaks. The spacious, upscale dining room has a relaxed, family-friendly resort vibe that is highly welcoming to infant travelers.

Fancy's Southern Cafe

Known for elevated comfort food like chicken and waffles and lobster mac and cheese. The large, plush booths provide plenty of room to keep Mary Claire comfortable, and the warm, lively environment is excellent for families.
