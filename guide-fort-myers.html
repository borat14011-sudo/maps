Fort Myers

Trade the Houston humidity for the gentle Gulf breezes of Fort Myers, where historic charm meets effortless family travel. This low-key Florida haven offers wide, stroller-friendly boardwalks, calm shallow waters, and shaded parks that keep your little one engaged without sensory overload. It is the perfect blend of coastal relaxation for you and structured, safe exploration for your toddler daughter.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 minute taxi or Uber from Southwest Florida International Airport (RSW) directly to downtown hotels, or ~35 minutes to beachside resorts.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Edison & Ford Winter Estates

Beautiful botanical gardens and paved paths make stroller navigation a breeze under the shade of massive banyan trees. The outdoor estate layout lets you stroll at your own pace while your toddler gazes at the colorful flowers.

Morning / 2-3h

Lakes Regional Park

Ride the miniature train through the park and let your toddler cool off at the fenced-in splash pad. Paved walking paths around the lake are perfect for an early morning stroller run before the midday heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Six Mile Cypress Slough Preserve

The 1.2-mile fully boardwalked trail is entirely stroller-accessible, offering a shaded, bug-free canopy to spot turtles and birds. It's a peaceful sanctuary where a sleeping baby can nap in the stroller while parents enjoy the quiet.

Afternoon / 2h

IMAG History & Science Center

Head to the interactive water table and the touch tank where your toddler can gently touch sea stars and tiny fish. The dedicated early-childhood play area provides a safe, padded space for toddlers to climb and explore indoors.

Morning / 1-2h

Manatee Park

Visit between December and March to see wild manatees seeking warmth in the canal, viewable from easy-to-navigate paved overlooks. The park features a playground and butterfly garden that are easily accessible with a stroller.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Alliance for the Arts GreenMarket

Held every Saturday morning, this spacious outdoor market is perfect for an early wake-up with a toddler to grab fresh pastries and local fruit. There is plenty of open grass for your little one to stretch her legs while live acoustic music plays.

Morning / 2-3h

Shell Factory & Nature Park

This classic attraction features a walkable nature park with over 400 animals, a petting zoo, and a playground area tailored for young kids. It is highly accessible and offers a fun, sensory-rich morning for a toddler.

Morning / 2-3h

Bowditch Point Park

Located at the tip of Fort Myers Beach, this quiet park offers accessible boardwalks through the preserve and a calm, bay-side beach shoreline. It is much quieter than the main pier area, making it perfect for a peaceful baby beach day with shade tents.

Morning / 1-2h

Calusa Nature Center and Planetarium

Stroll the easy, shaded boardwalk trail and visit the outdoor raptor aviary to see rescued hawks and owls up close. The quiet, natural setting is highly conducive to a stroller nap under the pine canopy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ford's Garage (Downtown Fort Myers)

Order the Black Angus burgers while enjoying the fun, vintage gas-station theme with a real Model-T hanging over the bar. The ambient noise level is high enough to drown out any toddler tantrums, and high chairs are plentiful.

Doc Ford's Rum Bar & Grille (Fort Myers Beach)

Try the famous Yucatan Shrimp on the spacious outdoor deck overlooking the bay where boats pass by to keep your baby entertained. The atmosphere is casual, lively, and highly welcoming to families with young children.

Pinchers (The Marina at Edison Ford)

Feast on fresh blue crabs right on the water while kids under 12 eat free. The waterfront patio offers plenty of space for stroller parking and early dining options starting at 11 AM.

Fancy's Southern Cafe

Order the chicken and waffles in this spacious restaurant featuring large, comfortable booths perfect for wrangling a toddler. The staff is exceptionally family-friendly, and the early dinner service is quiet and fast.

Love Boat Ice Cream (San Carlos Blvd)

Treat yourself to a scoop of homemade Salty Jack ice cream at this local institution with plenty of outdoor seating. It's the perfect quick, casual stop where messy toddler ice-cream hands are easily managed outside.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Marriott Sanibel Harbour Resort & Spa

This resort offers spectacular views of Sanibel Island, multiple heated pools (including a kid-friendly one), and spacious rooms with plenty of crib space. Its location right before the causeway means quick access to both the beaches and downtown Fort Myers.

Pink Shell Beach Resort & Marina

Situated directly on Fort Myers Beach, this resort boasts condo-style villas with full kitchens to easily prep baby bottles, plus a zero-entry lagoon pool. They offer complimentary cribs, high chairs, and beach toys for a completely stress-free toddler stay.

The Westin Cape Coral Resort at Marina Village

Located just across the river, this luxury resort features massive one- and two-bedroom suites with full kitchens and in-unit laundry. The quiet marina-side location offers scenic, stroller-friendly walking paths and a lovely, calm pool deck.
