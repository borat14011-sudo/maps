Fort Myers

← back to travel board
Gentle Gulf waves meet sun-drenched shores in Fort Myers, where historic palms line the streets and coastal wildlife thrives. Families can collect pristine seashells by morning, explore lush mangrove trails by afternoon, and catch stunning orange sunsets over the water. It is a laid-back tropical playground perfectly suited for slow-paced wandering with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or rideshare from Southwest Florida International Airport (RSW) to the beach hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Edison & Ford Winter Estates

Wander through the historic winter homes of Thomas Edison and Henry Ford, surrounded by massive banyan trees and lush botanical gardens. Toddlers will love the wide-open, paved pathways and seeing the giant laboratory equipment.

Morning / 3h

Lakes Regional Park

This massive park features a miniature train ride, splash pads, and scenic walking paths around the lake. It is a toddler paradise with fully fenced playgrounds and plenty of shaded picnic spots.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Manatee Park

Get up close to gentle Florida manatees that seek refuge in the warm water canal during the cooler winter months. Toddlers can easily view them from the shallow-water boardwalks without needing to board a boat.

Afternoon / 2-3h

IMAG History & Science Center

Touch a live stingray, run through interactive weather storm exhibits, and explore hands-on science displays designed for curious minds. The dedicated early-childhood area is perfect for toddlers to climb and play safely.

Morning / 2h

Calusa Nature Center and Planetarium

Walk the elevated boardwalks through a cypress swamp and meet rescued raptors, butterflies, and reptiles. Toddlers will enjoy the easy, flat trail and the weekly live animal presentations.

Late Afternoon / 3h

Fort Myers Beach near Times Square

Build sandcastles in the famously soft, white quartz sand and splash in the calm, shallow Gulf of Mexico waters. The gentle surf makes it incredibly safe for toddlers to dip their toes.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

GreenMarket at Alliance for the Arts

Stroll through this vibrant outdoor Saturday market to sample fresh local fruits, organic snacks, and listen to live music. Toddlers will love the open grassy lawns to stretch their legs while parents shop.

Early Morning / 1.5h

Six Mile Cypress Slough Preserve

Walk along a fully shaded, stroller-friendly 1.2-mile boardwalk over a wetland ecosystem teeming with turtles, herons, and frogs. It is an easy, quiet nature walk that keeps toddlers cool under the dense canopy.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Shell Factory & Nature Park

Browse a colossal collection of seashells, play a round of mini-golf, and visit the petting zoo to feed goats and camels. The playground and kid-friendly bumper boats are guaranteed toddler crowd-pleasers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Doc Ford's Rum Bar & Grille

Order the famous Yucatan shrimp, which is sweet, spicy, and perfectly paired with their outdoor, water-facing patio.

Love Boat Ice Cream

Treat the family to a scoop of homemade key lime pie ice cream at this legendary local shop.

Pinchers

Feast on fresh, locally caught blue crabs and golden fish baskets while the kids enjoy the colorful, lively waterfront atmosphere.

Ford's Garage

Order the classic gourmet burgers and crispy onion rings in a fun, auto-themed garage restaurant filled with vintage cars.

Dixie Fish Co.

Enjoy fresh-caught grouper sandwiches and key lime pie on a rustic open-air deck right on the harbor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack an all-terrain stroller for nature boardwalks and a sturdy wagon to haul beach gear and toddlers across the soft sand. Apply reef-safe sunscreen generously and schedule heavy outdoor exploring for the cooler morning hours to avoid the midday heat.
