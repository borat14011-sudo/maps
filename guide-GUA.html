Urban adventures and Mayan wonders for all ages

Guatemala City

← back to travel board
Nestled in a volcanic valley, Guatemala City blends vibrant modern culture with deep-rooted Mayan heritage. Families will discover colorful markets, lush green spaces, and interactive museums designed for curious little minds. It is an energetic and welcoming destination where urban exploration meets child-friendly adventures.

What to do

Morning / 3h

La Aurora Zoo

This beautifully landscaped zoo features spacious exhibits with tigers, penguins, and elephants. Toddlers will love the petting zoo area and the colorful train ride around the park.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Children's Museum (Museo de los Niños)

A fully interactive science and play museum with giant bubble makers and Lego building zones. It is completely safe, indoor, and tailored perfectly for toddlers to burn off energy.

Morning / 1-2h

Mapa en Relieve

A giant, open-air 3D map of Guatemala built in 1905 that lets kids view the country's volcanoes from viewing towers. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the scale, and there is plenty of space to run around.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Plaza de la Constitución

The historic heart of the city is perfect for watching street performers and chasing friendly pigeons. It is stroller-friendly, and you can easily view the majestic National Palace from the plaza.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

A bustling underground labyrinth of colors, handicrafts, and exotic tropical fruits. Toddlers will love the sights and smells of the flower section, though babywearing is recommended due to narrow aisles.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Paseo Cayalá

A sprawling, pedestrian-only white-stone city center featuring open plazas, playgrounds, and carousel rides. Stroller navigation is a breeze here, and there are many safe, clean family restrooms.

Morning / 2h

Parque Ecológico Jacarandas de Cayalá

A serene, forested escape right inside the city boundaries, featuring easy hiking paths and wooden bridges over small streams. Toddlers will enjoy spotting local birds and butterflies.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo Ixchel del Traje Indígena

A vibrant museum showcasing the rich history of Mayan textiles, filled with colorful woven clothing. The outdoor grassy gardens are perfect for toddlers to toddle around while parents appreciate the exhibits.

Full Day / 4-5h

Mundo Petapa IRTRA

A clean and brilliantly designed amusement park with a dedicated playground section for toddlers. It features gentle rides, a small pool, and beautiful gardens that the whole family will enjoy.

Where to eat

  • Kacao — Dine under a traditional thatched roof and sample rich Pepian stew in a highly welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere.
  • Pollo Campero — Guatemala's legendary fried chicken chain is a guaranteed hit for kids, featuring indoor play areas and ultra-fast service.
  • El Portalito — Located in the historic center, this iconic spot offers delicious chuchitos (Guatemalan tamales) that toddlers love for their soft texture.
  • Saúl Bistro Cayalá — Known for its whimsical design, delicious sweet crepes, and colorful outdoor seating that keeps children entertained.
