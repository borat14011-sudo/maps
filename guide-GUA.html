Guatemala City

← back to travel board
Guatemala City blends a bustling metropolis with hidden pockets of lush nature, towering volcanoes on the horizon, and rich Mayan heritage. It’s an energetic playground where families can wander through colorful plazas, explore interactive museums, and taste hand-pressed tortillas. Navigating this dynamic capital reveals a surprisingly warm, green, and kid-friendly heart.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

La Aurora Zoo

This beautifully maintained zoo features open-air exhibits, a massive playground, and close-up views of penguins and elephants. Toddlers will love the dedicated petting zoo and the flat, stroller-friendly paths.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Children's Museum

Located right near the zoo, this museum offers hands-on exhibits focusing on science, culture, and a miniature grocery store. There is a dedicated soft-play sensory area designed specifically for infants and toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de la Constitución

The historic heart of the city is perfect for watching the changing of the guard and feeding hundreds of friendly pigeons. Toddlers will delight in chasing the birds across the massive, open stone plaza.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

A bustling underground market filled with colorful textiles, wooden toys, and stalls selling fresh tropical fruits. It can be crowded, so keep toddlers in a carrier to enjoy the sensory sights and smells safely.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Paseo Cayalá

A European-style pedestrian village with white-stone architecture, wide walkways, and plenty of green lawns to run around. It is completely stroller-accessible and features carousel rides and toy shops perfect for little ones.

Morning / 1h

Relief Map

This giant, 120-year-old open-air scale model of Guatemala lets kids see the country's volcanoes and lakes from observation towers. Toddlers will enjoy pointing out the tiny mountains from the safety of the ground-level viewing paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Kaminaljuyu Archaeological Park

Explore the grassy ruins of a major ancient Maya city tucked right inside a quiet neighborhood park. It is a peaceful spot for toddlers to touch real history and run around on the wide green lawns.

Morning / 2h

Parque Ecológico Deportivo Cayalá

A lush nature reserve in the middle of the city featuring easy walking trails, canopy bridges, and butterfly spotting. The flat dirt paths are easy for toddlers to walk, though a carrier is best for the occasional stairs.

Full Day / 4-6h

Irtra Petapa Theme Park

A vibrant amusement park featuring beautifully themed gardens, a dinosaur park, and a dedicated castle area with gentle rides. Toddlers will love the colorful carousels, miniature train, and spacious family-friendly walkways.

Where to eat

  • Kak'ik at Kacao — Enjoy this rich, mildly spiced traditional Mayan turkey soup served in a gorgeous, plant-filled courtyard restaurant that is very welcoming to families.
  • Rellenitos de Plátano — These sweet, pan-fried plantain dumplings stuffed with sweetened black beans and chocolate are a warm, gooey treat that toddlers will eagerly devour.
  • Saúl Bistro — Featuring whimsical, colorful decor and a dedicated kids' menu with delicious crepes, this trendy spot offers a relaxed and visual dining experience for parents and little ones.
  • Chuchitos — These small, pocket-sized Guatemalan tamales stuffed with chicken or pork and mild tomato sauce are wrapped in corn husks and make the perfect, mess-free handheld snack for hungry toddlers.
