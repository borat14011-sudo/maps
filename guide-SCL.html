Andes views and urban adventures for the whole family

Santiago

← back to travel board
Framed by the dramatic, snow-capped peaks of the Andes, Santiago blends European elegance with vibrant South American energy. From sprawling hilltop parks accessed by historic funiculars to interactive museums, the Chilean capital offers an incredibly green and accessible playground for families. Its stroller-friendly neighborhoods and welcoming culture make exploring with little ones an absolute breeze.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Cerro San Cristóbal Funicular and Cable Car

Ride the historic 1925 funicular up the hill for sweeping city views, then take the modern cable car back down. Toddlers will love the gently moving cabins and the wide-open grassy spaces at the summit.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque Bicentenario

This beautifully manicured park in Vitacura features modern playgrounds, a large pond where kids can feed pink flamingos, and flat, paved paths. It is fully stroller-accessible and perfect for a relaxed family picnic.

Morning / 3-4h

Museo Interactivo Mirador (MIM)

A massive, hands-on science museum where kids of all ages can touch, play, and experiment with physics and art. The dedicated 'MIM Chiquito' zone is specifically designed for sensory play for toddlers.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas and Changing of the Guard

The historic heart of Santiago is alive with street performers, painters, and grand architecture. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the brass band during the alternating-day changing of the guard ceremony at the nearby Palacio de La Moneda.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado Central

Dive into a sensory spectacle of fresh seafood, colorful stalls, and lively local musicians under a historic wrought-iron roof. The bustling environment is highly engaging for toddlers, though keeping them in a carrier is best during peak hours.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque Araucano

This sprawling park features spectacular playground equipment, rose gardens, and a floor-fountain splash pad. Toddlers can run safely on the soft-turf outdoor play areas while parents enjoy the mountain backdrop.

Morning / 1-2h

Cerro Santa Lucía

A fairytale-like hill in the middle of the city, complete with stone castles, fountains, and secret pathways. While the climb to the top is steep, toddlers will love exploring the castle gates at the lower, stroller-friendly levels.

Morning / 2-3h

Santiago Metropolitan Zoo

Located on the slopes of Cerro San Cristóbal, this zoo offers close-up views of native Chilean animals like Humboldt penguins and condors. Its vertical layout can be tiring, so bring a sturdy carrier rather than a stroller.

Afternoon / 2h

Pueblito Los Dominicos

A charming artisan village styled like an old colonial town, filled with winding dirt paths, craft shops, and live farm animals. It is a quiet, traffic-free oasis where toddlers can safely wander and look at colorful birds in the aviary.

Where to eat

  • Mote con Huesillo — This traditional, sweet Chilean street drink made of dried peaches and cooked husked wheat is a cool treat that toddlers love eating with a spoon.
  • Liguria (Providencia) — A vibrant, vintage-themed local institution serving comforting Chilean classics like empanadas in a highly welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere.
  • Empanadas de Queso at Pastelería Las Rosas Chicas — Stop by this beloved bakery for fresh, mild cheese empanadas that make the perfect warm, mess-free toddler lunch.
  • Café Wonderland — An Alice in Wonderland-themed cafe in Barrio Lastarria with whimsical decor, delicious cakes, and high chairs that turn afternoon tea into an exciting fairy tale.
👨‍👩‍👧 While Santiago's metro is highly efficient, many historic stations lack elevators, making a lightweight stroller or baby carrier a necessity. Tap water is safe but high in minerals, so stick to bottled water for young children to prevent mild tummy aches.
