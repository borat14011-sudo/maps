Swiss Riviera (Montreux / Lavaux Vineyards

← back to travel board
Nestled between dramatic peaks and the sparkling waters of Lake Geneva, the Swiss Riviera offers a microclimate of palm trees and sun-kissed slopes. Families can wander through medieval fortress halls, ride vintage cogwheel trains, and stroll along stroller-friendly promenades framed by vibrant flowers. It is an enchanting alpine escape where Swiss efficiency meets Mediterranean ease.
🚕 Getting there: ~60 min direct train ride from Geneva Airport (GVA) to Montreux Station, or a scenic ~65 min drive via the A1 highway.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Chillon Castle

Explore Switzerland's most visited historic monument, a stunning medieval island fortress with secret passages. Toddlers will love running in the open courtyards, though you should leave the stroller at the ticket counter due to the stone steps.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Lavaux Express Vineyard Train

Hop aboard this charming, brightly colored mini-train that winds through the UNESCO-listed terraced vineyards of Lutry or Cully. It is an effortless, bumpy, and highly entertaining way for toddlers to enjoy the steep, breathtaking landscapes.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Montreux Lakeside Promenade

Stroll along this exceptionally flat, paved path adorned with exotic flowers, public art, and the famous Freddie Mercury statue. Kids can burn off energy at the lakeside playground while parents soak in the dramatic mountain backdrop.

Morning / 3-4h

Rochers-de-Naye Cogwheel Railway

Ride a vintage red cogwheel train up to 2,042 meters for panoramic views of Lake Geneva. Toddlers will be thrilled to visit the 'Marmot Paradise' at the summit to spot alpine marmots from around the world.

Morning / 1-2h

Vevey Food Market

Immerse yourselves in local culture at this vibrant open-air market at the Grande Place, bursting with fresh flowers, local cheeses, and warm pastries. Toddlers can enjoy a ride on the nearby vintage carousel right by the water.

Afternoon / 2h

Alimentarium Food Museum

Located right in front of the giant fork sticking out of Lake Geneva, this interactive museum explores the world of food through hands-on games. Toddlers will love the sensory exhibits and colorful play areas designed to spark culinary curiosity.

Afternoon / 1.5h

CGN Paddle Steamer Cruise

Step aboard a historic Belle Époque paddle steamboat for a gentle loop around the Swiss Riviera. The steady, soothing movement of the ship is perfect for a toddler nap while you marvel at the passing vineyards and castles.

Morning / 1h

Queen Studio Experience

Visit the original recording studio of Queen inside the Montreux Casino for a fun musical detour. It is free, stroller-accessible, and a quick sensory experience with flashing lights and legendary tunes that kids find surprisingly engaging.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardin de l'Ancien Stand Playground

Let the kids loose at this fantastic, modern playground located in neighboring La Tour-de-Peilz. It features safe wooden climbing structures, slides, and swings set on soft grass right alongside the tranquil lake shores.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café de la Vieille Ville

Grab a cozy wooden booth in Montreux's old town and order their famous, ultra-creamy Moitié-Moitié cheese fondue served with plenty of bread cubes and baby potatoes.

L'Atelier

This welcoming, family-friendly bistro in Vevey serves incredible homemade pastas, artisanal burgers, and delicious seasonal soups that appeal to both parents and toddlers.

Le Deck

Sip a glass of crisp local Chasselas wine on the stunning panoramic terrace in Chexbres while the kids enjoy fresh apple juice and a local cheese platter.

Tango

Sit on the lakeside terrace in Montreux and order their exceptionally crispy, wood-fired Margherita pizzas that are easy to slice and share with hungry little ones.

Confiserie Poyet

Visit this legendary bakery in Vevey to treat the family to their famous 'Chaplin's Shoes' milk chocolates and freshly baked, buttery brioche.

👨‍👩‍👧 Be sure to collect your free Montreux Riviera Card from your hotel for complimentary public bus travel and steep discounts on local trains and castle entries. While the lakeside promenades are perfectly flat and stroller-friendly, keep a baby carrier handy for the steep stairs and cobbled paths in the Lavaux vineyards.
