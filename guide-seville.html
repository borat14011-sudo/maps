Andalusian elegance for your growing family

Seville

← back to travel board
Seville seduces with its sun-drenched plazas, fragrant orange blossoms, and Moorish palaces that whisper stories of centuries past. For families traveling with an infant, the city’s stroller-friendly promenades along the Guadalquivir River and the shaded alleys of Santa Cruz offer a gentle rhythm. The Real Alcázar’s expansive gardens provide cool hideaways where a baby can nap in the dappled light. Luxury touches like private pool access at your hotel and room service from Michelin-starred kitchens make even nappy changes feel indulgent.
🚕 Getting there: 15 minutes by private transfer from Seville Airport (SVQ) to the city center; book a car with a baby seat (request upon reservation) and ample trunk space for a stroller and luggage.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am (2h)

Real Alcázar de Sevilla

A UNESCO World Heritage site with lush, stroller-accessible gardens and shaded patios; the courtyard of the Maidens is cool and quiet, perfect for a feeding break.

Late morning 10:30-12:30pm (2h)

Seville Cathedral & Giralda

The largest Gothic cathedral in the world with a ramp to the top (stroller-accessible) offering panoramic views; the vast interior is quiet and cool, ideal for an infant.

Late afternoon 4-6pm (2h)

Plaza de España

Majestic semicircular plaza with a canal; stroller-friendly paved paths, shaded arcades, and benches for nursing; your baby will love the colorful tile alcoves.

Early morning 9:30-11am (1.5h)

Metropol Parasol (Setas de Sevilla)

Giant wooden mushroom-like structure with an elevated walkway; elevator access (stroller ok) and a children’s play area beneath; early morning avoids crowds.

Midday 12-1:30pm (1.5h)

Triana Market

Covered market with ready-to-eat tapas and fresh produce; stroller-friendly aisles, highchairs at many bars, and a relaxed buzz that doesn't overwhelm little ears.

Morning 9-11am (2h)

Maria Luisa Park

Expansive park with fountains, duck ponds, and wide stroller paths; rent a paddleboat on the lake (baby can sit on your lap) or picnic under a palm tree.

Late morning 10-11am (1h)

Torre del Oro

13th-century watchtower along the river; small but manageable with a stroller (ground floor only unless you babywear); the riverfront promenade is perfect for a stroll.

Afternoon 1-2:30pm (1.5h)

Casa de Pilatos

A 16th-century palace with Mudejar tilework and quiet courtyards; stroller-friendly ground floor, and the gardens offer cool shade for a feeding stop.

Late afternoon 5-7pm (2h)

Barrio Santa Cruz

The historic Jewish quarter with narrow, winding lanes and hidden plazas; push your stroller slowly through the shaded streets and stop for a cold drink at Plaza de los Venerables.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Rinconcillo

Seville’s oldest bar (founded 1670) serving classic tapas like salmorejo and montaditos; highchairs available, early lunch seating (1pm) is quieter for baby.

La Azotea

Modern Andalusian tapas with a rooftop terrace (request ground floor table if noise is a concern); they provide highchairs and a children’s portion of croquettes.

Sobretablas

Trendy spot near the cathedral with a signature beef cheek in red wine; accommodates strollers at the bar, has a changing table in the restroom.

Eslava

Award-winning tapas including the famous cake of goat cheese and caramelized onion; early dinner seating (7pm) is calm, highchairs on request.

Ovejas Negras

Hip restaurant with outdoor terrace (stroller access) and a kids’ plate; the ‘smoked eggplant with honey’ is signature, and the noise level is moderate.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Alfonso XIII

$200-400/night

A Luxury Collection icon with a stunning Moorish courtyard pool; crib on request, family suites with separate sitting area, blackout curtains, and 24-hour room service for late-night feeds.

Palacio de Villapanés

$200-400/night

Boutique 5-star in a restored 18th-century palace; offers plunge pool (not heated but shallow for toddler splashing), cribs with organic linens, and a serene garden courtyard perfect for baby's nap.

Hotel Mercer Sevilla

$200-400/night

Luxury boutique in the old Jewish quarter with a rooftop pool (shallow area) and blackout curtains; suites have kitchenettes for warming bottles, and they provide a baby bathtub on request.

👨‍👩‍👧 Seville’s cobblestone streets are manageable with a sturdy all-terrain stroller (avoid umbrella strollers). Heat is intense from June to September; plan indoor activities during midday and keep baby in breathable cotton with a hat. For emergencies, Hospital Virgen del Rocío has a pediatric ER, and 24-hour pharmacies (Farmacia de guardia) are signposted with green crosses; stock up on formula at large supermarkets like El Corte Inglés.
