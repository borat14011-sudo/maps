Seville

Seville welcomes families with its orange-scented streets, vibrant culture, and relaxed, stroller-friendly pace. Children will love climbing ancient towers, exploring lush palace gardens filled with peacocks, and snacking on churros in lively squares. It is a city where history feels like a fairy tale come to life for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 min taxi or rideshare directly to historic center hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Real Alcázar of Seville

Explore the stunning Moorish palace halls and let toddlers run wild in the expansive gardens searching for the resident peacocks and ducks.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Plaza de España & Maria Luisa Park

Rent a rowboat on the canal or a family quad-bike to pedal through the shaded, playground-dotted paths of the adjacent park.

Morning / 1.5-2h

Seville Cathedral & La Giralda

Climb the Giralda tower's stroller-friendly ramps instead of stairs for a breathtaking view, then spot the hanging wooden elephant inside.

Sunset / 1h

Setas de Sevilla (Metropol Parasol)

Walk along the winding rooftop boardwalks of this giant wooden structure; toddlers will love the sensory experience and the shaded playground at the base.

Morning / 1-1.5h

Mercado de Triana

Walk through this vibrant indoor market built over castle ruins, perfect for trying fresh fruit, local cheeses, and watching fishmongers at work.

Afternoon / 1h

Guadalquivir River Boat Cruise

Take a relaxed 1-hour boat cruise down the river to see the Gold Tower and colorful Triana neighborhood without tiring out tiny legs.

Afternoon / 1h

Jardines de Murillo

A beautiful park next to the Alcázar wall featuring giant ficus trees with massive aerial roots to climb around and a dedicated children's playground.

Afternoon / 2h

Aquarium of Seville

Escape the midday heat in this modern aquarium themed around Magellan's voyage, featuring shark tunnels and interactive touch tanks perfect for toddlers.

Evening / 1h

Casa de la Memoria

Experience an intimate, high-energy flamenco performance in a 15th-century courtyard, which is short enough (1 hour) to keep older toddlers captivated by the footwork.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bar El Comercio

Go here for Seville's best traditional breakfast of thick, crispy churros dipped in rich, warm chocolate.

La Bartola

Enjoy family-friendly, modern tapas with plenty of vegetarian choices and a welcoming, casual atmosphere.

Bodega Santa Cruz (Las Columnas)

Grab quick, delicious, and cheap bites like montaditos (small sandwiches) and potato tortilla in a bustling, high-energy environment where kid noise blends right in.

Gusto

A fantastic spot near the Cathedral offering high-quality traditional paella and kid-friendly Mediterranean options in a bright, modern dining room.

Heladeria Bolas

Cool off with delicious, artisanal ice cream made with natural local ingredients like Seville orange and goat cheese with wild honey.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a lightweight stroller with good suspension to navigate the historic cobblestones, and always carry a carrier for climbing monuments like the Giralda. Since locals eat dinner late, plan for a heavy 'merienda' (afternoon snack) of churros or gelato around 6 PM to keep toddlers happy until restaurants open around 8 PM.
