St. Thomas welcomes families with its breathtaking turquoise harbors, emerald hills, and powdery white-sand beaches. This vibrant Caribbean jewel blends rich history with gentle, shallow waters that are perfect for little splashers. From close-up marine encounters to scenic mountaintop views, it is a tropical playground designed for stress-free family memories.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 30 minutes via open-air safari bus or private taxi, depending on whether you are staying in Charlotte Amalie or the East End resort area.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Magens Bay Beach

Renowned for its ultra-calm, wave-free waters and soft sand, this horseshoe-shaped bay is a dream for young swimmers. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, pool-like edge while parents relax under the shade of coconut palms.

Morning / 2-3h

Coral World Ocean Park

This interactive marine park lets kids get up close with sea turtles, stingrays, and sea lions without needing to swim. The Undersea Observatory is perfect for toddlers to safely view the coral reef and colorful fish.

Morning / 1-2h

Phantasea Tropical Botanical Garden

A lush, shaded oasis filled with orchids, bromeliads, and colorful butterflies that will captivate little eyes. The pathways are easy for toddlers to navigate, offering a cool respite from the midday beach sun.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

St. Thomas Skyride to Paradise Point

Ride the aerial cable car up 700 feet to witness breathtaking panoramic views of the harbor and cruise ships. Toddlers will be thrilled by the smooth tram ride, and there is plenty of space at the top to stretch small legs.

Morning / 2-3h

Coki Point Beach

Known for its crystal-clear water and abundant fish right near the shore, this beach is ideal for introducing little ones to marine life. Toddlers can easily stand in the shallows and watch colorful sergeant majors swim around their ankles.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Drake's Seat

This historic mountaintop lookout offers a stunning, picture-perfect view over Magens Bay and the surrounding British Virgin Islands. It is a quick, easy roadside stop to snap a family photo without tiring out toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

Fort Christian

The oldest standing structure in the Virgin Islands, this bright red fort features a spacious courtyard perfect for toddlers to explore safely. Kids will love looking at the old cannons and walking through the cool, historic brick corridors.

Morning / 1h

Rothschild Francis Market Square

Immerse your family in local culture at this historic, bustling market where vendors sell fresh tropical fruits like mangoes and genips. It is a sensory treat for toddlers to see the vibrant produce and enjoy a sweet, natural snack.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Lindquist Beach (Smith Bay Park)

Located in a protected park, this pristine beach features soft, pink-hued sand, calm shallow waters, and plenty of natural shade from sea grape trees. It is less crowded than Magens Bay, making it easier to keep a relaxed eye on wandering toddlers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gladys' Cafe

Order the famous coconut pancakes or local saltfish with fungi in a warm, family-friendly stone-walled courtyard.

Duffy's Love Shack

Dig into kid-approved fish tacos and non-alcoholic tropical smoothies served in fun souvenir cups at this quirky, open-air eatery.

Sunset Grille

Feast on fresh grilled mahi-mahi while your toddlers safely play in the sand right next to your oceanfront table at Secret Harbour.

The Greenhouse Restaurant

Grab a harborfront table for a diverse menu featuring easy crowd-pleasers like flatbread pizzas, burgers, and conch fritters.

Pie Whole

Share delicious, wood-fired artisanal pizzas in a relaxed, casual Frenchtown setting that welcomingly embraces noisy toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a sturdy car seat to safely navigate the island's steep, winding mountain roads, as open-air safari taxis do not have seatbelts. Always pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen, swim diapers, and snacks, as shade and convenience stores can be limited at the more secluded beaches.
