What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

The Blue Lagoon

While infants under two cannot enter the geothermal waters, parents can take turns experiencing the exclusive Retreat Spa while the other strolls the paved, stroller-friendly paths winding through the dramatic lava fields.

Late morning 11:30am-1pm / 1.5h

Harpa Concert Hall

This architectural masterpiece features entirely flat, stroller-accessible indoor concourses that are perfect for escaping the wind while enjoying harbor views and calm acoustic warmth.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Laugardalslaug Geothermal Pool

Reykjavík's premier public pool offers a warm, shallow indoor infant splash pool, clean baby changing facilities, and private family cabins for a stress-free transition.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Gullfoss Waterfall

The iconic Golden Circle waterfall features a wide, paved upper viewing deck that is fully stroller-accessible, allowing you to witness the roaring cascade safely out of the heavy mist.

Early afternoon 1-2:30pm / 1.5h

Geysir Geothermal Area

A smooth, paved boardwalk makes it easy to push a stroller directly to the erupting Strokkur geysir, and the spacious visitor center offers excellent infant changing stations.

Late morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Þingvellir National Park

The dramatic Almannagjá gorge features a wide, smooth gravel pathway that is stroller-friendly, offering a quiet, majestic walk between tectonic plates that is ideal for a baby nap.

Afternoon 3-4pm / 1h

Tjörnin Pond

This tranquil lake in central Reykjavík is bordered by flat, paved pathways where you can push the stroller, watch the local swans, and rest on plenty of scenic park benches.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Perlan Museum

The futuristic dome features elevators accessing a fully stroller-accessible indoor ice cave, an interactive planetarium, and an observation deck with panoramic city views.

Afternoon 1-2:30pm / 1.5h

Reykjavík Botanical Garden

Located in Laugardalur valley, these peaceful gardens feature smooth, flat pathways beneath leafy canopies, offering a quiet environment for Mary Claire to sleep in her stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Grillmarkaðurinn (The Grillmarket)

Upscale Icelandic cuisine serving premium grilled meats; they offer an early 6:00 PM seating with highchairs and a vibrant, warm ambient noise level that easily masks baby coos.

Kopar

Overlooking the Old Harbor, this refined venue serves fresh local seafood and features spacious table layouts to accommodate strollers, plus a very welcoming staff.

Slippbarinn

Located inside the Marina Hotel, this trendy but casual spot is perfect for an early dinner, offering cozy booths, highchairs, and a lively atmosphere that is highly forgiving of infants.

Matur og Drykkur

This Michelin-selected eatery serves elevated traditional dishes in a historic building with spacious, comfortable seating, highchairs, and an exceptionally attentive staff.

Café Loki

An ideal, casual lunch stop directly across from Hallgrímskirkja serving classic rye bread ice cream; they provide sturdy highchairs and a relaxed, family-friendly vibe.
