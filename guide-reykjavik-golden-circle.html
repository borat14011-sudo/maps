What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Þingvellir National Park

Walk between tectonic plates along the dramatic Almannagjá gorge on a wide, smoothly paved gravel path that is highly stroller-friendly and sheltered from high winds.

Midday 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM / 1h

Gullfoss Waterfall

Experience one of Europe's most powerful waterfalls from the upper viewing deck, which features a completely flat wooden boardwalk designed to protect strollers from mist and damp soil.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM / 1h

Geysir Geothermal Area

Stroll along smooth, paved flat trails to watch the Strokkur geyser erupt; ensure you keep baby Mary Claire upwind from the warm, sulfurous steam clouds.

Late Afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM / 1.5h

Friðheimar Greenhouse

Step into a wonderfully warm, sunlit indoor tomato farm with flat gravel floors, gentle buzzing bees, and a quiet ambiance that is perfect for an afternoon infant nap.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Perlan Museum

Explore a completely indoor, temperature-controlled dome featuring a stroller-accessible ice cave, interactive nature exhibits, and a calm, soft-lit planetarium show that baby will find soothing.

Late Afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM / 1h

Tjörnin Pond

Enjoy a peaceful stroll around the paved flat perimeter of this central city pond to watch the swans and ducks, complete with plenty of park benches for quiet feedings.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Laugardalslaug Geothermal Pool

Soak in Iceland's premier public pool complex featuring a dedicated, warm, shallow indoor baby wading pool and exceptionally clean, private family changing facilities.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM / 1h

Harpa Concert Hall

Wander through a striking glass architectural marvel with wide-open, stroller-friendly corridors, elevators, and beautiful harbor views that keep you completely shielded from arctic breezes.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Reykjavík Family Park and Zoo

Stroll through a quiet, paved coastal sanctuary where Mary Claire can safely see docile Icelandic horses, seals, and reindeer up close in a calm, uncrowded environment.
