Trade the Houston hustle for golf-cart-paced days on a sun-drenched barrier reef island where English is the official language and hospitality is deeply genuine. Ambergris Caye offers the perfect blend of shallow, calm turquoise waters and upscale resort amenities that make traveling with an infant entirely stress-free. From gentle docks to golf cart rides, your toddler will love the sensory warmth, while you enjoy top-tier seafood and breezy relaxation.
🚕 Getting there: Take a 15-minute Tropic Air flight from BZE to San Pedro (SPR), followed by a 10-to-20-minute open-air resort shuttle or golf cart ride directly to your hotel.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Hol Chan Marine Reserve Private Boat Charter

Book a private, shaded boat to explore the shallowest inner reef zones where your toddler can safely splash in lifejackets while looking at colorful fish. Private charters allow you to set your own schedule, ensuring you can head back the moment a naptime meltdown looms.

Morning to Early Afternoon / 4h

Secret Beach Splash Day

Located on the calm leeward side of the island, this area features crystal-clear, waist-deep water with absolutely no waves, making it a giant natural baby pool. Snag a shaded daybed at Sunset Palace Beach Club early in the day before the afternoon party crowd arrives.

Late Afternoon / 2h

The Truck Stop Shipping Container Food Park

This lively outdoor boardwalk food court offers plenty of space for a stroller and features an ice cream shop, outdoor games, and a sunset viewing deck. It's fully contained and family-friendly, allowing your toddler to stretch her legs while you grab a craft beer and wood-fired pizza.

Afternoon / 45m

Belize Chocolate Company Boutique

Stop in this air-conditioned sanctuary in the heart of San Pedro to sample organic, locally made single-origin chocolates. It's a quick, delicious sensory escape from the midday heat, and they offer toddler-approved chocolate milkshakes made with fresh local ingredients.

Morning / 1h

San Pedro House of Culture

This small, quiet community museum in town offers local history exhibits and photographic archives of Ambergris Caye. It's stroller-accessible and provides a quiet, cool, low-stimulation environment if your little one gets overwhelmed by the bustling streets.

Morning / 1-2h

San Pedro Artisans Market

Stroll this vibrant open-air market where local craftsmen sell colorful wooden toys, woven baskets, and art. The wide, flat walkways are easy to navigate with a sturdy stroller, and the bright colors will keep your toddler visually engaged.

Morning / 30m

Caye Coffee Roasting Co.

Pick up fresh, locally roasted beans at this fragrant facility where parents can get a premium caffeine fix. The staff is incredibly welcoming to families, and it's an easy, quick stop to make during your morning golf cart exploration.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Boca Del Rio Park Playground

This beachfront public park features a playground right on the sand with swings and climbing structures next to calm, shallow waters. It is an excellent spot to mingle with local families, and the constant sea breeze keeps the toddler comfortable.

Morning / 1.5h

GoFish Belize Family Dock Fishing

Arrange a gentle, dock-side fishing experience tailored for families where guides help even the youngest children watch colorful snapper swim up to the bait. The dock is stable and safe, offering an easy introduction to the marine world without the motion of a rocky boat.

🍽️ Where to eat

Elvi's Kitchen

Order the signature coconut curry shrimp; the dining floor is made entirely of soft white sand, which will keep your toddler happily playing with her beach toys right at your feet while you eat.

Caramba! Restaurant

Select your own fresh catch of the day from their ice display to be prepared Belizean-style; the bustling, high-energy atmosphere easily drowns out any toddler noise, and they open early at 5:00 PM.

Wild Mango's

Savor the absolute best ceviche on the island at this casual, open-air beachfront spot with plenty of space to park a stroller right next to your table. The soothing sound of the crashing waves keeps babies calm, and the service is exceptionally fast.

Estel's Dine By the Sea

Enjoy their legendary Mayan breakfast with fry jacks and eggs overlooking the water; the casual outdoor setup on the sand means you don't have to worry about spills, and the early 6:00 AM opening time is perfect for early-rising toddlers.

Blue Water Grill

Feast on wood-fired pizzas and fresh sushi at this beachfront restaurant inside SunBreeze Hotel, featuring a massive open deck and grass lawn. Toddlers can safely wander on the grass right next to your table under the watchful eye of the friendly staff.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Las Terrazas Resort

This luxury resort offers multi-bedroom townhomes with full kitchens, laundry facilities, and private plunge pools, which are absolute lifesavers for managing infant schedules. They provide high-quality wooden cribs and have a spectacular, quiet infinity pool.

The Phoenix Resort

Perfectly positioned in San Pedro town, this luxury resort features spacious two-bedroom suites, a dedicated kid’s playroom, and two beachside pools. They go above and beyond with baby-proofing kits, high chairs, and cribs delivered to your room.

Victoria House Resort & Spa

Located on a quiet, pristine stretch of white sand south of town, this luxury property offers manicured lawns, a flat stroller-friendly layout, and incredibly tranquil pools. The multi-room casitas provide the space and quiet necessary for seamless afternoon naps.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a 4-seater golf cart with a rear-facing seat and bring a portable, clip-on toddler safety harness, as this is the primary mode of transport on the island's bumpy cobblestone roads. While most resorts can procure cribs, packing a lightweight pop-up beach tent is essential for providing shade during beach days at Secret Beach. Stock up on diapers, wipes, and organic baby pouches at Super Buy South or Lino's Distribution Center in San Pedro, as smaller convenience shops have very limited, expensive selections.
