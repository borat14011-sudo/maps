Ambergris Caye

← back to travel board
Where the barrier reef meets sandy shores, Ambergris Caye invites families into a laid-back Caribbean paradise. Gentle turquoise waters lap against wooden docks, while golf carts zip along the vibrant streets of San Pedro. It is a tropical playground of shallow coral gardens, friendly marine life, and sweet treats perfect for little explorers.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 min golf cart ride or taxi from San Pedro Airport to most town hotels

What to do

Morning / 3h

Hol Chan Marine Reserve

Snorkel the vibrant coral gardens teeming with colorful fish and gentle sea turtles. Toddlers can view the marine life from the safety of a glass-bottom boat or float with a life vest.

Morning / 2h

Shark Ray Alley

Watch nurse sharks and southern stingrays glide through the crystal-clear shallow water right next to your boat. Toddlers will love peering over the boat's edge to see the friendly marine life.

Afternoon / 4h

Secret Beach

Enjoy the calm, wave-free shallow waters and soft sand on the island's leeward side. It's the ultimate toddler wading pool with shaded daybeds and overwater tables.

Afternoon / 1h

Belize Chocolate Company

Learn how local cacao beans are transformed into delicious chocolates and enjoy a family tasting. Kids will love the chocolate milkshakes and chocolate-dipped fruit.

Late Afternoon / 1h

San Pedro Artisan Market

Stroll through vibrant stalls filled with colorful wooden carvings, beaded jewelry, and handwoven crafts. The open-air setup is stroller-friendly and perfect for a casual family walk.

Evening / 2-3h

The Truck Stop

This shipping container food park offers diverse eats, a swimming pool, and outdoor movie nights. Toddlers can play garden games on the lawn while parents relax by the lagoon.

Morning / 4h

Bacalar Chico National Park

Explore the remote northern end of the island, where Mayan history meets coastal mangroves and wildlife. Toddlers can spot colorful birds and iguanas during an easy, guided boat tour.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Boca Del Rio Park

Play on the beachfront playground where kids can swing and climb with views of the barrier reef. The shallow, protected swimming area next to the park is ideal for little ones to splash safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Marco Gonzalez Maya Site

Walk the wooden boardwalks through the jungle to discover an ancient Maya trading outpost. It's a shaded, easy walk where toddlers can spot hermit crabs and exotic birds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Elvi's Kitchen

Order the famous Mayan-style fish cooked in banana leaves while sitting on the unique sand floor that toddlers love to play in.

Estel's Dine By the Sea

Try the hearty Mayan breakfast featuring freshly made fry jacks, eggs, and refried beans right on the beach.

Caramba! Restaurant

Choose your own fresh catch of the day from the ice display and have it prepared coconut-style for a sweet, kid-friendly flavor.

Lily's Treasure Chest

Feast on the freshest conch ceviche on the island while sitting on a beachside deck steps from the water.

Robin's Kitchen

Grab a plate of perfectly seasoned, smoky jerk chicken with coconut rice and peas from this beloved, casual roadside spot.
