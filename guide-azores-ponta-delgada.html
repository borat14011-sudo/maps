🚕 Getting there: ~10 min drive via airport taxi or pre-booked rental car directly to downtown Ponta Delgada hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Terra Nostra

Explore 30 acres of stunning botanical gardens filled with exotic plants and hidden pathways. The flat, gravel paths are highly stroller-accessible, though you should bring a carrier if you want to walk right up to the famous orange-tinted thermal pool.

Morning / 1-2h

Vista do Rei Viewpoint (Lagoa das Sete Cidades)

Marvel at the iconic blue and green twin crater lakes from this easily accessible clifftop lookout. There is paved, flat parking right at the viewpoint, making it incredibly simple to roll a stroller out for family photos without any strenuous hiking.

Morning / 1h

Mercado da Graça

Immerse your family in local culture at this vibrant farmers' market packed with fresh pineapples, local cheeses, and colorful flowers. The wide, covered concrete aisles are completely stroller-friendly and offer a cool, sensory morning walk for a curious toddler.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardim António Borges

Walk through a romantic 19th-century garden featuring majestic banyan trees, stone bridges, and mystical volcanic caves. It is a peaceful, shady oasis right in the city with paved paths perfect for stroller naps and soft grass areas for crawling.

Afternoon / 1h

Ananases A Arruda (Pineapple Plantation)

Visit the world's only pineapple plantation grown in glass greenhouses and taste their famous artisanal pineapple liqueur. The dirt paths between the historical greenhouses are flat and easy to navigate with a baby carrier or compact stroller.

Morning / 1-2h

Gorreana Tea Plantation (Chá Gorreana)

Stroll through Europe's oldest family-run tea plantation, featuring rolling green hills overlooking the ocean. The indoor factory museum has flat ground floors, and the outdoor grassy terraces are perfect for letting a toddler stretch her legs.

Morning / 1h

Portas da Cidade and Harbor Walk

Stroll past Ponta Delgada's iconic 18th-century city gates and along the wide, paved marina promenade. It is completely flat, traffic-free, and lined with cafes, offering a perfect, breezy route for an early morning stroller walk.

Afternoon / 2h

Monumento Natural da Caldeira Velha

Experience a lush, jungle-like thermal park with warm iron-rich waterfalls and exotic giant ferns. Sturdy wooden boardwalks make navigating the lower hot springs a breeze with a baby carrier, keeping you safe from slippery steps.

Afternoon / 1h

Ermida de Nossa Senhora da Paz

Take in the most breathtaking panoramic view of Vila Franca do Campo and its offshore volcanic islet from this historic hilltop chapel. You can drive and park directly at the top level, allowing you to enjoy the spectacular view without climbing the steep stairs with a toddler.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Associação Agrícola de São Miguel

Order the legendary Bife à Associação, a massive local tenderloin steak cooked with garlic and red pepper. The expansive, airy dining room has plenty of space between tables for strollers, high chairs, and quick escape routes if the baby gets fussy.

A Tasca

Try the melt-in-your-mouth sesame-crusted tuna steak and local octopus. Because it gets incredibly busy, arrive right at their early 6:30 PM opening time to easily secure a table with a baby before the crowds descend.

Restaurante Bar Caloura

Feast on the freshest daily catch of grilled fish right on the ocean's edge in a secluded cove. The spacious outdoor patio is ideal for families because the soothing sound of crashing waves easily masks any toddler noise.

Tasquinha Vieira

Savor creative, farm-to-table Azorean dishes like slow-cooked pork belly and fresh local cheese in a refined yet cozy setting. Booking an early 7:00 PM reservation ensures a quiet, relaxed atmosphere before the late-night local rush.

Restaurante Aliança

Enjoy a traditional, hearty Bife à Regional steak in a classic, family-friendly setting. The staff is exceptionally welcoming to children, and the spacious layout makes navigating with a baby highly stress-free.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Octant Ponta Delgada

This sleek, modern hotel overlooking the marina features spacious family suites and a stunning rooftop pool. They provide high-end wooden cribs, baby-friendly amenities, and a stellar breakfast buffet that caters perfectly to early risers.

Grand Hotel Açores Atlântico

A luxurious 5-star hotel located right on the flat, stroller-friendly main waterfront avenue. It offers spacious rooms, exceptional soundproofing, and a heated indoor swimming pool ideal for a relaxing swim with an infant.

Pedras do Mar Resort & SPA

Located on the quiet northern coast, this resort offers sweeping ocean views, large grassy lawns for outdoor play, and expansive family rooms. The property is exceptionally peaceful and features both indoor and outdoor pools, perfect for a relaxed family retreat.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rental cars are essential for exploring the island with a toddler, so be sure to book a mid-sized SUV early to guarantee space for a stroller and a pre-installed infant car seat. The weather in the Azores is famously unpredictable, changing from sun to rain in minutes, so always pack a high-quality rain cover for your stroller and dress the baby in versatile layers. Azoreans are incredibly warm and family-oriented, meaning you will find high chairs, diaper-changing facilities, and welcoming smiles almost everywhere you go.
