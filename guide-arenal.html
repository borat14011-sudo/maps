Arenal and La Fortuna offer a dramatic landscape where emerald rainforests meet the iconic, cone-shaped Arenal Volcano. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, this tropical playground delivers high-end comfort amidst untamed nature. The region features private thermal springs, lush resort grounds, and unexpectedly smooth, stroller-friendly pathways in select ecological reserves. It is the ultimate destination for active luxury travelers seeking an exotic escape without sacrificing infant safety and convenience.

What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Mistico Arenal Hanging Bridges Park

Experience the rainforest canopy safely with Mary Claire in a high-end carrier. The park offers a dedicated 1.5-mile accessible trail option that is entirely paved and barrier-free, making it perfect for strollers and offering spectacular, shaded views of the volcano. Early morning slots avoid the heat and crowds.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Arenal Volcano National Park (Sector Peninsula)

This specific sector of the national park features exceptionally smooth, paved panoramic trails and wooden boardwalks that are completely stroller-friendly. You will walk through lush secondary forest right to the edge of Lake Arenal. The quiet, shaded path is ideal for a peaceful mid-morning stroll while Mary Claire naps.

Afternoon 1-4pm / 3h

EcoTermales Fortuna

The most elegant and tranquil of the region's hot springs, EcoTermales limits entry to ensure a peaceful atmosphere. Rent a private pool-side cabana so Kathryn and Will can trade off soaking in the mineral-rich, naturally heated pools while baby rests in the shade. The setting is intimate, lush, and free of rowdy crowds.

Morning 8-9:30am / 1.5h

Bogarin Trail

A flat, completely flat and beautifully manicured trail famous for its wild sloth sightings. Take a private guided tour with an expert naturalist who will spot sleepy sloths, colorful toucans, and tiny frogs for you. The path is highly shaded and easy to navigate with a rugged stroller or a baby carrier.

Morning 9am-12pm / 3h

Peñas Blancas River Safari Float

Drift peacefully down a calm river on a private raft guided by a professional oarsman. There are no rapids on this gentle safari stretch, making it entirely safe and incredibly serene for an infant in a lap or carrier. Watch monkeys and river turtles slide by in absolute tranquility.

Late afternoon 4-5pm / 1h

La Fortuna Town Square (Parque de La Fortuna)

This beautifully manicured public park features wide, paved, stroller-ready concrete paths and a gorgeous central garden with a direct view of the volcano. Stroll past colorful tropical flowers and the historic church. It is the perfect spot for a relaxed afternoon walk with easy access to nearby upscale boutiques.

Morning 9:30-11am / 1.5h

Butterfly Conservatory in El Castillo

Located a short drive from the volcano, this peaceful conservatory boasts the largest butterfly exhibit in Costa Rica. The shaded, gently sloping paths wind through lush dome habitats that will mesmerize Mary Claire with vibrant colors. The serene atmosphere is highly conducive to a quiet, leisurely pace.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Proyecto Asis Wildlife Sanctuary

Book a private educational tour of this premier animal rescue center. You will get up close with monkeys, sloths, and macaws along flat, well-shaded gravel paths. The private guide tailors the pace to Mary Claire’s feeding schedule, ensuring a highly personalized and quiet experience.

Morning 8:30-9:15am / 45m

La Fortuna Waterfall Overlook

While the 500-step descent to the base of the waterfall is too strenuous for a baby carrier, the paved upper observation deck is easily accessible. Enjoy stunning, postcard-perfect views of the 230-foot cascade plunging into the canyon. You can capture magnificent family photos here without breaking a sweat.

🍽️ Where to eat

Don Rufino

The undisputed fine-dining crown jewel of La Fortuna, serving exquisite artisanal Costa Rican-fusion cuisine in a chic, contemporary setting. Book an early 5:30 PM reservation to secure a spacious booth with ample room for a highchair. The staff is famously accommodating to discerning families.

Restaurante Café Mediterráneo

Perched on a hill with breathtaking views of the volcano, this upscale eatery serves fantastic wood-fired pizzas and fresh pastas. Their airy, covered outdoor terrace offers a refreshing breeze and plenty of space to park a stroller. The atmosphere is upscale yet delightfully welcoming to young families.

La Choza de Laurel

An open-air, high-end traditional restaurant featuring wood-fired Costa Rican classics and freshly baked breads. The spacious, high-ceilinged rancho design provides excellent natural ventilation, while the lively acoustic music easily masks any infant vocalizations. Highchairs are readily available.

Chante Verde

A highly rated, health-conscious restaurant nestled in a lush garden setting that serves creative, organic dishes. The spacious outdoor seating allows baby Mary Claire to look at tropical flowers while you dine. The atmosphere is deeply relaxing, quiet, and perfect for an unhurried family lunch.

Mercadito Arenal

An upscale, trendy open-air food market offering a sophisticated collection of artisanal dining stalls. Its wide, flat layout is exceptionally stroller-friendly, allowing you to easily browse premium local culinary options. The casual, vibrant atmosphere is ideal for a stress-free lunch with a baby.
