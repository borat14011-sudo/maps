Grenada’s lush hills and cinnamon-scented breeze wrap you in a gentle Caribbean embrace. Grand Anse Beach’s two-mile arc of powdery sand is stroller-ready and lapped by calm, shallow waters perfect for infant wading. The island’s spice plantations, waterfalls, and fort-capped hills offer shaded escapes from the midday sun. At luxury resorts like Calabash, babies get welcome kits, cribs, and a dedicated bottle-warming service.
🚕 Getting there: 15-minute private transfer from Maurice Bishop International Airport to Grand Anse hotel strip; request a car seat with infant base for seamless arrival with stroller.

What to do

Morning 8-10am / 2h

Grand Anse Beach

Two miles of calm, shallow turquoise water with soft sand — ideal for infant paddling under an umbrella. Stroller-friendly beachside path and plenty of shaded palms.

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Belmont Estate

Family-run cocoa plantation offering guided tours of the chocolate-making process. Shaded veranda for tasting, and the gentle hum of machinery is toddler-soothing. Stroller accessible on paved paths.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Fort George

Historic fort with panoramic views of St. George’s harbour and the Carenage. Wide ramparts are stroller-friendly; the breeze keeps it cool. Skip cannon firings if baby is noise-sensitive.

Mid-morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Annandale Falls

A 30-foot cascade set in a lush garden with paved viewing platform and steps (stroller can park above). Calm, shaded spot for a picnic; the spray is refreshing.

Morning 8-10am / 2h

Grand Etang National Park

Crater lake surrounded by rainforest with a flat, stroller-accessible boardwalk and visitor centre. Keep baby shaded; the cool mountain air is a welcome break from beach heat.

Midday 11am-12pm / 1h

Gouyave Nutmeg Processing Station

See nutmegs sorted, dried, and graded in a large, airy warehouse. The aromatic spice scent is gentle; the process is quiet enough for napping babes. No stairs, easy stroller entry.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Underwater Sculpture Park

Snorkel or take a glass-bottom boat tour over 65 eerie sculptures in clear, calm water. Perfect infant activity: sit in the boat, watch fish, stay dry. Boat operators have life vests for babies.

Early morning 7-9am / 2h

St. George's Market

Vibrant open-air market under a large roof, with stalls of spices, fruits, and crafts. Go early to avoid crowds and heat; stroller navigates the perimeter easily. Avoid loud blender stands.

Late afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Levera National Park

Remote beach and lagoon with calm swimming and a flat trail through mangroves. Ideal for a quiet family picnic; watch for nesting turtles (seasonal). Stroller-friendly sandy path.

🍽️ Where to eat

Rhodes Restaurant

Fine dining at Calabash Resort with a la carte dishes like grilled lobster and rum-braised pork. Highchairs available, outdoor terrace with sea breeze, and early seating at 6pm accommodates infant bedtimes. Book ahead for a quiet corner.

The Beach House

Beachfront grill on Grand Anse serving fresh fish, burgers, and signature rum punch. Casual sand-floor dining; highchair and kids' menu. Sunset early seating is perfect — baby can play in the sand while adults eat.

Savvy's

French-Caribbean gem tucked in a historic St. George's building. Dishes like curried shrimp and chocolate mousse. Quiet indoor dining with air conditioning; highchairs and space for a stroller. Early reservations recommended.

Oliver's

Poolside restaurant at Mount Cinnamon Resort, open to the public. Caribbean buffet and a la carte with jerk chicken and callaloo. Shaded terrace, highchair, and gentle reggae — baby-safe noise level.

La Belle Créole

Spice Island Beach Resort's main restaurant, serving Creole and international cuisine in an elegant beachfront setting. Early dinner at 6pm, highchair, and stroller access. The soothing waves drown out any fussiness.

Grenada’s main pharmacy, HUB Pharmacy in Grand Anse, carries formula, diapers, and baby paracetamol; smaller groceries sell local baby food. Most hotel areas along Grand Anse have paved sidewalks suitable for a stroller, but be prepared for uneven paths near fort ruins — a baby carrier is recommended for those outings.
