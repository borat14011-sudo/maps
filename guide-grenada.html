Spice up your family adventure in tropical Grenada.

Grenada

← back to travel board
Known as the Spice Isle, Grenada wraps families in the sweet scents of nutmeg, cocoa, and salty sea breezes. From the calm, sapphire waters of Grand Anse Beach to lush rainforests filled with gentle monkeys, this Caribbean gem is both slow-paced and highly adventurous. It is a safe, welcoming paradise where toddlers can splash in gentle shallows while parents unwind under the palms.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 min taxi from Maurice Bishop International Airport to the beach hotels in Grand Anse

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Grand Anse Beach

Two miles of powdery white sand and incredibly calm, shallow waters make this the ultimate playground for little swimmers. Toddlers will love building sandcastles under the shade of sea grape trees while gentle waves lap the shore.

Morning / 1-2h

Annandale Waterfall

A highly accessible, paved path leads you to this lush waterfall oasis surrounded by wild ferns. It is entirely stroller-friendly, making it the easiest waterfall in Grenada to visit with a toddler in tow.

Afternoon / 1h

House of Chocolate

A mini-museum and sweet cafe in the heart of town celebrating Grenada's rich cocoa history. Toddlers will enjoy the homemade chocolate ice cream while parents sample hand-crafted artisanal truffles.

Morning / 2h

Grand Etang National Park

Walk the easy, flat trails around the volcanic crater lake to spot the friendly wild Mona monkeys in the canopy. Keep toddlers secure in a carrier as you watch these playful creatures swing from the trees.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Market Square

Immerse your senses in a colorful, bustling open-air market filled with mounds of fresh tropical fruits and aromatic spices. Toddlers will be captivated by the vibrant sights and the sweet taste of fresh, locally grown bananas.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Belmont Estate

This historic 17th-century working plantation offers a goat dairy, cocoa processing tours, and beautiful gardens. Toddlers will love meeting the farm animals, petting the goats, and seeing how chocolate is made.

Afternoon / 3h

La Sagesse Nature Center

A quiet, crescent-shaped cove that feels like a hidden treasure hunt with its gentle waves and mangrove estuary. It is incredibly peaceful and safe for toddlers to splash safely far away from any crowds.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Fort George

Climb up to this historic 1700s fort for panoramic views of the colorful harbor below. Skip the stroller here due to steep stone steps, and use a baby carrier to explore the old cannons safely with your little ones.

Morning / 3h

BBC Beach (Morne Rouge Beach)

Even calmer and quieter than Grand Anse, this sheltered horseshoe bay has almost no waves, making it ideal for toddlers. Relax under the shade of almond trees and watch the small sailboats bob in the distance.

🍽️ Where to eat

Coconut Beach Restaurant

Sit right on the sand of Grand Anse Beach and order the local callaloo soup and fresh lobster while your kids play safely in the sand next to your table.

Dodgy Dock

This vibrant, open-air waterfront eatery features a highly family-friendly atmosphere and delicious street-food-style fish tacos.

The Beach Club at Calabash

Enjoy a relaxed luxury lunch directly on L'Anse aux Epines beach, featuring their famous crab back dish and a fantastic children's menu.

Container Park

An incredibly casual, fun cluster of food stalls built from shipping containers where you can grab quick wood-fired pizzas that toddlers will love.

Patrick's Local Homestyle Restaurant

Taste a variety of authentic Grenadian tapas-style dishes like oil down, the national dish, in a warm and welcoming cottage setting.
