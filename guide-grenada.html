Grenada

← back to travel board
Grenada offers an effortlessly upscale, safe, and uncrowded escape that avoids the hectic mega-resort feel of neighboring islands. The southwest coast boasts some of the calmest, shallowest waters in the Caribbean, making it perfect for a toddler's first dip. For a finance dad from Houston, it delivers high-end hospitality, rich sensory experiences, and a refreshing lack of pretense that makes traveling with an infant remarkably stress-free.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 minute private taxi from Maurice Bishop International Airport (GND) to the main luxury resorts on Grand Anse Beach.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Grand Anse Beach

This world-famous two-mile stretch of sheltered, powdery white sand offers exceptionally calm waters and plenty of shade under natural sea grape trees. Skip the stroller here and use a baby carrier, as the sand is deep, but the gentle shoreline is perfect for dipping tiny toddler toes into the warm Caribbean Sea.

Afternoon / 1h

House of Chocolate Grenada

This charming, air-conditioned boutique museum in the heart of St. George's offers an educational look at the island's organic cocoa production. It is a fantastic cool-down spot to escape the midday heat, and they serve toddler-friendly, dairy-free cocoa sorbets that make for a mess-free treat.

Morning / 1-2h

Grand Etang National Park Visitor Centre

Located in the lush rainforest interior, this accessible spot allows you to experience Grenada's dramatic jungle scenery without a strenuous hike. The paved viewing platform and boardwalk near the visitor center are entirely stroller-accessible and offer a safe vantage point to spot the friendly resident Mona monkeys.

Morning / 3-4h

Belmont Estate

This fully functioning 17th-century organic cocoa and spice plantation offers a gorgeous, educational look at traditional farm-to-bar chocolate making. Kids will love the petting farm filled with goats and tortoises, though parents should opt for a baby carrier over a stroller due to the historic estate's uneven gravel pathways.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Fort Frederick

This historic hilltop fort offers some of the most dramatic, 360-degree panoramic views of St. George's harbor and the coastline below. Unlike Fort George, you can drive your rental car or taxi right up to the entrance, eliminating steep climbs and making it highly manageable with a toddler in a carrier.

Morning / 1h

Grand Anse Craft and Spice Market

This open-air beachside market features local vendors selling spices, crafts, and tropical fruits in a low-pressure environment. The flat, paved concrete walkways are easy to navigate with a stroller, and the vibrant colors and scents of fresh nutmeg and cinnamon provide wonderful sensory stimulation for a baby.

Morning / 1-2h

Laura Herb and Spice Garden

A beautifully manicured botanical sanctuary featuring a wide variety of Grenada's indigenous herbs, spices, and medicinal plants. The flat, shaded walking paths are covered in soft, crushed cocoa shells and are easy to traverse with a heavy-duty stroller or baby carrier during a quiet morning walk.

Morning / 1h

Annandale Waterfall & Forest Garden

This is by far the most accessible waterfall on the island, featuring a lush, paved pathway that leads directly from the entrance to the waterfall's pool. You can easily stroll right up to the misty viewing area with a stroller, making it a stress-free way to show your toddler a stunning tropical cascade.

Afternoon / 2h

Jessamine Eden Tropical Botanical Garden

Spanning over 6 acres of pristine rainforest valleys, this peaceful garden features therapeutic mineral springs and beautiful hummingbird-filled flora. The gentle, flat grassy paths are highly stroller-friendly, and the serene, quiet atmosphere is absolutely ideal for getting your infant to take an on-the-go contact nap.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sails Restaurant & Bar

Order the ginger-infused grilled snapper at this open-air restaurant directly overlooking the historic Carenage harbor. The constant ocean breezes, spacious seating, and entertaining views of passing yachts will keep your toddler thoroughly distracted.

Umbrellas Beach Bar

Order the Carib Beer battered fish bites at this vibrant, casual spot located directly on Grand Anse Beach. The lively family atmosphere means minor toddler noise goes completely unnoticed, and you can step right onto the soft sand if your baby gets fussy.

Coconut Beach Restaurant

Order the French-Creole style lobster creole at this elegant yet laid-back beachfront establishment. With widely spaced outdoor tables set directly on the sand and an early 6:00 PM dinner opening, it perfectly accommodates toddler bedtimes.

Aquarium Restaurant

Order the callaloo cannelloni at this stunning oceanfront restaurant located on Magazine Beach. The restaurant features a massive wooden deck with plenty of room for strollers, plus a flat, grassy lawn adjacent to the tables where a toddler can safely crawl and play.

Esther's Bar

Order a freshly blended virgin mango-coconut smoothie for the family at this legendary, casual open-air beach shack. It offers plastic beach-chair seating under shaded palms, allowing you to relax with a drink while your toddler plays in the sand right beside you.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Spice Island Beach Resort

This premier luxury all-inclusive sits directly on the sands of Grand Anse Beach and provides complimentary wooden cribs, high chairs, and incredibly spacious beachfront suites where parents can lounge on the private patio during afternoon baby nap times.

Mount Cinnamon Resort

Featuring highly spacious multi-bedroom villas with full kitchen facilities, this luxury hillside resort makes preparing baby bottles and toddler meals effortless. Their private beach club offers a flat grassy lawn, a shallow pool, and exceptional beachside service.

Royalton Grenada, An Autograph Collection All-Inclusive Resort

Located on a calm cove in Point Salines, this modern resort features fully paved, elevator-accessible pathways that are highly stroller-friendly. It boasts a dedicated, shallow toddler pool and splash area that is perfect for safe, low-stress water play with an infant.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack more diapers, swim pants, and your preferred brand of baby sunscreen than you think you will need, as premium organic baby brands are highly marked up and difficult to find in local pharmacies like Gittens. While Grenada's coastal roads are well-paved, the island's interior is incredibly steep and winding, so bring a high-quality, rear-facing car seat and prepare for potential toddler motion sickness during rainforest day trips. Book a dedicated private taxi driver for your entire stay through your hotel concierge to ensure you have a reliable, air-conditioned vehicle that easily accommodates your stroller and strict nap schedules.
