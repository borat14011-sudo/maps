Aguascalientes

← back to travel board
Aguascalientes offers a highly clean, walkable, and secure escape that feels remarkably easy to navigate with a toddler. The city boasts beautifully manicured gardens, wide pedestrian plazas, and a temperate climate that keeps outdoor activities pleasant all day. It is an ideal destination for a young family seeking authentic Mexican culture without the frantic pace or logistical hurdles of larger metropolitan hubs.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-30 minute ride via authorized airport taxi booked at the terminal arrival booth directly to downtown or northern district hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Tres Centurias

This historic railway plaza features beautiful old locomotives and outstanding dancing fountains that toddlers find mesmerizing. The entire complex is completely flat and paved, making it an absolute breeze to navigate with a heavy-duty stroller.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardín de San Marcos

A landmark gated park featuring elegant stone archways, mature shade trees, and friendly squirrels that your toddler will love watching. The wide, flat brick pathways are perfect for a peaceful stroller walk away from city traffic.

Morning / 2-3h

Museo Descubre

An interactive science and technology museum that features a dedicated 'Sala Infantil' play space perfect for early childhood development. The outdoor garden paths are wide and flat, offering a safe, low-stress environment for a toddler to stretch her legs.

Morning / 3h

Parque Rodolfo Landeros Gallegos

This massive public park features an artificial lake, aviaries, and a mini-zoo with safely fenced animals that captivate young kids. Rent a family pedal-cart or simply push the stroller along miles of paved, tree-lined walking tracks.

Morning / 2h

Isla San Marcos

A massive, beautifully landscaped family park home to the world's largest mechanical walk-on clock and peaceful artificial lakes. Outside of the annual spring fair dates, it is a serene oasis with wide, unobstructed concrete walkways ideal for strollers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Patria and Catedral Basilica

The central pedestrian plaza of the city is completely car-free, allowing your toddler to safely toddle around the open stone plaza. The stunning double-towered cathedral provides a beautiful backdrop, and local vendors nearby sell colorful balloons to keep little ones happy.

Morning / 1-2h

Baños Termales de Ojocaliente

Historic 19th-century thermal baths where you can rent a private family room with a deep stone tub filled with warm, mineral-rich spring water. It is a fantastic, highly controlled environment for an infant to splash around safely without the crowds of a public pool.

Morning / 1h

Museo José Guadalupe Posada

A quiet, culturally rich museum located in the historic Barrio del Encino next to a peaceful, tree-lined plaza. The museum interior is single-level and stroller-friendly, allowing you to take in the iconic printmaking art while your baby naps in her stroller.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Terán

A vibrant traditional market filled with colorful displays of local fruits, cheese, and crafts. While it is a sensory delight, the aisles can be tight and bustling, so ditch the stroller and use a chest baby carrier for this experience.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Saturnina

Order the traditional 'condoches' or the classic 'enchiladas tres centurias' in this vibrant, plant-filled colonial courtyard. The spacious outdoor seating easily accommodates high chairs, and the lively morning atmosphere naturally masks toddler chatter.

Restaurante La Estación

Housed in a beautiful, historic railway-themed building, this spot is famous for premium arrachera steaks and high-end Mexican fare. The cavernous dining room with high ceilings offers plenty of breathing room between tables for strollers and diaper bags.

El Carnes Toluca

Go-to spot for incredibly tender, quick-service slow-cooked meats and tacos that keep meal times short and stress-free. The casual, family-oriented environment means nobody will bat an eye if your toddler drops a toy on the floor.

Merendero San Marcos

A classic, highly energetic Mexican family diner right near the famous garden, famous for its hearty 'chamorro' (pork shank) and small plates. The open-air setup and bustling ambient noise level make it the perfect shield for an active, vocal infant.

Cenaduría Estela

An iconic spot to experience a traditional Mexican evening supper, opening early enough to beat late-night toddler meltdowns. Order the comforting pozole or crispy flautas in a warm, welcoming neighborhood environment that treats kids like royalty.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Quinta Real Aguascalientes

An exquisite, upscale hotel featuring lush, meticulously manicured gardens and a brilliant outdoor pool. The massive, quiet suites provide plenty of space for a travel crib, and the tranquil property layout ensures a peaceful night's sleep for the baby.

Aguascalientes Marriott Hotel

Located in the upscale northern district, this hotel offers predictable, high-end comfort with modern cribs on request, an outdoor heated pool, and incredibly spacious rooms. Its proximity to premium shopping plazas makes midday supply runs for diapers or milk effortless.

Fiesta Americana Aguascalientes

Boasting beautiful colonial-style architecture near the San Marcos area, this hotel features excellent family amenities including a heated pool and pristine gardens. The staff is exceptionally accommodating with infant requests, ensuring prompt delivery of cribs and bottle-warming water.
