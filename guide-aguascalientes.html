🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or private transfer from Lic. Jesús Terán Peredo International Airport (AGU) to downtown hotels.

What to do

Afternoon / 2h

Parque Tres Centurias

Walk through historic railway history with vintage trains, dancing fountains, and plenty of open space. Toddlers will love chasing the water jets and climbing on the old locomotive steps.

Morning / 3h

Museo Descubre

An interactive science and technology museum featuring an IMAX screen, planetarium, and hands-on exhibits. There is a dedicated early-childhood play area perfect for curious toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Baños Termales de Ojocaliente

Relax in historic, warm thermal pools that have been used since the 19th century. Rent a private family room where toddlers can safely splash in the soothing, shallow waters.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de la Patria and Cathedral

The central heart of the city, perfect for a gentle stroll past the beautiful cathedral and government palace murals. Toddlers will enjoy feeding the pigeons and eating ice cream on the wide, car-free plaza.

Morning / 3h

Parque Rodolfo Landeros Gallegos

This massive green park features a mini-zoo, a boating lake, and sprawling playgrounds. Rent a family quad-bike to ride around, or let your toddler run wild on the grassy fields.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Juárez

A bustling traditional market filled with local crafts, clothing, and delicious street food. It is great for sensory stimulation, but keep toddlers close or in a carrier during busy peak hours.

Afternoon / 2h

Isla San Marcos

Home to the world's largest mechanical clock and beautiful walking paths, this peaceful park is perfect for a family day out. Toddlers can enjoy pony rides and feed the ducks by the artificial lake.

Morning / 1.5h

Museo Nacional de la Muerte

A surprisingly colorful and whimsical museum exploring Mexico's relationship with death through art and toys. While older kids will find the skeleton toys fascinating, toddlers will be captivated by the bright colors.

Morning / 2h

Parque El Sabinal

A peaceful nature reserve filled with ancient, giant cypress trees and gentle walking paths. It is a serene spot where toddlers can safely touch nature and spot local birds.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Estación

Order the traditional tacos de arrachera and let the kids marvel at the historic, train-station-themed decor.

Merendero San Marcos

Try the classic enchiladas hidrocálidas in a festive, family-friendly cantina atmosphere with prompt service.

Cenaduría Esthela

Feast on crispy pozole and traditional flautas that are mild enough for young kids to enjoy.

Rincón Maya

Taste the authentic cochinita pibil tacos served in a relaxed, colorful setting that welcomes noisy toddlers.

El Carnes

Dig into savory carne asada plates and delicious cheese quesadillas that make for an easy toddler win.

👨‍👩‍👧 Aguascalientes is highly walkable, but historic cobblestones can make strollers bumpy, so a sturdy baby carrier is highly recommended. Always keep small change handy for public restrooms and street vendors selling fresh fruit cups.
