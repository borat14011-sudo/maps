Santorini

← back to travel board
Santorini captivates with its dramatic caldera views, whitewashed villages, and surprisingly family-friendly volcanic beaches. While its steep steps require some planning, the island offers magical boat tours, gentle black-sand shores, and warm Greek hospitality that welcomes little ones with open arms.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min private shuttle or taxi to most beach hotels in Kamari or Perissa, or ~15 min to Fira.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Kamari Black Sand Beach

A spacious beach with calm waters and a flat pedestrian promenade perfect for strolls. Toddlers will love playing in the unique dark sand, but make sure they wear water shoes as the black pebbles get very hot in the sun.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Oia Castle Sunset Walk

The most iconic spot on the island to watch the sun drop into the Aegean Sea. Go early to secure a safe spot along the wall and wear a toddler carrier since strollers are impossible on the crowded, steep stone steps.

Morning / 3h

Santorini Volcano Boat Tour to Nea Kameni

An exciting pirate-style boat ride across the caldera to the volcanic islands. Toddlers will love the boat journey itself, though parents should opt to stay near the shoreline rather than pushing a stroller up the steep volcanic crater hike.

Morning / 1-2h

Tomato Industrial Museum in Vlychada

A unique, interactive museum housed in an old factory that showcases the island's tomato paste history. The wide, flat courtyard and quirky old machinery offer plenty of space for toddlers to safely wander and explore.

Midday / 1-2h

Akrotiri Archaeological Site

An ancient Minoan city preserved in volcanic ash under a modern, climate-controlled bioclimatic canopy. The wide, flat wooden walkways are stroller-friendly and provide a shaded, cool escape for young children during midday heat.

Lunchtime / 2h

Amoudi Bay Exploration

A charming, tiny fishing port tucked directly under the red cliffs of Oia. Toddlers will love watching the bright red and blue fishing boats bobbing in the crystal-clear water while you enjoy a seaside stroll.

Afternoon / 3h

Lioyerma Lounge Pool Bar in Oia

A cliffside swimming pool with panoramic sunset views that is free to use if you purchase drinks or food. It is a fantastic, kid-friendly alternative to the crowded Oia streets where toddlers can splash safely while parents watch the sunset.

Morning / 2h

Fira to Imerovigli Caldera Path

A breathtaking pedestrian path that winds along the edge of the cliffs past beautiful churches. Stick to the flatter, paved stretches near Imerovigli, and use a baby carrier so you can easily navigate the occasional steps.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Perissa Beach Playground & Boardwalk

This side of the island features a long, stroller-friendly seaside path lined with cafes. Toddlers can burn off energy at the small public playground near the beach before you settle in for a relaxed family lunch.

🍽️ Where to eat

Metaxi Mas (Exo Gonia)

Ask for an outdoor terrace table and order the baked white eggplant with tomato and feta, along with their homemade Cretan pasta which is a massive hit with toddlers.

To Psaraki (Vlychada)

Overlooking the peaceful Vlychada marina, this relaxed taverna serves ultra-fresh grilled fish of the day and a smooth local fava bean dip that babies and toddlers love.

Lucky's Souvlakis (Fira)

A legendary, fuss-free spot to grab a quick, delicious pork or chicken gyro wrap loaded with fresh tzatziki and fries that are perfect for a fast toddler meal.

Anemoloos (Exo Gonia)

A highly family-friendly, spacious tavern where you should order the traditional Santorini tomato fritters (domatokeftedes) and tender grilled chicken skewers.

Lolita's Gelato (Oia)

Cool off after exploring Oia with a scoop of their incredibly creamy, handmade pistachio gelato or child-friendly intensive milk chocolate flavor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Leave your stroller at home and opt for a high-quality baby carrier instead, as Santorini's cliffside villages are packed with steep steps, narrow cobblestone paths, and uneven terrain. When booking your airport or ferry port transfer, reserve a private shuttle in advance and specifically request a car seat, as local taxis rarely carry them.
