Toronto

← back to travel board
Toronto welcomes families with a vibrant tapestry of soaring towers, sprawling green spaces, and diverse neighborhoods. From the breezy shores of the Toronto Islands to interactive indoor wonderlands, this multicultural metropolis blends big-city excitement with exceptionally welcoming, stroller-friendly charm.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

CN Tower

Peer down at the city from the sky-high observation deck, which features a thrilling glass floor that brave kids will love. Toddler note: Infants under three are free, and the elevators are fast, smooth, and fully stroller-accessible.

Morning / 2-3h

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada

Walk through the mesmerizing underwater tunnel beneath gliding sharks, green sea turtles, and glowing jellyfish. Toddler note: The dedicated Discovery Centre features a water play table and a slide that winds directly through a shark tank.

Afternoon / 4-5h

Toronto Islands and Centreville

Take a scenic ferry ride to an idyllic, car-free island complete with a vintage amusement park, sandy beaches, and swan boats. Toddler note: The antique carousel and miniature train ride are the perfect speed and size for toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

High Park and Jamie Bell Adventure Playground

Explore Toronto's premier park to find a massive, castle-themed wooden playground and a free, easy-to-walk llama zoo. Toddler note: Bring some seeds to feed the friendly ducks at Grenadier Pond, but watch out for bumpy stroller paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Riverdale Farm

Step into a historic, operating Victorian farm right in the heart of the city to meet pigs, sheep, goats, and cows. Toddler note: The paved pathways make it very easy to push a stroller, and there is a great wading pool nearby in the summer.

Late Morning / 1-2h

St. Lawrence Market

Immerse your senses in this historic food hall packed with fresh fruits, cheese stalls, and the city's famous peameal bacon sandwiches. Toddler note: It gets incredibly crowded on Saturdays, so visit on a weekday morning for room to navigate a stroller.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)

Gaze up at towering dinosaur skeletons and explore hands-on natural history galleries designed for curious young minds. Toddler note: Head directly to the second floor Discovery Gallery where toddlers can try on costumes and dig for fossils.

Morning / 4-5h

Toronto Zoo

Walk through global pavilions hosting over 5,000 animals, including majestic polar bears, gorillas, and giant pandas. Toddler note: Rent a wooden pull-cart at the entrance to keep tired toddlers entertained as you cover this massive, hilly park.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Scarborough Bluffs Park

Admire dramatic, towering white clay cliffs and enjoy a peaceful walk along the sandy shores of Lake Ontario. Toddler note: The park has wide, flat paved paths perfect for stroller walks and a great beach area for splashing in shallow water.

Where to eat

  • Carousel Bakery (Peameal Bacon Sandwich) — Try Toronto's signature peameal bacon sandwich inside St. Lawrence Market, served on a soft roll and perfect for sharing with hungry little ones.
  • Sweet Jesus — Indulge in soft-serve ice cream cones piled high with outrageously colorful toppings like cotton candy, cookies, and toasted marshmallows.
  • WVRST — Enjoy a noisy, casual beer-hall atmosphere where kids can munch on artisan hot dogs and share giant baskets of delicious duck fat fries.
  • Pizzeria Libretto — Feast on authentic, fast-cooking Neapolitan pizzas in a highly welcoming environment that gladly provides high chairs and quick service.
