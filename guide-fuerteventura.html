Fuerteventura

← back to travel board
Fuerteventura is a tranquil paradise of sprawling white-sand deserts, volcanic landscapes, and gentle shallow lagoons perfect for little feet. With its year-round spring weather and laid-back rhythm, it offers a stress-free escape where families can slow down and explore. From building sandcastles on golden shores to paddling in crystal-clear natural pools, it is an outdoor playground for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min rental car drive from El Matorral Airport (FUE) to Corralejo, or ~10 min taxi to Caleta de Fuste

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Corralejo Dunes National Park

An endless expanse of soft, golden sand dunes rolling right into the turquoise ocean, perfect for kids to safely climb and slide down. Toddlers will love playing in the sand away from the windy shoreline where the dunes provide natural shelter.

Morning / 2-3h

La Concha Beach Lagoons

A stunning beach in El Cotillo with naturally formed horseshoe reefs that block big waves, creating calm, shallow wading pools. It is the ultimate safe-swimming spot for toddlers to splash around without ocean currents.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Oasis Wildlife Fuerteventura

A massive interactive animal park and botanical garden featuring sea lion shows, camel rides, and giraffe feedings. The park is highly stroller-accessible and offers a free internal tractor-train for tired little legs.

Morning / 1h

Popcorn Beach

A fascinating shoreline near Corralejo covered in white algae fossils that look exactly like popcorn. Toddlers will be mesmerized sorting through the unique shapes, but keep a close eye to ensure none end up in tiny mouths.

Mid-day / 3-4h

Acua Water Park

The island's premier water park featuring fun slides, a lazy river, and wave pools. There is a beautifully designed, colorful splash area dedicated entirely to toddlers and infants with miniature slides.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Mercadillo de Lajares

A weekly craft market set in a trendy, bohemian village offering handmade toys, clothing, and local treats. It is a highly pedestrian-friendly, slow-paced atmosphere that is easy to navigate with a stroller.

Morning / 2h

Playa del Matorral and Promenade

A vast golden beach in Morro Jable bordered by a paved, flat promenade lined with shops and cafes. Toddlers will love spotting the wild, friendly green parakeets nesting in the palm trees along the walkway.

Morning / 1-2h

Betancuria Historic Village

Fuerteventura's ancient, picture-perfect capital nestled in a lush valley with whitewashed houses and bright flowers. The central square is completely pedestrianized, giving toddlers a safe, quiet space to wander free of cars.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Caleta de Fuste Beach

An incredibly sheltered, crescent-shaped bay boasting zero waves and warm, shallow water. It features a fantastic, modern children's playground right on the sand just steps away from family-friendly beach bars.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Tapita

Order the classic papas arrugadas (wrinkly Canarian potatoes with mild mojo sauce) which are soft, salty, and a massive hit with picky toddlers.

La Vaca Azul

Sit on the ocean-facing terrace in El Cotillo and order the fresh grilled catch of the day alongside a simple plate of buttered rice for the kids.

Restaurante El Horno

A cozy rustic tavern in Villaverde where you should order the tender, slow-roasted kid goat or a simple, kid-friendly grilled chicken breast.

La Giara

An incredibly welcoming Italian spot in Caleta de Fuste; order the homemade thin-crust Margherita pizza and fresh ravioli for a guaranteed family crowd-pleaser.

El Goloso

Stop by this legendary bakery in Lajares to grab freshly baked butter croissants and sweet fruit tarts for an easy, delicious family breakfast on the go.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat directly from the airport, as driving across the island's empty, volcanic roads is stress-free and essential for reaching remote beaches. Always pack a pop-up wind tent to shield your toddler from Fuerteventura's signature breezes, which can easily blow fine sand into sensitive little eyes.
