Fuerteventura

← back to travel board
Fuerteventura offers a dramatic, volcanic landscape paired with some of Europe's safest, shallowest white-sand lagoons, making it a dream for a family with a toddler. The island's laid-back, unpretentious vibe ensures you never feel out of place with a noisy stroller or diaper bag in tow. With short driving distances between rugged coastlines and sleepy inland villages, you can easily plan your day around nap times without missing the island's raw beauty.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min drive by rental car or pre-booked taxi with a baby seat from Fuerteventura Airport (FUE) to the family-friendly beach hotels in Caleta de Fuste.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Dunas de Corralejo

Walk along massive, golden sand dunes that roll directly into the turquoise ocean. Ditch the stroller at the roadside parking and carry your toddler in a front-pack to let them safely roll down the soft sand slopes.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Oasis Wildlife Fuerteventura

A massive, lush rescue zoo and botanical park featuring camel rides, giraffes you can feed by hand, and sea lion shows. The wide, paved pathways are highly stroller-friendly, though there are some gentle inclines.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de la Concha in El Cotillo

This crescent-shaped beach features a natural horseshoe reef that blocks waves, leaving a crystal-clear, shallow lagoon perfect for a toddler's first swim. Park just steps from the sand, making it easy to haul your beach shelter and diaper bag.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo del Queso Majorero

A beautifully restored historic estate in Antigua featuring a cactus garden, an old windmill, and interactive exhibits detailing the island's famous goat cheese. The entire compound is flat and paved, making it incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller.

Midday (check tide times) / 2h

Playa de Sotavento Lagoon

During high tide, a massive, knee-deep saltwater lagoon forms on this vast beach, creating a giant, warm paddling pool for toddlers. Bring a carrier as the walk from the parking lot over the dry sand to the lagoon can be tough with wheels.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Mercadillo de Lajares

A charming, low-key artisan market held every Saturday in the shady village square of Lajares. It's a great place to buy handmade baby clothes while enjoying live acoustic music in a baby-friendly, open-air environment.

Morning / 1-2h

Ecomuseo La Alcogida

An outdoor, living museum that recreates a traditional 19th-century agricultural village, complete with stone houses and farm animals. While the dirt paths can be slightly bumpy, it is highly manageable with a sturdy all-terrain stroller.

Morning / 2-3h

Acua Water Park Toddler Area

The island's only water park offers a dedicated, shallow splash zone with mini-slides and colorful water features designed specifically for infants and toddlers. Loungers are positioned right next to the splash zone so parents can easily trade off watch duties.

Evening / 1h

Paseo Marítimo de Corralejo

A flat, wide, paved pedestrian promenade that hugs the coast, offering stunning views of Lobos Island. It's perfect for a scenic stroller walk with easy access to beachfront gelato shops and shaded benches.

🍽️ Where to eat

Olivo Corso

Enjoy upscale, fresh Canarian tapas like grilled octopus and local goat cheese salad in a bright, modern setting. They have early evening opening hours and plenty of space between tables for a high chair.

Restaurante El Horno

Order the slow-roasted cabrito (baby goat), a melt-in-your-mouth local specialty prepared in a traditional wood-fired oven. The rustic, cavernous dining room is loud enough to easily drown out toddler squeals, and the staff is famously warm to families.

Restaurante Frasquita

A legendary, no-frills seafood shack right on the rocks of Caleta de Fuste where you choose your fresh catch from the counter. The outdoor terrace is chaotic, lively, and right by the beach, making it the ultimate stress-free spot for dining with an active toddler.

Restaurante El Mirador

Perched right above the ocean, this spot serves incredible seafood paella with spectacular views of the cliffs. The spacious outdoor terrace makes it easy to park your stroller right next to your table.

La Playita

Located right on the sand in Morro Jable, this casual eatery is perfect for a stress-free lunch of garlic prawns and papas arrugadas. Your toddler can play in the sand right next to your outdoor table while you wait for the food.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sheraton Fuerteventura Beach, Golf & Spa Resort

Located on a sheltered, wind-protected beach in Caleta de Fuste, this resort features a heated kids' pool, a children's playground, and incredibly spacious family rooms that easily fit cribs. It's only a 15-minute drive from the airport, minimizing post-flight travel fuss.

Barceló Fuerteventura Castillo

This resort offers bungalow-style accommodations complete with fully equipped kitchenettes, allowing you to easily prep baby formula and early morning snacks. The property features direct access to the calm waters of Playa del Castillo and multiple shallow pools.

Iberostar Selection Gaviotas Park

Set on the stunning sands of Jandía, this upscale resort offers a quiet, relaxed atmosphere with top-tier service, a heated family pool, and large rooms with black-out curtains for excellent toddler naps. They provide high-quality wooden cribs and baby-friendly amenities upon arrival.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a sturdy all-terrain stroller with large wheels, as many of Fuerteventura's natural sights and beach paths involve sand or uneven gravel. Always pack a windproof UV beach tent and a high-SPF sun hat for your toddler, as the island's trademark strong breeze can mask the true intensity of the sun. Book a rental car with a pre-installed, high-quality rear-facing car seat well in advance, as public transit is limited and taxis rarely carry toddler seats.
