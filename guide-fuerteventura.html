Fuerteventura welcomes refined families with its dramatic volcanic landscapes, endless golden sand dunes, and peaceful coastal pockets. What to do

Morning 9-11am

Playa de la Concha in El Cotillo

A naturally sheltered, horseshoe-shaped lagoon with crystal-clear, wave-free waters. It is incredibly safe for baby Mary Claire to dip her toes, and there is a nearby parking area for easy stroller transfer.

Morning 10am-1pm

Oasis Wildlife Fuerteventura

A lush, shaded botanical park and rescue zoo featuring flat, stroller-accessible pathways. It offers a calm, sensory-rich environment for an infant under the shade of thousands of tropical palms.

Late afternoon 5-6:30pm

Parque Natural de Corralejo

Spectacular organic white-sand dunes overlooking the ocean. While not stroller-friendly on the sand, a premium baby carrier allows parents to enjoy a gentle, scenic golden-hour walk with sweeping views.

Afternoon 3-5pm

Paseo Marítimo de Corralejo

A fully paved, wide seafront promenade perfect for a smooth stroller ride. Will and Kathryn can enjoy ocean breezes, upscale boutique shopping, and gorgeous views of Lobos Island without any steps.

Morning 10-11:30am

Salinas del Carmen and Museo de la Sal

A historic salt pan facility with flat, smooth outdoor walkways. It offers a peaceful, educational walk with stunning blue-and-white coastal backdrops, highly manageable with a stroller.

Midday 11:30am-1:30pm

Playa de Sotavento Lagoon

A massive, shallow tidal lagoon formed by sandbars. The warm, ankle-deep water stretching for miles is incredibly calm, making it a dream spot for a safe infant splash away from ocean waves.

Morning 9:30-11am

Betancuria Historic Village Walk

Fuerteventura's ancient, flower-filled capital nestled in a mountain valley. While some paths are paved in stone, the main plazas are easily navigable and offer a quiet, shaded refuge from the coast.

Late afternoon 4:30-6pm

Paseo Marítimo de Morro Jable

A modern, wide, and paved beachfront boardwalk that runs past the iconic lighthouse. Extremely stroller-friendly, flat, and lined with upscale cafes for a relaxing coffee stop.

Afternoon 2-2:30pm

Mirador de Guise y Ayose

A dramatic mountain viewpoint featuring massive bronze statues of ancient kings. Paved viewing platforms allow parents to easily step out of a private car with a baby to capture panoramic valley views.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Casa Santa María

Set in a beautifully restored 17th-century estate in Betancuria. Features a quiet, shaded interior courtyard, highchairs, and a highly praised traditional slow-cooked kid goat dish.

Restaurante Azzurro

Located on the coast of El Cotillo, offering fresh Mediterranean-Italian fusion. The spacious outdoor terrace is perfect for early dinners, allowing the ambient sea sound to soothe the baby.

Restaurante El Horno

A rustic-chic local institution in Villaverde famous for wood-fired meats. The outdoor garden terrace is highly spacious, easily accommodating large strollers with a relaxed, family-welcoming vibe.

Restaurante Los Caracolitos

Perched on the quiet volcanic bay of Pozo Negro. Will and Kathryn can enjoy fresh grilled local fish on a peaceful seaside terrace with zero stairs and a very welcoming attitude toward infants.

Chiringuito La Isla

Accessible via a flat wooden pier over the calm waters of Caleta de Fuste. Serves high-end tapas and fresh seafood on a spectacular overwater terrace that is entirely stroller-accessible.

$250-400/night

An exclusive, high-end sanctuary within a larger resort, offering tranquil, private pool areas and spacious junior suites equipped with black-out curtains. They provide high-quality cribs, bottle warmers, and premium family care.
