Banff

← back to travel board
Nestled in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, Banff offers a majestic playground of towering peaks, pristine glacial lakes, and abundant wildlife. Families will find an ideal mix of accessible wilderness trails, scenic gondolas, and a charming mountain town atmosphere. It is a magical destination where toddlers can wander safe paths and parents can soak in world-class vistas.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min drive via rental car or Banff Airporter shuttle from Calgary International Airport (YYC) to Banff town centre

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Banff Gondola on Sulphur Mountain

Soar up Sulphur Mountain for panoramic views of six mountain ranges, featuring an easy, stroller-friendly boardwalk at the summit. Toddlers will love the interactive hands-on exhibits at the Above Banff Interpretive Centre.

Early Morning / 2h

Lake Louise Lakeshore Trail

Walk the flat, stroller-accessible path along the edge of this world-famous turquoise lake. Toddlers can safely throw pebbles into the water while parents admire the towering Victoria Glacier.

Morning / 1.5-2h

Johnston Canyon Lower Falls Walk

Wander along suspended catwalks over a rushing canyon to a thundering waterfall. Keep toddlers in a carrier as some sections have drop-offs, but they will love walking through the rock cave at the end.

Afternoon / 1h

Bow Falls Viewpoint

An incredibly powerful, easily accessible waterfall right near the Banff Springs Hotel that requires virtually no hiking. It is perfect for toddlers to safely view massive moving water from behind secure railings.

Wednesday Morning / 1-2h

Banff Mountain Market

Stroll through this vibrant seasonal market filled with local crafts, fresh fruits, and delicious baked goods. Toddlers can run around on the grass while enjoying live music and a sweet treat in the fresh mountain air.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Lake Minnewanka Scenic Boat Cruise

Embark on a covered boat tour across Banff's largest lake to learn about local history and folklore. The gentle glide is highly soothing for toddlers, and the indoor cabin keeps them cozy and warm.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Explore the birthplace of Canada's National Parks, featuring a warm thermal cave and easy boardwalks over bubbling mineral springs. Toddlers will be fascinated by the tiny, endangered snails living in the pools.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Central Park and Play Park

Let the kids burn off energy at this scenic playground situated right along the Bow River in downtown Banff. The soft-surface play area is fully equipped with toddler-friendly swings and climbing structures.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Tunnel Mountain Drive and Hoodoos Viewpoint

Drive up this scenic loop to spot wild elk grazing and see the whimsical "Hoodoo" rock formations. The viewpoint is just a short, flat walk from the parking lot, making it effortless for tiny legs.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Grizzly House

Order the family-friendly cheese fondue for a fun, interactive dipping experience that kids find absolutely magical.

Tooloulou's

Get the fluffy Acadian French toast or giant pancake platters, which are perfect for sharing with hungry toddlers.

Rocky Mountain Flatbread Co.

Order the hand-tossed, organic cheese flatbread pizza and let the kids play in the small toy kitchen area while waiting.

Whitebark Cafe

Grab a premium flat white coffee for yourself and a fresh raspberry scone with a babyccino for the little ones.

BeaverTails Banff

Indulge in a classic, warm Canadian pastry stretched to look like a beaver's tail and topped with cinnamon and chocolate.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or carrier, as mountain weather changes rapidly and many scenic paths are gravel. Book popular shuttles and gondola tickets well in advance to avoid long, toddler-testing queues during peak seasons.
