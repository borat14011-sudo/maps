Samaná Peninsula

← back to travel board
Samaná Peninsula offers a sophisticated slice of the Dominican Republic, blending wild coconut groves with an unexpected European culinary influence in Las Terrenas. It is an ideal escape for a young family seeking remote, breathtaking landscapes without sacrificing upscale comforts, paved beach paths, and calm, shallow bays. Here, you can avoid the crowded mega-resorts of Punta Cana in favor of boutique, low-key luxury that accommodates a toddler's nap schedule perfectly.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min private SUV transfer from El Catey Airport (AZS) or ~2.5 hours from Santo Domingo (SDQ) via the secure, paved Juan Pablo II highway.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Bonita

A breathtaking crescent beach with a paved, car-free walking path running parallel to the sand. The western end is completely sheltered by reefs, creating a wave-free, shallow pool perfect for a toddler's first ocean dip.

Morning / 3-4h

Caño Frío at Playa Rincón

A crystal-clear, slow-moving freshwater river that flows into the ocean under a canopy of dense mangroves. The shallow, cool waters are incredibly calm, making it a refreshing and shaded natural splash zone for an infant.

Midday / 1-2h

Museo de las Ballenas

An educational, indoor museum in Samaná town featuring a massive, real skeleton of a humpback whale. It is fully air-conditioned and stroller-friendly, providing a perfect midday escape from the tropical sun.

Morning / 1-2h

Paseo de la Costanera

A colorful beachfront pedestrian strip in Las Terrenas lined with French bakeries and boutiques. The paved path is smooth enough for high-end strollers, allowing you to sip an espresso while baby naps.

Morning / 3h

Salto El Limón via Parada Sendero El Café

Samaná’s most famous waterfall, surrounded by lush tropical jungle. While strollers are impossible here, doing this short trail with a premium baby carrier allows you to safely experience the spectacular falls.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Las Ballenas

A wide, sandy beach named after the whale-shaped rocky islets on the horizon. It features ultra-shallow shorelines and plenty of natural shade from low-hanging coconut palms, perfect for setting up a baby tent.

Morning / 4h

Los Haitises National Park Boat Tour

A smooth boat ride from Samaná harbor through towering mangrove forests and limestone caves. The large catamaran options offer steady footing and shaded decks that keep infants comfortable and protected from the elements.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Supermercado Lindo

An upscale, French-owned supermarket in the heart of Las Terrenas. It is the ultimate spot to comfortably stock up on premium European baby formulas, organic purees, and high-quality French cheeses.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Morón

A secluded, golden-sand beach that remains largely untouched by crowds. It features direct, easy parking close to the sand, meaning you won't have to carry your heavy baby gear and coolers very far.

🍽️ Where to eat

Luis Restaurant

Order the signature fresh-caught red snapper fried whole and served with coconut rice. It is a legendary, feet-in-the-sand spot on Playa Cosón where your toddler can play in the soft sand right next to your table.

La Dolce Vita

Order the homemade lobster ravioli at this beachfront Italian icon. The spacious outdoor terrace on the Las Terrenas boardwalk easily accommodates wide strollers and offers an early 6:00 PM opening for family-friendly dining.

Porto Restaurant

Order the seafood paella at this upscale beachfront dining venue located within Balcones del Atlántico. It features access to a clean, shallow pool and manicured lawns where a toddler can get their energy out while you wait for the food.

La Playita Chez Céline

Order the grilled lobster tails with garlic butter right on the sands of Las Galeras. The restaurant sits on a calm, wave-free cove, allowing parents to take turns wading in the shallow water with the baby between courses.

Mi Corazón Restaurant

Order the slow-cooked Dominican beef tenderloin with a modern gourmet twist. This high-end establishment features a quiet, breezy garden courtyard with top-tier service that warmly welcomes families during early evening hours.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sublime Samana Hotel & Residences

An upscale property on Playa Cosón offering spacious multi-bedroom suites with full kitchens, perfect for prepping baby bottles. The resort features a massive, shallow canal-style pool and pristine lawns that make stroller navigation effortless.

Aparthotel Alisei

Located directly across from the calm shores of Las Terrenas, these luxury apartments offer private kitchens, separate living areas for nap times, and a large swimming pool with a dedicated toddler wading area.

Bahia Principe Grand El Portillo

An upscale, family-centric resort featuring a dedicated kids' water splash park, calm and shallow beach waters, and reliable, resort-vetted babysitting services so parents can enjoy a quiet dinner.
