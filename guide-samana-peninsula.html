Samaná Peninsula

← back to travel board
Samaná Peninsula is a lush tropical paradise where coconut groves meet calm, turquoise waters perfect for little splashers. Far from the crowded mega-resorts, it offers families a laid-back slice of Dominican life rich in wildlife and pristine nature. It's an ideal playground for parents seeking both adventure and peaceful beach days with toddlers.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min taxi from El Catey International Airport (AZS) to Las Terrenas beach hotels, or ~2 hours via private transfer from Santo Domingo Airport (SDQ)

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Cascada El Limón

Journey through a lush jungle on horseback or hike to reach this breathtaking 130-foot waterfall with a cool swimming pool below. Toddlers can easily ride on a saddle with an adult, but be sure to bring a child carrier for the steep muddy steps near the falls.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Playa Rincón & Caño Frío

This spectacular multi-mile beach features a calm, crystal-clear freshwater river at its western end that is perfect for toddlers. Little ones can safely splash in the gentle, shallow river current while parents relax under the shade of towering palms.

Morning / 3h

Humpback Whale Watching (Samaná Bay)

Witness thousands of migrating humpback whales that visit the warm bay waters from January to March. Book a larger, stable catamaran rather than a small speed boat to ensure a smoother, safer ride for toddlers prone to motion sickness.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Punta Popy

This lively town beach in Las Terrenas features shallow, warm waters and plenty of shade trees right by the sand. It is ideal for toddlers to build sandcastles safely while parents enjoy the convenient proximity to beachfront cafes.

Morning / 4-5h

Los Haitises National Park

Take a scenic boat ride through majestic mangrove forests to explore ancient caves filled with Taino petroglyphs. Kids will love spotting pelicans and frigatebirds, but make sure to bring a toddler-sized life jacket for peace of mind on the boat.

Morning / 1h

Boulangerie Française

Experience the French colonial influence of Las Terrenas with a morning walk to this iconic local bakery. Grab a flaky butter croissant and a fresh fruit juice to keep your toddler happy and fueled for the day.

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Playita

A picture-perfect crescent cove with incredibly calm, shallow, knee-deep water that stretches far out. It's an absolute dream for toddlers to wade safely, and you can rent lounge chairs and umbrellas steps from the water.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Samaná Town Malecón & La Churcha

Stroll along the colorful waterfront pedestrian path and view 'La Churcha,' a historic 19th-century wooden church built by freed African-American slaves. The wide, paved path is completely stroller-friendly and offers beautiful bay breezes.

Evening / 2h

Pueblo de los Pescadores

Explore this charming, brightly painted strip of beachfront wooden huts that have been converted into family-friendly restaurants. It’s a great car-free zone where toddlers can stretch their legs safely before dinner.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita Restaurant

Get the fresh-caught red snapper grilled to order with a side of crispy tostones, served right on the beach sand.

Mi Corazón

Get the passion fruit ceviche for parents and a side of homemade gourmet pasta from their highly accommodating kids' menu.

El Lugar

Get the perfectly cooked beef tenderloin with pepper sauce in a relaxing, family-friendly tropical garden setting.

La Terrasse

Get the savory French crepes or fresh lobster thermidor while sitting directly on the beachfront deck.

Le Tre Caravelle

Get the homemade seafood tagliatelle and delicious brick-oven pizzas that both adults and toddlers will love.
