Samaná Peninsula

← back to travel board
The Samaná Peninsula is a lush, coconut-forested paradise where emerald mountains plunge directly into calm turquoise waters. From watching majestic humpback whales to splashing in gentle, crystal-clear tidal pools, it offers a serene escape from the typical resort crowds. It is the ultimate Dominican destination for families seeking raw natural beauty alongside relaxed Caribbean towns.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi from El Catey Airport (AZS) to Las Terrenas beach hotels, or ~2.5 hours via private shuttle from Santo Domingo Airport (SDQ)

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

El Limón Waterfall

A spectacular 130-foot cascade tucked inside a lush jungle that families can access via horseback or a scenic hike. Toddlers can easily ride in a front-carrier on a guided walking path to avoid the bumpy horse trail.

Midday / 4-5h

Playa Rincón

A stunning, world-class beach with a gentle freshwater river running into the ocean at one end. Toddlers will love splashing safely in the calm, shallow waters of the freshwater Caño Frío river.

Morning / 3-4h

Whale Watching in Samaná Bay

An unforgettable boat excursion to see thousands of migrating humpback whales that visit the warm bay every winter. Book a large catamaran instead of a small speedboat to ensure a smooth, stable ride for toddlers.

Morning / 4-5h

Los Haitises National Park

A magical boat ride through towering rock formations, mangrove forests, and ancient caves once used by Taino Indians. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the dark caves and nesting birds, just be sure to pack a toddler-sized life jacket.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Bonita

A crescent-shaped beach lined with coconut palms and a paved, car-free walking path perfect for strollers. The western end has exceptionally calm, shallow pools ideal for toddler wading.

Morning / 1-2h

Las Terrenas Fruit Markets

A vibrant stroll through local open-air markets filled with colorful tropical fruits and friendly locals. Toddlers will love pointing out giant papayas and tasting sweet, freshly-peeled baby bananas.

Morning / 3-4h

La Playita Beach

A picture-perfect cove featuring crystal-clear, shallow water that stays knee-deep for yards out. The complete lack of strong waves makes it the absolute safest natural swimming pool for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa El Valle

A rugged, wild beach framed by dramatic cliffs and a peaceful freshwater river estuary. Avoid the rough ocean waves and let your toddler splash safely in the calm river stream running right onto the sand.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Bridges of Samaná

A scenic pedestrian footbridge connecting the mainland to several lush offshore cayes. The wide, paved path is completely stroller-accessible and offers a fun vantage point for toddlers to spot fish swimming below.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Lugar

Order the perfectly grilled local red snapper or the beef tenderloin while dining in a relaxed, family-friendly garden courtyard.

La Terrasse

Get the fresh lobster or the French crepes right on the beachfront where kids can play in the sand while you wait for your meal.

El Cabito

Order the seafood paella or fresh fruit smoothies while sitting on a dramatic cliffside terrace where kids can spot sea turtles in the water below.

Zucca

Order the delicious wood-fired thin-crust Margherita pizza which is always a massive hit with picky toddlers.

La Yuca Caliente

Get the traditional Dominican crispy chicken bites and sweet plantains that toddlers can easily eat with their hands.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a sturdy, lightweight baby carrier for jungle hikes and beach walks, as stroller-friendly pavements are limited outside of main town paths. Always carry cash in Dominican Pesos for beachside coconut water, fruit stands, and local boat captains who rarely accept credit cards.
