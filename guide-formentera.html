Formentera

← back to travel board
Spain’s laid-back Balearic gem swaps high-energy resorts for pristine white sands and shallow, pool-like waters. Perfect for little feet, this bohemian paradise offers a car-free ease where days are measured in sunbeams and gentle sea breezes. It is the ultimate Mediterranean escape to disconnect and reconnect as a family.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Ibiza Airport to Ibiza Port, ~30 min ferry to Formentera, then ~15 min taxi to your hotel

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa de Ses Illetes

Frequently ranked among the world's best beaches, its calm, shallow turquoise waters resemble a giant swimming pool. Toddlers can safely splash for hours while parents relax on the soft white sand.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Cap de Barbaria Lighthouse

Walk along the dramatic cliffs to see the iconic lighthouse and peer into the famous pirate cave. Hold toddlers tightly here, but enjoy the easy, stroller-friendly paved walk leading to the tower.

Morning / 2h

Sant Francesc Xavier Town Square

The island's charming capital features a pedestrian-only square where kids can run freely around the whitewashed church. Explore the local boutiques while the little ones spot island geckos on the stone walls.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Ses Salines Natural Park

Observe the shimmering pink salt pans and look out for migratory flamingos wading in the shallow waters. It's a gentle, flat walk that is highly engaging for curious toddlers with binoculars.

Evening / 2h

Es Pujols Beach Boardwalk

Stroll along the lively pedestrian promenade lined with shops, cafes, and evening craft stalls. It is highly stroller-friendly and steps away from a playground right on the sand.

Wednesday or Sunday Afternoon / 2-3h

La Mola Hippie Market

Immerse yourselves in the vibrant colors, live acoustic music, and handmade crafts of this historic artisan market. Toddlers will love the festive atmosphere and the colorful wooden toys on display.

Morning / 3h

Playa de Migjorn

Explore the wilder side of the island with its beautiful wooden boardwalks stretching over sand dunes. Toddlers will enjoy searching for seashells in the tiny, sheltered rocky coves nearby.

Morning / 3-4h

Boat Trip to Espalmador

Take a short, gentle catamaran ride to this uninhabited, pristine island just north of Formentera. The ultra-shallow bay is incredibly safe and warm, perfect for a toddler's first boat-and-swim adventure.

Morning / 1-2h

Far de la Mola

Perched on the edge of a towering cliff, this historic lighthouse offers breathtaking panoramic sea views. There is a small, easy-to-navigate museum inside that features cool maritime artifacts kids will enjoy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Juan y Andrea

Enjoy world-famous seafood paella with your toes buried in the soft sand under shady beach awnings.

Chezz Gerdi

Order the gourmet wood-fired pizzas and fresh pasta while the kids snap photos with the iconic red hippie van parked outside.

Beso Beach Formentera

Feast on delicious grilled sea bass and fresh Mediterranean salads at this lively beach club where kids can play safely in the sand.

Can Toni

Try the traditional Balearic flatbreads called cocas and homemade ice cream at this cozy, highly family-welcoming spot in El Pilar de la Mola.

Es Ministre

Savor a family-sized fideuà noodle paella right on the shores of Ses Illetes, perfect for a quick, casual lunch barefoot.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a small car rather than scooters, as many island roads leading to beaches are bumpy, unpaved, and dusty for little passengers. Always pack a pop-up beach tent and plenty of sunscreen, as natural shade is extremely limited on Formentera's pristine beaches.
