Dominica (Douglas-Charles Airport)

← back to travel board
Welcome to the Nature Island, where lush emerald peaks plunge into crystal-clear Caribbean waters. This eco-haven is perfect for adventurous families seeking pristine waterfalls, bubbling marine springs, and friendly local culture. Prepare for a sensory-rich playground where every turn reveals a new natural wonder.
🚕 Getting there: ~60 to 75-minute scenic taxi or rental car ride winding through the lush mountain rainforest to reach Roseau or Portsmouth hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Emerald Pool

An easy 10-minute rainforest walk leads to a stunning waterfall and emerald grotto. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, cool edges of the pool.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Indian River Boat Tour

Glide down a peaceful, shaded river on a hand-rowed boat surrounded by massive buttress roots. It's a gentle, no-engine ride perfect for keeping toddlers calm and fascinated.

Morning / 2h

Fort Shirley at Cabrits National Park

Explore a historic 18th-century fortress with wide grassy ramparts and panoramic bay views. The flat paved paths are highly stroller-friendly for toddlers.

Saturday Morning / 1h

Roseau Saturday Market

Immerse your senses in Dominica's vibrant local market, packed with colorful tropical fruits like mangoes and bananas. It's a great spot for toddlers to sample sweet local treats.

Afternoon / 2h

Bubble Beach Spa

Relax on a volcanic beach where geothermal vents warm the shallow ocean waters like a natural bath. Toddlers will be delighted by the magic warm bubbles tickling their feet.

Morning / 1h

Trafalgar Falls

Witness the twin Father and Mother waterfalls from an easy, well-maintained boardwalk viewing platform. The short trail is perfect for little legs and offers great photo ops.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Purple Turtle Beach

A lovely stretch of golden sand with calm, shallow waters ideal for safe toddler wading. Parents can relax under coconut palms while the little ones build sandcastles.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Dominica Botanic Gardens

Run around these expansive, peaceful lawns featuring exotic trees and a famous school bus crushed by a giant baobab tree. It's a safe, traffic-free space for toddlers to burn off energy.

Morning / 2h

Kalinago Barana Autê

Experience the heritage of the island's indigenous people with a gentle walk through a cliffside model village. Toddlers will enjoy watching traditional canoe carving and basket weaving.

🍽️ Where to eat

Keepin' It Real

Grab a shaded beachfront table and order the fresh grilled lobster or fish fingers with local passion fruit juice.

Ruins Rock Cafe

Dine in a cool, plant-draped stone ruin and enjoy their famous chicken roti or fresh fruit smoothies.

Coral Reef Restaurant

Savor local creole mahi-mahi and sweet potato fries right on the water's edge.

Sister's Beach Bar

Order the coconut shrimp and fresh banana fritters while the kids play safely in the sand just steps away.

Riverside Cafe at Citrus Creek Plantation

Sit by a rushing river and enjoy French-Creole fusion dishes like fresh river shrimp or homemade passionfruit tart.
