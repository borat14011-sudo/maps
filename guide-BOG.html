Bogotá

← back to travel board
High in the Andes, Bogotá welcomes families with a vibrant tapestry of mountain views, cobblestone charm, and lush green parks. Children will delight in soaring above the clouds in cable cars and tasting sweet, exotic fruits in bustling markets. It is an energetic, welcoming capital where historic streets easily transform into playgrounds of discovery.

What to do

Early Morning / 2h

Monserrate Cable Car

Soar up to 3,152 meters in a cable car or funicular for breathtaking panoramic views of the city. Toddlers will love the thrilling ride, and there are wide, paved pathways at the top for easy stroller pushing.

Afternoon / 3h

Maloka Interactive Center

This hands-on science and technology museum features interactive exhibits and a dedicated play area for young children. It's the perfect indoor escape on a rainy Andean afternoon where toddlers can touch and explore safely.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la 93

A vibrant, manicured park surrounded by family-friendly cafes, featuring a great modern playground with soft ground cover. It is an ideal spot for toddlers to run around safely while parents grab a world-class Colombian coffee nearby.

Morning / 1h

Plaza de Bolívar

The grand heart of the city is filled with spectacular colonial architecture and thousands of friendly pigeons. Toddlers will be endlessly entertained chasing the birds, though keep them close as the plaza can get quite busy.

Morning / 2-3h

Jardín Botánico de Bogotá

Colombia's largest botanical garden is a serene oasis featuring stunning orchid greenhouses, a waterfall, and gentle, paved walking paths. Toddlers will love pointing out the colorful flowers and giant lily pads in the duck ponds.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Usaquén Flea Market

Held on Sundays, this vibrant outdoor market features street performers, colorful local crafts, and delicious snacks. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the live music, and the cobblestone streets are easier to navigate with a baby carrier than a stroller.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque Simón Bolívar

Bogotá's version of Central Park offers sprawling green lawns, a massive lake with pedal boats, and excellent dedicated play areas. Toddlers can feed the ducks or enjoy a leisurely stroller ride along the flat, paved paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo del Oro

Home to the world's largest collection of pre-Hispanic gold, this dazzling museum features glittering, kid-level displays that spark the imagination. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the shiny objects, and the building is fully stroller-accessible.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Mercado de Paloquemao

A sensory explosion of colorful exotic fruits, fragrant flowers, and bustling local life. Toddlers will love tasting sweet granadillas and lulos, making it a delicious and educational morning adventure.

Where to eat

  • Crepes & Waffles — A beloved Colombian chain serving sweet and savory crepes, boasting an extensive ice cream menu that toddlers will absolutely adore.
  • Ajiaco Santafereño — This traditional, comforting chicken and potato soup is mild, creamy, and served with corn on the cob, making it a perfect, easy-to-digest meal for little ones.
  • Andrés Carne de Res — A famously whimsical, brightly decorated restaurant that feels like a carnival, complete with actors, climbing walls, and a dedicated kids' menu.
  • Obleas — A popular street food consisting of thin, crispy wafers sandwiched with sweet arequipe caramel, which is a guaranteed hit for toddlers with a sweet tooth.
👨‍👩‍👧 Bogotá sits at a high altitude and the weather can change rapidly, so dress your family in layers and keep everyone hydrated to prevent altitude sickness. Many colonial streets have uneven cobblestones, so opt for a sturdy baby carrier rather than a stroller for easier navigation.
