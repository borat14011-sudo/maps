Manuel Antonio

← back to travel board
Where the vibrant Costa Rican jungle spills directly onto pristine white sands, Manuel Antonio offers an unparalleled wildlife arena for families. Little eyes will marvel at sloths hanging from the canopy and curious capuchin monkeys peeking through the trees. It’s an easy, accessible paradise where adventure meets relaxed, kid-friendly beach vibes.
🚕 Getting there: ~2.5-hour private shuttle or rental car drive from Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO) in San José directly to Manuel Antonio

What to do

Morning / 3h

Manuel Antonio National Park Guided Tour

Spot sloths, monkeys, and colorful frogs with a professional guide's spotting scope. Toddlers will love the stroller-friendly flat boardwalk trail.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Espadilla Norte

The public main beach outside the park offers gentle waves and plenty of space for building sandcastles. Toddler note: Rent a beach umbrella for essential shade and buy fresh coconut water from local vendors.

Friday afternoon or Saturday morning / 1-2h

Quepos Farmers Market (Feria)

Wander the vibrant weekend market in nearby Quepos to taste exotic fruits like rambutan and passionfruit. Toddler note: The bright colors and lively atmosphere are highly engaging, and there's plenty of space to stroll.

Morning / 1-2h

Manuel Antonio Butterfly Atrium

Walk through a lush garden enclosed by thousands of fluttering, colorful butterflies. Toddler note: It is a calm, contained space where little ones can safely watch butterflies land on nearby flowers.

Midday / 2-3h

Espadilla South Beach

This protected beach inside the national park boasts incredibly calm, crystal-clear waters perfect for splashing. Toddler note: Watch your belongings closely as cheeky raccoons and monkeys love to raid unattended bags.

Morning / 2h

Kids Saving the Rainforest Sanctuary

Take a guided tour of this rescue center to see recovering monkeys, sloths, and parrots up close. Toddler note: The guided walk is educational and short enough to keep toddlers interested without exhaustion.

Morning / 2.5h

Rainforest Spices Tour at Villa Vanilla

Explore an organic spice farm sensory trail smelling real vanilla, cinnamon, and chocolate. Toddler note: Toddlers will love tasting the sweet treats and fresh fruit at the end of the easy walk.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Marina Pez Vela Boardwalk

Stroll along the safe, pedestrian-only marina boardwalk to watch the yachts and spot wild dolphins in the harbor. Toddler note: It’s completely stroller-accessible with grassy play areas and ice cream shops nearby.

Morning / 3h

Damas Island Mangrove Boat Tour

Glide through calm, shaded mangrove channels on a covered boat to spot boa constrictors, silky anteaters, and white-faced monkeys. Toddler note: The boat is completely covered, keeping little ones safely out of the direct sun.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Avion

Dine inside and around a massive, real C-123 Fairchild cargo plane converted into a restaurant, featuring delicious coconut shrimp and burgers.

Emilio's Café

Enjoy fresh yellowfin tuna, spectacular ocean views, and decadent homemade passionfruit pie in a relaxed, open-air setting.

Ronny's Place

Head here for the best sunset view in town while sharing a giant platter of fresh fish fingers and traditional Costa Rican casado.

Falafel Bar

Dig into fresh, healthy, and kid-friendly hummus plates, falafel pita pockets, and crispy French fries in an easygoing garden patio.

Café Milagro

Start your day with rich, locally roasted Costa Rican coffee, house-made mango pancakes, and kid-approved banana bread.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a sturdy, all-terrain carrier instead of a stroller, as many park paths and beach accesses have steps or sandy terrain. Always keep food tightly sealed and zipped away in your bags, as the local monkeys are highly skilled and fearless thieves.
