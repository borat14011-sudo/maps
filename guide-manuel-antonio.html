Manuel Antonio

← back to travel board
Manuel Antonio fuses lush rainforest with pristine beaches, offering a serene escape for families. The calm, shallow waters of Biesanz Beach are perfect for infant splashing, while the stroller-friendly boardwalk along Espadilla Beach provides easy access. Wildlife like howler monkeys and toucans will captivate your baby from the safety of a shaded villa. Luxury resorts here prioritize infant comfort with in-room cribs, baby menus, and private plunge pools.
🚕 Getting there: 2.5 hours from SJO airport via private transfer (book with any car seat provider like 'Green Transfers' or 'Carlos Tours' – they supply infant seats and stroller storage).

What to do

Early morning 7-10am

Manuel Antonio National Park

Guided tours keep to shaded trails; calm tidepools at Espadilla Sur are safe for dipping. Skip the steep main trail if carrying a baby – opt for the low-elevation loop.

Late morning 9-11am

Biesanz Beach

A tranquil cove with glass-calm water, natural shade from trees, and gentle entry – ideal for infant paddling. Bring a stroller to the parking area; the short path is manageable.

Early morning 6:30-8am

Espadilla Beach Boardwalk

Paved, flat, and stroller-friendly – perfect for an early morning walk past souvenir stands with ocean views. Baby can watch the waves from a shaded bench.

Not recommended

Nauyaca Waterfalls

Impressive 150-foot falls but requires a 4x4 ride + short hike. Skip for infants under 1 due to uneven path.

Morning 8-11am

Mangrove Boat Tour

A covered boat glides through calm channels – wildlife spotting (monkeys, iguanas) from a shaded seat. Stroller stays on the dock.

Late afternoon 3-6pm

Catamaran Sunset Cruise

Many catamarans offer baby life vests and shaded decks – calm Pacific waters, dolphin watching, and a gentle sunset. Avoid if baby is sensitive to boat motion.

2 hours, morning 9-11am

Sloth Sightseeing Tour

A short, flat walk in the forest with a naturalist spotting sloths and monkeys. Babywearing recommended; the path is dirt but manageable with a rugged stroller.

Evening 6-8pm

Rainforest Night Walk

Guided walk at dusk to see frogs and insects – use a baby carrier, keep little one close. Not suitable for crawling infants due to uneven ground.

45 minutes, early morning

Quepos Marina & Fish Market

A short, flat paved area with stroller access – watch fishermen unload catch and interact with pelicans. Great for a quick cultural stop before lunch.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Avion

Iconic restaurant built around a 1950s cargo plane – family-friendly with a large outdoor terrace, high chairs available, and kid-friendly pasta. Noise moderate, early dinner recommended.

Cafe Milagro

Local coffee institution with airy interior and a small play corner – high chairs, smoothies, and excellent breakfast. Baby can nap in a shaded outdoor seat.

Ronny's Place

Hilltop family-run spot with stunning sunset views – high chairs available, seafood specials and fruit plates for little ones. Quiet early seating (5pm) avoids crowds.

La Luna at La Mariposa

Upscale open-air restaurant with a glass floor – highchairs and a serene atmosphere. Infant-friendly menu on request. Avoid if your baby is loud at dinner.

Soda El Pueblo

Casual Tico soda with a calm garden – high chair, cheap gallo pinto and fresh juice. Perfect for a low-key lunch with an infant; shade and gentle breeze.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Arenas del Mar Beachfront & Rainforest Resort

$250-400/night

Beachfront luxury with private pools, family suites with separate living area, and complimentary cribs with bedding. Stroller-friendly pathways connect to two private beaches. Blackout curtains and soundproofing.

Tulemar Resort

$200-350/night

Boutique villas with private plunge pools, full kitchens for baby meals, and complimentary cribs with linens. The property includes a private beach (Tulemar Beach) with calm water. Laundry service for baby gear.

Los Altos Resort

$200-300/night

Spacious one-bedroom suites with king beds, pull-out couch, and kitchenette – all have ocean-view balconies. Pool with a shallow kids' area, free cribs, and 24-hour room service for late feeding.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack formula and diapers from home – local pharmacies (Farmacia San José in Quepos) stock limited brands. Most resort pathways are paved, but national park trails are not stroller-friendly; use a baby carrier for nature walks. Keep baby hydrated with frequent shaded breaks and a misting fan – humidity is high, so lightweight cotton clothing is essential.
