Mazatlán

← back to travel board
Nestled on Mexico's Pacific coast, Mazatlán blends rich colonial history with breezy coastal energy, making it an ideal playground for all generations. From the gentle waves of protected bays to the vibrant colors of the historic center, your family will be swept away by its warm hospitality. It is an easy, budget-friendly escape where toddler-paced strolls and coastal adventures live side-by-side.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

El Malecón

Walk, stroll, or rent a family surrey bike along one of the world's longest boardwalks, stopping to admire cliff divers and ocean views. Toddlers will love the wide, stroller-friendly paths and the colorful seaside playgrounds along the route.

Morning / 3h

Gran Acuario Mazatlán Mar de Cortés

Explore Latin America's largest aquarium, featuring immersive exhibits dedicated to the Sea of Cortez, interactive touch tanks, and a lush botanical setting. It is fully air-conditioned and stroller-accessible, making it the perfect midday escape with little ones.

Morning / 2h

Playa Norte

Watch local fishermen bring in their daily catch and pelicans dive for food at this beloved, lively local beach. The surf is famously gentle here, making the shallow shoreline perfect for toddlers to safely dip their toes in the water.

Evening / 2h

Plazuela Machado

This vibrant, tree-lined square in the historic district is alive with musicians, street performers, and open-air cafes. It is completely pedestrianized, giving toddlers plenty of safe space to wander around the central gazebo.

Full Day / 5-6h

Isla de la Piedra (Stone Island)

Take a short, exciting water taxi ride to this tranquil peninsula lined with coconut groves and calm, wave-free beaches. The shallow shorelines are ideal for building sandcastles with toddlers, while older kids can rent banana boats.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Pino Suárez

Dive into a sensory wonderland at this historic, bustling market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and friendly vendors. Keep toddlers secure in a carrier as the narrow aisles can get crowded but are fascinating to explore.

Early Morning / 2h

El Faro Lighthouse

Hike up to one of the world's highest natural lighthouses for breathtaking 360-degree views of the coastline and a glass-bottomed skywalk. Use a sturdy toddler backpack carrier for the steep steps, or stick to the paved path at the base.

Morning / 4h

Isla de Venados (Deer Island)

Board a catamaran to this pristine, uninhabited ecological reserve boasting crystal-clear, wave-less waters perfect for snorkeling. Toddlers can safely splash in the calm tide pools while you relax on the quiet, undeveloped sands.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Central

Relax at this expansive green oasis featuring modern playgrounds, walking trails around a scenic lagoon, and plenty of space to run. It's a peaceful, stroller-friendly spot where toddlers can watch ducks and feed local birds.

Where to eat

  • Panama Restaurante y Pastelería — This beloved local institution serves up comforting Mexican classics and incredible pastries in an ultra-family-friendly atmosphere complete with high chairs and kid-approved sweet treats.
  • Tacos Capeados (Fish Tacos) — Feast on Mazatlán's signature crispy, batter-fried fish and shrimp tacos loaded with fresh toppings at casual street-side stands like Tacos El Cobarde.
  • El Presidio — Set inside a stunning, ruined 19th-century mansion with lush gardens, this upscale yet welcoming restaurant offers modern Mexican cuisine where kids can admire the open-air courtyard trees.
  • Suaves — Treat the little ones to these iconic, locally-made soft coconut marshmallows that have been a sweet Mazatlán tradition for generations.
