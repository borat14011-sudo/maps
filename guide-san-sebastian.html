San Sebastián

← back to travel board
Nestled along a crescent-shaped bay, San Sebastián blends world-class Basque gastronomy with a relaxed beachside vibe perfect for families. Children will love the vintage amusement parks, calm shallow waters, and pedestrian-friendly streets. It is an effortlessly beautiful destination where culinary adventures and toddler-friendly play go hand in hand.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi from San Sebastián Airport (EAS) directly to central beach hotels, or ~1 hour bus ride from Bilbao Airport (BIO).

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

La Concha Beach (Playa de la Concha)

One of Europe's most beautiful city beaches features super calm, shallow waters and golden sand. It is incredibly safe for toddlers to splash safely without crashing waves.

Afternoon / 3h

Monte Igueldo Funicular & Amusement Park

Ride a vintage 1912 wooden funicular railway up the mountain to a charming, retro-style amusement park. Toddlers will love the old-school carousels, gentle boat rides, and unmatched bay views.

Morning / 2h

San Sebastián Aquarium

This historic aquarium features a spectacular 360-degree underwater tunnel and tactile touch tanks. It is a fantastic sensory experience for toddlers and the perfect indoor activity for a rainy afternoon.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Cristina Enea Park

A lush, expansive park filled with exotic trees, duck ponds, and free-roaming peacocks. Toddlers will love chasing the birds and burning off energy at the well-equipped playground.

Morning / 1-2h

San Martín Market (Mercado de San Martín)

A bustling, clean local market showcasing vibrant stalls of fresh Basque cheese, colorful fruits, and pastries. The wide, flat aisles make it highly stroller-accessible and great for an easy sensory walk.

Morning / 2h

Monte Urgull & Castillo de la Mota

A low-intensity hill climb surrounded by shady trees that leads to a historic castle lookout. The paved winding paths are stroller-friendly, and toddlers can spot old stone castle battlements along the way.

Afternoon / 1h

Alderdi Eder Gardens

Located right in front of the City Hall, these manicured gardens feature a stunning, ornate 1900s carousel. It is a magical, must-do ride for toddlers looking out over the sea.

Evening / 1h

Peine del Viento (Comb of the Wind)

An iconic coastal art installation where giant steel sculptures are fused into the rocky cliffs. Toddlers will be fascinated by the ground blowholes that hiss with sea air when the waves crash below.

Sunset / 1h

La Concha Promenade (Paseo de la Concha)

A completely flat, car-free seaside promenade flanked by the city's iconic white railings. It is the absolute best spot in town to push a stroller or let toddlers safely toddle alongside the sea.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Viña

Get a slice of their legendary, ultra-creamy Basque burnt cheesecake, which is sweet and soft enough for toddlers to love.

Bar Nestor

Arrive early to score a slice of their famous, melt-in-your-mouth Spanish potato tortilla, a massive hit with young kids.

Bodega Donostiarra

Order the classic, family-friendly 'Completo' tuna and anchovy sandwich or mini-burgers, and enjoy the spacious outdoor terrace seating.

Gandarias

Try the incredibly tender, cooked-to-order 'solomillo' (beef sirloin) pintxo, which is easy for toddlers to chew and loved by parents.

Gelateria Boulevard

Treat the family to premium, artisanal Italian gelato with child-friendly flavors right next to the main pedestrian park.

👨‍👩‍👧 To beat the late-night pintxo crowds, head out for dinner around 7:00 PM when bars open and are much calmer for strollers. Always carry a spare change of clothes in your daypack, as toddlers will inevitably want to splash in the tempting, calm waters of La Concha.
