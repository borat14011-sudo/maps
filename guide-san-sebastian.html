San Sebastián cradles a crescent of gold sand lapped by gentle Cantabrian waves, perfect for tiny toes. La Concha's stroller-ready promenade glides past Belle Époque railings and calm shallows ideal for splashing. The old town's pintxos bars welcome early dinners with high chairs and patient staff. Mary Claire will nap to the rhythm of the tide while you sip Txakoli on a terrace overlooking the bay.
🚕 Getting there: San Sebastián Airport (EAS) to city center is a 20-minute drive; book a private transfer with a car seat and stroller storage, or take the Euskotren train from the airport to the city (infant stroller friendly).

What to do

Morning 9-11am

La Concha Beach

The iconic shell-shaped beach offers calm, shallow water and soft sand. Bring a pop-up tent for shade and let baby crawl at the water's edge.

Evening 7-9pm

Old Town Pintxos Crawl

Stroll cobblestone streets (stroller-friendly in main alleys) and stop at bars for bite-sized treats. Infant note: choose outdoor seating or early 7pm visit to avoid crowds.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Monte Urgull

A gentle hill with a paved path from the old town, offering panoramic views. Stroller accessible up to the mid-level viewpoint; shaded areas and benches for feeding.

Midday 11am-1pm

Miramar Palace Gardens

The royal palace's vast lawns and coastal paths are perfect for a baby's first picnic. Wide, stroller-friendly gravel walks and a calm terrace overlooking the sea.

Late morning 10-11:30am

Peine del Viento Sculptures

Eduardo Chillida's iron sculptures at the western end of Ondarreta Beach. The wave-spray area can be loud—skip if baby is sensitive, but the adjacent flat boardwalk is ideal for a pram.

Morning 10am-12pm

San Sebastián Aquarium

A large oceanarium with a walk-through tunnel, touch pools, and a calm, air-conditioned environment. Stroller accessible and baby-friendly with nursing room.

Early afternoon 1-3pm

Monte Igueldo Funicular & Amusement Park

Ride the historic funicular to the top for sweeping bay views. The amusement park is small and quiet on weekdays; baby can enjoy the gentle carousel.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Zurriola Beach

The surfer beach is more exposed, but the eastern end has calm lagoons at low tide. Fewer crowds than La Concha, perfect for a quiet afternoon with a baby tent.

Mid-morning 11am-12pm

Cathedral of the Good Shepherd

A neo-Gothic landmark with a serene interior and a small square outside for stroller parking. The bell tower is not stroller accessible, but the nave is cool and peaceful for a feed.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Viña

Famous for its Basque cheesecake (burnt cheesecake). Has high chairs and a relaxed atmosphere; best for early lunch (1pm) to avoid evening crowds.

Bar Ganbara

Michelin-recommended pintxos bar with a terrace. Offers a children's menu and a quiet side street location. Try the mushroom and ham pintxo.

La Cuchara de San Telmo

A modern pintxos spot in the old town. No high chairs but staff are accommodating; go at 6pm when it's nearly empty and you can have a table.

Bodega Donostiarra

A classic bodega with hearty Basque stews and a large family table. Provides a high chair and outdoor seating; the cocido is perfect for sharing.

Rekondo

A Michelin-starred restaurant with a relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere during lunch. Has a dedicated children's menu, high chairs, and a beautiful garden terrace.
