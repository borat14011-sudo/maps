Bogotá

← back to travel board
Perched high in the Andes, Bogotá blends vibrant historic charm with lush, green mountain escapes that will captivate travelers of all ages. From cobblestone streets filled with colorful street art to interactive museums designed for curious little hands, this bustling capital offers a rich cultural tapestry. Families will love the contrast of high-energy urban parks and tranquil, cloud-shrouded peaks.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-45 min official airport taxi or pre-booked private shuttle to northern hotels or La Candelaria.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Monserrate Sanctuary & Cable Car

Ride the funicular or cable car up the mountain for breathtaking views of the sprawling city below. Toddlers will love the exciting ride, but keep them close at the crowded lookout points.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Maloka Interactive Center

A hands-on science and technology museum with interactive exhibits perfect for rainy days. Little ones can explore the sensory play areas and watch giant screen projections.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la 93

A vibrant, green public park surrounded by family-friendly restaurants with plenty of open space to run around. It's a safe, clean spot for toddlers to stretch their legs on the grassy lawns.

Morning / 2h

Museo del Oro (Gold Museum)

Home to the world's largest collection of pre-Hispanic gold artifacts, featuring glittering, kid-captivating displays. The spacious corridors are stroller-accessible, making it easy to navigate with toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

Bogotá Botanical Garden

A peaceful oasis in the city showcasing massive greenhouses, towering palms, and calm duck ponds. Toddlers can safely wander the paved, stroller-friendly paths away from the city traffic.

Morning / 2h

Plaza de Bolívar

The historic heart of Bogotá, filled with grand architecture and hundreds of friendly pigeons. Toddlers will be endlessly entertained by chasing the birds, but watch out for uneven cobblestone streets.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Usaquén Sunday Market

A vibrant, cobblestoned market featuring local crafts, live music, and delicious street food. It is incredibly lively, so a baby carrier is highly recommended over a stroller due to the crowds and curbs.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque de los Novios

A scenic park featuring beautiful lakes where families can rent pedal boats or feed the ducks. The flat, paved paths are perfect for pushing a stroller while enjoying the mountain views.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Mercado de Paloquemao

A bustling, traditional food market overflowing with colorful, exotic fruits to taste test. Toddlers will love the sensory explosion of smells and colors, especially trying fresh passionfruit and lulo.

🍽️ Where to eat

Andrés D.C.

Order the iconic lomo al trapo (beef tenderloin wrapped in cloth) while the kids enjoy the colorful, whimsical, and highly theatrical multi-story dining atmosphere.

La Puerta de la Catedral

Try the traditional ajiaco santafereño, a hearty chicken and potato soup served with corn, capers, and cream that is mild and comforting for toddlers.

Crepes & Waffles (Chicó location)

Get the kid-pleasing sweet crepes or a mini ice cream sundae from this beloved, socially conscious Colombian chain.

Pastelería La Florida

Treat the family to a traditional chocolate completo, featuring hot chocolate served with a slice of cheese to melt inside, alongside warm buttered bread.

Abasto

Enjoy a fresh, farm-to-table breakfast of arepas con huevo and artisanal fruit juices in a beautifully rustic, relaxed space.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack layers and rain gear, as Bogotá's high-altitude weather can change rapidly from warm sunshine to chilly rain showers within minutes. Stick to registered taxi apps like Cabify or Uber instead of hailing cabs on the street to ensure safe and easy navigation with small children.
