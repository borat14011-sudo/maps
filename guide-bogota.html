Bogotá

← back to travel board
Bogotá pairs sophisticated urban culture with lush, expansive green spaces, making it a surprisingly delightful destination for a family with a toddler. Staying in upscale, secure northern neighborhoods like Zona G or Usaquén offers walkable, paved streets and easy access to high-end dining. The city’s cool, temperate climate is ideal for outdoor walks without the worry of tropical heat, while locals warmly welcome young children everywhere you go.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-45 minute private SUV transfer from El Dorado International Airport (BOG) to northern hotel districts like Zona G or Usaquén.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Monserrate Cable Car

Take the smooth cable car rather than the funicular to the top of Monserrate for breathtaking mountain views of the city. The pathways at the summit can be steep and uneven, so bring a high-quality baby carrier instead of a stroller.

Morning / 2h

Jardín Botánico de Bogotá José Celestino Mutis

This lush botanical garden features flat, paved pathways that are incredibly stroller-friendly, alongside a stunning modern greenhouse. It's a tranquil oasis where your toddler can marvel at colorful orchids and giant Amazonian lily pads.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Museo Botero

Housed in a beautiful colonial estate with a quiet central courtyard, this museum showcases Fernando Botero's signature chubby, whimsical art that easily captivates young eyes. The wide corridors and single-level layout make pushing a stroller seamless and stress-free.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la 93

This secure, upscale park in northern Bogotá features a fantastic fenced-in playground perfect for a toddler to safely burn off energy. Surrounded by excellent cafes, parents can grab a premium Colombian espresso while keeping a close eye on the play area.

Sunday Morning / 2h

Mercado de las Pulgas de Usaquén

This vibrant Sunday flea market offers a sensory feast of artisan crafts, street performers, and colorful Colombian textiles. Arrive right at 9:00 AM to beat the crowds, making it much easier to navigate the cobblestone streets with a sturdy stroller.

Morning / 2h

Museo del Oro

The Gold Museum houses a dazzling collection of pre-Hispanic gold, with softly lit galleries that create a soothing, quiet environment for an infant. The facility is fully equipped with elevators and clean, modern diaper-changing stations.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque El Virrey

A beautiful, narrow linear park featuring paved walking paths, lush green grass, and multiple modern playgrounds. It is the ultimate spot for a relaxed morning stroll with the stroller, completely insulated from heavy city traffic.

Morning / 1h

Plaza de Bolívar

The historic heart of Bogotá is a massive, open pedestrian plaza perfect for letting a toddler walk around and chase the famous flocks of pigeons. Keep your baby in a carrier here to easily navigate the surrounding historic streets and cathedral steps.

Afternoon / 2h

Biblioteca Virgilio Barco

This architectural masterpiece by Rogelio Salmona is surrounded by peaceful water canals, bike paths, and vast green lawns. The interior features gentle ramps rather than stairs, making it exceptionally easy to explore with a stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Andrés D.C.

Order the 'Lomo al Trapo' (beef tenderloin wrapped in cloth) in this wildly decorated, multi-story culinary wonderland. While chaotic, it is incredibly family-friendly with loud music that easily drowns out toddler tantrums and a dedicated children's playroom.

Abasto (Usaquén)

Try the 'Huevos Abasto' served with local arepas in a rustic, airy setting. This market-to-table spot has plenty of space for strollers, high chairs, and opening hours that cater perfectly to early-rising toddlers.

Crepes & Waffles Arte-Sano

Savor the artisanal 'Waffle de Auyama' (pumpkin waffle) or local fruit ice creams at this elevated branch of Colombia’s beloved chain. It features spacious outdoor seating, a relaxed vibe, and a highly accommodating staff used to families.

Primi

Enjoy handmade rustic pastas and wood-fired pizzas in a chic yet highly welcoming atmosphere in Zona G. The restaurant is spacious enough to easily accommodate strollers, offers early dinner seating, and provides excellent high chairs.

La Juguetería (La Macarena)

Feast on hearty Colombian steaks surrounded by thousands of nostalgic toys hanging from the walls and ceilings. This whimsical, toy-themed restaurant will keep your toddler completely mesmerized while you enjoy a relaxed meal.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Four Seasons Hotel Casa Medina

This historic luxury hotel in Zona G offers quiet, residential-style rooms with working fireplaces and exquisite wooden cribs upon request. Its central location allows you to easily walk to Bogotá's best restaurants with a stroller.

Grand Hyatt Bogota

Boasting some of the largest standard rooms in the city, this hotel features a stunning, heated indoor pool and impeccable soundproofing. It is located near the park system, providing a serene, spacious retreat for families with infants.

W Bogota

Located in the safe, walkable Usaquén neighborhood, this modern hotel offers excellent crib amenities, spacious suites, and a heated indoor pool. It sits steps away from kid-friendly malls and the Sunday flea market.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bogotá sits at over 8,600 feet, so keep your toddler hydrated and plan a low-key first day to adjust to the high altitude. Stick to private taxi apps like Uber or Cabify instead of hailing street cabs, ensuring you can secure your own portable car seat safely. Sidewalks in older areas like La Candelaria are narrow and cobblestoned, so pack a sturdy, all-terrain stroller alongside a high-quality baby carrier for historical sightseeing.
