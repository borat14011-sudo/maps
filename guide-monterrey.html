For Will and Kathryn, this high-income metropolis offers a safe, ultra-modern haven perfect for traveling with baby Mary Claire. The city boasts the stroller-ready promenade of the Santa Lucía Riverwalk, allowing for breezy waterfront strolls away from traffic. Amidst soaring skyscrapers, you will find upscale family-friendly infrastructure and elite dining tailored to discerning parents.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Parque Fundidora

An expansive urban park featuring completely flat, paved stroller-ready paths. Perfect for an early morning stroll with Mary Claire under shaded tree canopies before the midday heat.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Paseo de Santa Lucía

This beautiful artificial riverwalk is entirely stroller-accessible. Opt for a calm, quiet morning boat ride where the gentle vibration of the water often lulls infants to sleep.

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Parque Ecológico Chipinque

A gorgeous pine-forested mountain reserve offering cool breezes and stunning views. The area near the main insectary has stroller-friendly paved trails and peaceful shade.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Museo de Arte Contemporáneo (MARCO)

A quiet, spacious, and air-conditioned cultural oasis. Wide ramps and elevators make it exceptionally stroller-friendly for an art-filled afternoon escape.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Museo de Historia Mexicana

Ideally located at the start of the Santa Lucía Riverwalk, this highly polished museum is fully air-conditioned and stroller-accessible, offering a peaceful indoor environment.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Horno3

Housed in a historic blast furnace inside Parque Fundidora, this modern science center has elevators and smooth ramps, though skip the loud steel-making simulation with a sleeping baby.

Morning 8-10:30am / 2.5h

Parque Natural La Estanzuela

A scenic natural park featuring a paved, stroller-friendly main path that runs alongside a trickling stream under massive oak trees. Extremely tranquil and shaded.

Sunday morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

San Pedro de Pinta

A fabulous Sunday morning community initiative where major avenues are closed to cars. It is the ultimate luxury family environment to stroll with a baby carriage among local high-society families.

Late afternoon 5-6:30pm / 1.5h

Mirador del Obispado

A paved lookout point hosting one of the world's largest flags. Stroller-accessible ramps lead to sweeping 360-degree views of the Sierra Madre mountains.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Gran Barra

Famous for its premium cuts and elegant atmosphere, this restaurant offers a lovely outdoor terrace and highchairs, making an early dinner booking highly comfortable.

Pangea

Monterrey's premier fine-dining establishment run by Chef Guillermo González Beristáin. Book an early outdoor terrace table to enjoy contemporary French-Mexican cuisine with baby in tow.

La Nacional

An upscale local institution famous for prime ribeye and local cabrito. The dining room is spacious and highly accommodating to strollers with excellent highchairs on hand.

Cara de Vaca

An incredibly chic, open-air garden spot in San Pedro focusing on wood-fired delicacies. The lush outdoor setting naturally absorbs infant noise.

El Gaucho

A highly respected, family-friendly classic steakhouse. Serves fantastic traditional cuts in a spacious dining room where baby-strollers and families are warmly welcomed.
