Monterrey

← back to travel board
Nestled beneath the dramatic, saddle-shaped Cerro de la Silla, Monterrey blends cutting-edge modernity with warm northern Mexican culture. Families will love the sprawling green parks, interactive museums, and kid-friendly culinary scene that make this mountain-ringed metropolis a breeze to explore. From boat rides on artificial rivers to wildlife encounters, it is an underrated paradise for travelers of all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi or Uber ride to the San Pedro or Centro hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Parque Fundidora

Sprawl across this massive industrial-turned-green park featuring playgrounds, paved rental-bike paths, and extensive lawns. Toddlers will love chasing the ducks near the ponds and watching the occasional toy train pass by.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo Santa Lucía

Take a relaxing, scenic boat ride down this artificial river connecting Fundidora Park to the historic city center. It is completely stroller-friendly and highly engaging for water-loving toddlers.

Full Day / 5-6h

Bioparque Estrella

Go on an exciting drive-through safari to feed giraffes, zebras, and antelopes right from your vehicle. Toddlers will be thrilled by the petting zoo and the gentle dinosaur-themed walking paths.

Morning / 2-3h

Chipinque Ecological Park

Head up into the misty mountains for crisp pine air, scenic family-friendly hiking trails, and a wonderful butterfly sanctuary. The wooden mountain playground is perfect for toddlers needing to burn off some energy.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Museo del Acero Horno 3

This interactive science museum is built inside a massive historic blast furnace and offers hands-on physics and chemistry exhibits. Toddlers will love the outdoor water-play tables and the sensory-rich colored light displays.

Late Afternoon / 2h

The Macroplaza

Explore one of the world's largest public plazas, featuring grand monuments, beautiful fountains, and manicured gardens. Toddlers will love splashing near the towering Neptune Fountain and eating local ice cream.

Afternoon / 3-4h

KidZania Monterrey

Let your kids try out real-world jobs in this incredibly detailed, scaled-down indoor city where Monterrey's global franchise was born. There is a dedicated, safe play zone designed specifically for toddlers under four.

Morning / 1-2h

Loro Aventuras

Get up close with colorful parrots, macaws, and friendly farm animals in this compact animal sanctuary inside Fundidora. Its flat, shady layout is exceptionally easy for toddlers to navigate on foot.

Midday / 1-2h

Mercado Juárez

Dive into traditional regiomontano culture at this bustling market filled with colorful pinatas, hand-carved toys, and local treats. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vibrant sights and sounds around every corner.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Rey del Cabrito

Order the legendary, tender roasted kid goat (cabrito) served with warm, toddler-approved flour tortillas and mild charro beans.

La Nacional

Try the melt-in-your-mouth ribeye tacos and their creamy, kid-approved artisanal milk ice cream for dessert.

Panem Bakery & Bistro

Grab their freshly baked chocolate croissants and fluffy, berry-topped hotcakes that toddlers will absolutely devour.

San Juan Grill

Feast on tender northern-style grilled steaks while your kids enjoy the dedicated indoor play area and child-friendly mini-burgers.

Tacos Orinoco

Enjoy their world-famous, savory trompo (marinated pork) tacos served with a side of crispy smashed potatoes that toddlers love.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to Uber or registered hotel taxis for effortless, car-seat-friendly transport around the city. Keep a light jacket handy even in the summer, as indoor air conditioning in museums, malls, and restaurants can be surprisingly chilly for little ones.
