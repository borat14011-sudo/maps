New Orleans

New Orleans dances to a rhythm that enchants both the young and the young at heart, where historic streetcars rattle past oak trees draped in Spanish moss. Beyond the lively nightlife lies a sensory wonderland of powdered-sugar beignets, brass bands playing on sunny street corners, and swamp adventures. It is a city where every corner holds a colorful story perfect for family curiosity.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or rideshare to Downtown or the French Quarter

What to do

Morning / 3h

Louisiana Children's Museum

Located in beautiful City Park, this museum offers interactive indoor and outdoor exhibits focusing on water play, sensory gardens, and local culture. Toddlers will love the dedicated First Adventures space designed just for early crawlers.

Afternoon / 1h

St. Charles Avenue Streetcar

Hop aboard the world's oldest continuously operating streetcar line for a breezy, nostalgic ride through the Garden District. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the clacking tracks, but be sure to fold up your stroller before boarding.

Morning / 3-4h

Audubon Zoo

This world-class zoo features a fantastic swamp exhibit with white alligators and a splash park to cool down. Toddlers will adore the lazy river and the gentle farm animal contact area.

Late Morning / 1-2h

French Market

Explore the oldest open-air market in America, filled with local crafts, music, and colorful sweet treats. It is highly sensory and bustling, so keep toddlers close in a carrier or compact stroller.

Afternoon / 2-3h

City Park Carousel Gardens & Storyland

Step into a real-life fairy tale where kids can climb on giant Humpty Dumpty sculptures and ride a historic 1906 carousel. It is the perfect size for toddlers, offering gentle rides and plenty of open green space.

Morning / 2h

Audubon Aquarium

Located right on the Mississippi River, this aquarium features a walk-through tunnel, playful penguins, and a hands-on stingray touch pool. It is fully stroller-friendly and a perfect air-conditioned escape from the midday heat.

Morning / 2h

Barataria Preserve

Take a short drive out of the city to walk along wooden boardwalks over wild, alligator-filled bayous. It is stroller-accessible and offers toddlers a safe, up-close view of real Louisiana wetlands and turtles.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Royal Street Pedestrian Zone

During the day, Royal Street closes to cars, turning into a vibrant pedestrian mall filled with street performers and brass bands. Toddlers can safely dance to live jazz on the pavement without worrying about traffic.

Afternoon / 2h

Steamboat Natchez Harbor Cruise

Board an authentic steam-powered paddlewheeler for a gentle cruise down the Mississippi River complete with live calliope music. Toddlers will be fascinated by the massive, spinning red paddlewheel at the stern.

🍽️ Where to eat

Cafe Du Monde

Order a plate of warm, pillowy beignets piled high with powdered sugar alongside a cup of chocolate milk for the little ones.

Dat Dog

Build your own custom hot dog with fun toppings in a bright, colorful, and highly casual setting that welcomes noisy toddlers.

Parkway Bakery & Tavern

Grab a classic, kid-friendly poor boy sandwich loaded with roast beef gravy or fried shrimp to eat at their spacious outdoor picnic tables.

Joey K's Restaurant & Bar

Enjoy comforting Creole classics like red beans and rice or fried catfish in a laid-back, kid-welcoming Garden District diner.

District Donuts Sliders Brew

Satisfy everyone with creative, oversized craft donuts and bite-sized cheeseburger sliders perfect for tiny hands.
