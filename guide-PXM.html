Puerto Escondido

← back to travel board
Puerto Escondido blends laid-back Oaxacan culture with dramatic Pacific coastlines, creating a playground for adventure-loving families. From releasing newborn sea turtles into the surf to splashing in calm, crystal-clear bays, this coastal haven offers a slower, magical pace. It's the ultimate destination where toddlers can dig in pristine sand while parents soak in golden Mexican sunsets.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Carrizalillo

A gorgeous, crescent-shaped bay with calm, shallow turquoise waters perfect for little swimmers. Note that the 150-plus steps down can be tough with a toddler, so pack light and use a baby carrier.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Baby Turtle Release at Playa Bacocho

Join the local conservation efforts by releasing newborn sea turtles into the ocean at sunset. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching the tiny hatchlings make their heroic crawl to the waves.

Morning / 2-3h

Laguna de Manialtepec Boat Tour

Take a gentle daytime boat ride through mangrove forests to spot herons, iguanas, and kingfishers. Toddlers will love the calm boat ride, and there are shaded options to keep them out of the midday sun.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Benito Juárez

A vibrant, bustling market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and delicious aromas. It's fully paved, making it easy to navigate with a stroller while grabbing fresh mangoes for the kids.

Midday / 3-4h

Playa Puerto Angelito

A highly sheltered beach with virtually no waves, making it the safest spot in town for toddlers to splash safely. You can rent a shaded lounger right by the water's edge and enjoy fresh coconut water.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

La Punta Tide Pools

Explore the rocky end of La Punta beach at low tide to discover small pools filled with tiny crabs and snails. Toddlers will love splashing in these shallow, natural splash pads, but wear water shoes to prevent slips.

Early Morning / 1h

Playa Principal Fishing Boats

Watch the colorful local fishing pangas launch and return with their daily catch right on the beach. Toddlers will be thrilled by the bustling boats, pelicans waiting for scraps, and tractor activity on the sand.

Evening / 1-2h

El Adoquín Pedestrian Zone

As the sun sets, this street closes to traffic, turning into a lively pedestrian walk filled with street performers, artisans, and balloon vendors. It's a stress-free environment to let toddlers walk around and stretch their legs.

Morning / 2-3h

Dolphin and Whale Watching Boat Tour

Embark on a boat ride to spot wild dolphins spinning and jumping right alongside the hull. Opt for a larger, covered boat to ensure your toddler stays shaded and comfortable during the ocean safari.

Where to eat

  • El Cafecito — A beloved local institution serving giant, kid-friendly pastries, fresh fruit platters, and excellent Mexican breakfasts in a relaxed, open-air setting.
  • La Olita — Gourmet, creative tacos with plenty of mild, cheesy options for kids and a very laid-back, welcoming backyard garden vibe.
  • Sativa Terraza — An absolute beachfront spot where parents can sip cocktails while toddlers play happily in the sand right next to the dining tables.
  • Oaxacan Fruit Paletas — Cool down with traditional ice pops made from fresh local fruits like mango, coconut, and passion fruit, a mess-free and delicious treat for toddlers.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to beaches like Puerto Angelito or Carrizalillo for swimming, as the open ocean waves at Zicatela are far too strong for children. Always carry cash for beach chair rentals and vendors, and use a lightweight baby carrier instead of a stroller due to sandy paths and steep stairs.
