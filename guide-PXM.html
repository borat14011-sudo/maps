Puerto Escondido

← back to travel board
Puerto Escondido blends wild Oaxacan coastlines with a remarkably relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere. From sheltered, wave-free coves perfect for little splashers to magical sunset turtle releases, this tropical haven offers slow-paced adventures. It is a place where toddlers can dig in the soft sand while parents sip fresh coconut water under a shady palapa.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Carrizalillo

This sheltered, turquoise cove has gentle waves and shallow waters, making it the absolute best beach in town for toddlers to splash safely. Parents will need to carry little ones down the 160-step descent, but the calm swimming conditions are worth every step.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Baby Turtle Release at Playa Bacocho

Run by local conservationists, this magical activity allows kids to help tiny newborn sea turtles make their first journey into the ocean. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching the hatchlings scurry across the sand at sunset.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Benito Juárez

A vibrant sensory experience filled with colorful tropical fruits, local cheeses, and hand-woven crafts. It is stroller-accessible in most aisles, and vendors are incredibly welcoming to toddlers, often offering sweet fruit samples.

Evening / 2-3h

Laguna de Manialtepec Bioluminescence Tour

Take a sunset boat ride through the mangroves to witness the water glow in the dark when agitated. While swimming is for older kids, toddlers will love watching the glowing water drip from your hands like stardust.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Puerto Angelito

A bustling, local beach with flat, pool-like waters and plenty of shade from beachfront seafood restaurants. It is ideal for toddler wading, and you can easily hire a glass-bottom boat right from the shore.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

La Punta Sunset Watching

Gather with locals on the soft sand of La Punta to watch the fiery sun dip below the Pacific horizon. Toddlers can run freely in the wide, flat tide pools while parents enjoy the bohemian, drum-circle atmosphere.

Early Morning / 2-3h

Dolphin and Whale Watching Boat Trip

Board a covered panga boat to spot massive pods of playful dolphins leaping out of the deep blue water. Life jackets are provided for all ages, but pack snacks and a hat to keep your toddler comfortable during the ocean ride.

Evening / 1-2h

Parque El Adoquín

When this coastal street closes to traffic in the evening, it becomes a pedestrian-only zone filled with street performers, artisans, and toy vendors. Toddlers can safely wander, dance to the live music, and enjoy a scoop of local coconut ice cream.

Morning / 1-2h

Bioparque y Mariposario

A lush, quiet sanctuary where children can walk among hundreds of fluttering, colorful butterflies in a natural garden. It is a peaceful, low-stimulation escape where toddlers can closely observe caterpillars and chrysalises.

Where to eat

  • El Cafecito — This family-friendly institution serves massive portions of fluffy banana pancakes and fresh fruit plates that toddlers love, alongside excellent Oaxacan coffee for parents.
  • Fish Tacos at Tacos El Banquito — A casual, welcoming spot serving some of the best, freshest fish and shrimp tacos in town with mild sides that are easy for toddlers to nibble on.
  • La Olita — Enjoy gourmet tacos in a relaxed garden setting with sand floors where toddlers can happily play with beach toys while you wait for your food.
  • Natural Paletas at La Michoacana — Beat the tropical heat with natural, freshly made fruit popsicles in flavors like mango, strawberry, and coconut that are a mess-free, delicious treat for little ones.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to taxis rather than public 'colectivo' trucks, as they are cheap, abundant, and much safer for navigating the hilly terrain with a toddler. Always carry plenty of cash in pesos, as many beachside palapas, markets, and street food stalls do not accept credit cards.
