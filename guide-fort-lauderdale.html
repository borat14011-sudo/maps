What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Bonnet House Museum & Gardens

An elegant 35-acre historic estate offering lush, shaded pathways perfect for keeping baby Mary Claire cool while you admire the whimsical art and tropical gardens. The flat gravel walkways are highly accessible for luxury strollers.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Stranahan House

Fort Lauderdale's oldest surviving home offers a quiet, educational escape on the New River where the gentle water breezes provide a peaceful backdrop for a morning carrier walk.

Afternoon 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM / 2h

NSU Art Museum Fort Lauderdale

This spacious, air-conditioned museum features world-class modern exhibitions and wide, polished concrete floors that make maneuvering a luxury stroller completely effortless.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Museum of Discovery & Science

While geared toward children, the museum offers incredibly clean, air-conditioned sensory spaces and early childhood developmental play zones perfect for an active infant's tummy time.

Early Morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM / 1.5h

Fort Lauderdale Beach & Promenade

A beautifully paved, continuous beachfront walk where you can stroll right beside the waves under the shade of gently swaying palm trees during the calm early morning hours.

Afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:30 PM / 2h

Hugh Taylor Birch State Recreation Area

A coastal state park offering a shady maritime hammock canopy, paved interior roads for smooth stroller walking, and tranquil intra-coastal views away from the beach crowds.

Late Afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM / 2h

Las Olas Boulevard

An upscale, sidewalk-lined stretch perfect for window shopping and terrace-hopping, featuring wide walkways and plenty of shaded alcoves for quick infant feeding stops.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Riverwalk Fort Lauderdale

A beautifully landscaped linear park along the New River where you can watch megayachts sail by from a fully paved, stroller-accessible path with plenty of park benches.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM / 1.5h

Fort Lauderdale Water Taxi

An open-air boat ride that serves as a scenic tour of the canals; the crew easily accommodates folded strollers and the gentle engine hum often lulls infants to sleep.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shooters Waterfront

Enjoy chic seafood on a spacious, upscale dock where the outdoor breezes and passing yachts keep baby entertained. Highchairs are readily available, and the ambient dockside hum naturally masks occasional infant fussiness.

Casa Blanca Cafe

A historic oceanfront house serving Mediterranean classics on a breezy outdoor terrace. Arrive for an early lunch to secure a spacious corner table with plenty of room to park a luxury stroller.

Tropical Acres Steakhouse

A classic, family-welcoming steakhouse near Griffin Road that offers a spacious dining room and early evening reservation options, perfect for Will and Kathryn to enjoy a premium steak while adhering to Mary Claire's bedtime schedule.

Aruba Beach Cafe

A vibrant beachfront dining experience where the sound of the ocean waves acts as natural white noise for a napping baby. Staff are incredibly family-friendly and quickly accommodate infant seating.

Takato

An upscale oceanfront Japanese restaurant featuring a sophisticated terrace where parents can indulge in premium sushi. The early evening seating offers a quiet, refined atmosphere with spacious table layouts suitable for a stroller.
