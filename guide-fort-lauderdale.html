Fort Lauderdale

← back to travel board
Fort Lauderdale offers the perfect blend of upscale waterway views, breezy Atlantic beaches, and highly accessible infrastructure for a family traveling with a toddler. Skip the frantic energy of Miami for wide, paved beachside promenades, quiet shaded parks, and exceptionally family-welcoming luxury resorts. This curated itinerary balances high-end dining and relaxing waterfront views with seamless stroller navigation and toddler-approved sensory play.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 minute drive via private black car or Uber XL from Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL) directly to the beachfront hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Museum of Discovery and Science

Perfect for an air-conditioned escape, this world-class science museum features a dedicated Discovery Center designed specifically for kids under seven. The spacious, wide-aisle layout is incredibly stroller-friendly, and the physical play areas will easily wear out an active toddler.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Fort Lauderdale Water Taxi

Experience the famous local waterways by hopping on and off this scenic water shuttle to view multi-million dollar yachts and mansions. Standard strollers can be easily folded and rolled aboard, making it a highly efficient, breezy transit option that doubles as a scenic tour.

Morning / 1.5h

Bonnet House Museum & Gardens

Stroll through this historic, 35-acre subtropical estate where your toddler can spot wild monkeys and colorful native birds. The main pathways are flat and paved, making it an easy push for premium strollers while offering a quiet, shaded refuge.

Early Morning / 1-2h

Hugh Taylor Birch State Park

A lush oasis nestled between the Intracoastal and the ocean, offering paved, shaded trails perfect for an early morning stroller run or walk. Rent a beach wheelchair or take advantage of the quiet playground shaded by massive banyan trees.

Morning / 1h

Secret Woods Nature Center

Walk along the fully stroller-accessible boardwalk winding through a dense mangrove swamp and butterfly garden. It is incredibly quiet and peaceful, providing a rich sensory experience for a toddler without the overwhelming tourist crowds.

Saturday or Sunday Morning / 1.5h

Las Olas Oceanside Park Farmers Market

Held every weekend right off the beach, this open-air market features local food vendors, artisan crafts, and plenty of green grass for a toddler to crawl around on. It is highly accessible and perfectly positioned for a post-market walk along the paved beach promenade.

Morning / 2-3h

Lauderdale-by-the-Sea Beach

Just north of the main strip, this low-key, wide sandy beach is far quieter than central Fort Lauderdale Beach, making it ideal for setting up a toddler sun tent. Paved plazas near the pier offer easy stroller access right up to the sand's edge.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Funderdome

If you hit a rainy Florida afternoon, this clean, air-conditioned indoor playground features a designated, padded Toddler Zone safe for infants and crawlers. Parents can easily supervise from the comfortable seating area while enjoying free Wi-Fi.

Morning / 1-2h

Snyder Park

This hidden gem features a fantastic, modern playground with dedicated toddler swings and a beautiful, quiet butterfly garden. The paved walking paths around the freshwater lakes are great for a quiet midday stroller nap walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

Coconuts

Order the famous local Coconut Shrimp or fresh Mahi tacos while sitting directly on the Intracoastal waterway. The outdoor patio is lively and spacious, meaning baby babbles are easily drowned out by the ambient harbor noise.

Shooters Waterfront

Perfect for an early weekend brunch, offering a massive lawn where toddlers can stretch their legs while parents enjoy prime dockside views. They are highly accommodating with high chairs and offer plenty of space to park a stroller right at your table.

Lona Cocina Tequila Bar

Enjoy upscale, authentic Mexican street corn and braised short rib tacos on a chic, oceanfront patio. The early dinner hours are quiet and highly welcoming to families, allowing you to dine in style without the typical Miami pretension.

The Floridian Restaurant

This iconic, neon-lit Las Olas diner is perfect for an early breakfast when your toddler wakes up before the rest of the city. Service is lightning-fast, the booths are spacious, and the extensive menu of classic diner fare satisfies everyone.

Quarterdeck Restaurants (Las Olas)

A casual beachside favorite with an extensive menu of sushi, burgers, and seafood that makes dining with an infant stress-free. The open-air layout and high-energy atmosphere make it easy to manage a toddler dinner before bedtime.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Ritz-Carlton, Fort Lauderdale

This luxury property offers premium cribs, ocean-view rooms with private balconies for post-bedtime drinks, and an elevated pool deck overlooking the Atlantic. The staff is exceptionally trained to anticipate the needs of families traveling with infants.

Pelican Grand Beach Resort

Features Fort Lauderdale’s only lazy river, which is perfect for gently wading with a toddler, along with a zero-entry pool and a private, quiet stretch of beach. The spacious oceanfront suites provide the essential separate living area needed for baby’s nap times.

The Westin Fort Lauderdale Beach Resort

Connected directly to the beach via a private pedestrian skywalk, allowing you to bypass busy traffic entirely with your stroller. The resort provides high-quality cribs, has a beautiful heated pool, and offers spacious family-friendly suites.

👨‍👩‍👧 Utilize the paved Fort Lauderdale Beach Promenade for your morning walks, as it features wide, flat paths and a continuous safety barrier separating strollers from traffic. Schedule your dinners for 5:00 PM to beat both the adult dining rush and your toddler's bedtime meltdown, as top waterfront spots are highly accommodating at this hour. When using the Water Taxi, opt for the larger double-decker boats as they offer significantly more deck space to park an open stroller without folding it.
