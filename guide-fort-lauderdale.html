Fort Lauderdale

← back to travel board
Welcome to the 'Venice of America,' where sparkling waterways meet miles of pristine, family-friendly Atlantic coastline. Kids will love the thrilling water taxi rides, interactive museums, and lush parks shaded by giant banyan trees. It's the ultimate laid-back Florida getaway designed for travelers of all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 minute rideshare or taxi from Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL) to beachfront hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Fort Lauderdale Beach

Relax on the wide, golden sandy shores and play in the gentle Atlantic surf. Toddlers will love building sandcastles in the quieter northern section near Sebastian Street, away from the main crowds.

Afternoon / 3h

Museum of Discovery and Science (MODS)

Ride a simulated Everglades airboat, meet playful river otters, and explore hundreds of hands-on science exhibits. The first-floor Discovery Center is a gated, safe haven tailored specifically for toddlers to climb and explore.

Morning / 2h

Water Taxi Fort Lauderdale

Hop on and off this iconic yellow boat to cruise the scenic canals and spot mega-yachts. The gentle motion of the boat and the refreshing breeze make it highly soothing for restless toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Hugh Taylor Birch State Park

A lush coastal oasis nestled between the Intracoastal Waterway and the ocean, perfect for spotting gopher tortoises and renting family surrey bikes. The paved, car-free loop trail is ideal for a smooth stroller ride.

Morning / 3h

Flamingo Gardens

Wander through botanical gardens and meet rescued native wildlife, including flamingos, alligators, and a Florida panther. Toddlers can safely hand-feed the free-roaming peacocks and mallard ducks.

Weekend Morning / 2h

Yellow Green Farmers Market

Explore a massive, vibrant covered weekend market filled with artisanal treats, fresh tropical fruit, and live acoustic music. Arrive right at opening to navigate the wide aisles easily with a stroller before the crowds build.

Afternoon / 2h

Funderdome

Beat the midday Florida heat at this massive indoor playground packed with climbing walls, slides, and obstacle courses. There is a dedicated, padded toddler zone specifically designed to keep little ones safe.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Riverwalk Fort Lauderdale

Stroll along the beautifully landscaped New River waterfront to watch yachts and tugboats pass by. Stop at Esplanade Park along the way, which offers wide-open grassy lawns perfect for toddlers to run off some energy.

Evening / 2h

Las Olas Boulevard

Walk down this charming, tree-lined thoroughfare filled with sidewalk cafes, colorful murals, and ice cream parlors. The wide, flat sidewalks are stroller-friendly, and there are plenty of shaded benches to take a break.

🍽️ Where to eat

Jaxson's Ice Cream Parlour & Restaurant

Order the legendary 'Kitchen Sink' sundae at this retro, toy-filled landmark that kids and adults will adore.

Coconuts

Get the famous local coconut shrimp on the outdoor patio where toddlers can watch boats cruise the Intracoastal Waterway.

Quarterdeck Restaurants

Enjoy casual beachside dining with a diverse kid's menu and highly-rated fish tacos for the parents.

Lulu's Bait Shack

Sip a tropical drink and eat fresh mahi-mahi sliders in a lively, oceanfront setting filled with colorful, eye-catching decor.

The Floridian Restaurant

Dig into a massive stack of chocolate chip pancakes at this iconic, neon-lit Las Olas diner that has been serving families since 1937.
