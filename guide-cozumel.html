Cozumel

← back to travel board
Cozumel is a sun-drenched Caribbean paradise where vibrant marine life meets ancient Mayan history. With its calm, shallow western beaches and warm island hospitality, it is an effortless playground for families of all generations. From exploring lush eco-parks to splashing in crystal-clear tide pools, adventure awaits around every corner.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Cozumel International Airport to downtown San Miguel, or ~30 min to southern beach resorts.

What to do

Morning / 4-5h

Chankanaab Adventure Beach Park

A spectacular marine park where kids can snorkel in a calm lagoon, see sea lions, and walk through botanical gardens. Toddlers will love the shallow, sandy beach area and the playground.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Playa Mia Grand Beach Park

A comprehensive beach club featuring a splash park, pirate ship waterslides, and floating obstacle courses. The dedicated Buccaneer's Bay is perfectly sized for toddlers to safely splash around.

Morning / 3-4h

Punta Sur Eco Beach Park

Climb the historic Celarain lighthouse for breathtaking views or take a boat ride to spot crocodiles in the lagoon. The calm, shallow waters of El Cielito beach nearby are incredibly safe for toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

San Miguel Mercado Municipal

Experience local life at this vibrant market filled with colorful tropical fruits, handcrafted souvenirs, and authentic food stalls. Toddlers will enjoy pointing out the exotic fruits and snacking on fresh empanadas.

Morning / 2h

Discover Mexico Park Cozumel

Walk through a lush garden showcasing scale models of Mexico's most famous pyramids and colonial buildings. It is highly stroller-friendly, and the chocolate-making workshop is a hit with older siblings.

Morning / 4-5h

Cozumel Pearl Farm

A family-owned pearl farm accessible by boat, offering a peaceful beach and educational insights into pearl cultivation. Toddlers can play in the soft sand of the secluded cove while parents relax.

Morning / 3h

El Cielo Sandbar Boat Tour

Take a boat charter to this shallow sandbar famous for hundreds of giant starfish dotting the white sand. Toddlers can easily float in the waist-deep, crystal-clear water with life jackets.

Afternoon / 2h

Cozumel Cha'an Ka'an Planetarium

Escape the midday heat in this interactive science center featuring dome shows about Mayan astronomy and space. The hands-on kids' area is great for toddlers to explore tactile exhibits.

Evening / 1-2h

Sunset walk along the Malecon

Stroll along the seaside promenade to admire giant sculptures, watch local street performers, and feel the sea breeze. It's fully paved for strollers, with plenty of ice cream shops along the way.

🍽️ Where to eat

Buccanos at Night

Enjoy upscale oceanfront dining where kids can play on the sand right by the table while you savor fresh grilled lobster.

Coconuts Bar and Grill

Perched on a cliff on the wild east coast, try the legendary fish tacos in a relaxed, sandy-floor jungle setting.

El Moro

A colorful, family-friendly hidden gem serving authentic Yucatan dishes; try the tender Cochinita Pibil.

Guido's

Indulge in wood-fired pizzas and fresh pasta in a charming, lush courtyard garden that is welcoming to noisy toddlers.

Maple Bakehouse

Stop by for freshly baked artisan pastries, fluffy pancakes, and excellent coffee to kickstart a family adventure day.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a rental car or Jeep with pre-arranged car seats to easily explore the wild, eastern side of the island at your own pace. Always pack reef-safe sunscreen and a portable shade tent, as the Caribbean sun is intense and many natural beaches have limited natural shade.
