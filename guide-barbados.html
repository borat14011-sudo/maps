Barbados

← back to travel board
Barbados welcomes families with its powdery pink-sand beaches, calm turquoise waters, and exceptionally warm Bajan hospitality. From encounters with curious green monkeys to relaxed catamaran cruises, the island offers a safe and vibrant Caribbean playground. It is the ultimate hassle-free tropical escape where toddlers can splash safely and parents can truly unwind.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-30 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle from Grantley Adams International Airport to Oistins, Bridgetown, or West Coast beach hotels

What to do

Afternoon (around 2:00 PM) / 1-2h

Barbados Wildlife Reserve

Stroll shaded mahogany paths to see free-roaming green monkeys, tortoises, and deer at feeding time. Toddlers will love the slow-moving tortoises that frequently cross the walking paths.

Morning / 1.5h

Harrison's Cave

Ride an underground tram through spectacular crystallized limestone caverns filled with flowing streams and deep pools. The tram ride is completely effortless for little legs and keeps toddlers captivated by the glowing lights.

Morning / 2-3h

Carlisle Bay Marine Park

Swim in incredibly calm, shallow turquoise waters where you can spot sea turtles just off the shore. The exceptionally gentle waves make it the safest beach on the island for toddlers to splash.

Friday Evening / 2h

Oistins Friday Night Fish Fry

Immerse your family in a lively Bajan street party filled with sizzling fish stalls, steel pan music, and local crafts. Go early around 6 PM to beat the heavy crowds, allowing toddlers to dance freely to the music.

Morning / 1.5h

Hunte's Gardens

Wander through a magical, lush sinkhole filled with exotic plants, classical music, and friendly local birds. Toddlers will feel like they are exploring a real-life fairy tale garden along the easy paved paths.

Morning / 2h

St. Nicholas Abbey and Steam Railway

Take a nostalgic ride on a beautifully restored steam locomotive through the plantation grounds to Cherry Tree Hill. The open-air train ride is an absolute dream for train-loving toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Folkestone Marine Park & Visitor Centre

Snorkel the shallow reef right off the shore or let the kids play on the shaded beachfront playground. The calm, rock-free shallows are perfect for toddler wading and looking for tiny crabs.

Morning / 1-2h

Welchman Hall Gully

Walk along a stroller-friendly path in a collapsed cave system filled with wild nutmeg trees and playful monkeys. It is shaded and cool, providing a peaceful, easy-paced walk for families.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Catamaran Lunch Cruise

Sail along the scenic coastline on a spacious catamaran with stops to swim with sea turtles and snorkel shipwrecks. Toddlers can safely nap in the shaded lounge areas while parents enjoy the sea breeze.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chefette

Try the famous local roti or broasted chicken at this iconic, ultra-kid-friendly Bajan fast-food chain that features indoor play areas.

The Tides Restaurant

Indulge in upscale oceanfront dining serving fresh pan-seared flying fish while children enjoy the dedicated kid's menu in an open-air setting.

Baby Doll Land & Sea Bites

Sit right on the lively Hastings Boardwalk and enjoy delicious wood-fired pizzas and fresh fruit smoothies perfect for fussy toddlers.

Zaccios Restaurant

Order the classic Bajan fish cakes and kid-friendly pasta dishes at this relaxed beachside spot where kids can play in the sand right next to your table.

Surfer's Cafe

Get the fluffy banana pancakes or the classic flying fish sandwich at this laid-back, second-story beach café with stunning views of Oistins Bay.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry reef-safe sunscreen and mineral insect repellent, as mosquitoes can be active in the shaded gullies and gardens. Stick to the calmer West Coast beaches for toddler swimming, as the East Coast has dangerously strong currents.
