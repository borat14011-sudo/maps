Cradle of Mariachi and Curated Luxury for Three

Guadalajara

← back to travel board
Guadalajara enchants with its brilliant blue skies, majestic pink-hued cantera stone cathedrals, and the rich echoes of mariachi. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the city offers a sophisticated tapestry of tree-lined avenues in the upscale Lafayette district and vibrant artisanal enclaves. The stroller-ready, wide pedestrian promenades of Tlaquepaque’s Andador Independencia provide a seamless, cobblestone-free way to explore world-class galleries together. Returning to a tranquil courtyard oasis after a day of tasting and touring offers the ultimate high-end family retreat.
🚕 Getting there: Guadalajara International Airport (GDL) to the luxury hotel districts is about a 30 to 45-minute drive. The best option is a pre-arranged private luxury SUV transfer booked through your hotel, which ensures a sanitized, pre-installed rear-facing infant car seat and ample trunk space for your stroller.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Bosque Los Colomos

A massive, lush forest sanctuary inside the city. The Japanese Garden area offers exceptionally quiet paved paths, dense shade canopy, and tranquil streams, making it the perfect spot for Mary Claire's morning stroller nap.

Late Morning 11:30 AM - 1:30 PM / 2h

Hospicio Cabañas

A UNESCO World Heritage site famous for Jose Clemente Orozco's murals. The historic building features wide, flat, stroller-friendly stone corridors and 23 symmetrical, peaceful open-air courtyards where you can easily step aside if baby gets fussy.

Early morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM / 1.5h

Basílica de Nuestra Señora de Zapopan

This stunning 17th-century sanctuary sits on the massive, flat Plaza de las Américas. The pedestrian plaza is entirely free of vehicle traffic, providing a spacious and smooth surface to push a luxury stroller in the gentle morning breeze.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:30 PM / 2.5h

Andador Independencia in Tlaquepaque

A vibrant pedestrian-only street lined with world-class art galleries, high-end pottery shops, and overhead color installations. It is entirely paved and smooth, making it exceptionally stroller-friendly compared to the rest of the city.

Morning 9:30 AM - 12:30 PM / 3h

Zoológico Guadalajara

A world-class zoo overlooking the stunning Santiago Gorge. To keep baby Mary Claire cool and comfortable, rent a private VIP golf cart with a driver-guide to navigate the hilly terrain while keeping your stroller tucked in the back.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 1h

Teatro Degollado

A neoclassical masterpiece in the historic center. While the interior is reserved for performances, the majestic columns and the wide, flat pedestrian plaza surrounding it are perfect for a relaxed family stroll and photo opportunity.

Early Afternoon 1:00 PM - 2:30 PM / 1.5h

Museo de Arte de Zapopan (MAZ)

A sleek, minimalist contemporary art museum. The climate-controlled, quiet galleries are highly stroller-accessible and offer a peaceful, cool environment to escape the afternoon heat with an infant.

Late Afternoon 5:00 PM - 6:30 PM / 1.5h

Parque Metropolitano de Guadalajara

An expansive urban park with wide, paved walking loops. It is highly popular with local families and perfect for a late afternoon sunset stroll when the temperature drops and the park is filled with gentle breezes.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM / 1h

Plaza de Armas

A historic central plaza featuring an ornate, French-designed iron bandstand. The paved pathways are shaded by mature trees, offering a lovely, classic setting for Will and Kathryn to sit on a bench with Mary Claire and people-watch.

🍽️ Where to eat

Alcalde

Fine dining celebrating local ingredients. Book the very first lunch seating at 1:30 PM when the dining room is quietest; the highly attentive staff is happy to accommodate a sleeping infant in a stroller beside your table. Signature dish: suckling pig with chicilo mole.

La Docena

A highly popular, vibrant oyster bar and grill. The expansive, upscale outdoor terrace at the Andares Mall location provides excellent natural white noise that easily masks any baby fussiness. Signature dish: grilled oysters with clarified butter.

La Tequila

An upscale institution serving elevated, traditional Mexican cuisine. The spacious, covered outdoor terrace features wide tables with plenty of room for strollers, and the staff is remarkably warm and accommodating with highchairs. Signature dish: slow-cooked pork shank in agave leaves.

Casa Luna

A breathtakingly beautiful restaurant in Tlaquepaque set in a high-ceilinged, open-air colonial courtyard. The spacious layout easily accommodates strollers, and the soft, live marimba music provides a soothing background for an early lunch. Signature dish: artisanal mole tasting.

I Latina

A chic, retro-cool culinary hotspot offering delicious fusion dishes. Request an early dinner reservation on their lush garden patio, which provides a relaxed, spacious environment with warm, welcoming hospitality for families. Signature dish: duck carnitas tacos.
