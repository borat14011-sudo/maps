Guadalajara

← back to travel board
Guadalajara blends rich Mexican heritage with incredibly spacious, stroller-friendly plazas and lush urban parks. Families will love the gentle pace of Tlaquepaque's pedestrian streets and the interactive wonders of the city's world-class museums. It is an easy, welcoming destination where toddlers can run free and parents can savor authentic flavors.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi or authorized airport cab to downtown hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Bosque Los Colomos

A peaceful forest oasis inside the city featuring a beautiful Japanese Garden and paved paths. Toddlers will love feeding the friendly squirrels with nuts bought at the park entrance.

Morning / 3-4h

Zoológico Guadalajara

One of Latin America's best zoos, set on a dramatic canyon with a panoramic chairlift and safari ride. Infant-friendly strollers are available to rent at the main gate.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Acuario Michin Guadalajara

A state-of-the-art aquarium featuring interactive touch tanks, colorful marine life, and outdoor obstacle challenges. It is fully air-conditioned and stroller-accessible, making it the perfect escape from the midday heat.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo Chapultepec Pedestrian Corridor

A wide, tree-lined boulevard featuring weekend artisan markets and live music. It is highly stroller-friendly, with plenty of shade and benches for quick snack breaks.

Morning / 2h

Centro Histórico & Plaza de Armas

Explore the magnificent twin-towered cathedral and let toddlers chase pigeons around the ornate French iron bandstand. The wide open plazas are completely car-free and safe for little ones to wander.

Afternoon / 3h

San Pedro Tlaquepaque Pedestrian Zone

Walk down the vibrant, car-free Calle Independencia lined with colorful hanging umbrellas and mariachi musicians. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the live glassblowing demonstrations at local workshops.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Metropolitano de Guadalajara

A massive green space featuring dedicated toddler playgrounds, pedal-kart rentals, and wide paved trails. On weekends, it is the perfect spot for a relaxed family picnic on the grass.

Mid-day / 1-2h

Mercado Libertad (San Juan de Dios)

Latin America's largest indoor market is a sensory explosion of colors, toys, and traditional crafts. Pop your toddler in a baby carrier to navigate the bustling, tight aisles with ease.

Morning / 3h

Trompo Mágico Museo Infantil

An interactive children's museum filled with hands-on science, art, and sensory play zones. There is a dedicated, soft-play area specifically designed for toddlers under three.

🍽️ Where to eat

Karne Garibaldi Santa Tere

Order the iconic, savory 'carne en su jugo' which is famously served literally seconds after ordering, saving parents from hungry toddler meltdowns.

La Chata Centro

Try the mild, kid-friendly chicken enchiladas or sweet pan dulce paired with warm Mexican hot chocolate in a bustling family setting.

Café Chai Plaza Patria

Indulge in fresh fruit smoothies and sweet crepes while the kids play safely in the restaurant's dedicated indoor play area.

Birriería Las Nueve Esquinas

Feast on tender, slow-cooked shredded goat or beef birria tacos, which are naturally soft and easy for little toddlers to chew.

El Sacristán Tlaquepaque

Dine in a quiet, spacious garden courtyard and order simple, delicious cheesy quesadillas that toddlers will love devouring.
