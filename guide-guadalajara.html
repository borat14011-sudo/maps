Guadalajara

← back to travel board
Guadalajara balances rich tapatío culture with modern, leafy neighborhoods like Providencia and Americana, offering a sophisticated but highly accessible escape for a young family. Wide, tree-lined avenues and vibrant parks provide plenty of breathing room, while the legendary culinary scene is surprisingly welcoming to toddlers. It is an easy, direct flight from Houston, making it a stress-free international getaway where you can enjoy world-class architecture without the exhausting travel times.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-40 min via authorized airport taxi purchased inside the arrivals terminal directly to the hotel zones of Americana or Providencia.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Bosque Colomos

This massive urban forest features paved paths winding through towering eucalyptus trees and a tranquil Japanese Garden perfect for a peaceful morning walk. The flat walkways are excellent for strollers, and the ducks in the ponds will keep your toddler endlessly entertained.

Morning / 3-4h

Zoologico Guadalajara

Widely regarded as one of Latin America's best zoos, it features a fantastic safari truck experience and panoramic views. The entire park is highly stroller-accessible with wide, paved paths, though there are some steep hills that will give dad a good workout.

Afternoon / 2h

Acuario Michin

A world-class, modern indoor aquarium featuring massive shark tanks, interactive touch pools, and traditional Mexican cultural exhibits. It is fully air-conditioned and stroller-friendly, offering a perfect, cool escape from the midday sun for a fussy toddler.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Calle Independencia in Tlaquepaque

This vibrant, car-free cobblestone pedestrian street is lined with colorful overhead umbrellas, mariachi bands, and gorgeous artisan shops. The pedestrian-only zone makes it safe and easy to navigate, though a sturdy suspension stroller is recommended to handle the cobblestones.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Metropolitano de Guadalajara

A sprawling, incredibly spacious park on the west side of the city with endless paved paths, playgrounds, and wide-open green lawns. It’s perfect for letting your toddler run around on the grass safely or taking a long, uninterrupted stroller jog in the fresh air.

Morning / 1.5h

Museo Cabañas

This historic former orphanage boasts stunning, expansive courtyards and world-famous murals by José Clemente Orozco. The flat, open-air courtyards are incredibly peaceful and easy to navigate with a stroller, offering a quiet cultural retreat for the parents while the baby naps.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas & Guadalajara Cathedral

The historic heart of the city features a beautiful French-style bandstand where live music often plays, flanked by the stunning double-spire cathedral. The flat, open plaza is entirely pedestrian-friendly, allowing you to wheel the stroller easily between historic landmarks and stop for a quick paleta.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque Alcalde

This newly renovated city park features a beautiful central lake with paddleboats, lush gardens, and wide, smooth concrete walking paths. It is extremely stroller-friendly and houses excellent, clean playground areas suitable for younger toddlers to stretch their legs.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Via RecreActiva

Every Sunday, the city closes major avenues like Vallarta to car traffic, transforming them into massive pedestrian lanes for cyclists, runners, and families. It is the absolute best time to push a stroller right down the middle of the street alongside thousands of locals enjoying the beautiful weather.

🍽️ Where to eat

Karne Garibaldi Santa Tere

Famous for holding the Guinness World Record for the fastest food service, they serve incredible, rich carne en su jugo (beef in its juice) almost instantly after you sit down. This lightning-fast service is a lifesaver for hungry toddlers, and the bustling, noisy atmosphere easily drowns out any baby cries.

La Tequila Cocina de México (Providencia)

A sophisticated yet welcoming upscale restaurant serving refined Mexican classics like cochinita pibil alongside high-end tequilas for dad. The spacious outdoor terrace in leafy Providencia offers plenty of room for a stroller and a relaxed, family-friendly vibe.

Café Estelar

An exceptional specialty coffee shop roasting some of Mexico's finest beans, perfect for a morning caffeine fix. It features a relaxed, open-air sidewalk seating setup where you can easily park a stroller and enjoy a pastry while the baby naps.

La Docena Oyster Bar & Grill (Punto Sao Paulo)

A trendy, high-energy oyster bar serving fresh Pacific seafood and excellent wood-fired steaks in a bustling atmosphere. The high ambient noise level, spacious indoor/outdoor layout, and early opening hours make it highly accommodating for dining with an infant.

El Sacromonte

A classic, beautifully decorated restaurant serving creative haute cuisine like rose petal quesadillas in a festive, colorful setting. They are exceptionally welcoming to children, with spacious tables and attentive staff who are happy to accommodate high chairs and baby food prep.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Fiesta Americana Guadalajara Country Club

Located in the safe, upscale financial district, this luxury hotel offers spacious, soundproofed rooms and excellent, sturdy cribs upon request. The quiet, high-end atmosphere and indoor pool make it a relaxing haven for a young family after a day of exploring.

Hyatt Regency Andares Guadalajara

Situated directly in the luxurious, pedestrian-friendly Andares shopping precinct, this hotel features massive modern rooms and a beautiful indoor pool. It provides top-tier cribs and allows you to easily wheel your stroller directly out of the lobby into high-end shops and outdoor restaurants.

Hilton Guadalajara Midtown

This modern hotel features a beautiful outdoor rooftop pool with shallow areas perfect for a toddler splash session, located right above a high-end shopping mall. The rooms are incredibly spacious, cribs are readily available, and the location is exceptionally safe and walkable.

👨‍👩‍👧 While the leafy neighborhoods of Providencia and Americana have relatively decent sidewalks, a sturdy, compact stroller with good suspension is essential to handle the uneven cobblestones of Tlaquepaque and the historic center. Uber is highly reliable and affordable in Guadalajara, but since child car seat laws are rarely enforced and Ubers do not provide them, you must bring your own lightweight travel car seat for rides. Tap water is not safe to drink or brush teeth with, so always use bottled water to mix baby formula and wash bottles, which can be easily purchased at any OXXO convenience store on every corner.
