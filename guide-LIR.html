Liberia

← back to travel board
As the gateway to Guanacaste, Liberia welcomes families with its warm colonial charm, leafy plazas, and easy access to dramatic natural wonders. From splashing in gentle thermal rivers to spotting monkeys in the dry forest canopy, this vibrant city serves as the perfect low-stress base for curious young explorers.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Ponderosa Adventure Park

Embark on an African-style safari where kids can feed giraffes and zebras right from the safety of an open-air truck. It is incredibly toddler-friendly with paved flat paths and a gentle petting zoo area.

Morning / 2h

Las Pailas Trail in Rincón de la Vieja

Walk along this easy, flat loop trail to see boiling mud pots, steaming fumaroles, and mini-geysers up close. The stroller-accessible wooden boardwalks make this volcanic wonderland a breeze to navigate with toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Central Liberia

This shaded colonial plaza is the perfect spot to let toddlers run around safely while soaking in local life. Grab a traditional shaved ice from a street vendor and relax under the massive, breezy trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Llanos de Cortés Waterfall

A spectacular, wide waterfall featuring a shallow, sandy pool at the bottom that acts like a natural wading pool for toddlers. The walk down from the parking lot is short, shaded, and fully paved with stairs.

Afternoon / 1h

Liberia Centro de la Cultura

Housed in a beautifully restored 19th-century adobe building, this quiet cultural center offers a cool, indoor escape to view local art. Toddlers will love the spacious, sunny courtyard perfect for a quick, calm stretch of the legs.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal de Liberia

Immerse yourselves in local culture at this bustling indoor market filled with colorful tropical fruits and artisan crafts. The sensory sights will keep toddlers engaged, and it is a great spot to pick up fresh, soft bananas for snacking.

Afternoon / 2h

Rio Negro Hot Springs

Soak in naturally heated thermal pools shaded by a dense forest canopy along a rushing river. Several pools are very mild and shallow, allowing toddlers to safely splash and play in the warm mineral waters.

Late Afternoon / 30m

Iglesia de la Agonía

Take a peaceful stroll to Liberia's oldest church, a stunning white adobe structure that looks like it is made of sugar. The quiet, grassy plaza surrounding it is a safe, traffic-free zone for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Morning / 2h

Santa Maria Sector Ranger Station Trails

Explore the gentler side of the national park with a flat, shaded walk around this historic ranch turned ranger station. Toddlers can easily spot giant butterflies, busy leafcutter ants, and the occasional coati roaming near the picnic tables.

Where to eat

  • Café de Posada — A charming garden café serving delicious local coffee, fresh fruit smoothies, and toddler-approved sweet crepes in a secure, breezy courtyard.
  • Restaurante Tierra Mar — A spacious, family-friendly spot offering sturdy high chairs and a great kids' menu alongside traditional Costa Rican casados.
  • Copos from Parque Central Vendors — A must-try local sweet treat of shaved ice topped with powdered milk, condensed milk, and syrup that toddlers absolutely adore on hot afternoons.
  • Panadería y Repostería Musmanni — The perfect quick-stop bakery to grab soft, fresh-baked cheese breads and sweet pastries for easy, on-the-go family picnics.
👨‍👩‍👧 Liberia gets exceptionally hot midday, so plan all outdoor nature walks for early morning and carry heavy-duty, baby-safe mosquito repellent. Renting a 4x4 vehicle with a pre-booked toddler car seat is highly recommended, as the scenic roads leading to nearby national parks can be bumpy and unpaved.
