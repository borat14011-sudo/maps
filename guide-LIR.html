Liberia

← back to travel board
Nestled in Costa Rica’s sun-drenched Guanacaste province, Liberia welcomes families with its colonial charm, lush volcanic parks, and gentle wildlife encounters. It serves as the perfect low-stress base camp where toddlers can spot sleepy sloths, splash in calm waters, and taste fresh tropical fruits. From historical plazas to nearby bubbling mud pots, this warm city blends authentic Tico culture with effortless outdoor play.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Parque Nacional Rincón de la Vieja

Hike past bubbling mud pots and mini-geysers on a flat, stroller-friendly paved loop that feels like a safe, volcanic playground. Toddler note: Keep curious hands close to the railings as the steam can be hot.

Morning / 3-4h

Ponderosa Adventure Park

Take a safari truck ride to feed giraffes and zebras right from your hands, a thrilling but safe experience for all ages. Toddler note: The bumpy ride is exciting, but hold onto little ones securely during the safari.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parque Central de Liberia

Relax under the shade of giant Guanacaste trees while kids run around the open plaza and watch locals sell shaved ice. Toddler note: It is completely pedestrianized, making it a safe spot for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo de Guanacaste

Discover local history and colorful art inside a historic former military barracks with a spacious, sunny courtyard. Toddler note: The wide-open courtyard is perfect for a quiet, low-sensory break from the heat.

Morning / 2-3h

Llanos de Cortés Waterfall

Swim in the shallow, sandy pool at the base of this spectacular, easily accessible jungle waterfall. Toddler note: The walk down has stairs, but the flat, sandy beach area is ideal for toddlers to splash safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal de Liberia

Wander through colorful stalls filled with ripe mangoes, local cheeses, and traditional toys. Toddler note: Grab a fresh "pipa fria" (cold coconut water) with a straw to keep your little one hydrated and happy.

Afternoon / 2h

Centro de Rescate Las Pumas

Walk along shady, stroller-friendly paths to see rescued jaguars, pumas, and colorful macaws up close. Toddler note: The compact layout means toddlers can easily walk the entire loop without getting overtired.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Calle Real Historic Walk

Stroll past beautifully preserved white adobe houses with red-tiled roofs that give Liberia its nickname, the "White City." Toddler note: Look out for the whimsical wood carvings on the traditional doors to keep toddlers engaged.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Hector Zuniga Rovira

Let the kids burn off energy at this green riverside park featuring playground equipment and shady picnic spots. Toddler note: There are dedicated toddler swings and plenty of grass for crawling or rolling a ball.

More to see

Where to eat

  • Café de Posada — Enjoy delicious local coffee and sweet crepes in a shady, beautiful colonial courtyard with plenty of space for toddlers to look at garden plants.
  • Taquería Mazatlán — This ultra-casual, family-friendly spot serves delicious, mild tacos and quesadillas that even picky toddlers will love.
  • Traditional Churchill Shaved Ice — Treat the family to a traditional "Churchill" (shaved ice with syrup, powdered milk, and condensed milk) from a street vendor for a sweet, cooling afternoon snack.
  • El Callejón — A vibrant, open-air food court offering a variety of food stalls from burgers to pizza, featuring a relaxed atmosphere where toddler noise is easily absorbed.
👨‍👩‍👧 Liberia can get exceptionally hot midday, so plan outdoor excursions for early morning and carry a clip-on stroller fan to keep toddlers cool. Sidewalks in the historic center can be uneven, so a lightweight baby carrier is often more practical than a bulky stroller for city walks.
