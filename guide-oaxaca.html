What to do

Morning / 3h

Monte Albán Archaeological Zone

Run wild on the grassy plateaus of this ancient Zapotec mountaintop city with incredible 360-degree valley views. Toddlers will love the wide-open spaces, but keep them close near the steep, unprotected stone steps.

Morning / 1-2h

Árbol del Tule

Marvel at the world's stoutest tree trunk, estimated to be over 2,000 years old, set in a lovely manicured garden. Local kids love pointing out animal shapes hidden in the knotted bark, making it a fun visual game for toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Jardín Etnobotánico de Oaxaca

Explore a stunning cactus garden filled with towering native plants that look like something out of a storybook. Toddler note: Tours are guided and require staying on the paths, so bring a baby carrier rather than a stroller.

Midday / 1-2h

Mercado 20 de Noviembre

Experience a lively food market filled with sizzling meats, fresh tlayudas, and sweet pan de yema. It is a sensory delight for kids, but stick to the wider outer aisles if the smoky meat alley gets too intense.

Afternoon / 1h

Chocolate Mayordomo

Watch cacao beans grind into sweet chocolate paste right before your eyes and customize your own family blend. Toddlers will love tasting the warm, sugary samples and stirring their own cups of traditional hot cocoa.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Oaxaca Zócalo and Alameda de León

Let your little ones run around this lively, car-free main square while watching balloon vendors, street musicians, and giant puppets. It's the perfect spot for a toddler to burn off energy while parents relax with a fresh hibiscus juice.

Morning / 3-4h

Alebrijes Painting in San Martín Tilcajete

Visit a traditional wood-carving village where whimsical, brightly colored fantasy animal sculptures are made. Many workshops let kids paint their own wooden animals, which is an unforgettable sensory craft.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Barrio de Jalatlaco Walking Tour

Stroll through Oaxaca's quietest neighborhood, filled with beautiful street murals, cobblestone lanes, and cozy cafes. It is incredibly stroller-friendly compared to the busy city center, offering plenty of peaceful photo ops.

Morning / 2-3h

Museo Infantil de Oaxaca (MIO)

Visit this fantastic, hands-on children's museum housed in a historic railway station with outdoor play areas and old train cars to explore. It's designed specifically for toddlers and young kids to learn through play and art.

🍽️ Where to eat

Criollo

Get the multi-course tasting menu in a gorgeous, spacious courtyard where kids can watch free-roaming chickens.

Itanoní Flor de Maíz

Get the freshly pressed heirloom corn tetelas and quesadillas, which are simple enough for picky toddlers but incredibly delicious for parents.

Boulenc

Get the freshly baked sourdough pastries, avocado toast, and artisanal pizzas in a beautifully rustic, casual setting.

Tlayudas El Negro

Get the giant, crispy folded tlayudas topped with stringy quesillo cheese and grilled meats, a local favorite that's great for sharing.

La Popular

Get the casual cheese quesadillas and fresh guacamole on the breezy patio, ideal for a quick and easy family lunch.

👨‍👩‍👧 Oaxaca's historic center has uneven cobblestone streets, so leave the heavy stroller at home and opt for a sturdy baby carrier. Always keep bottled water on hand, and stick to pasteurized cheeses or freshly cooked hot foods to keep little tummies happy.
