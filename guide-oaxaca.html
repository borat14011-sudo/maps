What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzman

This breathtaking 16th-century baroque church features magnificent gilded interiors and a cool, quiet sanctuary that is ideal for escaping the midday heat with baby Mary Claire. The wide, flat stone plaza directly outside is completely stroller-accessible, allowing parents to admire the majestic facade in comfort.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Jardin Etnobotanico de Oaxaca

A world-class botanical garden showcasing native cacti and botanical wonders. Because strollers are restricted on the gravel conservation paths, using a premium baby carrier is highly recommended for this peaceful, visually stunning guided walk.

Late Afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM / 2h

Andador Turistico

Oaxacas primary pedestrian corridor is paved with smooth green volcanic stone, making it the citys most stroller-friendly route. Stroll past colorful colonial mansions, high-end boutiques, and artisanal galleries at a leisurely pace without worrying about traffic.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Zocalo Oaxaca

The vibrant main plaza is shaded by massive, ancient laurel trees that keep the area cool. It is the perfect spot for parents to relax on a bench, enjoy the gentle marimba music, and let Mary Claire watch the colorful balloons and local life pass by.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Mercado de Organicos El Pochote

A peaceful, organic open-air market offering a relaxed alternative to the chaotic main markets. Parents can shop for organic baby purées, fresh fruit juices, and local crafts in a highly family-friendly, spacious courtyard setting.

Early Morning 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 3h

Monte Alban Archaeological Zone

The ancient Zapotec capital sits on a flattened mountaintop just 20 minutes outside the city. Plan an early morning trip to beat the sun, and use a sturdy baby carrier to hike up to the spectacular grassy ceremonial platforms and terraces.

Morning 10:00 AM - 1:00 PM / 3h

Centro Cultural Community Center Teotitlan del Valle

Located in a famous Zapotec weaving village 30 minutes away, this modern, quiet cultural center features flat, smooth gallery floors and gorgeous natural light. It offers a calm, spacious indoor environment to appreciate textile art away from the city crowds.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM / 1.5h

Arbol del Tule

A short, scenic drive leads to the worlds stoutest tree trunk, nestled in a quiet, manicured municipal park. The paved walkways surrounding this giant cypress are completely flat and stroller-friendly, offering an easy and shaded outdoor excursion.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Barrio de Jalatlaco

A quiet, artistic neighborhood characterized by colorful murals, quiet cobblestone lanes, and charming specialty coffee shops. Strap baby Mary Claire into a carrier for a morning walk through these quiet streets before the daily temperature rises.

🍽️ Where to eat

Criollo

Chef Luis Arellanos high-end establishment offers a daily rotating multi-course menu served in an exceptionally spacious, open-air gravel courtyard. Request an early 6:00 PM dinner seating where strollers can sit comfortably beside tables in a peaceful atmosphere.

Casa Oaxaca El Restaurante

Renowned chef Alejandro Ruiz serves masterful regional cuisine on a beautiful rooftop terrace. Reserve an early dinner table overlooking Santo Domingo Church; the lively ambient street noise below easily masks baby vocalizations, and highchairs are gladly provided.

Alfonsina

A highly praised culinary destination located in a quiet village near the airport, featuring a relaxed, rustic, and shaded backyard dining room. The casual, open-air environment is highly welcoming to families, allowing babies to experience authentic culinary magic in a peaceful setting.

Origen

Chef Rodolfo Castellanos delivers refined Oaxacan flavors in an elegant colonial-era dining room. Booking an early 1:30 PM lunch session ensures a peaceful, spacious dining experience where the attentive staff can easily accommodate a highchair.

Boulenc

This legendary local bakery serves artisanal sourdough and exceptional pastries in a beautiful, light-filled historic building. Arrive early at 8:30 AM to secure a spacious table with a highchair before the morning rush begins.
