Venice

← back to travel board
Venice is a floating fairytale world where shimmering canals replace busy roads and historic stone plazas become playground arenas. Children will love spotting winged lions, feeding pigeons in quiet campi, and hopping onto water buses for a breezy ride. It is a completely pedestrian paradise where every bridge reveals a new adventure for curious little feet.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min Alilaguna water bus or ~20 min private water taxi to historic center dock

What to do

Late Afternoon / 30 mins

Gondola Ride on Quiet Canals

Experience the magic of Venice from the water on a traditional hand-carved wooden boat. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the gentle rocking motion, but keep them securely on your lap during the ride.

Early Morning / 1-2h

Exploring Piazza San Marco

Marvel at the grand Basilica and let kids safely run around the massive, vehicle-free public square. Bring a stroller that folds easily as you navigate the steps leading up to the piazza.

Afternoon / 2h

Playtime at Giardini della Biennale

Escape the crowds to this massive green park featuring a shaded playground, winding gravel paths, and plenty of space to run. It's the perfect spot for toddlers to burn off energy under the pine trees.

Morning / 1h

Rialto Fruit and Fish Market

Immerse your senses in Venice's historic market filled with colorful seasonal fruits and curious seafood displays. Toddlers will love pointing at the strange fish, and you can grab fresh berries for an easy snack.

Morning / 1.5h

Mask Painting at Carta Alta

Create your own Venetian carnival souvenir during a hands-on painting workshop designed for all ages. Toddlers can easily join in by finger-painting or sponge-stamping their own mini paper-mâché masks.

Sunset / 45 mins

Vaporetto Ride down the Grand Canal

Take the local water bus Line 1 for a cheap and exciting cruise past beautiful historic palaces. Toddlers will love sitting near the open bow to feel the cool breeze and watch the waves.

Morning / 2-3h

Colorful Glass Demonstrations in Murano

Take a short boat ride to Murano to watch master artisans instantly transform molten glass into delicate animal figurines. Toddlers will be absolutely spellbound by the glowing, fiery glass-shaping process.

Afternoon / 3h

Exploring the Rainbow Houses of Burano

Wander through the famous, neon-painted streets of this quiet fishing island for incredible photos and a peaceful vibe. Toddlers will be delighted by the bright pink, blue, and yellow houses lining the calm canals.

Morning / 2h

Museo di Storia Naturale

Housed in a stunning 13th-century palace, this museum features giant dinosaur skeletons, taxidermy, and highly interactive touch-screens. It's very stroller-accessible and offers a quiet, air-conditioned respite from the heat.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gelateria Nico

Order the famous gianduiotto, a block of chocolate-hazelnut gelato submerged in whipped cream, and enjoy it on the floating wooden pontoon outside.

Pizzeria L'Angelo

Grab a quick slice of their legendary, thick-crust take-away pizza loaded with fresh mozzarella and prosciutto for an easy, kid-approved canal-side picnic.

Ostaria al Vecio Pozzo

Enjoy wood-fired Neapolitan pizzas and classic seafood pasta in a spacious, stroller-friendly dining room with exceptionally welcoming staff.

Pasticceria Rosa Salva

Pick up fresh, bite-sized Venetian pastries, cream-filled krapfen donuts, and mini savory pizzette for a quick breakfast on the go.

Trattoria Anzolo Raffaele

Dine outdoors on a quiet, car-free public square where kids can safely run around while you enjoy fresh seafood and Venetian bigoli pasta.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the heavy stroller and opt for a sturdy baby carrier to comfortably navigate the hundreds of stepped bridges spanning the canals. If you do bring a stroller, choose a lightweight, easily foldable model that you can quickly carry up and down steps.
