Bermuda

← back to travel board
Bermuda offers a sophisticated, low-stress escape just a short flight away, making it ideal for a finance family navigating toddler nap times. The island's signature pink sand beaches, especially the sheltered coves, provide gentle, wave-free wading for tiny feet. With high-end resorts offering top-tier nanny services and stroller-friendly pathways, you can easily balance upscale dinners with early infant bedtimes. It is a clean, safe, and highly manicured paradise that feels worlds away without the long-haul transit drag.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-30 minute taxi ride from L.F. Wade International Airport to the South Shore luxury resorts; note that standard rental cars are not available to tourists, making taxis the primary transit method.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Bermuda Aquarium, Museum and Zoo (BAMZ)

This compact, world-class facility is incredibly manageable for little legs and offers up-close views of seals, green turtles, and local marine life. The flat, paved pathways are perfectly suited for strollers, and the outdoor playground offers a great energy-burn spot.

Morning / 2-3h

Port Royal Cove at Horseshoe Bay

While the main beach gets crowded, this enclosed cove on the far right is completely shielded from waves, creating a natural shallow wading pool for toddlers. Arrive early to snag a parking spot close to the sand, minimizing the gear-schlep with a baby.

Morning / 1.5h

Crystal & Fantasy Caves

Walk along the well-lit wooden pontoon pathways inside these stunning underground caves to see clear, azure pools and dramatic formations. You must use a front baby carrier here as strollers are not permitted down the steep entrance steps.

Afternoon / 2h

Shelly Bay Beach

This is a local favorite for families due to its exceptionally shallow, calm waters that stretch out for yards, plus a shaded park area. A fantastic playground sits directly behind the beach, allowing you to easily transition from sand to swings.

Morning / 2h

National Museum of Bermuda

Located inside a historic fortress, this museum features massive grassy lawns where toddlers can run free and see resident sheep roaming around. The spectacular indoor, air-conditioned playground provides a welcome cool-down escape from the midday sun.

Morning / 2-3h

Cooper's Island Nature Reserve

A pristine, peaceful sanctuary featuring a paved, stroller-friendly main road that leads directly to shallow, wave-free beaches like Turtle Cove. It is significantly quieter than the main tourist beaches, offering peaceful spots for an outdoor contact nap.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Blue Hole Park

An easy, flat gravel walk through lush mangroves leads to a stunning, crystal-clear blue pool surrounded by caves. A rugged all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier is recommended for the slightly uneven forested paths.

Afternoon / 1h

Flatts Inlet Boardwalk

Take a gentle stroll along this scenic boardwalk to watch the dramatic, fast-moving tidal currents change color from turquoise to deep blue. It is an easy, paved walk right next to Flatts Village, perfect for pushing a stroller before or after lunch.

Morning / 1.5h

Spittal Pond Nature Reserve

Bermuda's largest bird sanctuary offers flat, grassy coastal trails where you can spot crabs, herons, and spectacular ocean waves crashing against the cliffs. Use a baby carrier to navigate the dirt loop trail comfortably while enjoying the dramatic Atlantic views.

🍽️ Where to eat

Devil's Isle Cafe

Order the signature avocado toast or local rockfish bowl at this trendy, spacious spot that serves top-tier coffee. The outdoor patio easily accommodates large strollers, and the bustling daytime noise level easily drowns out any toddler outbursts.

Coconuts

Dine on upscale coconut shrimp and fresh catch right on a wooden deck overlooking the ocean. Book an early 5:30 PM dinner slot to catch the sunset before bedtime while enjoying the relaxed beachside breeze.

Pink Beach Club at The Loren

Savor high-end, locally sourced ocean-to-table cuisine, including their famous grilled octopus, in a chic but breezy outdoor setting. The spacious, open-air layout means you can enjoy a fine dining experience without worrying about a fussy baby disturbing indoor diners.

Wahoo's Bistro & Rum Bar

Famous for its award-winning wahoo nuggets and authentic Bermuda fish chowder served in a casual, lively waterfront setting. The noisy, friendly patio overlooking St. George's Harbour is incredibly welcoming to families with young children.

Woody's Sports Bar & Drive-In

An iconic local dive serving the best Bermuda fish sandwich on raisin bread, packed with tartar sauce and coleslaw. The outdoor picnic tables provide a highly casual, zero-stress environment where you can eat quickly with a toddler in tow.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Rosewood Bermuda

This ultra-luxury resort offers top-tier crib rentals, spacious multi-room suites, and a private beach club with a stunning zero-entry pool perfect for babies. Their professional babysitting services allow parents to enjoy an uninterrupted, high-end dinner at Island Brasserie.

The Hamilton Princess & Beach Club

Known as the 'Pink Palace', this iconic harborfront hotel offers sprawling, flat lawns, large family-friendly rooms, and a sheltered private beach club with calm waters. Its proximity to downtown Hamilton makes pushing a stroller to shops and restaurants incredibly convenient.

Grotto Bay Beach Resort

Featuring a private, shallow beach with virtually zero waves, this resort is ideal for a baby's first ocean dip. The lush, flat property grounds are easy to navigate with a stroller, and the rooms sit close to the amenities for quick nap-time returns.
