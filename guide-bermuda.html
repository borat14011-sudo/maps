Bermuda

← back to travel board
Bermuda welcomes families with its incredibly gentle turquoise waters, powdery pink sand beaches, and warm island hospitality. Perfectly sized for easy exploration, this sub-tropical paradise offers a safe, clean, and vibrant backdrop for making unforgettable memories with your little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-30 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to most major resorts, as traditional car rentals are not permitted on the island.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Horseshoe Bay Cove (Baby Beach)

While the main beach has stunning pink sands and waves, the adjacent enclosed cove is entirely wave-free, shallow, and perfect for toddlers to splash safely.

Morning / 2h

Bermuda Aquarium, Museum and Zoo

A wonderfully compact and manageable zoo where toddlers can see harbor seals, green turtles, and local marine life without getting exhausted.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Crystal Caves

Walk along floating pathways over crystal-clear underground lakes filled with dramatic stalactites, which will baby-wearers can navigate easily.

Afternoon / 3h

Royal Naval Dockyard

Explore this historic fort area featuring wide pedestrian-only paths, a massive playground, and a grassy lawn perfect for toddlers to burn off energy.

Morning / 2-3h

Shelly Bay Beach

This is the ultimate local family beach, featuring incredibly shallow, calm water for yards out, plus a shaded playground right next to the sand.

Morning / 1-2h

Bermuda Railway Trail

Stroll along these scenic, car-free paved paths winding through lush greenery and coastal views, perfect for a smooth stroller ride.

Morning / 2-3h

Clearwater Beach at Cooper's Island

A peaceful sanctuary with shallow, wave-free beaches where toddlers can spot wild crabs in the sand and watch airplanes land in the distance.

Morning / 2h

Historic Town of St. George

Wander the colorful, car-free cobblestone alleys of this UNESCO World Heritage site, stopping to feed ducks at the harbor or grab a sweet treat.

Afternoon / 1h

Gibb's Hill Lighthouse

Older kids will love climbing the 185 steps for panoramic island views, while toddlers can enjoy the grassy hill at the base and watch the boats below.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Swizzle Inn Bailey's Bay

Order the famous fish chowder paired with kids' chicken tenders, while parents enjoy the legendary Rum Swizzle on the lively, casual patio.

Wahoo's Bistro & Rum Bar

Try the award-winning wahoo nuggets and local rockfish tacos while sitting on the family-friendly, breezy waterfront patio in St. George's.

Bailey's Bay Ice Cream Parlour

Treat the little ones to a scoop of homemade Bermuda banana or natural mint chocolate chip ice cream in a freshly baked waffle cone.

The Frog & Onion Pub

Housed in a historic stone cooperage, this spacious pub offers a great children's menu, giant games, and hearty local fish and chips.

Coconuts at The Reefs Resort

Enjoy upscale casual dining right on the sand where kids can play in the soft pink beach while parents savor fresh grilled snapper.

👨‍👩‍👧 Since rental cars are not allowed for tourists, rely on Bermuda's highly reliable and stroller-friendly ferry system, or pre-book licensed taxis with car seats. Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a lightweight travel stroller, as many historic sidewalks can be narrow and uneven.
