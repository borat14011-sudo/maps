Bermuda's pastel-colored cottages and turquoise waters set a serene stage for your family escape. The island's famous pink sand beaches, like Horseshoe Bay, offer gentle waves perfect for dipping tiny toes. Many luxury resorts provide stroller-accessible boardwalks and calm lagoon access, ensuring effortless seaside days with your infant. Mary Claire will love splashing in the shallow, crystal-clear coves while you soak up the subtropical sun.
🚕 Getting there: From L.F. Wade International Airport to the main hotel strip in Southampton is a 25-minute drive. Arrange a private transfer with a pre-installed infant car seat and ample trunk space for stroller and luggage—services like Bermuda Airport Transfers or Resort's own chauffeured SUV work best.

What to do

Morning 9-11am (cooler, quieter)

Horseshoe Bay Beach

Bermuda's most iconic pink sand beach with calm, shallow waters perfect for babies. Stroller-accessible via a ramp, but the sand itself is best for carriers. Shade umbrellas and family-friendly facilities nearby.

Late morning 10am-12pm or 2-4pm

Royal Naval Dockyard

Historic waterfront complex with stroller-friendly pathways, shops, and the National Museum of Bermuda. Plenty of shade and indoor exhibits to escape the midday sun. Baby-changing stations available.

2 hours, best early morning to avoid crowds

Crystal Caves

Underground limestone caverns with breathtaking crystal-clear lakes. The cave is stroller-accessible via a gentle ramp but requires some stairs inside. Great for a cool, dark escape from heat, but skip if baby is sensitive to loud echoes.

90 minutes to 2 hours

Bermuda Aquarium, Museum & Zoo

Compact but world-class aquarium with touch tanks and open-air enclosures. Stroller-friendly, indoor/outdoor mix, and quiet corners for nursing. Baby-friendly shaded picnic area.

45 minutes including climb

Gibbs Hill Lighthouse

Climb the cast-iron lighthouse for panoramic views of the island. Not stroller-friendly (strict stairs), but the base has a lovely garden with shade. Best for one parent to climb while the other waits with baby.

1-1.5 hours

Spittal Pond Nature Reserve

Bermuda's largest nature reserve with flat, stroller-accessible trails along a coastal lagoon. Perfect for a peaceful stroll, spotting birds and turtles. Shaded benches for nursing.

2-3 hours, start at 9am to beat heat

St. George's Historic Town

UNESCO World Heritage site with cobblestone streets (stroller may be bumpy, but manageable). Explore the Town Square, St. Peter's Church, and the Deliverance ship replica. Many shaded verandas and ice cream shops.

Morning or late afternoon 4-6pm

Elbow Beach

Calmer alternative to Horseshoe Bay with softer pink sand and gentle surf. Stroller-friendly path from the road. Shaded areas under casuarina trees and a family-friendly hotel (The Reefs) nearby for facilities.

3-4 hours

Bermuda Blue Adventure Park

Water park with a dedicated kiddie zone and lazy river. Baby-friendly shallow splash pad and plenty of shade umbrellas. Ideal for a half-day outing. Stroller parking available.

🍽️ Where to eat

Beau Rivage

Upscale oceanfront dining at The Reefs Resort. Signature bouillabaisse and catch-of-the-day. Highchairs available, outdoor terrace with sea breeze, early seating at 5:30pm accommodates babies. Noise level is moderate.

Tom Moore's Tavern

Historic 1652 cottage serving Bermudian classics like fish chowder with sherry pepper. Highchairs and a quiet garden patio. Early dinner reservations recommended for families. No noise issues.

The Lobster Pot

Casual yet refined seafood spot in Hamilton. Signature grilled lobster and Bermuda fish sandwich. Highchairs, indoor seating with AC, and a relaxed early dinner vibe (5pm opening). Baby-friendly.

La Coquille

French-Bermudian fine dining at The Reefs. Famous for pan-seared sea scallops and soufflé. Offers a children's menu and highchairs. Outdoor terrace overlooking the ocean. Early seating allows for quieter experience.

Rockfish Seafood & Grill

Family-friendly grill in Hamilton with a casual atmosphere. Signature rockfish tacos and conch fritters. Highchairs, kids' menu, and a shaded outdoor deck. Noise level moderate but manageable.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

The Reefs Resort & Club

$200-400/night

Luxury boutique resort on Elbow Beach with family suites, a heated infinity pool, and direct beach access. Cribs and blackout curtains on request. Club lounge offers complimentary snacks. Stroller-friendly paths. Rates from $300-400/night in shoulder season.

Fairmont Southampton

$200-400/night

Grand resort with multiple pools (including a kid's pool), private pink sand beach, and a spa. Family suites and cribs available. Blackout curtains and baby bath products. Large property with stroller-friendly walkways. Rates often within $250-400/night off-peak.

Cambridge Beaches Resort & Spa

$200-400/night

Adults-only originally, but now welcomes families with luxury cottages, pool, and private cove. Cribs and kitchenettes in suites. Blackout curtains and quiet atmosphere. Stroller-friendly paths. Rates from $250-350/night.

Brown's Pharmacy in Hamilton stocks infant formula, diapers, and sunscreen. For pediatric care, the King Edward VII Memorial Hospital has a 24/7 emergency department; call ahead for non-urgent advice. Avoid peak midday sun (11am-3pm) and use a UV-protective beach tent on the pink sands.
