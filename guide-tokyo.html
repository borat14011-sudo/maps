What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

This massive urban park features perfectly paved, stroller-friendly paths and wide lawns ideal for a quiet family picnic. The information center provides a clean, private nursing room perfect for mid-day feeding breaks with baby Mary Claire.

Early Morning 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Meiji Jingu Shrine

A serene forested sanctuary in the heart of the city, offering a peaceful morning stroll under towering torii gates. While the main pathways are gravel, paved side paths allow for smooth stroller navigation while keeping the atmosphere quiet and calming for a resting infant.

Afternoon 1:30 PM - 3:30 PM

Tokyo Toy Museum

Housed in a charming converted historical schoolhouse, this museum features a dedicated wooden play pavilion specifically designed for infants under two years old. It is a quiet, creative space where Mary Claire can safely explore beautifully crafted Japanese wooden toys.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

teamLab Planets TOKYO

An immersive digital art museum with stunning, color-shifting light displays that will deeply captivate an infant's visual senses. Parents should use a baby carrier instead of a stroller here, and can easily bypass the water-based exhibition rooms if preferred.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Sumida Aquarium

Located inside Tokyo Skytree Town, this fully indoor, climate-controlled aquarium offers wide, flat paths and mesmerizing floor-to-ceiling jellyfish displays. The ambient blue lighting and quiet murmur of the water tanks create a highly soothing environment for a baby.

Morning 9:30 AM - 12:00 PM

Ueno Zoological Gardens

Japan's oldest zoo offers spacious, paved walking paths and excellent nursing facilities throughout the grounds. Will and Kathryn can enjoy a leisurely walk to see the giant pandas while Mary Claire enjoys the gentle outdoor sights and shaded resting areas.

Late Afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Mori Garden

A pocket of historical green tranquility nestled within the modern Roppongi Hills complex, featuring a quiet pond and cherry trees. It is the perfect spot for a quick outdoor breather, located steps away from premium department store nursing rooms.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Odaiba Marine Park

Featuring expansive, flat seaside boardwalks, this coastal park offers gentle marine breezes and stunning afternoon views of the Rainbow Bridge. The wide, crowd-free promenades are ideal for pushing a stroller while enjoying a scenic sunset walk.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Hama-rikyu Gardens

This traditional Edo-period landscape garden features peaceful seawater ponds and bridges surrounded by a futuristic skyline. The wide, flat dirt paths are easy to navigate with a sturdy stroller, offering a highly tranquil environment away from the city crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Grand Kitchen at Palace Hotel Tokyo

This elegant dining venue offers a gorgeous outdoor terrace overlooking the imperial palace moat, providing plenty of space for strollers. The staff are highly accommodating to families, offering highchairs and a relaxed yet luxurious early lunch atmosphere.

The Oak Door at Grand Hyatt Tokyo

A lively and spacious premium steakhouse in Roppongi Hills that welcomes families during their early evening dinner seatings. The restaurant easily accommodates strollers at the tables and offers highchairs alongside an exceptionally professional, child-friendly service staff.

Cicada

A stylish Mediterranean oasis in Omotesando featuring a spacious, resort-style outdoor terrace that is perfect for families. The ambient outdoor noise level easily masks baby sounds, and the restaurant gladly provides highchairs for early dinner guests.

Seryna Roppongi

This high-end Japanese restaurant specializes in shabu-shabu and features private, traditional tatami mat rooms. Booking a private room allows baby Mary Claire to crawl or rest safely on the soft floor while Will and Kathryn enjoy a premium Kobe beef dinner.

Bills Futako-Tamagawa

Famous for its world-class ricotta hotcakes, this spacious and trendy restaurant is exceptionally stroller-accessible and child-friendly. Located in a high-end shopping plaza, it offers immediate access to pristine family restrooms and baby changing facilities.
