Tokyo

← back to travel board
Tokyo is a captivating playground where futuristic technology seamlessly blends with ancient traditions to enchant travelers of all ages. From sensory-rich digital art museums to quiet, stroller-friendly temple paths, the city is remarkably clean, safe, and welcoming for families.
🚕 Getting there: ~45-60 min via Narita Express train or ~30 min taxi from Haneda Airport directly to central Tokyo hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Ghibli Museum

Step into the whimsical, fairy-tale world of Hayao Miyazaki's classic animations. Toddlers will love playing in the exclusive, soft indoor Catbus and exploring the rooftop garden.

Morning / 2h

teamLab Planets

A fully immersive digital art museum where visitors walk barefoot through stunning projection-mapped installations. Toddlers will be mesmerized splashing in the warm, knee-deep water filled with digital glowing koi fish.

Afternoon / 3h

Ueno Zoo and Park

Japan's oldest zoo features giant pandas, elephants, and wide, stroller-friendly paths. There is an excellent children's zoo section perfect for letting toddlers see domestic animals up close.

Afternoon / 2h

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

A massive tranquil oasis blending Japanese, French, and English landscape designs. It features wide-open lawns perfect for toddlers to run around safely away from Tokyo's busy streets.

Morning / 1-2h

Sensō-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest and most iconic Buddhist temple located in the heart of historic Asakusa. Toddlers will be captivated by the giant red paper lanterns and the sensory experience of the incense burners.

Morning / 2h

Toyosu Fish Market Rooftop Garden

Explore Tokyo's modern seafood market and then head up to the grassy rooftop plaza. Toddlers can safely stretch their legs while enjoying panoramic views of the Tokyo skyline and Rainbow Bridge.

Afternoon / 2h

Tokyo Toy Museum

Housed in a former elementary school, this interactive museum is filled with beautifully crafted wooden toys from around the world. There is a dedicated, safe playroom designed specifically for toddlers under two years old.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Sumida Aquarium

A modern, beautifully designed indoor aquarium located right inside the Tokyo Skytree complex. Toddlers can easily look into the massive, open-topped indoor pool to watch penguins swim at eye-level.

Morning / 2h

Inokashira Park Swan Boats

A beautiful wooded park featuring a large central pond where families can rent foot-pedal swan boats. Toddlers will love looking at the ducks and fish from the safety of your lap on the water.

🍽️ Where to eat

Kura Sushi Asakusa

Get the conveyor-belt sushi plates and let your kids pop empty plates into the table slot to trigger a fun digital game that wins capsule toys.

Chao Bamboo Harajuku

Get the mild, kid-friendly chicken fried rice and noodles in a relaxed, open-air street food setting where toddler messes aren't a worry.

Sanrio Cafe Ikebukuro

Get the adorable Hello Kitty shaped pancakes and latte art that are guaranteed to put a massive smile on any toddler's face.

Sometarō Asakusa

Get the savory okonomiyaki pancakes cooked right at your table, which provides a highly visual and delicious dining experience for the whole family.

Tonkatsu Maisen Aoyama

Get the incredibly tender, melt-in-your-mouth pork cutlets and their famous, easy-to-hold mini tonkatsu sandwich pockets.
