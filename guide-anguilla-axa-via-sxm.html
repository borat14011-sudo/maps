Anguilla (AXA via SXM)

← back to travel board
Anguilla is a serene Caribbean paradise where blindingly white beaches meet calm, shallow waters perfect for young swimmers. Easily accessible via a scenic ferry ride from Saint Martin, this laid-back island offers an unhurried escape from hectic family routines. Here, days are measured in gentle waves, friendly local encounters, and postcard-perfect moments under the palms.
🚕 Getting there: ~25-minute public ferry or private charter boat from SXM to Blowing Point terminal, then a ~15-minute taxi to the main beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Shoal Bay East

Relax on one of the world's best beaches with powder-soft sand and shallow tidal pools. Toddlers will love splashing safely in the calm, reef-protected waters.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Meads Bay Beach

A stunning crescent of calm water flanked by family-friendly resorts. It is perfect for gentle stroller walks along the wide, hard-packed shoreline.

Morning / 2h

Little Bay Cove

A secluded cove accessible by a short, exciting boat ride from Crocus Bay where older kids can snorkel. Toddlers can spot sea turtles in the crystal-clear shallows.

Afternoon / 2h

Cove Bay

Enjoy the gentle, windy shores of Cove Bay where kids can run free on the wide sand. Toddlers will love watching the colorful kitesurfers glide by.

Afternoon / 3h

Rendezvous Bay

A massive, shallow crescent beach with water as calm as a bath. Toddlers can safely wade for yards while parents enjoy the views of neighboring St. Martin.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

The Valley Market

Explore the colorful local market stalls in Anguilla's capital for fresh tropical fruits. Toddlers will enjoy the lively Caribbean music and sweet local pastries.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Sandy Ground Beach

Watch colorful sailboats bob in this highly protected harbor while kids build sandcastles. The water here is exceptionally calm, making it perfect for baby steps.

Morning / 1-2h

Island Harbour

A charming local fishing village where kids can watch fishermen bring in colorful catches. Toddlers will be fascinated by the brightly painted boats docked on the sand.

Lunchtime / 2-3h

Scilly Cay

Take a free, two-minute boat ride to this tiny, coral-rimmed offshore island for lunch. Toddlers will love the quick boat ride and searching for seashells in the shallows.

🍽️ Where to eat

Blanchards Beach Shack

Order the crispy chicken bites, mahi-mahi tacos, and a homemade chocolate chip cookie to eat directly on the sand at Meads Bay.

Sunshine Shack

Grab some perfectly grilled BBQ ribs and let the kids play in the sand just steps from your rustic beach table.

Bankie Banx's Dune Preserve

Enjoy a relaxed lunch of chicken quesadillas in a whimsical, multi-level beachfront treehouse constructed entirely from driftwood.

Elvis' Beach Bar

Savor famous fish tacos and a cold drink while the kids play giant Jenga and ring-toss on the sandy floor of this boat-turned-bar.

Straw Hat Restaurant

Indulge in local red snapper Creole or kid-friendly sliders while sitting on a breeze-cooled deck directly over the water on Meads Bay.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a lightweight stroller with large wheels to easily navigate the sandy beach paths. Renting a car directly at the ferry terminal is highly recommended for hassle-free beach hopping with toddler gear.
