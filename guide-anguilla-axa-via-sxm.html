Pristine white sands and gentle turquoise waves for families

Anguilla (AXA via SXM)

← back to travel board
Just a short ferry ride from St. Martin, Anguilla welcomes families with thirty-three powdery white-sand beaches and incredibly calm, shallow waters. This unhurried Caribbean paradise offers a safe, laid-back escape where toddlers can splash safely while parents soak in the tranquil island rhythm. With its friendly locals and quiet roads, it is the ultimate stress-free beach getaway.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min sea shuttle from SXM to Blowing Point ferry terminal, then ~15 min taxi to the beach hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Shoal Bay East

Renowned for its blindingly white sand and blindingly blue waters, this beach has offshore reefs that block big waves. Toddlers can safely splash in the warm tidal shallows while you lounge under a rental umbrella.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Meads Bay Beach

A stunning stretch of calm, pristine water lined with excellent family-friendly dining options. The wide sandy shoreline is perfect for building sandcastles and gentle wading with toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

Rendezvous Bay

This sprawling, crescent-shaped bay offers incredibly still, pool-like waters and a stunning view of neighboring St. Martin. Toddlers will love walking along the endless flat shoreline searching for tiny seashells.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Sandy Ground Beach

The island's main harbor is a hub of gentle activity where colorful boats bob in calm, wave-free waters. Toddlers will be fascinated by the coming and going of sailboats on the gentle shoreline.

Morning / 2h

Crocus Bay Boat Ride to Little Bay

Take a quick, exciting 5-minute motorboat ride from Crocus Bay to the secluded cove of Little Bay. Toddlers will love the short boat adventure, and the sheltered cove is completely protected from wind and waves.

Morning / 1h

The Valley Agricultural Cooperative

Introduce your little ones to tropical agriculture and vibrant local produce at this small, welcoming market hub in the island's capital. Toddlers can touch and taste fresh local fruits like sweet mangoes and bananas.

Midday / 1h

Heritage Collection Museum

A small, air-conditioned museum filled with island artifacts, offering a quick, cool respite from the midday sun. It is short enough to keep a toddler's attention while teaching parents about Arawak history.

Midday / 3h

Offshore Excursion to Scilly Cay

Wave from the pier at Island Harbour for a free, 2-minute boat ride to this tiny coral island. It offers a completely self-contained, safe beach environment perfect for toddler exploration.

Morning / 2h

Cove Bay Beach

A vast, windswept but very shallow beach where local horses occasionally walk along the water's edge. The incredibly gentle, shallow entry makes it an ideal spot for toddlers to test their water wings.

🍽️ Where to eat

Blanchards Beach Shack

Grab delicious mahi-mahi tacos and hand-cut fries right on the sand at Meads Bay, where kids can play in the sand while you wait.

Sunshine Shack

Enjoy Garvey's legendary beachside BBQ ribs and chicken on Rendezvous Bay while your toddler plays with beach toys right at your feet.

Elvis' SandBar

Order the famous goat cheese quesadillas and a plate of kid-friendly nachos at this beachfront spot where the floor is pure sand.

Straw Hat

Treat the family to upscale Caribbean-style red snapper or high-end burgers at this welcoming spot with great high chairs and beach views.

Veya Restaurant

Dine in a magical treehouse-like setting on delicious Moroccan-spiced shrimp while listening to gentle, live acoustic music.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-arranged car seat since taxis get expensive and toddler-friendly public transit is non-existent on the island. Always carry plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a pop-up beach tent, as natural shade can be scarce on Anguilla's most pristine stretches of sand.
