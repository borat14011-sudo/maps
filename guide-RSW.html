Sun-soaked gulf shores and wildlife adventures for all ages

Fort Myers

← back to travel board
Fort Myers welcomes families with its gently sloping white-sand beaches, emerald Gulf waters, and rich history of innovation. Here, days are filled with spotting manatees in the wild, exploring hands-on science museums, and collecting delicate seashells under the warm Florida sun. It is a laid-back coastal playground where toddler paces and parent relaxation find the perfect balance.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Lakes Regional Park

Ride the miniature train through woods and splash in the water play areas. Toddlers will love the sensory-friendly playgrounds and renting a family surrey bike.

Morning / 2h

Edison & Ford Winter Estates

Walk among giant banyan trees and explore the historic winter homes of two famous inventors. The vast open gardens are perfect for toddlers to stroll without feeling restricted.

Afternoon / 2-3h

IMAG History & Science Center

Touch live marine life in the stingray tank and build in the interactive toddler exhibit space. It is a fantastic, air-conditioned escape from the midday Florida heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Manatee Park

See gentle giants up close from the viewing platforms, especially during the cooler winter months when they seek warm water. The paved, flat walking paths are completely stroller-friendly.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Fort Myers Beach at Times Square

Play in the calm, shallow Gulf waters and hunt for tiny coquina shells on the soft sand. The nearby pedestrian plaza is great for a quick, toddler-approved ice cream break.

Morning / 2h

Calusa Nature Center and Planetarium

Walk the elevated boardwalks through cypress swamps and meet rescued birds of prey. Toddlers will be captivated by the indoor butterfly garden and reptile displays.

Afternoon / 2h

The Shell Factory & Nature Park

Browse millions of seashells, feed goats in the petting zoo, and let kids run wild in the playground. It is a nostalgic, sensory-rich stop that keeps toddlers highly entertained.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Alliance for the Arts GreenMarket

Taste fresh tropical fruits, listen to live local music, and shop for handmade goods at this vibrant Saturday market. Toddlers can safely run on the grassy lawn while parents browse the stalls.

Morning / 2h

J.N. "Ding" Darling National Wildlife Refuge

Spot pink roseate spoonbills and alligators from the comfort of your car on the Wildlife Drive. It is an effortless way to experience wild Florida with toddlers who need an AC-cooled car nap.

Where to eat

  • Doc Ford's Rum Bar & Grille — Enjoy fresh, flavorful coastal seafood in a casual, noisy waterfront setting where energetic toddlers blend right into the lively atmosphere.
  • Flippers on the Bay — Dine on open-air decks directly over the water where kids can watch for swimming dolphins while eating fresh fish tacos.
  • Farmers Market Restaurant — Serving up comforting, kid-friendly Southern classic dishes since 1952 in an ultra-relaxed, family-welcoming environment.
