Vancouver

← back to travel board
Vancouver is a sparkling playground where towering temperate rainforests meet the glistening Pacific Ocean, offering an effortless blend of urban excitement and wild nature. From stroller-friendly seawall paths to vibrant public markets, the city makes traveling with little ones a breeze. Families can transition from feeding giant harbor seals to exploring interactive hands-on museums all in a single, memorable day.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Canada Line light rail ride to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Stanley Park Seawall & Miniature Railway

Walk or rent a bike with a trailer to cruise the scenic paved seawall, then ride the historic miniature train through the towering trees. Toddlers will love the dedicated water park and spotting ducks at Beaver Lake.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Granville Island Kids Market

Hop on a tiny Aquabus ferry to reach this bustling peninsula, featuring a multi-story market dedicated entirely to children's toys, crafts, and indoor play. Parents can grab fresh pastries at the main public market next door while toddlers enjoy the outdoor pond.

Morning / 3h

Science World at TELUS World of Science

Housed in an iconic geodesic dome, this hands-on museum features the Wonder Gallery designed specifically for kids under five to crawl, splash, and build. It is the perfect rainy-day escape with endless sensory exhibits.

Morning / 2-3h

Capilano Suspension Bridge Park

Walk high above the rainforest canyon on the thrilling suspension bridge and explore the Treetops Adventure canopy walkways. While strollers aren't allowed on the bridge, the boardwalks are highly secure, and front-pack carriers are perfect for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Bloedel Conservatory in Queen Elizabeth Park

Step inside a warm, glass-domed paradise filled with exotic plants, free-flying tropical birds, and colorful koi fish. It is highly stroller-accessible and the perfect size to keep toddlers engaged without causing walking fatigue.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Kitsilano Beach & Playground

Soak in the mountain views from the sandy shore while your little ones explore the massive, fully accessible playground complete with toddler swings. Afterward, take a dip in the heated, salt-water Kitsilano Pool, which features an ultra-shallow beach entry.

Morning / 2h

Maplewood Farm

Located just across the bridge in North Vancouver, this heritage farm allows kids to get up close with over 200 farm animals and birds. Toddlers can pet goats, feed the ducks, and see real working tractors.

Morning / 3h

Vancouver Aquarium

Located inside Stanley Park, this world-class aquarium lets toddlers come face-to-face with sea otters, jellyfish, and giant Pacific octopuses. The hands-on Clownfish Cove play area is tailored specifically for the youngest ocean explorers.

Afternoon / 2h

Lynn Canyon Park & Ecology Centre

A quieter, free alternative to Capilano, this lush park features its own suspension bridge and easy, stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks. Drop by the tiny Ecology Centre to see interactive nature displays tailored for curious young minds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Rocky Mountain Flatbread Co.

Order the organic Margherita flatbread while the kids play with the mini play-kitchen in the restaurant's dedicated family play area.

White Spot

Get the legendary Pirate Pak, a kid's meal served in a cardboard pirate ship complete with a gold coin, while you enjoy a classic Triple O burger.

Go Fish

Grab some crispy, ultra-fresh cod fish and chips from this colorful outdoor shack and enjoy it on the picnic tables overlooking the marina.

Lee's Donuts

Treat the family to a box of warm, fresh honey-dipped donuts from this legendary Granville Island institution.

Bella Gelateria

Cool off with a scoop of award-winning, handcrafted salted caramel or mango gelato right along the scenic seawall.

👨‍👩‍👧 Purchase a Compass Card for seamless family transit tap-ins, as kids under twelve ride all buses, SkyTrains, and SeaBuses for free. Pack a lightweight, durable stroller with good suspension to easily navigate the gravel park trails and paved seawalls.
