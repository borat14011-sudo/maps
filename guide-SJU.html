San Juan

← back to travel board
San Juan wraps your family in a warm embrace of pastel-hued Spanish colonial streets, salty ocean breezes, and the rhythmic beat of salsa. From flying kites on the breezy lawns of historic forts to splashing in calm, reef-protected tide pools, this tropical paradise offers a seamless blend of rich history and effortless island relaxation. It is a place where every corner promises a new sensory adventure for curious toddlers and parents alike.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Castillo San Felipe del Morro

Run across the massive green esplanade and fly colorful kites in the ocean breeze before exploring the stone tunnels. Toddler note: The grassy lawn is perfect for running off energy, but keep a close eye near the steep fort walls.

Morning / 3-4h

Balneario de Carolina

This pristine, crescent-shaped public beach features exceptionally calm waters protected by natural reefs, making it a safe haven for little splashers. Toddler note: Rent an umbrella and use the clean on-site restrooms and showers to wash off sand easily.

Morning / 2h

Wandering Old San Juan

Walk the vibrant cobblestone streets lined with rainbow-colored houses and spot the friendly street cats cared for by the local sanctuary. Toddler note: Ditch the stroller for a sturdy baby carrier because the historic cobblestones and narrow sidewalks are very bumpy.

Midday / 1-2h

Plaza del Mercado de Santurce

Experience a bustling, authentic market filled with tropical fruits, local crafts, and lively music during the daytime. Toddler note: Visit before 4:00 PM to avoid the loud, crowded evening party scene and enjoy a fresh fruit smoothie in peace.

Morning / 1h

Parque de las Palomas

Buy a bag of dried corn for a dollar and let the kids feed hundreds of gentle, friendly pigeons overlooking the beautiful bay. Toddler note: Bring hand sanitizer and wet wipes for a quick cleanup right after the feeding frenzy.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque Luis Muñoz Marín

A massive, beautifully landscaped urban park featuring water fountains to splash in, sensory playgrounds, and paved walking paths. Toddler note: Pack a change of dry clothes and water shoes so your little one can safely enjoy the interactive splash pads.

Morning / 2h

Piñones State Forest Boardwalk

Walk along the scenic wooden boardwalk winding through mangrove forests and stunning ocean views just minutes outside the city. Toddler note: The flat, barrier-free boardwalk is highly stroller-accessible and shaded by lush palm canopies.

Morning / 1-2h

Castillo San Cristóbal

America's largest Spanish fortification offers expansive ramparts, historic sentry boxes to peek through, and breathtaking coastal views. Toddler note: The main courtyards are flat and easy for toddlers to walk, but hold hands firmly on the stone staircases.

Evening / 1-2h

Sunset at Paseo de la Princesa

Take a breezy, tree-lined stroller walk along the historic city walls to watch the sunset, stopping for local street food vendors along the way. Toddler note: Grab a cup of shaved ice to keep toddlers cool and happy during the walk.

More to see

Where to eat

  • Mofongo at Café El Punto — This cozy, family-friendly spot in Old San Juan serves a mild, delicious mashed plantain mofongo that toddlers love to eat with their hands.
  • Piraguas from Street Carts — Look for the colorful wooden pushcarts in Old San Juan for a refreshing cup of hand-shaved ice topped with sweet local syrups like passion fruit or coconut.
  • Mallorca at La Bombonera — A legendary, bustling historic bakery where kids will devour the sweet, powdered-sugar dusted, butter-toasted mallorca buns.
  • Paletas at Señor Paleta — Grab handcrafted, kid-sized gelato and fruit popsicles in fun shapes with flavors ranging from strawberry-kiwi to creamy coconut.
👨‍👩‍👧 Protect your little ones from the intense tropical sun by packing reef-safe sunscreen, wide-brimmed hats, and scheduling outdoor activities during the cooler morning hours.
