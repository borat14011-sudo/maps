Los Cabos

← back to travel board
Where the desert meets the sea, Los Cabos welcomes families with calm warm waters, dramatic rock formations, and rich marine life. Little ones can splash safely in protected coves, while parents soak in the vibrant Mexican culture and stunning coastal vistas.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

El Arco & Land's End Boat Tour

Take a glass-bottom boat tour to see the dramatic rock arch and lazy sea lions. Toddlers will love peering through the glass bottom to watch colorful fish swim by.

Morning / 2-3h

Santa Maria Beach

This protected, horseshoe-shaped bay has calm, gentle waves perfect for toddlers to splash safely. The soft sand is ideal for building castles, and there are clean public restrooms nearby.

Evening / 2h

San Jose del Cabo Gallery District

Stroll the stroller-friendly pedestrian streets of the historic district to admire colorful flags and street performers. Toddlers will enjoy the lively atmosphere and local ice cream shops.

Morning / 2-3h

Chileno Beach Tide Pools

Another incredibly calm, blue-flag beach where natural tide pools form at low tide. Toddlers can safely observe tiny crabs and small fish in the shallow, warm pools.

Saturday Morning / 2h

San Jose del Cabo Organic Market

Browse local organic treats, fresh juices, and handmade crafts in a relaxed, open-air park setting. Toddlers can run safely on the grassy lawns while parents sample local snacks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Wirikuta Botanical Cactus Garden

Wander through thousands of cactus species along flat, easy-to-navigate paved paths. It's a quiet, stroller-friendly oasis where toddlers can marvel at giant, whimsical plants.

Morning / 1h

Estuary of San Jose del Cabo

This protected freshwater sanctuary features a flat, shaded wooden boardwalk. It is an easy, stroller-accessible nature walk where toddlers can spot ducks, herons, and turtles.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Marina Cabo San Lucas Boardwalk

Walk along the bustling marina to watch luxury yachts and spot hungry sea lions swimming near the docks. The wide, flat pathway is completely stroller-friendly.

Afternoon / 1h

Plaza Amelia Wilkes

This charming, shaded town square in downtown Cabo San Lucas features a small, fenced playground and gazebo. It is the perfect low-key spot for toddlers to burn off energy.

Where to eat

  • Flora Farms — A lush, family-friendly organic farm restaurant where kids can play in the grass and visit the turtle pond.
  • Tacos Guss — A casual, bustling taco spot with fast service, handmade tortillas, and mild cheese quesadillas perfect for little palates.
  • The Office on the Beach — Dine right with your toes in the sand, allowing toddlers to play with beach toys while you wait for your food.
  • La Coyota — An enchanting courtyard restaurant offering colorful Mexican decor, plenty of space, and simple, delicious bean-and-cheese burritos.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to swimmable 'Blue Flag' beaches like Chileno or Santa Maria, as many coastal areas in Los Cabos have dangerously strong undertows. Renting a car with a pre-booked car seat is highly recommended for safe and easy travel between the two main towns.
