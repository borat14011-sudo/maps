Sun-soaked adventure where the desert meets the sea

Los Cabos

← back to travel board
Where the golden Baja desert crashes into the brilliant turquoise Sea of Cortez, Los Cabos offers a magical playground for families. From gentle, shallow bays perfect for tiny splashers to vibrant arts districts and close-up marine encounters, it is a destination that balances relaxation with rich Mexican culture. Parents and toddlers alike will find their rhythm amid swaying palms and warm, welcoming locals.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Glass-Bottom Boat Ride to El Arco

Sail past the dramatic Land's End rock formations to see sea lions basking in the sun. Toddlers will love peering through the glass bottom at the colorful tropical fish below without getting wet.

Morning / 3-4h

Snorkeling and Splashing at Chileno Beach

This Blue Flag beach features calm, shallow waters and tide pools teeming with tiny fish, making it the safest swimming spot in the area. Bring sand toys, as there is plenty of soft sand for building castles, though shade is limited.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Flora Farms Organic Playground

Nestled in the foothills of the Sierra de la Laguna, this lush organic farm offers beautiful gardens to stroll and a family-friendly turtle pond. Toddlers can run safely on the grassy lawns while parents enjoy the tranquil, car-free environment.

Thursday Evening / 2h

San José del Cabo Art Walk

Wander the pedestrian-friendly historic district filled with vibrant colors, live street music, and local art. The wide, stroller-friendly plaza is perfect for a relaxed evening stroll with a popsicle in hand.

Morning / 1-2h

Bird Watching at San José Estuary

A peaceful freshwater oasis where families can stroll along flat dirt paths to spot herons, egrets, and colorful butterflies. The quiet atmosphere and flat terrain make it an easy, stroller-accessible morning walk for little ones.

Morning / 2-3h

Exploring Santa Maria Bay

A horse-shoe-shaped beach protected by craggy cliffs, perfect for swimming and looking for seashells in the coarse pink sand. Toddlers can safely wade in the calm, protected shoreline under your watchful eye.

Midday / 1h

Dolphin Encounter at Cabo Dolphins

Little ones can get up close to friendly dolphins from a safe, shallow platform without needing to swim. It is a highly interactive experience where toddlers can touch, splash, and wave at these gentle marine mammals.

Morning / 1h

Exploring Mercado Municipal Hugo Chávez

Dive into local life at this bustling indoor market filled with colorful tropical fruits, artisanal toys, and friendly vendors. Toddlers will love the sensory explosion of smells and sights, and it is a great spot to grab a fresh mango smoothie.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Playtime at Plaza Amelia Wilkes

A charming, shady town square in downtown Cabo San Lucas featuring a small playground, gardens, and a gazebo. It is a perfect, low-key spot for toddlers to burn off energy while parents relax on the benches under the shade of massive trees.

Where to eat

  • Tacos Guss — A casual, high-energy taqueria serving incredible pastor tacos where kids can watch the trompo spin and toddlers are welcomed with warm smiles and simple quesadillas.
  • Los Tres Gallos — Dine in a romantic yet incredibly kid-friendly outdoor courtyard shaded by fruit trees, serving authentic Mexican dishes with plenty of space for toddlers to marvel at the colorful decorations.
  • La Lupita Taco & Mezcal — Famous for its gourmet tacos and lively courtyard, this spot offers a fun, noisy environment where a fussy toddler easily blends into the cheerful background music.
  • The Office on the Beach — Enjoy fresh seafood with your toes literally in the sand at Medano Beach, letting toddlers play with sand toys right at your table while you wait for your food.
👨‍👩‍👧 Always stick to designated swimmable beaches like Chileno or Santa Maria, as the undertow on the Pacific side of Los Cabos is extremely dangerous for children. Renting a sturdy, large-wheeled stroller is highly recommended for navigating the sandy paths and cobblestone streets of the historic districts.
