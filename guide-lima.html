Lima

← back to travel board
Lima surprises young families with its dramatic coastal cliffs, lush green parks, and an approachable high-end culinary scene. For a finance dad who appreciates efficiency and quality, neighborhoods like Miraflores and San Isidro offer clean, secure, and walkable streets perfect for stroller strolls. The city's gentle Pacific breezes temper the equatorial sun, making outdoor exploration easy on an infant's schedule. It is a sophisticated South American hub where luxury meets colonial history, and babies are warmly welcomed.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 to 60-minute ride via pre-arranged private transfer or Taxi Green from Jorge Chávez International Airport (LIM) to Miraflores/San Isidro hotels, ensuring a safe vehicle with an advance-requested car seat.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parque del Amor

Stroll along the clifftop Malecon to admire vibrant mosaic walls and sweeping views of the Pacific Ocean. The wide paved pathways are incredibly stroller-friendly, and there is plenty of grassy space to lay down a blanket for tummy time.

Evening / 1.5h

Circuito Mágico del Agua

This park features thirteen interactive, illuminated water fountains that put on a spectacular light show synchronized with music. Go for the early evening show when it is not too past bedtime, and bring a stroller cover to protect your little one from the mist.

Morning / 1.5h

Parque El Olivar

Walk among more than 1,600 century-old olive trees in this peaceful, upscale residential sanctuary. It is an ultra-quiet oasis perfect for getting your toddler to nap in the stroller while you watch local birds and turtles in the central pond.

Morning / 2h

Huaca Pucllana

Marvel at a massive 1,500-year-old clay pyramid built by the Lima culture right in the middle of the modern city. While the main pyramid climb is not stroller-accessible, the flat gravel paths around the base and the small farm area with llamas are great for toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Surquillo No. 1

Introduce your child to the sights and scents of exotic Amazonian fruits like chirimoya and lucuma at this bustling local market. Keep your baby in a carrier here rather than a stroller, as the narrow, lively aisles can get crowded with shoppers.

Morning / 2.5h

Museo Larco

Housed in an 18th-century mansion built over a 7th-century pyramid, this museum displays stunning pre-Columbian gold and pottery surrounded by vibrant gardens. The garden paths are stroller-accessible, and the on-site cafe offers a quiet, beautiful setting to feed the baby.

Afternoon / 2h

Malecon de Miraflores

Walk the six-mile clifftop strip that winds through manicured parks, past paragliders, and over scenic bridges. It is entirely paved, flat, and features several modern, fenced-in playgrounds like Parque Isaac Rabin where toddlers can swing and slide safely.

Morning / 3h

Parque de las Leyendas

This massive park combines a zoo featuring native Peruvian wildlife like penguins and jaguars with fifty actual archaeological ruins. The wide, paved paths are excellent for strollers, and there are plenty of shady picnic areas to rest.

Afternoon / 2h

Puente de los Suspiros

Explore Lima's artistic heart in Barranco with its colorful murals, historic architecture, and the famous Bridge of Sighs. Use a baby carrier for the steps leading down to the Bajada de Baños beach path, but enjoy the flat stroller paths around the central plaza.

🍽️ Where to eat

Isolina Taberna Peruana

Order the Seco de Asado de Tira (beef short rib stew); the portions are huge and served family-style. Go right when they open at 12:00 PM to beat the massive lines and secure a spacious table where a high chair easily fits.

Restaurante Huaca Pucllana

Sit on the outdoor terrace overlooking the illuminated ruins and order the classic Lomo Saltado. The ambient outdoor noise, spacious terrace layout, and spectacular views keep babies entertained without worrying about noise.

La Mar Cebichería

Gaston Acurio's legendary lunch-only spot serves the freshest Clasico Ceviche and whole fried fish. The bustling, high-ceilinged, open-air atmosphere naturally masks any toddler squeals, and the staff is incredibly accommodating to families.

Tanta (Larcomar)

Try the pastel de choclo (corn cake) or a classic chaufa rice dish at this casual upscale favorite. Located in the cliffside Larcomar mall, it offers easy elevator stroller access, clean changing tables, and a very welcoming family vibe.

Mangos

Enjoy a massive buffet breakfast or a refreshing passion fruit juice with panoramic Pacific ocean views. Its spacious layout comfortably accommodates strollers at the table, and the menu features familiar kid-friendly options alongside Peruvian classics.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Belmond Miraflores Park

Located right on the clifftop Malecon, this ultra-luxury hotel offers spacious suites, a heated rooftop pool, and complimentary high-end cribs. It is exceptionally family-friendly, offering baby-proofing kits and babysitting services upon request.

Country Club Lima Hotel

A historic, elegant oasis in quiet San Isidro that provides a peaceful, low-stress environment with a lovely outdoor pool and manicured gardens. The spacious guest rooms easily fit cribs, and the staff treats children like royalty.

Swissôtel Lima

Located in safe, walkable San Isidro, this high-end hotel features interconnected indoor/outdoor heated pools and large family suites. They provide top-tier wooden cribs, baby bath amenities, and are steps from the quiet El Olivar park.

👨‍👩‍👧 Sidewalks in Miraflores and San Isidro are generally well-paved, but a sturdy stroller with good suspension is highly recommended to navigate occasional high curbs and cobblestones. Always look for restaurants that open early for lunch (around 12:00 PM) to avoid peak Peruvian dining crowds and keep your toddler on their routine schedule. Traffic in Lima can be notoriously congested and chaotic, so plan your transit times carefully and always use trusted, pre-booked private drivers with car seats rather than hailing street cabs.
