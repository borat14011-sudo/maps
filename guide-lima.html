Lima

← back to travel board
Perched on dramatic Pacific cliffs, Lima blends ancient history with vibrant parks and world-class culinary delights. Families will love the sprawling green spaces, ocean breezes, and surprisingly stroller-friendly coastal boardwalks. It is a sensory-rich playground where colorful fountains and friendly alpacas delight travelers of all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~40-60 minutes via official airport taxi (such as Taxi Green) to the family-friendly beachfront hotels in Miraflores or San Isidro.

What to do

Evening / 2 hours

Parque de la Reserva (Magic Water Circuit)

Watch dazzling light and music shows that will leave kids spellbound, featuring interactive fountains where toddlers can safely splash and play. Bring a change of dry clothes for the little ones.

Late Afternoon / 1 hour

Parque del Amor (Love Park)

Stroll along the colorful mosaic walls inspired by Antoni Gaudí while enjoying panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean. The wide paved paths are perfect for stroller walks and watching paragliders float by.

Morning / 1.5 hours

Huaca Pucllana

Explore an ancient adobe pyramid right in the middle of Miraflores, complete with flat dirt paths that are easy for little legs to navigate. Toddlers will love seeing the native llamas and alpacas kept in the small onsite farm.

Morning / 2 hours

Larco Museum (Museo Larco)

Wander through beautiful, bougainvillea-filled gardens and admire thousands of years of pre-Columbian history. The lush outdoor garden cafe is highly welcoming and provides a tranquil spot for restless toddlers.

Afternoon / 2 hours

Malecon de Miraflores

Walk or bike along this continuous clifftop greenway featuring playgrounds, flower gardens, and stunning ocean views. Stop at the Faro de la Marina lighthouse park where toddlers can run freely on the grass.

Morning / 1 hour

Surquillo Market No. 1 (Mercado Surquillo No. 1)

Introduce your family to Peru's exotic fruits like sweet chirimoya and lucuma at this bustling local food market. Toddlers will be captivated by the colorful fruit displays and friendly vendors offering fresh tastes.

Morning / 1.5 hours

Plaza Mayor (Plaza de Armas)

Marvel at the grand yellow colonial architecture, the central bronze fountain, and the changing of the guard in Lima's historic heart. The wide pedestrianized plaza is great for letting toddlers stretch their legs away from traffic.

Afternoon / 1.5 hours

Parque El Olivar (The Olive Grove)

Relax in this serene forest of centuries-old olive trees located in the upscale San Isidro district. Toddlers will enjoy feeding the resident fish and spotting birds near the peaceful central pond.

Late Afternoon / 2 hours

Barranco District Street Art Walk

Walk through Lima's bohemian district to admire vibrant murals, colorful colonial houses, and the famous Bridge of Sighs. Keep a close hand on your toddler as you navigate the steps leading down to the ocean path.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tanta (Miraflores)

Order the comforting Arroz con Pollo (cilantro rice with chicken) or the family-friendly beef empanadas in a highly welcoming, casual setting.

Isolina Taberna Peruana

Get the massive, family-style portions of Seco de Asado de Tira (beef short ribs stewed in cilantro) which are perfect for sharing with kids.

La Lucha Sanguchería Criolla

Grab their legendary chicharrón (pork) or turkey sandwiches paired with crispy sweet potato fries and fresh-squeezed fruit juices.

El Bodegón

Try the classic, kid-friendly tallarines verdes (green pesto pasta with steak) or the comfort-food favorite pastel de papa.

4D Cafeteria Gelateria

Treat the family to premium artisan gelato with unique Peruvian flavors like lucuma, chirimoya, and classic chocolate.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to authorized airport taxis like Taxi Green or Taxi Direct to ensure a safe, hassle-free ride to your hotel. Keep in mind that many traditional sidewalks have steep, high curbs, so a lightweight, sturdy umbrella stroller is highly recommended over a bulky travel system.
