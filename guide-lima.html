Lima

← back to travel board
Perched on dramatic cliffs overlooking the Pacific, Lima blends pre-Inca history with modern South American luxury. The city's upscale districts of Miraflores and San Isidro offer a sophisticated oasis perfect for stylish parents seeking culinary adventures and ocean views. For little Mary Claire, the centerpiece of this coastal getaway is the stroller-friendly Malecón de Miraflores, a six-mile continuous greenway safe from traffic. Here, you can breeze past manicured gardens and paraglider launch sites with the refreshing ocean wind keeping the midday heat perfectly manageable.
🚕 Getting there: A private transfer from Jorge Chávez International Airport to the upscale hotel districts of Miraflores or San Isidro typically takes 45 to 60 minutes. The most seamless luxury option for traveling with an infant is a private Mercedes V-Class service arranged directly through your hotel concierge, pre-configured with a premium, sanitised rear-facing infant car seat so you can bypass chaotic local taxi queues.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Malecón de Miraflores

This six-mile cliffside boardwalk offers spectacular Pacific Ocean views, beautifully manicured parks, and wide, perfectly paved paths that are ideal for a smooth stroller ride. Mary Claire will love the gentle sea breezes, and the abundant shade structures near Parque del Amor provide the perfect spots for an outdoor bottle feed.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Parque El Olivar

Located in the quiet, upscale San Isidro district, this historic olive grove features over 1,600 ancient olive trees, flat shaded paths, and quiet ponds. It is a peaceful, whisper-quiet sanctuary away from the city traffic, perfect for putting the baby to sleep in the stroller while you enjoy the architecture.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Museo Larco

Housed in an 18th-century mansion built over a 7th-century pyramid, this museum displays pre-Columbian gold and pottery. The lush, flower-filled gardens have paved ramps making it stroller-accessible, and the tranquil open-air cafe is an ideal spot to relax under the shade of bougainvillea with a sleeping baby.

Evening 6:00 PM - 7:30 PM

Circuito Mágico del Agua

This spectacular water park holds the record for the largest fountain complex in the world, featuring illuminated water shows set to music. Visiting during the earliest evening session ensures it is not too crowded, and the paved walkways allow you to easily navigate the dazzling light displays with a stroller.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Huaca Pucllana

Explore this striking great clay pyramid built by the Lima culture around 500 AD, situated right in the heart of modern Miraflores. Because the paths are made of packed earth and gravel, skip the stroller and use a comfortable baby carrier to easily navigate the fascinating archaeological site together.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Barranco Plaza and Puente de los Suspiros

Stroll through Lima's bohemian district to admire historic architecture, colorful street murals, and the famous Bridge of Sighs. The morning hours are peaceful and uncrowded, allowing you to easily maneuver the paved streets before the afternoon crowds arrive, though some steps near the bridge require a carrier.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Plaza Mayor de Lima

The historic heart of Lima features the grand Government Palace, the Cathedral, and ornate colonial balconies. The grand stone-paved square is flat and highly accessible for strollers, making it easy to capture family photos in front of the grand bronze fountain without worrying about uneven terrain.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Calle Miguel Dasso

This highly exclusive, tree-lined shopping street in San Isidro is lined with luxury boutiques, artisanal cafes, and high-end bookstores. The wide, immaculate sidewalks are perfect for a relaxed stroller push, allowing you to browse upscale Peruvian goods and stop for an iced latte in a very baby-friendly environment.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Malecon de Barranco

A quieter, more artistic alternative to the Miraflores boardwalk, this cliffside path offers beautiful views of the ocean framed by historic Republican-era mansions. It is fully paved and incredibly peaceful, making it the perfect destination for a late afternoon stroll to watch the sunset while Mary Claire naps.

🍽️ Where to eat

Astrid y Gastón

Housed in the magnificent 300-year-old Casa Moreyra, this world-renowned restaurant serves refined Peruvian cuisine like suckling pig and local seafood. The spacious, sun-drenched courtyard is perfect for booking an early 7:00 PM seating, and the staff are incredibly accommodating with premium wooden highchairs.

La Mar Cebichería

Gastón Acurio's celebrated open-air lunch spot is famous for its fresh ceviche and grilled catch of the day. The bustling, high-energy atmosphere easily masks any baby babble or crying, and the spacious outdoor-covered seating easily accommodates strollers and highchairs right at your table.

Huaca Pucllana Restaurant

Dine on classic Peruvian dishes like lomo saltado right alongside the illuminated ancient ruins. Request an outdoor terrace table with space for your stroller; the ambient noise of the gentle outdoor heaters and soft breeze creates a calming environment for a baby during an early dinner.

Mangos Restaurant (Larcomar)

Perched on the cliffs of Miraflores inside the Larcomar shopping complex, this spot offers spectacular ocean views and classic international and Peruvian fare. It features a fully stroller-accessible terrace, easy highchair access, and a casual, welcoming vibe that makes family dining completely stress-free.

Tanta (San Isidro)

An upscale, family-friendly bistro serving comforting Peruvian classics like empanadas and pastel de choclo. It is highly regarded by local families for its exceptionally welcoming staff, clean highchairs, spacious diaper-changing stations, and outdoor terrace seating that is perfect for a relaxed family lunch.

$220-350/night

Located in the heart of San Isidro, this modern luxury hotel is incredibly well-equipped for babies. It features large, soundproofed family suites, complimentary high-quality cribs, a massive heated indoor-outdoor pool, and an executive club lounge that serves light bites perfect for feeding a hungry infant.

Belmond Miraflores Park

$300-400/night

Positioned directly on the cliffside boardwalk, this ultra-luxury hotel offers quick access to the stroller-friendly Malecón. They offer heated rooftop pools, complimentary high-end cribs, and large junior suites with heavy blackout curtains perfect for keeping Mary Claire's sleep schedule on track.
