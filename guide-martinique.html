What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Les Salines Beach (Sainte-Anne)

This iconic beach offers miles of soft white sand and calm, shallow water shaded by leaning coconut palms. It's the ultimate spot for toddlers to splash safely and build sandcastles.

Morning / 2h

Jardin de Balata (Fort-de-France)

A magical botanical garden filled with vibrant tropical flowers, giant bamboo, and friendly hummingbirds. The gravel paths can be tricky for strollers, so using a baby carrier is ideal for toddlers here.

Morning / 1-2h

Grand Marché (Fort-de-France)

A covered market bursting with sweet tropical fruits, colorful spices, and local crafts. Toddlers will love the sensory explosion of smells and sights, and it is easy to navigate during quieter mid-morning hours.

Morning / 2-3h

Anse Dufour (Les Anses-d'Arlet)

A golden sand cove famous for the sea turtles that swim close to the shore. The waters are exceptionally calm, allowing toddlers to safely peer into the shallows with a baby float.

Afternoon / 2h

Le Domaine de l'Émeraude (Le Morne-Rouge)

A lush nature park featuring easy, sensory-rich walking trails through the rainforest. Toddlers can explore interactive wooden displays and touch various leaf textures along the flat paths.

Morning / 2h

La Savane des Esclaves (Les Trois-Îlets)

An outdoor museum showcasing traditional Creole lifestyle, gardens, and history. The spacious grassy walkways are great for toddlers to wander safely while looking at reconstructed historic huts.

Afternoon / 2h

Habitation Clément (Le François)

A historic estate with sprawling botanical gardens and an open-air sculpture park. The wide, manicured lawns give toddlers plenty of room to run around safely while parents enjoy the art.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Anse Mitan (Les Trois-Îlets)

A vibrant beach featuring calm waters, soft sand, and close proximity to kid-friendly cafes. It is a fantastic, low-stress option for swimming with toddlers and watching the ferry boats pass.

Morning / 2h

Zoo de Martinique (Le Carbet)

Built amidst the historic ruins of a sugar refinery, this zoo features monkeys, jaguars, and colorful birds. The entire path is a wooden boardwalk, making it highly stroller-friendly and easy for toddler legs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Petibonum

Get the fresh grilled fish and accras de morue right at your beach table while toddlers play in the sand at your feet in Le Carbet.

Ti Sable

Order the traditional Creole lobster or chicken colombo in this beautifully shaded, beachfront restaurant in Les Anses-d'Arlet.

Le Kano

Savor local fruit smoothies and passionfruit pork ribs in a relaxed, colorful setting right next to the water in Les Trois-Îlets.

Chez JuJu

Try the fresh coconut sorbet and Creole snapper skewers at this highly casual, family-friendly beachside spot in Sainte-Anne.

La Table de Mamy Nounou

Indulge in the traditional lambi (conch) stew or coconut flan while enjoying panoramic ocean views from the terrace in Tartane.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat, as public transport is limited and driving is the best way to access calm, toddler-friendly beaches. Pack plenty of high-SPF, reef-safe sunscreen and a lightweight baby carrier, as some natural sights have stairs or uneven forest terrain.
