What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Jardin de Balata

This world-renowned botanical garden features paved, stroller-accessible walkways winding through thousands of tropical plants. The dense canopy provides excellent shade for Mary Claire, and the morning air is beautifully cool and calm.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Habitation Clément

A historic estate and modern art foundation with immaculate, flat lawns and wide gravel paths suitable for all-terrain strollers. Parents can enjoy a world-class rum tasting in the open-air pavilion while baby naps in the shaded gardens.

Early morning 8-10:30am / 2.5h

Grande Anse des Salines

Widely considered Martinique's finest beach, this postcard-perfect stretch features exceptionally calm waters and coconut palms leaning over the sand to offer natural shade. Arrive early to secure a prime spot close to the parking area for easy gear hauling.

Morning 9:30-11am / 1.5h

La Savane des Esclaves

An open-air museum detailing the island's history, featuring flat, grassy paths under a lush canopy. This tranquil outdoor setting is ideal for a gentle carrier walk with Mary Claire during her mid-morning wake window.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Château Dubuc

Set on the scenic Caravelle Peninsula, these atmospheric oceanfront ruins offer a smooth, stroller-friendly main path to the spectacular viewpoint. The constant Atlantic breeze keeps the site beautifully cool and bug-free for the baby.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Anse Mitan

A sheltered beach in Les Trois-Îlets featuring remarkably calm, shallow waters with no strong currents. A flat, paved beachfront promenade runs parallel, letting you seamlessly transition from a stroller walk to a gentle dip in the sea.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Le Domaine de l'Émeraude

An eco-park in the lush northern rainforest featuring flat, sensory wooden boardwalks designed for easy stroller access. The refreshing mountain air and gentle rustle of giant ferns provide a soothing sensory experience for Mary Claire.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

La Savane Park

This elegant 12-acre park in the heart of Fort-de-France features wide paved promenades, manicured lawns, and towering royal palms. It is the perfect spot for an afternoon stroller walk before catching the express ferry back to Trois-Îlets.

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Anse Dufour

A sheltered, golden-sand cove famous for calm waters and resident sea turtles swimming close to shore. Arrive early to sit under the sea grape trees, providing Mary Claire with natural sun protection while you take turns snorkeling.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Zandoli

Located inside La Suite Villa, this artistic fine-dining terrace overlooks the bay. They offer highchairs, spacious seating layout to accommodate infant carriers, and a quiet, breezy atmosphere perfect for an early 7:00 PM dinner reservation.

Le Petibonum

A refined beachfront institution in Le Carbet where tables are set directly on the sand. The soothing sound of the waves provides natural white noise for Mary Claire, and the staff is incredibly welcoming to young families.

Le Belem

This elegant French-Creole restaurant features a quiet, spacious outdoor deck. The gentle ocean breeze and distant waves create an ambient hum that helps soothe infants, while parents enjoy gourmet seafood with impeccable service.

Ti Sable

A chic, bohemian-style beach club on the calm shores of Anse d'Arlet. With large shaded daybeds, manicured grass lawns, and early lunch seatings, it is exceptionally convenient for dining with a baby in a relaxed, open-air setting.

La Table de Marcel

An upscale, air-conditioned culinary venue by Michelin-starred chef Marcel Ravin. Book the earliest dinner seating to enjoy a quiet, refined dining experience with spacious booth seating ideal for a sleeping baby in a bassinet.
