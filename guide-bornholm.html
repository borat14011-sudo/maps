Bornholm

← back to travel board
Bornholm offers a sophisticated but utterly relaxed island escape where dramatic granite cliffs meet powder-soft Baltic beaches. For a young family, the island is remarkably easy to navigate, featuring stroller-friendly seaside paths and a slow-paced, welcoming Scandinavian culture. Parents can indulge in world-class craft breweries and artisanal smokehouses while their toddler safely explores pristine nature and quiet historic harbor towns.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min drive via rental car from Bornholm Airport (RNN) to the Rønne hotels, or ~30 mins to Gudhjem.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Hammershus Castle Ruins

Scandinavia's largest medieval fortress ruin perched dramatically over the sea. The modern visitor center has a smooth, stroller-friendly exhibition and boardwalk, but you should use a baby carrier for the steep dirt paths leading into the ruins themselves.

Afternoon / 2h

NaturBornholm

An interactive natural history museum featuring live crocodiles, dinosaur models, and hands-on geology exhibits. The entire facility is fully stroller-accessible with wide, flat ramps and plenty of sensory-rich displays that will fascinate a toddler.

Morning / 3-4h

Brændesgårdshaven

A nostalgic amusement park and zoo featuring lush gardens, rowboats, and friendly farm animals. The wide gravel paths are very easy to navigate with a stroller, and there are many quiet, shaded grassy lawns perfect for a picnic and baby nap.

Afternoon / 2h

Dueodde Beach

Famous for its ultra-fine white sand that was historically used in hourglasses. A long, sturdy wooden boardwalk stretches from the parking lot through the pine trees directly to the beach, allowing you to push a stroller right up to the water's edge.

Morning / 1h

Østerlars Rundkirke

The largest and oldest of Bornholm's iconic whitewashed round churches, dating back to 1160. The exterior gravel grounds are highly stroller-friendly, but the interior stairs are very narrow, so plan to take turns babywearing if you want to climb to the upper loft.

Morning / 2h

Landbrugsmuseet Melstedgård

A living history farm museum where toddlers can see historic breeds of horses, cows, and pigs in an authentic setting. The open-air farmyard paths are wide and stroller-friendly, and the atmosphere is wonderfully slow-paced.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Bornholms Rovfugleshow

An educational bird of prey show where eagles, owls, and falcons fly close overhead. The seating area is a flat, grassy outdoor lawn with plenty of space to park your stroller and let your toddler watch the birds in a relaxed setting.

Morning / 1-2h

Bornholms Sommerfuglepark

A tropical glasshouse filled with over 1,000 brightly colored, free-flying butterflies, parrots, and exotic plants. The indoor pathways are flat and smooth for strollers, and the warm, humid environment is incredibly soothing for infants.

Morning / 1-2h

Ekkodalen

Bornholm's longest rift valley, famous for its dramatic rock walls and impressive natural echo. A well-maintained, flat wooden boardwalk runs along the valley floor, making it an incredibly scenic and stroller-friendly nature walk.

🍽️ Where to eat

Nordbornholms Røgeri

Order the traditional 'Sol over Gudhjem' (smoked herring with raw egg yolk and chives). The restaurant features a fantastic outdoor patio right on the rocks with a built-in sandbox to keep your toddler safely occupied while you eat.

Det Røde Pakhus

Order the pan-fried Baltic cod with seasonal vegetables. This spacious, upscale-casual restaurant has wide aisles easily accommodating strollers, high chairs, and a bustling, family-friendly evening atmosphere.

Svaneke Bryghus Restaurant

Order the slow-roasted Bornholm pork belly paired with an organic craft pilsner. This lively, spacious microbrewery has a highly welcoming family vibe, fast service, and plenty of room for baby gear.

Hallegård Gårdbutik og Café

Order the artisanal charcuterie board made with organic local meats. This rustic farm cafe features a massive, safe grassy courtyard where your toddler can crawl or toddle freely while you enjoy lunch in the sun.

Fru Petersen's Café

Order the traditional Danish 'kagebord' (an historic cake buffet). The nostalgic, retro living-room interior is incredibly cozy, high chairs are readily available, and there is a lovely shaded garden.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Green Solution House (GSH)

An award-winning, eco-friendly hotel in Rønne with spacious, modern family rooms, private patios, and beautiful forest trails surrounding the property that are perfect for quiet stroller walks.

Fredensborg Badehotel

A classic, upscale seaside hotel offering beautiful sea-view rooms, manicured gardens, high-end cribs on request, and direct access to a quiet coastal path ideal for soothing an infant to sleep.

Melsted Badehotel

A chic, recently renovated boutique hotel set right on the beach with expansive sandy lawns where your toddler can play safely, plus highly accommodating staff who can easily arrange baby monitors and cribs.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent an estate-sized station wagon or SUV with a pre-booked rear-facing baby seat directly at Bornholm Airport for seamless transport. Avoid bringing a lightweight city umbrella stroller; instead, opt for a robust stroller with large, all-terrain wheels to handle the historic cobblestone streets in Svaneke and Gudhjem. Local supermarkets like Netto and Meny are exceptionally well-stocked with organic Danish baby food, diapers, and sensitive formula, so there is no need to overpack essentials.
