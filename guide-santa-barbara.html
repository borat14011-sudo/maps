The city's relaxed coastal luxury ensures that dining and exploring with baby Mary Claire feels seamless and upscale. What to do

Morning 9-11am

Santa Barbara Zoo

A boutique, beautifully landscaped zoo with wide, stroller-friendly paved paths, abundant shade trees, and quiet grassy areas perfect for Mary Claire's afternoon bottle.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Stearns Wharf

California's oldest working wharf features a wide, flat wooden boardwalk that is highly accessible for strollers and offers cool ocean breezes to keep baby comfortable.

Morning 9-11am

Santa Barbara Botanic Garden

Explore paved, sensory-rich paths through native redwood groves; the quiet atmosphere is excellent for lullabies and stroller naps.

Morning 10am-12pm

Ganna Walska Lotusland

An exquisite, tranquil estate garden offering peaceful, shaded walking paths; bookings must be secured in advance to enjoy this quiet botanical sanctuary.

Afternoon 2-4pm

Alice Keck Park Memorial Garden

This sprawling park features sensory gardens, a large turtle pond, and flat, shaded grassy lawns perfect for spreading out a blanket so Mary Claire can crawl.

Evening sunset 5-7pm

Shoreline Park

A scenic clifftop park featuring a long, paved walking path overlooking the ocean, ideal for a sunset stroll with an UPPAbaby stroller.

Midday 11am-1pm

Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History

Features quiet indoor galleries to escape the midday heat, plus a shaded outdoor creek pathway that is peaceful and stroller-accessible.

Morning 10am-12pm

MOXI, The Wolf Museum of Exploration + Innovation

A modern, air-conditioned hands-on museum featuring interactive sensory exhibits and a quiet rooftop deck with panoramic city views.

Late afternoon 3-5pm

Arroyo Burro Beach County Park

Also known as Hendry's Beach, this spot features flat, hard-packed sand near the tide line that is perfect for a gentle, low-impact beach walk with baby.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Lark

Savor their signature crispy Brussels sprouts with dates in the spacious outdoor patio; early dining starts at 5pm which perfectly aligns with infant bedtimes.

Brophy Bros.

Famous for New England clam chowder and fresh oysters, this lively harbor-side spot easily masks baby noises while offering highchairs and patio seating.

Jeannine's Restaurant & Bakery

An upscale breakfast favorite offering gourmet French toast and outdoor patio seating where strollers can be parked directly at the table.

Lure Fish House

Highly accommodating to families with spacious booths, highchairs, and a fantastic charbroiled seafood menu that keeps service quick and efficient.

La Super-Rica Taco

A legendary local taco stand with a casual, open-air patio; arrive early at 11am to beat the crowd and secure a shaded table for the family.
