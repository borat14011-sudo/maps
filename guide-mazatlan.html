What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Plaza Machado

The historic heart of Old Mazatlán, lined with French and Spanish-influenced architecture. Highly stroller-accessible, early mornings are delightfully quiet and shaded for a peaceful walk with Mary Claire before the heat sets in.

Late morning 10:30 AM - 12:30 PM / 2h

Observatorio 1873

A meticulously restored historical complex featuring terraced gardens, a whale museum, and a funicular ride. The funicular is smooth and infant-safe, though we recommend a baby carrier for navigating the garden stairs.

Sunset 5:30 PM - 7:00 PM / 1.5h

Malecón de Mazatlán

One of the world's longest scenic boardwalks. The wide, paved pedestrian pathway is absolutely perfect for high-end strollers, offering pristine ocean views and refreshing sea breezes, especially during the golden sunset hour.

Morning half-day 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM / 4h

Isla de las Piedras (Stone Island)

A tranquil coconut grove peninsula with exceptionally calm, wave-free waters. Hire a private water taxi to access the beach, which is perfect for Mary Claire's first gentle dip in the ocean under a shaded private palapa.

Day trip 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM / 5h

El Quelite

A charming, colorful colonial village situated about 30 minutes from the city. Walk past historic homes and cobblestones using a baby carrier, enjoying the slow-paced rural charm and vibrant, photogenic backdrops.

Morning 8:30 AM - 10:30 AM / 2h

Playa Bruja

A scenic, peaceful stretch of beach north of the bustling tourist zones. Perfect for a quiet morning blanket setup, allowing Mary Claire to play in the soft sand away from crowds under a premium UV beach tent.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:30 PM / 2h

Gran Acuario Mazatlán Mar de Cortés

A stunning, world-class modern aquarium dedicated to the Sea of Cortez. Fully air-conditioned with ultra-smooth stroller ramps and massive, floor-to-ceiling viewing panels that will completely mesmerize your infant.

Morning trip 8:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 4h

Las Labradas

A unique, protected archaeological beach site home to ancient petroglyphs. The rocky shoreline requires a sturdy baby carrier and solid sun protection, but offers a breathtaking, quiet coastal walk for history-loving parents.

Late afternoon 4:30 PM - 5:15 PM / 45m

El Clavadista

Watch daring cliff divers plunge into the rocky ocean depths from the oceanfront platform. Keep a comfortable distance on the wide paved sidewalk to protect Mary Claire's sensitive ears from sudden, loud crowd cheers.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Presidio Cocina de México

Upscale contemporary Mexican dining set within a gorgeous, ruined 19th-century mansion courtyard. The lush open-air garden terrace provides natural ambient white noise, spacious table gaps, and highly attentive service for families during early dinner seatings.

Casa 46

Sophisticated fine dining overlooking the historic Plaza Machado. Request a balcony table during their early dinner service to enjoy gourmet seafood while Mary Claire is lulled by the distant plaza musicians; highchairs are available upon request.

Pancho's Restaurant

An iconic, upscale beachfront institution in the Golden Zone. Indulge in fresh coconut shrimp on the covered oceanfront deck, where the soothing sound of the waves masks baby noises, and the staff is famously welcoming to families.

El Mesón de los Laureanos

Located in El Quelite, this renowned restaurant offers impeccable traditional Sinaloan cuisine in a historic hacienda-style courtyard. Exceptionally family-friendly, offering spacious open-air tables and vibrant, stimulating decor for an infant.

Hector's Bistro

A chic, upscale European-style bistro located in Centro Histórico. Excellent for a relaxed lunch or early dinner of artisanal handmade pastas; the dining room is highly accommodating for stroller placement next to your table.
