Mazatlán

← back to travel board
Nestled along Mexico's Pacific coast, Mazatlán beautifully blends historic colonial charm with vibrant beachfront energy. Families can easily explore the stroller-friendly historic center, splash in gentle ocean waves, and ride in open-air fun taxis. It is a warm, welcoming, and budget-friendly destination perfect for travelers of all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min taxi or private shuttle to the Golden Zone beach hotels, or ~25 min to the Historic Center.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

The Malecón Boardwalk

Stroll along one of the world's longest boardwalks to enjoy sea breezes, street performers, and stunning sunsets. It is completely stroller-friendly, and toddlers will love watching the brave cliff divers at El Clavadista.

Morning / 2-3h

Gran Acuario Mazatlán Mar de Cortés

This world-class, modern aquarium features immersive marine exhibits, touch tanks, and a massive ocean wave simulator. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant jellyfish tanks and the dedicated interactive kids' zones.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Norte Beach

A lively local beach with gentle waves perfect for splashing, sandcastle building, and watching traditional fishing boats bring in their daily catch. Bring a beach umbrella, as natural shade is limited for little ones.

Evening / 1-2h

Plazuela Machado

The beating heart of the historic center, this pedestrian-friendly square is surrounded by colorful colonial buildings, gardens, and live music. It’s an easy, vehicle-free spot for toddlers to safely run around while parents sip coffee.

Early Morning / 1-2h

El Faro Lighthouse Hike

Climb up to one of the world's highest natural lighthouses for breathtaking 360-degree views of the coastline. The trail has stairs, so use a baby carrier instead of a stroller, and go early to beat the heat.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Pino Suárez

Immerse your senses in this bustling historic market packed with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and fresh juices. Toddlers will love the vibrant sights and sampling sweet mangoes, but keep them close in the busy aisles.

Day Trip / 4-5h

Isla de la Piedra (Stone Island)

Take a short, fun panga boat ride to this tranquil peninsula lined with gentle, shallow waters and swaying coconut palms. It’s the ultimate stress-free beach day for parents and toddlers, with beachside restaurants offering shady palapas.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Central Mazatlán

A lush urban park featuring scenic walking paths, lagoons, and a fantastic, modern playground for children. It’s the perfect, shaded retreat to let toddlers burn off some energy on the swings and slides.

Morning / 3-4h

Deer Island Catamaran Cruise

Board a family-friendly catamaran to this protected island nature reserve with crystal-clear, wave-free waters. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow tide pools while older family members paddleboard or snorkel.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Presidio

Dine in a stunning, plant-filled historic ruin courtyard and order the grilled octopus or the refined takes on traditional Mexican slow-cooked pork.

Pastelería y Restaurante Panamá

A beloved Mazatlán institution offering an extensive, kid-approved menu where you must order the famous banana cream pie and savory chilaquiles.

La Concha

Enjoy beachfront dining with your toes in the sand while devouring fresh shrimp ceviche and classic fish tacos as the kids play nearby.

Mariscos El Torito

Head to this lively, casual spot for outstanding local seafood, especially the Sinaloa-style aguachile and cheese-stuffed shrimp.

Pedro y Lola

Sit outside on the vibrant plaza and enjoy live music alongside delicious coconut shrimp and traditional Mexican fajitas.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for 'Pulmonías'—the open-air, golf-cart-style taxis—which are an absolute blast for toddlers and provide a breezy, fun way to navigate the coast. Always pack plenty of mosquito repellent and high-SPF sunscreen, and stick to bottled water for the entire family to keep everyone healthy.
