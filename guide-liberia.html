Liberia

← back to travel board
Affectionately known as 'La Ciudad Blanca' for its historic white-walled houses, Liberia serves as the perfect sun-kissed basecamp for family expeditions. From splash-friendly majestic waterfalls to close encounters with rescued wildlife, this Guanacaste gem blends rich Costa Rican culture with effortless natural wonders. Parents will love the relaxed pace, walkable plazas, and easy access to both pristine beaches and active volcanoes.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min drive via official red taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to downtown Liberia hotels, or ~35-45 min to nearby Gulf of Papagayo family resorts

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Llanos de Cortés Waterfall

A stunning, wide waterfall with a shallow sandy pool perfect for wading and swimming. The short, paved path down is highly manageable for toddlers, and the cool spray is incredibly refreshing.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Ponderosa Adventure Park

An African-style safari in the heart of Guanacaste where kids can safely feed giraffes from open-air vehicles. Toddlers will love the gentle animal encounters and the shaded swimming pool area.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Central

The vibrant heart of the city where kids can chase pigeons and watch locals while enjoying sweet shaved ice. It is completely stroller-friendly and surrounded by beautiful historic buildings.

Morning / 3-4h

Rincón de la Vieja National Park (Las Pailas Sector)

See bubbling mud pots, steaming fumaroles, and mini-geysers along an easy-to-walk, shaded loop trail. Toddlers will be fascinated by the sights, but keep them close on the boardwalks.

Morning / 2-3h

Sector Santa Maria Cold Springs

A quieter sector of the national park offering relaxing cold springs and shallow streams for splashing. It is a peaceful, uncrowded spot for a picnic with toddlers who love water play.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal de Liberia

A bustling indoor market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and friendly vendors. Toddlers will love tasting sweet fresh papaya and watching the lively daily commerce.

Morning / 2-3h

Santa Rosa National Park (La Casona)

Explore Costa Rica's rich history and dry forest ecology at this historic ranch turned museum. The paved paths around the main hacienda are great for strollers, and you might spot wild monkeys.

Afternoon / 1h

Museo de Guanacaste

Housed in a historic former city jail, this museum offers kid-friendly cultural exhibits and a spacious courtyard to run around. It is a quick, educational stop that stays cool in the midday heat.

Late Afternoon / 30m-1h

La Agonía Church

A beautiful 19th-century whitewashed colonial church with a wide, grassy plaza in front. It is the perfect spot for toddlers to safely burn off energy on the grass while parents admire the architecture.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café de Posada

Get the traditional Costa Rican casado with sweet plantains and a fresh, toddler-friendly fruit smoothie.

Restaurante Tierra Mar

Try the fresh sea bass ceviche for adults and the crispy, kid-approved chicken fingers with french fries.

Sabor Porteño

Order the comforting arroz con pollo (chicken rice) which is flavorful, mild, and a massive hit with toddlers.

Panadería y Repostería Musmanni

Grab some fresh 'pan de queso' (cheese bread) and sweet pastries for an easy, cheap, and delicious family picnic snack.

El Callejón

Enjoy artisanal wood-fired pizzas in an open-air garden setting that is highly welcoming to busy, active toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Carry plenty of high-SPF sunscreen and insect repellent, as the Guanacaste heat is intense and natural shade can be scarce. Opt for a rental car with a sturdy car seat if you plan to explore the national parks, as local shuttle schedules can be unpredictable for toddler nap times.
