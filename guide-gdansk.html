Fairytale streets and sandy beaches on the Baltic

Gdansk

← back to travel board
Gdańsk captivates families with its colorful Hanseatic architecture, rich maritime history, and easy access to the soft sands of the Baltic Sea. Kids will love climbing aboard historic ships, exploring interactive science museums, and hunting for glittering pieces of amber along the shoreline. It is a highly walkable, budget-friendly Polish gem that perfectly balances cultural discovery with relaxed seaside play.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Uber from Gdańsk Lech Wałęsa Airport to the city center, or ~35 min to the beachside hotels in Jelitkowo

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Neptune's Fountain & Long Market

Marvel at the bronze sea god fountain and the pastel-colored merchant houses along this vibrant, pedestrian-only boulevard. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles and watching street performers in the wide, car-free square.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Hevelianum

Set inside a 19th-century military fort on a hill, this interactive science center features hands-on exhibits designed for curious young minds. There is a dedicated play area for toddlers and the surrounding park offers stroller-friendly pathways with panoramic city views.

Morning / 3-4h

Jelitkowo Beach & Park

Build sandcastles on the soft Baltic coast and stroll through the adjacent shaded park filled with excellent modern playgrounds. The shallow, calm shoreline is perfect for little toddlers to safely splash around.

Afternoon / 1-2h

SS Sołdek & Motława Riverwalk

Stroll along the wooden boardwalk of the Motława River to view the historic steamship SS Sołdek and the medieval port crane. Toddlers will be fascinated watching the footbridges dynamically swing open to let yachts pass.

Morning / 2h

Oliwski Park

This beautifully manicured historic park features peaceful weeping willows, a quiet palm house, and duck ponds that toddlers adore. The smooth, flat paths are ideal for strollers and offer a serene escape from the urban core.

Morning / 1h

Hala Targowa

Explore this bustling indoor market hall where locals shop for fresh Baltic berries, local cheeses, and pastries. The lower level reveals preserved medieval ruins, which older kids will find incredibly cool.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Oliwa Zoo

Nestled in a lush forest valley, this expansive zoo is one of the largest in Poland and home to penguins, giraffes, and elephants. Wooden pull-carts are available to rent at the entrance, making it easy to transport tired toddlers.

Morning / 1h

St. Mary's Church Astronomical Clock

Step inside one of the largest brick churches in the world to watch the whimsical 15th-century astronomical clock with its moving wooden figures. While the tower climb is too steep for toddlers, the vast, cool interior is highly stroller-accessible.

Afternoon / 2h

Galeon Lew Boat Cruise

Embark on a fun family boat tour along the river to Westerplatte aboard a stylized 17th-century wooden pirate ship. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the costumed pirate crew and the gentle sea breeze on the open deck.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pierogarnia Mandu Centrum

Order a plate of traditional boiled pierogi stuffed with sweet summer berries and sweet cream for the kids, and savory wild mushroom pierogi for the adults.

Pyra Bar

This casual, potato-themed spot is perfect for families; try the crispy potato pancakes or baked potato wedges served with mild, creamy herb dip.

Billy's American Restaurant

Located right on the picturesque Motława riverfront, this lively diner offers a great kids' menu with mini-burgers, fries, and thick milkshakes.

Słony Spichlerz

A modern, family-friendly food hall on Granary Island where everyone can choose their own meal, ranging from wood-fired Neapolitan pizza to Asian noodles.

Cukiernia Pellowski

Stop by this historic local bakery for a classic Polish sweet bun filled with fresh wild blueberries (jagodzianka) or a warm, soft pretzel.

👨‍👩‍👧 Gdańsk’s Old Town features historic, bumpy cobblestones, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier. Always keep small Polish Złoty coins on hand, as many public beach and park restrooms require coin payment to enter.
