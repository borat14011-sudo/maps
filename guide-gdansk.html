What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Sopot Pier (Molo)

Take a gentle morning walk on Europe's longest wooden pier. The wide, flat wooden planks are perfectly stroller-accessible, and the refreshing, iodine-rich sea breeze is wonderfully soothing for baby Mary Claire.

Late Morning 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM / 2h

Gdańsk Old Town (Royal Route)

Walk the grand Royal Route down Długa Street. While gorgeous, the historic cobblestones require a sturdy stroller suspension; baby Mary Claire will love the gentle hum of street musicians and the colorful historic facades.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 2.5h

Museum of the Second World War

An architecturally stunning and deeply moving museum. It features wide, smooth concrete ramps and spacious elevators that make navigating with a premium stroller completely effortless.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Opera Leśna (Forest Opera)

This legendary open-air amphitheater is nestled in the quiet, shaded woods of Sopot. The paved pathways leading through the surrounding forest are perfect for a peaceful baby nap under a cool canopy of green trees.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Sopot Museum (Muzeum Sopotu)

A beautifully restored historic villa located just steps from the beach. The quiet, uncrowded interior and beautiful beachside gardens provide a serene, low-sensory environment for a relaxed family outing.

Day Trip 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM / 6h

Malbork Castle (Zamek w Malborku)

Take a day trip to the world's largest brick castle. Book a private Mercedes transfer with an infant car seat; the castle grounds are grand and dramatic, though we highly recommend a high-end baby carrier over a stroller due to historical stone paths.

Afternoon 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM / 1h

Tightrope Walker Statue (Rzeźba Rybak Pierwszy)

Admire this whimsical balancing statue suspended high above the ground on Sopot's main pedestrian promenade. It is a fantastic, traffic-free walking zone with plenty of shade and nearby luxury boutiques.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM / 2h

Jelitkowo Park and Beach

Avoid the crowded central sands and enjoy a tranquil stroll through the paved walkways of Jelitkowo Park. The stroller paths run parallel to the calm Baltic shoreline, offering peaceful views and plenty of clean, shaded grassy lawns.

Afternoon 2:30 PM - 3:30 PM / 1h

St. Mary's Church (Bazylika Mariacka)

Marvel at the soaring vaults of this massive Gothic church in Gdańsk. The main nave is fully flat and accessible for strollers, offering a cool, quiet sanctuary where baby Mary Claire can relax away from the midday sun.

🍽️ Where to eat

White Marlin

An elegant, beachside dining institution in Sopot featuring sophisticated seafood and a gorgeous, wind-sheltered outdoor terrace. They happily accommodate families with luxury highchairs and spacious table layouts for strollers during the early dinner seating.

Goldwasser Restaurant

Located right on the picturesque Motława River in Gdańsk, this upscale restaurant offers fine dining on a waterfront terrace. The soothing ambient sound of passing boats is excellent for baby, and the service is exceptionally welcoming to high-end families.

Polskie Smaki (at Sheraton Sopot)

Specializing in refined Kashubian and traditional Polish cuisine with direct views of the Sopot Pier. Exceptionally family-friendly with robust highchair availability, a dedicated infant-friendly menu, and a quiet, spacious dining room.

Manna 68

A highly rated, sophisticated vegan and vegetarian restaurant in Gdańsk's historic center. The spacious, modern dining room easily accommodates premium strollers, and the peaceful, health-focused atmosphere is perfect for a quiet lunch.

Wiśniewski (Gdańsk)

An upscale local favorite known for its signature artisanal cherry liqueur. Parents can enjoy a refreshing, ice-cold cherry spritz on the outdoor street-side terrace while baby Mary Claire enjoys a peaceful afternoon nap in her stroller.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sofitel Grand Sopot

$250-400/night

The pinnacle of luxury on the Polish coast, this historic 5-star hotel features a private beach, a heated indoor swimming pool, and beautifully appointed suites with blackout curtains. They provide high-end wooden cribs on request and are located steps from the Sopot Pier.

Sheraton Sopot Hotel

$220-350/night

Perfectly situated right next to the beach and the pier, this 5-star property offers spacious family suites, an excellent indoor pool, and premium crib setups. Its private, wind-sheltered terrace provides a serene seaside escape for parents traveling with young infants.

Hilton Gdansk

$200-330/night

Located in the heart of Gdańsk's Old Town along the Motława River, this hotel boasts a stunning indoor rooftop pool and exceptionally large family suites. They offer top-tier infant amenities, excellent cribs, and quiet, soundproofed rooms for flawless baby naps.

👨‍👩‍👧 While Sopot's seaside boardwalk is beautifully paved and ideal for luxury strollers, Gdańsk's Old Town is heavily cobblestoned, making an all-terrain stroller or a high-end baby carrier essential.
