Gdańsk offers a sophisticated maritime escape that beautifully balances Hanseatic history with the fresh, therapeutic air of the Baltic Coast. For a young family, the city's flat waterfront promenades, expansive green parks, and pristine sandy beaches offer stress-free navigation with a stroller. It is a destination where you can enjoy high-end dining and rich culture without the chaotic crowds of Western Europe's larger capitals.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-minute private Mercedes van transfer from Gdańsk Lech Wałęsa Airport (GDN) to the city center or ~25 minutes to the seaside hotels in Sopot.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Park Oliwski

A peaceful French-style historic garden featuring paved, stroller-friendly paths, whispering streams, and a massive glass palm house. It is the perfect spot for a quiet morning nap walk while parents admire the perfectly manicured hornbeam alleys.

Morning / 1-2h

Molo w Sopocie (Sopot Pier)

Europe's longest wooden pier offers wide, smooth timber planks that make pushing a stroller a breeze while treating you to clean, iodine-rich Baltic air. Go early in the morning to avoid the crowds and let your toddler spot the swans gathering near the shoreline.

Morning or Afternoon / 3h

Miejski Ogród Zoologiczny Wybrzeża (Gdańsk Zoo)

Nestled in a lush, forested valley in Oliwa, this massive zoo features wide, paved paths and plenty of shaded resting spots beneath ancient trees. Toddlers will love the dedicated petting zoo area, and parents will appreciate the seamless stroller accessibility throughout the entire park.

Morning / 1-2h

Długi Targ (The Long Market)

Walk the historic Royal Way past Neptune's Fountain, noting the smooth granite center slabs specifically designed to ease stroller transit over the bumpy cobblestones. It is best visited in the early morning before the tour groups arrive, allowing your toddler to safely toddle around the wide-open plaza.

Late Afternoon / 30m

AmberSky Ferris Wheel

Located on Ołowianka Island, this modern observation wheel features fully enclosed, air-conditioned cabins that easily accommodate a baby and a folded stroller. It offers stunning, effortless views of the Old Town skyline and the Motława River without any exhausting climbs.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Park Jelitkowski

This quiet, shaded coastal park runs parallel to the beach, featuring smooth paved paths perfect for pushing a stroller under a canopy of trees. It features direct wooden ramp access to the soft sand, making it easy to dip your toddler's toes in the Baltic Sea.

Morning / 1h

Bazar Natury

An indoor ecological food market in the trendy Garnizon district, ideal for picking up organic, locally sourced toddler snacks and premium Polish delicacies. The surrounding neighborhood is exceptionally flat, pedestrian-friendly, and filled with spacious, modern cafes.

Morning / 2h

Westerplatte Peninsula

A historic, park-like peninsula with wide, paved walking paths leading through quiet woods to the iconic coastal monument. It is incredibly peaceful, stroller-friendly, and offers refreshing, open sea breezes away from the bustling city center.

Morning / 2h

Akwarium Gdyńskie (Gdynia Aquarium)

Located a short, scenic coastal drive north, this indoor aquarium features fascinating Baltic sea life and easily navigable ramps for strollers. It serves as the ultimate high-quality rainy-day backup plan for an engaging, visually stimulating morning with an infant.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mandu (Oliwa)

Order the traditional pierogi with sweet cheese and blueberries for the toddler, and the savory wild boar pierogi for yourselves. This exceptionally spacious restaurant features a dedicated, clean kids' play corner and plenty of room for stroller parking.

Piwna47 Resto Bar

Savor the slow-cooked duck breast or fresh Baltic cod while sitting in their bright, spacious glass orangery right next to St. Mary's Church. It offers a sophisticated atmosphere with ample room for high chairs and a highly accommodating, professional staff.

Słona Woda

Try the fresh Baltic halibut served with seasonal local vegetables while enjoying the quiet, coastal breeze. Located right by the beach in a quiet zone, this modern, airy venue is highly welcoming to families and provides clean baby changing facilities.

White Marlin

Indulge in premium seafood and grilled octopus right on the Sopot beach with unmatched, upscale Baltic Sea views. The spacious outdoor wooden terrace allows your toddler to safely play in the clean sand right next to your dining table.

Chleb i Wino (Chmielna)

Order the artisan wood-fired pizzas and homemade pasta paired with their famous, freshly baked bread. Arrive early for lunch to secure one of their spacious booth tables that comfortably fits an infant carrier or stroller.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sofitel Grand Sopot

This historic 5-star beachfront icon offers ultimate luxury, an indoor swimming pool, and direct, private access to the sand. They provide top-tier wooden cribs, baby welcome amenities, and professional babysitting services upon request.

Radisson Hotel & Suites, Gdansk (Deo Plaza)

Located directly on the Motława River, this hotel offers massive family suites with kitchenettes and an excellent indoor swimming pool. It is highly stroller-accessible and places you just steps away from the pedestrian footbridge to the Old Town.

Hilton Gdansk

Situated on the quiet end of the Motława waterfront, it features a stunning indoor rooftop pool with panoramic views of the river. The rooms are incredibly spacious and quiet, and the staff proactively provides high-quality baby cribs and bottle warmers.

👨‍👩‍👧 While the historical Old Town features bumpy cobblestones, look for the smoother, flat granite paths running down the middle of major streets like Długi Targ to save your stroller's wheels. Uber is highly reliable and affordable in the Tri-City area, but be sure to select premium options to guarantee a vehicle spacious enough for your stroller and baby gear. Always carry small change or your contactless card for beachside public restrooms, as some require a small fee to access their baby-changing facilities.
