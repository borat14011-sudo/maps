Gran Canaria

← back to travel board
Gran Canaria is a miniature continent where towering volcanic peaks melt into golden Sahara-like dunes and calm turquoise waters. Families can wander through vibrant historical towns, explore lush botanical gardens, and build sandcastles on sheltered beaches. It is an island playground perfectly tailored for sun-drenched, stress-free memory-making with little ones.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Maspalomas Dunes

Climb giant, golden sand dunes that look like the Sahara Desert and slide down the gentle slopes. Toddlers will love playing in the sand, but visit early in the morning to avoid hot sand underfoot.

Morning / 3-4h

Palmitos Park

A lush botanical garden and zoo featuring dolphin shows, exotic birds, and a walk-through butterfly house. Stroller-friendly paths make it incredibly easy to navigate with toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de Las Canteras

A sweeping city beach protected by a natural lava reef, keeping the waters calm and perfect for paddling. The wide promenade is ideal for stroller walks and quick access to ice cream.

Morning / 2h

Jardín Botánico Canario Viera y Clavijo

Spain’s largest botanical garden showcases giant cacti and prehistoric-looking dragon trees. Stick to the flat lower paths for an easy, magical nature walk with a toddler.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado del Puerto

A lively iron-canopied food market where parents can sample delicious local tapas while kids enjoy fresh fruit cups. It is a bustling, sensory experience with plenty of outdoor seating.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Poema del Mar Aquarium

This world-class aquarium features a massive curved viewing window that will leave toddlers spellbound by sharks and rays. The entire facility is fully air-conditioned and highly stroller-accessible.

Morning / 3h

Puerto de Mogán

A picturesque fishing village with colorful houses, calm canals, and a sheltered beach. Toddlers will enjoy watching the boats in the marina and eating gelato by the harbor.

Morning / 2h

Roque Nublo Paths

This iconic volcanic rock offers breathtaking mountain views. Families with toddlers should stick to the easy, flat pine forest paths near the parking area rather than the steep climb.

Afternoon / 3h

Angry Birds Activity Park

A massive outdoor play park packed with splash zones, slides, and soft play areas designed to burn off toddler energy. It is fully fenced and has plenty of shaded seating for parents.

Where to eat

  • Papas Arrugadas con Mojo — These salted, wrinkled baby potatoes served with mild red and green dipping sauces are a Canarian staple that toddlers love eating with their fingers.
  • Restaurante El Herreño — A casual, family-friendly spot in Las Palmas serving authentic Canarian dishes like hearty chickpea stew in a welcoming, relaxed atmosphere.
  • Helamore — An artisan gelato shop in Puerto de Mogán offering delicious, child-friendly flavors and a sweet treat right by the marina.
  • Cofradía de Pescadores — A harbor-side restaurant in Arinaga where parents can enjoy ultra-fresh seafood while kids enjoy simple, grilled fish fillets with ocean views.
