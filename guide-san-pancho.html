San Pancho

← back to travel board
San Pancho is Mexico's ultimate slow-travel coastal escape, where cobblestone streets meet a sweeping golden shoreline. Families will fall in love with its creative, community-first spirit, vibrant street art, and gentle jungle-meets-ocean breeze. It offers the perfect, unhurried pace for parents traveling with curious toddlers.
🚕 Getting there: ~60 min private shuttle or taxi ride north from Puerto Vallarta International Airport (PVR)

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa San Francisco

Build giant sandcastles on this wide, golden beach and watch spectacular Pacific sunsets. Toddlers will love playing in the soft sand, but keep them close as the shorebreak waves can be quite strong.

Morning / 1-2h

EntreAmigos Community Center

An extraordinary, eco-friendly community hub featuring a giant children's library, art spaces, and play areas. It's a colorful, welcoming air-conditioned sanctuary where toddlers can read and play with local toys.

Sunset (Seasonal) / 1h

Sea Turtle Release

Witness baby sea turtles making their journey to the ocean at the beachside conservation nursery run by Grupo Ecológico. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the tiny hatchlings scrambling across the sand.

Early Morning / 1h

Estero San Pancho

Stroll along the quiet lagoon at the north end of the beach to spot nesting herons, egrets, and colorful birds. The flat, easy path is perfect for stroller pushing or toddler-paced wandering.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza del Sol

The town’s vibrant central square comes alive in the evening with local kids playing and street performers. Toddlers can safely run around the open paved plaza while parents enjoy fresh churros nearby.

Sunday Morning / 2h

La Patrona Polo & Equestrian Club

Visit this world-class equestrian center tucked into the jungle to see beautiful horses up close. It is a peaceful, lush setting where animal-loving toddlers can watch riding practices from a safe distance.

Tuesday Morning / 2h

Mercado del Sol

A lively, colorful weekly market showcasing local organic foods, handmade crafts, and live music. It's a sensory delight for little ones who will love the fresh tropical fruit cups and vibrant atmosphere.

Morning or Late Afternoon / 1h

Calle Tercer Mundo Walk

Wander down the main avenue filled with bright murals, whimsical shops, and shade-giving palm trees. Toddlers will be captivated by the colorful street art of jaguars and sea life painted on the walls.

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Las Clavellinas Day Trip

Take a quick 10-minute taxi to this nearby sheltered cove, known for its calm and gentle waters. Unlike the main beach, it's a safe and tranquil haven perfect for toddlers to splash and wade safely.

🍽️ Where to eat

Taquería Los Arbolitos

Order the melt-in-your-mouth tacos al pastor served on fresh, warm corn tortillas.

Bistro Organico

Savor the signature hibiscus flower quesadillas and fresh-squeezed passion fruit juice in a leafy, toddler-friendly courtyard.

La Rústica San Pancho

Feast on the crowd-pleasing wood-fired pizzas, featuring a perfectly thin, bubbly crust that kids and parents will both devour.

Café d'Moka

Grab fluffy house-made banana pancakes and an iced vanilla latte for a relaxing, shaded family breakfast.

La Ola Rica

Treat the family to the famous sweet coconut shrimp served in a lively, tropical open-air dining room.

👨‍👩‍👧 San Pancho is highly walkable, but cobblestone streets can be bumpy, so opt for a sturdy all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier. Always keep cash on hand for street vendors and small restaurants, as many local spots do not accept credit cards.
