Querétaro

← back to travel board
Nestled in Mexico’s high central valley, Querétaro charms families with its pedestrian-only plazas, dramatic pink stone aqueduct, and relaxed colonial pace. Kids will love chasing pigeons in lush gardens, while parents appreciate the safe, walkable streets and vibrant culinary scene. It is the ultimate stress-free base for an authentic Mexican adventure with little ones in tow.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1h

Aqueduct of Querétaro (Los Arcos)

Marvel at the 74 towering pink stone arches that define the city skyline. It is an easy stroller-friendly walk along the Mirador, and toddlers will love counting the massive arches from the viewing platform.

Morning / 1-2h

Alameda Hidalgo Park

This fenced, shady park in the city center is a green oasis filled with paved paths, statues, and playgrounds. It is completely car-free, making it the perfect safe spot for toddlers to run free and spot squirrels under giant ash trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado de la Cruz

Immerse your senses in this bustling traditional market packed with colorful fruits, pinatas, and cheap eats. Toddlers will be captivated by the toy stalls, but keep them close or in a carrier during busy peak hours.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas

The heartbeat of the historic center, this beautiful pedestrian plaza is lined with outdoor cafes and a central fountain. Little ones can safely toddle around the car-free square while parents enjoy a coffee under the shade of manicured trees.

Afternoon / 1h

El Queretano Tranvía (Trolley Tour)

Hop on this open-air, old-fashioned trolley bus for a scenic, low-effort tour of the historic center. It is an excellent way to see the sights when little legs get tired, and the gentle breeze is perfect for a stroller-free toddler nap.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Parque Bicentenario Querétaro

A massive amusement park featuring a small water park, a lake with paddleboats, a scenic train, and dinosaur replicas. Toddlers will love the gentle carousels, playgrounds, and petting zoo area.

Morning / 1-2h

Cerro de las Campanas National Park

This peaceful hilltop park is famous for its history and unique ringing stones that sound like bells when struck. There are wide grassy lawns for toddler picnics and plenty of paved paths for a stroller-friendly stroll.

Afternoon / 2h

Centro Cultural Manuel Gómez Morín

A vibrant cultural center featuring an interactive science museum, a kids library, and outdoor play spaces. Toddlers will love the dedicated sensory play areas and colorful indoor play zones designed for early childhood.

Morning / 3-4h

Peña de Bernal Monolith

Visit one of the world's tallest stone monoliths in a colorful nearby Pueblo Mágico. While hiking to the top is too tough for toddlers, exploring the flat, vibrant village streets and taking a mototaxi ride is pure magic for them.

Where to eat

  • Gorditas de Maíz Quebrado — Try these thick, hand-pressed corn cakes stuffed with cheese or stews, which are soft, easy for toddlers to chew, and incredibly flavorful.
  • La Mariposa — Head to this iconic, family-friendly 1940s diner to try their famous mantecado ice cream and sweet pastries in a retro, welcoming environment.
  • Tikua Sur Este — This colorful restaurant serves authentic southern Mexican cuisine with high chairs available and a spacious, welcoming outdoor patio perfect for noisy toddlers.
  • Guajolotes at Cenaduría Blas — These local sandwiches feature bolillo bread dipped in guajillo chili sauce, lightly fried, and filled with beans, cheese, and meat—an easy-to-share local favorite.
