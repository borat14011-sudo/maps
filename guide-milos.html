Milos

← back to travel board
Milos enchants families with its surreal landscapes, dramatic chalk-white cliffs, and some of the calmest, shallowest turquoise waters in the Aegean. Unlike busier Greek islands, its sleepy fishing villages and gentle pace offer a stress-free playground for parents and toddlers alike. From exploring colorful boathouses to splashing in natural wading pools, every day feels like a safe, sun-kissed adventure.
🚕 Getting there: ~12 min drive by pre-booked taxi or rental car from Milos Island National Airport to Adamas port or Pollonia beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Sarakiniko Beach Exploration

Wander the otherworldly, smooth white volcanic rocks that look exactly like the surface of the moon. Go early in the morning to avoid the heat, and let toddlers explore the flat, sandy inlet area where there are no waves.

Morning / 2-3h

Firopotamos Beach Splashing

This postcard-perfect bay features a tiny church, colorful fisherman houses, and a very calm, protected cove. The shallow, crystal-clear water is ideal for toddlers to safely splash and play in.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Klima Village Sunset Walk

Stroll along the water's edge to admire the 'syrmata'—traditional fisherman houses with vibrant, brightly painted wooden doors. It is a flat, easy walk where toddlers will love watching the boats bobbing in the harbor.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Plaka Village Alley Wandering

Explore the charming, vehicle-free capital with its whitewashed houses and blooming bougainvillea. Ditch the stroller for a baby carrier here, as the winding paths feature steps that lead up to beautiful panoramic views.

Morning / 1h

Papafragas Caves Viewing

Marvel at the dramatic, narrow sea channels carved deep into the towering volcanic cliffs. Keep toddlers safely at the cliffside viewing area to enjoy the dramatic views rather than climbing down the steep path.

Morning / 3-4h

Provatas Beach Day

Spend a relaxed morning at this wide, golden sand beach known for its exceptionally warm, shallow waters. It is fully organized with sunbeds and umbrellas, making it the most comfortable beach on the island for young families.

Midday / 1h

Milos Mining Museum

Take a cool, air-conditioned break in Adamas to learn about the island's unique volcanic geology. Toddlers will be fascinated by the colorful displays of shiny minerals, obsidian rocks, and giant fossilized shells.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Pollonia Beachfront Playground

Let the kids run wild at the shaded beachside playground right in the heart of Pollonia. Afterwards, take a gentle, sandy stroll along the calm, tree-lined bay just steps away.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Kleftiko Caves Catamaran Cruise

Embark on a family-friendly catamaran tour to see the spectacular white cliffs and ancient pirate hideouts of Kleftiko. Choose a tour with a shaded deck and shallow swim stops so toddlers can safely float in life vests.

🍽️ Where to eat

O! Hamos! Tavern

Savor the slow-cooked pork in parchment paper and handmade cheese pies in a whimsical, shaded garden patio where toddlers can look at handwritten menus and clay pots.

Medusa

Feast on fresh grilled octopus, zucchini fries, and local cheese while sitting right at the water's edge in the quiet fishing village of Mandrakia.

Yialos

Enjoy modern Greek seafood like sea bass carpaccio and handmade orzo while your toddler plays safely in the sand right next to your beachfront table.

Astakas Cafe Restaurant

Dine on fresh lobster pasta and local tomato fritters at tables placed directly on the sand, offering the perfect spot for kids to play while parents watch the sunset.

Palaios Pastry Shop

Indulge in traditional Greek orange pie and homemade vanilla ice cream in a quiet, pedestrian-only courtyard in Plaka.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a compact SUV with a pre-booked car seat, as public transport is limited and many scenic beach roads are unpaved. Always pack a sturdy baby carrier because the cobblestoned, stepped alleys of Plaka and rocky beach descents are highly stroller-unfriendly.
