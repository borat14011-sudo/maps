Zihuatanejo

← back to travel board
Nestled along a protected Pacific bay, Zihuatanejo offers a slow-paced, authentic Mexican escape perfect for little feet and tired parents. Here, cobblestone streets wind past vibrant markets, leading to tranquil, wave-free beaches where toddlers can safely splash in the warm tide. It is a sleepy fishing village turned tropical paradise, where family memories are made under the shade of swaying palms.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa La Ropa

This mile-long stretch of soft, golden sand features exceptionally calm waters, making it the safest spot in the bay for tiny swimmers. Toddler note: Rent a beachfront lounge chair with an umbrella so your little ones can nap comfortably in the shade.

Morning / 4h

Playa Las Gatas Boat Adventure

Take a quick, cheap water taxi from the town pier to this reef-protected beach where there are virtually no waves and plenty of colorful fish to spot in the shallows. Toddler note: The boat ride is short and thrilling for toddlers, but bring water shoes to protect sensitive feet from coral fragments.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo del Pescador (Fisherman's Walk)

Stroll along this car-free, scenic pedestrian boardwalk that hugs the shoreline, perfect for stroller rides and spotting local fishermen bringing in their daily catch. Toddler note: Go at sunset when the ocean breeze cools things down and street vendors sell fresh, kid-friendly fruit cups.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal Zihuatanejo

Immerse your senses in this bustling, authentic market filled with vibrant tropical fruits, colorful pinatas, and friendly local vendors. Toddler note: Keep toddlers close in a carrier as the aisles can get narrow and busy, and buy a fresh banana or sweet mango slice to keep them happily distracted.

Morning / 3h

El Refugio de Potosí

Located just outside town, this ecological reserve features a sperm whale skeleton, a butterfly pavilion, and a rescue center for native animals like coatis and macaws. Toddler note: The flat, shaded dirt paths are very stroller-friendly, and kids will love spotting the colorful iguanas.

Morning / 2-3h

Barra de Potosí Lagoon Boat Tour

Rent a small, covered panga boat to glide through the calm mangrove lagoons where kids can spot herons, pelicans, and occasionally roseate spoonbills. Toddler note: The gentle, rocking motion of the boat is incredibly soothing and often coaxes toddlers into an easy mid-day nap.

Evening / 1-2h

Zihuatanejo Zócalo (Main Plaza)

The heart of town comes alive in the evening with families, street performers, and local children playing basketball or running around the open plaza. Toddler note: It is entirely pedestrian-only, giving toddlers plenty of safe, open space to burn off energy before bedtime.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Sea Turtle Release at Playa Larga

Participate in a magical conservation effort where children can help guide tiny, newly hatched sea turtles safely across the sand to the ocean. Toddler note: Toddlers will be mesmerized by the baby turtles, but keep a close eye on them to ensure they handle the fragile creatures gently.

Morning / 3h

Xihuacan Archaeological Site (La Chole)

Explore ancient ball courts and a unique pyramid built from river stones, offering a fascinating glimpse into pre-Hispanic history with a small, air-conditioned museum. Toddler note: The open grassy areas around the ruins are great for toddlers to stretch their legs, but bring a sun hat and plenty of water as shade is limited.

Where to eat

  • Carmelitas Café — This beloved local institution serves legendary, authentic Guerrero-style breakfasts like fluffy chilaquiles and sweet conchas in a highly welcoming, family-friendly courtyard.
  • Bandido's Restaurant & Bar — A vibrant, festive spot in the downtown area offering great tacos, high chairs, a kid-approved menu, and occasional live music that will get toddlers dancing.
  • La Perla Restaurant — Located right on the sand of Playa La Ropa, this casual spot lets parents enjoy fresh seafood and cold drinks while kids play safely in the sand right next to the table.
  • Paletería La Michoacana — Cool down with an endless variety of fresh fruit ice pops (paletas) made with real mango, strawberry, or coconut, which are the ultimate toddler-approved tropical treat.
