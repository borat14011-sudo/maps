Sun-soaked desert adventures for the whole family

Palm Springs

← back to travel board
Nestled against the dramatic San Jacinto Mountains, Palm Springs is a retro-cool desert oasis bathed in year-round sunshine. Families can splash in sparkling pools, explore whimsical desert gardens, and ride rotating cable cars high above the valley floor. It's a colorful, easygoing playground where mid-century charm meets kid-friendly outdoor adventure.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

Ride the world's largest rotating tramcar up to cooler mountain temperatures with stunning valley views. Toddlers will love the giant pinecones and easy, flat walking paths at the mountain station.

Morning / 3h

The Living Desert Zoo and Gardens

Feed giraffes, watch model trains chug along a massive outdoor track, and spot desert animals in their natural habitats. Stroller-friendly paved paths and plenty of shaded misting stations make it perfect for toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Moorten Botanical Garden

Wander through a historic 'cactarium' packed with thousands of bizarre, beautiful cacti and desert plants. Toddlers will be fascinated by the resident turtles and the easy-to-navigate dirt paths.

Thursday Evening / 2h

Palm Springs VillageFest

Stroll this vibrant Thursday night street fair filled with local crafts, live music, and delicious street food. It's bustling but highly stroller-friendly, allowing little ones to safely enjoy the sights and sounds.

Early Morning / 2h

Tahquitz Canyon

Embark on an easy, scenic hike to a spectacular 60-foot seasonal waterfall. While the rocky trail requires a carrier for toddlers, older kids will love searching for wildlife along the stream.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Cabazon Dinosaurs

Climb inside a giant T-Rex and snap photos with massive, colorful steel-and-concrete dinosaurs just outside the city. The sandy play area and fossil-dig activity are absolute hits for high-energy toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

Ruth Hardy Park

Let the kids burn off energy at this massive local park featuring two modern playgrounds, grassy fields, and tennis courts. There is plenty of shade and open space for toddlers to safely toddle around.

Afternoon / 2h

Palm Springs Air Museum

Explore massive air-conditioned hangars filled with historic aircraft that kids can actually climb inside and touch. Toddlers can run around the spacious indoor areas safely out of the desert heat.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Shields Date Garden

Walk the peaceful, winding garden path through towering date palms and enjoy a world-famous date shake. Toddlers will love watching the ducks in the pond and sampling the sweet fruit.

Where to eat

  • Elmer's Restaurant — Famous for giant, delicious German pancakes served in a highly welcoming, family-friendly diner atmosphere.
  • Great Shakes — Indulge in gourmet milkshakes topped with a tiny, fresh-baked donut on the straw, which toddlers will absolutely adore.
  • Sherman's Deli & Bakery — A bustling New York-style deli with massive portions, kid-friendly booths, and an endless display case of colorful cakes and cookies.
  • Las Casuelas Terraza — Enjoy classic Mexican fare on a lively, mist-cooled outdoor patio featuring upbeat live music that will keep toddlers entertained.
👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense desert heat by scheduling outdoor activities for early morning and utilizing air-conditioned museums or pool time during the afternoon. Always pack a high-quality stroller with a sun canopy, lots of water, and plenty of toddler-friendly sunscreen.
