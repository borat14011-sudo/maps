Here, stroller-friendly paths lead to vibrant playgrounds, while shallow sandy bays offer safe, sun-soaked splashing. It is the perfect Mediterranean playground where ancient history effortlessly blends with relaxed beachside living.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 min taxi to Rhodes Town, or ~30 min to family-friendly beach resorts in Faliraki

What to do

Morning / 1.5-2h

Palace of the Grand Master

Step back in time inside this magnificent medieval fortress featuring spacious courtyards. Toddler note: The interior floors can be slippery and the cobbled entrance is bumpy, so use a baby carrier instead of a stroller.

Morning / 2h

Valley of the Butterflies

Walk along shaded rustic pathways beside cool streams where thousands of Jersey Tiger moths rest on the trees. Toddler note: The forest paths involve steps and narrow wooden bridges, making a baby carrier essential.

Late Afternoon / 3-4h

Tsambika Beach

A golden-sand paradise with incredibly shallow, warm, and calm waters perfect for safe splashing. Toddler note: Rent a sun lounger close to the shoreline so toddlers can play in the softest sand away from hot spots.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Rhodes Toy Museum

An interactive countryside museum featuring vintage Greek toys from the 1930s to the 1980s. Toddler note: There is a fantastic outdoor playground and a hands-on games room where toddlers can play freely.

Morning / 3h

Lindos Village and Beach

Wander the car-free, whitewashed streets of Lindos down to the calm, shallow waters of the main bay. Toddler note: Skip the steep, hot climb to the Acropolis with toddlers and stick to the sandy beach and local gelato shops.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Rhodes Old Town Moat Walk

Stroll through the lush, green dry moat surrounding the medieval city walls, offering a peaceful escape from crowds. Toddler note: This wide, grassy pathway is completely flat and stroller-friendly, perfect for toddlers to run safely.

Morning / 1h

Mandraki Harbour and Deer Statues

Watch yachts and fishing boats glide past the historic harbour entrance marked by two iconic bronze deer statues. Toddler note: The paved harborfront is flat and stroller-friendly, but keep active toddlers away from the unfenced water edges.

Morning / 2h

Farma of Rhodes Petting Zoo

Get up close with ostriches, llamas, and friendly lemurs in a spacious, interactive animal park. Toddler note: Toddlers can feed the gentle animals directly, and the flat dirt paths are easily navigated with a sturdy stroller.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Seven Springs

An oasis of pine trees and flowing water offering cool relief from the summer heat, complete with resident ducks. Toddler note: Avoid the dark, narrow water tunnel with toddlers and instead enjoy the easy forest paths and lakeside cafe.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ta Kardasia

Order the tender, slow-cooked lamb kleftiko and fresh Greek salad in their welcoming, family-friendly outdoor courtyard.

Kostas Village Taverna

Savor traditional baked moussaka and grilled halloumi in a lively, warm village square setting with plenty of space for kids.

Romios Restaurant

Dine on handmade seafood pasta and local meze in a beautiful, shaded courtyard garden that offers a quiet oasis from Old Town crowds.

Tambakio

Enjoy fresh grilled sea bass and calamari with your toes in the sand at this stunning beachside restaurant on St. Paul's Bay.

To Marouli

Try the delicious homemade veggie burgers and fresh fruit smoothies in their colorful, relaxed, and highly welcoming courtyard.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a lightweight, durable carrier for historical sites like Lindos and the Palace of the Grand Master, as the cobblestones and steep steps are not stroller-friendly. Stick to East Coast beaches like Tsambika for the calmest, shallowest waters ideal for safe toddler swimming.
