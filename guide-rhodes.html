Rhodes blends ancient history with sun-drenched beaches, offering a serene escape for luxury families. Stroll the stroller-ready boardwalk along Elli Beach, where calm waters lap the shore. The Palace of the Grand Master in the Old Town captivates with its medieval grandeur, and many resorts provide private pool villas with cribs and blackout curtains for infant comfort. The island's gentle sea breeze and shallow lagoons make it ideal for a baby's first Mediterranean holiday.
🚕 Getting there: Rhodes Airport to hotel strip: 15–30 minutes (depending on location). Best luxury family transport: private transfer with infant seat and stroller storage, available via companies like Welcome Pickups or Rhodes Private Transfers.

What to do

Early morning 9–11am

Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes

A stunning medieval fortress with ramparts offering panoramic views. Stroller-accessible in the courtyard; shady corridors keep babies cool. Avoid inside if crowds are heavy.

Late afternoon 4–6pm (avoid midday heat)

Acropolis of Lindos

Ancient hilltop ruins overlooking a turquoise bay. Not stroller-friendly (steep steps), but the beach below is perfect with calm, shallow water. Bring a baby carrier.

Morning 10am–12pm

Anthony Quinn Bay

A sheltered cove with crystal-clear, calm water ideal for baby paddling. Shady spots under tamarisk trees. Limited stroller access on the path; park at the top and carry baby down.

Morning 9–11am

Valley of the Butterflies (Petaloudes)

A lush nature reserve with wooden boardwalks perfect for strollers. Thousands of butterflies in late summer. Shaded and cool, but avoid loud noises.

Morning 10am–12pm

Kallithea Springs

Elegant Art Deco thermal springs with a sandy beach and calm sea. Stroller-friendly paved paths, shaded terraces, and a large swimming area. Great for a relaxing morning.

Late afternoon 5–6pm

Mandraki Harbour

Picturesque harbor with medieval windmills and the Colossus statue site. Flat, stroller-friendly promenade with benches and ice cream shops. Gentle sea breeze.

Early morning 8–10am

Rodini Park

Ancient park with peacocks, ducks, and a small lake. Shady paths and paved walkways suitable for strollers. A quiet escape from the heat.

Morning 9–11am

Faliraki Beach

Long sandy beach with calm, shallow waters and lifeguards. Many beach clubs offer shaded loungers and baby changing facilities. Stroller-friendly boardwalk.

Midday 12–1pm (beat the heat)

Seven Springs (Epta Piges)

A cool, forested area with springs and a small lake. Narrow paths are tricky for strollers, but the microclimate is refreshing. Baby carrier recommended.

🍽️ Where to eat

Kerasma

Michelin-starred restaurant in Rhodes Old Town. Signature dishes include lamb with yogurt and truffle. Offers highchairs, quiet outdoor terrace, and early seating at 7pm. Accepts babies; no minimum age.

Mavrikos

Iconic family-run taverna in Lindos since 1930. Famous for grilled octopus and moussaka. Has a shaded courtyard with highchairs, calm atmosphere, and early dinner times.

Ta Kala Kathoumena

Quaint ouzeri in Rhodes Old Town with a hidden garden. Specializes in local meze (fava, stuffed vine leaves). Stroller-accessible courtyard, quiet, and baby-friendly.

Niohori Restaurant

Traditional family restaurant in Rhodes Town with a large playground area for kids. Serves grilled meats and seafood. Highly accommodating with highchairs and early seating.

La Veranda Restaurant

Elegant rooftop dining in the medieval city with sea views. Serves Mediterranean fusion dishes. Offers highchairs and a calm ambiance; best for early dinner (7pm).

👨‍👩‍👧 Most pharmacies in Rhodes Town and larger resorts carry infant paracetamol and European formula brands (e.g., Aptamil). Cobblestone streets in the Old Town are challenging for strollers—use a travel stroller with large wheels; boardwalks along Ellis Beach are smooth. For medical care, the Rhodes General Hospital (Pediatrics Department) is well-regarded; in summer, keep your baby out of direct sun 11am–4pm, and use UV-protective swimsuits and a clip-on stroller fan.
