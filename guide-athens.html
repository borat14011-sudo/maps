Athens

← back to travel board
Athens blends monumental history with a relaxed, outdoor-centric lifestyle that keeps both design-conscious parents and energetic toddlers happy. Skip the rushed tours and embrace the "siga-siga" (slowly-slowly) pace, spending mornings among ancient marbles and afternoons shaded by orange trees in quiet neighborhoods like Plaka or Pangrati. With a culture that absolutely adores babies, you will find warm hospitality, spacious outdoor squares for stroller walks, and easy access to coastal breezes just south of the city center.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-40 minute private van transfer booked via Welcome Pickups from Athens International Airport to downtown Athens, complete with pre-installed, rear-facing infant car seats.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Acropolis Museum

Fully stroller-accessible with wide, smooth glass floors and spacious elevators, making it easy to admire the Parthenon marbles without breaking a sweat. The ground-floor cafe has plenty of room for highchairs and offers a quiet, air-conditioned respite from the midday heat.

Afternoon / 1.5h

National Garden

A lush, shady oasis in the heart of the city featuring wide dirt paths perfect for stroller walks, a duck pond, and a wooden playground. It is the ultimate spot to escape the midday heat and let your toddler nap in the stroller under canopy shade.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center (SNFCC)

This modern waterfront complex offers expansive, flat, stroller-friendly pathways, a dancing water fountain display, and a massive playground. Parents can grab a high-quality espresso at the Canal Cafe while the little one watches sailboats on the artificial canal.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Flisvos Marina

A clean, pedestrian-only marina in Palaio Faliro where you can push the stroller right along the yachts, catch a sea breeze, and access clean public restrooms. It features a dedicated play area and family-friendly cafes lining the promenade.

Morning / 1.5h

Mount Lycabettus Funicular

Ride the indoor cable car up the mountain to avoid the steep hike, offering a thrilling sensory experience for a toddler without the physical strain. The peak provides panoramic views of the Aegean Sea, and you can easily fold your stroller for the quick cabin ride.

Morning / 1.5h

Philopappos Hill

Paved, pine-shaded paths designed by architect Dimitris Pikionis offer a peaceful hike with incredible views of the Acropolis away from the crowds. A sturdy stroller or baby carrier is recommended here to navigate some of the gravel transitions near the base.

Morning / 3h

Attica Zoological Park

Located near the airport, this beautifully maintained zoo features flat, paved paths ideal for strollers and interactive animal feeding sessions. It is a fantastic, low-stress day trip where your toddler can see dolphins, giraffes, and Greek farm animals up close.

Afternoon / 3h

Lake Vouliagmeni

A thermal spring surrounded by dramatic red cliffs on the Athenian Riviera, featuring calm, warm waters perfect for a gentle family dip. The upscale sunbed area is highly organized, stroller-accessible, and has an on-site restaurant serving fresh juices and simple kids' snacks.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Megaron Athens Concert Hall Garden

This sprawling, beautifully manicured lawn hosts relaxed outdoor family picnics and occasional daytime musical events in the summer. It is a peaceful, fenced-in green space where your toddler can safely crawl and toddle on the grass while you relax on a blanket.

🍽️ Where to eat

Avocado

Order the Luminous Lucid macrobiotic bowl or their fresh avocado pasta; it is a vibrant, health-conscious spot with a relaxed, smoke-free indoor environment and plenty of high chairs.

Strofi Tavern

Order the traditional baked lamb with rosemary; their spacious outdoor rooftop terrace offers stunning Acropolis views and plenty of room to park a stroller if you book an early dinner slot.

To Kafeneio

Order the meatballs in tomato sauce and handmade savory pies; its location on a pedestrian-only Plaka street means no traffic noise and plenty of space for outdoor highchair seating.

Ergon House

Order the artisanal Greek cheese board and peynirli flatbreads; this bustling indoor culinary market has wide aisles, accessible restrooms with changing tables, and a vibrant, noisy atmosphere where baby babbles blend right in.

Sardelaki me Thea

Order the fresh grilled sea bream and zucchini chips; this breezy seaside spot in Vouliagmeni has outdoor deck seating with endless ocean views and a highly accommodating staff who love kids.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Four Seasons Astir Palace Hotel Athens

Located on the Athenian Riviera, this luxury resort offers expansive family bungalows, dedicated baby pools, complimentary high-quality wooden cribs, and private beach access away from city noise.

Grecotel Pallas Athena

Known for its whimsical, family-oriented design, this hotel offers spacious family suites and a specialized baby portal service where you can pre-order strollers, bottle warmers, and baby bathtubs.

The Margi

A chic, boutique oasis in Vouliagmeni with a gorgeous, shallow-entry pool, excellent baby cot provisions, and a calm, residential vibe that makes bedtime routines painless.

👨‍👩‍👧 Skip the bulky stroller in favor of a sturdy baby carrier when visiting the ancient ruins, as historic sites like the Roman Agora and Parthenon feature extremely uneven, rocky, and slippery stone paths. Always carry cash for neighborhood bakeries and look for signs reading pharmakeio for high-quality European baby formulas, organic purees, and diapers which aren't always stocked in small minimarkets. Embrace the local dining schedule by booking early 7:00 PM dinners, which guarantees you will beat the local crowd and secure the best outdoor, stroller-friendly tables.
