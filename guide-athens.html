Athens blends monumental history with a surprisingly relaxed, family-friendly rhythm under the Aegean sun. Between climbs up legendary hills and strolls through orange-scented alleys, the city invites slow exploration and endless scoopfuls of gelato. It is a vibrant outdoor museum where kids can run free among ruins that shaped the world.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-40 min taxi to central hotels or a direct 40 min Metro Line 3 ride from the airport

What to do

Early Morning / 2h

The Acropolis of Athens

Step back in time to marvel at the Parthenon; it's a majestic site where older kids can imagine Greek myths. Toddlers should be carried in a baby carrier as strollers are not allowed on the rocky, uneven paths.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Lycabettus Hill Funicular

Ride a fun, glass-topped cable car through a mountain tunnel to reach the highest peak in Athens. Toddlers will love the quick train ride, and parents will appreciate the stunning panoramic views from the top.

Morning / 1h

Varvakios Central Market

A sensory explosion of colors, sights, and sounds where local vendors shout out prices for fresh olives, cheeses, and spices. Keep toddlers close in a carrier to navigate the bustling, crowded aisles safely.

Midday / 1-2h

Athens National Garden

A lush, shady oasis in the heart of the city featuring duck ponds, a playground, and wandering turtles. It is the perfect stroller-friendly escape for a picnic or to let toddlers nap in the shade.

Sunday at 11:00 AM / 30m

Changing of the Guard at Syntagma Square

Watch the Evzones soldiers perform their highly synchronized, slow-motion march in traditional pom-pom shoes. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the rhythmic clatter of their steps and the unique uniforms.

Afternoon / 2h

Hellenic Children's Museum

A hands-on, interactive museum designed specifically for children to learn through play, featuring water play, a mock marketplace, and building zones. There is a dedicated safe sensory play area tailored just for toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Wandering through Plaka and Anafiotika

Explore the charming, vehicle-free labyrinth of whitewashed houses that feel like a Greek island village nestled right under the Acropolis. Strollers can be tough on the steep stairs of Anafiotika, but Plaka's flatter paths are ideal for a slow family wander.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Flisvos Marina and Park

Stroll along the waterfront to admire mega-yachts, enjoy a sea breeze, and play at the massive, gated playground. It is fully stroller-accessible and has soft-floored play zones perfect for toddlers to run safely.

Morning / 1-2h

The Panathenaic Stadium

Run on the tracks of the only stadium in the world built entirely of marble, home to the first modern Olympic Games. Toddlers can safely run wild on the grassy center while parents capture great photos from the podium.

🍽️ Where to eat

Kostas (Pentelis Street)

Grab their legendary, mildly spiced pork souvlaki wrapped in warm pita bread with fresh tomato and yogurt sauce.

Ta Karamanlidika tou Fani

Order the cured meat platters, saganaki cheese, and traditional handmade meat pies in a cozy, welcoming deli setting.

Avli

Sit in a hidden, rustic courtyard and share small plates of Keftedes (Greek meatballs) and fried potatoes that kids love.

Lukumades

Treat the family to hot, fresh Greek donut holes drizzled with local honey, cinnamon, or melted chocolate.

Oineas Restaurant

Enjoy a family-friendly dinner of moussaka or chicken souvlaki surrounded by colorful vintage toy and sign decor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the heavy stroller for a baby carrier when visiting historical ruins due to steep, uneven stone steps. Always carry bottled water and schedule a late-afternoon rest to align with the relaxed, late-night Greek dining culture.
