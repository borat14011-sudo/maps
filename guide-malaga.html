Malaga

← back to travel board
Malaga blends rich Andalusian history with a breezy, pedestrian-friendly coastline perfect for little explorers. Here, ancient castles overlook parks filled with green parrots, and the gentle Mediterranean waves lap against wide, stroller-friendly promenades. It's a relaxed, sun-kissed haven where toddler pace matches the easygoing Spanish lifestyle perfectly.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi ride or a direct 12-minute C1 commuter train ride from Malaga Airport to the city center.

What to do

Morning / 2h

La Alcazaba

This majestic Moorish fortress features beautiful palace gardens, winding paths, and fountains that toddlers love to splash their fingers in. Note that stroller access is highly limited, so a baby carrier is highly recommended here.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa de la Malagueta

A spacious sandy beach right in the city, boasting playground areas and calm waves perfect for wading. Toddlers will love playing in the soft sand while parents enjoy the nearby beach bars.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de Málaga

A lush botanical oasis in the heart of the city, filled with towering palm trees, playgrounds, and wild green parakeets that kids adore watching. It is completely flat, paved, and shaded, making it ideal for stroller-bound afternoon naps.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Central de Atarazanas

A vibrant, historic indoor market filled with colorful mounds of fresh fruit, olives, and cheese that will captivate young eyes. Toddlers will love snacking on fresh strawberries while you browse the lively stalls.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo Alborania Aula del Mar

An interactive marine museum located at the port, featuring small touch tanks and educational exhibits about Alboran Sea life. It is compact and highly engaging for toddlers who love turtles and colorful fish.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Castillo de Gibralfaro

A historic castle offering breathtaking views of the bullring and harbor, accessible via a fun, scenic ride on tourist bus line 35. Walk the shaded ramparts with older kids, but keep toddlers close on the uneven stone steps.

Evening / 2h

Muelle Uno

A sleek, pedestrian-only waterfront promenade lined with shops, restaurants, and a dedicated playground area. Toddlers can safely toddle along the flat paths while you enjoy the sea breeze and views of the yachts.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Museo Interactivo de la Música Málaga

A unique 'please play' museum where children are encouraged to touch and try out various instruments from around the world. It’s a wonderful, noisy rainy-day option that helps toddlers explore sound and rhythm.

Full Day / 3-4h

Bioparc Fuengirola

Located just a short train ride away, this immersive, barrier-free zoo recreates lush rainforest habitats for lemurs, gorillas, and tigers. It is stroller-friendly and incredibly engaging for toddlers who can see animals up close without glass barriers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Aranda

Get the thick, crispy churros served with rich hot chocolate in a bustling, pedestrian-only alleyway.

El Pimpi

Order the kid-friendly ham croquettes (croquetas de jamón) and sweet potato fries in their spacious, vibrant outdoor courtyard.

Chiringuito El Cabra

Try the fresh, skewered sardines (espetos de sardinas) roasted over open coals right on the sand while kids play nearby.

La Recova

Order the traditional Andalusian breakfast, which comes with toasted bread and a colorful tray of sweet and savory spreads to share.

Pastelería Casa Kiki

Treat the family to one of their legendary, giant-sized chocolate palmera pastries that are bigger than a toddler's head.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep in mind that dinners in Malaga start late, so opt for early evening tapas at outdoor plazas where toddlers can run around while you graze. Always carry a lightweight stroller for the flat seaside promenades, but bring a carrier for the cobblestones and castle climbs.
