Family luxury on the Costa del Sol

Malaga

← back to travel board
Malaga blends Andalusian charm with modern luxury, offering a sun-soaked escape for families. The stroller-friendly promenade along Muelle Uno leads to soft sand beaches like La Malagueta, perfect for infant play. The historic Alcazaba provides shaded courtyards away from the midday heat. At Gran Hotel Miramar, the crib is ready and the pool is just steps from the sea.
🚕 Getting there: Malaga Airport (AGP) to hotel strip is a 15-20 minute drive. Best luxury family transport is a private transfer with car seat and stroller pre-loaded — book via Welcome Pickups or Blacklane for infant-safe service.

What to do

Morning 9–11am (avoid heat)

Alcazaba of Malaga

Ancient Moorish fortress with lush gardens and fountains — stroller-accessible ramps, shaded paths, calm atmosphere.

2 hours (midday break)

Picasso Museum

World-class art in a 16th-century palace — stroller friendly, quiet galleries, baby change facilities.

Morning 9am–12pm

La Malagueta Beach

Urban sandy beach with calm shallow water — bring a UV tent, nearby cafes with highchairs.

Late afternoon 4–6pm

Muelle Uno

Modern port promenade with shops, fountains, and wide walkways — perfect for stroller strolls and baby gazing at boats.

Early morning 8:30–10am

Gibralfaro Castle

Historic hilltop fortress with panoramic views — steep walk but stroller can navigate lower paths; shady pine groves.

Morning 10am–1pm

Jardín Botánico Histórico La Concepción

Lush botanical garden with palms, orchid house, and a children's play area — stroller-accessible paths, shaded benches.

2 hours after lunch

Museo Automovilistico de Malaga

Classic car and fashion museum in a tobacco factory — cool indoors, stroller friendly, visually exciting for babies.

Late morning 11am–12:30pm

Cathedral of Malaga (La Manquita)

Renaissance cathedral with one unfinished tower — spacious interior, quiet, stroller allowed; nearby plaza for crawling.

Afternoon 3–5pm

Centre Pompidou Malaga

Colorful contemporary art in the port's cube building — interactive family tours, baby-friendly, air-conditioned.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Pimpi

Iconic bodega with Andalusian tapas — highchairs available, outdoor terrace, early dinner seating at 7pm, moderate noise. Signature: grilled sardines.

La Cosmopolita

Modern tapas bar with a creative menu — highchairs, quiet back room, child-friendly staff. Signature: tuna tartare.

Casa Lola

Traditional Andalusian restaurant with a family room — highchairs, early bird menu, low noise level. Signature: oxtail stew.

Baobab

Healthy international restaurant with a dedicated kids' menu and play corner — highchairs, outdoor terrace shaded. Signature: quinoa bowls.

Kaleja

Michelin-starred tasting menu (lunch only) — accepts older infants with notice, quiet elegant setting, but no highchairs; call ahead. Signature: Malaga-raised goat.
