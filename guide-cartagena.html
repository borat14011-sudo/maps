Cartagena

← back to travel board
Cartagena wraps families in a warm Caribbean embrace, where technicolor colonial streets echo with the rhythm of drums and the scent of sweet coconut rice. Beyond the massive stone walls, gentle beaches and green plazas shaded by sloth-filled trees offer magical moments for little explorers. It is a sensory playground where history feels like a storybook come to life.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi from Rafael Núñez International Airport (CTG) to the Walled City or Bocagrande hotels

What to do

Early Morning / 1.5-2h

Castillo de San Felipe de Barajas

Explore the winding tunnels and massive stone ramparts of this giant fortress, which feels like a real-life pirate castle. Toddler note: Keep a close grip on little hands near steep drop-offs, and bring a baby carrier instead of a stroller.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parque Centenario

This green public park is a leafy oasis right outside the city walls where kids can spot wild iguanas, monkeys, and slow-moving sloths in the canopy. Toddler note: Flat, paved pathways make this extremely stroller-friendly and perfect for a shady run-around.

Morning / 1h

Las Bóvedas

Wander through these colorful archways built into the city walls, which once served as dungeons and now house vibrant craft markets filled with toys and textiles. Toddler note: The wide, open plaza in front is ideal for letting toddlers toddle safely away from vehicle traffic.

Afternoon / 1h

Plaza de Bolívar

This heavily shaded square is surrounded by grand colonial buildings, featuring bubbling fountains and frequent live folk dance performances. Toddler note: It is a wonderful, car-free spot to sit with a fresh fruit juice while toddlers watch the local pigeons.

Morning / 2h

Playa de Bocagrande

A bustling urban beach with warm, shallow waters where kids can splash in the gentle Caribbean waves and build dark-sand castles. Toddler note: Rent a tent-shade from a local vendor to keep delicate toddler skin protected from the intense tropical sun.

Midday / 1.5h

ChocoMuseo Cartagena

A hands-on chocolate workshop where kids can learn how cocoa beans turn into sweet treats and mold their own custom chocolate shapes. Toddler note: Even if they are too young for the full workshop, the free samples and indoor air-conditioning are total lifesavers.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Getsemaní Street Art Walk

Walk through this trendy neighborhood's whimsical streets adorned with giant, colorful murals, hanging umbrellas, and fluttering flags. Toddler note: Look out for Calle de la Sierpe, which is mostly pedestrian-only and ideal for vibrant family photos.

Morning / 3h

Aviario Nacional de Colombia

Located just outside the city on Baru Island, this world-class aviary lets families walk through massive immersive habitats filled with pink flamingos and toucans. Toddler note: Stroller-friendly gravel paths and lush tropical shade keep this exotic bird paradise comfortable for little legs.

Evening / 1.5h

Sunset Walk on the City Walls

Stroll along the massive stone walls built to keep pirates out, offering sweeping views of the Caribbean Sea as the sun dips below the horizon. Toddler note: Walk near the inner side of the wide wall paths where there is plenty of room to run safely away from the ocean-side edge.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cevichería

Get the kid-friendly coconut shrimp or the fresh, lime-cured fish ceviche that made Anthony Bourdain fall in love with this colorful spot.

Crepes & Waffles

Order the sweet banana-chocolate crepes or savory paneer pockets at this highly family-friendly Colombian staple featuring an incredible, cheap ice cream counter.

La Mulata

Get the traditional pargo frito (fried red snapper) served with sweet coconut rice and crispy patacones in a casual, highly vibrant environment.

Pastelería Mila Vargas

Indulge in decadent artisanal cakes, dulce de leche pastries, and fluffy baked goods in a beautifully decorated, air-conditioned cafe.

Café del Mar

Grab an early table for fresh fruit smoothies and passionfruit mocktails right on the historic ramparts with prime sunset views before the late-night crowd arrives.

👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense tropical heat and humidity by scheduling outdoor strolls for early mornings or late afternoons, reserving the air-conditioned indoor spaces for midday. Keep a baby carrier handy as the historic cobblestones and narrow, high sidewalks inside the Walled City are highly challenging for standard strollers.
