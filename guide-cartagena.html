Cartagena

← back to travel board
Cartagena’s Walled City is a pastel dream of bougainvillea-draped balconies and cobblestone alleys, where horse-drawn carriages clatter past centuries-old churches. The Rosario Islands offer a tranquil lagoon with calm, shallow waters perfect for baby Mary Claire to paddle. For parents, the easy beachfront access at Bocagrande means stroller-ready boardwalks and gentle sea breezes. This is a destination where luxury hotels anticipate every infant need, from bottle warmers to blackout curtains.
🚕 Getting there: Rafael Núñez International Airport to the Walled City or Bocagrande hotels: 15–20 minutes by private transfer with a pre-installed infant car seat and stroller portage — book via Lux Car Cartagena.

What to do

Early morning 9–11am

Walled City Walking Tour (Calle de las Bóvedas)

Stroll the massive fortification walls with ocean views; stroller-friendly paved sections, plenty of shade from the arched vaults. Stop for a fresh coconut at a street vendor.

Late afternoon 4–6pm

Castillo San Felipe de Barajas

A colossal fortress with ramps (stroller accessible) and shady tunnels — the breezy top offers a panoramic view. Avoid midday heat; bring a noise-canceling cover if the baby naps.

Full day 9am–3pm

Islas del Rosario (private boat charter)

A calm lagoon with crystal-clear, shallow water perfect for infant splashing. Many charters offer shaded cabins and crib storage; Playa Blanca has soft sand for crawling.

Mid-morning 10am–12pm

Museo del Oro Zenú (Gold Museum)

Small, air-conditioned museum with pre-Columbian gold — quiet enough for a napping baby. Stroller-friendly elevator and a courtyard for a break.

Late afternoon 5–7pm

Getsemani Street Art & Plaza de la Trinidad

Vibrant neighborhood with colorful murals and a lively square. Flat sidewalks (some cobbles) and shady benches; baby can watch dancers in the evening.

Morning 9–10:30am

Convento de la Popa (Hilltop Monastery)

Drive up to Cartagena’s highest point for sweeping views — stroller accessible via ramp. Cool breezes and a small garden for crawling. Skip if baby is fussy about car rides.

Morning 8–10am

Bocagrande Beach (Malecón)

A family-friendly stretch with a stroller-ready boardwalk, calm waves, and shaded palapas. Rent a sun lounger with an umbrella; beach vendors sell fresh fruit.

Midday 11am–12:30pm

Palacio de la Inquisición

Historic building with colonial architecture and a small museum. Quiet, air-conditioned rooms; stroller can navigate the ground floor. Skip the torture exhibits with a baby.

Half-day 9am–12pm

Aviario Nacional de Colombia (National Aviary – Barú)

A 20-minute drive from the city, this aviary has wide shaded paths, stroller access, and colorful birds that fascinate infants. Bring a sun hat and water spray.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Cevichería

Famous for its ceviche mixto and coconut rice. Ask for a quiet table on the terrace; highchair available. Early dinner (6pm) is calmer. Noise can be high later.

Carmen Cartagena

A chic restaurant serving contemporary Colombian cuisine, like crispy pork belly with mango. Outdoor courtyard with fans; highchair, no minimum age. Reserve a corner table for baby space.

Donde Magola

Local institution for seafood and arepas. The airy terrace is stroller-friendly; staff are very accommodating with babies. Try the fried whole fish with patacones.

Marea by Rausch

Upscale beachfront dining at Sofitel Santa Clara. Elegant but relaxed; outdoor seating with ocean breeze. Highchair and children's menu available. Their grilled octopus is a highlight.

El Boliche Cebichería

Modern ceviche bar in Getsemani. Bright and casual; have a highchair and a small play mat. The leche de tigre and tuna tartare are signature. Best visited for an early lunch (12pm).

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sofitel Legend Santa Clara Cartagena

$250–400/night

A converted 17th-century convent with a large pool, lush gardens, and family suites. Crib and bottle warmers on request; blackout curtains and soundproof windows for naps. Two restaurants, one with a kid’s menu.

Charleston Santa Teresa Cartagena

$220–380/night

Boutique luxury in the Walled City with a rooftop pool and panoramic views. Family suites have separate living areas; crib and baby bathtub provided. Quiet courtyard ideal for early bedtimes.

Hotel Capilla del Mar

$200–350/night

Bocagrande beachfront with an ocean-view pool and direct boardwalk access. Spacious suites with kitchenettes; crib and highchair available. Rooftop club lounge with evening snacks for parents.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a stroller with wide wheels for the cobblestone streets of the Walled City; the Bocagrande boardwalk is much smoother. Heat is intense — use a clip-on fan, SPF 50+, and a UV shade on the stroller; plan outings between 8-11am and 4-6pm. For medical needs, the nearest pediatric clinic is Clínica Cartagena del Mar (Bocagrande), and you can buy formula and diapers at Olímpica supermarkets or Farmacia Pasteur.
