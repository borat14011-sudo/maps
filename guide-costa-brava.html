Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire will experience a flawless blend of historic Catalan charm and modern, high-end infant convenience.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Castell de Tossa de Mar

Explore the iconic medieval castle overlooking the sea; while the steep cobblestones require a high-quality baby carrier, the lower paved pathways allow easy stroller access to dramatic, shaded coastal viewpoints.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Platja de la Mar Menuda

A protected, shallow cove featuring calm waters and a natural rocky pool nicknamed 'sa banyera de ses dones' (women's bathtub), which is perfect for dipping Mary Claire's toes safely away from waves.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM / 1.5h

Passeig del Mar Promenade

Stroll along this wide, perfectly flat, palm-lined beachfront boardwalk that is ideal for a smooth stroller nap while enjoying the ocean breeze and stopping at high-end artisan gelato shops.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM / 1.5h

Museu Municipal de Tossa de Mar

Step inside Spain's first museum of modern art, housed in the historic Governor's Palace, offering a quiet, air-conditioned sanctuary to admire Marc Chagall's masterpieces while keeping baby cool.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM / 1.5h

Parc de Sa Riera

A lush, shaded green park located along the riverbed with flat paved walking paths, towering pine trees for sun protection, and a peaceful environment ideal for an outdoor blanket picnic with Mary Claire.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 3:30 PM / 30m

Capella de la Mare de Déu dels Socors

Visit this charming, flat-access 16th-century chapel situated in the heart of the shopping district, providing a quick, peaceful stop with beautiful historical architecture.

Morning 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM / 4h

Cala Giverola

Embark on a private, skippered boat excursion to this stunning, semi-sheltered cove just north of town, featuring calm turquoise waters and excellent shade beneath the surrounding pine cliffs.

Morning 10:30 AM - 12:00 PM / 1.5h

Far de Tossa (Tossa Lighthouse)

Ride the eco-friendly, slow-moving 'Carrilet' tourist land train up the hill to the lighthouse for breathtaking views, saving your legs while keeping baby entertained by the gentle ride.

Late Afternoon 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM / 1h

Villa Romana dels Ametllers

An exceptionally preserved ancient Roman villa located right in the center of town with flat viewing platforms that make it easy to push a stroller while viewing ancient mosaics.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant Can Sophia

Located inside a glass-covered courtyard, this sophisticated venue serves exquisite Catalan-fusion dishes like wild sea bass, offering a quiet, spacious ambiance and early dinner reservations perfect for families.

Restaurant Castell Vell

Nestled inside the walled fortress, this classic establishment offers a spacious, shaded outdoor terrace where you can enjoy traditional seafood paella while Mary Claire relaxes in a provided highchair.

Restaurant Bahia

An upscale beachfront institution right on the promenade, serving fresh local cod and lobster rice with an outdoor terrace that accommodates premium strollers easily during early lunch seatings.

Sa Barca

An elegant seafood restaurant overlooking the bay, known for its pristine wild-caught fish baked in salt and highly accommodating staff who readily assist with warming baby food bottles.

Restaurant Es Portal

Housed in a charming historic building near the old town entrance, offering a peaceful, spacious garden terrace, excellent local wines, and a welcoming atmosphere for families with young children.
