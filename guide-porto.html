Porto

← back to travel board
Porto charms families with its dramatic river views, vintage trams, and warm, welcoming culture. While its hilly streets present a workout, the city rewards slow exploration with magical libraries, seaside parks, and sweet pastelarias. Transitioning from Lisbon is a breeze, opening up a compact wonderland perfect for little legs and stroller-free carriers.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or Metro do Porto (Line E) to central Ribeira hotels, or a 3-hour scenic Alfa Pendular train ride from Lisbon directly to Campanhã station.

What to do

Morning / 1h

Livraria Lello

Step into a gothic fairy tale that inspired Harry Potter, featuring a stunning crimson staircase. Go early to avoid crowds and keep a tight hold of little hands on the stairs.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Teleférico de Gaia

Glide high above the Douro River for panoramic views of the colorful Ribeira riverfront. It is stroller-accessible and short enough to keep toddlers completely mesmerized.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque da Cidade

Portugal's largest urban park features lush lawns, wild ducks, and paved paths leading straight to the beach. It is perfect for a family picnic and letting toddlers run wild near the peaceful lakes.

Morning / 2h

World of Discoveries

Embark on a multi-sensory journey through Portugal's history of exploration, complete with a gentle indoor boat ride. The boat ride and costumed guides are incredibly engaging for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1h

Douro River Six Bridges Cruise

Relax on a classic wooden rabelo boat as you sail under Porto's six iconic bridges. The gentle rocking of the boat and the cool breeze often make for a perfect stroller naptime.

Morning / 1-2h

Jardins do Palácio de Cristal

Wander through beautifully landscaped dome gardens offering stunning river views and free-roaming peacocks. Toddlers will love chasing the friendly peacocks and exploring the leafy maze paths.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado do Bolhão

Immerse your senses in this newly renovated historic market filled with fresh fruit, pastries, and lively local vendors. Pick up fresh berries for easy, healthy toddler snacks as you stroll.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Foz do Douro Promenade

Walk or ride the vintage Tram 1 to where the river meets the Atlantic, featuring a lovely lighthouse and sandy beaches. Bring a bucket and spade for some sandy play, but watch out for the strong Atlantic waves.

Morning / 2h

Sea Life Porto

Walk through an underwater tunnel surrounded by sharks, rays, and giant turtles at this oceanfront aquarium. There is an outdoor splash area and a harbor seal feeding session that toddlers adore.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café Santiago

Get the iconic Francesinha sandwich for yourself, but ask for a plain grilled chicken and rice plate for the little ones.

Chocolataria Equador

Treat the family to handmade chocolates, rich hot cocoa, and beautifully wrapped sweet treats in a whimsical, retro-styled shop.

Confeitaria do Bolhão

Grab fresh-out-of-the-oven Pasteis de Nata and soft, sweet milk bread (Pão de Deus) that toddlers can easily chew and enjoy.

Mercado Beira-Rio

Enjoy a relaxed family dinner at this indoor food hall where parents can try local codfish cakes while kids munch on simple pasta or pizza.

Cantina 32

Order the famous squid ink pasta and the whimsical banana cheesecake served in a ceramic flowerpot, which will delight kids of all ages.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the heavy stroller and opt for a sturdy baby carrier, as Porto’s steep cobblestone streets and narrow tram steps are highly unforgiving to wheels. When dining out, remember that restaurants open later for dinner, so pack plenty of snacks and look for 'prato do dia' (plate of the day) for fast, kid-friendly lunches.
