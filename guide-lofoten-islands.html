Lofoten Islands

← back to travel board
Rising sharply from the Norwegian Sea, the Lofoten Islands offer a fairy-tale landscape of jagged mountains, turquoise waters, and red fishing cabins. It is a playground where families can witness the midnight sun, play on white-sand shores, and explore cozy fishing villages. This dramatic archipelago balances raw, wild nature with easily accessible, stroller-friendly coastal paths.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min drive via rental car from Leknes Airport (LKN) to Leknes town center, or ~2 hours via E10 highway from Evenes Airport (EVE) to Svolvær

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Haukland Beach

Play on Arctic white sands and dip toes in crystal-clear turquoise waters. Toddler note: The flat, sandy shoreline is perfect for little ones to dig in without steep drop-offs.

Afternoon / 3h

Lofotr Viking Museum

Step back in time inside the reconstructed 83-meter Chieftain's House and meet real Vikings. Toddler note: Toddlers will love seeing the farm animals and boarding the gentle wooden Viking ship replica.

Morning / 2h

Nusfjord Historic Fishing Village

Wander through one of Norway's oldest and best-preserved fishing villages, preserved as an open-air museum. Toddler note: The wooden boardwalks are stroller-friendly, but keep a close hand near the dock edges.

Afternoon / 1h

Svolvær Harbor Park

Marvel at the iconic twin-pinnacle Svolværgeita rock formation towering over the town. Toddler note: Skip the steep mountain climb and enjoy the views from this harbor park where toddlers can run safely.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Henningsvær Football Stadium

See the world's most photogenic football pitch laid out on a tiny, rocky island. Toddler note: The pitch itself is flat and perfect for kicking a ball around, though supervise little ones near the surrounding rocky edges.

Morning / 1-2h

Stockfish Museum

Learn about the dried cod industry that shaped Lofoten's history inside a vintage warehouse in the village of Å. Toddler note: The sensory experience of smelling the drying fish and touching the wooden racks will intrigue curious little minds.

Evening / 1-2h

Rambergstranda Beach

Walk along a stunning crescent of white sand with a backdrop of dramatic mountain peaks. Toddler note: A flat wooden boardwalk leads right to the sand, making it highly accessible for strollers.

Morning / 2h

Kabelvåg Square

Explore the historic market square of Kabelvåg, featuring local crafts, a playground, and delicious bakeries. Toddler note: The car-free square lets toddlers wander safely while parents enjoy a hot coffee.

Afternoon / 1h

Magic Ice Svolvær

Walk through a sub-zero gallery filled with glowing ice sculptures depicting Viking lore. Toddler note: Bundle up in the provided warm ponchos; toddlers will be mesmerized by the colored lights reflecting on the ice.

🍽️ Where to eat

Børsen Spiseri

Order the traditional stockfish prepared with stewed peas and bacon in a historic 1800s warehouse in Svolvær.

Anita's Sjømat

Get the famous 'underdog' fish burger topped with shrimp and salmon, eaten on the outdoor deck in Sakrisøy.

Klatrekafeen

Try the hearty homemade bacalao stew or a slice of warm apple cake in this cozy climber's cafe in Henningsvær.

Restaurant Karoline

Savor the fresh pan-fried halibut with seasonal vegetables while looking out at the dramatic Reinefjord in Reine.

Unstad Arctic Surf Cafe

Indulge in the legendary, freshly baked giant cinnamon buns in a cool, surfer-vibe cafe in Unstad.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and windproof outerwear even in summer, as Arctic weather can change in minutes. Rent a rental car equipped with a pre-booked child car seat, as public transport is infrequent and a car is essential for reaching remote family-friendly trailheads.
