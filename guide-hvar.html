Hvar

← back to travel board
Hvar welcomes families with its lavender-scented breeze, sparkling turquoise waters, and ancient stone streets polished smooth by centuries of footsteps. Beyond its glitzy reputation, the island offers quiet pine-shaded coves, gentle shallow bays, and car-free piazzas perfect for little explorers. It is a Mediterranean dream where slow-paced island life meets rich history.
🚕 Getting there: ~2 hours total: 45-minute Split Airport taxi to the ferry port, then a 1-hour catamaran ride directly to Hvar Town

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Spanish Fortress (Tvrdjava Fortica)

Climb up to this historic fortress for panoramic views of the red-roofed town and Pakleni Islands; the path is stroller-accessible but steep, so a baby carrier is best.

Morning / 4-5h

Pakleni Islands Boat Trip

Rent a taxi boat to the pine-shaded beaches of Mlini, where calm, crystal-clear shallow waters are perfect for splashing. Toddlers will love the boat ride and searching for tiny seashells along the shore.

Morning / 3-4h

Pokonji Dol Beach

A lovely gravel beach within walking distance of Hvar Town, offering sunbed rentals and a flat, shallow entry for safe paddling. Toddlers can easily play in the smooth pebbles under the shade of beach umbrellas.

Evening / 1-2h

St. Stephen's Square (Pjaca)

The largest piazza in Dalmatia is completely car-free, allowing toddlers to run freely while parents enjoy a coffee. Admire the historic cathedral and listen to the gentle bubbling of the central well.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Brusje Lavender Fields

Take a short drive to this peaceful inland village surrounded by beautiful stone-walled lavender fields that bloom in summer. It is a sensory delight for toddlers who will love smelling the fresh flowers and spotting butterflies.

Morning / 1h

Hvar Town Green Market

Browse the colorful stalls of this open-air market for fresh local peaches, figs, and sweet pastries. Toddlers can sample local fruits while you soak up the lively morning atmosphere.

Afternoon / 1h

Park Djardin

A shady oasis right next to the cathedral, featuring a modest children's playground under towering pine trees. It is the perfect spot for a quick toddler energy burn in the cooler shade.

Morning / 3-4h

Dubovica Cove

A picture-perfect emerald bay with a rustic stone house restaurant, ideal for a quiet family beach day. Note that the trail down is steep and rocky, so carry toddlers in a backpack carrier.

Morning / 1-2h

Franciscan Monastery and Museum

Visit this tranquil waterfront monastery featuring a peaceful courtyard, a giant cypress tree, and ancient exhibits. Its quiet garden offers a calm, shaded retreat where toddlers can rest away from the crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Dalmatino

Order the homemade gnocchi with black truffles or the fresh sea bass, and enjoy the incredibly welcoming staff who treat kids like royalty.

Macondo

Tucked in a charming stone alley, order the traditional Gregada fish stew which is mild and easy for toddlers to share.

Fig Hvar

Try the sweet potato fries and flatbreads in this cozy courtyard, offering a relaxed vibe and creative, kid-approved vegetarian-friendly options.

Alviz

Sit in the leafy garden near the bus station and order their excellent wood-fired pizzas, a guaranteed hit for toddlers.

Icy Bar Hvar

Grab a scoop of fresh, creamy lavender-honey or wild berry gelato to enjoy during your evening stroll along the harbor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a high-quality baby carrier instead of a bulky stroller, as Hvar's historic streets are filled with steep stone steps and cobblestones. Pack water shoes for the whole family, as nearly all beaches are pebbly and can harbor sea urchins.
