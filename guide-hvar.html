Hvar

Hvar welcomes discerning families with its intoxicating blend of Venetian-era architecture, fragrant lavender fields, and the shimmering Adriatic Sea. The car-free paved Lungomare promenade stretches beautifully along the coastline, offering an ultra-smooth, stroller-friendly path shaded by fragrant pine trees. Parents can easily wheel baby Mary Claire from the historic town square to the iconic Bonj les Bains beach club without negotiating difficult stairs or rough terrain. This islet paradise pairs rich Dalmatian culture with private beachside cabanas, ensuring Will and Kathryn can savor fine local Pošip wine while their little one naps in cool sea breezes.
🚕 Getting there: From Split Airport (SPU), book a private luxury minivan to Split Harbor (25 minutes), followed by a private motorboat charter directly to Hvar Riva (50 minutes) to avoid crowded public catamarans and easily store your stroller.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Hvar Public Square (Pjaca)

The largest square in Dalmatia is completely paved and car-free, making it exceptionally easy for a smooth stroller ride while admiring the cathedral. Will and Kathryn can enjoy morning espresso at an outdoor table while Mary Claire watches the gentle bustle in a safe, open environment.

Mid-day 11am-3pm / 4h

Bonj les Bains

An iconic 1930s stone colonnade beach club offering private waterfront cabanas. Perfect for a luxury family beach day where you can secure shade, enjoy personalized food service, and let Mary Claire splash in the shallow, crystal-clear coastal waters.

Morning to afternoon 10am-2pm / 4h

Palmižana (Sveti Klement)

Take a private water taxi to this nearby car-free island sanctuary. The botanical gardens offer shaded paths and the shallow, sandy cove of Vinogradišće Bay is ideal for dipping an infant's toes into calm waters away from the Hvar crowds.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Tvrdalj Castle

Located in nearby quiet Stari Grad, this fortified renaissance castle features a serene interior seawater fish pool surrounded by stone arches. It is exceptionally peaceful, well-shaded, and cool, making it an excellent quiet spot for an infant's afternoon nap in a carrier.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Vrboska Town

Often called 'Little Venice', this incredibly quiet and flat canal town is highly stroller-friendly. Walking along the stone bridges and calm waterways offers a tranquil alternative to the busier Hvar Town, perfect for a peaceful family walk.

Morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Hvar Lungomare

This flat, paved seaside promenade connects Hvar Town to the western resort bays. Pine trees provide ample natural canopy and shade, making it the premier route for a scenic stroll to lull Mary Claire to sleep with the sound of the waves.

Morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Pokonji Dol Beach

A stunning, accessible pebble beach located just a short drive from Hvar town. A flat dirt road leads to the bay, where the family can rent sun loungers with umbrellas and enjoy a quiet, calm swim in highly transparent, shallow waters.

Late afternoon 5-7pm / 2h

Stari Grad Plain

A UNESCO World Heritage site preserving ancient Greek agricultural landscapes. The flat, paved agricultural paths are perfect for an easy, flat stroller stroll through historic olive groves and vineyards, away from any coastal winds.

Sunset 6-7:30pm / 1.5h

Spanish Fortress (Fortica)

Skip the steep uphill stairs and take a private luxury taxi directly to the fortress entrance. The historic ramparts are wide, offering Will and Kathryn legendary panoramic views of the Pakleni Islands while Mary Claire rests comfortably in a baby carrier.

🍽️ Where to eat

Zori Restaurant

High-end Mediterranean dining located on the Palmižana waterfront. Their spacious outdoor terrace accommodates strollers easily, and booking an early lunch ensures a quiet atmosphere to savor fresh lobster while baby enjoys the sea breeze.

Gariful

Hvar's premier luxury seafood spot directly on the Riva. Known for its ultra-fresh catch of the day, they provide excellent infant highchairs and spacious outdoor seating where the gentle ambient hum of the harbor easily masks any baby noises.

Giaxa

Set inside a historic 15th-century Gothic palace, this refined restaurant serves traditional Dalmatian cuisine in a peaceful courtyard. The quiet, enclosed outdoor area is perfect for avoiding the busy main streets, offering a calm and elegant early dinner setting.

Val Marina Restaurant

An elegant waterfront restaurant at the Adriana Hotel. Savor premium grilled fish on their breeze-cooled terrace which features spacious table spacing, highchairs, and immediate access to the flat harbor front if baby needs a quick walk.

Macondo

Tucked away in a stone alley, this legendary seafood establishment opens early for dinner. The friendly staff are exceptionally welcoming to babies, and their outdoor street-side tables provide a lively but relaxed setting for a classic Gregada fish stew.

$200-400/night
