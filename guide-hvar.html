Hvar

← back to travel board
Hvar balances high-end coastal sophistication with a remarkably relaxed, slow-paced Mediterranean charm that is perfect for families traveling with a toddler. The island's car-free marble plazas, gentle pine-shaded coastal paths, and crystal-clear, shallow bays offer a safe, stress-free playground for little ones. While famous for its summer nightlife, visiting during the day reveals a historic, sensory-rich haven where you can easily push a stroller from a waterfront café to a scenic park. It is the ultimate destination for parents seeking world-class seafood, stunning historic vistas, and a premium, highly manageable European getaway with an infant.
🚕 Getting there: ~45-minute taxi from Split Airport to Split Ferry Port, followed by a ~1-hour catamaran ride to Hvar Town, or a ~2-hour car ferry to Stari Grad.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

St. Stephen's Square (Pjaca)

The largest public square in Dalmatia is entirely pedestrianized, paved with smooth marble that is perfect for strollers and toddler steps. Sip an espresso at an outdoor café while your little one safely explores the vast, open space away from car traffic.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Fortica Fortress (Tvrđava Španjola)

This historic castle offers breathtaking, panoramic views of the Pakleni Islands and Hvar Town. The climb is steep and paved with stairs, so leave the stroller behind and use a premium baby carrier to reach the pine-shaded summit.

Morning / 3h

Pokonji Dol Beach

A stunning, accessible pebble beach featuring shallow, calm waters ideal for a toddler dipping their toes in the Adriatic. Walk the flat, scenic coastal path from town with a stroller, or take a quick 5-minute taxi.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Palmižana Botanical Garden and Beach

Take a quick 15-minute taxi boat to this car-free island oasis featuring lush exotic plants, sandy shores, and shaded gravel paths. The calm, shallow bay of Vinogradišće is perfect for baby splashing, though a carrier is recommended over a stroller.

Morning / 1h

Green Market Hvar (Pazar)

An open-air morning market located right near the bus station, bustling with local vendors selling fresh figs, peaches, and lavender. It is a sensory treat for a toddler and a great spot to stock up on healthy, fresh fruit snacks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Jelsa Municipal Park (Perivoj)

One of the largest and most beautiful parks in Dalmatia, featuring towering poplars, pine trees, and flat, paved stroller-friendly paths. It includes a modern, safe playground where your toddler can slide and swing under the cool shade of Mediterranean trees.

Morning / 2h

Stari Grad Plain (Ager)

A protected UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing ancient Greek agricultural geometry with flat, quiet dirt paths perfect for a stroller walk. Stroll past ancient olive groves and vineyards while enjoying a peaceful, crowd-free country vibe.

Midday / 1h

Franciscan Monastery Cloister

A serene, quiet sanctuary right on the waterfront with a shady courtyard home to a spectacular, ancient cypress tree. It offers a cool, peaceful escape from the midday sun where you can walk your stroller in quiet comfort.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Vrboska Canal Walk

Stroll along the stone bridges of this quiet, picturesque town often called 'Little Venice'. The flat, paved harbor pathways make for exceptionally smooth stroller pushing alongside gently bobbing wooden boats.

🍽️ Where to eat

Alviz

Located near the main bus station, this family-run eatery features a spacious, shaded garden courtyard with plenty of room for strollers. Order the wood-fired Capricciosa pizza or a classic Dalmatian peka while your toddler enjoys the relaxed, casual atmosphere.

Lungo Mare

Perched on a quieter stretch of the waterfront, this restaurant offers stellar sea views and exceptionally warm service that welcomes babies. Order the fresh grilled sea bass with Swiss chard and request an early outdoor terrace table for a relaxed dinner.

Restaurant Mustać

Right on the shoreline of Pokonji Dol Beach, this casual spot is perfect for a midday lunch while your toddler naps under a beach umbrella. Try the signature black risotto or fresh grilled squid, steps away from the water.

Fig Hvar

Tucked away in a charming, open courtyard, this highly popular spot offers creative, flavorful dishes in a relaxed, casual setting. Go right at opening for dinner to secure a spacious table and enjoy the sweet potato fries and flatbreads.

Konoba Kokot

Located in the inland village of Vrbanj, this highly authentic, rustic tavern features a spacious outdoor courtyard with plenty of space for a toddler. Indulge in home-raised lamb, fresh local goat cheese, and wild fig liqueurs in a warm, welcoming environment.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Amfora Hvar Grand Beach Resort

Boasts a massive, multi-level freshwater cascade pool, a dedicated children's pool, and a private beach area. Located along a stroller-friendly waterfront promenade, it offers spacious family rooms and complimentary cribs.

Valamar Amicor Green Resort

This eco-friendly resort in Stari Grad is designed specifically for families, featuring family suites, outdoor pools with slides, a soft-play indoor area, and a dedicated baby club. The quiet, pine-shaded grounds are entirely stroller-friendly.

Palace Elisabeth, Hvar Heritage Hotel

For a luxury option, this historic five-star hotel sits directly on the flat, stroller-friendly main square. It offers premium baby-proofing services, a heated indoor pool, spacious suites, and serene soundproofing to ensure uninterrupted baby naps.

👨‍👩‍👧 Skip the heavy stroller for exploring historic alleys and opt for a high-quality baby carrier to navigate the steep stone staircases easily.
