Gdańsk & Sopot

← back to travel board
Where colorful, Dutch-style facades meet the salty breeze of the Baltic Sea, Gdańsk and Sopot offer a double-dose of European charm and seaside relaxation. Children will love exploring pirate ships on the Motława River, while parents can stroll down Europe’s longest wooden pier in Sopot. It is a playground of history, soft sand, and legendary amber treasures perfect for tiny explorers.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Uber from Gdańsk Lech Wałęsa Airport to central Gdańsk, or ~30 min to Sopot beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Neptune's Fountain & Długi Targ

Walk down the colorful Royal Route to marvel at the grand bronze fountain of Neptune. Toddlers will love chasing bubbles from street performers and spotting the stone gargoyles on historical buildings.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Sopot Pier (Molo)

Take a breezy walk along Europe's longest wooden pier for stunning Baltic views and swan-spotting. It is entirely stroller-friendly, and there is a great playground right on the beach next to the entrance.

Morning / 3-4h

Oliwa Zoo (Gdańsk Zoological Garden)

Nestled in a lush forest valley, this expansive zoo features wide, flat paths and a miniature train ride. Toddlers can get close to friendly farm animals at the petting zoo section.

Evening / 1-2h

Słony Spichlerz Food Hall

This vibrant, modern food market on the riverfront offers diverse culinary stalls under one roof, satisfying picky eaters easily. High chairs are available, and the casual atmosphere means nobody minds a noisy toddler.

Afternoon / 2h

Oliwa Park & Whispering Caves

A peaceful oasis with manicured gardens, quiet ponds, and a greenhouse. Toddlers will be enchanted by the acoustic whispering caves where they can hear soft sounds from across the path.

Morning / 2h

SS Sołdek & National Maritime Museum

Explore a real steamship docked on the Motława River. While the ship has steep stairs, the interactive 'People-Ships-Ports' exhibition next door has excellent hands-on water play areas for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Galeon Lew Pirate Ship Cruise

Sail from Gdańsk to Westerplatte on a fantasy-styled pirate galleon complete with maritime music. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the costumed crew and the gentle bobbing of the river water.

Morning / 2-3h

Sopot Beach & Playground

Run along the soft, powdery white sand of the Baltic coast and dip tiny toes in the gentle surf. The beach is lined with excellent wooden playgrounds, making it easy to transition from sandcastle building to swinging.

Morning / 2-3h

Hevelianum

Located in a 19th-century military fort on a hill, this science center features interactive exhibits. The 'Creative Playground' zone is specifically designed for toddlers to learn through sensory play.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pierogarnia Mandu

Get the traditional boiled pierogi stuffed with sweet berries and sweet cream for the kids, or savory wild mushroom pierogi for adults.

Słona Woda (Sopot)

Enjoy fresh Baltic cod served with seasonal vegetables in a beautifully bright, kid-friendly restaurant with a dedicated play corner.

Pyra Bar

Order the crispy potato pancakes topped with sour cream or sweet applesauce, a guaranteed toddler hit.

Dwie Zmiany

This artistic café serves excellent local breakfasts, vegan cakes, and specialty coffee in a relaxed atmosphere with toys and books for children.

Piekarnia Pajda

Grab a traditional Polish sweet bun filled with blueberries (jagodzianka) or a fresh butter croissant for a quick, delicious park snack.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a lightweight stroller for navigating Gdańsk's historic cobblestones, but switch to a baby carrier or wide-wheeled stroller when heading onto the sandy beaches of Sopot.
