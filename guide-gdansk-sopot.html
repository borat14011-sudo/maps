Gdańsk & Sopot

← back to travel board
Where cobblestone Hanseatic streets meet the whispering waves of the Baltic Sea, Gdańsk and Sopot offer an enchanting double-feature for families. Kids will love chasing seagulls on Europe’s longest wooden pier, while parents soak in the rich history and vibrant cafe culture. It is a seaside playground where fairy-tale architecture hides modern, toddler-friendly discoveries around every corner.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or private transfer from Gdańsk Lech Wałęsa Airport (GDN) to the historic Old Town, or ~30 min to Sopot's beachside hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Sopot Pier (Molo) and Beach

Stroll along Europe’s longest wooden pier for endless sea views, breezy walks, and soft sand play. Toddlers will love chasing the swans near the shore and playing on the wide, clean beach.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Historic Long Market (Długi Targ)

Walk down Gdańsk's colorful royal route to admire towering architecture and the iconic Neptune's Fountain. It is wide and pedestrian-only, making it incredibly safe for toddlers to toddle freely.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Hevelianum

An interactive science center housed in a 19th-century military fort with stellar views over the city. The 'Creative Playground' zone is tailor-made for toddlers to build, touch, and learn through sensory play.

Afternoon / 2 hours

Oliwa Park (Park Oliwski)

A beautifully manicured French-style park featuring quiet ponds, whispering bamboo groves, and a greenhouse. It is an ideal, quiet spot for a stroller nap or feeding the ducks.

Morning / 1.5 hours

Gdańsk Pirate Ship Cruise (Galeon Lew)

Board a stylized 17th-century galleon ship for a scenic ride down the Motława River to Westerplatte. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the pirate staff, live sea shanties, and passing ships.

Evening / 30 mins

Gdańsk Carousel (Karuzela Gdańska)

Take a spin on this gorgeous, traditional double-decker carousel featuring hand-painted scenes of the city. Located right in the heart of the old town, it’s a quick and magical treat for little ones.

Morning / 3-4 hours

Gdańsk Zoo (Miejski Ogród Zoologiczny)

One of Poland's largest zoos, nestled in a lush valley with spacious, natural enclosures for the animals. There's a mini-zoo section where toddlers can pet goats and a small train to ride when little legs get tired.

Morning / 1 hour

Gdańsk Market Hall (Hala Targowa)

Dive into this historic covered market filled with local cheeses, fresh berries, pastries, and daily essentials. It’s a sensory treat for toddlers to see the colorful stalls, and parents can grab quick, cheap bites.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

President Ronald Reagan Park (Park Reagana)

A massive seaside park located right behind the beach with giant, modern wooden playgrounds, cycling paths, and duck ponds. The sprawling play areas have dedicated zones safe for crawling and climbing toddlers.

Get the homemade pierogi or the pan-seared Baltic salmon in a cozy, elegant atmosphere that warmly welcomes families.

Pyra Bar

Get the potato pancakes (placki ziemniaczane) topped with sour cream or sweet applesauce, which toddlers absolutely devour.

Słona Woda

Get the fresh fish of the day or the kids' menu chicken tender strip plates right next to Sopot's beach dunes.

Pierogarnia Mandu

Get the traditional boiled pierogi filled with sweet cottage cheese and berries for the kids, and savory wild mushroom pierogi for yourself.

Cały Gaweł Cantine

Get the fluffy pancakes with seasonal fruits and local honey for a perfect, family-friendly brunch near Sopot station.
