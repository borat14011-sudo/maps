What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Molo w Sopocie (Sopot Pier)

Walk along Europe’s longest wooden pier for endless Baltic views and brisk sea air that will soothe any fussy toddler. The wide wooden planks are perfectly smooth for strollers, and there are plenty of benches for quick bottle breaks.

Afternoon / 2h

Park Oliwski im. Adama Mickiewicza

A serene, manicured English garden oasis in Gdańsk featuring quiet shaded paths, a whispering palm house, and duck ponds. It’s the ideal spot for an afternoon contact nap in the carrier while walking through the peaceful Japanese garden section.

Late Morning / 1h

Karuzela Gdańska

Let your toddler marvel at this beautiful, traditional double-decker carousel located right in the heart of the historic center. It features hand-painted figures and offers a slow, magical ride that is perfectly safe for a parent to ride alongside an infant.

Morning / 2h

Akwarium Gdyńskie (Gdynia Aquarium)

Take a short, scenic drive north to Gdynia to explore this compact, stroller-friendly aquarium showcasing Baltic sea life. The glowing jellyfish displays and colorful coral reefs are mesmerizing sensory experiences for a toddler.

Morning / 2-3h

Wydział Zabaw at Europejskie Centrum Solidarności

An exceptional, modern indoor play department specifically designed for young children inside this striking architectural landmark. While parents appreciate the historic exhibits, the toddler will love the safe, soft-play sensory elements and maritime-themed obstacles.

Morning / 2h

Hevelianum

Located in a scenic hilltop 19th-century military fort, this science center features interactive, low-to-the-ground sensory exhibits perfect for curious toddlers. The surrounding park offers panoramic views of Gdańsk and paved, stroller-accessible walking paths.

Morning / 3-4h

Gdański Ogród Zoologiczny

Spanning a massive, lush forest valley in Oliwa, this is one of Poland's most beautiful zoos with spacious enclosures and paved paths. Rent a pull-along wooden wagon at the entrance to easily transport your toddler and diaper bags across the shaded terrain.

Afternoon / 2h

Park Brzeźnieński im. J. J. Haffnera

This coastal park features flat, paved pathways winding through pine trees directly parallel to the sandy beach. It is a fantastic stroller-friendly alternative to the crowded city center, complete with seaside playgrounds and direct stroller ramp access to the sand.

Evening / 1-2h

Motława River Embankment Walk

Stroll along the vibrant, newly redeveloped Granary Island waterfront boardwalk with its incredibly smooth pathways and pedestrian-only bridges. You can admire the historic Gdańsk Crane and passing boats without navigating any bumpy cobblestones.

🍽️ Where to eat

Słona Woda

Order the delicate Baltic cod loin served with seasonal vegetables. Located inside the quiet Hotel Testa near the Sopot beach, it offers a spacious, sunlit dining room and outdoor garden terrace where a sleeping toddler in a stroller is warmly welcomed.

Chleb i Wino Chmielna

Sip on their house-made prosecco cocktails and order the artisanal truffle paste pasta. The spacious, bustling atmosphere on Granary Island naturally masks occasional toddler squeals, and they have high chairs readily available.

Tawerna Rybna

Try the traditional Polish zander fillet pan-fried in butter. Located right along the Motława River, its outdoor seating area is spacious enough for a stroller and provides constant boat-watching entertainment for your little one.

Polskie Smaki Restaurant

Feast on their signature handmade pierogi stuffed with slow-cooked duck. Because it is located inside the Sheraton Sopot Hotel, it features world-class family facilities, ample space between tables, and exceptionally accommodating service for infants.

White Marlin Sopot

Enjoy a premium seafood platter of fresh Baltic turbot right on the beach. This chic, Hamptons-style beachfront venue has a spacious deck perfect for strollers and an elegant yet breezy atmosphere where children are common.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sofitel Grand Sopot

An iconic, ultra-luxury historic hotel right on the beach with a private park and indoor pool. They provide high-end wooden cribs, baby bath amenities, and a stellar babysitting service so you can enjoy a quiet drink overlooking the pier.

Radisson Hotel & Suites, Gdańsk

Situated on the ultra-accessible Granary Island, this modern hotel features spacious family suites and a beautiful indoor swimming pool. The location is entirely flat and stroller-accessible, allowing you to walk straight into the historic old town without steps.

Sheraton Sopot Hotel

Boasting a prime location directly adjacent to the Sopot Pier and the beach, this resort features incredibly spacious family rooms, a dedicated kids' playroom, and a pristine indoor pool. They are exceptionally well-equipped with premium baby cribs and bottle warmers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Skip the historic cobblestones of central Gdańsk by choosing the smooth, newly paved boardwalks of Granary Island and the Sopot beach promenade for effortless stroller navigation. Utilize Poland's highly reliable and modern taxi app, FreeNow or Uber, which easily allows you to request comfort-class vehicles with plenty of trunk space for a full-size travel stroller. When dining out, look for the 'Kącik dla dzieci' (kids' corner) sign, as most premium Tri-City establishments are exceptionally baby-friendly and offer high-end changing facilities.
