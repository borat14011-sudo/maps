Veracruz

← back to travel board
Veracruz enchants families with its breezy coastal boardwalks, colonial fortresses, and the rhythmic beat of marimba music echoing across the Zócalo. From the depths of Latin America's premier aquarium to the lush tropical canopy of the surrounding lagoons, this historic port city offers a rich tapestry of culture and nature. It is a warm, welcoming destination where slow-paced days easily accommodate travelers of all generations.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Acuario de Veracruz

Explore one of Latin America's largest aquariums, featuring majestic sharks, playful manatees, and colorful toucans. Toddlers will love the stroller-friendly ramps, touch tanks, and the mesmerizing underwater glass tunnel.

Morning / 2h

San Juan de Ulúa Fortress

Step back in time at this historic island fortress filled with stone bridges, ramparts, and tales of pirates. A sturdy baby carrier is highly recommended here, as the uneven cobblestones and steep steps are not stroller-friendly.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

El Malecón

Stroll down the wide, breezy oceanfront boardwalk to watch massive cargo ships navigate the harbor and see local divers. It is completely flat and paved, making it the perfect spot for a smooth, sunset stroller ride.

Evening / 1-2h

Zócalo de Veracruz

Immerse yourselves in the city's heartbeat where live marimba bands play and locals dance danzón in the plaza. The open, car-free pedestrian zone gives toddlers plenty of safe space to dance along and burn off energy.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Infantil Cri-Cri

This whimsical city park is dedicated to Mexico's famous children's music composer, featuring playgrounds and colorful themed areas. It is an easy, low-stress spot for toddlers to slide, swing, and play with local kids.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Villa del Mar

Relax on this popular city beach characterized by shallow, calm waters and convenient palapa restaurants right on the sand. Toddlers can safely splash in the gentle surf while parents enjoy fresh coconut water under the shade.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

Navigate this bustling indoor market filled with towering stacks of tropical fruits, colorful pinatas, and local crafts. Keep your toddler close in a carrier to absorb the vibrant sensory sights and sweet aromas of fresh mangoes.

Afternoon / 3h

Laguna de Mandinga

Take a gentle boat ride through lush mangrove tunnels just south of the city to spot white herons and pelicans. The calm water ensures a smooth ride, and toddlers will be captivated by the crabs scurrying along the tree roots.

Afternoon / 2h

Museo Naval México

Discover maritime history inside this beautiful historic building featuring interactive navigation simulators and scale-model ships. Toddlers will enjoy the large, air-conditioned indoor courtyard and the hands-on toy boat displays.

Where to eat

  • Gran Café de la Parroquia — Tap your glass with a spoon to summon the waiter for hot milk poured from high above, paired with a fluffy, toddler-approved sweet concha.
  • Pescado a la Veracruzana — Taste this iconic regional dish of mild, tender fish simmered in a savory tomato, olive, and caper sauce that is flavorful yet gentle on toddler palates.
  • Nieves at Nevería Güero Güera — Cool off with a cup of traditional, hand-churned tropical water ice in sweet, refreshing flavors like coconut, mango, or soursop.
  • Villa Rica Mocambo — Dine on superb, fresh coastal seafood at this family-friendly establishment featuring an outdoor play area where kids can dig in the sand.
