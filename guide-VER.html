Veracruz

← back to travel board
Veracruz blends lively marimba music on the Zócalo with rich maritime history and lush coastal nature. Perfect for families, it offers gentle Gulf breezes, delicious street eats, and the biggest aquarium in Latin America. Kids will love the friendly local energy and easygoing coastal pace.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Acuario de Veracruz

Explore Latin America's largest aquarium to see sharks, manatees, and vibrant coral reefs. Toddlers will love the giant walk-through glass tunnel and the playful penguin exhibit.

Morning / 2h

San Juan de Ulúa

Wander through this historic 16th-century island fortress filled with stone bridges and legends of pirates. Keep toddlers secure in a carrier as there are many uneven steps and drops.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

El Malecón

Take a breezy evening stroll along the bustling harbor boardwalk to watch passing cargo ships and street performers. It is completely stroller-friendly and great for letting toddlers stretch their legs.

Evening / 1-2h

Zócalo de Veracruz

Experience the lively heart of the city, where marimba bands play and locals dance the danzón in the evenings. Toddlers can safely run around the pedestrian-only plaza while you enjoy a cold drink.

Afternoon / 1h

Parque Zamora

This lush downtown park offers a shaded escape from the tropical heat with a charming vintage tram display. Toddlers will enjoy feeding the friendly pigeons and riding the small coin-operated rides.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

Immerse your senses in this bustling market packed with colorful tropical fruits, traditional toys, and local snacks. It can get crowded, so carry your toddler to navigate the narrow, vibrant aisles easily.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Villa del Mar

This popular city beach features calm, shallow Gulf waters perfect for splashing and building sandcastles. Rent a shaded palapa so your toddler can nap comfortably out of the strong sun.

Midday / 2h

Mandinga Lagoon

Take a relaxing boat ride through tranquil mangrove tunnels filled with herons and egrets just outside the city. The gentle rocking of the boat is highly soothing for toddlers, and life vests are provided.

Afternoon / 2h

Museo Naval México

Discover Mexico's maritime history through highly interactive exhibits, detailed ship models, and a real simulator. Toddlers will love the spacious, grassy central courtyard and the colorful children's playroom.

Where to eat

  • Gran Café de la Parroquia — Clink your spoon against your glass to summon the waiter for traditional café con leche while kids enjoy sweet concha breads slathered in butter.
  • Nieves del Malecón — Cool off with a scoop of artisanal tropical-flavored shaved ice, like sweet coconut or mamey, from this legendary boardwalk stand.
  • Pescado a la Veracruzana — Taste the city's signature mild dish of fresh white fish cooked in a savory, toddler-approved tomato, olive, and mild bell pepper sauce.
  • Picadas Jarochas — Try these soft, freshly pressed corn patties pinched at the edges and topped with mild salsa and crumbled cheese, which make the perfect hand-held toddler snack.
👨‍👩‍👧 Keep your family cool by planning outdoor walks during the breezy early mornings or lively late afternoons to avoid the intense midday coastal heat. Use a lightweight stroller for the flat Malecón, but pack a baby carrier for navigating the uneven historic stones around San Juan de Ulúa.
