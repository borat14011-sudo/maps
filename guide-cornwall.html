It is the ultimate British seaside escape where childhood memories are made under salty sea breezes.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

The Eden Project

Explore giant biomes housing the world's largest indoor rainforest, complete with canopy walkways. Toddlers will love the sensory nature trails and the dedicated outdoor play areas.

Afternoon / 2-3h

St Michael's Mount

Cross the historic cobblestone causeway at low tide to reach a fairy-tale castle on its own rocky island. For toddlers, take the amphibious boat back if the tide rolls in.

Morning / 3h

Lanhydrock House and Garden

A magnificent Victorian estate featuring expansive, flat gardens perfect for little legs to run around. Toddlers will especially enjoy the dedicated balance bike track and the wooden adventure playground.

Morning / 2-3h

Tintagel Castle

Walk across the spectacular footbridge to discover the cliffside ruins linked to the legend of King Arthur. The steep steps can be tough, so use a sturdy baby carrier for toddlers.

Afternoon / 3h

Porthcurno Beach

A paradise of fine white sand and turquoise waters sheltered by high cliffs, ideal for sandcastle building. The stream flowing into the sea provides a safe, shallow paddling spot for toddlers.

Morning / 3h

The Lost Gardens of Heligan

Wander through magical restored gardens featuring giant sleeping stone sculptures and a boardwalk jungle. Toddlers will love meeting the rare-breed farm animals at the on-site barn.

Afternoon / 2h

Newquay Harbour and Fistral Beach

Watch the colorful fishing boats bobbing in the harbor and look out for resident wild seals. The adjacent sandy beaches are perfect for toddlers to splash in shallow rock pools.

Morning / 1-2h

Truro Farmers Market

Stroll through this vibrant, bustling weekly market to sample local cheeses, fresh pastries, and Cornish fudge. Toddlers will love the lively atmosphere and the delicious, warm sweet treats on offer.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Gannel Estuary

A tranquil tidal estuary offering expansive sandy flats at low tide, ideal for a peaceful family walk. Toddlers can safely splash in the warm, shallow tidal pools left behind by the retreating sea.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Beach Hut

Order the famous Extreme Hot Chocolate loaded with marshmallows and cream alongside a fresh local crab burger.

Stein's Fish & Chips

Treat the family to locally caught cod in crispy batter served with traditional mushy peas and chunky chips.

The Hidden Hut

Feast on fresh wood-fired seafood or a hearty homemade pasty right on the beach at this outdoor wooden lunch hut.

Godrevy Cafe

Stop by for a classic Cornish cream tea with warm scones, clotted cream, and strawberry jam after a beach walk.

Lusty Glaze Beach Restaurant

Enjoy stone-baked pizzas and fresh seafood directly on the sand while the kids play safely nearby.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry a sturdy all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier, as many coastal paths and historic sites feature steep steps and uneven, rocky terrain. Pack layers and windproof jackets even in summer, as the Atlantic breeze can change the weather quickly on the beaches.
