What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Giardino di Boboli

These historic Medici gardens offer wide gravel pathways and expansive lawns perfect for letting your toddler stretch her legs and crawl safely. Bring a high-performance travel stroller or a baby carrier to navigate the sloped gravel pathways leading up to the gorgeous fountain views.

Lunchtime / 1.5 hours

Primo Piano at Mercato Centrale

The spacious second floor of this historic market is a vibrant, bustling food hall where toddler noise easily blends into the lively local atmosphere. It features diverse food stalls so parents can enjoy premium Tuscan wines and fresh pasta while the baby rests in a high chair.

Late Afternoon / 30 minutes

Antica Giostra della Toscana

Located in the heart of Piazza della Repubblica, this beautiful, nostalgic 19th-century carousel is a quick and magical treat for a toddler. It is easily accessible for strollers, and parents can ride along with infants on the stationary bench seats.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Giardino dell'Orticoltura

A serene local park featuring a magnificent 19th-century glass tepidarium, a dedicated toddler playground, and plenty of paved walking paths. It is the perfect off-the-beaten-path spot to escape the tourist crowds and let your daughter play on the grass.

Morning / 2-3 hours

Fattoria di Maiano

Located just 15 minutes outside the city center in Fiesole, this real working estate features an educational farm where toddlers can see donkeys, geese, and goats. It offers a refreshing green break from the city's stone streets with flat, stroller-friendly farm paths.

Afternoon / 1.5 hours

Parco delle Cascine

Florence's largest public park runs flat along the Arno River, offering miles of paved, car-free paths ideal for a long stroller run or nap-time walk. Stop by the Pavoniere area for a coffee while your toddler watches local ducks swim in the nearby ponds.

Morning / 2 hours

Uffizi Gallery Private Family Tour

Book an early morning private family-tailored tour to breeze past the long queues with skip-the-line tickets. While strollers are permitted, utilizing a baby carrier allows you to navigate the gallery's grand staircases and historic corridors with maximum ease.

Morning / 1.5 hours

La Specola (Museum of Natural History)

Recently renovated, this fascinating museum houses spectacular taxidermy and anatomical collections that visually captivate toddlers without the overwhelming crowds of art museums. The halls are wide, quiet, and highly stroller-accessible, making for a peaceful morning indoor stroll.

Afternoon / 1 hour

Palazzo Vecchio Courtyard and Family Kit

The ground-floor courtyard of this medieval fortress is free to enter, stroller-friendly, and features a beautiful central fountain that toddlers love to watch. Families can rent a specialized museum 'Family Kit' bag filled with tactile activities to keep young children engaged.

🍽️ Where to eat

Trattoria dell'Oste (Duomo location)

Famous for serving the finest Bistecca alla Fiorentina, this spacious restaurant easily accommodates larger strollers and opens early at 12:00 PM for lunch. The staff is incredibly welcoming to young children, often providing high chairs and prompt service.

Trattoria ZaZa

This lively restaurant in Piazza del Mercato Centrale features a large, heated outdoor patio that is perfect for families with active toddlers. The vibrant, bustling atmosphere easily absorbs baby noises, and their creamy truffle ravioli is world-class.

Gelateria dei Neri

Arguably the best artisanal gelato in Florence, offering wide indoor standing space and a convenient location near the Piazza della Signoria. Grab a cup of their salted caramel or pistachio to enjoy while push-strolling your daughter along the Arno.

Osteria del Cinghiale Bianco

Housed in a historic 13th-century tower, this incredibly warm and cozy spot opens for dinner at 6:30 PM, fitting perfectly into a toddler's early bedtime schedule. Order the wild boar pappardelle and enjoy the exceptionally friendly, family-loving Italian hospitality.

Caffè Gilli

An elegant, historic cafe on Piazza della Repubblica featuring spacious outdoor seating that directly overlooks the vintage carousel. It is the perfect spot for parents to enjoy a high-end espresso and fresh pastry while keeping the toddler safely seated in a high chair.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Four Seasons Hotel Firenze

Boasting the largest private garden in Florence (Gherardesca) and an outdoor pool, this luxury resort is an oasis for families. They provide complimentary cribs, childproofing kits, high-end baby toiletries, and highly professional babysitting services.

Hotel Savoy (Rocco Forte)

Perfectly situated in Piazza della Repubblica right next to the carousel, this hotel offers spacious multi-room suites ideal for infant nap schedules. They feature a dedicated 'Families R Forte' program providing baby cribs, play mats, and bottle warmers on demand.

Villa La Massa

Located just 15 minutes outside the city center in Candeli with a complimentary shuttle, this luxury estate offers a quiet, green retreat away from urban noise. It features vast lawns, a heated swimming pool, and spacious rooms perfect for a baby-friendly Tuscan resort experience.
