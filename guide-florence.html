Florence

← back to travel board
Florence welcomes families with its walkable, cobblestone streets, towering domes, and the sweet aroma of fresh gelato at every turn. Children will feel like knights and princesses as they explore historic palaces, vibrant carousel-filled piazzas, and lush hillside gardens. It is a sensory-rich playground where art history meets kid-friendly Italian hospitality.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or direct T2 tramway ride to the historic city center.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Piazza della Signoria Open-Air Museum

Marvel at giant statues like the replica of David in this stroller-friendly outdoor gallery. Toddler note: The nearby Neptune Fountain is great for looking at water splashes, and the vast pedestrian piazza offers plenty of space to toddle safely.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Antique Carousel of Piazza della Repubblica

Ride a beautifully restored, nostalgic wooden horse carousel right in the heart of the city. Toddler note: It's an easy, low-cost win for active toddlers who need a quick, magical break from sightseeing.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Boboli Gardens Exploration

Let the kids run wild among grand stone fountains, hidden pathways, and sweeping lawns behind the Pitti Palace. Toddler note: Bring a carrier rather than a stroller, as some gravel paths and terraced slopes are quite steep.

Mid-day / 1.5h

Mercato Centrale Food Adventure

Experience the sights, sounds, and smells of Florence's premier indoor market, packed with fresh fruits, cheese, and pasta. Toddler note: Head upstairs to the spacious food hall for easy, casual dining where kids can pick their favorite bites.

Evening / 1-2h

Sunset Views at Piazzale Michelangelo

Soak in the ultimate postcard-perfect panoramic view of the Florence skyline and the Arno River. Toddler note: Climb the steps early to grab a spot, and bring snacks to keep little ones happy while waiting for sunset.

Morning / 1.5h

Leonardo da Vinci Interactive Museum

Touch, pull, and test wooden machines built from Leonardo's original designs in a highly tactile exhibition. Toddler note: It is highly interactive and perfect for toddlers who love to push buttons, turn gears, and explore physical cause-and-effect.

Afternoon / 2h

Parco delle Cascine Playground

Escape the stone pavements for Florence's largest green park, stretching along the Arno River with playgrounds and bike rentals. Toddler note: The flat, paved paths are perfect for strollers and the shaded play areas offer a cool respite in summer.

Morning / 1h

Duomo Marveling & Gelato Walk

Gaze up at the magnificent pink, green, and white marble of the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore. Toddler note: Skip the long climb up the dome with a toddler and instead enjoy a leisurely stroll around the perimeter with a gelato.

Late Morning / 1-2h

The Florence Rose Garden

Wander through hundreds of rose varieties and whimsical bronze sculptures by Folon just below Piazzale Michelangelo. Toddler note: This garden is much quieter and easier to navigate for toddlers than the massive Boboli Gardens, plus it has great views.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Giostra

Try the famous pear and pecorino ravioli in a warm, welcoming atmosphere run by real Tuscan nobility.

Trattoria ZaZà

Order the creamy truffle pasta or a simple tomato pasta for the kids in their eccentrically decorated, highly family-friendly dining rooms.

Gelateria Santa Trinita

Indulge in a scoop of their signature dark chocolate or fresh strawberry gelato while looking out over the Arno River.

Osteria Santo Spirito

Savor the famous baked gnocchi with cheese and truffle oil, served hot in a rustic clay dish that is perfect for sharing.

I' Girone de' Ghiotti

Grab a massive, freshly baked schiacciata sandwich loaded with mortadella, pistachio cream, and stracciatella cheese for an easy family picnic.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry cash for the carousel and small gelato shops, and book your major museum tickets months in advance to avoid long, toddler-testing queues. Be sure to request a high chair ("seggiolone") when booking restaurants, as spaces are often tight in historic Florentine dining rooms.
