Exuma

← back to travel board
With its blindingly white sands and impossibly clear sapphire waters, Exuma is a playground for families seeking pure tropical bliss. From feeding friendly iguanas to wading in shallow tidepools, this tranquil archipelago offers a safe, unhurried slice of paradise. It is the ultimate destination for parents wanting to unplug and create lifelong beach memories with their little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min rental car drive from Exuma International Airport (GGT) to George Town, which is highly recommended for families needing flexibility

What to do

Morning / 4-5h boat tour

Swimming Pigs at Big Major Cay

Take a boat charter to meet the world-famous swimming pigs who love to splash alongside visitors. Toddlers will love watching them from the safety of the boat or shallow water, but keep an eye out as the pigs can be quite eager for snacks!

Low Tide / 2-3h

Shelling at Coco Plum Beach

Famous for its massive sandbar at low tide, this shallow beach is perfect for collecting sand dollars and safe paddling. It is incredibly toddler-friendly because the crystal-clear water remains ankle-deep for hundreds of yards.

Morning / 1-2h stop on boat tour

Feeding Iguanas at Allen's Cay

Meet the rare, prehistoric-looking Northern Bahamian Rock Iguanas that rush down to the beach to greet incoming boats. Toddlers will be fascinated watching these gentle lizards munch on grapes fed from the end of a stick.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Stocking Island Day Trip

Take the water taxi to Stocking Island to feed the wild stingrays that glide right up to the shore. Toddlers can safely play in the calm, shallow bay while parents enjoy the laid-back beach club vibe.

Morning / 2-3h

Swimming at Tropic of Cancer Beach

This crescent-shaped stretch of powder-soft white sand boasts some of the clearest water in the world. It is ideal for a quiet family picnic, and the soft sand is perfect for toddler sandcastle building.

Morning / 1h

Shopping at George Town Straw Market

Wander through this colorful open-air market to pick up authentic Bahamian hand-woven straw baskets, hats, and toys. It is a sensory delight for toddlers, filled with bright colors and friendly local crafters.

Morning / 2-3h

Kayaking Moriah Harbour Cay

Explore a pristine marine ecosystem of mangroves, sandbanks, and sea grass beds teeming with juvenile fish. Rent a double kayak so your toddler can sit safely in the middle and spot turtles and baby sharks in the shallows.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Sea Turtle Spotting at Hoopers Bay

This calm, sheltered bay is famous for the friendly green sea turtles that swim close to the shoreline. Toddlers can easily peer through the crystal-clear water from the shallows or the dock to watch these graceful creatures swim.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Wading the Little Exuma Flats

Discover the endless, shallow bonefish flats on the south side of the island where the water is warm and calm. It acts like a giant, natural wading pool where toddlers can safely splash and spot tiny crabs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shirley's at the Fish Fry

Grab a table at this legendary local spot and order the outstanding cracked conch or the kid-friendly fried chicken fingers.

Santanna's Bar and Grill

Located in Little Exuma, you must try their famous fresh-caught fried lobster tail served right by the ocean.

Chat 'N' Chill

Order the freshly prepared conch salad made right in front of you at the beachside shack.

Rusty Anchor

Enjoy a family dinner overlooking the marina at February Point and order the delicious grilled snapper or gourmet wood-fired pizzas.

Splash Beach Bar & Grill

Located at the Hideaways resort, order the cheesy flatbreads and tropical fruit smoothies that kids and adults will love.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen, swim diapers, and a lightweight beach tent, as shade can be scarce on Exuma’s pristine, undeveloped beaches. Renting a 4WD vehicle is highly recommended to easily navigate the bumpy dirt roads leading to the best hidden family spots.
