Exuma

← back to travel board
Exuma offers a masterclass in slow-paced luxury, boasting some of the world’s shallowest, warmest waters that act as natural wading pools for your toddler. Free from cruise ship crowds and high-rise mega-resorts, this pristine archipelago lets you set a stress-free schedule dictated only by the tides. It is the ultimate destination for a busy professional looking to trade screen time for high-quality, uninterrupted family connection in a safe, tranquil paradise.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 minute taxi from Exuma International Airport (GGT) to George Town hotels, or ~25 minutes to Emerald Bay resorts; private rental SUVs with pre-arranged car seats are also available directly at the terminal.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Coco Plum Beach

This sprawling beach features incredibly shallow water and vast sandbars at low tide, making it a safe natural wading pool for a toddler. Skip the stroller entirely and let your little one hunt for giant sand dollars in water that barely reaches their ankles.

Midday / 3-4h

Chat 'N' Chill

Take the short water taxi from George Town to this legendary beach spot where wild but docile stingrays glide right up to the shore. While the vibe is lively, parents can easily find a shaded picnic table under the trees; use a baby carrier as the deep sand is not stroller-friendly.

Morning / 1-2h

Hooper's Bay

This calm, wind-sheltered bay is famous for the friendly green sea turtles that swim right up to the shallow shoreline. It is an easy, low-stress spot to show your toddler marine life without needing a boat excursion; access is via a short, flat walk from the main road.

Morning / 3-4h

Moriah Harbour Cay National Park

An breathtaking expanse of protected mangroves and sand dunes with pristine, current-free shallows perfect for floating with an infant. Rent a private pontoon boat for a gentle, shaded cruise where your toddler can nap safely under the boat's canopy.

Morning / 1h

George Town Straw Market

Located in the heart of George Town, this open-air market is a great spot to pick up a hand-woven sun hat for your toddler and local wooden crafts. The paved, flat paths make it highly stroller-accessible, and the local vendors are incredibly welcoming to young families.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Tropic of Cancer Beach

Famous for its stunning crescent shape and powder-soft white sand, this quiet beach has no crowds and plenty of space to set up a baby pop-up tent. Park close to the wooden steps entrance to minimize carrying heavy toddler gear across the dunes.

Morning / 1-2h

Compass Cay Marina

Walk along the sturdy wooden docks to view the school of nurse sharks that lounge in the crystal-clear, shallow marina water. It is an easy, structured way to let your toddler observe these gentle creatures safely from above without having to swim.

Afternoon / 2h

Exuma Point Beach

Located at the quiet northern tip of Great Exuma, this beach offers massive expanses of knee-deep water and iconic water swings that make for a perfect photo op. The adjacent restaurant provides easy access to restrooms and shade when your toddler needs a break from the sun.

Morning / 4h

Pig Beach

Book a private, half-day boat charter to see the famous swimming pigs on your own timeline before the larger tour groups arrive. Keep your toddler safely on the boat canopy while you feed the larger pigs, or bring a small piglet on board for a gentle, closely supervised meet-and-greet.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shirley's At The Fish Fry

Order the signature cracked conch and peas 'n' rice at this highly regarded local joint. The casual, open-air deck is very loud and lively, meaning you won't have to worry about your toddler making noise or fussing.

Santanna's Bar and Grill

Feast on freshly caught, flash-fried lobster tails on an outdoor deck overlooking the ocean. The service is fast, the atmosphere is incredibly laid-back, and there is plenty of space to park a stroller right by your wooden table.

The Palapa Grill

Enjoy upscale pan-seared grouper in a relaxed, poolside setting within the Grand Isle resort. It is perfect for an early dinner with an infant, offering plenty of high chairs, spacious seating, and a resort path that is highly stroller-friendly.

Splash Beach Bar & Grill

Order the Bahamian conch fritters and a tropical cocktail right on the beach. This spot features a very family-friendly pool area and lawn games, allowing parents to eat while keeping an eye on a toddler playing in the shade.

Rusty Anchor

This breezy, casual spot at Hideaways Exuma serves excellent mahi-mahi tacos and classic burgers. The staff is exceptionally warm towards kids, and they are happy to pureé fresh fruit for your toddler's meal upon request.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Isle Resort & Residences

Offers spacious one- to four-bedroom villas with fully equipped kitchens and en-suite laundry, which are essential for managing baby bottles and dirty toddler clothes. The resort features a beautiful zero-entry infinity pool, reliable crib rentals, and paved pathways that make stroller navigation effortless.

Hideaways Exuma

Features excellent beachfront cottages with separate living areas and kitchenettes, ideal for prepping early morning baby meals. The resort boasts a private, calm beach, complimentary water toys, and a highly welcoming, family-first environment close to George Town.

Fowl Cay Resort

A luxury, all-inclusive private island resort that provides each villa with its own dedicated golf cart and motorboat, offering ultimate flexibility for a family schedule. It is perfect for a high-end traveler wanting secluded beaches, spacious multi-room villas, and personalized meals tailored for a toddler.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack all of your infant essentials, including diapers, swim pants, and specialized baby sunscreen, from Houston, as the local grocery stores in George Town have limited stock and highly inflated prices.
