Exuma's translucent waters and powdery sandbanks create a serene playground for your growing family. The calm, shallow bays at Stocking Island are perfect for wading with baby Mary Claire, with stroller-friendly boardwalks at Grand Isle. A private beachfront villa with a crib and pool ensures effortless luxury. The swimming pigs at Big Major Cay are a delight, but keep the little one shaded and watch for strong sun.
🚕 Getting there: From Exuma International Airport (GGT) to your hotel, a 20-minute private transfer with a car seat and stroller acclimation is offered by Exuma Transfers or your hotel’s luxury SUV service.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chat N Chill

Signature conch fritters and ice-cold Kalik. Infant note: outdoor terrace under shade, highchair available, early seating recommended to avoid loud live music.

Santana's Grill

Family-run spot on Little Exuma serving cracked conch. Infant note: laid-back atmosphere, plastic highchair, kids' menu; no stairs, easy stroller access.

The Fish Fry (Potter's Cay)

Cluster of shacks with fresh grilled lobster and conch. Infant note: choose a stall with shade and early (5pm) to avoid noisy crowds; typical highchair may be absent.

Blue Bar at Grand Isle Resort

Upscale Caribbean cuisine with ocean view. Infant note: highchair, stroller-friendly path, kids' menu, outdoor terrace with fans and shade; perfect for early dinner.

The Palapa Grill

Beachfront Italian-inspired dishes at Exuma Yacht Club. Infant note: quiet open-air setting, highchair available, early seating (5:30pm) avoids rush; no age policy.
