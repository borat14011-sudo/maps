For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the island's gentle, shallow shorelines provide the ultimate natural wading pool for an infant's first dips. What to do

Morning low tide 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Coco Plum Beach

This beach features shallow sandbars that stretch for miles during low tide, creating an ideal and incredibly safe wading pool for an infant. The shoreline is bordered by mature casuarina trees that offer plenty of natural shade for baby's nap time.

Lunchtime excursion 11:30 AM - 2:00 PM

Chat 'N' Chill

Reachable via a quick, breezy 5-minute water taxi from George Town, this iconic beach spot features soft sand and shallow water where Mary Claire can watch docile wild stingrays glide by. Shaded picnic tables make it easy to escape the midday sun.

Early morning stroll 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Hoopers Bay

This tranquil bay is famous for its resident green sea turtles that swim in the calm, wave-free shallows. The water is exceptionally clear and peaceful, allowing parents to easily hold baby in their arms while viewing the marine life.

Mid-morning relaxation 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Jolly Hall Beach

A protected, scenic crescent-shaped beach that is virtually wind-free, making it a quiet haven for young families. Fringed by shady trees, it provides a quiet and private atmosphere that is perfect for spreading out a beach blanket for baby to play.

Late afternoon 3:30 PM - 5:30 PM

Tropic of Cancer Beach

Also known as Pelican Beach, this world-famous stretch of sand on Little Exuma offers impossibly clear water for unforgettable family photos. While there is minimal natural shade, bringing a small pop-up UV tent allows for safe, comfortable infant wading.

Morning boat charter 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Moriah Harbour Cay National Park

A breathtaking, protected marine park comprised of pristine mangroves and calm channels. Booking a private, slow-paced pontoon charter allows you to glide through the quiet waters without loud engine noise, keeping baby relaxed and comfortable.

Morning walk 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

George Town Straw Market

This traditional Bahamian open-air market features friendly local vendors selling woven crafts and souvenirs. The surrounding pathways are flat and easy to navigate with a stroller, offering a gentle dose of local culture in a shaded setting.

Half-day charter 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Exuma Water Sports Private Charter

A highly customizable private boat excursion tailored to your family's specific pace. You can bypass the crowded, loud speedboats and opt for a smooth, gentle cruise to calm sandbars, ensuring baby's feeding and nap schedules are fully respected.

Afternoon wading 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Little Exuma Bonefish Flats

These endless, ankle-deep flats offer incredibly tranquil, warm, and wave-free waters. It is an exceptionally low-sensory environment that is ideal for wearing baby in a carrier while walking over the reflective, glass-like water.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Palapa Grill

Savor freshly caught Bahamian lobster tail at this upscale poolside restaurant located at Grand Isle Resort. They offer spacious outdoor terrace seating, highchairs, and an early dinner service that makes it easy to maintain baby's bedtime routine.

Rusty Anchor

Enjoy pan-seared local grouper on a breezy, shaded outdoor deck overlooking the marina at February Point. The quiet, relaxed atmosphere offers plenty of room to park a stroller next to your table while enjoying top-tier service.

Shirley's Multi-Seafood Restaurant

A beloved local institution famous for its coconut curry conch and relaxed island hospitality. The casual, open-air dining setup ensures that any unexpected infant fussiness is easily masked by the natural ocean breeze and ambient island sounds.

Peace & Plenty Restaurant

Dine on Bahamian rock lobster at this historic hotel restaurant overlooking Elizabeth Harbour. Reserving an early terrace table at 5:30 PM allows you to enjoy the stunning sunset and attentive service before the main dining crowd arrives.

Santanna's Bar and Grill

This iconic open-air oceanfront eatery in Little Exuma is famous for its deep-fried lobster. Choosing an early lunch slot ensures a quiet, uncrowded experience where baby can peacefully enjoy the cool Atlantic breezes from a shaded deck table.

An eco-luxury boutique resort located on Stocking Island that features pristine, crowd-free beachfront bungalows. The resort provides cribs on request and sits on an ultra-calm, protected cove, making it a peaceful and private oasis for families with infants.
