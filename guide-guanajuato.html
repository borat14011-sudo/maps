Guanajuato

← back to travel board
Guanajuato’s pedestrian-friendly historic core, colorful architecture, and European-style plazas offer a magical, low-stress escape for a young family. The city’s relaxed morning pace is perfect for early-rising toddlers, while the warm, family-centric local culture means children are welcomed warmly everywhere. Staying slightly outside the bustling center provides a peaceful retreat, making this vibrant mountain town surprisingly comfortable for a luxury-loving family with a little one.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min private car service from Del Bajío International Airport (BJX) to Guanajuato center.

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardín Unión

This beautiful, laurel-shaded triangular plaza is the lively heart of the city, filled with street performers and gentle marimba music. It is flat, paved, and completely car-free, making it the perfect spot to push a stroller while enjoying an afternoon ice cream.

Morning / 1h

Funicular Panorámico

Ride the glass cable car up the steep mountainside to the iconic El Pípila monument for panoramic views of the colorful city. You must fold your stroller before boarding, but the short, smooth ride is thrilling for toddlers and saves you a brutal uphill climb.

Morning / 2h

Presa de la Olla

This historic 18th-century reservoir is surrounded by lush green parks, walking paths, and families renting colorful rowboats. It is highly stroller-accessible and offers a tranquil, quiet respite from the bustling downtown crowds.

Morning / 2-3h

Ex-Hacienda San Gabriel de Barrera

A stunning historical estate featuring 17 distinct, manicured gardens ranging from English to Italian styles. While there are some stone steps, the wide dirt and grass paths offer plenty of open space for a toddler to safely toddle around the fountains.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

Housed in a massive, high-ceilinged iron building designed by Eiffel's studio, this bustling market is a sensory feast of colors, fruits, and crafts. The aisles can get crowded and tight, so skip the stroller and use a baby carrier to navigate this lively local landmark.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Plaza de la Paz

A picturesque, open plaza anchored by the brilliant yellow Basilica of Our Lady of Guanajuato. The wide pedestrian zones surrounding the plaza are incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller, offering plenty of space to let your toddler look at the pigeons.

Evening / 1h

Teatro Juárez Steps

The grand steps of this neoclassical theater serve as the city's favorite outdoor living room. While the steps themselves aren't stroller-friendly, the wide, flat stone plaza directly in front is ideal for letting your child stretch her legs while you enjoy the architecture.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Florencio Antillón

Located just below Presa de la Olla, this peaceful park is filled with ancient trees, stone bridges, and quiet, paved pathways. It is one of the best spots in the city to rock a baby to sleep in a stroller away from traffic noise.

Morning / 1-2h

Alhóndiga de Granaditas

A massive historic fortress-turned-museum with a spacious, flat interior courtyard that is highly stroller-accessible. It offers a calm, shaded environment to walk around while learning about Mexico’s independence history.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Valadez

Order the high-end arrachera or fresh sea bass at this elegant restaurant overlooking Jardín Unión. The white-glove service is incredibly accommodating to families, offering sturdy high chairs and a spacious, noise-forgiving indoor dining room.

El Jardín de los Milagros

Savor creative, upscale Mexican cuisine by Chef Bricio Domínguez set in a gorgeous, expansive garden. The outdoor courtyard seating is ideal for a toddler to quietly explore while parents enjoy a leisurely, high-end lunch.

Truco 7

This cozy, historic eatery serves comforting, authentic Mexican classics like chilaquiles and enchiladas mineras. It opens early, has a relaxed, rustic atmosphere, and the background chatter easily drowns out any toddler fussiness.

La Virgen de la Cueva

An upscale restaurant celebrating local flavors with signature dishes like duck in black mole, set in a beautifully designed, spacious interior. The large, comfortable booths and highly attentive staff make dining with an infant seamless.

Amatxi

Enjoy excellent Mediterranean-Mexican fusion and artisanal pizzas in a charming, airy courtyard setting. The relaxed outdoor environment is perfect for an early dinner with a baby before the evening chill sets in.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Villa María Cristina

This Relais & Châteaux property in the quiet Paseo de la Presa neighborhood offers spacious suites, indoor/outdoor pools, and beautiful lawns. It is a peaceful luxury haven away from the city noise, with cribs available upon request.

Camino Real Guanajuato

Built on the grounds of a historic 17th-century silver mill, this hotel offers massive, quiet rooms and sprawling, walled gardens perfect for stroller walks. Its layout provides a serene, safe environment for families seeking luxury and space.

Hotel Quinta Las Acacias

A charming, upscale boutique hotel set in a 19th-century French-style villa near the peaceful Presa de la Olla park. The quiet location, beautiful mountain views, and lush gardens make it a wonderful, relaxing retreat for a family with a baby.

👨‍👩‍👧 Prioritize a high-quality baby carrier over a stroller, as Guanajuato’s historic center is packed with steep hills, narrow sidewalks, and uneven cobblestones. Take advantage of the quiet mornings before 10:00 AM to explore the main plazas when they are peaceful and completely uncrowded. Keep cash pesos on hand, as many small taxis (which you will want to take to get back up the steep hills) do not accept credit cards.
