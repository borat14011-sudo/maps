What to do

Morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Jardín de la Unión

A vibrant, manicured plaza shaded by giant Indian laurel trees. Ideal for early morning stroller walks before the crowd arrives, offering plenty of benches for nursing.

Late morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Museo Exhacienda San Gabriel de Barrera

A stunning 17th-century hacienda featuring 17 distinct international gardens. The lush, shaded lawns provide a perfect quiet sanctuary for Mary Claire to rest under the trees.

Afternoon 1-2pm / 1h

Teatro Juárez

An opulent 19th-century theater with an iconic columned facade. Walk the stroller around the grand steps to admire the architecture, but swap to a carrier if touring the quiet interior.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Presa de la Olla

A peaceful historic reservoir surrounded by parkland in the upscale Paseo de la Presa district. Excellent flat, paved pathways for seamless stroller navigation and duck feeding.

Morning 10-11am / 1h

Basílica de Nuestra Señora de Guanajuato

An iconic yellow baroque basilica dominating the main plaza. The interior is cool and whisper-quiet, perfect for escaping the afternoon heat and rocking a sleeping baby.

Midday 12-1:30pm / 1.5h

Plaza de San Fernando

A picturesque, pedestrian-only cobblestone square filled with cafes and bookshops. Safe and spacious for Mary Claire to watch the fountains play from her stroller.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Funicular Panorámico

A scenic cable car climbing up to the El Pípila monument. Opt for a private cabin slot during off-peak hours to comfortably board with your folded compact stroller.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Alhóndiga de Granaditas

A massive historic fortress turned regional museum. The large open-air central courtyard is ramp-accessible, cool, and spacious enough to navigate with a stroller.

Early morning 8-8:30am / 0.5h

Callejón del Beso

Guanajuato's most famous, ultra-narrow alleyway. This site is highly congested and has steep steps; absolutely swap the stroller for a baby carrier for this quick, iconic photo-op.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Valadez

Gourmet Mexican-international fusion. Request an outdoor terrace table facing Jardín de la Unión; they offer highchairs, excellent infant hospitality, and early evening seatings.

La Virgen de la Cueva

Upscale contemporary Mexican cuisine in a stunningly designed artistic interior. The spacious dining room easily accommodates strollers, and the staff is remarkably welcoming to families.

Restaurante Las Mercedes

Famed upscale traditional restaurant serving exquisite regional recipes in a quiet, residential setting. Booking an early dinner ensures a serene environment with low noise levels for baby.

Amatxi

Premium Basque-Mexican fusion located in the quieter Paseo de la Presa area. The beautiful outdoor garden patio is highly spacious, stroller-friendly, and naturally dampens baby noises.

El Jardín de los Milagros

Sophisticated Mexican cuisine by Chef Bricio Domínguez, set in a historic hacienda garden. The outdoor lawn seating provides a beautiful, peaceful luxury dining experience for parents while baby naps.
