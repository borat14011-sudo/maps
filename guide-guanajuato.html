Guanajuato

← back to travel board
Guanajuato enchants with its kaleidoscopic colonial architecture and winding alleyways, a vibrant UNESCO World Heritage site. The city's temperate climate makes strolling pleasant year-round, while the Jardín Unión offers a shaded, stroller-friendly plaza perfect for afternoon rests. For luxury with an infant, the Hotel Boutique 1850 provides a private plunge pool and crib upon request, ensuring serene moments amid the historic bustle. The famous Callejón del Beso is a must-see, but best enjoyed early or late to avoid crowds and noise with a little one.
🚕 Getting there: León/Guanajuato International Airport (BJX) to your hotel: 35 minutes via private transfer with infant car seat and stroller storage recommended; Blacklane or local luxury services offer child-safety options.

What to do

Late morning 10am-12pm

Alhóndiga de Granaditas

Historic grain storage turned museum with grand stone architecture; stroller-accessible ground floor, shaded courtyards, and quiet corners for feeding breaks. Skip the upper exhibits if carrying baby.

Early morning 9-11am

Teatro Juárez

Opulent neoclassical theatre with guided tours; strollers permitted in lobby and main hall. The ornate interiors captivate adults while babies enjoy the cool, quiet atmosphere.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Jardín Unión

Central square with shaded iron benches, live music on weekends, and stroller-friendly paved paths. A calm spot for baby to watch passersby; nearby cafés have highchairs.

Early morning 8-9am

Callejón del Beso

Famous narrow alley with romantic legend; best visited early (before 10am) to avoid crowds. Stroller might not fit—carry baby in carrier for the short walk.

Sunset 5-7pm

Monumento al Pípila

Hilltop statue with panoramic city views; accessible via funicular (stroller-friendly cabin) or steep walkway. Bring shade for baby at the top; breeze is refreshing.

Morning 10-11am

Museo de las Momias

Museum of mummified bodies—intriguing but potentially intense for infants. Visit a short time (30 min) using a baby carrier; strollers allowed but tight spaces. Skip if baby is sensitive.

Mid-morning 10am-12pm

Mina de la Valenciana

Historic silver mine with guided tours; baby carriers recommended over strollers due to uneven paths. The shaded entrance area is cool, and older children enjoy the glow of silver ore.

Late morning 11am-12:30pm

Universidad de Guanajuato

Iconic university building with sweeping stairways; stroller-accessible via side ramp. The courtyard is shaded and quiet, perfect for a break; nearby gelato shop delights parents.

Midday 12-1pm

Iglesia de San Diego

Beautiful baroque church adjacent to Jardín Unión; stroller accessible via side entrance. Cool interior offers a peaceful retreat from midday heat; lit candles fascinate little eyes.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Valadez

Upscale Mexican cuisine with terrace overlooking Jardín Unión; highchairs available and early seating (5pm) recommended for families. Signature dish: chiles en nogada. Stroller-friendly entry.

El Gallo Pitagórico

Rooftop restaurant with panoramic views and modern Mexican dishes; reserve a table near the edge for baby to gaze—no loud music. Cribs available upon request; try the cochinita pibil.

La Vie en Rose

French-Mexican fusion in a restored mansion, with a quiet garden terrace perfect for babies. Highchairs and changing table in restroom. Signature: duck confit tacos. No age restrictions.

Truco 7

Intimate restaurant in a charming alley; early dinner (6pm) avoids crowds. Shaded patio with a fountain soothes babies. Specializes in regional stews and handmade tortillas.

Los Campos

Farm-to-table spot with a spacious outdoor courtyard, highchairs, and a small play corner. The menu changes daily; ask for the baby-friendly puree option. Quiet atmosphere in the early evening.

Boutique hotel with spacious suites featuring separate living areas, private terraces, and a small heated pool. Cribs and highchairs on request; concierge arranges babysitting. Quiet garden courtyard for relaxation.

$220-380/night

Elegant villa-style hotel with lush gardens and a large outdoor pool (heated). Family suites include mini-fridges and blackout shades. The hotel provides pack 'n plays and step stools for toddlers. Peaceful yet near the historic center.

👨‍👩‍👧 Guanajuato's cobblestone streets are tough for strollers—use a rugged all-terrain stroller or baby carrier; many alleys are pedestrian-only. For formula and diapers, visit a Farmacia San Pablo or Farmacias Similares in the city center; the Hospital General de Guanajuato (Av. Juárez) has 24/7 pediatric care. Keep baby hydrated with frequent shade breaks and a portable fan; the dry heat is deceptive, so dress in light layers and use sun hats.
