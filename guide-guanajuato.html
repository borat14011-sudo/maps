Colorful tunnels, cobblestones, and family magic in Guanajuato

Guanajuato

← back to travel board
Nestled in a narrow valley, Guanajuato is a fairy-tale labyrinth of subterranean tunnels, rainbow-colored alleys, and sun-drenched plazas. Street performers fill the air with music while majestic historic theaters tower over bustling pedestrian-only streets. It is a whimsical highland playground where every corner reveals a new story to captivate both parents and children.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-45 min official taxi or pre-booked private shuttle from BJX Airport to downtown hotels

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1h

Funicular Panorámico to El Pípila

Ride the glass cable car up the steep hillside for a breathtaking, bird's-eye view of the colorful city. Toddlers will love the thrill of the short ascent, but keep a close grip on them at the crowded lookout platform.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardín de la Unión

This bustling, tree-lined main plaza is perfect for listening to live mariachis and chasing pigeons under manicured green canopies. It is completely pedestrianized, making it a safe space for toddlers to wander safely while you enjoy a coffee.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

This massive, iron-roofed market designed by Eiffel's studio is a sensory wonderland filled with colorful toys, sweet treats, and local crafts. It is a bit loud and crowded, so using a baby carrier instead of a stroller is highly recommended here.

Early Morning / 30m

Callejón del Beso

Visit this ultra-narrow, legendary alley where balconies nearly touch, bringing a classic Mexican folk tale to life. The tight space and stairs can be tricky with a toddler, so visit early in the morning to beat the crowds.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque El Cantador

A lush, historic park just outside the busy center featuring paved paths, fountains, and a dedicated playground area. It is the perfect, shady spot for toddlers to run free and burn off energy away from the cobblestone streets.

Morning / 2h

Ex-Hacienda San Gabriel de Barrera

Wander through seventeen beautiful, themed gardens and a historic colonial home filled with tranquil ponds and fountains. The spacious grassy lawns are ideal for toddlers to explore safely, though keep them away from the deep decorative pools.

Afternoon / 30m

Teatro Juárez

Marvel at the grand neoclassical facade adorned with bronze statues of the Greek muses. While the interior might not hold a toddler's attention, the wide, smooth steps outside are a favorite local gathering spot for watching street performers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de San Fernando

This quiet, bohemian plaza is lined with colorful cafes and offers plenty of open space for kids to play safely. It's much calmer than Jardín de la Unión, making it a relaxing spot for a family lunch.

Morning / 1h

Museo Iconográfico del Quijote

A colorful museum dedicated entirely to Don Quixote, featuring whimsical paintings, statues, and tapestries that spark young imaginations. The quiet courtyards offer a peaceful respite, and kids will love spotting the different depictions of the knight and his horse.

🍽️ Where to eat

Casa Valadez

Order the traditional enchiladas mineras in this upscale but family-friendly classic overlooking the main plaza.

Estación Joaquín

Indulge in artisanal ice cream and sorbets with unique local flavors like garambullo (cactus fruit) that kids will love.

La Clave Azul

Stop by this quirky, antique-filled tavern early in the afternoon for traditional cantina-style tapas and a cozy, welcoming vibe.

Santo Café

Grab a table directly on the small bridge crossing over a pedestrian alley and order the delicious chilaquiles or fresh crepes.

Los Campos Bistro

Savor modern Mexican small plates like hibiscus-flower tacos in a cozy, rustic setting that offers excellent high chairs for toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller in favor of a sturdy baby carrier, as Guanajuato's steep hills, narrow sidewalks, and endless cobblestones are highly stroller-unfriendly. Since many central streets are actually underground tunnels, keep a close eye on toddlers near open stone stairwells that lead beneath the city.
