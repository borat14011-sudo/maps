What to do

Early morning 8:30-10:30am

Lydgate Beach Park

This park features a rock-walled, fully enclosed ocean pool that blocks big waves, creating a calm, saltwater wading pool perfect for Mary Claire's first ocean dip. There is also a massive wooden playground with shaded areas nearby.

Morning 10am-12pm

Allerton Garden

Stroll through beautifully manicured tropical grounds with towering Moreton Bay fig trees. The main garden paths are wide and stroller-accessible, offering plenty of cooling shade under the lush canopy.

Afternoon 1-2:30pm

Kilauea Point National Wildlife Refuge

Enjoy breathtaking coastal views and spot nesting seabirds. The paved pathway to the historic lighthouse is highly stroller-friendly, though it can get windy, so a secure baby carrier or stroller canopy is recommended.

Late morning 11am-12pm

Kilohana Plantation Railway

An open-air, vintage diesel locomotive ride that winds gently through a historic 105-acre plantation. It is a smooth, quiet ride that will keep Mary Claire entertained with views of farm animals and exotic fruit groves.

Morning 9-11am

Poipu Beach Park

A popular South Shore beach featuring a natural wading pool protected by a lava rock reef. It offers grassy lawns, shaded picnic pavilions, and frequent sightings of Hawaiian monk seals resting on the sand.

Morning 9-10am

Kauai Coffee Company

Stroll along the flat, paved paths of the largest coffee orchard in the United States. Parents can enjoy fresh estate-grown coffee while walking Mary Claire in her stroller through the shaded outdoor walking tour.

Afternoon 2-4pm

Anini Beach

Boasting the longest and widest fringing reef in Hawaii, this beach features incredibly calm, shallow water with virtually no waves. Shaded by ironwood trees, it is the safest natural spot on the island for a baby to splash.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm

Ke Ala Hele Makalae (Kapaa Bike Path)

A world-class, multi-use coastal path that is completely paved and flat. It is the absolute perfect spot for an uninterrupted stroller walk to soothe Mary Claire to sleep while enjoying panoramic ocean views.

Morning 9-11am

Limahuli Garden and Preserve

Located on the dramatic North Shore, this botanical paradise features a gentle, accessible lower loop path. It provides a quiet, peaceful mountain backdrop with native Hawaiian plants and cool valley breezes.

🍽️ Where to eat

Duke's Kauai

Savor their famous Kimo's Original Hula Pie right on Kalapaki Beach. The lively open-air atmosphere and the soothing sound of the waves easily mask any unexpected baby noise from Mary Claire.

Merriman's Kauai

Indulge in the Macadamia Nut Crusted Mahi Mahi on the spacious outdoor lanai. They gladly provide highchairs and offer an early 5:00 PM seating, which is ideal for matching an infant's bedtime routine.

The Beach House Restaurant

Famous for its upscale seafood and front-row sunset views. The restaurant has a spacious grassy lawn directly adjacent to the dining deck, allowing parents to take turns walking with Mary Claire if she gets restless.

Lava Lava Beach Club

Enjoy island-style coconut shrimp with your feet literally in the sand. This ultra-casual beachside venue allows Mary Claire to sit on a blanket in the sand right next to your table while you dine.

Keoki's Paradise

Feast on fresh fish tacos in a lush, garden-like setting complete with meandering streams. The tropical ambiance and gentle water sounds are highly soothing for infants, and the booth seating is spacious and comfortable.
