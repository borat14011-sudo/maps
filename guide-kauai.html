Kauai

← back to travel board
Kauai enchants families with its emerald valleys, dramatic sea cliffs, and laid-back island rhythm. Here, golden beaches meet gentle shallow pools perfect for little feet, while lush rainforests spark endless wonder. It is a slow-paced paradise where nature does the entertaining.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min rental car drive from Lihue Airport to South Shore Poipu hotels, or ~45 min to North Shore Princeville.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Poipu Beach Park

With a protected, shallow baby beach blockaded by a natural lava rock reef, this is Kauai's ultimate playground for tiny swimmers. Toddlers can safely splash in the calm tide pools while parents keep an eye out for sleeping Hawaiian monk seals on the sand.

Morning / 1-2h

Waimea Canyon Lookout

Nicknamed the Grand Canyon of the Pacific, this stunning geological marvel offers dramatic red-and-green vistas right from the easily accessible viewing platforms. Toddlers will love the bright colors, and the paved walkway is stroller-friendly.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Lydgate State Park & Kamalani Playground

This park features a massive, wooden community-built playground with slides and swings, alongside a giant rock-walled ocean pool perfect for safe snorkeling. Toddlers can build sandcastles in the protected lagoon without worrying about strong waves.

Morning / 1.5h

Kauai Plantation Railway at Kilohana

Chug through a working 105-acre historic plantation on a charming conductor-led open-air train. Toddlers will be thrilled to step off mid-ride to feed friendly wild pigs, goats, and sheep.

Morning / 1-2h

Anahola Farmers Market

Sample fresh tropical fruits like apple bananas and rambutan while browsing handmade Hawaiian crafts at this vibrant community market. It's a low-key, spacious outdoor market where toddlers can snack on fresh fruit cups without the crowds.

Late Afternoon / 30m

Spouting Horn Blowhole

Watch in awe as the ocean shoots high into the air through a natural lava tube, accompanied by a roaring hiss. The paved barrier keeps toddlers safely contained while they watch the dramatic water show.

Morning / 1.5h

Limahuli Garden and Preserve

Walk through a lush, ancient Hawaiian valley filled with native plants, taro fields, and dramatic mountain backdrops. The self-guided loop is a manageable, sensory-rich stroll that will fascinate curious toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Kilauea Point Lighthouse

Visit a historic lighthouse perched on a dramatic cliffside that serves as a sanctuary for thousands of nesting seabirds. Toddlers can easily spot giant albatrosses and red-footed boobies soaring overhead through provided binoculars.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Hanalei Bay and Pier

With its soft white sand and backdrop of emerald green mountains laced with waterfalls, this bay offers incredibly gentle summer waves. Toddlers can splash in the calm river mouth near the historic pier where the water is warm and shallow.

🍽️ Where to eat

Keoki's Paradise

Order the fresh local fish tacos and the famous Koloa Rum hot fudge sundae in a lush, family-friendly rainforest-themed dining room complete with indoor lagoons.

Puka Dog Hawaiian Style Hot Dogs

Grab a unique Hawaiian-style hot dog toasted from the inside out and drizzled with sweet mango relish and spicy lilikoi mustard.

JoJo's Shave Ice

Cool down with a classic Hawaiian shave ice layered with macadamia nut ice cream at the bottom and topped with lilikoi and coconut cream.

Duke's Kauai

Enjoy beachfront dining with kids' favorite coconut shrimp and a massive slice of legendary Kimo's Original Hula Pie.

Hanalei Taro & Juice Co.

Try the traditional Kalua pork lunch plate and fresh, sweet taro fruit smoothies from this multi-generational family farm truck.
