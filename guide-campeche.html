What to do

Late Afternoon / 2h

Centro Histórico (Walled City)

Stroll through the pastel-colored streets protected by historic stone walls, where kids can safely run along the pedestrian-only Calle 59. It is highly stroller-friendly, though some cobblestones require a bit of pushing.

Morning / 1-2h

Baluarte de San Francisco & City Walls

Climb the ancient stone ramparts where kids can pretend to be pirates guarding the city from the sea. Toddlers will love the wide, flat stone paths on top, but keep a close hand on them near the edges.

Sunset / 1-2h

Campeche Malecón

A wide, paved waterfront boardwalk perfect for renting a family surrey bike or letting toddlers run free as the sun sets over the Gulf. Bring a light jacket as the evening sea breeze can get cool.

Morning / 2h

Fuerte de San Miguel

Explore a real moat and drawbridge at this hilltop fortress, which houses incredible Mayan jade masks. The grassy outdoor areas are great for toddlers to stretch their legs after looking at the artifacts.

Evening / 1h

Parque Principal

The vibrant heart of the city, complete with a beautiful gazebo, balloon vendors, and frequent live music. Toddlers can chase pigeons while parents relax on the shaded benches with a cup of local sorbet.

Morning / 1.5h

Mercado Principal Pedro Sáinz de Baranda

A bustling local market filled with exotic fruits, colorful flowers, and local snacks. It is a sensory adventure, though best navigated with a baby carrier rather than a stroller due to narrow, busy aisles.

Morning / 3h

Edzná Maya Ruins

A magnificent ancient Mayan city just 45 minutes away, featuring a massive central plaza where kids can run freely. Toddlers will love the wide-open grassy spaces, but stick to the flat areas rather than climbing the steep structures.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parque Recreativo Jaina

A local fenced playground with swings and slides where your toddler can interact with local kids. It is a great low-key spot to let off some steam in a shaded, relaxed environment.

Afternoon / 1h

El Guapo Tranvía Tour

Hop aboard this open-air, pirate-themed trolley for a breezy, narrated ride around the historic forts and neighborhoods. It is the perfect, low-effort way to sightsee when little legs are tired of walking.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Pigua

Order the iconic 'pampano empapelado' (fish cooked in parchment paper) in a bright, upscale, yet highly family-friendly dining room.

Marganzo

Try the traditional 'pan de cazón' (baby shark tortilla tower) while enjoying the vibrant, colorful atmosphere and friendly servers in traditional dress.

Luz de Luna

Grab an outdoor table on Calle 59 and order the cheese-filled empanadas and fresh fruit smoothies that toddlers love.

Chocol'Ha

Indulge in authentic Mexican hot chocolate and freshly baked crepes in a quiet, beautiful courtyard that feels like a secret garden.

La Parroquia

Get the sweet horchata and a plate of eggs motuleños at this casual, fast, and highly kid-welcoming historic diner.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy baby carrier over a stroller, as the historic cobblestones and high curbs can be challenging to navigate. Keep your family schedule flexible to avoid the midday heat, planning outdoor fort explorations for the cooler mornings and breezy strolls on the Malecón for the evening.
