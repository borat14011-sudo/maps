Steeped in pirate lore and painted in soft pastel hues, San Francisco de Campeche offers an exclusive, low-pressure coastal retreat for discerning families. The UNESCO-listed walled city boasts quiet, pedestrianized streets like Calle 59, making it remarkably manageable for a premium stroller. Parents can savor world-class seafood while baby Mary Claire relaxes under the gentle Gulf breeze. The smooth, modern Malecón provides a pristine beachfront promenade perfect for sunset strolls away from the typical tourist crowds.
🚕 Getting there: A 2-hour private luxury SUV transfer from Mérida International Airport (MID) directly to your historic center hotel, featuring a pre-installed premium rear-facing infant car seat and ample trunk space for your Uppababy stroller.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 2h

Centro Histórico de San Francisco de Campeche

Stroll the pastel-hued streets of the walled city. The pedestrianized Calle 59 offers a smooth path for your stroller with plenty of shade from historic arches.

Late afternoon 5:00 PM - 6:30 PM / 1.5h

Malecón de Campeche

Walk along the smooth, paved coastal boardwalk. It is ideal for an early evening stroller ride with Mary Claire to enjoy the calm Gulf breeze and watch the sunset.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 2h

Fuerte de San Miguel

Explore this 18th-century stone fortress housing exquisite Mayan jade masks. The wide courtyard is stroller-friendly, though some ramparts require a carrier vest.

Afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM / 1.5h

Plaza de la Independencia

Relax in the shaded central plaza under mature laurel trees. Mary Claire will love watching the birds while you admire the majestic, twin-towered Cathedral.

Early morning 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 3h

Edzná Archaeological Zone

Witness a spectacular, uncrowded Mayan city just 50 minutes away. Book a private morning tour and use a baby carrier to navigate the grassy plazas comfortably.

Morning 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM / 1h

Baluarte de San Francisco

Visit one of the primary bastions defending the historic city. The garden area is exceptionally quiet and shady, offering a peaceful spot for a baby contact nap.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM / 1.5h

Fuerte de San José el Alto

Enjoy panoramic views of the Gulf from this beautifully preserved hilltop fort. Bring a baby carrier to navigate the historic stone steps securely.

Morning 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM / 1h

Ex-Templo de San José

Marvel at the stunning blue-and-yellow tiled facade of this historic Jesuit church. The quiet plaza outside is perfect for pushing the stroller in the afternoon shade.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM / 1h

Baluarte de Santa Rosa

Discover this charming defense bastion featuring a lovely interior courtyard. It is a highly peaceful, low-traffic site that makes for an easy, quiet baby excursion.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Pigua

Savor their legendary coconut shrimp in a refined, air-conditioned dining room. The spacious layout easily accommodates strollers, and the staff is famously welcoming to infants.

Marganzo

Enjoy traditional Campechano seafood and regional Yucatecan specialties. They offer sturdy highchairs, early dinner seatings at 6:00 PM, and a lively but family-friendly atmosphere.

Luan

An upscale, contemporary cafe serving exceptional artisanal breakfasts and espresso. Features cozy indoor seating, highchairs, and a peaceful environment perfect for a morning bottle feed.

La Recova Campeche

Indulge in prime steaks and Mexican fusion on a beautiful, covered outdoor terrace. The early evening breeze keeps the space cool, and the noise level is forgiving for a baby.

Bastión de San Francisco Restaurant

Dine on authentic local dishes right next to the historic city walls. Their spacious outdoor seating allows Mary Claire to look around while you enjoy fresh ceviche.
