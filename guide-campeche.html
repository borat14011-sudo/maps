Campeche

← back to travel board
Campeche offers a peaceful, crowd-free alternative to the Riviera Maya, wrapping your family in walled-city history and safe, pedestrian-friendly streets. The pastel-painted colonial buildings of the Centro Histórico provide a visually stimulating backdrop that will captivate your toddler from the safety of a stroller. With its gentle Gulf breezes, relaxed dining culture, and compact footprint, this UNESCO World Heritage site allows a slow-paced, luxury-infused getaway where baby’s nap schedule remains intact.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 minute taxi ride from Campeche International Airport (CPE) directly to downtown hotels; private transfers are easily pre-booked for seamless car seat installation.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Fuerte de San Miguel

Explore this spectacular 18th-century stone fortress overlooking the ocean, featuring authentic cannons and drawbridges. The paved access ramps are highly stroller-accessible, though the interior museum exhibits are best navigated with a baby carrier.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Malecón de Campeche

This sweeping, paved waterfront boardwalk is ideal for a sunrise stroller jog or a sunset family stroll. It is completely flat, traffic-free, and offers cool ocean breezes to keep your toddler comfortable.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Calle 59 Centro Histórico

The heart of the historic center, this vibrant, pedestrian-only street is lined with beautifully restored colonial facades. It is entirely car-free, allowing your toddler to safely toddle around while you enjoy an outdoor coffee.

Morning / 3h

Edzná Archaeological Zone

An ancient Mayan city located just 45 minutes from town, featuring impressive temples and massive green lawns. Skip the stroller here in favor of a hiking carrier so you can easily wander the grassy plazas and shaded pathways.

Morning / 45m

Jardín Botánico X'much Haltún

Housed inside a historic stone bastion, this compact botanical garden offers a shady, quiet oasis filled with tropical plants and local fauna. It is a quick, sensory-rich stop that is fully paved and easily managed with a stroller.

Morning / 1h

Baluarte de San Francisco

This massive historic bastion showcases the city's defensive walls and offers an exciting, safe rampart walk with panoramic views. The wide stone paths are safe for walking toddlers, but keep a hand held as there are historical drop-offs.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Parque Principal

The central town square is a shaded, lively hub featuring a beautiful central gazebo, manicured trees, and plenty of benches. It is the perfect spot for your toddler to watch local birds while you enjoy a fresh pastry in the shade.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Bonita

A clean, protected public beach located just 15 minutes south of the city center. It features incredibly calm, shallow, wave-free waters and palapas for shade, making it the safest spot in the area for a toddler’s first ocean dip.

Morning / 1-2h

Fuerte de San José el Alto

A historic hilltop fortress boasting bright yellow walls, old cannons, and a fascinating maritime museum. The surrounding grassy moat area is perfect for letting a toddler burn off energy, and the paved paths make stroller access a breeze.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Pigua

Order the signature Pámpano en Escabeche at this legendary seafood establishment. The spacious, air-conditioned dining room easily accommodates strollers and the professional staff is highly welcoming to young families.

Marganzo

Try the iconic Pan de Cazón in a vibrant, lively dining room where waitstaff wear traditional regional attire. The cheerful, bustling atmosphere naturally masks toddler chatter, and high chairs are readily available.

La Parroquia

Enjoy a casual, 24-hour diner experience and order the rich Horchata de Coco alongside traditional Huevos Motuleños. Its high-energy, local vibe means you never have to worry about a fussy baby disrupting other diners.

Chacool

Savor gourmet international fusion on their spacious outdoor patio located directly on pedestrian-only Calle 59. The outdoor setting provides plenty of room to park a stroller next to your table while enjoying a relaxed early dinner.

Chocol-Ha

Indulge in thick, artisanal hot chocolate and fresh churros at this charming, tranquil cafe courtyard. The quiet garden setting is perfect for a relaxing mid-afternoon pause to feed the baby away from the sun.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hacienda Puerta Campeche

This ultra-luxurious property features a dramatic, cave-like indoor-outdoor pool built directly into historic 17th-century ruins. It offers a tranquil, quiet atmosphere, spacious suites with cribs, and exemplary service tailored to traveling families.

Hotel Plaza Campeche

A highly reliable, modern hotel located just steps from the historic gates, offering excellent air conditioning and quiet double-paned windows. The central outdoor pool is perfect for an afternoon splash, and the staff is highly accommodating with infant amenities.

Hotel Boutique Casa Don Gustavo

A meticulously restored 18th-century manor house offering a cozy, intimate environment that feels like a private residence. The spacious, high-ceilinged rooms provide plenty of space for a travel crib, and the central pool courtyard is a peaceful sanctuary.

👨‍👩‍👧 While the historic center is highly walkable, the historic cobblestones can be exceptionally bumpy, making a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a high-quality baby carrier essential. Plan your outdoor excursions for the early morning or late afternoon to avoid the intense midday heat, reserving the peak sun hours for indoor naps or pool splashes. Pack high-quality mosquito repellent and lightweight long-sleeve clothing, as the proximity to the Gulf of Mexico means insects can emerge near the waterfront at dusk.
