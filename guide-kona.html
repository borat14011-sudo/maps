Kona’s sun-drenched volcanic coast offers a pristine sanctuary where dramatic lava fields meet the gentle, turquoise waters of the Pacific. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, this Hawaiian paradise blends upscale leisure with effortless infant-friendly adventures. The calm, shallow waters of Kamakahonu Cove provide a protected, wave-free saltwater wading pool perfect for a baby's first dip in the ocean. Along the paved Kailua Village oceanfront path, strollers glide smoothly past historic sites, allowing for breezy sunset walks with dramatic coastal views.
🚕 Getting there: The drive from Ellison Onizuka Kona International Airport (KOA) to the main Kailua-Kona hotel strip takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes. The best luxury family transport is a pre-arranged private SUV transfer through Kona Executive Car Service, which provides a clean, pre-installed rear-facing infant car seat and ample cargo space for your stroller and luggage.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Kamakahonu Beach

This ultra-calm, shallow cove is protected by a stone breakwater, making it virtually wave-free. It is the premier spot in Kona for baby Mary Claire to safely splash in the saltwater while Kathryn and Will relax under the shade of renting beach umbrellas.

Early morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM

Kaloko-Honokōhau National Historical Park

Stroll along the flat, well-graded gravel trails and boardwalks to spot Hawaiian green sea turtles basking on the shore. The quiet environment is perfect for a peaceful morning stroller nap under the shade of coastal trees.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Kahaluʻu Beach Park

Famous for its abundant marine life, this park features a sheltered shallow reef area. While one parent snorkels the calm waters, the other can sit with Mary Claire in the cool, shaded pavilion watching the colorful fish close to shore.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Kona Farmers Market

Browse local stalls for fresh, tropical fruits like papaya and apple bananas, which make perfect fresh purees for Mary Claire. The open-air market is completely flat and highly stroller-accessible, though arriving early avoids the midday heat.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Sadie Seymour Botanical Gardens

A tranquil, shaded oasis featuring tiered levels of native Hawaiian flora. The peaceful ambiance and cool canopy shade make it an excellent spot for a comfortable baby-carrier walk away from the midday coastal sun.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM

Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau National Historical Park

Walk beneath towering coconut palms along flat, sandy pathways rich with cultural history. This sacred site offers a serene atmosphere with gentle ocean breezes, making it ideal for a slow-paced family walk with an all-terrain stroller.

Late afternoon 4:30 PM - 5:30 PM

Old Kona Airport State Recreation Area

The former runway of Kona's old airport has been converted into a unique beach park with a massive, flat paved surface perfect for uninterrupted stroller walking. Shaded pavilion areas and gentle grassy lawns sit right next to the shore.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Mountain Thunder Coffee Plantation

Escape to the cloud forest of Kaloko, where temperatures are significantly cooler than the coast. The plantation offers shaded walking decks and easy gravel paths where parents can enjoy premium Kona coffee while baby rests comfortably in the cool mountain breeze.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Anaehoʻomalu Bay (A-Bay)

A expansive, crescent-shaped sandy beach boasting exceptionally calm waters and massive salt-and-pepper sands. It features a scenic, flat coastal trail lined with ancient fishponds and plenty of shady palm trees for a relaxing afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Huggo's

This iconic oceanfront destination offers spectacular sunset views and fresh seafood like ginger-crusted fresh catch. Book an early 5:00 PM outdoor terrace seating so the gentle crashing of the waves directly below can naturally mask any baby noises.

On the Rocks

The casual, sand-floor sister restaurant to Huggo's allows you to dine with your toes in the sand. It features a lively yet relaxed outdoor atmosphere, highchairs, and early evening live music that keeps Mary Claire thoroughly entertained.

Papa Kona Restaurant & Bar

Boasting an expansive, second-story open-air lanai with unobstructed harbor views, this spot is perfect for a breezy family brunch. They readily provide highchairs and serve excellent local avocado toast and house-roasted Kona coffee.

Foster's Kitchen

An upscale, scratch-kitchen venue located in the heart of Kailua-Kona. It offers convenient elevator access to its spacious, second-floor dining room, large comfortable booths, and a welcoming atmosphere for families with young children.

Umekes Fish Market Bar & Grill

Acclaimed for its ultra-fresh, locally caught poke bowls and Hawaiian plates. The large, open-air garden patio offers plenty of space for strollers next to the tables and a friendly, bustling environment where baby babbles blend right in.

Perched on the lava cliffs of Keauhou Bay, this resort features a multi-level pool complex with a sandy-bottom kid's play area. They provide complimentary cribs, spacious ocean-view suites perfect for naptime separation, and a quiet setting away from downtown noise.

Royal Kona Resort

$200-350/night

An iconic oceanfront hotel situated walking distance from Kailua-Kona shops. It features a scenic private saltwater swimming lagoon, oceanfront family-friendly dining, robust blackout curtains for baby’s midday naps, and pack-and-plays available upon request.

Courtyard by Marriott King Kamehameha's Kona Beach Hotel

$250-400/night

Located directly on the shores of the calm, wave-free Kamakahonu Beach, this hotel is a dream for families with toddlers. It offers spacious newly renovated double-queen rooms, high-quality cribs, and immediate stroller access to the flat Kailua Village pier.
