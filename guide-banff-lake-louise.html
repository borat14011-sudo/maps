Banff & Lake Louise

← back to travel board
Banff and Lake Louise offer soaring peaks and turquoise lakes, a serene alpine escape perfect for a young family. The stroller-friendly Banff Gondola provides gentle summit access, while the Lake Louise shoreline boardwalk is ideal for infant walks. Mary Claire can nap in a shaded spot by the calm waters of Moraine Lake, and the Fairmont’s heated pool is a quiet retreat. Luxury here means unhurried moments in nature’s grandeur.
🚕 Getting there: From Calgary International Airport (YYC) to Banff: 90-minute private SUV transfer with car seat and stroller storage, bookable via Banff Airporter or pre-arranged luxury sedan.

What to do

2-3 hours (morning)

Banff Gondola

Ride to the Sulphur Mountain summit for panoramic views; enclosed cabins are stroller-friendly and quiet for infants.

1-2 hours

Lake Louise Shoreline

Walk the paved Lakeshore Trail; flat and shaded, with benches for feeding and stellar mountain reflections.

1-2 hours (early to avoid crowds)

Moraine Lake

Iconic glacier-fed lake; accessible by shuttle or private car, with a short stroller-accessible path to the viewpoint.

1.5 hours

Johnston Canyon

Lower Falls boardwalk is paved and stroller-friendly; the sound of rushing water soothes babies.

1-2 hours

Banff Upper Hot Springs

Outdoor mineral pool with baby-friendly temperature; life jackets available, and the mountain air is refreshing.

30 minutes

Bow Falls

Short paved walk from Banff Springs Hotel to the falls; calm flow and a great spot for a family photo.

2 hours

Tunnel Mountain Trail

Gentle hike with a baby carrier (not stroller); partial shade and a rewarding summit view of the valley.

1-2 hours

Lake Minnewanka

Scenic drive and paved waterfront path; quieter than Lake Louise, with picnic areas and shaded benches.

45 minutes

Banff National Park Visitor Centre

Interactive exhibits and a children’s play area; air-conditioned stop for learning and a diaper change.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bison Restaurant

Wood-fired bison and local trout; highchairs, early seating available, and a warm rustic atmosphere.

Sky Bistro

Perched atop Sulphur Mountain via gondola; panoramic views, kids' menu, and calm lunch service.

Park Distillery

Farm-to-table comfort food with an outdoor patio; stroller storage and a relaxed vibe for a family dinner.

Maple Leaf Grille

Canadian classics like Alberta beef and maple-glazed salmon; highchairs and a quiet early-bird seating.

The Station Restaurant

Historic train station in Lake Louise with lake views; highchairs, outdoor seating, and a kids' pancake option.
