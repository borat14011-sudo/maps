Banff & Lake Louise

← back to travel board
Towering turquoise lakes, majestic peaks, and wildlife-filled meadows await your family in Canada's oldest national park. Banff and Lake Louise combine breathtaking rugged wilderness with incredibly accessible, stroller-friendly paved pathways and cozy alpine towns. Here, toddlers can splash in gentle glacier streams while parents drink in views that look like living postcards.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min drive via rental car or Banff Airporter shuttle from Calgary International Airport (YYC) to Banff town centre hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Banff Gondola

Ride to the top of Sulphur Mountain for panoramic views and an interactive discovery center that toddlers will love exploring.

Morning / 1-2h

Lake Louise Lakeshore Trail

Walk the flat, stroller-friendly pathway along the edge of this world-famous turquoise lake, perfect for little legs to run around.

Wednesday Afternoon / 1-2h

Banff Farmers Market

Browse local crafts, fresh fruits, and delicious baked goods at this vibrant outdoor market held every Wednesday in Central Park.

Early Morning / 1.5h

Johnston Canyon Lower Falls

Walk along suspended catwalks through a dramatic canyon to see a roaring waterfall, featuring flat, fenced walkways for safety.

Afternoon / 30-45 min

Bow Falls Viewpoint

Watch the roaring, powerful waters right next to Banff town, with an easy paved viewing area that requires zero hiking for tired toddlers.

Morning / 1.5h

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Explore the birthplace of Canada's national parks, featuring bubbling thermal waters, boardwalks, and interactive kids' exhibits.

Midday / 1h

Lake Minnewanka Cruise

Board a covered, heated boat for a scenic cruise across Banff’s largest lake, keeping toddlers entertained with wildlife sightings from the window.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Central Park Playground

Let the kids burn off energy at this scenic playground situated right along the Bow River, featuring mountain views and toddler swings.

Early Morning / 1h

Moraine Lake Rockpile Trail

Climb the short, step-laden Rockpile trail to capture the absolute best views of this intense blue lake, best done with a toddler carrier.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tooloulou's

Get the fluffy Acadian French toast or kid-friendly blueberry pancakes at this cozy, cajun-infused breakfast spot.

The Grizzly House

Enjoy a fun, interactive fondue experience where kids love dipping bread into melted cheese and cooking on hot rocks.

Rocky Mountain Flatbread Co.

Order the artisan wood-fired pizzas made with organic ingredients, featuring a great play area for restless toddlers.

Lake Louise Railway Station & Restaurant

Dine inside a beautifully restored 1910 railway heritage car and order the rich, comforting shepherd's pie.

Cows Banff

Grab a scoop of their famous, creamy handmade ice cream in a freshly baked waffle cone for the ultimate family treat.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and a sturdy, rugged stroller or baby carrier, as mountain weather changes rapidly and some scenic pathways are unpaved. Always book park shuttles and gondola tickets in advance to avoid long, toddler-testing queues at the trailheads.
