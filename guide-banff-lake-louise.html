Banff & Lake Louise

← back to travel board
Trade the humid Houston heat for crisp mountain air and turquoise waters that look photoshopped. Banff and Lake Louise offer a seamless blend of rugged Canadian Rockies scenery and pristine, high-end infrastructure perfect for navigating with a stroller. From wide, paved lakefront paths to cozy mountain lodges with crackling fires, this is an active but deeply comfortable escape where you can easily balance afternoon nap times with bucket-list alpine views. Your days will be filled with easy-access nature, warm hospitality, and upscale comfort that makes traveling with a toddler surprisingly stress-free.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min drive via private SUV transfer or premium rental car from Calgary International Airport (YYC) to Banff townsite along the scenic, divided Trans-Canada Highway.

What to do

Morning / 1.5h

Lake Louise Lakeshore Trail

Walk the wide, flat, gravel-paved trail along the shoreline to escape the crowds while taking in the iconic turquoise water. It is entirely stroller-accessible and features plenty of benches for feeding breaks or outfit changes.

Morning / 2h

Banff Gondola

Ride the fully enclosed cabins up Sulphur Mountain for 360-degree views without the grueling hike. The boardwalk at the top has stairs, but the multi-level summit building is fully stroller-friendly with elevators, clean family restrooms, and a sensory-friendly interpretive center.

Afternoon / 45m

Bow Falls Viewpoint

Experience a roaring waterfall right on the edge of Banff town with absolutely zero hiking required. The paved parking lot and flat viewing platforms make it incredibly easy to roll a stroller right up to the rushing waters of the Bow River.

Early Morning / 2h

Johnston Canyon Lower Falls

Walk the sturdy, suspended catwalks built into the canyon walls to see roaring waterfalls and deep pools. The trail to the Lower Falls is relatively flat and paved, making it manageable with an all-terrain stroller, though a baby carrier is best for the narrow sections.

Afternoon / 1h

Cascade of Time Garden

Explore terraced stone pathways, rustic wooden bridges, and vibrant flower beds just across the bridge from downtown Banff. It is a peaceful, quiet escape with paved paths where your toddler can toddle safely away from the busy main street crowds.

Midday / 1.5h

Lake Minnewanka Cruise

Board a heated, covered boat for a scenic cruise across Banff’s largest lake while learning about local history and folklore. The enclosed cabin keeps the mountain wind off the baby, and the gentle vibration of the boat is a proven toddler nap-inducer.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Discover the birthplace of Canada's National Parks, featuring a warm, bubbling thermal cave and easy boardwalk trails. The indoor museum exhibits are interactive for young minds, and the flat boardwalks outside are highly stroller-accessible.

Early Morning / 2h

Moraine Lake Shoreline Trail

Marvel at the surreal blue waters of Moraine Lake from the flat, easy-to-navigate shoreline trail. Taking the official Parks Canada shuttle makes transport seamless, and the trail is wide enough to navigate comfortably with a rugged stroller.

Morning / 1h

Fenland Trail

Take an easy, flat, 2-kilometer loop through a quiet spruce forest alongside Echo Creek. This hidden gem is highly stroller-friendly, deeply shaded, and offers a peaceful nature walk right on the edge of Banff town.

🍽️ Where to eat

Three Bears Brewery and Restaurant

Order the hand-stretched artisan pizzas and locally brewed beers in this spacious, high-ceilinged space with a retractable roof. It’s loud enough to easily drown out toddler squeals, offers high chairs, and has plenty of room to park a stroller right at the table.

The Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise - Louiza

Savor fresh, Mediterranean-inspired dishes like the signature pan-seared sea bass with stunning views of Lake Louise. The upscale yet highly family-accommodating atmosphere offers early dinner seatings and plenty of space for high chairs and strollers.

Park Distillery Restaurant & Bar

Dig into campfire-inspired rotisserie chicken and wood-fired steaks while sipping house-made gin. The bustling, casual-cool vibe is perfect for an early 5:00 PM family dinner, and the staff are incredibly welcoming to young families.

Tooloulou's

Enjoy a hearty, Cajun-inspired breakfast of Acadian French toast or a customized benedict. This cozy spot opens early at 8:00 AM—perfect for early-rising toddlers—and features a warm, bustling environment where babies fit right in.

The Maple Leaf Grill & Lounge

Indulge in premium Alberta beef tenderloin or wild BC salmon in a sophisticated, rustic cabin setting. They offer a refined dining experience that remains highly accommodating to families, complete with high chairs and a great kids' menu.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Fairmont Banff Springs

Live like royalty in this iconic 'Castle in the Rockies,' featuring a massive indoor kid-friendly pool, complimentary high-end cribs, and professional babysitting services. Its close proximity to Banff town makes mid-day nap returns effortless.

Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise

Enjoy unparalleled, doorstep access to Lake Louise, allowing you to beat the morning crowds before the baby’s first nap. The luxury resort offers spacious suites, top-tier crib setups, and stellar on-site dining so you don't have to travel far after bedtime.

The Rimrock Resort Hotel

Perched high on Sulphur Mountain, this luxury resort offers dramatic valley views, a large indoor pool, and exceptionally quiet, spacious family suites. It is located just steps from the Banff Upper Hot Springs and Banff Gondola, keeping drive times to a minimum.

👨‍👩‍👧 Book the Parks Canada shuttles to Moraine Lake and Lake Louise months in advance, as private vehicles are highly restricted and the shuttle ride is the most seamless way to travel with a toddler. Mountain weather changes rapidly, so pack layers like merino wool and a windproof stroller cover to keep your baby cozy during sudden alpine temperature drops. Opt for an all-terrain stroller with larger air-filled rubber wheels to easily navigate the gravel lakeside paths and light dirt trails around Banff.
