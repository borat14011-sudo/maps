What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Banff Gondola

Ride to the summit of Sulphur Mountain for panoramic views, a hands-on family interpretive center, and an easy boardwalk. Toddlers will love the enclosed cabin ride and the interactive multisensory theater at the top.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Lake Louise Lakeshore Stroll

Walk the flat, stroller-friendly paved path along the edge of this world-famous turquoise lake. Toddlers can safely throw pebbles into the water while parents admire the Victoria Glacier.

Late Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Banff Upper Hot Springs

Soak in the warm, mineral-rich waters surrounded by stunning alpine scenery. The pool is family-friendly with shallow areas and life jacket rentals available for little swimmers.

Early Morning / 2 hours

Johnston Canyon Lower Falls Walk

Meander along sturdy, railed catwalks suspended over a rushing canyon to a dramatic waterfall. The trail to the Lower Falls is relatively flat and highly engaging for toddlers, though a baby carrier is recommended over a stroller.

Afternoon / 1 hour

Bow Falls and Recreation Area

Experience a powerful, roaring waterfall right next to Banff town with zero hiking required. The wide, flat viewing area is perfect for toddlers to run around safely near the water's edge.

Midday / 1-2 hours

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Discover the birthplace of Canada's national parks with an easy boardwalk trail and thermal waters. Toddlers will be fascinated by the bubbling warm springs and the tiny, endangered snails.

Wednesday Morning / 1-2 hours

Banff Mountain Market

Stroll through this vibrant summer market to sample fresh local fruits, baked goods, and handmade crafts. It is a relaxed space where toddlers can snack on fresh berries while parents browse the stalls.

Morning / 2-3 hours

Moraine Lake Rockpile and Shoreline

Take the park shuttle to witness the jaw-dropping Valley of the Ten Peaks reflected in brilliant blue water. The shoreline trail is flat and easy for toddlers, offering plenty of safe spots to watch canoes glide by.

Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Banff Central Park Playground

Let the kids burn off energy at this scenic playground situated right along the Bow River in downtown Banff. It features modern play structures, grassy picnic areas, and clean public restrooms.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Grizzly House

Order the interactive family-style cheese and chocolate fondue, which keeps kids entertained and fed in a fun, retro lodge setting.

Tooloulou's

Try the fluffy, thick-cut French toast or the locally famous Acadian breakfasts that are perfect for sharing with hungry toddlers.

Whitebark Cafe

Grab a perfectly crafted flat white for yourself and a fresh-baked organic blueberry muffin for your toddler before heading out for hikes.

Wild Flour Bakery

Get the artisanal grilled cheese sandwich on freshly baked sourdough bread and a warm chocolate chip cookie for an easy family lunch.

Bear Street Tavern

Order the specialty pizza served with honey and chili oil for dipping the crust, a huge hit with both adventurous parents and picky toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Book your Moraine Lake and Lake Louise shuttle tickets months in advance to ensure stress-free transport with little ones. Always pack layers and a sturdy baby carrier, as mountain weather changes rapidly and many scenic trails are too rugged for strollers.
