Surrounded by the towering, snow-capped peaks of the Canadian Rockies, Banff and Lake Louise offer an elite alpine sanctuary of unparalleled natural beauty. Majestic turquoise lakes and crisp mountain air provide a breathtaking backdrop for sophisticated relaxation and memory-making. The paved, stroller-ready path along the Bow River trail offers seamless, low-effort exploration amidst dense pine forests. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, this pristine wilderness balances exclusive mountain culture with gentle, highly accessible outdoor adventures.
🚕 Getting there: Calgary International Airport (YYC) to Banff is a scenic 1.5-hour drive; the best luxury family transport is a private SUV transfer booked through Banff Sedan, featuring pre-installed rear-facing infant car seats and ample cargo space for a full-size stroller.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Lake Louise Lakeshore Trail

This iconic flat, paved stroll offers stunning views of the turquoise lake. It is completely stroller-friendly, with ample shade from towering evergreens and benches for peaceful feeding stops.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Banff Gondola

Ride to the summit of Sulphur Mountain in fully enclosed, draft-free cabins. The state-of-the-art boardwalk at the top is stroller-accessible and offers breathtaking alpine panoramas with minimal wind exposure.

Afternoon 2-3pm / 1h

Bow Falls Viewpoint

An easily accessible, flat paved viewpoint located right near the town center. The roaring falls provide a soothing natural white noise that is perfect for helping baby Mary Claire nap in her stroller.

Early Morning 8-10am / 2h

Johnston Canyon Lower Falls

Walk along sturdy cantilevered catwalks built into the canyon walls. Best experienced using an infant carrier rather than a stroller; the trail is shaded, cool, and features refreshing, gentle mountain mist.

Late Afternoon 4-5pm / 1h

Vermilion Lakes Drive

A peaceful scenic drive and flat lakeside trail system just outside town. Perfect for a quiet afternoon car nap for Mary Claire, or a gentle stroller walk along the flat, quiet wooden docks.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Explore the birthplace of Canada's national parks via flat, highly stroller-accessible wooden boardwalks. The outdoor geothermal pools are quiet and fascinating for parents, with gentle terrain for baby.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Cascade of Time Garden

A beautifully terraced garden located at the historic Administration Building. Paved pathways wind past stone bridges, ponds, and vibrant flower displays, offering a quiet, peaceful stroller walk.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Lake Minnewanka Shoreline

Banff's largest lake features a flat, paved path near the main day-use area. It is highly stroller-friendly, offers expansive mountain views, and features quiet picnic areas perfect for a relaxed family pause.

Early Morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Moraine Lake Lakeshore Trail

Skip the steep rockpile and enjoy the flat, quiet shoreline trail. This shaded path offers iconic turquoise lake views with very low ambient noise, making it highly suitable for an infant carrier hike.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bison Restaurant

Sophisticated farm-to-table Canadian cuisine. Excellent early 5:00 PM dinner seating, highchairs are available, and the dining room has a lively upscale atmosphere that easily absorbs baby sounds.

Three Bears Brewery and Restaurant

A modern, bright garden-themed space that is highly welcoming to families. Features spacious booths for baby gear, highchairs, an outdoor terrace, and a relaxed, bustling vibe where infants fit right in.

Park Distillery Restaurant & Bar

Famous campfire-inspired mountain cuisine. Great for early dining with highchairs, a spacious layout, and a casual yet stylish atmosphere that accommodates stroller parking easily.

Tooloulou's

A beloved, high-energy breakfast and lunch institution. Perfect for early-rising families with an infant, offering plenty of highchairs and a bustling atmosphere where a babbling baby is never an issue.

Juniper Bistro

Boasts spectacular, elevated views of the Bow Valley. Highchairs are available, and the outdoor terrace is ideal for an early, scenic dinner with baby Mary Claire enjoying the fresh mountain breeze.
