Reykjavík

← back to travel board
Reykjavík wraps families in a cozy, colorful embrace of Norse folklore, steaming thermal waters, and pristine coastal beauty. This walkable, exceptionally safe capital feels like a giant playground where toddlers can safely splash in public pools and spot whales right from the harbor. It is the perfect, stress-free gateway to Iceland's wild, dramatic landscapes for travelers of all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min Flybus shuttle or private taxi from Keflavík International Airport to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Laugardalslaug Geothermal Pool

Splash in the warm, family-friendly geothermal pools while toddlers enjoy the shallow, heated paddling areas and safe water slides. It is a fantastic local ritual that keeps everyone warm even on chilly days.

Late Morning / 1 hour

Hallgrímskirkja Church Tower

Take the smooth elevator up this landmark church for breathtaking, 360-degree views of the colorful city roofs. Toddlers will love pointing out the toy-like houses and boats in the harbor below.

Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Whales of Iceland

Walk among life-sized, soft-to-touch model whales in a dimly lit, soothing blue hall that feels like an underwater dreamscape. Stroller-friendly paths make it easy to wander while toddlers gaze up in awe at the giant blue whale.

Lunchtime / 1 hour

Hlemmur Mathöll Food Hall

A bustling, indoor historic food hall offering a cozy spot to sample diverse local treats without long waits. It has plenty of high chairs and a lively atmosphere where toddler noise blends right in.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Perlan (Wonders of Iceland)

Explore a real indoor ice cave made of safe, packed snow and watch a dazzling northern lights show in the planetarium. Toddlers will love running around the interactive glacier exhibits and the secure outdoor viewing deck.

Morning / 1 hour

Tjörnin Lake (The Pond)

Feed the ducks, geese, and majestic swans at this peaceful city center lake surrounded by colorful historic houses. Strollers roll easily along the paved paths, and there is a great playground nearby.

Morning / 1.5 hours

Reykjavík Maritime Museum

Discover Iceland's seafaring history with kid-friendly interactive exhibits, including a dedicated wooden play-boat area. Toddlers can play captain, dress up, and steer their own imaginary voyages.

Afternoon / 2 hours

Nauthólsvík Geothermal Beach

Dig in golden sand and splash in a warm, geothermally heated sea lagoon that is surprisingly comfortable for little feet. It is the perfect spot for a relaxed summer afternoon of safe sandcastle building.

Afternoon / 3 hours

Reykjavík Family Park and Zoo

Get up close with Icelandic farm animals, seals, and reindeer in a gentle, low-key setting. Toddlers will love the miniature train rides, carousels, and massive outdoor play structures.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bæjarins Beztu Pylsur

Grab a famous, quick Icelandic hot dog loaded with sweet mustard and crunchy onions that toddlers adore.

Kaffi Loki

Sample traditional Icelandic rye bread ice cream and sweet pancakes in a cozy space directly opposite the big church.

Cafe Babalú

Enjoy grilled cheese sandwiches and giant slices of cake in a quirky, toy-filled cafe with a Star Wars themed bathroom.

Brauð & Co.

Grab a warm, freshly baked cinnamon roll with gooey glaze from this vibrant, graffiti-covered bakery.

Messinn

Feast on family-style pan-fried fish served hot in copper pans with sweet potatoes that appeal to toddler palates.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack windproof, waterproof layers for the entire family, as Icelandic weather can shift from sunny to rainy in minutes. Rent a sturdy, large-wheeled stroller to navigate gravel paths easily, and take advantage of the free high chairs and changing tables widely available in nearly every establishment.
