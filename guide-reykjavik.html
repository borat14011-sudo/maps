Reykjavik

← back to travel board
Reykjavik wraps families in a cozy, sophisticated embrace of volcanic landscapes and sleek Scandinavian design. The city is exceptionally welcoming to young families, offering incredibly clean air and pristine, stroller-friendly harbor paths. Stroll along the scenic, paved sculpture path of Sæbraut past the Sun Voyager to experience dramatic sea views with baby Mary Claire. The city's legendary geothermal pools, like Laugardalslaug, provide a safe, warm environment for a gentle splash with your infant.
🚕 Getting there: Keflavík International Airport to central Reykjavik is a 45-minute drive best navigated via a pre-booked private luxury SUV transfer equipped with a rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Laugardalslaug

Iceland's flagship geothermal pool complex features a warm, shallow paddling pool perfect for baby's first thermal dip, with pram parking nearby.

Midday 11:30am-1pm / 1.5h

Harpa Concert Hall

This architectural marvel features wide, stroller-friendly indoor ramps and peaceful glass-paneled harbor views that keep babies visually engaged.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Tjörnin (The Pond)

A tranquil city-center lake offering flat, paved walking paths and plenty of benches perfect for scenic outdoor nursing and feeding the ducks.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Perlan

The Wonders of Iceland exhibition features a climate-controlled planetarium and a stroller-accessible observation deck offering 360-degree views.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Hallgrímskirkja

Take the smooth tower elevator for panoramic views; the spacious pedestrian plaza outside is perfect for a calm stroller nap afterward.

Late Afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Grótta Lighthouse

A peaceful nature reserve at the tip of the peninsula boasting a flat, paved coastal path ideal for a breezy sunset stroller walk.

Morning 10am-1pm / 3h

Viðey Island via Elding Ferry

Enjoy a calm, five-minute ferry ride to a serene island with wide gravel paths and peaceful, quiet fields perfect for an outdoor infant picnic.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Reykjavik Family Park and Zoo

Introduce baby to friendly Icelandic horses and harbor seals along wide, quiet stroller paths with dedicated, heated nursing rooms on-site.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Nauthólsvík Geothermal Beach

This unique beach features a warm-water sea lagoon and soft golden sand that provides a perfect, safe sensory play experience for your baby.

🍽️ Where to eat

Matarkjallarinn (Food Cellar)

Enjoy upscale Icelandic cuisine in a sophisticated basement venue; book an early 5:30 PM slot for highchairs and soothing live piano music.

Slippbarinn

This trendy eatery near the old harbor offers spacious booth seating, highchairs, and an early dinner service that easily accommodates strollers.

Kaffi Loki

Savor traditional rye bread ice cream directly opposite Hallgrímskirkja in a bright, family-welcoming space equipped with convenient highchairs.

Grandi Mathöll

An upscale harbor food hall where the vibrant, gentle buzz of conversation easily masks any unexpected baby noise while you dine.

La Primavera (Marshall House)

Located in a chic art space, this restaurant offers spacious seating configurations, fine Italian fare, and an exceptionally welcoming staff.
