Isla Holbox

← back to travel board
Isla Holbox is a sun-drenched sanctuary where shallow, wave-free turquoise waters stretch for miles, making it an absolute dream for families. Kids will love searching for seashells along pristine sandbars and spotting wild pink flamingos wading in the shallows. Here, the slow pace of life is dictated only by the gentle sway of palm trees and the soft rumble of golf carts on sandy roads.
🚕 Getting there: ~2.5-hour private shuttle from Cancun Airport to Chiquila port, followed by a 20-minute ferry and a 5-minute golf cart taxi to your hotel

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Holbox (Main Beach)

The main beach offers miles of knee-deep, wave-free turquoise water perfect for safe splashing. Toddlers can easily wade far out into the calm ocean without any sudden drop-offs.

Early Morning / 2-3h

Punta Mosquito Sandbar Walk

Walk along a magical, shallow sandbar stretching out into the ocean to spot wild flamingos. Bring a toddler carrier as the walk can be long, but the shallow wading is an unforgettable adventure.

Afternoon / 2h

Explore by Rental Golf Cart

Rent a golf cart to explore the island's sandy roads and vibrant street art at your own pace. Toddlers will love the open-air breeze and the novelty of riding in a cart.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa Punta Cocos

A quiet beach on the western tip famous for its calm waters, shallow tidal pools, and wooden overwater swings. It's the ultimate spot for a relaxed sunset with toddlers.

Night / 1-2h

Bioluminescence Tour

Experience the magic of glowing water under the night sky in the shallow, calm bays. Opt for an early evening tour during a new moon so toddlers can marvel at the sparkles before bedtime.

Morning / 3-4h

Yalahau Cenote & Isla Pasion Boat Tour

Take a family-friendly boat excursion to a freshwater cenote spring and a tiny island populated by birds. Toddlers will enjoy the breezy boat ride and splashing in the shallow cenote wading pool.

Morning / 1-2h

Holbox Street Art Hunt

Wander the sandy downtown streets to admire massive, colorful murals depicting local life and whimsical animals. Ditch the stroller and use a baby carrier to view these masterpieces.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal

Visit the small municipal market to sample fresh, tropical fruits and cheap, delicious local snacks. Toddlers will love tasting sweet mangoes, bananas, and freshly squeezed juices.

Afternoon / 1h

Refugio Holbox

A heartwarming volunteer-run shelter caring for injured island animals, from raccoons to friendly dogs. Toddlers will love meeting the rescued animals and learning about wildlife care.

🍽️ Where to eat

Roots Pizza a Leña

Get their famous, legendary lobster pizza cooked in a wood-fired oven while sitting in a fun, sandy-floored garden.

Painapol

Get the beautifully presented, nutrient-packed fresh fruit smoothie bowls and avocado toast in a jungle-chic setting.

Taco Queto

Get the classic al pastor tacos and cheesy gringas at this casual, incredibly kid-friendly spot with plastic tables on the sand.

Luuma

Get the artisanal tapas, fresh fish tacos, and non-alcoholic mocktails served in a gorgeous, sandy-floored garden courtyard.

El Guayabo

Get the fresh whole fried fish, garlic shrimp, and kid-friendly chicken quesadillas right near the main square.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring plenty of cash pesos and reef-safe mosquito repellent, as ATMs frequently run out of money and mosquitoes are fierce at sunset. Ditch the stroller entirely for a rugged baby carrier, as the unpaved, sandy roads are impossible for wheels.
