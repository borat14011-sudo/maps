Lake Constance (Bodensee)

← back to travel board
Nestled where Germany, Austria, and Switzerland meet, Lake Constance offers a fairytale landscape of sparkling blue waters, medieval castles, and flower-filled islands. Families can easily hop between countries by scenic ferry, exploring shallow lake beaches, alpine cable cars, and interactive outdoor museums. It is an exceptionally clean, safe, and stroller-friendly destination perfect for relaxed European wandering with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min train ride or taxi from Friedrichshafen Airport (FDH) directly to central harbor hotels in Lindau or Friedrichshafen

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Mainau Island (Flower Island)

A magical island paradise bursting with spectacular botanical gardens, a massive butterfly house, and sprawling wooden playgrounds. Toddlers will love splashing in the dedicated water play area and meeting goats at the petting zoo.

Morning / 2-3h

Pfänder Cable Car (Pfänderbahn)

Whisk the family up to the peak of Mount Pfänder for panoramic views of 240 alpine peaks and the glittering lake below. The stroller-friendly mountain path leads to a free alpine wildlife park where toddlers can spot deer, wild boars, and ibex.

Full Day / 5-6h

Ravensburger Spieleland

A massive, beautifully themed amusement park dedicated to beloved Ravensburger board games and puzzles. It features incredibly gentle, toddler-friendly rides, interactive tractor tours, and giant soft-play areas perfect for burning off energy.

Afternoon / 2h

Lake Dwelling Museum (Pfahlbauten)

Step back in time at this UNESCO-listed open-air museum featuring reconstructed Bronze Age stilt houses built directly over the water. A sensory barefoot path and wide, stroller-accessible wooden walkways make it fun and safe for toddlers to explore.

Morning / 2h

Affenberg Salem (Monkey Mountain)

Walk through a beautiful forested sanctuary where over 200 Barbary macaques roam completely free in the trees. Toddlers will be absolutely spellbound watching the playful monkeys interact right beside the walking paths.

Afternoon / 2h

Zeppelin Museum

Explore the birthplace of giant airships inside this harbor-side museum, which features an incredible walk-in replica of the Hindenburg. It offers a fantastic indoor option with a dedicated interactive kids' play area and harbor views.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Lindau Harbor and Lighthouse

Stroll the historic harbor of Lindau island, guarded by a magnificent Bavarian Lion statue and a towering stone lighthouse. Toddlers will love watching the colorful passenger ferries dock while enjoying a scoop of local gelato along the promenade.

Morning / 2-3h

Rheinfall (Rhine Falls)

Experience the roaring power of Europe's largest waterfall up close via a safe, exciting boat ride to the central rock. Little ones can easily view the rushing water from wide, stroller-friendly platforms directly over the rapids.

Morning / 1-2h

Konstanz Weekly Market (Wochenmarkt)

A bustling, sensory-rich open-air market filled with colorful local fruits, alpine cheeses, and fresh baked goods. It is the perfect spot to pick up giant soft pretzels and fresh berries for a relaxed toddler picnic by the lake.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant Zeppelinhof (Friedrichshafen)

Order the traditional Swabian Maultaschen (savory meat-filled dumplings) served in a kid-friendly, cozy garden setting near the harbor.

HafenKantine (Lindau)

Grab the crispy, freshly caught backfisch (battered lake fish) served with golden fries, ideal for a casual family lunch on the water.

Gasthaus zum Becher (Meersburg)

Enjoy the classic, perfectly crispy Wiener Schnitzel with warm potato salad inside this historic, welcoming tavern.

Restaurant Seekuh (Konstanz)

Try the homemade Kässpätzle (cheesy German noodles) at this highly welcoming spot featuring a dedicated kids' indoor play corner.

Wirtshaus am See (Bregenz)

Feast on fresh-caught Bodensee Kretzer (perch fillets) while dining on a spacious, scenic lakeside terrace with plenty of space for strollers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Purchase the Bodensee Card Plus for all-inclusive ferry crossings and hassle-free entry to almost every major kid-friendly attraction around the lake. Always carry a mix of Euros and Swiss Francs in your bag, and keep swimsuits handy since tempting splash parks and grassy lakeside beaches are around every corner.
