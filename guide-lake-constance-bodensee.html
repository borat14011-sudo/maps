Lake Constance (Bodensee)

← back to travel board
Nestled where Germany, Austria, and Switzerland meet, Lake Constance offers a fairytale landscape of shimmering blue water, medieval castles, and alpine backdrops. Families can cycle flat shoreline paths, explore lush flower islands, and hop between countries via scenic ferry rides. It is a wonderfully stress-free European playground designed for slow-paced wandering with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min train ride from Friedrichshafen Airport or ~1 hour drive from Zurich Airport to most lakeside hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Mainau Island (Flower Island)

Explore a tropical paradise filled with millions of flowers, a giant butterfly house, and massive wooden playgrounds. Toddlers will love the petting zoo and water play areas designed just for splash-friendly fun.

Morning / 2-3h

Pfänder Cable Car (Pfänderbahn)

Ride a panoramic cable car up to the peak for sweeping three-country views and an alpine wildlife park featuring deer and mountain goats. The path is stroller-friendly, and there is a great playground at the top.

Full Day / 5-6h

Ravensburger Spieleland

This massive theme park based on the famous Ravensburger board games is tailor-made for families with younger kids. Toddlers can enjoy gentle tractor rides, soft play zones, and meeting their favorite characters.

Afternoon / 2h

Lake Dwelling Museum (Pfahlbauten)

Step back into the Stone and Bronze Ages in this open-air museum of reconstructed stilt houses over the water. Toddlers will be fascinated by the wooden walkways and the interactive hands-on archaeology zones.

Morning / 2h

Affenberg Salem (Monkey Mountain)

Walk through a pine forest where over 200 Barbary macaques roam free, and you can feed them specially provided popcorn. The wide, flat gravel paths are perfect for sturdy strollers and curious toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Lindau Old Town

Stroll the car-free cobblestone streets of this island town, ending at the iconic harbor flanked by a stone lion and a lighthouse. Toddlers can safely toddle along the harbor wall and watch the giant ferries glide in.

Morning / 2-3h

Rhine Falls (Rheinfall)

Witness the sheer power of Europe's largest waterfall, with options for exciting boat rides that get you close to the spray. The paved viewing platforms are easily accessible with a stroller.

Midday / 1-2h

Konstanz Market Hall (Markthalle)

Immerse your senses in this bustling indoor market filled with regional cheeses, fresh pastries, and local fruits. It’s a great, low-stress spot to pick up picnic supplies that even picky toddlers will enjoy.

Afternoon / 2h

Meersburg Castle (Alte Burg)

Explore the oldest inhabited castle in Germany, complete with a dungeon, knight's hall, and beautiful lakeside gardens. Kids will love the medieval atmosphere, though note that some towers require carrying toddlers up stairs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gasthof weisses Kreuz

Feast on crispy, golden Wiener Schnitzel served with traditional Austrian potato salad in a cozy, highly welcoming environment.

Hafenmeisterei

Grab a table by the harbor and enjoy fresh-caught Bodensee whitefish (Kretzer) overlooking the water.

Seerestaurant Bellevue

Indulge in Swiss cheese fondue or lakeside perch fillets while the kids enjoy the dedicated indoor play corner.

Konstanzer Konzil

Order the homemade Swabian Maultaschen (stuffed pasta pockets) in this historic, cavernous, child-friendly lakeside building.

Gutsschänke Meersburg

Savor local Flammkuchen (flatbread with sour cream and bacon) on the sun-drenched terrace overlooking the vineyards.
