Framed by the snow-capped Andes, Santiago offers a sophisticated blend of European-style boulevards and modern Latin energy. The city's upscale districts of Vitacura and Las Condes feature remarkably flat, stroller-ready parkways perfect for leisurely walks with baby Mary Claire. Parents can savor world-class Cabernet while the little one naps under the shade of giant palms in the manicured Parque Bicentenario. This high-altitude capital combines pristine private medical infrastructure with tranquil, wind-sheltered valley breezes for an effortless family escape.
🚕 Getting there: Private Mercedes V-Class transfer with pre-installed premium infant car seat, approximately 30 minutes from Arturo Merino Benítez International Airport (SCL) to the luxury hotel districts of Las Condes and Vitacura.

What to do

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Parque Bicentenario

A meticulously landscaped oasis in Vitacura with wide, paved, stroller-friendly paths, shade-giving palms, and a serene lagoon where Mary Claire can watch pink flamingos and black-necked swans.

Early morning 9:00-10:30am / 1.5h

Cerro San Cristóbal Funicular

Ride the historic, recently modernized funicular railway up the hill for sweeping Andean views; the cabins easily accommodate a folded luxury stroller, and the summit breeze is incredibly refreshing for baby.

Afternoon 2:00-4:00pm / 2h

Parque Araucano

Located in Las Condes, this park features beautiful rose gardens, completely flat paved walkways, and plenty of shaded benches under mature trees, ideal for a quiet mid-day bottle feeding.

Late afternoon 4:30-6:30pm / 2h

Templo Bahá'í de Sudamérica

A spectacular domed temple set in the Andean foothills with peaceful, manicured gardens and fully paved ramps; the incredibly quiet, spiritual atmosphere is perfect for a gentle stroller nap.

Morning 10:00-11:30am / 1.5h

Sky Costanera

South America's tallest building features glass-walled observation decks reached by ultra-smooth, rapid elevators; it is fully enclosed, temperature-controlled, and stroller-accessible, making it comfortable for infants.

Midday 12:00-2:00pm / 2h

Centro Cultural La Moneda

Located beneath the presidential palace, this spacious, modern cultural center is fully air-conditioned and features wide ramps, pristine family-friendly restrooms, and quiet exhibition halls.

Morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Pueblito Los Dominicos

A charming, tree-shaded artisan market designed like an old colonial village; the dirt paths are flat and easy for all-terrain strollers, and the resident peacocks and ducks will delight Mary Claire.

Morning 9:30-11:00am / 1.5h

Jardín Mapulemu

A botanical garden hidden within San Cristóbal Hill showcasing native Chilean flora, featuring paved pathways, gentle slopes, and absolute tranquility away from city noise.

Afternoon 3:30-5:00pm / 1.5h

Parque Forestal

A historic, European-style linear park lined with massive plane trees that provide a dense canopy of shade, ideal for a relaxing afternoon stroll along the Mapocho River.
