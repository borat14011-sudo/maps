Santiago

← back to travel board
Framed by the majestic, snow-capped Andes, Santiago blends vibrant South American energy with stroller-friendly parks and European-style charm. Families can glide above the treetops in colorful cable cars, run through expansive green plazas, and feast on sweet dulce de leche treats. It is a welcoming metropolis where urban adventure meets natural wonder at every turn.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 min official taxi (booked inside the terminal via Transvip or Taxi Oficial) to downtown or Providencia hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Parque Metropolitano and Funicular

Ride the historic funicular railway to the summit of Cerro San Cristóbal for breathtaking Andean views and a peaceful sanctuary. Toddlers will love the colorful cable car ride and the spacious plazas at the top.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque Bicentenario

A sprawling, modern park in Vitacura featuring a beautiful pond filled with pink flamingos and black-necked swans that kids can feed. There are excellent wooden playground structures and plenty of flat paths for strollers.

Morning / 3h

Museo Interactivo Mirador

This massive, hands-on science museum is a dream for curious kids with interactive physics, space, and sensory exhibits. The dedicated MIMitos zone is specifically designed for toddlers to explore safely.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

Dive into a sensory wonderland of fresh seafood, lively vendors, and historic wrought-iron architecture. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant king crabs, though it is best to use a baby carrier due to narrow, busy aisles.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de Armas and Changing of the Guard

The historic heart of the city features palm trees, street performers, and the grand Metropolitan Cathedral. Toddlers can chase pigeons while parents admire the architecture and watch the musical changing of the guard ceremony.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Parque Araucano

This giant park features an incredible adventure playground, a rose garden, and giant shade trees. Toddlers will love the Aviary and the musical water fountains that are perfect for splashing in on warm days.

Morning / 1-2h

Cerro Santa Lucía

Climb this fairytale hill in the middle of the city, complete with stone castles, fountains, and secret gardens. While the top steps are too steep for toddlers, the lower terraces and Neptune Fountain are highly accessible and magical for little ones.

Morning / 1-2h

La Vega Central

A bustling, chaotic, and vibrant fresh produce market loaded with sweet tropical fruits and giant avocados. It is a great place to buy cheap, healthy snacks like fresh berries for your toddler, but keep them close in the crowds.

Afternoon / 2h

Bahá'í Temple of South America

Set in the Andean foothills, this architectural marvel looks like a blooming flower and offers a serene atmosphere. The lush surrounding gardens are highly stroller-friendly and provide spectacular, breezy views over the entire valley.

🍽️ Where to eat

Emporio La Rosa

Get a scoop of their award-winning ice cream, specifically the chocolate-basil or traditional dulce de leche flavors, at their flagship location by Parque Forestal.

Liguria

Order the classic Chilean empanadas de pino or a comforting plate of pastel de choclo in a highly welcoming, colorful, retro atmosphere.

Galindo

Try the hearty, traditional cazuela de ave chicken soup or pastel de choclo at this legendary, ultra-casual neighborhood spot in Bellavista.

Mestizo

Order the fresh ceviche, local fish dishes, or artisan empanadas while enjoying a stylish, open-air dining experience right on the edge of Parque Bicentenario.

Café Wonderland

Get the Alice in Wonderland-themed high tea set and adorable decorated cupcakes that toddlers will find absolutely magical.

👨‍👩‍👧 Book transfers through official counters like Transvip inside the arrivals hall to secure safe rides with child seats. Santiago's metro is highly efficient and stroller-accessible, but it is best to completely avoid the heavy rush hours of 8:00 to 9:30 AM and 6:00 to 8:00 PM.
