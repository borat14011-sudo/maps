Portland

← back to travel board
Where lush giant forests meet a vibrant, donut-loving urban core, Portland offers a whimsical playground for families of all ages. Easily navigated and packed with hands-on museums, bubbling fountains, and sprawling parks, it's a city designed for curious little wanderers.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 minute drive via taxi/rideshare, or a direct 40-minute MAX Light Rail ride from PDX to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Oregon Museum of Science and Industry (OMSI)

A massive, hands-on science playground featuring a dedicated Science Playground for kids under six. Toddlers will love splashing in the water area and digging in the sensory sand.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Washington Park & International Rose Test Garden

Stroll through thousands of blooming roses with stunning views of Mt. Hood. Toddlers can freely wander the paved pathways and enjoy smelling the colorful blossoms.

Morning / 3h

Oregon Zoo

Navigate the shady, stroller-friendly paths to see elephants, sea otters, and primates in naturalistic exhibits. The Family Farm area allows toddlers to get up close with friendly goats.

Early Afternoon / 1-2h

Portland Japanese Garden

A serene, peaceful oasis with meandering streams, koi ponds, and lush greenery. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the giant, colorful koi fish swimming in the lower pond.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Powell's City of Books

An iconic, multi-story bookstore with an entire room dedicated to children's literature. There are cozy reading nooks and low shelves perfect for toddlers to pick out a souvenir storybook.

Mid-day / 1-2h

Jamison Square

Located in the Pearl District, this urban park features a safe, cascading tidal fountain that mimics a beach. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow, recirculated water on warm days.

Morning / 2h

Forest Park (Lower Macleay Trail)

Walk along a lush, tree-canopied creek to see the mysterious stone 'Witch's Castle'. The flat dirt path is perfect for toddler-led hiking and spotting mossy trees.

Late Morning / 2h

Portland Saturday Market

An open-air arts and crafts market right along the riverfront with live music and local food. Stroller-friendly aisles make it easy to browse while toddlers enjoy the sights and sounds.

Morning / 2-3h

Multnomah Falls

A breathtaking 620-foot waterfall just outside the city with an easily accessible, paved viewing platform at the base. Toddlers can enjoy the misty air without needing to hike the steep upper trail.

🍽️ Where to eat

Voodoo Doughnut

Grab the colorful 'Marshall Mathers' M&M donut or a classic pink-frosted ring in their famously whimsical, neon-lit shop.

Slappy Cakes

Indulge in a fun breakfast where you pour your own batter onto built-in table griddles and add fun toppings like chocolate chips.

Pine State Biscuits

Get 'The Reggie', a massive, flaky buttermilk biscuit sandwich stacked with fried chicken, bacon, cheese, and gravy.

Hopworks Urban Brewery (HUB)

Order organic craft beers for the parents and delicious wood-fired pizzas for the kids while they play in the dedicated toddler play area.

Salt & Straw

Treat the family to hand-crafted, uniquely Portland ice creams like Sea Salt with Caramel Ribbons or Honey Lavender.
