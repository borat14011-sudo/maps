Santa Teresa / Mal Pais

← back to travel board
Where the lush Costa Rican jungle meets the wild Pacific surf, Santa Teresa and Mal Pais offer an enchanting escape for active families. Here, days are measured by low-tide strolls, gentle wildlife encounters, and spectacular golden sunsets. It is a laid-back paradise where children can connect deeply with nature.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min taxi from Tambor domestic airport, or ~5-6 hours via private shuttle and scenic ferry from San José

What to do

Morning during low tide / 2h

Playa Hermosa Tide Pools

At low tide, these giant natural rock pools emerge, creating crystal-clear, calm swimming baths filled with tiny colorful fish. Toddlers will love wading safely in the shallow water while searching for hermit crabs.

Early Morning / 3h

Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve

Costa Rica's oldest nature reserve offers lush jungle trails where you can spot white-faced capuchin monkeys, coatis, and colorful butterflies. The Danish trail is flat enough for toddlers in a carrier to enjoy the canopy sounds.

Morning / 2-3h

Mal Pais Canopy Tour

Soar through the jungle canopy with professional guides on an exhilarating zipline that offers stunning ocean views. Toddlers can safely ride tandem with a certified guide or watch from the shaded garden platforms below.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Playa Carmen Sunset Gathering

Join the friendly local crowd on the wide sandy expanse of Playa Carmen to watch the sky turn brilliant shades of pink and orange. It is the perfect spot for toddlers to build sandcastles in the soft, dry sand away from the surf.

Mid-morning / 2h

Surf Lessons at Playa Hermosa

Playa Hermosa features gentle beach breaks ideal for beginners and older kids learning to surf. Toddlers can safely play in the shallow, whitewash foam with close parent supervision.

Morning / 3-4h

Montezuma Waterfalls

A scenic drive away, these iconic cascading waterfalls feature refreshing pools perfect for a cool dip. Stick to the lower falls trail, which is short and manageable for families with young children.

Saturday Afternoon / 1-2h

Santa Teresa Organic Market

Held every Saturday afternoon near the beach, this vibrant outdoor market features live music, fresh tropical fruits, and handmade crafts. It is a wonderful sensory experience for toddlers to taste sweet local mangoes.

Morning / 2h

Finca Cobano Organic Farm Tour

Take a guided family tour of a local organic farm to harvest tropical fruits and feed the farm animals. Toddlers will love interacting with the friendly goats and tasting fresh sugarcane juice.

Midday / 1h

Mal Pais Fish Market

Visit the rustic fishing village harbor of Mal Pais to watch the colorful artisanal boats return with their daily catch. Toddlers will be fascinated seeing the big, freshly caught fish and spotting pelicans waiting for scraps.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bakery

Get the freshly baked almond croissants and a fluffy stack of banana pancakes that toddlers will devour.

Banana Beach

Enjoy fresh fish tacos and cold coconuts while your toddlers play in the sand right beside your beach lounge chair.

Zula Restaurant

Order the creamy hummus plate with warm pita bread and the delicious, kid-approved chicken schnitzel.

El Facón Grill & Bar

Savor the wood-fired grilled skirt steak accompanied by sweet plantains that make an excellent side for toddlers.

Soda Pura Vida

Order a traditional casado with sweet fried plantains, black beans, and mild shredded chicken perfect for little palates.
