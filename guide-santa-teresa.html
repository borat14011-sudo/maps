Santa Teresa / Mal Pais

← back to travel board
Where the lush Costa Rican rainforest meets the roaring Pacific, Santa Teresa and Mal Pais offer a laid-back paradise of dusty roads, tide pools, and surf culture. Families can spend their days exploring hidden coves, spotting howler monkeys in the canopy, and watching unforgettable sunsets over the water. It is a rustic, barefoot haven where slow travel and nature-filled days rejuvenate everyone from toddlers to parents.
🚕 Getting there: ~50 min taxi/shuttle from Tambor domestic airport, or a ~5-hour drive/shuttle from San Jose including the scenic Gulf of Nicoya ferry

What to do

Morning (at low tide) / 1-2h

Playa Hermosa Tide Pools

Explore these massive, natural rock pools during low tide to see colorful small fish and crabs. It is a perfect, wave-free natural baby bathtub for toddlers to safely splash around.

Morning / 2h

Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve

Hike the short, flat Danes Trail in Costa Rica's oldest nature reserve to spot howler monkeys, coatis, and white-tailed deer. The trail is shaded by dense canopy, keeping toddlers cool and entertained by jungle sounds.

Morning / 2-3h

Mal Pais Canopy Tour

Soar through the jungle canopy with ocean views on a safe and professional zip line course. Toddlers can join the adventure by riding tandem with experienced guides or visiting the on-site butterfly garden.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Sunset and Sandcastles at Playa Carmen

Gather with locals and travelers alike at this wide beach to watch the sun sink into the Pacific. The soft, spacious sand is perfect for toddler sandcastle building while parents relax with a fresh coconut.

Morning / 3-4h including drive

Montezuma Waterfalls Lower Pool

Take a short, exciting riverbank hike from the nearby town of Montezuma to reach a beautiful 80-foot waterfall. The lower pool has shallow, calm rocky edges where toddlers can dip their feet under leafy shade.

Saturday Afternoon / 1-2h

Feria de Santa Teresa Saturday Market

Stroll through this lively outdoor market next to the beach featuring organic local produce, handmade toys, and live music. Toddlers will love sampling sweet, fresh mangoes and listening to the acoustic guitars.

Morning / 2h

Family Surf Lessons at Playa Hermosa

This wide, sandy-bottom beach features gentle waves ideal for surf beginners. Instructors are highly skilled at keeping older kids safe on boards, while toddlers can play safely in the shallow whitewash.

Full Day / 6-7h

Tortuga Island Boat and Snorkel Trip

Take a scenic boat ride from Montezuma to a picture-perfect white sand island. The calm, crystal-clear turquoise waters are incredibly safe for toddlers to swim and spot colorful tropical fish right off the shore.

Afternoon / 1h

Mal Pais Fisherman's Wharf

Visit the quiet local port where artisanal fishing boats pull in with their daily catch. Toddlers will be absolutely fascinated by the colorful wooden boats and the chance to spot pelicans waiting for fish scraps.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bakery

Order the freshly baked almond croissants and shakshuka for a hearty family breakfast in a shaded, stylish garden.

Banana Beach Restaurant

Grab the fish tacos and fresh fruit smoothies while the kids play on the beachfront swings and sand right next to your table.

Zula Restaurant

Try the loaded falafel plate with fresh hummus and warm pita bread in a relaxed, open-air, toddler-friendly space.

Earth Cafe

Order the colorful tropical acai bowls and healthy gluten-free pancakes served in a peaceful, child-friendly garden patio.

Katana Asian Cuisine

Get the kid-friendly chicken pad thai and fresh sushi rolls served in a beautifully lit, tropical garden setting.
