Santa Teresa offers a rugged, laid-back paradise where lush jungle meets world-class surf, perfect for a modern family seeking an authentic escape. The unpaved roads and slow pace naturally dictate a relaxing schedule, while upscale amenities cater to a discerning eye. For an active finance dad and his young family, the daily rhythm of beach walks, tide-pool wading, and premium open-air dining provides the ultimate digital detox. It is a place where you can trade concrete for sand without sacrificing comforts like artisanal espresso or high-end wellness.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min domestic flight from San José (SJO) to Tambor (TMU), then a ~50 min private 4x4 transfer to your resort.

What to do

Morning (at low tide) / 2h

Playa Hermosa Tide Pools

Located just north of the main strip, these natural, reef-protected saltwater pools emerge beautifully at low tide. It is a calm, wave-free haven where your toddler can safely splash and spot tiny colorful fish without fighting the heavy surf.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Cuevas (Secret Beach)

Tucked away near the fishing port of Mal Pais, this shaded white-sand cove is protected from strong swells and wind. It is an ideal spot for a peaceful family picnic under the trees, offering gentle waters for a toddler to dip her toes.

Afternoon / 30m

Green World Store

An air-conditioned organic market in the heart of town that is perfect for stocking up on high-quality baby supplies. Grab fresh local papaya purees, organic goat milk formula alternatives, and healthy snacks for beach days.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Playa Carmen Sunset Walk

The wide, flat expanse of hard-packed sand at low tide makes this beach highly accessible for a rugged-wheeled stroller. Enjoy a breathtaking Pacific sunset while walking alongside surfers, with plenty of room for your toddler to run safely.

Mid-day / 30m

Pescadería El Carmen (Mal Pais Fish Market)

A small, authentic local fish cooperative where you can watch the colorful panga boats land the daily catch of yellowfin tuna and snapper. It is a fascinating, quick sensory experience for a toddler, and you can buy ultra-fresh fish to grill at your villa.

Morning / 2h

Playa Manzanillo

A quiet, reef-protected beach further north that features remarkably calm waters compared to the main surf breaks. The shallow, lagoon-like shore is incredibly safe for toddlers and offers a highly relaxed, local vibe away from the crowds.

Morning / 2h

Cabo Blanco Nature Reserve (Sendero Sueño Trail)

The oldest protected area in Costa Rica, featuring the short, flat 'Sueño' trail which is easily navigable with a high-end baby carrier. Walk under a dense canopy of old-growth forest while spotting howler monkeys and coatis that will delight your daughter.

Morning / 1-2h

Montezuma Butterfly Gardens (Mariposario Butterfly Garden)

An easy 30-minute private drive to nearby Montezuma reveals a lush garden filled with hundreds of active, iridescent Blue Morpho butterflies. The flat, shaded gravel paths are stroller-friendly and provide an engaging, low-energy activity for little eyes.

Afternoon / 2h

Zeneidas Garden Beach Front

A beautifully shaded beachfront garden area with hammocks, low tables, and soft grass under coconut palms. It is an ideal spot to base yourselves for a relaxed afternoon where dad can grab a quick surf while mom and baby play in the shade.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bakery

Order the signature Shakshuka or fresh almond croissants in their spacious, air-conditioned indoor seating area or shaded garden. It opens early at 7:00 AM, matching a toddler's wake-up schedule, and has plenty of room for stroller parking.

Banana Beach Club

Request a table with your feet directly in the sand and order the grilled local mahi-mahi tacos. The open-air, casual atmosphere easily absorbs toddler noise, and the beach-front playground keeps little ones entertained while you wait for food.

Koji's

Indulge in the finest local catch sashimi and the signature 'Koji Roll' in an elegant, ambient outdoor jungle garden. The early evening seating (starting at 5:30 PM) is peaceful, visually engaging for a baby, and highly accommodating for families.

Zula Restaurant

Famous for its authentic, ultra-fresh Israeli falafel plates and hummus bowls served in a very casual, open-air setting. The service is incredibly fast and warm, which is a lifesaver when dining with an impatient toddler.

Al Chile Viola

Savor handmade fresh pasta, like their signature truffle tagliolini, in a romantic yet family-welcoming outdoor garden setting. The gentle acoustic background music and spacious table layouts make dining with an infant in a highchair stress-free.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Florblanca Resort

An ultra-luxury, beachfront resort offering expansive individual villas with private open-air living rooms and plunge pools. They provide high-end wooden cribs, professional nanny services, and a gorgeous main pool surrounded by dense, shaded jungle canopy.

Nantipa - A Tico Beach Experience

A luxury boutique hotel featuring spacious beachfront bungalows with private plunge pools, situated directly on a quiet stretch of sand. The property is flat and highly stroller-accessible, offering top-tier amenities, high-quality highchairs, and customized baby-friendly meals.

Pranamar Oceanfront Villas

Stunning Indonesian-style villas built around a free-form, saltwater swimming pool that features shallow entry steps perfect for toddlers. The lush, grassy oceanfront property provides ample room for baby playtime under the shade of mature palm trees.

👨‍👩‍👧 Skip the stroller for the main town strip, as the dusty, unpaved main road lacks sidewalks and is highly uneven; a rugged baby carrier like an Ergobaby is essential. Always book a private 4x4 SUV transfer rather than a standard sedan to handle the notoriously bumpy dirt roads between Mal Pais and Santa Teresa comfortably. Plan all outdoor beach activities before 10:00 AM or after 3:30 PM, as the equatorial sun heats up the dark sand rapidly, making it too hot for sensitive toddler skin.
