Orlando

Orlando sparkles with world-class theme parks, but its lush gardens and vibrant neighborhood markets offer a surprisingly relaxed side. Beyond the rollercoasters, families will find gentle nature encounters and sensory-friendly spots that make traveling with little ones a breeze.

What to do

Full Day

Magic Kingdom Park

The ultimate fairytale experience where toddlers can meet Mickey Mouse and ride the gentle Dumbo flying elephant. Classic parades and fireworks make it a core childhood memory.

Morning / 2h

Harry P. Leu Gardens

Stroll along paved, stroller-friendly pathways beneath towering camphor trees and vibrant rose gardens. Toddlers will love chasing butterflies and exploring the whimsical, wide-open green spaces.

Afternoon / 1-2h

East End Market

A vibrant neighborhood food hub in the Audubon Park Garden District with an outdoor courtyard perfect for kids to wiggle. Grab a giant, gooey cookie while toddlers enjoy the open-air garden space.

Morning / 1h

Winter Park Scenic Boat Tour

Take a relaxing, 1-hour pontoon boat cruise through the tranquil canals and lakes of historic Winter Park. It is gentle and quiet enough for a stroller-nap, yet engaging enough for toddlers to spot cranes and baby alligators.

Afternoon / 3h

Orlando Science Center

Head straight to KidsTown, an interactive, hands-on exhibit designed specifically for children under seven to splash in water tables and climb orange trees. It is the perfect air-conditioned escape from the Florida afternoon heat.

Evening / 2h

Icon Park

Ride the giant, air-conditioned Ferris wheel for panoramic views of the city, or let toddlers run wild on the central grassy lawn. It is a low-stress entertainment hub with plenty of quick-service dining options nearby.

Morning / 3h

Central Florida Zoo & Botanical Gardens

Located just north of the city, this manageable, shady zoo features a splash pad and a miniature train ride. It is a fantastic, crowd-free alternative to the massive theme parks for animal-loving toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Lake Eola Park

Located in downtown Orlando, this scenic park features a great playground and a paved loop perfect for stroller walks. Rent a swan-shaped paddleboat to get out on the water and feed the real resident swans.

Morning / 3h

Crayola Experience Orlando

A colorful indoor wonderland inside the Florida Mall where kids can melt crayons into custom shapes and star in their own coloring pages. Toddlers will love the Toddler Town indoor playground and sensory play areas.

Where to eat

  • Gideon's Bakehouse — Famous for their almost half-pound, chocolate-chip-loaded cookies that are perfect for sharing with the whole family.
  • Seito Sushi Baldwin Park — An upscale yet incredibly family-friendly spot situated right next to a scenic lake park where kids can stretch their legs before dinner.
  • Yellow Dog Eats — A quirky, multi-level BBQ joint with a dog-friendly garden patio that serves up delicious pulled pork sandwiches in a relaxed, kid-welcoming atmosphere.
  • Kelly's Homemade Ice Cream — A beloved local parlor serving unique, creamy flavors like Cookie Monster and Sweet Cream that will put a smile on any toddler's face.
