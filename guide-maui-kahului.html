Maui (Kahului)

← back to travel board
Maui offers a serene blend of golden beaches, lush rainforests, and world-class resorts, perfect for a family escape with your infant. The calm, shallow waters of Wailea Beach provide a safe splash zone for baby, while the stroller-ready boardwalk along Kaanapali Beach makes for effortless sunset strolls. Many luxury hotels offer cribs on request, blackout curtains, and private lanais for quiet feedings. Your 7–18-month-old will love the gentle ocean breezes and the sound of ukulele at a luau.
🚕 Getting there: Kahului Airport to Wailea resort strip is a 25–35 minute drive; book a private SUV transfer with car seat and stroller storage, such as SpeediShuttle or Maui Private Chauffeured Transportation.

What to do

Morning 9-11am

Wailea Beach

Calm, protected waters perfect for dipping baby's toes; soft sand for blanket play; nearby shade trees and restrooms with changing tables.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Kaanapali Beach Boardwalk

3-mile paved path along the ocean, stroller-friendly, with benches and shade; stop at Whalers Village for shops and restrooms.

2-3 hours, mid-morning

Maui Ocean Center

Indoor aquarium with impressive shark tunnel and touch pools; stroller-accessible, air-conditioned, and quiet enough for napping baby; avoid feeding times if too loud.

1 hour, early morning

Iao Valley State Monument

Short, paved walk to the iconic Iao Needle; shaded, easy for stroller, but watch for uneven edges; calm stream at base for cooling off.

Sunrise 5-8am (drive + viewing)

Haleakalā National Park (summit)

Sunrise drive and brief stroller-friendly overlook; bring warm layers and ear protection for baby at high altitude; skip if baby is fussy with early wake-up.

2 hours, morning or late afternoon

Kapalua Coastal Trail

Paved 1.7-mile trail along rugged coastline, stroller-accessible with stunning views; shaded sections; stop at Kapalua Bay for a calm swim.

2-3 hours, mid-day

Maui Tropical Plantation

Family-friendly farm with tram tour (stroller storage), playground, and duck pond; shaded paths and a café with highchairs.

1.5 hours, morning

Lahaina Historic Trail

Stroll the paved Front Street (rebuilt after 2023 fire) with old banyan tree, galleries, and baby-friendly shops; earlier hours quieter.

2-3 hours, mid-morning

Baby Beach (Napili Bay)

Sheltered cove with gentle waves, shallow entry, and shade; ideal for a first ocean experience; limited parking but uncrowded.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mama's Fish House

Iconic oceanfront restaurant; signature stuffed fish with macadamia nuts; highchairs available, outdoor terrace, early seating (5pm) recommended for quieter vibe; no min age.

Merriman's Kapalua

Farm-to-table on the cliffs; signature wok-charred ahi; highchairs, early dinner, and a lawn for baby to crawl; noise level moderate.

The Mill House at Maui Tropical Plantation

Casual but upscale dining in a lush garden; wood-fired pizza and fresh salads; highchairs, stroller parking, and relaxed atmosphere.

Pacific'O (Lahaina)

Tropical courtyard with fire pits; seafood and Pacific Rim dishes; highchairs, early seating, and quiet alcove tables for nursing.

Leoda's Kitchen and Pie Shop

Local favorite for savory pies and sweet treats; outdoor picnic tables (stroller-friendly) and fast service for fussy baby; no highchair but booster available.
