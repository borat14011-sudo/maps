Naxos

← back to travel board
Naxos welcomes families with its endless golden sand beaches, gentle shallow waters, and a labyrinthine old town primed for exploration. Children will love running through ancient marble ruins, while parents savor the island's legendary local cheeses and relaxed Aegean pace. It is the ultimate Cycladic playground where myth meets effortless family comfort.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi or rental car to Naxos Town or Agios Prokopios beach hotels

What to do

Sunset / 1h

Portara (Temple of Apollo)

Walk up the paved causeway to this colossal marble gateway for sunset views. Toddlers will love watching the ferries glide by, but keep them close along the windy, un-railed path.

Morning / 3-4h

Agios Georgios Beach

This extremely shallow, calm bay is essentially a giant wading pool perfect for toddlers to splash safely. Parents can relax on sunbeds with family-friendly tavernas just steps away.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Naxos Castle (Kastro) Exploration

Wander the car-free, winding alleys of the medieval Venetian castle which feels like a giant stone maze. Toddler strollers are tough on the stairs, so opt for a baby carrier here.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Plaka Beach Dunes

A stunning stretch of endless golden sand with calm waters and beautiful, soft sand dunes to explore. It is spacious enough that toddlers can run free without disturbing other sunbathers.

Morning / 2-3h

Apiranthos Mountain Village

A marble-paved mountain village that feels frozen in time, offering cool breezes and beautiful squares. Toddlers will love chasing harmless stray cats while parents enjoy a Greek coffee under the trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Eggares Olive Press Museum

Learn how olive oil was traditionally made in this interactive, kid-friendly restored press. Toddlers can safely taste mild olive bread and run around the shady courtyard.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Filoti Village Square and Playground

Relax under the massive plane tree in the heart of this lively village while kids play nearby. There is a great, fenced local playground close to the main square perfect for burning toddler energy.

Morning / 1h

Naxos Town Green Market

Walk down Papavasiliou Street to sample sweet local figs, Naxian potatoes, and sweet pastries. It is a sensory feast for toddlers who can snack on fresh local fruits as you stroll.

Morning / 2h

Alyko Cedar Forest

Walk through a unique, shady coastal cedar forest that leads to quiet, wind-sheltered sandy coves. Toddlers will enjoy the sensory experience of playing with pinecones and soft sand away from the crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

To Elliniko

Order the slow-cooked Naxian lamb in paper and the exceptionally creamy, kid-approved local graviera cheese Saganaki.

Yialos Tavern

Grab a beachside table right in the sand and order the fresh grilled octopus and crispy zucchini balls for the kids.

Axiotissa

Reserve well in advance for this inland gem to enjoy their legendary pork loin with sun-dried tomatoes and hand-cut Naxian fries.

Sarris Tavern

Head here in Naxos Town for outstanding fresh sea bass grilled over charcoal and a simple, toddler-friendly Greek salad.

Waffle House

Treat the kids to freshly baked waffle baskets topped with homemade ice cream in child-friendly flavors like biscuit and strawberry.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat to easily reach the mountain villages and remote beaches. Bring a sturdy baby carrier for navigating the cobblestone steps of the Kastro, as strollers are nearly impossible there.
