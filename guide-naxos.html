What to do

Early morning 8-10am

Portara (Temple of Apollo)

The iconic marble gate on the islet—stroller-accessible via a flat paved causeway, best at sunrise or late afternoon for shade and fewer crowds. Baby can enjoy the breeze and open space.

Morning 9:30-11:30am

Agios Prokopios Beach

Golden sand with gentle waves and shallow entry—perfect for baby to sit and splash. Umbrellas and loungers available; stroller rolls easily on the boardwalk. Plenty of shaded spots.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Naxos Old Town (Kastro)

Wander the maze of narrow alleys—stroller may be tricky but doable. Visit the Archaeological Museum (quiet, air-conditioned). Stop at a cafe for a frappe while baby naps in the carrier.

Mid-morning 10am-12pm

Agia Anna Beach

Secluded cove with crystal-clear, calm water—ideal for a baby's first swim. Shallow lagoon-like area near the shore. Beachfront tavernas offer highchairs and sun shades.

Late afternoon 4:30-6:30pm

Plaka Beach

Long, wide sandy beach with fewer crowds—great for a stroll with the stroller on the packed sand. Calm waters and a laid-back vibe. Many sections have natural shade from tamarisk trees.

Morning 9-10:30am

Temple of Demeter

Ancient marble temple in the countryside—quiet and open, with a gentle slope accessible by stroller. Baby can crawl on the grass nearby. Less than 20 minutes from the hotel strip.

Late morning 10am-1pm

Apeiranthos Village

Picturesque mountain village with marble-paved streets—stroller possible but better with a baby carrier. Visit the Folklore Museum and enjoy a taverna lunch with a view. Cooler temperatures than the coast.

Early afternoon 12-2pm

Chalki Village

Charming former capital with a central square and a small church. Stroller-friendly flat paths. Visit the Vallindras Distillery for a kitron tasting (adults only) while baby plays in the shaded courtyard.

Evening 6-7:30pm

Naxos Castle Waterfront

The scenic promenade along the harbor—flat, stroller-perfect, with views of the port and Portara. Many benches for feeding breaks. Evening breeze keeps it cool.

🍽️ Where to eat

To Sosti

Family-run taverna in Naxos Town known for grilled octopus and fresh seafood. Offers highchairs, outdoor terrace with shade, and a welcoming atmosphere for babies. Early seating at 7pm avoids noise.

Rotunda

Seafood restaurant on Agios Prokopios beach—signature lobster spaghetti. Large terrace with a view, highchairs available, and calm early evening vibe. Staff are used to families.

Meze2

Lively but family-friendly spot in Agia Anna—famous for meze platters and grilled fish. Outdoor seating with a small play area nearby for toddlers. Highchairs and a kids' menu.

Taverna to Perasma

Traditional Greek cuisine in Chalki village—slow-roasted lamb and moussaka. Shaded courtyard with a fountain, highchairs provided. Quiet lunchtime atmosphere perfect for baby naps.

O Kosmos

Cozy ouzeri in Naxos Town—great for a casual dinner with baby. Outdoor tables on a quiet pedestrian street. Staff bring a highchair without asking. Try the fava and keftedes.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Naxos Palace Hotel

$250-400/night

Five-star beachfront resort on Agios Prokopios—offers cribs on request, a large outdoor pool with shallow kids' section, family suites with separate living area, and blackout curtains. Direct beach access with stroller ramp.

Nissaki Beach Hotel

$220-380/night

Boutique luxury hotel on Agios Prokopios—elegant suites with kitchenette and pool view. Crib and baby bath provided. Two large pools (one quiet), a spa for parents, and a boardwalk to the beach.

Kavos Naxos Boutique Hotel

$200-350/night

Adults-friendly but welcomes families with infants—suites have separate sleeping areas and private balconies. Crib on request, infinity pool with shallow edge, and a dedicated concierge for baby needs (formula, diapers). Close to Plaka Beach.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pharmacies in Naxos Town (e.g., Farmakeio Naxos) stock infant formula, sunblock, and nappies—but bring enough for the first days. Strollers work well on the beach boardwalks (Agios Prokopios, Agia Anna) but the Old Town's marble cobblestones are best tackled with a baby carrier. The nearest pediatric clinic is Naxos Health Center (open 8am-8pm) in the town; for heat management, plan outdoor activities before 11am and after 4pm, and use a UV tent on the beach.
