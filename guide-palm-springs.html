What to do

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Moorten Botanical Cactarium

A historic, family-owned botanical garden featuring over 3,000 desert plants. The flat dirt paths are easy to navigate with a sturdy stroller, and the unique greenhouse offers a peaceful, shaded environment for Mary Claire to gaze at the colorful desert flora.

Morning 10am-1pm / 3h

Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

Ascend 2.5 miles aboard the world’s largest rotating tramcar to the pristine wilderness of Mt. San Jacinto State Park. The tram is fully stroller-accessible, and the cooler mountain air offers instant relief from the desert heat, though feeding baby during the ascent helps pop ears during the elevation change.

Early Morning 8-11am / 3h

The Living Desert Zoo and Gardens

An expansive, beautifully landscaped desert conservation park with paved, stroller-friendly walkways and plenty of shaded pavilions. Mary Claire will love watching the gentle giraffes and rhinos, while parents can take advantage of clean, air-conditioned family nursing lounges.

Afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Palm Springs Art Museum

An air-conditioned cultural sanctuary featuring world-class modern and contemporary art. The spacious, multi-level galleries are highly stroller-friendly with wide elevators, offering a quiet, sensory-calming environment perfect for baby's morning nap in her carrier.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Sunnylands Center & Gardens

A breathtaking historic estate in nearby Rancho Mirage featuring nine acres of desert art gardens inspired by impressionist paintings. The wide, smoothly paved paths are dreamlike for strollers, and the peaceful reflection pools offer a quiet spot for a relaxing family stroll.

Late Afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Ruth Hardy Park

A lush, manicured green park located in the beautiful Movie Colony neighborhood. The expansive grass lawns are perfect for setting up a premium linen picnic blanket for Mary Claire to enjoy safe crawling time under the shade of mature palm trees.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Tahquitz Canyon Visitor Center

While the trail itself is too rugged for strollers, the beautiful modern visitor center features educational cultural exhibits, peaceful indoor viewing areas, and a paved outdoor patio showcasing spectacular mountain views that you can easily explore with an infant.

Saturday Morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Palm Springs Certified Farmers' Market

A vibrant indoor or outdoor market (depending on season) offering fresh local dates, organic fruits, and artisan goods. It features wide, accessible aisles and a welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere where you can stroll casually with the stroller.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Cabot's Pueblo Museum

A unique four-story Hopi-inspired adobe structure built by hand. While the interior is tight and requires a baby carrier rather than a stroller, the scenic outdoor courtyard and grounds are peaceful, beautifully shaded, and visually stimulating for an infant.

🍽️ Where to eat

Cheeky's

The ultimate Palm Springs breakfast spot, famous for its rotating bacon flights and fresh-squeezed juices. The vibrant, shaded outdoor patio features highchairs, and the bustling morning energy naturally masks any happy baby squeals from Mary Claire.

Trio Restaurant

An upscale yet approachable mid-century modern dining landmark in the Uptown Design District. They offer spacious booths, premium highchairs, an early dinner seating, and a wonderfully accommodating staff who make dining with an infant seamless.

The Tropicale

A festive, neon-lit retro oasis featuring a massive, beautifully misted outdoor patio. The lively atmosphere is ideal for masking baby noises, and their delicious wood-fired pizzas and global small plates make for a stylish and relaxed family dinner.

Copley's on Palm Canyon

An award-winning restaurant housed on the former Cary Grant estate, boasting a romantic and spacious garden patio. The tables are spaced far enough apart to easily park a premium stroller, allowing Will and Kathryn to enjoy a fine dining experience with baby resting nearby.

Lulu California Bistro

A bright, multi-level dining institution right on the main strip with a huge, stroller-accessible sidewalk patio. Known for its incredibly friendly service, rapid food delivery, and high energy, it is one of the most infant-welcoming dining spots in the city.
