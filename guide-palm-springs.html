Palm Springs

← back to travel board
Nestled against the dramatic San Jacinto Mountains, Palm Springs is a vibrant desert oasis boasting year-round sunshine and mid-century charm. Families can splash in sparkling resort pools, explore rugged canyon trails, and ride a rotating tramway high above the valley floor. It is the ultimate laid-back playground where retro cool meets outdoor adventure for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min drive via rental car or rideshare to downtown hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

Ride the world's largest rotating tramcar up to pristine mountain trails and cooler temperatures. It's a thrill for kids, and toddlers will love looking out the giant windows, but bring a jacket as it gets chilly at the top.

Morning / 3h

The Living Desert Zoo and Gardens

Explore this desert conservation park featuring giraffes, rhinos, and a massive model train layout. Toddlers will love feeding the giraffes and walking the flat, stroller-friendly paths.

Morning / 1h

Moorten Botanical Garden

Wander through a historic cactarium filled with thousands of desert plants of all shapes and sizes. It is a quick, peaceful walk, but keep toddlers close to prevent curious hands from touching prickly cacti.

Afternoon / 2h

Palm Springs Air Museum

Get up close to historic aircraft inside massive, air-conditioned hangars where kids can climb into select cockpits. The wide-open spaces are perfect for strollers, and there is a fun kids' play area.

Early Morning / 2h

Tahquitz Canyon

Hike a scenic 2-mile loop trail leading to a beautiful 60-foot seasonal waterfall. While the trail has some rocky steps, energetic toddlers can do parts of it with help, or ride comfortably in a carrier.

Evening / 2h

Palm Springs VillageFest

Walk through this lively Thursday evening street market packed with local art, crafts, and delicious food vendors. It is very family-friendly, though crowds can get dense, so a stroller is recommended for toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Ruth Hardy Park

Let the kids burn off energy at this spacious park featuring two shaded playgrounds, tennis courts, and sprawling grassy fields. It is the perfect spot for a relaxed family picnic and has plenty of safe space for toddlers.

Morning / 1-2h

Cabazon Dinosaurs

Visit the iconic giant roadside dinosaurs made famous in movies, located just a short drive from Palm Springs. Toddlers will be awestruck by the massive T-Rex and Brontosaurus, and there is a fun dino-themed dig area.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Shields Date Garden

Walk through the historic date groves and enjoy a famous, naturally sweet date shake. Toddlers will enjoy the outdoor path featuring biblical statues and the sweet, icy treat on a warm day.

🍽️ Where to eat

Order the colossal pastrami sandwich and save room for a slice of their famous giant chocolate cake.

El Jefe

Enjoy the vibrant, colorful atmosphere while feasting on fresh fish tacos and chips with guacamole.

Cheeky's

Try the famous bacon flight and their fluffy buttermilk pancakes in a trendy, kid-friendly outdoor seating area.

Great Shakes

Treat the family to a gourmet date shake topped with a miniature donut on the straw.

King's Highway

Grab a booth in this retro-cool converted diner and order the classic buttermilk waffles or a smash burger.

👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense desert heat by scheduling outdoor activities for early morning and planning pool or indoor museum time for the afternoon. Always carry extra water, apply high-SPF sunscreen frequently, and keep a close eye on little ones around the many unfenced resort pools.
