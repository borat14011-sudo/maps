Maui

← back to travel board
Maui welcomes families with its warm, turquoise waters, dramatic volcanic valleys, and the gentle rustle of swaying palm trees. From building sandcastles on golden shores to spotting humpback whales from the coast, this island paradise offers a slow-paced, magical escape. It is a playground where natural wonders meet rich cultural traditions, ensuring smiles for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-45 min rental car drive from Kahului Airport (OGG) to West Maui (Kaanapali) or South Maui (Wailea) resorts.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Maui Ocean Center

Discover Hawaii's unique marine life through interactive tide pools and a massive open-ocean walk-through tunnel. It is fully stroller-accessible and offers a shaded, air-conditioned respite from the midday sun for toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Baby Beach in Lahaina

This protected lagoon features calm, shallow waters with virtually no waves, making it the ultimate safe haven for toddlers to splash. Parents can relax on the soft sand while little ones wade safely near the shore.

Morning / 1-2h

Garden of Eden Arboretum

Experience a simplified, toddler-safe version of the Road to Hana with paved, stroller-friendly paths winding through lush tropical gardens and duck ponds. It offers gorgeous waterfall views without the strenuous hiking.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Maui Tropical Plantation Tram Tour

Hop aboard the open-air tram for a scenic loop around duck ponds, coconut husking demonstrations, and fields of sugarcane. Toddlers will love feeding the ducks and the gentle, breezy ride.

Morning / 1.5h

Surfing Goat Dairy

Meet, pet, and feed friendly baby goats at this charming Upcountry dairy farm. Toddlers will be delighted by the playful animals while parents sample award-winning artisanal goat cheeses.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Kaanapali Beach Beach Walk

Walk along the paved, stroller-friendly beach path that connects world-class resorts, perfect for watching the sunset. Toddlers can play in the soft, golden sand away from the stronger shore breaks.

Morning / 2-3h

Napili Bay Beach

This crescent-shaped golden beach is sheltered from strong currents, creating a gentle swimming environment. Toddlers can easily spot sea turtles swimming close to the shoreline in the crystal-clear water.

Morning / 1h

Kealia Pond Boardwalk

Stroll along the flat, elevated wooden boardwalk to spot endangered Hawaiian wetland birds nesting in the peaceful coastal salt marsh. The entire path is completely flat, stroller-friendly, and offers sweeping ocean views.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Upcountry Farmers Market

Sample fresh tropical fruits like lilikoi and mango while enjoying live local music at this vibrant outdoor market. It is a fantastic sensory experience for toddlers, with wide aisles and lots of colorful stalls to explore.

🍽️ Where to eat

Leoda's Kitchen and Pie Shop

Order a savory chicken pot pie followed by a miniature banana cream pie that is the perfect size for little hands to share.

Monkeypod Kitchen by Merriman

Feast on wood-fired pizzas and fresh fish tacos while enjoying live music in a highly energetic, family-friendly environment.

Flatbread Company

Dig into organic, wood-fired flatbreads made with local Hawaiian ingredients inside an open-concept restaurant that kids find fascinating.

Kihei Caffe

Fuel your day early with massive, fluffy macadamia nut pancakes topped with coconut syrup at this casual, open-air spot.

Ululani's Hawaiian Shave Ice

Try a finely shaved ice topped with passion fruit, mango, and coconut syrups over a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
