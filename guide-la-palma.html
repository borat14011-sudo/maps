La Palma

← back to travel board
Known affectionately as 'La Isla Bonita,' La Palma captivates families with its lush prehistoric forests, dramatic volcanic craters, and incredibly clear night skies. Children can safely explore black-sand beaches, splash in natural tide pools, and wander through colorful colonial towns. It is a peaceful, slow-paced paradise perfect for relaxed outdoor bonding and exploration.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or rental car drive from La Palma Airport (SPC) to the capital Santa Cruz de La Palma, or ~45 min to the west coast family resorts of Tazacorte.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Roque de los Muchachos Viewpoint

Stand above the clouds at the island's highest point to marvel at the giant space telescopes and the deep caldera. Toddlers will love the sensory feeling of being 'in the clouds,' but make sure to pack warm layers.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Charco Azul Natural Pools

Splash safely in these beautiful, volcanic rock tide pools protected from the rough ocean waves. There is a dedicated, shallow children's pool perfect for toddlers to wade in safely.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Los Tilos Laurel Forest

Walk through a lush, Jurassic-like jungle of giant ferns and ancient trees with an easy, flat path leading to a spectacular hidden waterfall. The main trail to the waterfall is short and highly stroller-accessible.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Barco de la Virgen Ship Museum

Explore a life-sized wooden replica of Christopher Columbus’s flagship, the Santa María, parked right in a city plaza. Toddlers will love playing pirate on deck while parents enjoy the surrounding historic architecture.

Late Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Playa de Tazacorte

Bask on a sunny, volcanic black-sand beach with exceptionally calm, shallow waters sheltered by a giant sea wall. Toddlers can safely build dark sandcastles while parents relax on the colorful seafront promenade.

Saturday Morning / 1-2 hours

Mercadillo de El Paso

Sample sweet local bananas, fresh almond cheese, and traditional pastries at this vibrant covered farmers market. It is a low-stress, delicious environment for toddlers to try new tropical fruits.

Morning / 1-2 hours

San Antonio Volcano Crater Walk

Walk right along the rim of a real volcanic crater and feel the geothermal heat inside the visitor center. The walking path is wide and securely fenced, making it safe and easy for little legs.

Morning / 2 hours

Maroparque Rescue Zoo

Wander through a unique vertical rescue park dedicated to exotic animals, offering close encounters with birds, monkeys, and reptiles. The winding paths are highly engaging for toddlers, though a baby carrier is recommended over a stroller.

Late Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Salinas de Fuencaliente

Witness the striking contrast of bright pink salt-evaporation pans against black volcanic soil and the deep blue ocean. Toddlers will love spotting the nesting migratory birds and tasting the salty ocean breeze.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante El Canal

Order the grilled local goat cheese drizzled with sweet palm syrup and traditional green mojo sauce.

Chipi Chipi

Request a private stone dining booth in their lush, garden courtyard and order the slow-grilled pork skewers with wrinkly potatoes.

Restaurante El Jardín de la Sal

Dine right next to the salt flats on fresh local fish cooked in sea salt, paired with a remarkably welcoming family atmosphere.

Kiosko Montecarlo

Grab an outdoor table by the beach for family-friendly tapas like crispy fried calamari and fresh banana croquettes.

Restaurante La Pradera

Feast on hearty Canary stews and grilled chicken in a rustic setting with plenty of outdoor lawn space for restless toddlers to wiggle.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a compact car with a high-quality car seat to navigate the island's steep, winding mountain roads comfortably. Keep a baby carrier handy as many volcanic trails and historic cobblestone streets are too bumpy for standard strollers.
