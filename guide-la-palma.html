La Palma, known affectionately as La Isla Bonita, welcomes discerning travelers to a pristine volcanic sanctuary of lush biosphere reserves and world-class stargazing. For Houston finance professionals Will and Kathryn, this island offers an exclusive, crowd-free refuge where wild Atlantic beauty meets refined Spanish charm. Mary Claire will stay perfectly comfortable rolling along the stroller-ready seaside promenade of Los Cancajos, which offers flat, smooth access to sheltered, baby-friendly tidal pools. Enjoy quiet sunset drives through dramatic pine forests and relaxing afternoons in secluded, lush estate gardens overlooking the ocean.
🚕 Getting there: The journey from Santa Cruz de La Palma Airport (SPC) to the luxury enclaves of Breña Baja or Fuencaliente takes between 10 to 40 minutes. The premier family option is a pre-booked private transfer in a Mercedes V-Class executive van, complete with a certified rear-facing Maxi-Cosi infant car seat and ample space for your premium stroller system.

What to do

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Plaza de España

Stroll through this exquisite Renaissance square in Santa Cruz de La Palma, surrounded by elegant 16th-century architecture. The plaza is completely flat and highly stroller-accessible, with plenty of shade from palm trees and quiet nearby cafes perfect for an afternoon bottle feed.

Late morning 11am-1pm / 2h

Charco Azul Natural Pools

These beautiful natural volcanic tide pools in San Andrés y Sauces feature a dedicated, calm children's pool sheltered from the open ocean waves. Paved walkways make it easy to wheel your stroller right up to the sunbathing decks, allowing Will and Kathryn to take alternating dips.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Los Tilos Forest Visitor Center

Explore a prehistoric laurel forest that feels like a fairy tale. The visitor center offers easy parking, and the short, paved path leading to the immediate viewing decks is fully stroller-accessible and deeply shaded by the canopy, keeping baby Mary Claire cool and comfortable.

Late afternoon 4:30-6pm / 1.5h

Salinas de Fuencaliente

A stunning landscape where brilliant white salt flats contrast with black volcanic soil and the deep blue ocean. The site features a flat, well-maintained dirt path suitable for sturdy strollers, offering a quiet, sensory-rich outdoor walk for the family.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Playa de Bajamar

This tranquil black-sand beach located near Santa Cruz is highly favored by local families for its protective breakwaters and calm, wave-free waters. A wide, smooth seaside promenade allows for effortless strolling right alongside the shoreline.

Morning 9:30-11am / 1.5h

Volcán San Antonio Visitor Center

Walk along the rim of a dramatic volcanic crater via a wide, fenced, and stroller-friendly gravel path. The modern visitor center is fully air-conditioned, providing a quiet indoor space to feed Mary Claire while learning about the island's unique geology.

Morning 10-10:45am / 45m

Mirador de la Concepción

Perched on the edge of a volcanic caldera, this viewpoint offers breathtaking, panoramic vistas of Santa Cruz and the ocean. The viewing platform is paved, highly secure, and easily accessible directly from the adjacent parking lot with no strenuous walking required.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Maroparque Rescue Zoo

This boutique rescue zoo is built into a steep hillside but features ramp-accessible paths that can be navigated with a stroller. Shaded by dense vegetation, it offers a peaceful, quiet morning walk where Mary Claire can gaze at colorful exotic birds and gentle raptors.

Midday 11:30am-1:30pm / 2h

Roque de los Muchachos Observatory Visitor Center

While the high-altitude peaks can be windy, the state-of-the-art Visitor Center is fully climate-controlled, quiet, and stroller-accessible. It offers majestic cloud-level views and educational astronomical displays in a highly comfortable, luxury indoor setting.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante El Jardín de la Sal

A Michelin-recommended destination situated directly within the Fuencaliente salt pans. Book an early outdoor terrace table at sunset to enjoy fresh local fish in salt crust, where the gentle ocean breeze keeps the atmosphere relaxed and baby-friendly.

El Duende del Fuego

Located in Los Llanos de Aridane, this gourmet restaurant specializes in allergen-conscious, organic Canarian gastronomy. The spacious, quiet indoor dining room easily accommodates strollers, and the chef is happy to purée fresh organic vegetables for baby Mary Claire.

Chipi Chipi

A legendary, historic restaurant in Santa Cruz de La Palma famous for its grilled meats and traditional Canarian mojo. Request one of the private, leafy garden stone booths, which provide a wonderful, self-contained, and low-noise environment perfect for dining with an infant.

Restaurante Carmen

An upscale, creative dining spot in El Paso featuring beautiful interior design and a lovely, quiet outdoor courtyard. The staff are exceptionally welcoming to families, readily providing highchairs and accommodating early dinner seatings.

Restaurante La Casa del Volcán

A beautifully restored traditional tavern in Fuencaliente offering refined local dishes and estate wines. The spacious terrace has plenty of room to park a sleeping stroller, offering Will and Kathryn a peaceful, slow-paced dining experience.
