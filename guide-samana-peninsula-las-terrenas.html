Samaná Peninsula (Las Terrenas)

← back to travel board
Samaná Peninsula is a lush, eco-diverse paradise where dramatic green mountains plunge directly into calm, turquoise waters. Las Terrenas pairs this untamed natural beauty with a charming French-Italian culinary flair, making it an incredibly welcoming and easy-to-navigate tropical escape for families. From gentle, reef-protected shallows to hidden jungle waterfalls, it is the ultimate playground for little explorers.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi from El Catey Airport (AZS) or ~2.5 hours via pre-arranged private shuttle from Santo Domingo Airport (SDQ) to Las Terrenas beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Las Ballenas

A calm, shallow beach perfect for safe wading with tiny waves. Toddlers will love building sandcastles under the shade of low-hanging palms.

Morning / 3-4h

El Limón Waterfall (Cascada El Limón)

Journey through the lush jungle to a spectacular 130-foot waterfall. Toddlers can double-up with a parent on a guided, gentle horse ride to make the trek easy.

Morning / 3-4h

Whale Watching in Samaná Bay

Board a comfortable boat to see massive humpback whales breach and play in the warm sanctuary waters. Choose a larger catamaran with shaded decks to keep toddlers comfortable and safe.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Bonita Boardwalk

A picture-perfect, boardwalk-lined beach with crystal-clear waters and a very relaxed vibe. The flat, paved pathway is excellent for stroller walks while watching the waves.

Morning / 4-5h

Los Haitises National Park

Explore mystical mangrove forests, ancient pirate caves, and towering rock formations rising from the sea. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the nesting pelicans and cool cave petroglyphs.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Cosón Freshwater Streams

A wild, expansive beach lined with thousands of coconut palms and shallow river outlets. Toddlers will spend hours splashing in the warm, calm freshwater pools where the river meets the sea.

Morning / 1h

Boulangerie Française

Grab freshly baked croissants and sweet pastries at this iconic local spot reflecting the town's European influence. It is a quick, delicious morning stop with toddler-approved finger foods.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Pueblo de los Pescadores

Stroll along the colorful beachfront wooden shacks once used by local fishermen, now housing shops and eateries. It's a pedestrian-friendly zone where toddlers can wander safely away from traffic.

Morning / 1h

Las Terrenas Local Fruit Market

Immerse your senses in a vibrant local market filled with sweet, exotic tropical fruits. Toddlers will love sampling fresh mango slices and drinking coconut water straight from a straw.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita (at Playa Cosón)

Order the fresh, crispy whole fried snapper served with plantain tostones right on the sand.

Zu Ceviche Cafecito

Try the fresh passion fruit ceviche or a mild coconut chicken bowl that toddlers will happily share.

El Lugar

Get the perfectly cooked beef tenderloin or a side of creamy homemade pasta that is a guaranteed hit for kids.

Pizzeria Al Lado

Order the authentic, wood-fired Margherita pizza with a perfectly crispy crust that the whole family can share.

El Mosquito Art Bar

Sit beachside and enjoy their signature fresh passion fruit mojitos for parents and delicious fruit smoothies for the kids.
