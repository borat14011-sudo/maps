Samaná Peninsula (Las Terrenas)

← back to travel board
Samaná Peninsula is the Dominican Republic's lush, eco-friendly secret, where wild jungle meets calm, turquoise waters. Las Terrenas offers a unique blend of vibrant French-Caribbean culture, making it incredibly welcoming for families. Here, days are measured in tide changes, fresh coconuts, and easy, barefoot adventures.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-minute taxi from El Catey Airport (AZS) or a 2.5-hour scenic private shuttle from Santo Domingo Airport (SDQ) directly to Las Terrenas beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Las Ballenas

This wide, tranquil beach features incredibly calm, shallow waters protected by reefs, making it a natural wading pool. Toddlers can safely splash for hours while parents relax under the shade of leaning palms.

Morning / 3-4h

Cascada El Limón

Journey through lush tropical forests on horseback to reach this breathtaking 130-foot jungle waterfall. Toddlers can ride safely in a carrier or sit upfront with an experienced local guide on a gentle horse.

Morning / 4-5h

Los Haitises National Park Boat Tour

Speed across the bay to explore ancient caves filled with Taino petroglyphs and winding mangrove forests. The smooth, covered boat ride keeps toddlers cool, shaded, and mesmerized by the passing scenery.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa Bonita Boardwalk Stroll

This picture-perfect crescent beach features a paved, car-free walking path lined with lush gardens and surf shacks. It is ideal for pushing a stroller while stopping for fresh coconut water and gentle dips in the shore.

Morning / 3-4h

Whale Watching in Samaná Bay

Witness majestic humpback whales breaching and nursing their calves in the warm sanctuary waters from January to March. Opt for a larger, stable catamaran for a smoother ride that is comfortable and safe for younger children.

Midday / 3h

Playa Cosón River Splash

Where the calm, freshwater river meets the sea, you'll find a perfectly still, warm pool ideal for toddlers to play. The surrounding soft sand is perfect for building castles under the shade of towering coconut palms.

Morning / 1-2h

Taino Park Samaná

Walk through this outdoor, interactive museum that brings the history of the indigenous Taino people to life with life-sized figures. The flat, easy paths are highly stroller-friendly, and the sound effects keep little ones engaged.

Morning / 1h

French Bakery Crawl at Boulangerie Française

Experience Las Terrenas' unique European heritage by starting your day with warm, flaky croissants and fresh fruit juices. The casual outdoor seating allows toddlers to eat crumbs and watch the colorful local life go by.

Morning / 4-5h

Playa Frontón Adventure

Take a scenic boat ride to this remote beach framed by a dramatic 150-foot vertical cliff face and vibrant coral reefs. The shallow shoreline is teeming with colorful fish, offering an easy, first-time snorkeling experience for young kids.

🍽️ Where to eat

Luis Restaurant

Feast on freshly caught grilled red snapper and crispy tostones right on the sand while your kids play at the water's edge.

La Dolce Vita

Indulge in authentic, homemade Italian pasta and wood-fired pizzas served on a beautiful beachfront terrace perfect for families.

Mojitos

Grab casual, giant savory crepes, fresh burgers, and fruit smoothies at this beachfront shack with feet-in-the-sand seating.

El Lugar

Enjoy perfectly cooked steaks, French-infused seafood dishes, and kid-friendly sides in a cozy, garden-like tropical courtyard.

Zucca

Order delicious, thin-crust artisanal pizzas and fresh salads in a welcoming, lively atmosphere right near the beach.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of high-SPF, reef-safe sunscreen and quality mosquito repellent, as the lush tropical jungle environment means bugs can be active at dusk. Opt for private taxi transfers with pre-arranged car seats, as local 'motoconchos' (motorcycle taxis) are not safe for traveling with toddlers.
