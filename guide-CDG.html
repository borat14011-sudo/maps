Paris

Paris transforms into an enchanting playground when explored at a slower, child-led pace. From carousel rides under the Eiffel Tower to crumbly pastries in sun-drenched parks, this guide unlocks the city's gentler side. Embrace the relaxed rhythm of Parisian life with these handpicked, family-tested adventures.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Eiffel Tower & Champ de Mars Carousel

Marvel at the iconic tower from below, then let toddlers ride the vintage carousel right at its base. It's a low-stress way to experience Paris's grandest icon without waiting in massive elevator lines.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Jardin du Luxembourg Toy Sailboats

This park is a toddler paradise where you can rent vintage wooden toy sailboats to push with a stick across the grand octagonal pond. There is also a fantastic, fenced-in playground (Ludo Jardin) with equipment tailored for little climbers.

Morning / 2h

Grande Galerie de l'Évolution

An awe-inspiring parade of life-sized taxidermy animals under a magnificent glass dome that captivates toddlers instantly. The wide, stroller-accessible walkways and soft lighting make it a peaceful indoor escape.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Jardin d'Acclimatation

Paris's oldest amusement park features gentle vintage rides, a splash pad, farm animals, and puppet shows perfect for toddlers. It offers a shady, secure environment designed specifically for young children to run free.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Parc des Buttes-Chaumont

A dramatic park featuring a suspension bridge, waterfalls, and a lake, offering plenty of grassy hills for running wild. Toddlers will adore the classic Guignol puppet theater located right inside the park.

Morning / 1-2h

Marché d'Aligre

A vibrant, bustling daily market filled with colorful fruit stalls, fresh cheeses, and friendly vendors who often toss a free berry to toddlers. It is a fantastic sensory experience that is far less crowded than other tourist markets if you go early.

Morning / 1.5h

Atelier des Lumières

Walk through giant, animated art projections set to music in a spacious, darkened hall where toddlers are free to toddle, dance, and touch the illuminated walls. It's an immersive art experience with no quiet rules, making it completely stress-free for parents.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Seine River Cruise with Vedettes du Pont Neuf

Drift down the river on a smaller, open-air boat that offers close-up views of the city's monuments without tiring little legs. Toddlers are easily entertained by waving at the people on the bridges above.

Morning / 2h

La Ménagerie du Jardin des Plantes

One of the oldest zoos in the world, home to red pandas, orangutans, and giant tortoises in a historic, compact layout. Its flat paths are incredibly easy to navigate with a stroller, making for a relaxed wildlife encounter.

Where to eat

  • Breizh Café — Incredible sweet crêpes and savory buckwheat galettes served in a casual setting where a warm butter-and-sugar crêpe is guaranteed to please any toddler.
  • Chez Alain Miam Miam — Located in the Marché des Enfants Rouges, this stall serves massive, customizable grilled sandwiches and savory galettes that you can take to nearby park benches.
  • La Grande Épicerie de Paris — This high-end food hall is perfect for assembling a custom toddler-friendly picnic of fresh baguettes, mild cheeses, and fresh berries.
  • Angelina Paris — Treat the family to their legendary, ultra-thick African Hot Chocolate and delicate pastries in a grand, bustling tearoom that welcomes kids with sweet teeth.
👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a lightweight, compact umbrella stroller as historic metro stations lack elevators and narrow cafe aisles are tight. Always carry a small picnic blanket, as local parks have dedicated lawns perfect for spontaneous toddler nap times and snack breaks.
