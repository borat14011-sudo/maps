Amsterdam

← back to travel board
Amsterdam is a storybook city of winding canals, secret courtyards, and world-class museums that warmly welcome little explorers. From pedaling through lush parks in a cargo bike to discovering sweet pancake houses, this walkable wonderland turns history into an interactive playground. Here, families can embrace a relaxed Dutch pace where every bridge leads to a new, child-friendly adventure.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Vondelpark

This sprawling green oasis features multiple wooden playgrounds, paddling pools, and paved paths perfect for a family stroll. Toddlers will love splashing in the wading pool near the Groot Melkhuis cafe while parents sip coffee.

Afternoon / 3h

NEMO Science Museum

A giant green ship-shaped museum packed with hands-on science experiments designed to be touched and explored. The rooftop terrace offers stunning city views and a fun water play area perfect for energetic toddlers.

Morning / 3-4h

ARTIS Amsterdam Royal Zoo

One of Europe's oldest zoos, combining historic architecture with lush gardens, shady paths, and a fantastic planetarium. Toddlers can easily view the animals and enjoy the dedicated nature playground designed for tiny climbers.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Albert Cuyp Market

Amsterdam’s most famous outdoor market is a sensory delight of colorful stalls, fresh flowers, and local treats. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the sights and smells, especially watching giant stroopwafels being pressed fresh.

Afternoon / 1h

Classic Canal Cruise

See the historic canal houses from the water on a relaxed, glass-topped boat tour that reveals the city's magic without tired legs. Opt for an open-air boat on nice days so toddlers have a better view and fresh air.

Morning / 3h

Amstelpark

A massive, peaceful park featuring a miniature train ride, a petting zoo, a maze, and the largest playground in Amsterdam. It is exceptionally toddler-friendly with dedicated soft-play structures and plenty of space to run free.

Afternoon / 1h

Rijksmuseum Gardens

While the indoor museum can be overwhelming, the surrounding free-to-enter gardens feature beautiful sculptures, water fountains, and manicured lawns. Toddlers can run around the hedge mazes and splash safely in the temporary water installations.

Morning / 3h

Ridammerhoeve Goat Farm

Located in the lush Amsterdamse Bos forest, this organic farm lets kids bottle-feed baby goats, feed chickens, and climb on hay bales. Toddlers will love the sandy playground and tasting the house-made goat's milk ice cream.

Morning / 2h

National Maritime Museum

Step aboard a life-sized replica of an 18th-century merchant ship and explore interactive exhibits about the sea. Toddlers will adore the 'Tales of the Whale' sensory exhibit, which is filled with soft textures and gentle sounds.

Where to eat

  • The Pancake Bakery — Housed in a 17th-century warehouse, this beloved spot serves massive, kid-friendly Dutch pancakes topped with everything from fresh fruit to savory cheese.
  • Foodhallen — An indoor food market in a converted tram depot where parents can enjoy craft beers and global street food while kids munch on artisanal fries and wood-fired pizza.
  • Winkel 43 — Famous for serving the best Dutch apple pie in the city, complete with a mountain of whipped cream that kids and adults will happily devour together.
  • Kinderkookkafé — An incredibly unique cafe near Vondelpark where children can bake their own cookies, pizzas, and cakes while parents relax with a cup of tea.
