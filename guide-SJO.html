Volcanic wonders and lush gardens for little explorers

San José

← back to travel board
Nestled in a lush volcanic valley, Costa Rica’s capital blends rich cultural treasures with vibrant, tropical green spaces. Families can wander through butterfly-filled gardens, explore interactive castle museums, and savor sweet tropical fruits. It is the perfect, easy-paced gateway to Central American adventure with little ones in tow.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Museo de los Niños

Housed in a castle-like former penitentiary, this interactive museum features hands-on science and cultural exhibits that spark curiosity. Toddlers will love the dedicated soft-play areas and giant model airplanes.

Morning / 1-2h

Spirogyra Butterfly Garden

This peaceful oasis right in the city offers a lush pathway where hundreds of colorful butterflies flutter freely. It's a gentle, stroller-accessible walk perfect for toddlers to spot caterpillars and hummingbirds.

Midday / 1-2h

Mercado Central

Dive into a sensory maze of colorful fruit stalls, local crafts, and bustling energy. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vibrant sights and the sweet taste of fresh mango slices.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque La Sabana

Known as the lungs of San José, this massive green space features a lake, playgrounds, and plenty of room to run. It's an ideal spot for a family picnic and letting toddlers feed the ducks by the pond.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo del Oro Precolombino

Located beneath the Plaza de la Cultura, this subterranean museum showcases dazzling gold artifacts that look like ancient treasures. The cool, quiet underground spaces offer a soothing sensory break for toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Teatro Nacional de Costa Rica

This architectural masterpiece boasts lavish marble lobbies and beautiful ceiling frescoes that feel like a fairy tale. Kids will be enchanted by the grand scale, and the onsite café serves delicious kid-friendly treats.

Morning / 3-4h

Rescate de Wildlife

Located just outside the city, this lush sanctuary rehabilitates rescued sloths, macaws, and monkeys in a natural forest setting. The wide, paved paths are incredibly stroller-friendly, allowing toddlers to get up close to exotic animals.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Parque Diversiones

This classic amusement park features a mix of thrilling rides and a colorful replica of historic Costa Rica. There are plenty of gentle, toddler-specific spinning cups and train rides.

Morning / 2h

Museo Nacional de Costa Rica

Housed in a historic yellow fortress, this museum features a stunning glass-enclosed butterfly atrium right at the entrance. Toddlers will love watching the giant blue morpho butterflies hatch and flutter around them.

Where to eat

  • Spoon — A beloved local chain perfect for families, serving delicious casual Costa Rican meals and an array of kid-friendly pastries and milkshakes.
  • Soda Tapia — An iconic, retro-style diner where you can introduce kids to hearty gallo pinto and legendary, massive fruit salads topped with ice cream.
  • Restaurante Nuestra Tierra — This vibrant, rustic restaurant serves authentic casados with rice, beans, plantains, and mild proteins in a warm, welcoming atmosphere.
  • Pops — The ultimate Costa Rican ice cream parlor chain, offering tropical flavors like passionfruit and coconut that toddlers will happily gobble up.
