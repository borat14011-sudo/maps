Palermo

← back to travel board
Palermo is a sensory playground where chaotic outdoor markets, glittering golden palaces, and sweet gelato-filled brioche create an unforgettable Sicilian adventure. Parents will love the rich Norman history and walkable piazzas, while kids can burn off energy in lush botanical gardens or at nearby sandy beaches.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min taxi or private transfer to central Palermo hotels, or a 50-minute Prestia e Comandè bus ride.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Palermo Cathedral

Climb to the roof for sweeping city views, while toddlers will love wandering the wide, traffic-free pedestrian piazza out front.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercato del Capo

A bustling sensory explosion of colorful fruit stalls, fresh seafood, and lively vendors, where toddlers can snack on sweet fresh strawberries.

Afternoon / 2h

Orto Botanico di Palermo

A lush, peaceful botanical oasis filled with giant ficus trees with hanging roots, perfect for kids to safely explore and find quiet shade.

Morning / 3-4h

Mondello Beach

A beautiful white-sand beach with calm, shallow turquoise water that is ideal for toddlers to splash in safely.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Antonio Pasqualino International Puppet Museum

Home to thousands of historic, colorful Sicilian puppets that will fascinate kids, with regular live shows that bring local legends to life.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Villa Giulia

Palermo's oldest public park features beautiful fountains, paved paths for strollers, and plenty of space for toddlers to run around away from traffic.

Morning / 1.5h

Palazzo dei Normanni & Cappella Palatina

Marvel at the dazzling, golden Byzantine mosaics inside the chapel; keep toddlers engaged by spotting the animals hidden in the glittering artwork.

Evening / 30m

Piazza Pretoria

A magnificent, circular fountain adorned with statues; toddlers will love watching the water tiers, but keep them close to prevent climbing.

Morning / 1.5h

Castello della Zisa

An ancient Norman castle with cool stone rooms and a beautiful front garden featuring long water basins that kids will love to look at.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ke Palle

Try the classic 'Arancina Bomba' or child-friendly melted cheese and ham arancini at this popular street food shop.

Pasticceria Cappello

Indulge in their famous 'Setteveli' cake, a rich seven-layer chocolate masterpiece, or grab sweet fresh cannoli.

Antica Focacceria San Francesco

Sit outside in the historic piazza and order the panelle (chickpea fritters) and crocche, which make perfect finger foods for toddlers.

Brioscià

Choose from dozens of gelato flavors stuffed inside a warm, fluffy sweet brioche bun topped with liquid chocolate.

Osteria Ballarò

Located in a historic stable, order the handmade busiate pasta with fresh tomato sauce, a crowd-pleaser for both adults and toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy stroller with large wheels to handle the bumpy, uneven cobblestones, or use a baby carrier for crowded street markets. Be aware that many shops and some restaurants close for an afternoon riposo from 1:30 PM to 4:30 PM, so plan your meals and toddler nap times accordingly.
