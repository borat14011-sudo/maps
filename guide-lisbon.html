Lisbon

← back to travel board
Lisbon enchants families with its pastel-colored streets, historic yellow trams, and the sweet aroma of warm custard tarts. From the majestic riverside plazas to world-class marine life, the Portuguese capital offers a relaxed, coastal rhythm that perfectly suits family adventures. It is a city where old-world charm meets modern playground spaces, making every corner a discovery for little eyes.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 minute taxi or rideshare directly to central Lisbon hotels, or a 35-minute journey via the Metro red line.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Oceanário de Lisboa

One of the world's best aquariums, featuring a massive central tank that will mesmerize kids of all ages. Toddlers will love the adorable sea otters and penguins at eye level.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Castelo de São Jorge

Explore the ancient stone ramparts of this hilltop castle which offers stunning panoramic views of the city and river. Little ones can run safely in the shaded courtyards and spot the resident peacocks.

Early Morning / 1h

Tram 28 Scenic Ride

Ride the iconic vintage yellow tram through the historic neighborhoods of Alfama and Estrela. Toddlers will love the bumpy, roller-coaster-like ride, but bring a carrier as strollers must be folded.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Pavilhão do Conhecimento

An interactive science museum located in the Parque das Nações with hands-on exhibits designed specifically for children. There is a dedicated 'Trakinas' area perfect for toddlers to build and play safely.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Jardim da Estrela

A lush, shaded park featuring duck ponds, exotic plants, and a fantastic gated playground. It is the perfect spot for parents to grab a coffee at the kiosk while toddlers climb and slide.

Morning / 2h

Belém Tower & Pastéis de Belém

Marvel at this fortress on the river's edge, then walk to the nearby bakery for world-famous egg tarts. The wide, flat promenade is stroller-friendly and great for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Time Out Market (Mercado da Ribeira)

A bustling food hall offering everything from gourmet seafood to traditional Portuguese treats under one roof. The high energy masks any toddler noise, and the communal benches make family dining easy.

Morning / 3-4h

Lisbon Zoo (Jardim Zoológico)

Home to over 300 species, this classic zoo features a cable car ride offering a bird's-eye view of the animal enclosures. The presentation of dolphins and sea lions is a massive hit with toddlers.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Telecabine Lisbon Cable Car

Take a gentle, scenic cable car ride along the Parque das Nações waterfront for stunning views of the Vasco da Gama bridge. It is quiet, smooth, and completely enclosed, making it safe and thrilling for toddlers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bonjardim

Feast on the city's best spit-roasted piri-piri chicken served with crispy french fries in a casual, highly family-friendly alleyway setting.

Pois, Café

Relax on comfy mismatched sofas in this ultra-family-friendly cafe in Alfama that serves hearty brunch plates and has a dedicated kids' play corner.

Mercado de Campo de Ourique

Enjoy a relaxed meal at this neighborhood food market where you can grab gourmet burgers and sweet waffles in a much calmer, toddler-friendly environment than Time Out Market.

Pizzeria Lucca

Indulge in authentic, thin-crust wood-fired pizzas and fresh pasta in a noisy, welcoming atmosphere where children are treated like royalty.

Pinóquio

Sit at the bustling outdoor terrace and order the legendary garlic clams and steak sandwich while kids enjoy the lively plaza atmosphere.

👨‍👩‍👧 Lisbon's iconic cobblestones can be incredibly slippery and bumpy, so opt for a sturdy, suspension-friendly stroller or a baby carrier. Always look for the 'Atendimento Prioritário' signs, as Portuguese law grants families with babies and toddlers priority access to skip lines at museums, supermarkets, and attractions.
