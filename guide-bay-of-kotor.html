Experience Montenegro's dramatic limestone cliffs and glassy waters without the stress of rugged logistics. The Bay of Kotor offers a highly walkable, compact slice of European coastal charm that is surprisingly stroller-friendly along its modern marina promenades. From ultra-luxury resorts with private beach clubs to calm, shallow bays, it is an ideal haven for a young family seeking both high-end comfort and rich culture.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 minute private taxi from Tivat Airport (TIV) to Tivat hotels, or a scenic ~2 hour private Mercedes V-Class transfer from Dubrovnik Airport (DBV).

What to do

Morning / 2h

Kotor Cable Car (Žičara Kotor-Lovćen)

Ascend from the coastal flatlands to the rugged heights of Lovćen in a smooth, modern, fully enclosed glass cabin that easily accommodates a folded stroller. The summit station features paved, flat viewing platforms and clean family restrooms, offering breathtaking panoramic bay views without any hiking.

Morning / 1.5h

Our Lady of the Rocks (Gospa od Škrpjela)

Hire a private, covered speed boat from the Perast marina to visit this iconic islet with a flat, paved stone surface that is easy to navigate with a carrier or sturdy stroller. The calm morning waters ensure a smooth ride for the baby, and the breeze keeps the open-air island cool.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Porto Montenegro Yacht Marina Promenade

Push your stroller along this pristine, entirely flat, pedestrian-only waterfront lined with superyachts, palm trees, and high-end boutiques. It features wide paved pathways, manicured green spaces, and a clean, modern children's playground away from traffic.

Morning / 1h

Kotor City Park (Gradski Park)

Located just outside the medieval walls of Kotor, this shady park features a well-maintained playground with soft ground cover, swings, and slides perfect for a toddler to burn off energy. It offers excellent shade from mature trees and sits right next to the boat docks, making it a great transition stop.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Perast Waterfront Promenade

Walk along this historic, virtually car-free coastal stone road lined with grand Baroque palaces and quiet cafes. It is flat and highly stroller-accessible, offering gorgeous views of the bay's twin islands without the heavy tourist traffic found inside Kotor's walls.

Morning / 1h

Kotor Farmers Market (Markat)

Introduce your toddler to the vibrant sights and smells of local life at this bustling market built directly into the exterior stone walls of the Old Town. It is flat, lively but not overwhelming in the early morning, and perfect for picking up fresh figs, local cheeses, and soft berries.

Morning / 3h

Plavi Horizonti Beach

Drive to this sheltered, shallow sandy bay surrounded by pine forests on the Lustica Peninsula, which offers calm, wave-free waters ideal for a toddler's first swim. The soft sand is a rare find in the region, and there are sun loungers, clean changing facilities, and plenty of natural shade.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Maritime Heritage Collection

Explore this family-friendly museum in Tivat featuring a massive, real Yugoslav submarine parked right outside on a wide, flat concrete plaza. Toddlers will love running around the open space, and parents can easily wheel strollers through the spacious ground-floor indoor exhibitions.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Portonovi Marina Promenade

Stroll along this brand-new, ultra-luxury residential marina village in Kumbor featuring perfectly paved, car-free stone walkways and gorgeous water views. It offers a pristine, controlled environment with spacious family restrooms, elegant gelato shops, and zero stairs to navigate.

🍽️ Where to eat

Verige 65

Order the signature black cuttlefish risotto or grilled wild sea bass at this ultra-modern restaurant built right over the bay's narrowest strait. The spacious outdoor deck is highly stroller-accessible, and the constant sea breeze and marine traffic will keep your toddler mesmerized.

Konoba Ćatovića Mlini

Savor homemade Njeguški prosciutto and hand-rolled gnocchi at this world-class tavern set inside a beautifully restored old flour mill. The restaurant features expansive, lush green lawns where ducks and geese roam freely, offering perfect entertainment for an active toddler while you dine.

Stari Mlini

Feast on fresh octopus cooked under a traditional iron bell (sač) on a shaded wooden terrace overlooking rushing freshwater streams. The gentle white noise of the water masks any baby noise, and the spacious, historic mill grounds are ideal for stroller naps.

Forza Mare Restaurant

Enjoy a high-end, waterfront fine-dining experience featuring fresh John Dory in a salt crust on a quiet, sophisticated stone deck. Go for an early dinner at 6:00 PM to secure a peaceful waterfront table with plenty of room to park a stroller next to you.

Konoba Portun

Dine on fresh seafood right on a stone pier projecting into the bay, featuring local classics like stuffed squid. It is highly welcoming to families, spacious, and offers a relaxed atmosphere where toddlers can watch small fishing boats go by.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

One&Only Portonovi

This ultra-luxury resort offers stunning family suites, multiple heated indoor and outdoor pools, and a private, sandy beach. They provide top-tier baby amenities including high-quality wooden cribs, bottle warmers, and stroller rentals, alongside flat, perfectly manicured grounds.

Regent Porto Montenegro

Located in the pedestrian-only Tivat marina, this five-star hotel features massive multi-bedroom suites equipped with full kitchens and washers/dryers. The pool podium overlooks the harbor and includes a shallow children's splash area, with stroller-friendly paths immediately outside the lobby.

Hyatt Regency Kotor Bay Resort

Nestled in quiet Stoliv, this modern resort offers peaceful premium apartments with kitchenettes, direct access to a calm beach, and a kids' splash zone. Its location is highly protected from heavy tourist traffic, ensuring quiet, soundproofed rooms for your infant's nap times.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to the flat, paved coastal promenades of Dobrota, Tivat, and Portonovi for stroller walks, as the old towns of Kotor and Herceg Novi feature uneven cobblestones and steep stone steps. Book a private speed boat tour to Our Lady of the Rocks early in the morning to beat the midday cruise crowds and summer heat, ensuring a smoother ride for the baby. Tap water is safe to drink but highly mineralized, so stick to bottled water for baby formula to avoid sensitive stomach upsets while traveling.
