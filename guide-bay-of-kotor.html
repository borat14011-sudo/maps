Bay of Kotor

← back to travel board
Nestled between towering limestone cliffs and glassy cobalt waters, the Bay of Kotor offers a fairytale-like setting that feels entirely magical for families. Wandering the car-free maze of Kotor's Old Town is easy and safe for children, while scenic boat rides across the calm waters provide effortless, high-reward adventures. It is the perfect blend of medieval history, dramatic nature, and gentle seaside living.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Tivat Airport to Kotor Old Town, or ~2 hours via private transfer from Dubrovnik Airport

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Our Lady of the Rocks Islet

Take a short, scenic boat ride from Perast to this unique artificial island holding a historic church and museum. Toddlers will love the breezy boat trip and spotting starfish in the shallow, clear harbor waters.

Morning / 2-3h

Kotor Cable Car to Lovcen

Ascend from the coast to the peaks of Lovcen National Park in just 11 minutes inside a spacious, stroller-accessible cabin. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the bird's-eye view, and there is a safe playground at the top station.

Afternoon / 1h

Kotor Cats Museum and Square

Visit this quirky, budget-friendly museum packed with antique cat illustrations, coins, and books. Afterwards, let your toddler feed the friendly, well-cared-for street cats that lounge in the nearby Piazza of the Cats.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Plavi Horizonti Beach

This sweeping, sandy crescent bay on the Lustica Peninsula offers exceptionally shallow, calm water perfect for tiny paddlers. A lush pine forest borders the sand, providing natural shade for afternoon naps.

Morning / 1h

Kotor Open-Air Farmers Market

Located just outside the medieval town walls, this vibrant market is a sensory delight where friendly vendors offer free samples of local cheese, honey, and fresh figs. It is a fantastic, low-key spot to pick up a healthy picnic for the kids.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Perast Waterfront Promenade

Stroll along this entirely car-free stone promenade lined with grand Venetian palaces and outdoor cafes. It is stroller-friendly and perfect for letting toddlers feed the ducks while parents enjoy a relaxed harbor view.

Midday / 1-2h

Maritime Museum of Montenegro

Housed in an 18th-century palace, this museum features model ships, pirate weapons, and vintage steering wheels that capture young imaginations. The cool stone rooms offer a welcome, air-conditioned escape from the midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Exploring Kotor Old Town Maze

Get lost in the pedestrian-only stone labyrinth of Kotor, where kids can safely run around without car traffic. Keep an eye out for hidden courtyards, ancient stone wells, and the gentle local dogs sleeping in the shade.

Morning / 3h

Bajova Kula Beach

A clean, pebble beach surrounded by laurel trees with crystal-clear, calm waters that are highly sheltered from waves. Rent a sunbed and enjoy beachside service while your toddler splashes safely in the shallows.

🍽️ Where to eat

Konoba Portun

Grab a waterfront table in Dobrota and order the fresh black ink risotto or homemade gnocchi for a delicious, family-friendly meal.

Tanjga

Head to this casual, ultra-welcoming local joint just outside the walls for a massive, family-sized platter of perfectly grilled meats and roasted potatoes.

Conte Restaurant

Sit on the beautiful stone terrace jutting into the water in Perast and order the fresh local sea bass fillet with Swiss chard.

Cesarica

Dine inside this rustic, stone-walled tavern in Kotor's Old Town and try the traditional slow-cooked beef stew known as Pasticada.

Forza Cafe

Sit in the main square of Kotor and indulge in a slice of their famous, towering traditional Krempita cream cake while the kids watch the square's pigeons.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller inside Kotor's Old Town and opt for a baby carrier, as the medieval steps and historic cobblestones can be extremely bumpy. Bring plenty of water shoes for the kids since most of the bay's beaches are pebbly and can harbor sea urchins near the rocks.
