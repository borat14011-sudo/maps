Budapest

← back to travel board
Budapest charms families with its majestic Danube views, whimsical architectural wonders, and surprisingly kid-friendly thermal waters. Tram cars zip past historic monuments, while lush green island parks offer the perfect escape for little legs to run wild. It is a fairy-tale European capital where history feels like a playground for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi or Bolt ride to downtown hotels, or the 100E direct airport express bus taking about 40 mins.

What to do

Afternoon / 2-3h

Margaret Island (Margitsziget)

A car-free island oasis in the middle of the Danube with musical fountains, a mini-zoo, and pedal-car rentals. Toddlers will love chasing the water jets and spotting local deer.

Morning / 3-4h

Budapest Zoo & Botanical Garden

One of the oldest zoos in the world, featuring beautiful Art Nouveau pavilions and interactive petting areas. It is highly stroller-friendly with plenty of shady benches for quick snack breaks.

Morning / 2-3h

Szechenyi Thermal Bath

Soak in the warm, mineral-rich outdoor pools of Budapest's most iconic bathhouse. Toddlers will love the swirling whirlpool channel in the main outdoor pool, but keep swim diapers handy.

Morning / 1-2h

Fisherman's Bastion

A fairy-tale fortress offering panoramic views of the Danube and Parliament. Toddlers will feel like kings and queens climbing the safe, wide ramparts and exploring the turrets.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Children's Railway (Gyermekvasút)

A unique scenic railway through the Buda Hills run almost entirely by school-aged children. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vintage train cars and the novelty of kid conductors.

Morning / 1-2h

Great Market Hall

A bustling, colorful indoor market filled with fresh fruits, pastries, and souvenirs. Keep toddlers strapped in a stroller to navigate the busy crowds while tasting sweet Hungarian lángos.

Afternoon / 1-2h

City Park Playground (Városligeti Nagyjátszótér)

Budapest's ultimate playground features a giant hot-air balloon climbing structure, water play areas, and slides for all ages. It is fully fenced, making it incredibly safe for energetic toddlers.

Morning / 30m

Buda Castle Hill Funicular

Ride a historic, wood-paneled cable railway up to Castle Hill for a thrilling short journey. Toddlers will love looking out the glass windows as the city shrinks below them.

Evening / 1-2h

Karavan Street Food

An open-air food court offering a relaxed vibe with diverse food options. It is a great, casual spot for toddlers to eat pizza or chimney cake without the stress of a formal restaurant.

🍽️ Where to eat

Két Szerecsen Bisztró

Try the classic Hungarian chicken paprikash with butter noodles, served in a cozy, welcoming bistro environment that easily accommodates families.

Mazel Tov

Order the beautifully presented hummus plates, falafel, and pita bread in a spacious, airy, ruin-pub style garden that is loud enough to drown out toddler giggles.

Gundel Cafe Patisserie Restaurant

Get the famous Gundel crepes (palacsinta) stuffed with walnuts and chocolate sauce in a legendary, family-friendly setting next to the zoo.

VakVarjú Restaurant (Pest)

Order the traditional Hungarian flatbreads (kenyérlángos) at this highly family-oriented restaurant that even features a dedicated play corner for toddlers.

Ruszwurm Confectionery

Treat the family to a slice of their legendary Ruszwurm cream cake (krémes) in Budapest's oldest pastry shop near Fisherman's Bastion.

👨‍👩‍👧 Most historic streets in Castle Hill and downtown are cobblestoned, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier for easier mobility. Note that children under six travel free on Budapest's highly efficient public transit system, making trams and metros both cheap and fun for the family.
