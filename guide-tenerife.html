Tenerife

Tenerife blends dramatic volcanic landscapes with gentle, sun-warmed shores perfect for little feet. From the heights of Mount Teide to the sparkling tide pools of Garachico, the island offers a vibrant playground for all generations. Families can easily balance outdoor exploration with relaxed resort life under Spain’s eternal spring skies.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or rental car from Tenerife South Airport (TFS) to the main Costa Adeje beach hotels.

What to do

Full Day / 5-6h

Loro Parque

A world-famous animal embassy featuring majestic gorillas, penguins, and vibrant parrot shows. Toddlers will love the stroller-friendly paths and the shaded Kinderlandia play area.

Full Day / 4-5h

Siam Park

Europe’s leading water kingdom offers spectacular Thai-themed slides and a massive wave pool. Toddlers can safely splash in The Lost City, a dedicated shallow water fortress.

Morning / 3-4h

Mount Teide National Park

Explore otherworldly volcanic landscapes and easy walking paths high above the clouds. Avoid the cable car with toddlers due to rapid altitude changes, and enjoy the Roques de Garcia flat trails instead.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa de Las Teresitas

A golden sand beach with calm, barrier-protected waters that are incredibly safe for little swimmers. Toddlers can build sandcastles peacefully without worrying about strong waves.

Morning / 2h

Mercado Nuestra Señora de África

A vibrant, bustling market housed in a beautiful pink archway building filled with fresh tropical fruits and local treats. It is highly stroller-friendly and has a small playground located in the central courtyard.

Afternoon / 2h

Garachico Natural Pools

Beautiful lava rock pools where families can safely observe ocean life in calm, shallow waters. Stick to the paved viewing decks with toddlers to avoid slippery rocks.

Morning / 2h

Anaga Rural Park

Walk through an ancient, misty laurel forest that feels like a fairytale woodland. The Sendero de los Sentidos is stroller-friendly and perfect for sensory toddler exploration.

Morning / 3-4h

Jungle Park

A lush, wild botanical and zoological park featuring exciting birds of prey shows and suspension bridges. It is quieter and easier to navigate with a toddler than larger theme parks.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa de El Médano

A relaxed bohemian beach town with a shallow, sandy bay perfect for toddlers to splash around while watching colorful windsurfers. The boardwalk is completely flat, making it ideal for stroller walks.

🍽️ Where to eat

Guachinche El Primero

Order the traditional carne fiesta marinated pork and local Canarian wrinkled potatoes with mojo sauce.

Restaurante El Burgado

Savor fresh local seafood and grilled octopus under a unique fishing-net canopy right on the ocean's edge.

Mesón Castellano

Try the authentic Spanish tapas like Iberian ham and garlic prawns in a warm, rustic environment that welcomes children.

Chiringuito Pirata

Grab a casual beachside lunch of homemade empanadas and fresh fruit juices while the kids play in the sand nearby.

Restaurante Los Abrigos

Enjoy a fresh catch of the day, fileted tableside, while watching the fishing boats bob in the harbor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always rent a car with pre-booked car seats to easily navigate the island's winding mountain roads and scenic coastal towns. Pack layers and sun protection, as the weather can shift quickly from breezy volcanic peaks to sunny, warm beaches.
