What to do

Early morning 9-11am

Playa del Duque

This premium golden-sand beach features stroller-accessible wooden ramps, premium sun lounger rentals, and excellent shade under thatched palapas, making it the perfect spot for baby Mary Claire to safely dip her toes in the gentle shorebreak.

Morning 10am-1pm

Loro Parque

A world-renowned conservation park with stroller-friendly paved paths, lush tropical foliage providing natural canopy shade, and dedicated baby-care stations for quiet feedings away from the crowds.

Morning to early afternoon 10am-2pm

Siam Park VIP Cabana

Book a private VIP Cabana at this world-famous water park to secure a quiet, air-conditioned sanctuary with a crib-like daybed for infant naps while parents alternate visiting the lazy river and baby splash zones.

Morning 9-11am

Teide National Park Visitor Center

Skip the high-altitude cable car peak to protect the infant's ears, and instead stroll the flat, paved sensory paths around the El Portillo Visitor Center to admire the dramatic volcanic peaks and unique flora in cooler mountain air.

Late afternoon 5-7pm

Costa Adeje Promenade Walk

A perfectly flat, beautifully paved coastal path ideal for a sunset stroller ride; it offers cooling ocean breezes, shaded resting benches, and stunning vistas of neighboring La Gomera island.

Morning 10am-12pm

Palmetum of Santa Cruz de Tenerife

A spectacular botanical garden specializing in palm trees, featuring wide, paved pathways, abundant shade, and tranquil waterfall features that provide a peaceful, sensory-rich stroller walk for the baby.

Morning 9:30-10:30am

Sendero de los Sentidos (Anaga Forest)

Experience the ancient laurel forest on 'Trail 1,' a short, fully wooden-boardwalked path that is entirely stroller-accessible and deeply shaded by a dense, magical canopy of trees.

Afternoon 3-5pm

San Cristobal de la Laguna Historic District

Stroll this UNESCO World Heritage town’s pedestrian-only streets to admire pastel colonial architecture; select a sturdy stroller to navigate the historic flat cobblestones and stop in quiet, sunlit interior courtyards.

Afternoon 4-5pm

Charco de La Laja Lookout

Witness the dramatic power of northern Tenerife's natural ocean pools from the safety of a flat, concrete-paved viewing platform that is easily accessible with a stroller and well away from any ocean spray.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Nonna

Located on the Playa del Duque beachfront, this upscale Mediterranean restaurant offers an elegant outdoor terrace, accommodating highchairs, and an early 6:30 PM dinner service perfect for keeping the baby's sleep schedule intact.

Kensei - Contemporary Japanese

Situated in the tranquil gardens of Bahia del Duque, this high-end Japanese venue welcomes families for early evening seatings on their peaceful lakeside terrace, where a sleeping infant in a stroller is easily accommodated.

Coqueluche

A chic, relaxed beach bistro on Playa de la Enramada; parents can enjoy fresh Mediterranean-Canarian fusion with their toes in the sand, utilizing highchairs on the terrace during a quiet, breezy lunch.

Restaurante El Burgado

Perched dramatically on the northern coast, this seafood institution features a spectacular shaded outdoor terrace covered in fishing nets, offering a sensory coastal experience and highchairs for a relaxed family lunch.

La Posada del Pez

Renowned for exquisite traditional seafood near San Andres, this refined local gem offers a welcoming, casual family environment during its early afternoon lunch hours, with spacious outdoor seating and highchairs.
