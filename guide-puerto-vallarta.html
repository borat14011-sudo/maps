Puerto Vallarta

← back to travel board
Nestled between the lush Sierra Madre Mountains and the sparkling Banderas Bay, Puerto Vallarta offers a vibrant escape where cobblestone streets meet gentle ocean waves. Families can explore colorful local markets, splash in calm shallow waters, and encounter majestic marine life in a warm, welcoming Mexican paradise. It is the perfect blend of rich culture and easy, kid-friendly beachside relaxation.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle to the main Hotel Zone and Centro areas

What to do

Sunset / 1-2h

El Malecón Boardwalk

A pedestrian-only seaside boardwalk filled with whimsical bronze sculptures, street performers, and sweet treat vendors. It is completely flat, stroller-friendly, and perfect for an evening stroll with a toddler.

Morning / 3-4h

Vallarta Botanical Gardens

Wander through lush jungle trails, cross hanging bridges, and admire thousands of colorful orchids. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, refreshing river running right through the property.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de los Muertos

Relax on the soft sand near the iconic sail-shaped pier, where the waves are relatively gentle for safe splashing. Toddlers can build sandcastles in the shade while parents enjoy beachside food service.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Olas Altas Farmers Market

A colorful weekend market filled with local crafts, fresh pastries, and live music that delights little eyes and ears. Toddlers will enjoy tasting sweet, freshly sliced tropical fruits directly from local vendors.

Full Day / 6-7h

Las Caletas Day Trip

An exclusive beach cove accessible by boat, featuring a dedicated kids' adventure park and close encounters with friendly parrots. The calm cove waters are incredibly safe and easy for toddlers to paddle in.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Zona Romántica Exploration

Walk through charming cobblestone streets lined with colorful paper flags (papel picado) and vibrant murals. Toddlers will love the sensory delight of the bright colors and the frequent local ice cream shops.

Morning / 2h

Estero El Salado

A protected mangrove forest in the middle of the city where you can take a guided boat ride to spot baby crocodiles and colorful birds. The quiet boat ride is gentle and engaging enough to keep toddlers captivated.

Morning / 1-2h

Municipal Market of Rio Cuale

A bustling indoor market filled with Mexican toys, textiles, and local food stalls. Kids can cross the wooden suspension bridges nearby to spot wild green iguanas resting in the river trees.

Afternoon / 1.5h

ChocoMuseo Puerto Vallarta

Learn about the history of cacao and participate in a hands-on chocolate-making workshop. Toddlers will love the tactile experience of tasting sweet chocolate and painting with edible chocolate paints.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Palapa Restaurant

Dine right on the sand under a giant palapa and order the coconut shrimp while kids safely play in the sand at your feet.

Fredy's Tucan

Go for a hearty traditional Mexican breakfast and try the fluffy banana pancakes that toddlers absolutely adore.

Mariscos Ocho Tostadas

Indulge in fresh, crispy ceviche tostadas and order a simple side of plain buttered rice and fresh avocado slices for your toddler.

Panchos Takos

Savor their legendary tacos al pastor shaved fresh off the spit, paired with a refreshing, kid-friendly hibiscus agua fresca.

Coco's Kitchen

Enjoy a peaceful breakfast in a lush, shaded garden patio and try the stuffed French toast or fresh fruit plates.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to bottled water for everyone, including when brushing teeth, and carry a lightweight stroller with sturdy wheels to navigate the historic cobblestone streets. Don't hesitate to take local yellow taxis, as they are abundant, affordable, and highly accommodating to families traveling with young children.
