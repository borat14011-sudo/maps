St. Maarten

← back to travel board
Where French sophistication meets Dutch vibrant charm, St. Maarten offers a playground of turquoise waters and gentle sands perfect for tiny feet. Families can watch planes fly overhead, explore lush rainforest reserves, and build sandcastles on calm, sheltered bays. It is a dual-nation paradise where warm Caribbean breezes make every day feel like a breezy celebration.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 30 min taxi or rental car ride from Princess Juliana International Airport to major resort areas like Simpson Bay or Grand Case

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Maho Beach Airplane Watching

Watch giant passenger jets fly incredibly low over the sand, an absolute thrill for older kids. Toddlers should wear noise-canceling headphones due to the loud engine roars as planes land.

Morning / 3-4h

Loterie Farm Nature Reserve

Hike lush forest trails or relax by the stunning spring-fed pool surrounded by tropical trees. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, shaded water areas while listening to native birds.

Morning / 3h

Mullet Bay Beach

Enjoy one of the island's most beautiful stretches of white sand with calm, crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming. The soft shoreline is ideal for toddlers to splash safely under parent supervision.

Wednesday or Saturday Morning / 1-2h

Marigot Open-Air Market

Wander through this colorful open-air market filled with fresh tropical fruits, local spices, and handmade crafts. Toddlers will be captivated by the bright colors and sweet smells of freshly cut coconuts.

Morning / 1-2h

Parrot Ville Bird Sanctuary

Walk through a vibrant aviary where friendly, colorful parrots land right on your hands to feed. It is incredibly engaging for toddlers who can safely hold cups of seed with parental assistance.

Morning / 2h

Soualiga Sky Explorer Chairlift

Ride the secure, slow-moving chairlift up to Sentry Hill for breathtaking 360-degree views of the neighboring Caribbean islands. Toddlers can ride safely and comfortably in a parent's lap.

Afternoon / 3h

Le Galion Beach

Known locally as Baby Beach, this protected bay features shallow, calm, reef-protected waters where toddlers can wade out yards without waves. It is the ultimate worry-free beach day for parents.

Evening / 2h

Grand Case Boulevard Stroll

Take an evening walk down this seaside strip filled with mouthwatering aromas and gentle sea breezes. Toddlers can enjoy eating sweet crepes from street vendors in a highly stroller-friendly environment.

Morning / 2h

Seaside Nature Park

Meet friendly goats, ducks, and miniature horses at the petting zoo, or take a scenic pony ride along the coast. The gentle petting farm is perfectly sized and paced for curious toddlers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Talk of the Town Lolo

Order the famous grilled garlic shrimp, barbecue ribs, and traditional sweet Johnny cakes in a relaxed, open-air seaside setting.

Karakter Beach Bar & Restaurant

Grab a beachside table on Simpson Bay and enjoy the delicious mahi-mahi tacos while your toddler plays safely in the soft sand right next to you.

La Villa Restaurant

Indulge in exquisite French-Caribbean fusion like roasted red snapper, featuring a warm welcome and great children's options in Grand Case.

Chesterfields Restaurant

Savor the coconut shrimp or classic burgers at this family-friendly dockside spot overlooking the bustling Great Bay marina.

Zee Best Bakery

Treat the family to flaky, freshly baked chocolate croissants and savory ham-and-cheese crepes for the ultimate island breakfast.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and a high-quality baby carrier, as some of the natural paths and narrow market sidewalks are not stroller-friendly. Renting a car is highly recommended for family travel as it allows you to hop between the Dutch and French sides easily while accommodating your toddler's nap schedule.
