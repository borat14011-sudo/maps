What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Parnidis Dune

This breathtaking shifting sand dune features a massive sundial and panoramic views of the Russian border. The main observation deck is completely paved and stroller-accessible, though a baby carrier is required if you wish to step down into the soft white sands with Mary Claire.

Late morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Hill of Witches

An enchanting outdoor sculpture trail in Juodkrantė woven through a dense pine forest. The whimsical wooden carvings of Lithuanian folklore will fascinate a baby, and the tall canopy provides excellent shade; navigate using a rugged stroller or a baby carrier due to occasional tree roots.

Afternoon 2-3:30pm / 1.5h

Thomas Mann Cultural Centre

The historic summer cottage of the Nobel Prize-winning author sits atop Mother's Hill, offering peaceful lagoon views. The beautifully manicured, quiet gardens are ideal for a peaceful stroller nap, and the quiet indoor museum is a cool retreat for parents.

Morning walk 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Nida Promenade

A perfectly flat, paved pathway stretching along the shoreline of the Curonian Lagoon. Lined with colorful weathercocks and benches, it is the ultimate stroller-friendly route for a relaxing walk during Mary Claire's morning nap while enjoying the gentle breeze.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Nagliai Nature Reserve

Also known as the Dead Dunes, this protected area features dramatic wind-swept landscapes. The trail consists of a wooden boardwalk that transitions into soft sand, so leave the stroller behind and use a baby carrier to enjoy this spectacularly quiet, desert-like setting safely.

Mid-day 1-2pm / 1h

Mizgiris Amber Gallery-Museum

An elegant, quiet gallery in Nida showcasing masterfully crafted Baltic amber. The pristine, modern interior is air-conditioned and highly visual, making it an easy, stroller-friendly indoor stop to keep baby calm and entertained.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Amber Bay (Gintaro įlanka)

A highly scenic, reed-lined historic bay in Juodkrantė. The flat, stroller-accessible gravel paths offer a peaceful waterside walk away from the crowds, surrounded by stunning reed sculptures that gently sway in the wind.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Nida Beach

A pristine Blue Flag Baltic beach backed by dramatic, pine-covered dunes. Elevated wooden boardwalks make pushing a stroller directly to the beach viewing platforms easy, and the soft sand is perfect for baby's first sensory barefoot experience.

Morning 11am-12pm / 1h

Nida Ethnographic Cemetery

Located next to the red-brick Evangelical Lutheran Church, this historic, shaded cemetery features unique wooden grave markers called krikštai. The quiet, solemn atmosphere under ancient trees provides a wonderfully peaceful spot to soothe a fussy infant in a stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Nerija

Nida's premier fine-dining establishment, known for wild Baltic turbot and exquisite venison. Book an early outdoor terrace seating where the sophisticated ambiance is welcoming to families, and highchairs are gladly provided.

Nidos Seklyčia Restaurant

Featuring panoramic glass walls overlooking the Curonian Lagoon, this upscale venue serves exceptional local fish dishes. The outdoor patio has plenty of room to park a stroller next to your table, and the staff is famously accommodating to infant diners.

Sofa de Pancho Nida

A chic, upscale Mexican-fusion pop-up in Nida offering vibrant culinary flavors in a luxury setting. The relaxed but high-end terrace atmosphere makes it easy to dine early with an active baby without feeling out of place.

Juodasis Kalnas

An exceptional boutique restaurant in Juodkrantė serving elevated local game and fresh Baltic catches. The shaded garden terrace is incredibly peaceful, offers robust highchairs, and provides a quiet retreat for a family lunch.

Bo House Nida

A trendy waterfront spot combining gourmet pizzas and fresh seafood with stunning lagoon views. The spacious outdoor deck easily accommodates large luxury strollers, and the lively but relaxed vibe absorbs baby babbles effortlessly.
