Miami

← back to travel board
Miami sparkles with a colorful mix of turquoise waters, lush tropical parks, and rich cultural neighborhoods waiting to be explored. Families can seamlessly transition from building sandcastles on iconic beaches to spotting wild manatees in quiet canals. It is a warm, high-energy playground where kids of all ages find their own slice of paradise.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Miami Children's Museum

This interactive wonderland features two floors of hands-on exhibits, including a giant sandcastle climb and a replica cruise ship. Toddlers will love the dedicated Pet Central veterinary clinic and soft toddler play areas.

Morning / 3-4h

Crandon Park Beach

Located on Key Biscayne, this tranquil beach offers calm, shallow waters protected by an offshore sandbar and plenty of shade from soaring palm trees. It is perfect for toddlers to splash safely without dealing with heavy ocean surf.

Morning / 2-3h

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden

Wander through 83 acres of lush tropical rainforest, towering palms, and a stunning butterfly conservatory where hundreds of colorful wings flutter around you. The paved paths are highly stroller-friendly, and toddlers will adore the Children's Garden play area.

Morning / 4-5h

Zoo Miami

As the only sub-tropical zoo in the continental US, this open-air park groups animals by geographic territory in lush, cage-free habitats. Families can rent multi-person safari cycles, and toddlers can cool off in the multiple splash pads scattered throughout the park.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Wynwood Walls

Marvel at the giant, colorful murals painted by world-renowned artists in this pedestrian-friendly outdoor street art museum. Toddlers will be captivated by the bright, high-contrast imagery, and the wide concrete paths make pushing a stroller effortless.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

Home to a historic 1825 lighthouse, this state park offers serene nature trails, wild iguanas, and gentle ocean breezes. Toddlers can safely wade in the quiet surf or watch yachts sail by from the grassy seawall.

Morning / 3h

Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science

This state-of-the-art museum features a stunning three-level aquarium where kids can peer at hammerhead sharks and touch friendly stingrays. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glowing jellyfish and the interactive, sensory light displays.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Lincoln Road Mall

This pedestrian-only promenade is perfect for a leisurely stroll without worrying about street traffic. Toddlers can run free on the grassy lawn areas while parents grab fresh local fruit and snacks from the weekly outdoor green market.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Matheson Hammock Park & Atoll Pool

This scenic park features a unique man-made atoll pool flushed naturally by the tidal action of Biscayne Bay. Toddlers can safely splash in the warm, completely wave-free salt water surrounded by a sandy beach and shady mangroves.

Where to eat

  • Versailles Restaurant — The ultimate Miami staple, serving kid-friendly ham croquetas, sweet plantains, and mild picadillo in a bustling, high-energy atmosphere where noisy toddlers blend right in.
  • The Salty Donut — Indulge in artisanal, melt-in-your-mouth brioche donuts with fun flavors like maple bacon or traditional glazed that kids will absolutely devour.
  • El Palacio de los Jugos — An open-air, market-style eatery perfect for families to grab fresh-squeezed mango juice, tropical fruits, and easy-to-share roasted pork chunks.
  • Lincoln Road Greenmarket — A lively Sunday market offering fresh empanadas, coconut water straight from the shell, and locally made fruit popsicles perfect for warm-weather snacking.
👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense midday Miami heat by planning outdoor beach and park visits for the early morning, then heading to air-conditioned museums or shade-covered splash pads after lunch. Always pack a change of clothes and high-SPF reef-safe sunscreen, as water play opportunities are around almost every corner.
