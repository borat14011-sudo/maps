Costa Verde (Póvoa de Varzim area)

← back to travel board
Nestled along Portugal's breezy northern coast, the Costa Verde offers a sunny haven of wide sandy beaches, historic treasures, and stroller-friendly promenades. Póvoa de Varzim blends traditional maritime heritage with relaxed modern parks, making it an effortless coastal escape for families. Here, fresh Atlantic breezes meet warm Portuguese hospitality, ensuring a gentle pace for travelers with toddlers.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi ride or a direct 45-minute metro trip from Porto Airport (OPO) to the beach hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Praia da Redonda

A wide, golden sand beach sheltered from strong winds, offering calm waters ideal for families. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow tidal pools left behind during low tide.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Passadiços de Póvoa de Varzim

Stretching for miles along the coastline, these wooden boardwalks offer flat, scenic paths over the sand dunes. It is completely stroller-friendly, safe from coastal traffic, and perfect for a breezy walk.

Morning / 1-2h

Nau Quinhentista (Vila do Conde)

Step aboard a meticulously reconstructed 16th-century wooden pirate ship docked in the neighboring harbor. Toddlers will be wide-eyed exploring the wooden decks, ropes, and captain's quarters.

Afternoon / 2h

Parque da Cidade

A lush, expansive city park featuring paved walking paths, tranquil lakes, and a massive modern playground. Toddlers can safely run on the soft turf while watching the resident ducks and geese swim by.

Morning / 1-2h

Monte de São Félix

The highest point in the region, featuring traditional stone windmills and sweeping panoramic views of the ocean. Toddlers will love pointing out the giant windmills that look straight out of a storybook.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal da Póvoa de Varzim

A bustling indoor market filled with colorful displays of local fruits, vegetables, and fresh Atlantic fish. Toddlers will enjoy the lively sights and sounds while snacking on sweet, locally grown strawberries.

Afternoon / 1h

Praça do Almada

The heart of the town, this fully pedestrianized central square features beautiful historic buildings, blooming flower beds, and a small play area. It's the perfect spot to let toddlers wander freely while you enjoy a coffee nearby.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Santa Clara Aqueduct

Marvel at the monumental 18th-century stone aqueduct that spans gracefully across the landscape. The grassy fields surrounding the arches are ideal for a casual family picnic and toddler tumble.

Afternoon / 1h

Diana Bar

An iconic, round Art Deco building located right on the beach promenade that now serves as a beachfront library and cultural space. It features a cozy children's reading nook where toddlers can wind down with picture books.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante O Roberto

Order the fresh charcoal-grilled sea bass, which is incredibly tender, mild, and easy to share with a toddler.

Caximar

Try the legendary 'Pescada à Poveira' (boiled hake with potatoes, egg, and warm olive oil sauce) in a spacious, highly accommodating setting.

Restaurante Bodegão

Get the rich but gently seasoned 'Arroz de Marisco' (seafood rice) served in a traditional clay pot, perfect for a family feast.

Amorosa Caffé

Stop by for their freshly baked sweet crepes and a scoop of artisanal strawberry gelato that toddlers will adore.

Restaurante O Pote

Order the melt-in-your-mouth 'Vitela Assada' (slow-roasted veal) served with soft roasted potatoes that even picky eaters will love.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a lightweight stroller with sturdy wheels to seamlessly transition between the smooth wooden beach boardwalks and the historic cobblestones in town. Keep in mind that local restaurants typically open for dinner around 7:00 PM, so pack extra snacks to keep toddler hunger at bay during the late afternoon.
