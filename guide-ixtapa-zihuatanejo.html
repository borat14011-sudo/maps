Ixtapa Zihuatanejo

← back to travel board
Nestled along Guerrero's Pacific coast, Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo offers a double dose of paradise with its high-end resort conveniences and authentic, sleepy fishing village charm. Gentle waves, vibrant markets, and incredibly welcoming locals make this twin destination an effortless tropical escape for families. Whether you are building sandcastles on quiet shores or spotting wild crocodiles, it is a playground for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle from Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo International Airport (ZIH) to the main beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa La Ropa

This wide, crescent-shaped beach boasts incredibly calm, shallow waters perfect for swimming and building sandcastles. Toddler note: The exceptionally gentle waves make it the safest and most relaxing beach in the bay for tiny splashers.

Morning / 4h

Playa Las Gatas

Take a short, scenic water taxi from the town pier to this sheltered cove where an ancient stone barrier reef keeps the water lake-still. Toddler note: Bring water shoes for the rocky entry points, but enjoy the completely wave-free tide pools.

Early Morning / 2h

Ixtapa Bike Path (Ciclopista)

Rent a bike with a child seat and ride through this beautiful, lush ecological corridor lined with tropical trees and wild bamboo. Toddler note: Keep an eye out for coatis, birds, and iguanas along the paved, stroller-friendly pathway.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Paseo del Pescador (Fisherman's Walk)

Stroll along this scenic, pedestrian-only waterfront boardwalk to watch local fishermen bring in their fresh morning catch. Toddler note: The path is completely stroller-friendly and entirely car-free, making it a stress-free walk at sunset.

Afternoon / 1h

El Popoyote Crocodile Sanctuary

Spot massive crocodiles, roseate spoonbills, and giant iguanas in their natural mangrove habitat from a safe, fenced viewing platform. Toddler note: It is free, quick, and located right next to the Playa Linda pier, holding short toddler attention spans perfectly.

Morning / 1-2h

Zihuatanejo Mercado Municipal

Immerse your senses in this bustling local market filled with colorful tropical fruits, fresh-squeezed juices, and local crafts. Toddler note: Go early to avoid the heaviest crowds, and treat your little one to a sweet, fresh banana or mango slice.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Isla Ixtapa (Ixtapa Island)

Hop on a quick 10-minute water taxi from Playa Linda to explore this car-free island featuring four beautiful, calm beaches. Toddler note: Playa Cuachalalate has incredibly shallow water and plenty of shaded palapas for naptime.

Morning / 4h

Barra de Potosi Lagoon

This small fishing village features a massive, calm lagoon where the river meets the ocean, creating a vast wading pool. Toddler note: Rent a shaded flat-bottom boat ride to spot herons and baby crocodiles safely without walking far.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Playa El Palmar Sunset Walk

This expansive stretch of golden sand in Ixtapa is perfect for evening strolls and watching parasailers glide through the sky. Toddler note: The waves here can be rough, so stick to sandcastle building on the dry shore rather than swimming.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Perla

Order the fresh coconut shrimp or grilled red snapper while your kids play directly in the sand right next to your beachside table.

Bandido's

Get the sizzling beef fajitas and fresh guacamole at this lively, colorful spot in downtown Zihua featuring a vibrant atmosphere and great kids' options.

El Manglar

Try the traditional fish tiritas (Zihua-style ceviche) while sitting on a rustic deck overlooking a mangrove where kids can spot turtles and birds.

Carmelitas Cafe

Treat the family to authentic, traditional breakfasts like chilaquiles or sweet corn tamales in a charming, colorful open-air courtyard.

Emilio's Pizza

Grab a delicious wood-fired artisanal pizza with fresh ingredients in a casual, highly welcoming outdoor garden setting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry Mexican Pesos in small denominations, as many beach water taxis, market stalls, and small palapa restaurants do not accept credit cards. Plan to use a lightweight baby carrier rather than a stroller, as the cobblestone streets of Zihuatanejo and sandy beaches are difficult to navigate with small wheels.
