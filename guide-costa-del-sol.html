What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de los Naranjos

Explore this car-free historic square filled with fragrant orange trees, where parents can enjoy a café con leche while toddlers safely watch the central fountain. The colorful, flower-adorned historic walls make for a beautiful, relaxed morning stroll.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de la Bajadilla

This family-friendly beach features shallow, calm waters and fine golden sand perfect for castle building. It has excellent stroller access and a nearby playground right on the sand.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la Constitución

A peaceful green oasis right in the city center featuring a lovely children's playground and paved pathways. Toddlers can play safely in the shade of towering palm trees and colorful bougainvillea.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Paseo Marítimo

Walk or push a stroller along this beautiful, flat, car-free seaside promenade that stretches for miles. There are plenty of ice cream shops and casual cafes along the way to keep little ones motivated.

Morning / 3-4h

Bioparc Fuengirola

An award-winning, ethically-designed zoo where animals live in recreated natural habitats. Toddlers will love getting close-up views of lemurs and giant tortoises on the easy-to-navigate pathways.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Marbella

A vibrant, bustling indoor market filled with colorful stalls of fresh fruit, local cheeses, and olives. Toddlers will love the lively sensory experience and sampling sweet, fresh Andalusian strawberries.

Morning / 4-5h

Selwo Aventura

Ride in open-top safari trucks to see elephants, giraffes, and lions roaming in vast natural valleys. The park has wide, smooth pathways and a petting zoo area that is perfect for younger children.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Puerto Banús Marina

Marvel at the mega-yachts and luxury sports cars parked along this glamorous marina. The wide docks are completely flat, making it an easy, breezy stroll for families with strollers.

Morning / 2-3h

Aventura Amazonia

An outdoor adventure park with ziplines and ropes courses, featuring a dedicated 'MiniKids' zone for younger children. Toddlers can safely climb low-to-the-ground obstacles under parent supervision.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chiringuito Pepe's Bar

Grab beachside tables on the sand and order the traditional 'espetos' (grilled sardine skewers) while the kids play steps away in the sand.

Churrería Ramón

Treat the family to giant, wheel-shaped Andalusian churros dipped in rich hot chocolate at this legendary, family-friendly spot in Plaza de los Naranjos.

Pizzeria Roma

A warm, family-run Italian spot in Marbella serving delicious, thin-crust Mickey Mouse-shaped pizzas that toddlers will adore.

La Red

Enjoy fresh seafood, traditional paella, and child-friendly options right on the beachfront promenade with plenty of space for strollers.

El Balcón de la Virgen

Dine on traditional Spanish dishes like grilled meats and tapas in a charming, flower-filled alleyway setting that warmly welcomes families.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a baby stroller with sturdy wheels as some historic side streets have bumpy cobblestones. Many beaches offer sunbed rentals with umbrellas, which are essential for keeping toddlers shaded during peak midday heat.
