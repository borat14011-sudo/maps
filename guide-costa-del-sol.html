What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Paseo Marítimo de Marbella

This wide, flat seafront promenade features smooth marble paving that is absolutely flawless for stroller naps. It is lined with shaded cafes and palm trees, making it easy to stop for an espresso while your toddler sleeps.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la Constitución

A peaceful oasis of mature trees and manicured lawns just a block back from the beach, offering a welcome respite from the midday sun. It features a fenced-in playground suitable for toddlers and an outdoor cafe where parents can grab a drink.

Morning / 1-2h

Plaza de los Naranjos

Located in the heart of Marbella's Old Town, this historic square is filled with fragrant orange trees providing natural shade. It is quietest in the morning, making it easy to navigate the cobblestones with a stroller before the lunch crowds arrive.

Morning / 1h

Marbella Club Beach Boardwalk

A pristine stretch of wooden boardwalk running along the Golden Mile right in front of the iconic Marbella Club. The wooden path is wide, completely flat, and offers spectacular sea views for a breezy, low-effort morning walk with an infant.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Marbella

A vibrant indoor food market where you can introduce your toddler to colorful displays of fresh local fruits, fish, and olives. The aisles are wide enough for a compact stroller during the early morning hours.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Selwo Aventura

Located just a short drive away in Estepona, this massive open-air safari park lets kids see elephants and giraffes in naturalized habitats. The main routes are paved and stroller-accessible, though a baby carrier is helpful for the suspension bridges.

Morning / 1-2h

Puerto Banús Marina

Walk along the flat, paved docks of this world-famous marina to admire the mega-yachts and luxury cars. It is highly entertaining for a toddler and entirely flat for easy strolling, especially during the calm, quiet morning hours.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Dunas de Artola (Cabopino)

A protected natural dune reserve featuring a raised wooden boardwalk that is completely stroller-friendly. It leads directly to the calm, shallow waters of Cabopino Beach, which are ideal for a toddler to splash safely.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque de la Represa

A sprawling urban park featuring large duck ponds, manicured lawns, and the unique Bonsai Museum. The paved paths are perfect for pushing a stroller while your toddler enjoys watching the resident ducks and turtles.

🍽️ Where to eat

MC Beach

Order the salt-baked sea bass at this luxury beachside chiringuito owned by the Marbella Club. The chic outdoor tables sit right on the sand with plenty of space for stroller parking and a relaxed breeze that masks any baby noise.

La Red

Try the authentic seafood paella at this casual but excellent beachfront spot directly on the Paseo Marítimo. The outdoor terrace allows you to wheel a sleeping baby right to the table, and the beach is steps away if a toddler needs to run around.

BiBo Marbella by Dani García

Order the famous oxtail brioche at this vibrant, high-end tapas restaurant in Puente Romano. Booking a table at their earliest 7:30 PM seating ensures a quieter atmosphere and highly attentive staff who are incredibly accommodating to families.

Chiringuito El Ancla

Feast on fresh grilled sardines and salt-baked snapper while sitting over the water in San Pedro de Alcántara. This high-end beach club features a massive saltwater pool and spacious outdoor seating that easily fits high chairs.

Rachel's Eco Love

Enjoy organic avocado toast and cold-pressed juices at this beautiful health cafe nestled inside Puente Romano. The tranquil poolside garden setting has wide pathways, making it a very peaceful spot for a healthy family breakfast.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Marbella Club Hotel

The ultimate luxury resort for families, offering gorgeous multi-bedroom villas, lush botanical gardens, and top-tier baby amenities. They provide premium wooden cribs, in-room bottle sterilizers, and have a legendary kids' club for when your toddler is a bit older.

Puente Romano Beach Resort

An upscale, village-style resort featuring spacious suites with separate living rooms, perfect for managing infant nap schedules. The property is completely step-free with smooth paths, multiple heated pools, and a dedicated family-friendly concierge.

Don Carlos Resort & Spa

Located on one of Marbella's best sandy beaches, this resort offers expansive tropical gardens and a magnificent family pool. The resort provides excellent infant kits with high-quality cribs, baby baths, and bottle warmers upon request.
