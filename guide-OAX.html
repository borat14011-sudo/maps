Oaxaca

← back to travel board
Nestled in a fertile valley, Oaxaca cradles families in a warm embrace of cobblestone streets, sweet chocolate aromas, and dazzling textile art. Children will find wonder in towering ancient trees, interactive play spaces, and vibrant plazas that transform into communal living rooms at sunset. It is a slow-paced wonderland where rich Mesoamerican history meets a perfectly relaxed family tempo.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Monte Albán

High above the valley, these ancient Zapotec ruins offer wide, grassy plazas perfect for little legs to run around. Keep toddlers close near steep, unfenced pyramid steps and stick to the flatter central courts.

Afternoon / 1-2h

El Árbol del Tule

Marvel at one of the world's widest trees, whose gnarled trunk resembles various animals that kids will love pointing out. The surrounding gated park is traffic-free and highly safe for toddlers to explore.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado 20 de Noviembre

A sensory explosion of sizzling meats, colorful fruits, and sweet breads. Toddlers will love snacking on fresh pastries, but visit during off-peak morning hours to avoid heavy smoke in the meat aisle.

Morning / 2h

Oaxaca Ethnobotanical Garden

Walk among towering, sculptural cacti and lush native plants in this beautiful, secure garden. You must join a guided tour, so bring a baby carrier since strollers are difficult on the gravel paths.

Morning / 2-3h

Museo Infantil de Oaxaca (MIO)

Housed in a restored railway station, this interactive children's museum features sensory gardens, old train cars, and hands-on art stations. It is specifically designed for toddlers to play freely.

Early Morning / 4-5h

Hierve el Agua

These dramatic petrified mineral waterfalls feature shallow, natural infinity pools that are safe and refreshing for a family splash. The hike down is steep, so stick to the top pools if you have a toddler.

Evening / 1-2h

Zócalo (Plaza de la Constitución)

The beating heart of the city comes alive in the evening with balloon vendors, street musicians, and local families. It is a vibrant, car-free space to let your toddler stretch their legs and people-watch.

Afternoon / 2h

Centro Cultural Santo Domingo

This stunning former monastery features wide, breezy stone corridors and beautiful courtyards that are great for peaceful stroller walks. Toddlers will be captivated by the shiny gold interior of the adjacent temple.

Morning / 3h

Teotitlán del Valle

Visit this peaceful Zapotec weaving village where families can watch wool being dyed naturally with bugs and plants. Kids will love seeing the vibrant colors change instantly when lime juice is added.

Where to eat

  • Chocolatería Mayordomo — Watch cacao beans grind into warm paste before enjoying a comforting cup of traditional sweet hot chocolate and egg-bread.
  • Tlayudas El Negro — Share a giant, crispy Oaxacan 'pizza' topped with refried beans, mild string cheese, and grilled meats that can be customized for picky eaters.
  • Mercado de la Merced — Grab a casual, toddler-friendly breakfast of fresh corn empanadas stuffed with yellow mole and shredded chicken in a relaxed open-air setting.
  • Paletería Popeye — Cool down on a hot afternoon with authentic, fresh-fruit ice pops in kid-approved flavors like mango, strawberry, and creamy coconut.
