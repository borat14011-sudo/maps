London

← back to travel board
London unfolds like a giant, real-life storybook where red double-decker buses and ancient castles stir young imaginations at every corner. Royal parks offer endless grassy stretches for toddler tantrums to melt away, while world-class museums turn history into interactive play. It's a city designed for wandering, making memories, and finding magic in the everyday.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Diana Memorial Playground

This giant wooden pirate ship set in a massive sandy beach is a dream come true for kids who love to climb. Toddlers will love the dedicated sensory trail and teepees, plus the soft sand is perfect for gentle play.

Morning / 2-3h

Natural History Museum

Marvel at towering dinosaur skeletons and interactive earthquake simulators in this stunning, cathedral-like building. The dedicated 'Investigate' family zone is perfect for toddlers to touch and explore real specimens.

Afternoon / 2h

London Transport Museum

Kids can climb aboard real historic double-decker buses, steam trains, and tube carriages. Toddlers will spend hours in the 'All Aboard' play area pushing mini buses and fixing toy engines.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Borough Market

A sensory explosion of sights, smells, and delicious street food that will tempt even the pickiest eaters. Go early so toddlers can easily spot the colorful fruit displays without the dense crowds.

Afternoon / 1h

St. James's Park

Stroll past the resident pelicans and ducks with stunning views of Buckingham Palace in the background. It is the perfect, peaceful green lung for toddlers to run free and chase pigeons after a busy morning.

Late Afternoon / 1h

The London Eye

Get a bird's-eye view of the city from a giant glass pod that moves slow enough to keep everyone calm. The spacious pods are fully enclosed, meaning toddlers can safely walk around and peer at the tiny boats below.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Tate Modern

The colossal Turbine Hall offers a vast, safe indoor space where toddlers can run around and marvel at giant art installations. The gallery also hosts regular family workshops and hands-on digital creation zones.

Morning / 3-4h

ZSL London Zoo

The world's oldest scientific zoo features intimate animal encounters right in the heart of a gorgeous royal park. Toddlers will particularly love the sensory 'Animal Adventure' zone where they can meet goats and sheep.

Midday / 1h

Uber Boat by Thames Clippers

Skip the stressful underground and glide down the river past the Tower of London on this high-speed catamaran. It's a budget-friendly boat ride where toddlers can sit comfortably by the window and watch the drawbridges go by.

Where to eat

  • Dishoom King's Cross — This bustling, spacious Indian café offers an incredibly welcoming atmosphere for families alongside delicious, mild breakfast bacon naans and child-friendly mocktails.
  • Poppies Fish & Chips — An iconic, retro-themed spot in Spitalfields serving crispy, authentic fish and chips with a dedicated kids' menu that toddlers will devour.
  • Giraffe Southbank — Highly family-focused restaurant featuring a global menu, cheerful giraffe-themed decor, and quick service that caters perfectly to restless toddlers.
  • Seven Dials Market — An indoor food hall with a cheese conveyor belt and plenty of space, making it easy to grab various quick bites for picky toddlers.
