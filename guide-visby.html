Medieval charm meets Baltic coastal calm for young families

Visby

← back to travel board
Visby offers a fairytale Baltic escape where dramatic medieval ruins stand alongside rose-covered cottages and cobblestone lanes. For a family traveling with an infant, the town combines a safe, slow-paced Scandinavian environment with beautiful coastal parks and exceptional family infrastructure. It is a wonderfully walkable, stress-free destination where historic sights are easily enjoyed with a stroller and the summer days stretch late into the evening.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 minute taxi ride from Visby Airport (VBY) directly to the historic town center inside the medieval walls.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Visby Ringmur (Visby City Wall)

Walk along this spectacularly preserved 3.4km medieval stone wall to take in towering guard towers and sea views. The paved pathway running along the exterior of the wall is completely flat and smooth, making it the ultimate spot for a peaceful stroller nap.

Afternoon / 1h

DBW's Botaniska Trädgård (Botanical Garden)

A lush, wind-sheltered garden oasis filled with exotic trees, roses, and ruins near the sea. Its wide, paved gravel paths provide a quiet and highly scenic environment perfect for wheeling a sleeping infant under giant shade trees.

Morning / 1-2h

Almedalen Park and Playground

This expansive green park features a scenic duck pond and a fantastic, modern wooden playground (Almedalens lekplats) designed for all ages. Smooth asphalt paths wind along the shoreline, offering parents beautiful sea views while the toddler stretches her legs.

Morning / 2h

Gotlands Museum (Fornsalen)

Explore world-class Viking silver hoards and medieval picture stones inside a fully climate-controlled environment. The museum is highly stroller-accessible via elevators and features a fantastic interactive play room and clean, spacious baby-changing facilities.

Morning / 3-4h

Kneippbyn Resort (Villa Villekulla)

Located just 10 minutes south of Visby, this park houses the actual wooden villa used in the classic Pippi Longstocking films. It is highly stroller-friendly with flat grassy lawns, family picnic spots, and shallow, heated splash pools ideal for a toddler's feet.

Afternoon / 1h

Sankt Karins Ruin (St. Karin Church Ruin)

Step inside the soaring, roofless gothic arches of this medieval church ruin located right on the main square. The interior is flat, grassy, and safely enclosed, allowing your toddler to toddle around and explore the massive stone pillars safely.

Morning / 2h

Snäckstranden (Snäck Beach)

A beautiful sandy beach located 4km north of the city walls, offering calm, shallow Baltic waters perfect for dipping tiny toes. A wooden boardwalk extends onto the sand, making it incredibly easy to roll a stroller right down to the water's edge.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Galgberget Nature Reserve

A clifftop nature area just north of the city wall offering sweeping panoramic views of the town and ocean. The gravel walking trails are wide and stroller-friendly, taking you safely past historical stone gallows and grazing sheep.

Morning / 2h

Fenomenalen Science Centre

A hands-on science museum located in Visby's harbor that focuses on interactive learning for younger kids. It features a dedicated soft-play sensory area and water tables specifically suited for curious infants and toddlers on a rainy afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Bakfickan

Order the signature 'rökta räkor' (smoked shrimp with aioli) at this famous seafood spot on Stora Torget. The outdoor patio tables are perfect for parking a stroller, and the relaxed, bustling atmosphere easily absorbs any baby noise.

Creperie & Logi

Feast on savory galettes made with local Gotland lamb, cheese, and beets in a charming historic alleyway. Dine in their spacious cobblestone courtyard during their early 5 PM dinner service when it is quiet and highly accommodating to families.

Glassmagasinet

Treat the family to local saffron ice cream (saffransglass) at Europe's largest ice cream parlor located on the harbor. The venue features wide entry ramps, endless outdoor seating, and over 300 flavors, making it a stress-free sweet stop.

Lilla Bjers Krog

Enjoy an extraordinary farm-to-table lunch organic tasting menu on a historic farm just 10 minutes outside Visby. The spacious outdoor garden dining tables allow toddlers to look at growing vegetables and tractor paths while parents enjoy high-end cuisine.

Bolaget

Indulge in classic French-bistro cuisine and local craft beers in a grand former historic building on the main square. Their massive, heated outdoor terrace easily accommodates large strollers and offers plenty of room for an early family dinner.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotell S:t Clemens

This eco-certified hotel features direct access to a private, peaceful garden courtyard adjacent to a church ruin, ideal for quiet stroller laps. They offer spacious family rooms, premium wooden cribs, and a exceptionally warm, family-first staff.

Clarion Hotel Wisby

A historic hotel inside the city walls featuring spacious multi-room family suites and an indoor heated pool with designated family hours. Its central, flat location near the harbor makes returning for midday baby naps incredibly convenient.

Best Western Strand Hotel

Perfectly situated steps from Almedalen Park, this hotel offers spacious family apartments equipped with kitchenettes. They provide complimentary baby cribs, high-chairs in the breakfast room, and convenient guest laundry facilities.

👨‍👩‍👧 Visby's historic medieval streets are laid with rugged cobblestones, so packing a sturdy, all-terrain stroller with inflatable tires or robust suspension is absolutely essential. Swedish 'fika' culture is incredibly family-friendly, meaning cafes gladly welcome strollers and will happily warm baby bottles or food jars upon request. Public restrooms throughout Visby are exceptionally clean and almost universally equipped with well-maintained, modern baby-changing tables.
