Rose-draped medieval walls and seaside adventures await families

Visby

← back to travel board
Step into a real-life fairy tale where limestone town walls wrap around cobblestone streets filled with wild roses and playground-dotted parks. Visby blends medieval history with gentle Baltic beaches, offering families an enchanting, slow-paced Scandinavian escape. It's a walkable wonderland where children can chase knights' legends and splash in shallow coastal waters.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi or local bus 18/59 to the medieval town center from Visby Airport

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Visby Ringmuren (Town Wall)

Explore Europe's best-preserved medieval city wall with its towering stone gates and grassy moats. Toddlers will love running along the safe, open paths of Nordergravar below the ramparts.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Almedalen Park

A lush seaside park featuring a fantastic, modern playground, sprawling lawns, and a duck pond. It is the perfect spot for parents to relax while toddlers climb, swing, and feed the ducks.

Rainy Day / 2-3h

Gotland Museum (Fornsalen)

An engaging museum featuring Viking silver treasures and interactive history exhibits. The dedicated kids' area, Fenomenalen, offers hands-on science experiments and toddler-friendly wooden play zones.

Morning / 1h

DBW's Botanical Garden

A peaceful oasis filled with exotic trees, colorful rose gardens, and a picturesque church ruin. The flat, paved paths are ideal for stroller walks and safe toddler exploration.

Afternoon / 1h

Stora Torget and St. Karin Ruins

Visby's vibrant main square centered around the spectacular, roofless arches of St. Karin church ruin. Toddlers will love running through the safe, open ruins while parents enjoy a coffee nearby.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Snäckgärdsbaden Beach

A sandy beach just north of town featuring very shallow, calm waters ideal for little splashers. A wooden boardwalk, playground, and ice cream kiosk make it an easy family beach day.

Morning / 1-2h

Galgberget Nature Reserve

A coastal nature reserve offering walking trails through pine forests and dramatic views over the Baltic Sea. Toddlers will love exploring the flat dirt trails and spotting local wooden troll carvings.

Afternoon / 30m

Visby Glassblåseri

Watch master glassblowers shape glowing, molten glass into beautiful artwork in a historic workshop. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the glowing colors from the safe viewing benches.

Full Day / 4-6h

Kneippbyn Summerland

A resort theme park just south of Visby featuring the actual Villa Villekulla house from the Pippi Longstocking films. It offers toddler-friendly water slides, bouncy castles, and live character meet-and-greets.

🍽️ Where to eat

Glassmagasinet

Visit this harbor-side parlor offering over 300 flavors of ice cream, where kids can try local Gotlandic honey-saffron ice cream.

Bakfickan

Get the traditional smoked shrimp platter or a kid-friendly fish-and-chips basket at this cozy, retro-styled seafood spot on the main square.

Crêperie & Logi

Indulge in delicious, sweet butter-and-sugar crepes for the kids and savory buckwheat galettes for the adults inside a quirky, historic medieval building.

Bageriet Visby

Order a slice of traditional Gotland Saffranspannkaka (saffron pancake with dewberry jam and whipped cream), a soft and sweet local treat toddlers love.

Badhusparkens Café

Grab freshly baked Swedish cinnamon buns (kanelbullar) and juice boxes at this outdoor garden cafe right beside the medieval city wall.

👨‍👩‍👧 Visby's historic cobblestone streets can be bumpy, so bring an all-terrain stroller with large wheels or a baby carrier. Public restrooms with changing tables are most easily found inside the Gotland Museum, Almedalen library, and larger cafes.
