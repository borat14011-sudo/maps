Chic coastal charm meets effortless toddler-friendly lakeside adventures

Door County

← back to travel board
Experience New England charm with Midwestern warmth in Wisconsin's legendary peninsula. This escape balances the refined tastes of a finance professional with the slow, nap-centric pace required for an active toddler. From stroller-friendly forest boardwalks to casual bayside dining, you'll find upscale comfort without the stifling pretension of typical coastal resorts.
🚕 Getting there: ~1 hour and 15 minutes drive from Green Bay Austin Straubel International Airport (GRB) via a rental SUV, driving north on Highway 57 directly to your resort.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Eagle Tower at Peninsula State Park

Walk the 850-foot fully accessible canopy ramp that gently winds through the treetops to a stunning 75-foot viewing platform. It is completely stroller-friendly, allowing you to bypass the stairs entirely while keeping your toddler safe and secure.

Morning / 2h

The Farm

A living museum where your toddler can bottle-feed goat kids, pet piglets, and see vintage farm equipment up close. The gravel paths are wide enough for heavy-duty strollers, and the shaded paths offer plenty of cooling relief.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Hidden Brook Boardwalk at The Ridges Sanctuary

This 1,800-foot state-of-the-art wooden boardwalk protects fragile ecosystems while offering a perfectly flat, bump-free ride for your stroller. Keep an eye out for frogs and gentle streams that will capture your little one's imagination.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Sister Bay Waterfront Park

Boasting the largest public waterfront in the county, this park features a massive expanse of soft grass and a well-maintained playground. It is the perfect spot for your toddler to burn off energy while you enjoy views of the marina.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Lautenbach's Orchard Country Winery & Market

Let your daughter experience picking her own Door County cherries or apples depending on the season, followed by fresh bakery treats in the market. The flat orchard rows are highly accessible, and there is plenty of room for a stroller in the spacious farm store.

Morning / 2h

Plum Loco Animal Farm

Specifically geared towards younger children, this farm park features gentle farm animals and a unique play-farm village filled with toys. It is fully fenced, allowing you to let your toddler explore with minimal stress.

Morning / 2h

Cana Island Lighthouse

Enjoy a unique tractor-drawn hay wagon ride across the shallow, rocky causeway to this historic island. While the 97-step spiral staircase isn't toddler-safe, the grassy grounds and lake breezes are perfect for a stroller walk.

Evening / 1h

Egg Harbor Marina

Walk along the smooth paved breakwall path and enjoy a quiet sunset with your family. The adjacent harbor park features a modern playground with rubberized turf, which is much safer for wobbly toddlers than woodchips.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Door County Maritime Museum

Beat the afternoon heat in this climate-controlled, multi-level museum featuring an elevator for seamless stroller access. Interactive exhibits and miniature boat displays provide great visual stimulation for younger kids.

🍽️ Where to eat

Al Johnson's Swedish Restaurant & Butik

Order the thin Swedish pancakes with wild lingonberries while your toddler is mesmerized by the real goats grazing on the sod roof. The bustling, loud atmosphere easily drowns out baby noise, and they open early at 7:00 AM.

Wild Tomato Wood-Fired Pizza and Grille

Order the 'D-Lux' wood-fired pizza in a highly energetic, family-centric setting right outside the park. They offer fast table service and spacious outdoor patio dining with plenty of room to park a stroller.

PC Junction

Order the classic cheeseburger and watch your toddler's eyes light up as a model train delivers your food directly to the counter. The outdoor play area features ride-on toys to occupy little ones while you wait for your table.

Pelletier's Restaurant & Fish Boil

Experience a boiling-over show featuring fresh Lake Michigan whitefish outside, then head in for an early 4:30 PM seating. The quick service and exciting fire show make this iconic tradition easy and fun for short attention spans.

Chef's Hat Cafe

Enjoy a premium breakfast scramble or a turkey avocado wrap on the outdoor patio overlooking the bay. The spacious lawn next door allows one parent to walk the baby while waiting for the upscale comfort food to arrive.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Landmark Resort

This sprawling resort offers one- to three-bedroom suites with full kitchens, allowing you to prepare baby food and enjoy separate nap spaces. Families love the indoor pool, playground, and extensive wooded grounds for peaceful stroller walks.

Glidden Lodge Beach Resort

Located right on a quiet, golden sand beach with shallow water, this resort features spacious suites equipped with full kitchens and laundry. It provides a peaceful retreat away from the busy highway with a heated indoor pool perfect for splashing.

Open Hearth Lodge

An exceptionally family-friendly lodge featuring a heated indoor pool, outdoor playground, and a large lawn with fire pits. Pack-n-plays are readily available, and the quiet location on the edge of town ensures undisturbed naps.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep your travel schedule aligned with early restaurant openings, as spots like Al Johnson's get incredibly busy after 8:30 AM but are highly accommodating at dawn. Opt for a rugged all-terrain stroller, as many state park paths are packed gravel or dirt, even if they are flat and accessible. Stock up on toddler essentials at the Tadych's Market Marketplace in Sturgeon Bay before heading further north, as smaller village shops have limited baby selections.
