Door County

← back to travel board
Door County wraps pristine Lake Michigan shorelines in a timeless, sophisticated cape-like charm tailored for discerning families. Here, days are measured by the gentle breeze whispering through cedar forests and the soft glow of watercolor sunsets over Green Bay. The peninsula's exceptionally flat, stroller-ready sunset promenade in Ephraim offers an effortless way to enjoy the waterfront with baby Mary Claire. This coastal haven seamlessly pairs upscale dining and historic boutique lodges with absolute convenience for a refined, multi-generational getaway.
🚕 Getting there: Green Bay Austin Straubel International Airport (GRB) is the closest commercial airport, located roughly 1 hour and 30 minutes from the hotel strip in Ephraim. The most seamless option for a family with an infant and stroller is hiring a private luxury SUV transfer from Green Bay Executive Limo, pre-arranged with a premium rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Eagle Tower at Peninsula State Park

The fully stroller-accessible canopy ramp winds gently through the forest treetops, offering sweeping panoramic views of Green Bay with zero climbing required for baby Mary Claire. Sheltered by leafy shade, it is a peaceful and scenic spot for a morning stroll.

Late morning 11am-12:30pm / 1.5h

The Ridges Sanctuary

This pristine nature preserve features extremely flat, shaded wooden boardwalks that are completely stroller-friendly. It is incredibly quiet and peaceful, making it the perfect setting for an outdoor contact nap while walking among beautiful wild orchids.

Early afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Lautenbach's Orchard Country Winery & Market

Stroll through spacious, flat cherry orchards and let Mary Claire enjoy the sensory experience of fresh breezes. Parents can sample local estate wines and cherry treats on the shaded outdoor patio with plenty of space to park a luxury stroller.

Afternoon 3:30-5pm / 1.5h

Sister Bay Marina Park

A expansive, grassy waterfront lawn with a paved, stroller-ready harbor promenade. Ideal for afternoon tummy time on a picnic blanket while watching yachts sail in, featuring plenty of shade trees and refreshing lakeside breezes.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Cave Point County Park

Stunning limestone cliffs where the waves crash dramatically. While the shoreline rocks require care, the paved overlook pathways near the parking lot are safe and stroller-accessible, allowing parents to enjoy the dramatic mist and lake views safely.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Door County Maritime Museum

This world-class, fully air-conditioned museum in Sturgeon Bay features wide, elevator-accessible galleries that are perfect for a hot or rainy day. It offers a cool, quiet environment to push the stroller while exploring the region's rich shipbuilding history.

Late afternoon 4-5pm / 1h

Anderson Dock and Hardy Gallery

Walk along the historic, completely flat concrete dock in Ephraim to view the colorful graffiti art. It is a highly scenic, low-effort photo spot right on the calm water, with a wide harbor wall that makes maneuvering a premium stroller simple and safe.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Whitefish Dunes State Park

Explore the shaded, flat Red Pine trail which is highly accessible for strollers and baby carriers. The quiet forest path leads near the shore, providing a tranquil soundtrack of waves and birdsong that is excellent for keeping an infant calm.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Schoolhouse Beach Park

Famous for its smooth, ocean-like limestone pebbles and crystal-clear, incredibly calm water. Since strollers do not roll on the stones, skip the stroller and use a luxury baby carrier to dip Mary Claire's toes in the gentle, shallow cove.

🍽️ Where to eat

Al Johnson's Swedish Restaurant

Famous for wild goats grazing on the sod roof and signature Swedish pancakes with lingonberries. The outdoor 'Stabbur' beer garden has wide spaces, highchairs, and a lively, family-friendly noise level that easily absorbs any baby babbles or fussiness.

White Gull Inn

Savor their award-winning Door County cherry stuffed French toast in a historic, upscale dining room. Highly accommodating to families during early morning breakfast seatings, providing comfortable highchairs and swift, attentive service.

Wickman House

An upscale, farm-to-table culinary destination featuring handcrafted cocktails and seasonal plates. Their beautiful, sprawling outdoor garden gazebo seating is perfect for an early 5pm reservation, allowing baby Mary Claire to nap in the stroller nearby.

Wild Tomato Wood-Fired Pizza and Grille

Features gourmet wood-fired pizzas made with local ingredients. The spacious outdoor patio in Sister Bay is highly welcoming, with a casual upscale vibe, highchairs, and a vibrant dining atmosphere where families feel entirely at ease.

Trixie's

A chic, intimate European-style bistro offering natural wines and seasonal small plates. The relaxed outdoor patio tables offer a sophisticated atmosphere for Will and Kathryn to enjoy a glass of wine during an early dinner before baby's bedtime.
