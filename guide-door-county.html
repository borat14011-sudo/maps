Door County

← back to travel board
Wisconsin's beloved peninsula welcomes families with its breezy shoreline highways, historic lighthouses, and sweet-scented cherry orchards. Here, days are filled with lakeside splashes, easy forest hikes, and the simple joy of watching goats graze on a restaurant roof. It is the ultimate Midwest escape where modern parenting stress melts into retro lakeside charm.
🚕 Getting there: ~1 hour 15 minutes by rental car from Green Bay Austin Straubel International Airport (GRB) to the family-friendly resorts in Sturgeon Bay or Sister Bay.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Farm

An interactive living museum where kids can bottle-feed goat kids, piglets, and calves. Toddlers will love petting the free-roaming farm animals and exploring the vintage tractors.

Morning / 1-2h

Peninsula State Park Eagle Tower

Climb or wheel up the fully accessible 850-foot canopy ramp to enjoy breathtaking views of Green Bay. Toddlers can easily navigate the wide, flat wooden ramp in a stroller.

Afternoon / 2h

Cana Island Lighthouse

Ride a tractor-drawn hay wagon across the shallow cobblestone causeway to explore this iconic 1869 lighthouse station. While the tower climb is for older kids, toddlers will be thrilled by the bumpy wagon ride.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Sister Bay Beach and Waterfront Park

This expansive sandy beach offers a fantastic playground, grassy areas for picnics, and shallow water for wading. It is exceptionally toddler-friendly with nearby restrooms and easy stroller access.

Morning / 1-2h

Lautenbach's Orchard Country

Pick your own sweet cherries in the summer or juicy apples in the fall at this bustling family-owned estate. Toddlers will love the spacious outdoor play area and sampling fresh cherry donuts.

Morning / 1-2h

The Ridges Sanctuary

Walk the stroller-accessible Hidden Brook Boardwalk through gorgeous forest and wetland ecosystems. The nature center features hands-on sensory exhibits designed specifically for toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Cave Point County Park

Marvel at the dramatic limestone cliffs and underwater caves where waves crash spectacularly against the rocks. Keep toddlers secure in a carrier here to safely enjoy the dramatic views and splashing spray.

Morning / Half-day

Washington Island Ferry

Take a scenic 30-minute ferry ride across the Death's Door passage to explore Washington Island. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching the water churn from the open-air upper deck of the boat.

Afternoon / 2h

Door County Maritime Museum

Discover rich shipwreck history and climb aboard a real 149-foot historic tugboat. Toddlers will love the interactive, kid-sized captain's wheel and hands-on lighthouse play table.

🍽️ Where to eat

Al Johnson's Swedish Restaurant

Order the thin Swedish pancakes served with sweet lingonberries while kids watch real goats grazing on the sod roof.

Wilson's Restaurant & Ice Cream Parlor

Grab a classic flame-broiled burger followed by a giant, old-fashioned hot fudge sundae in a souvenir dish.

Pelletier's Restaurant & Fish Boil

Experience a historic Door County fish boil where fresh whitefish is cooked over a roaring open fire that boils over dramatically.

PC Junction

Enjoy kid-friendly favorites like grilled cheese or chicken tenders delivered directly to your counter seat by a model train.

Wild Tomato Wood-Fired Pizza and Grille

Feast on the local-favorite 'Green sentiments' or a classic pepperoni wood-fired pizza in a relaxed, highly family-friendly outdoor garden.
