Bacalar

← back to travel board
Forget the chaotic party crowds of Cancun; Bacalar offers a pristine, Maldives-like freshwater sanctuary perfect for a recharging finance dad and his young family. The crystal-clear, warm, shallow waters of the Lagoon of Seven Colors act as a massive, natural baby pool with no crashing ocean waves to worry about. Days here move at a slow, restorative pace, transitioning smoothly from shaded garden lunches to private sailboat charters that easily accommodate afternoon naps.
🚕 Getting there: ~35-minute private SUV transfer from Chetumal International Airport (CTM), or a scenic 2.5-hour drive south from Tulum International Airport (TQO) via Federal Highway 307.

What to do

Morning / 3h

Sailing Tour with Veleando Ando

A private, wind-powered sailboat charter is incredibly peaceful and quiet, avoiding loud motor noises that might startle an infant. The spacious deck allows room to lay out a baby play mat under the shaded canopy.

Morning / 2-3h

Los Rapidos Bacalar

A natural, gentle lazy river where you can float through crystal-clear water surrounded by ancient stromatolites. The onsite restaurant provides shaded decks right on the water's edge, ideal for keeping a toddler cool while enjoying a snack.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Fuerte de San Felipe

An 18th-century stone fortress featuring lush, flat grassy lawns outside that are perfect for a stroller walk. The elevated position offers breathtaking, panoramic lagoon views without the hassle of navigating deep sand.

Morning / 1-2h

Balneario Ecológico

A tranquil, grassy public park with massive shade trees and exceptionally shallow, sandy-bottom water access. It is the safest, most relaxed spot in town for a baby's very first splash in the lagoon.

Morning / 2h

Canal de los Piratas

This legendary channel features white sulfur clay sand and incredibly shallow, waist-deep water that stretches for yards. It acts as a giant, warm-water splash pad where your toddler can safely sit and play in the shallows.

Evening / 1-2h

Parque Central de Bacalar

The main town square features smooth paved paths, a small playground, and a vibrant, family-friendly evening atmosphere. It is the perfect place for a sunset stroller walk while sampling local street treats.

Midday / 1-2h

Cenote Azul

A massive, open-air cenote surrounded by lush jungle foliage that looks like a deep blue lake. The onsite restaurant offers shaded, lakeside tables where you can relax with a drink while your toddler watches the local ducks.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Bacalar

A vibrant, authentic public market filled with colorful stalls of fresh, tropical fruits. It is an excellent morning stop to stock up on ripe papayas, bananas, and avocados for easy, healthy baby purees.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Mármol Beach Club

A beautifully designed, upscale day club offering wide, comfortable daybeds shaded by palms. It provides a clean, controlled environment with shallow lagoon entry, clean restrooms, and high-end service for parents.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita

Order the grilled octopus and fresh fish tacos. The spacious, open-air jungle garden setting easily accommodates large strollers and naturally absorbs baby noise.

Mango y Chile

Order the classic plant-based burger and a passionfruit milkshake. A casual, elevated counter-service spot overlooking the fort with a highly relaxed, family-friendly vibe.

El Manatí Bacalar

Order the Chilaquiles Verdes and fresh green juice. This lush, artistic backyard garden oasis provides plenty of natural distractions like flowers and fish ponds for an active toddler.

Pizzeria Bertilla

Order the thin-crust prosciutto pizza and homemade gnocchi. An extremely welcoming, family-run Italian spot with quick service and simple, toddler-approved menu options.

Savora Bakhalar

Order the fresh grilled catch of the day with coconut rice. Located right on the main square with breezy outdoor sidewalk seating, making it easy to step away if the baby gets restless.
