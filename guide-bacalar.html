Nestled in southern Quintana Roo, Bacalar offers an ultra-exclusive, serene alternative to the crowded Riviera Maya. The town radiates a tranquil, bohemian-luxury aesthetic perfect for elite finance professionals seeking a slower, highly personalized pace of travel. Will and Kathryn will find the crystal-clear, entirely wave-free shallow waters of the lagoon to be nature's ultimate private wading pool for baby Mary Claire. Private docks, shaded overwater cabanas, and intimate boutique sanctuaries ensure a seamless, crowd-free tropical escape.
🚕 Getting there: Chetumal International Airport (CTM) is a 35-minute drive from the Bacalar hotel strip; the most seamless option is a pre-arranged private luxury SUV transfer equipped with a premium, deep-cleaned rear-facing infant car seat.

What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Cenote Cocalitos

Known for its ancient stromatolites, this cenote features incredibly shallow, calm, crystal-clear water where Mary Claire can safely splash; arrive early to snag a shaded palapa.

Late Afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Fort of San Felipe

This 18th-century stone fortress offers sweeping, elevated views of the lagoon; the grassy inland ramparts are easily stroller-accessible and provide plenty of shade under historic stone archways.

Morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Canal de los Piratas

Book a private, shaded pontoon charter to this historic channel; the sandbars here are waist-deep, warm, and have a soft sulfur-clay bottom perfect for a gentle baby swim.

Evening 6-7:30pm / 1.5h

Parque de Bacalar

The charming, flat central town square is highly stroller-friendly; stroll under the shade of massive trees during the cooler early evening hours when local artisans set up.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Cenote Azul

A massive open cenote resembling a deep blue forest lake; while the center is deep, the shore features a highly shaded, paved restaurant area perfect for a cool, breezy lunch.

Morning 8-10am / 2h

Isla de los Pájaros

A tranquil bird sanctuary best viewed from the deck of a private electric boat; the gentle purr of the eco-friendly motor and quiet environment are perfect for inducing a stroller-free baby nap.

Morning 10am-1pm / 3h

Los Rápidos

A narrow, river-like channel of the lagoon; rent a private shaded cabana along the grassy banks to relax with Mary Claire while watching the crystal-clear water flow past.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Cenote Negro

Also known as Cenote de la Bruja, this dramatic drop-off is best visited via a private, covered luxury pontoon boat, offering spectacular deep-blue views in complete shade.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Cenote Esmeralda

A beautiful, shallow cove inside the lagoon where the water turns a brilliant shade of light teal; perfect for a quiet, private family swim right off the side of a chartered luxury vessel.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita

Feast on fresh octopus tacos under the shade of a magnificent, ancient rubber tree; this upscale lagoon-side venue offers highchairs, spacious outdoor decking, and a relaxed daytime vibe.

Macario

Indulge in high-end contemporary Mexican cuisine like tender pork belly with complex mole; the open-air garden design is highly sophisticated, and an early 6:00 PM seating easily accommodates a sleeping baby.

Nixtamal

Savor premium wood-fired local fish in an enchanting, dimly lit jungle courtyard; the natural ambient noise of the garden provides an excellent sound buffer for infants.

El Manatí Bacalar

An iconic, artistic garden cafe serving organic chilaquiles and fresh green juices; the lush back lawn is perfect for laying down a blanket so Mary Claire can have some tummy time.

Siete Restaurant

Located inside Mia Bacalar, this fine-dining spot offers upscale local fusion; spectacular panoramic lagoon views, pristine highchairs, and highly accommodating service make for an easy luxury dinner.
