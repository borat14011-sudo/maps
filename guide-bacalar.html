Bacalar

← back to travel board
Bacalar captivates families with its breathtaking Lake of Seven Colors, where crystal-clear freshwater meets soft white sand. This laid-back pueblo mágico offers a peaceful alternative to busy beach towns, perfect for slow-paced exploring. Toddlers and parents alike will fall in love with the calm, shallow waters and gentle jungle breezes.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min taxi from Chetumal Airport (CTM) or a ~4 hour private transfer from Cancun Airport (CUN)

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Cenote Azul

A deep, open-air cenote surrounded by lush jungle, offering calm swimming areas and an onsite restaurant. Toddler note: Keep young ones in life jackets near the shore as the water drops off to extreme depths quickly.

Morning / 3h

Los Rapidos Bacalar

A natural water canal where gentle currents float you down the crystal-clear lagoon alongside ancient stromatolites. Toddler note: Hold your toddler tightly in a double float or baby life jacket to safely enjoy this lazy river ride.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Fuerte de San Felipe

An 18th-century stone fortress built to ward off pirates, featuring old cannons and an air-conditioned museum. Toddler note: The grassy inner courtyard and wide ramparts are great for little legs to explore safely.

Morning / 2-3h

Balneario Ecológico Cocalitos

A relaxing beach club known for its shallow, crystal-clear water and iconic overwater swings. Toddler note: The water is exceptionally shallow for yards out, making it the safest spot in Bacalar for toddlers to wade.

Morning / 3h

Private Pontoon Boat Tour

A relaxing private boat ride across the lagoon to visit landmarks like Cenote Esmeralda and the Pirates Channel. Toddler note: Booking a private pontoon guarantees a shaded canopy and a flexible schedule tailored to nap times.

Midday / 1-2h

Canal de los Piratas

A shallow, sandy-bottom channel where the water glows bright turquoise and the sand is rich in sulfur. Toddler note: The knee-deep, warm water acts as a giant natural baby pool for stress-free splashing.

Evening / 1-2h

Parque Central Bacalar

The vibrant town square featuring a colorful playground, local street food vendors, and evening gazebo music. Toddler note: Kids will love the playground swings while parents sample local marquesitas (crispy sweet crepes).

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Bacalar

A bustling indoor market filled with fresh tropical fruits, handmade crafts, and cheap, delicious breakfast empanadas. Toddler note: Pop in early to grab fresh bananas or sweet mangoes for healthy, toddler-approved day snacks.

Sunset / 1-2h

Bird Island (Isla de los Pájaros)

A protected mangrove island in the lagoon that serves as a sanctuary for exotic parrots, herons, and egrets. Toddler note: Pointing out the nesting birds from the quiet drift of a boat is a mesmerizing and calming activity.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Playita

Savor fresh fish tacos, wood-fired pizzas, and tropical drinks under a massive, beautiful banyan tree right next to the lagoon dock.

Mango y Chile

Feast on delicious plant-based burgers, loaded hot dogs, and creamy vegan milkshakes on an elevated deck overlooking the water.

El Manatí Bacalar

Order the hearty chilaquiles and fresh green juices in a whimsical, jungle-like backyard garden that doubles as an art gallery.

Pizzeria Bertilla

Indulge in authentic, family-friendly Italian thin-crust pizzas and homemade pasta served in a cozy, welcoming garden setting.

Macario

Enjoy elevated modern Mexican cuisine, like slow-cooked cochinita pibil, in a stylish yet highly welcoming open-air courtyard.
