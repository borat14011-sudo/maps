What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Retiro Park

Row wooden boats on the grand lake and let kids run free along the shaded paths. The playground near the Ibiza gate is perfect for toddlers to burn off energy.

Morning / 1.5-2h

Royal Palace of Madrid

Marvel at the grand armor room and royal carriages that will fascinate older kids. Stroller access is available, but using a baby carrier is easier for navigating the grand staircases.

Afternoon / 2h

Madrid Río Park

This massive riverside park features creative eco-playgrounds, water jets to cool off in summer, and giant slides. Toddlers will love the dedicated soft-play climbing areas.

Midday / 1-1.5h

San Miguel Market

A historic iron-and-glass market packed with colorful tapas, fresh fruit cups, and easy-to-eat finger foods. Grab a cone of jamón ibérico for an easy, toddler-approved snack.

Evening / 1h

Temple of Debod

Explore an authentic ancient Egyptian temple rebuilt in a park with stunning sunset views over the city. The surrounding grassy lawns are ideal for toddlers to stretch their legs and run around.

Morning / 3-4h

Faunia

A beautifully designed biological park where kids can walk through different ecosystems, from polar regions to tropical rainforests. It is highly stroller-friendly and very interactive for toddlers.

Afternoon / 1h

Plaza Mayor

Madrid's grand central square is completely pedestrianized, making it a safe place for kids to run around while parents enjoy a coffee. Street performers and bubble artists often entertain toddlers here.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Casa de Campo Cable Car

Glide high above the city skyline in a cable car that takes you to Madrid's largest public park. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the window views, and there is a great playground at the destination.

Morning / 2h

National Museum of Natural Sciences

See massive dinosaur skeletons, taxidermy animals, and interactive biodiversity displays. It is a highly engaging indoor option that is fully accessible for strollers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chocolatería San Ginés

Order the classic, freshly fried churros with thick hot chocolate for dipping, a guaranteed hit for all ages.

Lateral Plaza de Santa Ana

Get the mini-burgers, croquetas, and patatas bravas in a lively, family-friendly outdoor plaza setting.

Casa Julio

Feast on some of the city's most famous, creamy croquetas with kid-friendly fillings like cheese and ham.

Taberna El Madroño

Try the traditional, easy-to-share montaditos and classic Spanish omelet in a historic, cozy setting.

Federal Café Conde de Barajas

Enjoy a relaxed family brunch with fluffy pancakes, fresh juices, and plenty of space for high chairs.

👨‍👩‍👧 Spaniards dine late, so plan lunch around 2:00 PM and keep snacks handy before restaurants reopen for dinner at 8:30 PM. Since many historic metro stations lack elevators, bringing a lightweight, foldable stroller or baby carrier is essential.
