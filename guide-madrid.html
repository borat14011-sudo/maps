Madrid

Madrid dazzles with its grand boulevards, world-class art, and sun-drenched plazas, offering a vibrant yet relaxed backdrop for luxury family travel. The Prado Museum’s stroller-friendly galleries let you admire Goya and Velázquez without stress, while Retiro Park’s calm lake and shaded paths provide perfect infant afternoon escapes. At the Royal Palace, skip the steep stairs and enjoy the expansive courtyards. For your baby, the city’s efficient elevators and cobblestone-friendly stroller tires make exploration seamless.
🚕 Getting there: Madrid-Barajas Airport to the central hotel strip is a 25-minute drive; book a private transfer with an infant car seat and boot space for your stroller (e.g., Welcome Pickups or Blacklane) for door-to-door comfort.

What to do

2h

Royal Palace of Madrid

Stroll through the grand courtyards and opulent state rooms—stroller-accessible ground floor and elevators. The shaded gardens offer a calm spot if the interior gets too busy. Early morning 9-11am for fewer crowds.

2-3h

Prado Museum

World-class art collection with wide, stroller-friendly corridors and a dedicated family visit route. Skip the loud audio guides—use the quiet morning hours (10am-12pm) for a serene experience.

2-3h

Retiro Park

Expansive green space with smooth paths ideal for stroller walks, a boating lake (calm water, no loud rides), and shaded playgrounds. The Palacio de Cristal offers a cool indoor break.

1h

Plaza Mayor

Historic arcaded square with flat cobblestones—easy for strollers. Grab a seat at one of the outdoor cafés (quiet midday) and watch the street performers. Avoid the crowded evening hours.

1h

Temple of Debod

An ancient Egyptian temple with a peaceful grassy area and gentle ramps. Perfect sunset spot but avoid peak heat—visit late afternoon. Stroller-friendly paths around the small lake.

1-1.5h

Mercado de San Miguel

A vibrant glass market with tapas stalls—early afternoon is quieter than evening. Stroller accessible but narrow aisles; carry baby in a carrier for easier maneuvering. Highchairs available at some counters.

1.5-2h

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

Tour the legendary Real Madrid stadium—elevators and ramps throughout, baby-friendly areas. The museum is stroller-friendly and not too loud. Avoid match days for calm visits.

30min-1h

Plaza de Cibeles

Iconic fountain and palace with wide sidewalks—perfect for a stroller photo stop. The nearby Cibeles Palace has a rooftop terrace (elevator access) with city views. Morning light is best.

1-2h

Gran Vía

Madrid’s main shopping thoroughfare with wide, flat pavements and plenty of shade from buildings. Great for a stroller stroll past grand architecture. Stop at a café for a break.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sobrino de Botín

World’s oldest restaurant—famous for roast suckling pig and lamb. Highchairs available, early seating (1pm) is quieter, and the ground floor is stroller-friendly. No age restrictions.

Casa Lucio

Classic Castilian cuisine with a signature huevos rotos (broken eggs with fries and ham). Outdoor terrace in summer, highchairs provided. Reserve early to avoid noise.

La Mi Venta

Authentic tapas bar with a baby-friendly attitude—highchairs and a calm corner. Try the croquetas and jamón. Outdoor seating on a quiet plaza. Ideal for lunch.

El Club Allard

Two Michelin-starred restaurant offering a tasting menu—surprisingly welcoming for families if you request the early seating (1:30pm). They provide a highchair and can adjust dishes for baby. Quiet, elegant atmosphere.

Casa Revuelta

Famous for bacalao (salt cod) fritters and a lively but family-friendly vibe. Highchairs and a small back room that’s quieter. Come early for lunch to avoid the rush.
