San Salvador

Nestled beneath a towering volcano, San Salvador blends rich cultural heritage with lush, accessible nature. Families can explore colorful historic plazas, run through botanical gardens, and savor delicious local food. It is a surprisingly welcoming capital where tropical warmth meets vibrant Salvadoran hospitality.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 minutes by pre-arranged private shuttle or registered taxi to hotels in the San Benito or Escalón neighborhoods

What to do

Morning / 2h

Parque Nacional El Boquerón

Peer into a massive volcanic crater along easy forested trails. The paved walkways are stroller-friendly, and toddlers will love spotting colorful wild flowers and butterflies.

Afternoon / 2h

Plaza Libertad & Historic Center

Marvel at the Metropolitan Cathedral and watch street performers in this lively plaza. Toddlers will enjoy chasing pigeons and listening to the local marimba bands playing in the open air.

Morning / 3h

Tin Marín Museo de los Niños

A hands-on children's museum featuring a real airplane, a mock supermarket, and a butterfly house. It is completely interactive, meaning toddlers can touch, climb, and play to their heart's content.

Morning / 2h

Jardín Botánico La Laguna

Built inside an extinct volcanic crater, this lush garden features calm ponds filled with massive koi fish and turtles. Toddlers will love feeding the fish and walking the shaded, easy pathways.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Mercado de Artesanías de San Salvador

Wander through colorful stalls packed with hand-painted wooden toys, miniature hammocks, and bright textiles. It's a relaxed market where toddlers can pick out a small souvenir without the overwhelming crowds.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Saburo Hirao

A peaceful, Japanese-inspired park with multi-level playgrounds, bamboo forests, and a small natural history museum. Toddlers will adore the open green spaces and the mini-train ride that loops around the park.

Afternoon / 1h

Iglesia El Rosario

Step inside this architectural wonder where rainbows of stained glass illuminate the concrete interior. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the shifting colors of light dancing on the floor.

Afternoon to Evening / 3h

Planes de Renderos & Balboa Park

Head up the mountain for cool breezes, panoramic city views, and lush pine trails. Toddlers can burn off energy at the stone-carved playgrounds while parents sample the country's best pupusas nearby.

Morning / 2h

Eco-Parque El Espino

A serene ecological park on the slopes of the volcano offering easy, shaded hikes and a wooden lookout tower. Toddlers can easily manage the short flat loops under the canopy of coffee plantations.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pupusería La Única

Try their famous pupusas de queso con loroco (cheese and edible flower buds) served with fresh, mild curtido.

Cadejo Brewing Company

Enjoy artisanal burgers and craft root beer in a spacious, highly family-friendly garden terrace setting.

Rustico Bistro

Treat the family to gourmet, kid-approved burgers with melted cheese skirts in a casual, welcoming atmosphere.

El Sopón Típico

Order the traditional Sopa de Gallina India (rich chicken soup), a comforting dish that kids and toddlers love.

Pastelería Shaw

Indulge in artisanal Salvadoran chocolates, fruit tarts, and creamy ice cream at this beloved historic bakery.

👨‍👩‍👧 Use Uber for safe, reliable, and air-conditioned point-to-point travel around the city with young kids. Always carry small cash denominations in US Dollars for market vendors and pupuserías, as many local spots do not accept credit cards.
