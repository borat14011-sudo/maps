What to do

Late afternoon after 4 PM, when the sun softens and the beach is less crowded.

Playa de San Lorenzo

Gijón's main beach: a wide, sandy stretch with a long, flat promenade perfect for strollers. The water is typically calm with gentle waves, and there's shade near the Paseo de Begoña walls. Bring a UV umbrella for the baby.

Morning, 10:30 AM – 12:30 PM, before the heat peaks.

Jardín Botánico Atlántico

A huge botanical garden with paved paths winding through Atlantic forests and ponds. Shade is abundant, strollers roll easily, and there's a calm indoor greenhouse with AC for a cool break. Baby can watch ducks and native plants.

Early morning (9–11 AM) for calmest water and fewer people.

Playa de Toró (Llanes)

A tiny, sheltered cove with golden sand and very calm, shallow water—ideal for splashing with a baby. Access is via a short, gentle ramp from the parking area, so strollers are fine. No waves, just a family haven.

Late afternoon (5–7 PM) for golden light and lower UV.

Paseo de la Playa de Ballota (Llanes)

A beautiful seaside walkway along the Ballota beach cliffs, fully paved and stroller-friendly. Benches with shade overlook the ocean, and there’s a small sandy beach below. Perfect for a quiet stroll with baby in the cool breeze.

Mid-morning, 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM, before lunch rush.

Mercado de Jovellanos (Gijón)

Gijón's covered market: cool, air-conditioned, and packed with local cheeses, cider, and produce. Strollers easily navigate the wide aisles. Buy fresh fruit for baby and let her watch the busy fish counters—low noise, no crowds early.

Midday (12–2 PM) to escape the heat and afternoon crowds.

Acuario de Gijón

A modern aquarium with a huge shark tunnel and touch pools. Fully air-conditioned, stroller accessible throughout, and generally quiet enough for a baby. The dim lighting and fish movement often mesmerize little ones.

Late morning (11 AM – 1 PM) before the playground fills up.

Parque de Los Andes (Gijón)

A large, shady park with wide gravel and paved paths perfect for strollers. There's a calm pond with ducks, a playground for when Mary Claire is older, and plenty of benches under tree cover. Great for a relaxed picnic.

Early morning (9:30–11:30 AM) for the quietest experience.

Playa de Poo (Llanes)

A small, peaceful beach between cliffs, known for its calm, crystal-clear water. The sand is soft, and the beach is reached by a short, flat path from the roadside parking—no steps. Ideal for baby’s first beach day.

Morning or late afternoon, 1 hour round-trip at a slow pace.

Ruta del Río de la Piedad (Llanes)

An easy, flat riverside trail in the heart of Llanes, shaded by trees and paved for strollers. It passes the old bridge and ends at the marina. Perfect for a short, cool walk with baby in the carrier or stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sidrería La Galana (Gijón)

A beloved cider house serving fabada asturiana and grilled meats. They have high chairs, a quiet back room, and the staff are used to babies. Order the fabada—it's hearty but mild for parents—and a bottle of natural cider for you.

El Corzo (Llanes)

A bright, modern restaurant with a calm atmosphere and a terrace partially shaded. They offer a children's menu and high chairs. Try the arroz con leche (rice pudding) and the catch of the day. Noise level is low during lunch.

Casa de Comidas El Cher (Gijón)

A cozy, family-run spot with simple Asturian cuisine. They have a booth near the window where a stroller can fit, and the low noise makes it easy for baby to nap. Their cachopo and homemade cheesecake are crowd-pleasers.

La Mar de Bien (Gijón)

Right on the San Lorenzo promenade, this seafood place has a large covered outdoor terrace with ocean breeze and shade. High chairs available, and the clatter of waves drowns out baby fuss. Order the grilled octopus and a bowl of seafood soup.

Restaurante El Faro (Llanes)

Overlooking the port, this restaurant offers a calm upstairs dining room with high chairs and a wide menu. Their signature dish is the arroz de pedrín (a local rice with lobster). Ask for a table near the window—great views, low noise.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

NH Gijón

Modern hotel in central Gijón with spacious family rooms, blackout curtains, and a pool (shallow children's section). Cribs available on request. The beach is a 5-minute walk via the flat promenade, and the AC is strong—great for naps.

Hotel Puerto de Llanes

Four-star hotel steps from the beach and marina. They provide free cribs, have a heated outdoor pool, and offer a family room with separate sitting area. Soundproofed windows keep noise low, and the breakfast buffet includes baby-friendly options.

Hotel Gran Costa Verde (Llanes)

A resort-style hotel with a large outdoor pool, direct beach access, and lush gardens with shaded paths for strollers. Cribs are standard, and rooms have blackout curtains. The calm, family-oriented vibe means you’ll see many other babies.

👨‍👩‍👧 Formula and baby food are available at supermarkets like Alimerka and Mercadona in Gijón and Llanes; pharmacies (farmacias) also sell liquid formula and pediatric supplies. Most coastal promenades are flat and paved, but old-town streets (especially in Llanes) can have cobblestones—stick to the beachfront paths for easy stroller movement. For medical needs, the Hospital de Cabueñes in Gijón has a pediatric ER, and Llanes has a Centro de Salud with English-speaking staff (call ahead).
