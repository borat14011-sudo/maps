Asturias

← back to travel board
Asturias presents an enchanting blend of dramatic limestone cliffs, cider orchards, and quiet luxury far removed from the crowded southern coasts of Spain. Here, the majestic Picos de Europa mountains sweep directly down to meet the pristine Cantabrian Sea, framing historic fishing villages and elegant maritime towns. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the stroller-ready paved path of the historic Paseo de San Pedro in Llanes offers an effortless way to soak in panoramic clifftop ocean views. It is a region where gourmet Michelin-starred dining meets tranquil, sheltered coves, providing a deeply relaxing yet sophisticated escape for a discerning young family.
🚕 Getting there: From Asturias Airport (OVD) to the luxury coastal strip in Gijón takes approximately 35 minutes, while reaching Llanes takes 1 hour and 15 minutes. The most seamless family option is a pre-arranged private luxury Mercedes V-Class transfer, equipped with a sanitized infant car seat, providing ample cabin space for your luggage, stroller, and baby gear.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Playa de San Lorenzo

Stroll along the iconic 2.5-kilometer paved promenade 'El Muro' wrapping around this gorgeous golden crescent beach. It features completely flat, stroller-friendly paths with ocean breezes, perfect for Mary Claire’s morning nap.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:30 PM

Jardín Botánico Atlántico

Explore over 30,000 plants in this sprawling, lush botanical garden located on the edge of Gijón. Wide paved and hard-packed dirt pathways are shaded by ancient 400-year-old oak trees, offering a cool, serene oasis for a luxury family walk.

Late Afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM

Paseo de San Pedro

A breathtaking clifftop coastal walk in Llanes covered in a lush carpet of green grass with a paved pedestrian sidewalk. Stroller-accessible and quiet, it delivers dramatic views of the sea crashing against the cliffs without any strenuous climbing.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Acuario de Gijón (Bioparc)

A world-class aquarium showcasing marine life from the Cantabrian Sea to the Caribbean. The indoor facility is climate-controlled and fully stroller-accessible, offering quiet, dimly lit, visually stimulating tunnels that will captivate Mary Claire.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Playa de Barro

Known as one of Asturias' most sheltered beaches, this cove is protected from strong waves by two rocky islets, creating a calm, lake-like lagoon. It is ideal for dipping Mary Claire’s feet in the crystal-clear water safely away from rough currents.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Museo del Jurásico de Asturias (MUJA)

Perched high on the Colunga coastline, this dinosaur museum is shaped like a giant three-toed footprint. The outdoor gardens feature life-sized dinosaur replicas and stroller-friendly pathways, providing gorgeous sea views for parents and visual fun for baby.

Evening sunset walk 6:30 PM - 7:30 PM

Playa de Toró

A spectacular beach in Llanes famous for its unique karst rock formations emerging from the sand. A paved overlook trail allows you to enjoy the dramatic coastal topography effortlessly with a luxury stroller without getting wheels stuck in the sand.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM

Centro Niemeyer

An architectural masterpiece designed by Oscar Niemeyer in nearby Avilés, featuring sleek, futuristic white curves. The vast, flat pedestrian plaza is completely stroller-accessible, offering a serene, ultra-modern environment for a leisurely walk.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Santuario de Covadonga

A breathtaking historic shrine nestled in a mountain waterfall cave within the Picos de Europa foothills. Fully paved ramps lead to the striking pink-limestone Basilica, offering fresh mountain air and a majestic backdrop for memorable family photos.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Auga

Enjoy Michelin-starred seafood at Gijón’s elegant marina, where floor-to-ceiling windows overlook luxury yachts. They offer spacious tables easily accommodating a stroller and early evening reservations starting at 8:00 PM.

La Salgar

Located next to the Pueblo de Asturias in Gijón, this light-filled glass pavilion offers refined Asturian gastronomy. The spacious garden setting is peaceful, highchairs are available, and the quiet atmosphere is highly welcoming to infants.

Restaurante El Retiro

A Michelin-starred gem hidden in Pancar, just minutes from Llanes, serving exquisite local dishes. The staff is exceptionally accommodating to families, and booking an early lunch seating provides a relaxed, uncrowded fine-dining experience.

Casa Gerardo

This legendary culinary institution in Prendes has been perfecting Asturian gastronomy since 1882. The spacious, elegant dining rooms provide plenty of room for a high-end stroller, making it the perfect spot to enjoy their famous creamy Fabada at lunch.

Restaurante El Balamu

Perched directly over the fishing port of Llanes, this chic restaurant serves incredibly fresh fish caught daily. The panoramic harbor windows keep baby entertained, the outdoor terrace is breezy, and the casual luxury vibe is very infant-friendly.

A stunning 20th-century palacete sitting directly on the beachfront promenade of Ribadesella. It features spacious rooms with blackout curtains, immediate flat stroller access to the beach, and luxury cribs provided on request.

$300-400/night

An ultra-luxury valley resort in Cofiño featuring breathtaking views of the Picos de Europa. It offers spacious, private mountain-view suites with blackout drapes, customized infant amenities, and a magnificent indoor wellness pool.
