Skiathos

← back to travel board
Skiathos welcomes families with its pine-scented breeze, emerald waters, and over sixty easily accessible golden beaches. Children can splash safely in the shallow, crystalline shallows of Koukounaries while parents relax under the shade of ancient pine forests. The vibrant town offers cobblestone paths to explore, promising a perfect blend of Greek island charm and effortless family comfort.
🚕 Getting there: ~10-15 min taxi or pre-booked shuttle to Skiathos Town and nearby southern beach hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Koukounaries Beach

Famous for its fine golden sand and extremely shallow, calm waters, this pine-fringed bay is a paradise for little swimmers. Toddler note: The nearby wetland has swans and ducks that toddlers love feeding.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Bourtzi Fortress

A small, pine-covered peninsula dividing the port, offering a breezy stroll to a Venetian fortress ruin with wide, stroller-friendly paths. Toddler note: There is a lovely shaded playground and cafe right on the islet.

Evening / 2h

Skiathos Town Open-Air Cinema

Enjoy a magical night under the stars watching family-friendly movies or Mamma Mia! with the family. Toddler note: Bring a light jacket and arrive early to snag cozy front-row seating.

Morning / 1-2h

Skiathos Strofilia Wetland

Located just behind Koukounaries, this protected lagoon is great for a stroller-friendly nature walk to spot migratory birds. Toddler note: Look out for the resident turtles sunbathing on the logs!

Morning / 2-3h

Boat Trip to Lalaria Beach and Blue Caves

Board a gentle, spacious glass-bottom boat from the old harbor to marvel at the dramatic white cliffs and sea caves. Toddler note: Choose a short 2-hour morning tour to avoid midday heat and naptime disruptions.

Morning / 30-45 min

Papadiamantis House Museum

Step inside the preserved 19th-century home of one of Greece's most famous writers, located right in the heart of town. Toddler note: It's a very short, highly visual visit that won't test short toddler attention spans.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Banana Beach

This lively beach offers incredibly soft sand and clear waters, with a quieter side that is perfect for families. Toddler note: Rent an umbrella early, as the sand can get quite hot for tiny feet.

Morning / 2h

Skiathos Dog Shelter

Located up in the hills, families can volunteer to take friendly, rescued dogs for a scenic walk along peaceful mountain paths. Toddler note: Call ahead to ensure they have ultra-gentle, older dogs suitable for walking with young kids.

Afternoon / 3h

Agia Paraskevi Beach

A beautifully calm beach with sweet, golden sand and plenty of space for sandcastles and shallow wading. Toddler note: The beach is lined with family tavernas that feature grassy lawns and small play areas within eyesight of tables.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Windmill Restaurant

Treat the family to upscale Mediterranean dining in a restored 1880s windmill with breathtaking panoramic views.

Marmita

Dine on fresh, modern Greek cuisine in a quiet, beautifully shaded courtyard garden that keeps toddlers cool and contained.

Taverna Sklithri

Eat ultra-fresh seafood with your feet literally in the sand at this charming, rustic beachside taverna.

En Plo

Grab a harbor-front table for incredible breakfasts and fluffy Greek pancakes while watching the boats glide in.

Borzoi

Enjoy delicious wood-fired pizzas and Mediterranean small plates in a historic courtyard with plenty of space for kids to wiggle.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for accommodations along the flat southern coast, like Megali Ammos or Koukounaries, to ensure easy, stroller-friendly access to the beach. Avoid bringing bulky strollers to the cobblestone steps of upper Skiathos Town; a lightweight baby carrier is much easier for navigating the town's charming but steep alleys.
