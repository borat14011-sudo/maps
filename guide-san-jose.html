San José

← back to travel board
San José blends vibrant Central American culture with lush green escapes, making it an exciting launching pad for family adventures. Wander through historic plazas, encounter colorful butterflies, and taste sweet tropical fruits amidst the city's bustling, friendly streets. It is a surprisingly accessible urban playground where toddlers and parents alike can discover Costa Rica's spirit of Pura Vida.
🚕 Getting there: ~30-40 min taxi or pre-arranged private shuttle from Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO) to downtown San José hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Museo de los Niños

Housed in a castle-like former penitentiary, this interactive children's museum offers hands-on science and cultural exhibits. Toddlers will love the dedicated soft play areas and the giant bubble-making station.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Parque Metropolitano La Sabana

Known as the lungs of San José, this massive park features a large lake, playgrounds, and wide paved paths. It is the perfect spot for toddlers to run around freely and watch locals play soccer.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

A bustling indoor market filled with vibrant sights, local crafts, and exotic fruits. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the colorful flower stalls, though keeping them in a carrier is best due to tight, busy aisles.

Morning / 1-2h

Spirogyra Butterfly Garden

A peaceful oasis right in the city where kids can walk among hundreds of colorful butterflies. Toddlers can easily walk the short, flat paths and watch butterflies hatch in the nursery.

Afternoon / 1h

Teatro Nacional de Costa Rica

A stunning architectural masterpiece with opulent gold-leaf interiors and beautiful ceiling frescoes. Young kids can marvel at the marble statues, and parents can enjoy a quick treat at the elegant cafe.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo del Oro Precolombino

Located beneath the Plaza de la Cultura, this subterranean museum showcases glittering ancient gold artifacts. Toddlers will be fascinated by the shiny objects, and the spacious plaza above is perfect for chasing pigeons afterward.

Full Day / 4-6h

Parque Diversiones

A vibrant amusement park featuring a reconstructed traditional Costa Rican village and toddler-friendly rides. Young children will love the gentle carousel and train ride through the miniature town.

Afternoon / 1h

Parque Francia

A charming, shady neighborhood park in the trendy Barrio Escalante district. It is a fantastic spot to grab an ice cream, relax on the grass, and let toddlers watch friendly local dogs play.

Afternoon / 2h

Museo del Jade

This modern museum holds the world's largest collection of pre-Columbian jade artifacts with interactive digital screens. The wide, stroller-friendly ramps make navigating between floors with a toddler incredibly easy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Grano de Oro

Get the Costa Rican coffee-crusted beef tenderloin or a slice of their famous signature coconut pie in the gorgeous garden courtyard.

Spoon

Get the Torta Chilena, a delicious multi-layered dulce de leche cake that kids absolutely adore.

La Criollita

Order the traditional Casado plate with rice, beans, plantains, and mild stewed beef for a family-friendly taste of authentic Costa Rica.

Apotecario

Get the artisanal sourdough pizzas and fresh kombucha in their spacious, family-friendly backyard garden patio.

Soda Tapia

Order the famous Fruit Salad with Ice Cream or a classic Gallo Pinto at this iconic, highly casual diner that has been welcoming families for decades.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always travel with a sturdy, compact stroller for parks but keep a baby carrier handy, as city sidewalks can be uneven or crowded. Utilize Uber for safe, easy, and affordable point-to-point transportation around the city with young kids to avoid navigating heavy traffic.
