San José

← back to travel board
San José greets sophisticated families with a surprising blend of historic European-style architecture, contemporary culinary scenes, and majestic mountain backdrops. The city's upscale districts like Escazú and Barrio Amón offer a peaceful, secure oasis away from the bustling downtown core. For a seamless experience with baby Mary Claire, the pedestrian-only promenade of Avenida Escazú provides an ultra-smooth, stroller-ready environment lined with high-end boutiques and shady palms. Here, the temperate highland climate ensures comfortable outdoor strolls without the intense coastal heat, making it an ideal urban retreat for discerning parents.
🚕 Getting there: The drive from Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO) to the luxury hotel district in Escazú or Belén takes approximately 25 to 35 minutes depending on traffic. The most comfortable family option is a pre-arranged private Mercedes-Benz SUV transfer through 'Costa Rica Premium Transfers,' which guarantees a sanitized, rear-facing Maxi-Cosi infant car seat and ample cargo space for your upscale stroller system.

What to do

Mid-morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 1.5h

Teatro Nacional de Costa Rica

Costa Rica's architectural crown jewel features a lavish lobby and plush marble details. Skip the echoing main theater performances to avoid startling the baby, and instead stroll through the wide, stroller-accessible corridors before enjoying a quiet espresso at the gourmet Alma de Café inside.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Museo del Oro Precolombino

Located beneath the Plaza de la Cultura, this modern, subterranean museum is fully climate-controlled and features smooth, flat concrete floors and elevators perfect for a luxury stroller. The sparkling gold artifacts are highly visual for Mary Claire in a quiet, hushed environment.

Early morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM / 1.5h

Parque Metropolitano La Sabana

San José's version of Central Park offers a massive green escape with wide, paved stroller loops around a central lake. Towering eucalyptus trees provide excellent shade spots to spread a blanket for baby's tummy time while watching local families jog past.

Morning tour 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Doka Estate Costa Rica

A short, scenic drive up the slopes of Poás Volcano leads to this historic coffee plantation. The gentle walking paths through the shaded coffee fields are perfect for a baby carrier, offering fresh, cool mountain air and beautiful, tranquil garden views.

Afternoon 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM / 1.5h

Museo del Jade

This state-of-the-art museum houses the world's largest collection of pre-Columbian jade. It features exceptionally smooth, modern architectural ramps and spacious elevators, ensuring a completely seamless, crowd-free stroller walk through beautifully dim, soothing galleries.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center

Located just outside the city center, this premier rescue facility features flat, shaded concrete trails winding through a lush tropical canopy. Mary Claire can quietly observe colorful macaws, sloths, and spider monkeys in a peaceful, natural setting.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 1h

Spirogyra Butterfly Garden

Tucked away in a quiet valley near Barrio Amón, this peaceful urban oasis is home to hundreds of fluttering, colorful butterflies. The soft humid air and gentle hum of the nearby river make it an incredibly soothing sensory experience for an infant.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM / 1h

Parque Francia

The heartbeat of the trendy, upscale Barrio Escalante district, this quiet, European-style pocket park is surrounded by mature trees. It is the perfect place to sit on a park bench, enjoy an artisan pastry, and let Mary Claire watch local dogs play in a gentle, low-noise environment.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM / 2h

Avenida Escazú

This ultra-modern, high-end lifestyle center is completely pedestrianized, paved, and flat, offering the city's most comfortable luxury stroller stroll. Take advantage of shaded outdoor seating, beautiful water fountains, and upscale baby-changing facilities.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante Grano de Oro

Fine dining in an exquisite, open-air Victorian inner courtyard. The soothing sound of the central stone fountain elegantly masks baby babbles, and the staff warmly provides high-quality highchairs and early 6:00 PM dinner reservations.

Sikwa

Acclaimed contemporary restaurant celebrating indigenous Costa Rican ingredients. The spacious, semi-open-air garden design offers excellent air circulation, wide seating arrangements that accommodate strollers, and a welcoming, relaxed early evening vibe.

La Divina Comida

Located in upscale Avenida Escazú, this chic establishment serves exceptional Peruvian-Costa Rican fusion. Parents can sit on the spacious, stroller-friendly outdoor terrace where the ambient outdoor sounds make an occasional infant cry completely unnoticeable.

Cafeoteca

An elegant coffee boutique in Barrio Escalante featuring specialty regional pours. The beautiful, quiet backyard garden patio is filled with lush ferns and comfortable seating, making it incredibly welcoming for nursing mothers and sleepy infants.

Restaurante Silvestre

Sophisticated contemporary dining located in a beautifully restored historic Barrio Amón townhouse. Book an early evening table in one of their semi-private, high-ceilinged dining rooms to enjoy a world-class tasting menu with Mary Claire resting comfortably beside you.
