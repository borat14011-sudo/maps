Mexico City

← back to travel board
Mexico City is a high-altitude wonderland where leafy neighborhoods meet ancient ruins and world-class museums. Families will love the pedestrian-friendly plazas, whimsical castles, and sprawling green spaces that offer endless room to run. It is a sensory-rich playground where every corner reveals a new treat or colorful surprise for young travelers.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 to 45 minutes by authorized airport taxi purchased inside the terminal to central family-friendly neighborhoods like Polanco, Roma, or Condesa.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Chapultepec Park and Castle

Climb up to a real hilltop castle with stunning city views, then stroll through the massive park's winding paths. Toddlers will love feeding the ducks at the lake and watching the colorful street performers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Xochimilco Canal Boat Ride

Float along historic canals in a brightly colored wooden boat while listening to live mariachi music. Toddlers are easily entertained by the passing puppet-adorned boats and sweet treats sold water-side.

Morning / 3h

Papalote Museo del Niño

A world-class interactive children's museum featuring giant climbing structures, sensory exhibits, and an IMAX theater. It has a dedicated safe-play zone specifically tailored for toddlers to touch and explore.

Morning / 4-5h

Teotihuacan Pyramids

Marvel at the colossal Pyramids of the Sun and Moon in this ancient holy city. While climbing is restricted now, the wide dirt pathways are great for stroller-pushing and active toddlers to safely burn off energy.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque Lincoln

A beautifully manicured park in Polanco featuring a dedicated aviary, toy boat sailing ponds, and excellent modern playgrounds. Toddlers will spend hours in the safe, enclosed play area while parents enjoy a coffee.

Lunchtime / 2h

Mercado de Coyoacán

Immerse your family in a vibrant indoor market filled with colorful toys, fresh tropical fruits, and incredible tostadas. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the hanging piñatas and the gentle bustle of local shoppers.

Morning / 2-3h

Acuario Michin CDMX

Walk through spectacular underwater tunnels, touch friendly stingrays, and view penguins up close at this massive indoor aquarium. Its air-conditioned, stroller-friendly pathways make it an easy escape on rainy days.

Morning / 2-3h

National Museum of Anthropology

Discover towering Aztec stone carvings, including the famous Sun Stone, in a stunning architectural marvel. The giant outdoor courtyard waterfall is a sensory hit for toddlers, and the surrounding gardens are perfect for quick tantrums.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Parque México and Parque España

Wander through lush, jungle-like pathways, watch dozens of friendly dogs socializing, and enjoy the whimsically designed play zones. Toddlers can safely run wild on the soft-turf playgrounds under the shade of giant palms.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Cardenal

Order the hot chocolate frothed tableside and the freshly baked sweet conchas served with rich clotted cream.

Churrería El Moro

Order the classic, freshly-fried churros dusted with cinnamon sugar alongside a cup of sweet Spanish-style dipping chocolate.

Lardo

Grab a seat at this casual, plant-filled spot and order the house-made pastries, wood-fired pizzas, and fresh orange juice.

El Califa

Order the mild, melt-in-your-mouth pastor tacos on fresh flour tortillas with a side of melted cheese gringas that kids love.

Contramar

Order the iconic, split-marinated red and green grilled whole fish (pescado a la talla) and the tuna tostadas while kids munch on sweet potato fries.
