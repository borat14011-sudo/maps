Paros

← back to travel board
Paros welcomes families with its gentle Aegean breezes, shallow azure waters, and car-free village labyrinths. It strikes the perfect balance between Cycladic charm and effortless convenience, making it incredibly welcoming for little travelers. From building castles on wind-sheltered sands to exploring quiet paved alleys, it is a playground for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or rental car drive from Paros National Airport (PAS) to Naoussa or Parikia hotel zones.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Kolymbithres Beach

Famous for its bizarre, smooth granite rock formations and calm, shallow coves that mimic natural wading pools. Toddlers will love splashing safely in the crystal-clear water without worrying about waves.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Paros Park (Environmental and Cultural Park)

Walk the easy walking paths that lead to a beautiful lighthouse and wild beaches, ending at the sandy Monastiri Beach. The flat pathways near the entrance are stroller-accessible, and there is plenty of space for toddlers to safely roam.

Morning / 1-2h

Exploring Naoussa Harbor

Wander the iconic, Venetian-castle-guarded fishing harbor when it's quiet in the morning before the crowds arrive. Toddlers will love spotting the colorful blue-and-white fishing boats and watching octopus tentacles drying in the sun.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Golden Beach (Chrissi Akti)

A vast expanse of soft, golden sand with exceptionally shallow entry, making it Paros's premier family beach. The wide shoreline is perfect for toddler sandcastle construction, and there are plenty of sun loungers for parents.

Morning / 1-2h

Butterfly Valley (Petaloudes)

A lush, shaded oasis filled with ivy-covered trees where thousands of Jersey Tiger moths gather during summer. The cool, shaded canopy offers a sensory, magical walk that is highly engaging for toddlers, though carriers are recommended over strollers due to steps.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Lefkes Village Exploration

Step back in time in Paros’s historic mountain village, featuring quiet, completely pedestrianized marble-paved alleys. Toddlers can safely toddle up and down the car-free lanes while parents admire the bright pink bougainvillea and blue doors.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Day Trip to Antiparos via Pounta Ferry

Take the ultra-short, 7-minute car ferry to the sleepy sister island of Antiparos for a slow-paced adventure. The boat ride itself is a thrilling, quick novelty for toddlers, leading to very calm, shallow beaches like Psaralyki.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Paros Clay Therapy at Kalogeros Beach

A unique, natural beach surrounded by red and green clay cliffs where you can paint yourselves with natural therapeutic mud. Toddlers will find painting their parents and themselves with 'mud paint' incredibly fun before washing off in the sea.

Morning / 1h

Frankish Castle (Parikia)

Explore this unique 13th-century castle built entirely from the ruins of ancient Greek temples, located right in the old town. While the climb is short, toddlers will be fascinated by the giant stone blocks protruding from the walls like a real-life puzzle.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sigi Ikona

Sit right by the harbor edge and enjoy fresh grilled octopus, kid-friendly handmade pasta, and traditional Greek salads.

Yemeni Taverna

Tucked into a quiet alley, this cozy tavern serves incredible slow-cooked lamb and local cheeses that are soft and toddler-approved.

Klarinos Tavern

A spacious, highly welcoming family tavern in the mountains serving legendary grilled meats and hand-cut fries that kids love.

Siparos Restaurant

A beautiful seaside spot offering elevated seafood where kids can safely play on the adjacent sand while parents finish their fresh grilled fish.

Ragoussis Bakery

The perfect pitstop for sweet and savory treats, featuring giant spanakopita (spinach pie), fresh ice cream, and traditional Greek biscuits.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a baby carrier instead of a stroller when visiting hilly, cobbled villages like Lefkes, as the historic stone streets are full of stairs. Always carry plenty of sunscreen and cash, as some beachside sunbed vendors and small village shops do not accept credit cards.
