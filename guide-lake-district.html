The Lake District enchants families with its shimmering bodies of water, emerald peaks, and storybook villages that inspired Beatrix Potter. Children can splash along shallow lakeshores, ride historic steam trains, and explore mystical castle ruins tucked into the woods. It is a playground of natural wonder where every winding path leads to a new outdoor adventure.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min drive via rental car from Manchester Airport to Windermere hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

The World of Beatrix Potter Attraction

Step into the magical world of Peter Rabbit and friends with immersive 3D displays. Toddlers will love meeting the life-sized characters and exploring the Peter Rabbit Garden.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Brockhole on Windermere

An outdoor lakeside adventure hub featuring a massive playground, archery, and kayak rentals. Toddlers can safely enjoy the indoor soft play area and the gentle woodland trails.

Morning / 1-2h

Lakeside and Haverthwaite Railway

Ride an authentic, vintage steam train through the scenic Leven Valley. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the sights, sounds, and gentle chugging of the historic locomotives.

Morning / 2-3h

Wray Castle

A gothic revival castle on the shores of Windermere where kids can dress up and build castles. Toddlers can safely wander the expansive, flat lakeside paths and lawn areas.

Morning / 1-2h

Keswick Market

Browse a lively, award-winning outdoor market filled with local crafts, hot food, and fresh pastries. It is easy to navigate with a stroller, and toddlers will love sampling the artisanal baked goods.

Morning / 2h

Aira Force Waterfall

Walk through ancient woodland to see a spectacular 65-foot waterfall plunging into a deep pool. The lower path is well-paved, making it manageable for sturdy toddler steps or carrier backpacks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Ullswater 'Steamers'

Cruise across England's most beautiful lake on a historic heritage vessel. The indoor cabins keep toddlers cozy and warm, while the outdoor decks offer breathtaking mountain views.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Lowther Castle & Gardens

Explore dramatic castle ruins and the massive 'Lost Castle' adventure playground hidden in the woods. Toddlers have their own dedicated, safe wooden play area with slides and swings.

Afternoon / 30m

Sarah Nelson's Grasmere Gingerbread Shop

Visit the tiny, historic 160-year-old cottage shop to try world-famous, freshly baked ginger biscuits. Toddlers will love the sweet aroma and the soft, chewy texture of this legendary local treat.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Drunken Duck Inn

Enjoy a hearty meal of slow-cooked Lakeland venison suet pudding in a cozy, historic pub setting.

Chesters by the River

Indulge in giant, freshly baked cinnamon buns and wood-fired flatbreads on a beautiful deck right beside the rushing river.

The Flying Pig Pub

Feast on classic, golden-brown fish and chips served with mushy peas in a highly welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere.

Baldry's Tea Room

Treat the family to vintage afternoon tea with their legendary, towering lemon meringue pie.

Fellpack

Warm up with their signature creative 'Fellpots', which are hearty, slow-cooked stews served with toasted sourdough.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack robust all-weather layers and a heavy-duty all-terrain stroller, as Lakeland weather shifts quickly and many scenic paths are gravelly. Buying a National Trust family pass beforehand will easily save you money on parking and entry fees across the region's best historical sites.
