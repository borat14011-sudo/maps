What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Kuhio Beach

Relax on the calm, walled-off section of Waikiki Beach where toddlers can safely splash in pool-like waters. The gentle surf makes it perfect for introducing little ones to the ocean.

Morning / 2h

Honolulu Zoo

Located right at the edge of Waikiki, this lush zoo features a fantastic Keiki Children's Zoo and a great playground for burning off extra energy. It is stroller-friendly with plenty of shade from giant banyan trees.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Waikiki Aquarium

A small, manageable aquarium right on the shoreline that won't overwhelm toddlers but showcases colorful Hawaiian reef fish and monk seals. The outdoor interactive exhibits are perfect for curious little hands.

Morning / 2-3h

Ho’omaluhia Botanical Garden

Walk through a lush, green rainforest paradise with dramatic mountain backdrops and a peaceful lake where kids can feed the fish. The paved paths are stroller-accessible, though watch out for occasional tropical showers.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

KCC Farmers Market

A vibrant Saturday morning market packed with local tropical fruits, fresh pastries, and family-friendly food stalls. Toddlers will love tasting sweet local bananas and watching the bustling, friendly crowd.

Morning / 3h

Bishop Museum

Hawaii's premier natural and cultural history museum features an incredible interactive Science Adventure Center where kids can crawl through a simulated volcano. The expansive, grassy lawn is also perfect for toddlers to run around safely.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Magic Island Lagoon

A beautiful, man-made peninsula with a protected, shallow lagoon that has absolutely no waves, making it the ultimate toddler wading pool. Parents can enjoy stunning views of Diamond Head while kids splash safely.

Morning / 2h

Manoa Falls Trail

An easy hike through a bamboo forest to a dramatic waterfall that feels like a real-life Jurassic Park adventure. Use a baby carrier rather than a stroller here, as the dirt path can get muddy and uneven.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Hawaii Children's Discovery Center

A fantastic indoor play museum where toddlers can role-play in a miniature town, pilot a pretend cargo ship, and explore sensory exhibits. It is the perfect air-conditioned refuge on a hot afternoon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Duke's Waikiki

Get the iconic, towering Hula Pie with chocolate fudge and macadamia nuts in a lively, ultra-casual beachfront setting.

Leonard's Bakery

Grab a box of warm, sugar-dusted malasadas that are soft, doughy, and absolutely loved by kids.

Helena's Hawaiian Food

Share traditional, kid-friendly Hawaiian plates like pipikaula short ribs and smoky kalua pig in an unassuming, welcoming neighborhood spot.

Rainbow Drive-In

Order a classic plate lunch featuring savory loco moco or barbecue beef with double scoops of rice and creamy macaroni salad.

Waiola Shave Ice

Try the ultra-fine shave ice topped with sweet condensed milk, azuki beans, and colorful tropical syrups.
