Zadar

← back to travel board
Zadar blends ancient Roman history with whimsical modern art installations right on the edge of the sparkling Adriatic Sea. Its compact, car-free Old Town peninsula makes it incredibly easy for families to explore without worrying about traffic. From the magical sounds of the waves to shaded parks, it is a coastal paradise designed for slow-paced family wandering.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or private transfer to the Old Town or Borik beach hotels

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

The Sea Organ (Morske Orgulje)

Listen to the sea play music through a series of underwater pipes carved into white stone steps. Toddlers will love sitting on the broad stairs watching the boats, but keep a close hand on them near the edge.

Sunset / 1h

Greeting to the Sun (Pozdrav Suncu)

This giant, solar-powered circular monument lit up with multi-colored lights mimics the solar system. Toddlers can safely run and chase the colorful light patterns as night falls.

Morning / 1-2h

The Roman Forum Ruins

An open-air archaeological park right in the middle of town where kids can roam freely among ancient columns and stones. It is completely pedestrianized, making it a stress-free spot for toddlers to burn off energy.

Midday / 1-2h

Queen Jelena Madijevka Park

A lush, elevated green oasis built on historical city bastions offering deep shade and winding pathways. There is a great children's playground here, perfect for a peaceful midday retreat from the sun.

Morning / 1h

Zadar Open-Air Market (Pijaca)

A vibrant daily market filled with color, smells, and friendly local vendors selling fresh fruits and pastries. It is an engaging sensory experience where you can grab fresh figs and berries for healthy toddler snacks.

Morning / 2-3h

Kolovare Beach

A popular public beach close to the Old Town featuring shallow waters, a playground, and nearby cafe bars. The pine trees provide excellent natural shade for a toddler's afternoon nap.

Midday / 1-2h

Museum of Illusions Zadar

An interactive indoor space filled with mirrors, holograms, and optical illusions. Toddlers will be delighted by the sensory playroom and the gravity-defying rooms.

Morning / 15 mins

Barkajoli Boat Ride

Take a short, traditional rowboat ride with the local 'barkajoli' rowers who have been crossing the harbor for centuries. It is cheap, quick, and just long enough to thrill a toddler without losing their attention.

Morning / half-day

Nin Lagoon (Queen's Beach)

A short 25-minute drive from Zadar, this long sandy beach features incredibly shallow, warm waters and healing mud. It is one of the absolute best beaches in Croatia for toddlers to safely splash and build sandcastles.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant Bruschetta

Get the homemade Dalmatian pasta with slow-cooked beef sauce, which is a massive hit with kids.

Pet Bunara

Get the local organic fig cake and the homemade gnocchi in a relaxed, family-friendly square setting.

Konoba Rafaelo

Get the massive, juicy grilled meat platter served with kid-friendly french fries in a very casual environment.

Slasticarna Donat

Get a couple of scoops of their famous, creamy wild berry and hazelnut gelato right by the Roman Forum.

Papica Gourmet Burger Bar

Get the mini-sliders and sweet potato fries, which are perfectly sized for small hands.

👨‍👩‍👧 Zadar's Old Town is paved with smooth, polished stone that is exceptionally stroller-friendly, though a lightweight carrier is useful for climbing old towers. Always pack high-quality water shoes for the family, as the local beaches are mostly pebbly and can be tough on tiny feet.
