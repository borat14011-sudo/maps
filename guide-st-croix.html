St. Croix

← back to travel board
With its blend of majestic Danish colonial architecture and tranquil Caribbean shores, St. Croix offers an exclusive, unhurried escape for discerning families. Will and Kathryn can stroll the historic, stroller-friendly boardwalk of Christiansted before retreating to pristine white-sand bays. Baby Mary Claire will delight in the calm, reef-protected waters of Buck Island Reef National Monument, perfect for gentle wading. The island’s laid-back luxury ensures private beachfront access and high-end conveniences are always within easy reach.
🚕 Getting there: A 20-minute private luxury SUV transfer from Henry E. Rohlsen Airport to the Christiansted resort area is the most seamless option, easily accommodating a bulky stroller and offering pre-installed rear-facing infant car seats upon request.

What to do

Morning 9am-1pm / 4h

Buck Island Reef National Monument

Take a private catamaran charter to this uninhabited marine sanctuary. The white sand of Turtle Beach is incredibly soft, and the shallow, crystal-clear shoreline provides a calm, shaded wading area for Mary Claire under the sea grape trees. Use a baby carrier for the beach landing.

Early morning 8-9:30am / 1.5h

Christiansted Boardwalk

Stroll along this scenic, fully stroller-accessible wooden boardwalk. Enjoy the cool harbor breeze in the early morning while watching the sailboats, and stop at one of the waterfront cafes for a relaxed iced latte.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Fort Christiansvaern

This well-preserved, buttercup-yellow Danish fort features wide, grassy lawns perfect for Mary Claire to crawl on under the shade of historic brick walls. The lower courtyard is stroller-accessible, though the ramparts require carrying the baby.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Sandy Point National Wildlife Refuge

This breathtaking, pristine peninsula offers miles of soft sand and exceptionally calm, turquoise waters. Because it is a nesting site for leatherback turtles, it is highly protected and quiet; bring a baby beach tent for shade.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Cane Bay Beach

A picture-perfect beach lined with shady palm trees and gentle waves. The sandy entry is very gradual, making it ideal for holding Mary Claire while dipping her toes in the warm water, with beachfront dining just steps away.

Morning 9:30-11am / 1.5h

St. George Village Botanical Garden

Meander along paved and flat gravel pathways wound through the lush ruins of a 19th-century sugar plantation. Towering mahogany trees and blooming orchids provide ample shade, making it a highly tranquil and stroller-friendly walk.

Early morning 7:30-8:30am / 1h

Point Udall

Visit the easternmost point of the United States to witness the stunning Millennium Monument. The paved viewing area is completely stroller-accessible, offering dramatic cliffside ocean views and a refreshing breeze, best enjoyed before the midday sun.

Midday 11:30am-1:30pm / 2h

Cramer's Park

This local favorite on the East End features a wide, shallow, reef-protected beach with virtually no waves, perfect for an infant. The adjacent grassy park has picnic tables shaded by large trees, ideal for a quiet family afternoon.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Estate Whim Museum

Explore the sprawling grounds of the oldest sugar plantation museum in the valley. The flat, grassy lawns are shaded by historic trees, providing a serene environment to walk with a baby carrier while learning about the island's complex history.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Terrace at The Buccaneer

Savor upscale Caribbean-infused fine dining overlooking the manicured grounds and the Caribbean Sea. Highchairs are readily available, and the spacious, open-air terrace layout easily accommodates strollers during their early 5:30 PM dinner seating.

Savant

Indulge in gourmet Thai-Caribbean fusion within a romantic, candlelit historic courtyard. While upscale, the open-air garden setting is wonderfully welcoming for families during the early evening, with soft ambient noise that easily masks baby sounds.

Shupe's on the Boardwalk

This high-end, casual boardwalk eatery specializes in fresh local seafood and hand-crafted burgers. The spacious outdoor deck is stroller-friendly, and the gentle lapping of the harbor waves below provides a soothing white noise for Mary Claire.

Too Chez

An elegant French-Caribbean bistro tucked into a charming, quiet historic courtyard in downtown Christiansted. The outdoor seating is spacious enough for a stroller, and the staff is highly accommodating to families seeking an early, upscale dinner.

Leatherback Brewing Company

A spacious, ultra-family-friendly craft brewery offering gourmet flatbreads and local island bites. The outdoor patio features a large grassy lawn where Mary Claire can stretch her legs, and the casual, bustling atmosphere is stress-free for parents.
