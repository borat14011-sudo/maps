St. Croix

← back to travel board
St. Croix welcomes families with its unique blend of Danish history, lush tropical rainforests, and some of the calmest turquoise waters in the Caribbean. Unlike its bustling sister islands, St. Croix offers a laid-back, unhurried pace that is perfect for parents and toddlers alike. From sandy shallow coves to colorful historic streets, this island is an easy-going paradise for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 min taxi or rental car ride from Henry E. Rohlsen Airport to Christiansted or beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 4-5h

Buck Island Reef National Monument

Take a family-friendly catamaran tour to this pristine uninhabited island. Toddlers will love splashing in the ultra-shallow, crystal-clear water at Turtle Cove while parents admire the white sand.

Afternoon / 3h

Cane Bay Beach

This iconic sandy beach offers plenty of natural shade from palm trees and gentle waves. It is ideal for building sandcastles while toddlers splash safely in the calm shallows.

Morning / 1-2h

Fort Christiansvaern

Explore this well-preserved, bright yellow Danish fort overlooking the harbor. Toddlers can safely burn off energy running around the wide, grassy lawns of the courtyard.

Morning / 2h

Sandy Point National Wildlife Refuge

Visit one of the most stunning, pristine white-sand beaches with incredibly calm, shallow waters. It is perfect for early morning toddler walks, but remember to bring your own umbrella as there is no natural shade.

Morning / 1h

Point Udall

Stand at the easternmost point of the United States to enjoy breathtaking panoramic ocean views. The paved area around the giant sundial monument is completely stroller-friendly.

Morning / 2h

St. George Village Botanical Garden

Wander through lush tropical gardens set among the historic ruins of a 19th-century sugar plantation. The flat, shaded gravel paths are easy for strollers, and toddlers will love spotting lizards and bright flowers.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

La Reine Farmers Market

Immerse your family in local culture at this vibrant outdoor market. Toddlers will love tasting sweet, freshly sliced local mangoes and bananas in a lively, sensory-rich environment.

Afternoon / 1h

Cruzan Rum Distillery

Though famous for spirits, the historic grounds offer a highly visual and engaging outdoor walking tour. Toddlers will enjoy exploring the expansive paths and seeing the massive, historic wind mill structure.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Frederiksted Pier and Beach

Stroll along this quiet pier where you can easily spot sea turtles swimming in the clear water below. The adjacent calm beach and nearby playground make it a perfect toddler-paced afternoon stop.

🍽️ Where to eat

Shupe's on the Boardwalk

Order the juicy, fresh-ground burgers and hand-cut fries to enjoy on the casual, toddler-friendly outdoor deck overlooking the Christiansted harbor.

The Mill Boardwalk Bar & Brick Oven Pizza

Grab a delicious, family-sized custom brick-oven pizza while sitting right on the water watching the boats go by.

Rhythms at Rainbow Beach

Enjoy the coconut shrimp and a refreshing fruit smoothie while dining directly on the sand where toddlers can play happily at your feet.

Leatherback Brewing Company

Sip on a flight of local craft beer paired with hot soft pretzels on their massive, fenced-in outdoor lawn designed for kids to run free.

Armstrong's Ice Cream

Treat the little ones to a scoop of legendary homemade tropical ice cream in local flavors like gooseberry, guava, or sweet mango.

👨‍👩‍👧 Renting a car is highly recommended for families to easily carry beach gear and explore both ends of the island at your own pace. Be sure to pack reef-safe sunscreen and a pop-up beach tent, as shade can be scarce on some of the most beautiful, secluded shores.
