Telluride

← back to travel board
Nestled in a dramatic box canyon, Telluride blends rugged Alpine beauty with a welcoming, historic Victorian town. Its pedestrian-friendly layout and free scenic gondola make navigating this mountain paradise effortless for families. Children will love the splashing creeks, open meadows, and whimsical parks tucked under towering peaks.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or shuttle from Telluride Regional Airport (TEX), or ~1.5 hour shuttle from Montrose Regional Airport (MTJ) directly to town hotels.

What to do

Morning or Sunset / 1h

The Free Telluride Gondola

Enjoy a breathtaking, free 13-minute aerial ride connecting the historic town to Mountain Village. It is completely stroller-friendly, and toddlers will be mesmerized by the bird's-eye view of the pine trees below.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Telluride Town Park

A massive outdoor haven featuring a state-of-the-art playground, a heated outdoor pool, and scenic beaver ponds. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow, gentle entry points of the adjacent San Miguel River.

Morning / 1-2h

San Miguel River Trail

A flat, shaded, red-dirt path that meanders right through the heart of town along the rushing river. It is perfect for an easy stroller walk or letting toddlers ride balance bikes while looking for ducks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Mountain Village Pop-Up Science & Games

The pedestrian-only plaza in Mountain Village regularly hosts giant chess, lawn games, and interactive kids' pop-ups. Toddlers can run free without any worry of car traffic.

Morning / 1-2h

Telluride Historical Museum

Housed in a historic 1896 hospital, this museum brings the region's Ute heritage and mining boom days to life. Little ones will love the outdoor gold-panning trough and the cozy, toy-filled children's discovery room.

Friday Morning / 1-2h

Telluride Farmers' Market

A vibrant Friday market on South Oak Street packed with local organic produce, artisan bakery treats, and live music. It is highly stroller-accessible and offers plenty of kid-friendly finger foods.

Morning / 2-3h

Bear Creek Trail (Lower Section)

A popular dirt trail leading to a waterfall, surrounded by aspen groves and pine forests. While the full hike is steep, the lower section is wide and gentle enough for a sturdy toddler carrier or a slow-paced toddler-led wander.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Wilkinson Public Library

An award-winning library featuring an expansive, dedicated children's room filled with toys, climbing blocks, and puzzle stations. It is the perfect cozy, indoor refuge on a rainy or overly chilly mountain afternoon.

Morning / 1h

Bridal Veil Falls Viewpoint

Marvel at Colorado's tallest free-falling waterfall cascading down the canyon headwall. Families can drive right up to the base parking lot to enjoy the mist without any strenuous hiking for little legs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Brown Dog Pizza

Grab a booth at this casual, award-winning spot for world-class Detroit-style square pizzas that toddlers love to pull apart.

The Butcher & The Baker

Order handmade pastries, gourmet breakfast burritos, and fresh organic juices at this bustling, family-friendly marketplace cafe.

Baked in Telluride

Stop by this legendary local bakery for giant, toddler-pleasing frosted sugar cookies, fresh bagels, and quick-slice pizzas.

La Cocina de Luz

Enjoy organic, locally sourced Mexican fare, featuring a great kids' menu with mild cheese quesadillas and homemade fruit popsicles.

Allred's Restaurant

Dine at the top of the gondola station for unmatched panoramic views; go early at 5:00 PM for a relaxed, family-friendly fine dining experience.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry a high-SPF sunscreen and plenty of water, as Telluride's high altitude of 8,750 feet makes toddlers dehydrate and sunburn much faster. Take advantage of the free Galloping Goose shuttle bus that loops around town, saving tired little legs after a long day of exploring.
