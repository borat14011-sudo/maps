Telluride

← back to travel board
Nestled in a dramatic box canyon, Telluride combines rugged Old West history with majestic Rocky Mountain beauty. This pedestrian-friendly town is perfect for families, offering stress-free transit, gorgeous nature trails, and a welcoming, laid-back vibe. Whether you are riding the free gondola or splashing in the creek, it is a mountain paradise for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi from Telluride Regional Airport (TEX), or ~1.5 hour scenic pre-arranged shuttle from Montrose Regional Airport (MTJ) to downtown hotels.

What to do

Afternoon / 1h

Telluride Gondola

Enjoy a free, breathtaking aerial ride connecting the historic town to Mountain Village. Toddlers will love looking for deer and dogs riding along with their owners.

Morning / 2h

Town Park and Playground

A massive park at the east end of town featuring an excellent playground, open fields, and shallow creek access. It has a dedicated toddler play area and plenty of shade trees.

Morning / 3h

Bear Creek Falls Trail

A scenic hike along a steady dirt road that ends at a spectacular rushing waterfall. It is wide enough for a sturdy jogging stroller, though carrying toddlers in a pack is recommended for the incline.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Telluride Historical Museum

Discover the town's mining and ski history through interactive, kid-friendly exhibits housed in an 1896 hospital. Toddlers will enjoy the outdoor panning-for-gold activity.

Morning / 1-2h

Bridal Veil Falls

Marvel at Colorado's tallest free-falling waterfall at the end of the canyon. You can drive close to the base, making it easy to view with toddlers without a grueling hike.

Friday Morning / 1-2h

Telluride Farmers Market

Stroll down South Oak Street to sample fresh local fruits, baked goods, and artisan crafts. Toddlers will love the live music, soft pretzels, and bustling, friendly atmosphere.

Evening / 1-2h

Mountain Village Ice Rink

An outdoor ice rink in the heart of Mountain Village that offers skate rentals and helpful push-along plastic trainers for little ones. In the summer, this plaza transforms into a grassy lawn with outdoor games.

Afternoon / 1h

San Miguel River Trail

A flat, gravel path winding gently along the river right through town. It is completely stroller-friendly and has many spots where toddlers can throw small pebbles into the water.

Morning / 1-2h

Wilkinson Public Library

Widely regarded as one of the best libraries in Colorado, featuring a fantastic, dedicated kids' room with toys, climbing structures, and books. It is the perfect cozy indoor refuge for toddlers on a rainy or snowy day.

🍽️ Where to eat

Brown Dog Pizza

Grab a famous, award-winning Detroit-style deep-dish pizza that the whole family will love in a casual sports bar setting.

Gorrono Ranch

Sip a drink on the snow beach while kids play in the snow or on the grass outside this historic mid-mountain wooden lodge.

The Butcher & The Baker

Stop by for fresh-baked morning pastries, handmade sandwiches, and locally sourced organic breakfast plates.

Floradora Saloon

Enjoy elevated American comfort food like grass-fed burgers and truffle fries in a historic, family-friendly saloon that has been operating for decades.

Cornerhouse Grille

Feast on casual pub grub, including great kid-friendly chicken tenders and famous tater tots, in a laid-back, converted historic home.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep your family well-hydrated and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent altitude sickness and sunburn at 8,750 feet. Utilize the free Galloping Goose shuttle bus and the gondola to easily navigate town with a stroller without needing to drive.
