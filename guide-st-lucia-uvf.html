St. Lucia (UVF)

← back to travel board
St. Lucia welcomes families with its dramatic emerald Pitons, warm turquoise waters, and vibrant tropical energy. From splashing in gentle bays to exploring bubbling volcanic springs, the island is a playground of natural wonder. It is a sensory paradise where toddlers can touch soft sand while parents soak in majestic mountain views.
🚕 Getting there: ~60 min private taxi to Soufrière hotels, or ~90 min transfer to Rodney Bay family resorts.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Sulphur Springs Mud Baths

Dip into the warm, mineral-rich mud baths of the world's only drive-in volcano. It is messy, sensory fun, though toddlers may prefer splashing in the cooler rinse streams or just watching the bubbling steam.

Morning / 1-2h

Diamond Falls Botanical Gardens

Wander through flat, stroller-friendly paved paths surrounded by vibrant tropical flowers and a color-changing waterfall. Toddlers will love spotting giant green lizards and bright heliconias along the easy walking trail.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Reduit Beach

Reduit Beach offers calm, shallow waters perfect for toddlers to splash safely, with plenty of shade and vendors. Parents can rent umbrellas while kids build sandcastles right next to the gentle surf.

Morning / 1-2h

Castries Central Market

Immerse your family in local culture at this bustling, colorful market filled with sweet tropical fruits and handmade crafts. It is a sensory delight for kids, but keep toddlers in a carrier due to the busy crowds.

Morning / 2-3h

Pigeon Island National Landmark

Explore historic ruins, flat grassy lawns, and two sheltered, family-friendly beaches inside this peaceful national park. Toddlers can run freely on the grass while older kids climb the lower fort ruins.

Early Evening / 2h

Gros Islet Street Party (Early Evening)

While the late-night party is for adults, arriving early in the evening lets families enjoy delicious street food and gentle steel pan music. Toddlers will love dancing to the lively rhythms before bedtime.

Morning / 1h

Tet Paul Nature Trail

This trail offers breathtaking views of the Pitons with an easy, 45-minute loop hike. It is highly manageable for toddlers with some hand-holding, featuring flat steps and beautiful organic gardens.

Morning / 2-3h

Rainforest Adventures Aerial Tram

Glide safely above the rainforest canopy in an open-air gondola, spotting colorful birds and lush giant ferns. The slow, smooth ride is incredibly safe and peaceful for babies and toddlers.

Afternoon / 2h

Splash Island Water Park

This inflatable open-water sports park is located on Reduit Beach and features slides, hurdles, and climbing walls. While best for older kids, toddlers can safely paddle along the shoreline right next to the park.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Coal Pot

Order the fresh catch of the day cooked in a traditional Creole clay pot right on the waterfront.

Trou au Diable

Enjoy beachside dining with their famous rotis and kid-friendly wood-fired pizzas right on the sand.

Orlando's Restaurant

Taste the mini-degustation of traditional Caribbean dishes like fish cakes and plantains in a relaxed garden setting.

Marie's Local Kitchen

Fill up on a hearty Creole buffet featuring macaroni pie, stewed chicken, and sweet fried plantains.

Buzz Seafood and Grill

Try the coconut-crusted shrimp or the kids' fish and chips in a breezy, family-friendly garden patio.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of toddler-safe reef insect repellent and high-SPF sunscreen, as the tropical sun and forest bugs are intense. Opt for private transfers with pre-booked car seats, since the winding, hilly island roads can cause motion sickness in little ones.
