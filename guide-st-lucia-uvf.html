St. Lucia (UVF)

← back to travel board
St. Lucia charms families with its dramatic volcanic Pitons, lush rainforests, and warm, calm bays perfect for little splashers. From sulfur springs to chocolate-making estates, this island offers a sensory-rich tropical playground where adventure meets relaxation. It is a Caribbean paradise where family memories are framed by vibrant green peaks.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 minutes to Soufriere or ~90 minutes to Rodney Bay via private family-friendly taxi or pre-booked shuttle from Hewanorra International Airport (UVF)

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Sulphur Springs Mud Baths

Slather yourselves in warm, mineral-rich mud at the world's only drive-in volcano before rinsing off in natural hot springs. Toddlers will love the messy sensory play, but stick to the cooler pools to protect sensitive skin.

Afternoon / 2h

Project Chocolate by Hotel Chocolat

Learn how cacao grows and craft your own chocolate bar in a beautiful rainforest setting. Little ones can taste-test sweet cacao pulp and enjoy walking under the shady estate trees.

Morning / 3h

Pigeon Island National Park

Hike up to historic military ruins for breathtaking coastal views, or stay on the flat grassy paths for an easy stroll. The park features two calm, shady beaches ideal for toddlers to splash safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Castries Central Market

Immerse your family in local culture at this bustling market filled with colorful tropical fruits, spices, and handmade crafts. It's a great spot to grab a sweet, fresh banana for a toddler snack.

Morning / 2-3h

Reduit Beach and Splash Island

Relax on the soft sands of the island's most popular family beach while older kids tackle the inflatable water park. The calm, shallow shoreline is ideal for toddlers to paddle and build sandcastles safely.

Morning / 1h

Diamond Falls Botanical Gardens

Wander through flat, paved pathways lined with vibrant tropical flowers to reach a stunning, color-changing waterfall. The easy walk is stroller-accessible and perfectly sized for tiny, curious feet.

Morning / 1-2h

Tet Paul Nature Trail

This gentle, 45-minute hike offers some of the most spectacular views of the Pitons without the strenuous climb. Toddlers can easily walk parts of the well-maintained dirt path with a helping hand.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Marigot Bay Ferry and Beach

Take a tiny, fun ferry ride across the bay to a secluded sandy spit shaded by towering palm trees. It is a peaceful spot for toddlers to watch yachts glide by while digging in the soft sand.

Morning / 2.5h

Rainforest Adventures Aerial Tram

Glide safely above the rainforest canopy in an open-air gondola, spotting colorful birds and giant ferns. It is a peaceful, no-effort way for toddlers to experience the deep jungle without long hikes.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Coal Pot

Treat the family to fresh, Creole-style catch of the day on a lovely seaside deck where kids can watch boats.

Martha's Tables

Enjoy hearty, family-friendly local dishes like Creole chicken, macaroni pie, and fresh plantains in an open-air, casual setting near the Pitons.

Orlando's Restaurant

Savor farm-to-table Caribbean small plates crafted by a legendary chef in a relaxed, colorful courtyard that welcomes families.

Spinnakers Beach Bar & Grill

Dine right on the sand of Reduit Beach on kid-pleasing chicken fingers, burgers, and fresh local lobster while toddlers play at your feet.

The Pink Plantation House

Dine on delicious Creole crab back and fresh tropical juices inside a historic French colonial home surrounded by lush, kid-friendly gardens.

👨‍👩‍👧 St. Lucia's winding, mountainous roads can easily cause motion sickness, so pack remedy bands or plan drives around your toddler's nap time. Always carry high-SPF reef-safe sunscreen and plenty of insect repellent to protect sensitive skin from the tropical sun and rainforest mosquitoes.
