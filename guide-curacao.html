Curaçao

← back to travel board
With its rainbow-colored colonial waterfront in Willemstad and calm, shallow turquoise coves, Curaçao is a tropical playground designed for family bonding. Gentle trade winds keep the island comfortable as you transition from exploring historic forts to splashing in crystal-clear tide pools. It’s an easygoing paradise where European charm seamlessly blends with vibrant Caribbean soul.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or pre-arranged rental car to Willemstad or the popular Jan Thiel beach hotels

What to do

Sunset / 1-2h

Handelskade and Queen Emma Bridge

Watch the historic pontoon bridge swing open for boats while admiring the bright, candy-colored Dutch colonial buildings. Toddlers will love chasing the gentle harbor waves or riding the free ferry when the bridge is open.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa Lagun

A narrow, cliff-sheltered cove where gentle green sea turtles swim right up to the shallow shore. The calm, wave-free waters make it incredibly safe for toddlers to splash around with a life vest.

Morning / 3h

Curaçao Sea Aquarium

Built right on the ocean, this interactive aquarium lets kids touch starfish and view marine life through underwater observatories without getting wet. It’s highly stroller-friendly with plenty of shaded pathways for midday breaks.

Morning / 2h

Shete Boka National Park

Witness the raw power of nature as massive waves crash into volcanic caves and blowholes along the rocky coast. Stick to the paved boardwalks at Boka Tabla, which are easy to navigate even with toddlers in tow.

Lunch / 1-2h

Plasa Bieu

Dive into local culture at this bustling indoor market where open-air kitchens cook up authentic, hearty Creole dishes. Toddlers can enjoy simple, mild sides like stoba gravy on white rice or sweet fried plantains.

Afternoon / 1h

Hato Caves

Walk through limestone chambers filled with ancient stalactites, dripping water, and harmless long-nosed bats. The paved path has some stairs, but the cool underground air offers a refreshing escape from the Caribbean heat.

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Porto Mari

This beautiful white-sand beach is famous for its two friendly resident wild pigs that love to lounge in the sand. With double reefs close to shore and excellent amenities, it's a dream spot for easy family beach days.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Children's Museum Curaçao

A fantastic indoor-outdoor interactive museum filled with sensory exhibits, water play areas, and climbing structures. It is specifically designed for early childhood play, making it the ultimate safe haven for toddler energy.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Jan Thiel Beach

A vibrant, family-centric beach resort area with a calm, protected salt-water pool ideal for tiny swimmers. Parents can rent comfortable daybeds right next to the shallow water while enjoying full-service food and drinks.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gouverneur de Rouville

Sit on the breezy outdoor balcony and order the traditional Karni Stoba beef stew or Keshi Yena stuffed cheese dome while watching ships pass.

Karakter Curaçao

Enjoy fresh seafood and Dutch-style bitterballen served right to your beach lounger while the kids play in the sand nearby.

Jaanchie's Restaurant

Experience legendary local hospitality at this rustic Westpunt spot and try the mild, slow-cooked goat stew accompanied by the songs of dozens of wild sugarthieves birds nesting in the rafters.

Sol Food

Grab a relaxed family dinner of delicious, kid-friendly wood-fired pizzas and homemade brownies overlooking the ocean in Westpunt.

Number Ten

Indulge in a fantastic brunch of fluffy pancakes, fresh smoothies, and specialty coffee in a gorgeous, shady garden oasis.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with a pre-booked car seat, as public transport is limited and driving is the safest, easiest way to reach the island's best west-coast beaches. Always pack water shoes and reef-safe sunscreen, as some shallow beach entries can have coral fragments or sharp volcanic rock.
