Sophisticated Dutch-Caribbean charm meets effortless baby-friendly beaches

Curaçao

← back to travel board
Curaçao offers a perfect blend of Dutch infrastructure and tranquil, wave-free turquoise coves that are ideal for an infant's first ocean dip. The island's colorful, paved historic districts are highly stroller-accessible compared to wilder Caribbean spots, letting you explore without logistical headaches. With excellent modern grocery stores stocked with European baby brands and a relaxed, family-centric culture, it's a low-stress escape for busy parents.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 20 minutes by private rental car or pre-arranged private shuttle to the main resort areas of Piscadera and Willemstad

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Porto Mari

This beautiful white-sand beach features a unique double reef and incredibly calm, shallow water perfect for holding your toddler in the sea. The beach has excellent facilities, a flat wooden boardwalk path for strollers, and famous friendly feral pigs that lounge in the sand.

Morning / 2h

Curaçao Sea Aquarium

An oceanfront aquarium built directly on the reef, allowing natural seawater to pump through the touch tanks and lagoons. The entire facility is open-air, flat, and highly stroller-accessible, making it easy to show your toddler nurse sharks and flamingos up close.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Queen Emma Pontoon Bridge

This famous 'Swinging Old Lady' is a pedestrian-only floating bridge connecting Punda and Otrobanda. It is wide and completely flat for strollers, offering great views of the colorful Handelskade waterfront and passing ships.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Mambo Beach Boulevard

A vibrant, clean, two-story beachside strip featuring paved boardwalks, shops, and restaurants right on the sand. The beach here is protected by a man-made breakwater, creating a giant, wave-free swimming pool ideal for infants.

Morning / 1-2h

Landhuis Chobolobo

The historic 19th-century mansion where the genuine Curaçao Liqueur is distilled. While dad samples the blue liqueur, the spacious, shady courtyard provides a great, quiet spot to walk with a stroller and enjoy locally made artisanal gelato.

Morning / 1.5h

Parke Tropikal Curacao Zoo

A small, quiet, and leafy local zoo that offers a peaceful escape from the midday beach sun. It is very stroller-friendly with flat paved paths, plenty of shade trees, and a dedicated playground area for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Morning / 2h

Playa Lagun

A narrow, picturesque cove nestled between tall cliffs where green sea turtles frequently swim close to the shore. The water is calm and there is direct car parking right at beach level, saving you from hauling heavy baby gear down long stairs.

Afternoon / 2h

Sambil Curaçao (Kidi's Park)

This modern, air-conditioned shopping mall is perfect for escaping the intense midday Caribbean heat. It features a massive, clean indoor soft-play playground called Kidi's Park where your toddler can burn off energy safely.

Late Afternoon / 3h

Jan Thiel Beach

An upscale, managed beach area with a paved promenade, infinity pool, and excellent sunbeds. The water entry is very gradual and sandy, allowing you to sit at the shoreline with your baby in complete comfort.

🍽️ Where to eat

Brakkeput Mei Mei

Order the grilled tenderloin or fresh red snapper at this open-air restaurant set in a historic landhuis. It is exceptionally family-friendly, featuring an adjacent wooden playground where toddlers can play within eyesight of your table.

Karakter Curaçao

Savor the seared tuna or lionfish while sitting at tables placed directly on the sand under shady palapas. The incredibly relaxed beachside vibe means you don't have to worry about toddler noise, and babies can play in the sand right at your feet.

Zanzibar Beach & Restaurant

Grab an early dinner of authentic wood-fired Quattro Formaggi pizza right on the beach at Jan Thiel. The restaurant opens early, has plenty of high chairs, and the ambient ocean breeze and acoustic music easily soothe fussy infants.

De Gouverneur

Try the famous local banana stoba (beef and sweet plantain stew) on the airy second-floor terrace overlooking the harbor. Request a table in the spacious indoor courtyard if you have a stroller, as it offers a quiet, cool, and private atmosphere.

Plasa Bieu

Experience authentic local cuisine like kabritu stoba (goat stew) or dradu (mahi-mahi) at this bustling covered food market. The high energy, open-air layout, and loud chatter easily drown out any baby cries, and the long picnic tables offer plenty of space.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

LionsDive Beach Resort

Built with a natural, eco-friendly design, this resort features a 50-meter sports pool alongside a shaded toddler pool. The resort sits on a calm, protected beach lagoon and offers spacious 2-bedroom apartments with full kitchens, making bottle sterilization and nap times seamless.

Curacao Marriott Beach Resort

Located on the Piscadera bay, this resort offers a beautiful zero-entry family pool perfect for wading with an infant. They provide high-quality wooden cribs upon request, and the rooms are incredibly spacious with plenty of floor space for a pack-and-play.

Renaissance Wind Creek Curacao Resort

Positioned right next to the historic Rif Fort, this hotel boasts a unique, elevated second-story saltwater beach pool with real sand and zero waves, offering a completely controlled beach experience for your baby. It is steps away from paved, stroller-friendly shopping and dining.
