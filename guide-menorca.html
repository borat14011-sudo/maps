Menorca

← back to travel board
Menorca is the Balearic Islands' peaceful sanctuary, where turquoise waters lap gently against shallow white-sand shores. Unhurried and blissfully quiet, it offers families a safe haven of pine-shaded paths, historic towns, and easygoing Mediterranean charm. It is a playground designed by nature, perfect for slow-paced family bonding.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 min taxi or pre-booked private shuttle from Menorca Airport (MAH) to popular family beach hubs like Cala Galdana or Son Bou

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Cala Galdana Beach

A crescent-shaped bay with calm, shallow, turquoise waters and plenty of shade under the pine trees. Perfect for toddlers to splash safely without strong waves.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Binibeca Vell

A charming, labyrinth-like replica of a traditional white-washed fisherman's village. Toddlers will love the toy-town feel, but leave the stroller behind and use a baby carrier for the narrow steps.

Morning / 2-3h

Lloc de Menorca Zoological Park

A lovely, interactive rescue zoo where kids can feed lemurs, pet goats, and see native farm animals. It features plenty of shady play areas and stroller-friendly pathways.

Afternoon / 1h

Monte Toro Sanctuary

Drive up to Menorca's highest point for breathtaking 360-degree views of the island. Toddlers will enjoy running around the safe courtyard and grabbing an ice cream at the mountaintop cafe.

Morning / 2-3h

Cala Mitjana Pine Forest Trail

A flat, shaded 15-minute walk through pine trees leading to a stunning cove. The walk is easy enough for toddlers, but an all-terrain stroller or carrier is recommended for the sandy sections.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercat de Peix Mahón

A lively market where parents can sample fresh tapas while toddlers enjoy watching the colorful fish displays. It has open-air seating that makes family dining relaxed and casual.

Evening / 2h

Ciutadella Old Harbour Stroll

A pedestrian-only harbor walk perfect for watching boats bob in the water. Toddlers can safely wander the car-free promenade while parents enjoy the historic architecture.

Morning / 2h

Subaida Dairy Farm

A working farm where families can see how Mahón cheese is made and visit the cows and piglets. Kids will love the outdoor play area and tasting the mild, toddler-friendly cheeses.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Son Bou Beach and Dunes

The island's longest sandy beach, offering endless space for building sandcastles and shallow entry into the sea. A wooden boardwalk makes it exceptionally easy to navigate with a stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurante El Faro

Order the traditional seafood paella to share while sitting on the sea-facing terrace with plenty of space for restless toddlers.

Es Cranc

Try the legendary caldereta de langosta lobster stew in a rustic, welcoming setting that happily accommodates families with high chairs.

Café Balear

Get the ultra-fresh catch of the day or kid-friendly grilled chicken skewers right on the harbor docks.

Es Molí de Foc

Enjoy creative rice dishes in a charming, breezy garden courtyard where kids can relax away from the street.

Pizzeria Roma Mahón

Feast on authentic thin-crust wood-fired pizzas in a highly welcoming atmosphere that treats children like royalty.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with pre-booked child seats, as public transport to the best secluded coves is limited and parking lots fill up fast. Always pack an all-terrain stroller or a sturdy baby carrier, as many of Menorca's most beautiful beaches require a scenic walk down unpaved, rustic paths.
