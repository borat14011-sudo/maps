Hallstatt

← back to travel board
Nestled between dramatic Dachstein peaks and a glassy lake, Hallstatt looks like a storybook come to life. Cobblestone lanes wind past pastel-colored 16th-century homes, while gentle swan encounters and mountain railways keep little ones utterly enchanted. It's a pedestrian-friendly haven where alpine wonder meets slow-paced family discovery.
🚕 Getting there: ~75 min drive from Salzburg Airport via rental car or private shuttle to the Lahn parking area

What to do

Morning / 30 mins

Classic Postcard Viewpoint

Walk to the iconic northern viewpoint to capture the ultimate family photo against the backdrop of historic steeples. Toddlers will love pointing out the ducks swimming near the water's edge just below the wooden railings.

Morning / 2-3h

Salzwelten Hallstatt Funicular

Ride the thrilling, panoramic funicular railway high up the mountainside. While the deep salt mine tunnels have a minimum age of four, toddlers will love the exciting ride up and the alpine playground at the top.

Afternoon / 1h

Electric Boat Rental on Lake Hallstatt

Rent a small, easy-to-drive electric boat shaped like a swan or a classic motorboat to cruise the calm waters. It is completely safe, highly engaging for little captains, and offers the best views of the town away from the crowds.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Badeinsel Hallstatt (Bathing Island)

This man-made island is a short, flat walk from the town center and features a fantastic wooden playground, shallow lake access, and sprawling grassy areas. It is the absolute best spot for toddlers to run free, slide, and splash safely.

Morning / 2h

Echerntal Trail Nature Walk

Wander along a flat, gravel path through magical pine forests, wooden footbridges, and past rushing streams. This stroller-friendly trail offers a peaceful, crowd-free escape into nature that is easy on little legs.

Morning / 1h

Hallstatt Market Square

Relax in the heart of the car-free old town, surrounded by vibrantly colored heritage buildings and blooming flower boxes. Toddlers will be happily entertained watching the central stone fountain while parents enjoy a pastry nearby.

Morning / 1h

Welterbe-Blick Skywalk

Stand on the dramatic viewing platform that hovers 350 meters directly above the rooftops of Hallstatt. The platform is completely stroller-accessible via the funicular lift, making this high-altitude view stress-free for families.

Morning / 3-4h

Dachstein Krippenstein Cable Car

Take a scenic cable car ride to the high alpine station in neighboring Obertraun. The dramatic mountain landscapes and breezy pathways are easily navigable, offering toddlers a literal cloud-nine experience.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Hallstatt Beinhaus & St. Mary's Churchyard

Visit the unique, historic chapel housing beautifully hand-painted skulls. While it sounds spooky, the peaceful hillside setting and the colorful flower gardens surrounding the church make it an intriguing, gentle stop for families.

🍽️ Where to eat

Brauhaus Lobisser

Order the fresh, local pan-fried trout served with buttery potatoes in their cozy, kid-friendly garden patio.

Rudolfsturm

Savor a traditional, crispy Wiener Schnitzel with cranberry sauce while sitting on the historic outdoor terrace overlooking the lake.

Seewirt Zauner

Try the legendary Lake Hallstatt whitefish, prepared using traditional regional recipes in a warm, wood-paneled dining room.

Café Derbl

Indulge in a warm, homemade Austrian apple strudel topped with vanilla sauce right in the bustling market square.

Gasthof Simony

Feast on hearty Austrian beef goulash with dumplings in a historic lakeside garden where kids can watch the boats pass by.

👨‍👩‍👧 Since the historic center of Hallstatt features several steep staircases and cobblestones, opt for a sturdy, compact stroller or an ergonomic baby carrier. Public restrooms can be sparse, so keep small Euro coins handy and plan diaper changes around your sit-down dining stops.
