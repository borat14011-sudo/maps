The green island of São Miguel enchants discerning families with its dramatic volcanic calderas, steaming thermal pools, and hydrangea-lined roads. Here, dramatic Atlantic vistas meet the old-world European charm of Ponta Delgada. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the island offers stroller-friendly lakeside paths alongside the mystical twin lakes of Sete Cidades. It is a rare paradise where wild, pristine nature blends seamlessly with upscale estate living.

What to do

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Parque Terra Nostra

Explore centuries-old botanical gardens filled with giant ferns and towering palms. The paved paths are highly stroller-accessible, and the lush canopy provides excellent shade for Mary Claire's morning nap.

Late Morning 11:00am-12:00pm / 1h

Vista do Rei Viewpoint

Admire the iconic blue and green twin lakes of Sete Cidades. The main viewing platform is paved and stroller-friendly, offering breathtaking panoramic photo opportunities without any strenuous trekking.

Early Afternoon 1:30-2:30pm / 1h

Fábrica de Chá Gorreana

Stroll through Europe's oldest tea plantation. The main processing facility has flat ground-floor access, and the outdoor terrace is a tranquil, breezy spot to enjoy a fresh brew while nursing.

Morning 10:00-11:30am / 1.5h

Lagoa das Furnas Fumaroles

Watch geysers and bubbling mud pools up close along a completely flat, wide wooden boardwalk. It is highly stroller-accessible, though be mindful of the sulfur steam direction with the baby.

Afternoon 3:00-4:30pm / 1.5h

Caldeira Velha

A lush, jungle-like natural monument featuring exotic tree ferns and thermal springs. The wide dirt pathways are well-maintained and stroller-friendly, offering a deeply sensory, shaded forest walk.

Late Morning 10:30-11:30am / 1h

Plantação de Ananases Augusto Arruda

Discover how pineapples are grown in historic glass greenhouses. The central gravel paths are easy to navigate, and the quiet, shady gift shop area is perfect for a relaxed family pause.

Late Afternoon 5:00-6:30pm / 1.5h

Portas da Cidade Promenade

Walk through Ponta Delgada's historic city gates and along the wide, paved Avenida Infante Dom Henrique harbor promenade. It avoids the bumpy cobblestones of the inner city, ideal for a sunset stroll.

Morning 9:00-10:30am / 1.5h

Parque Natural da Ribeira dos Caldeirões

A stunning natural park showcasing dramatic waterfalls and historic watermills. The lower viewing areas and surrounding gardens have paved, stroller-friendly paths and a peaceful, shaded café.

Afternoon 2:00-3:00pm / 1h

Cerâmica Vieira

Visit a historic, family-run pottery factory operating since 1862. The open-air courtyard and workshop floors are flat and spacious, providing a quiet, visually stimulating artisan experience for baby.

🍽️ Where to eat

Anfiteatro Restaurant

Savor refined Azorean gastronomy at Ponta Delgada's marina. The spacious waterfront terrace easily accommodates luxury strollers, and the early dinner service ensures a quiet, infant-friendly dining environment.

A Tasca

Famous for its local petiscos and pineapple cake. Book the earliest dinner seating at 6:30 PM to secure a spacious table with a highchair before the lively crowd arrives.

Terra Nostra Garden Restaurant

Dine on the famous slow-cooked Cozido das Furnas inside an elegant art deco dining room. They feature spacious tables, high-end highchairs, and a highly accommodating staff ready to warm baby purees.

Restaurante da Associação Agrícola de São Miguel

Renowned for serving the absolute best steaks on the island. The dining room features soaring ceilings and a bustling, family-friendly atmosphere where infant noises blend in effortlessly.

Otaka

Experience upscale Japanese-Azorean fusion. Book the 7:00 PM opening slot for a serene tasting menu experience, and the attentive staff will happily position your stroller at a quiet corner table.
