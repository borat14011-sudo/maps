Frankfurt

← back to travel board
Frankfurt pairs a dramatic futuristic skyline with cozy, timber-framed charm along the winding Main River. Children will love the abundance of lush green spaces and interactive museums, while parents can savor authentic apple wine in historic neighborhoods. It is a surprisingly walkable, stroller-friendly metropolis that feels both bustling and wonderfully relaxed.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min taxi or a quick 15 min S-Bahn train ride to Frankfurt Central Station.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Römerberg

Explore the iconic reconstructed medieval square with its colorful timber houses and wide open pedestrian plaza. It is stroller-friendly and perfect for letting toddlers safely wander while you admire the architecture.

Morning / 2-3h

Senckenberg Natural History Museum

Marvel at massive dinosaur skeletons and life-sized animal replicas in one of Europe’s largest natural history museums. Toddlers will be wide-eyed at the giant T-Rex at the entrance and the colorful biodiversity exhibits.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Palmengarten

Wander through giant glass greenhouses filled with tropical plants, waterfalls, and a lovely outdoor pond. Toddlers can burn off energy at the fantastic playground and ride the miniature railway train.

Morning / 3h

Frankfurt Zoo

A historic green oasis right in the city center featuring over 4,500 animals, including penguins, seals, and gorillas. The layout is compact enough for little toddler legs, with plenty of shaded benches and play areas.

Midday / 1-2h

Kleinmarkthalle

Experience a bustling indoor food market packed with fresh fruits, cheese, and regional treats like green sauce. Grab a warm, mild sausage for your toddler to munch on as you stroll the vibrant aisles.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Main River Promenade

Walk or push a stroller along the scenic, car-free riverbank paths offering spectacular views of the skyline. There are several grassy lawns and excellent playgrounds, like the one near the Holbeinsteg bridge, perfect for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Experiminta ScienceCenter

A hands-on science center where kids can touch, build, and experiment with physics and math. There is a dedicated toddler play area with giant foam blocks and sensory exhibits perfect for the youngest visitors.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Stadtwald and Goetheturm

Venture into Germany's largest inner-city forest to climb the wooden Goetheturm tower and explore a massive forest playground. The shallow paddling pool in summer and slide park are absolute paradises for active toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Main Tower Observation Deck

Take a high-speed elevator 200 meters up to the open-air platform for breathtaking 360-degree views of the city. Toddlers will love pointing out the tiny cars and trains moving far below from behind the safe glass barriers.

🍽️ Where to eat

Adolf Wagner

Order the traditional schnitzel with Grüne Soße (herb green sauce) and a side of fried potatoes for a classic taste of Frankfurt in a lively tavern setting.

Café Hauptwache

Dine on authentic mild Frankfurter sausages with potato salad in a spacious historic guardhouse right in the city center.

Ginnheimer Wirtshaus

Enjoy hearty German comfort food in a relaxed beer garden setting that features a great kids' menu and a dedicated outdoor play area.

Oheim

Head to this cozy, stroller-friendly cafe for delicious homemade cakes, babyccinos, and a dedicated play corner stocked with toys.

Pizzeria Montana

Indulge in some of the city's best wood-fired Neapolitan pizzas with fresh ingredients, served in a casual, highly welcoming atmosphere.

👨‍👩‍👧 Buy a Frankfurt Card for unlimited public transit travel and generous discounts at family-friendly museums, making it easy to hop on and off trams. Most public transit stations have elevators, but packing a lightweight, compact stroller is ideal for navigating some of the older cobblestone streets in the historic areas.
