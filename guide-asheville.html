Asheville

← back to travel board
Asheville blends Blue Ridge Mountain majesty with a vibrant arts scene, offering a serene escape for new parents. Stroll the manicured grounds of the Biltmore Estate, where wide pathways are perfect for a stroller and quiet corners invite nursing breaks. The city's elevated altitude brings cool breezes even in summer, ideal for baby Mary Claire's delicate skin. With a focus on Southern hospitality and organic cuisine, Asheville welcomes even the tiniest travelers with open arms.
🚕 Getting there: From Asheville Regional Airport (AVL) to downtown hotels is a 20-minute drive; book a private car service like Black Mountain Transportation with an infant seat and stroller storage for a seamless arrival.

What to do

Early morning 9–11am / 2h

Biltmore Estate Gardens

Wander the vast, flat garden paths among fountains and seasonal blooms; stroller-friendly and shady spots for feeding. Skip the house interior with a baby if crowds are heavy.

Late morning 10am–12pm / 2h

North Carolina Arboretum

Explore easy, stroller-accessible trails through native plants and a tranquil bonsai garden. The indoor conservatory offers a quiet, climate-controlled break.

Afternoon 1–3pm / 2h

Blue Ridge Parkway – Folk Art Center

Drive a short stretch of the Parkway to the Folk Art Center, which has stroller-friendly exhibit halls and a shaded picnic area. The baby can enjoy the colorful crafts.

Late afternoon 3–5pm / 2h

River Arts District

Meander through open studios along the French Broad River – many have wide doors, low lighting, and calm atmosphere. Stop at the Orange Peel for a coffee break; baby can nap in carrier.

Morning 9–10:30am / 1.5h

Asheville Botanical Gardens

A compact, free garden with a flat loop trail, shaded benches, and a pond. Perfect for a short, sensory-rich walk with Mary Claire. No loud noises, just birdsong.

Early morning 8–9:30am / 1.5h

Craggy Pinnacle Trail (short, easy option)

A 0.6-mile paved path to a sweeping mountain view – manageable with a sturdy stroller or carrier. The breeze at the top is refreshing. Go early to avoid crowds.

Late morning 10am–12:30pm / 2.5h

WNC Nature Center

Small, family-friendly zoo with wide paths and shaded areas. See otters, bears, and red wolves – low noise compared to large zoos. Restrooms with changing tables available.

Afternoon 1–3pm / 2h

Biltmore Village

Charming historic shopping district with smooth sidewalks and a relaxed vibe. Browse boutiques, grab a pastry, and let baby watch people from the stroller. Many cafes have outdoor seating.

Late afternoon 3–5pm / 2h

Downtown Asheville Urban Trail

A self-guided art walk with 30+ sculptures and murals – the route is flat and stroller-friendly, with frequent benches. The baby will love the bright colors and movement.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tupelo Honey

Famous for Southern classics like shrimp & grits – highchairs available, early dinner seating at 5pm, and a relaxed patio with ceiling fans. Noise level moderate, fine for infants.

Cúrate

Spanish tapas bar with a signature Ibérico ham; reserve early seating (5pm) to avoid loud crowds. Outdoor terrace is stroller-friendly, and they will prepare a simple plate for baby upon request.

The Market Place

Upscale farm-to-table fare like roasted trout; they offer a private dining room for families and have highchairs. Lunch service is quieter and walk-ins welcome with a baby.

Early Girl Eatery

Beloved for breakfast all day – their buttermilk pancakes are a hit. The West Asheville location has a spacious patio and quick service; highchairs and changing table in restroom.

Biscuit Head

Known for giant, flaky biscuits and gravy – a casual spot with counter seating. The outdoor picnic tables are perfect for stroller parking, and they offer a kid's biscuit plate. No reservations, but line moves fast.
