Rovinj & Istrian Coast

← back to travel board
Rovinj rises from the Adriatic like a pastel-colored dream, where narrow stone alleyways lead to sun-drenched plazas and quiet swimming spots. Beyond the town, the Istrian coast unfurls with fragrant pine forests, gentle pebble beaches, and kid-friendly olive groves. It is the perfect, stress-free Mediterranean escape for families seeking culture wrapped in coastal ease.
🚕 Getting there: ~40 min taxi or private transfer from Pula Airport (PUY) directly to Rovinj's pedestrian zone border or nearby forest park hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

St. Euphemia Church & Old Town Exploration

Climb the winding, polished stone streets of Rovinj's old town up to this iconic hilltop church for panoramic sea views. Toddler note: The cobblestones are slick and steep, so leave the stroller behind and use a baby carrier for this walk.

Afternoon / 3-4 hours

Golden Cape Forest Park (Zlatni Rt)

A car-free paradise of towering pine trees, flat gravel paths perfect for strollers, and shady pebble beaches. Toddler note: Rent a bike with a child seat or bring a stroller to explore the shaded shoreline paths easily.

Morning / 1 hour

Rovinj Open-Air Market

A vibrant sensory experience packed with colorful local figs, fresh berries, truffles, and olive oil near the harbor. Toddler note: Grab a basket of sweet, fresh stone fruits for an easy, healthy, and mess-free toddler snack.

Evening / 1.5-2 hours

Sunset Dolphin-Watching Boat Cruise

Board a gentle family-friendly boat from Rovinj harbor to spot wild dolphins playing in the wake as the sun sets. Toddler note: Choose a larger, stable boat with a shaded deck and toilet facilities to keep little ones comfortable.

Full Day / 5-6 hours

Brijuni National Park Safari

Take a short ferry from nearby Fažana to this car-free island featuring an open-air safari park with zebras, llamas, and an ancient elephant. Toddler note: Rent an electric golf cart on the island to zip between sights without tiring little legs.

Morning / 3 hours

Ambrela Beach in Pula

A blue-flag pebble beach with shallow, calm waters, dense pine shade, and great family amenities just a short drive south. Toddler note: The gentle slope makes it incredibly safe for toddlers to splash around, and there is a playground nearby.

Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Lim Fjord Boat Ride & Pirate Cave

Explore a dramatic, green-lined estuary resembling a Nordic fjord, complete with calm waters and a fun pirate cave stop. Toddler note: The water here is incredibly calm, making it a very smooth ride for children prone to motion sickness.

Afternoon / 2 hours

Aquarium Pula

Housed inside a 130-year-old Austro-Hungarian fortress, this unique aquarium showcases Adriatic marine life and a sea turtle rescue center. Toddler note: The cool stone walls offer a perfect escape from the midday summer heat, though some areas have steps where you will need to carry the stroller.

Late Afternoon / 2 hours

Lone Bay & Mulini Beach Promenade

Stroll along a beautifully paved, completely flat seaside path lined with parks, leading from the marina to Lone Bay. Toddler note: Fully stroller-accessible with plenty of grassy spots to stop, run around, and grab an ice cream.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Puntulina

Order the fresh seafood pasta and sit on the lower rock terrace early in the evening to watch the sunset right at the water's edge.

Pizzeria Da Sergio

Order the classic Margherita pizza with its famously thin, crispy crust in a cozy, relaxed old town setting that welcomes families.

Kantinon Tavern

Try the traditional pljukanci pasta with braised beef beef ragout, a delicious, kid-approved local hand-rolled pasta dish.

Balbi

Get the homemade gnocchi with local cheese or wild berries on their lively, family-friendly outdoor square.

Gelateria Italia

Indulge in a scoop of artisanal pistachio or wild strawberry gelato served by the incredibly friendly Italian family owners.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack sturdy water shoes for everyone to easily navigate the pebbly beaches and slick, polished old town cobblestones. Use a lightweight baby carrier rather than a stroller for exploring the hilly, step-heavy historic center of Rovinj.
