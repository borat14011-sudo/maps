Rovinj & Istrian Coast

← back to travel board
Rovinj rises like a pastel-colored dream from the Adriatic, where narrow medieval alleys lead to sparkling, pine-fringed bays. This Istrian gem pairs rich Venetian history with a relaxed Mediterranean rhythm that is deeply welcoming to families. Little ones will love chasing seagulls in seaside piazzas and splashing in the calm, crystal-clear shallows of the Golden Cape.
🚕 Getting there: ~40 min taxi or pre-arranged private transfer from Pula Airport (PUY) directly to Rovinj's pedestrian border or your forest-park resort

What to do

Morning / 2 hours

Wander the Old Town Alleys

Get lost in the car-free, pastel-hued labyrinth of Rovinj's historic peninsula, ending at the hilltop Church of St. Euphemia. Toddler note: Leave the stroller behind and use a carrier, as the polished cobblestones can be extremely slippery and steep.

Afternoon / 3-4 hours

Bike and Splash at Golden Cape Forest Park

This car-free nature oasis offers flat, shaded gravel paths perfect for rental bikes with toddler seats, leading to gentle pebble beaches. Toddler note: The dense pine canopy provides excellent natural shade for midday naps in the stroller.

Morning / 1 hour

Sample Berries at the Rovinj Green Market

An open-air market bustling with local colors, smells, and friendly vendors selling fresh figs, truffles, and olive oil. Toddler note: Pick up a paper cone of sweet, locally grown raspberries or cherries for a healthy, mess-free walking snack.

Full Day / 5-6 hours

Safari Adventure on Brijuni Islands

Take a short ferry ride from nearby Fažana to this car-free island national park featuring an open-air safari park with zebras, llamas, and Lanka the elephant. Toddler note: Rent an electric golf cart at the dock to easily zip around the island at your own pace.

Morning / 1.5 hours

Step Back in Time at the Pula Arena

Explore one of the world's best-preserved Roman amphitheaters, located just a short drive south of Rovinj. Toddler note: The wide grassy areas and flat interior dirt paths are great for toddlers to run off steam safely away from traffic.

Late Afternoon / 2-3 hours

Pebble Play at Lone Bay

A highly accessible, family-friendly beach with calm, shallow waters and plenty of amenities right on the edge of the forest park. Toddler note: Bring water shoes for your little ones as the beach is pebbly, and buy an ice cream at the beachside cafe.

Evening / 1.5 - 2 hours

Sunset Dolphin Watching Cruise

Board a family-friendly boat from the harbor to spot wild bottlenose dolphins playing in the wake as the sun sets over the Adriatic. Toddler note: Choose a larger, stable boat with indoor seating and bathrooms for maximum comfort.

Midday / 4-5 hours

Splash Around at Aquacolors Poreč

Just a 30-minute drive north, Croatia's largest water park features a massive dedicated kids' zone with safe, shallow pools and mini-slides. Toddler note: There is a wonderfully relaxing lazy river where toddlers can float safely in double-tubes with parents.

Morning / 2 hours

Explore the Lim Fjord Viewpoint

A stunning, green-draped canyon where fresh and saltwater meet, offering dramatic views and quiet boat rides. Toddler note: Grab a wooden pirate toy at the lookout stalls and enjoy a gentle, flat walk along the shoreline.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Puntulina

Request a table on the lower rocks right above the water and order the handmade Istrian pljukanci pasta with fresh truffles while the kids watch the waves.

Pizzeria Da Sergio

Grab a table at this legendary, bustling spot in the Old Town for ultra-thin, wood-fired pizzas that are guaranteed crowd-pleasers for hungry toddlers.

Trattoria Balbi

Located on a lively square, this is the perfect spot to enjoy local seafood risotto and gnocchi while your toddler safely plays within eyesight on the car-free stones.

Kantina Di San Giacomo

Stop by this cozy tavern for local cheeses, prosciutto, and Istrian pasta bowls served in a quiet, toddler-friendly courtyard away from the main tourist stream.

Gelateria Italia

Indulge in authentic, artisanal Italian gelato served by a warm, welcoming family—the dark chocolate and pistachio flavors are absolute must-tries.

👨‍👩‍👧 Invest in a sturdy, lightweight baby carrier rather than a heavy stroller, as Rovinj's steep, polished medieval cobblestones are notorious for shaking wheels and slipping. Always pack high-quality water shoes for the entire family, as Istria's beaches are exclusively pebbly or rocky, making barefoot walking tough for little feet.
