From feeding friendly ducks in royal gardens to spotting red buses from a double-decker window, the city is a playground for all ages. It's a place where modern ease meets historic charm, making every street corner a discovery for curious little eyes.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min via the Heathrow Express train to Paddington Station, or a ~1 hour taxi directly to central London hotels.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Diana Memorial Playground

A giant wooden pirate ship set in a beach of white sand, inspired by Peter Pan. Toddlers will love the sensory trail and dedicated soft-play areas away from the big kids.

Morning / 1h

London Eye

Get a bird's-eye view of the city from a slow-moving, fully enclosed glass pod. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the tiny cars below, and there is plenty of room to move around inside.

Morning / 2-3h

Natural History Museum

Wander beneath a giant blue whale skeleton and marvel at the animatronic dinosaurs. Toddlers will love the interactive sensory galleries on the ground floor.

Morning / 1.5-2h

Borough Market

A bustling historic food market filled with aromatic treats, fresh fruits, and colorful displays. It can get crowded, so visit early in the day to navigate strollers easily.

Afternoon / 1-2h

St. James's Park

Watch the famous resident pelicans get fed and enjoy a peaceful stroll past the lake toward Buckingham Palace. It is perfect for toddlers to run off steam on the grassy lawns.

Morning / 2h

London Transport Museum

Hop aboard historic red buses, steam trains, and tube carriages in the heart of Covent Garden. The 'All Aboard' play area is specifically designed for babies and toddlers to drive mini buses.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Kew Gardens

Explore the towering glasshouses, tree-top walkways, and the magical Children's Garden designed specifically for kids to climb and run. Toddlers will love the giant lily pads and sensory kitchen.

Afternoon / 2h

Sea Life London Aquarium

Walk through a glass ocean tunnel with sharks swimming overhead and watch playful penguins dive. It is fully buggy-accessible and provides a calm, sensory-friendly environment for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2h

Regent's Park

Visit the beautiful Rose Gardens and let the little ones splash around near the boating lake. Toddlers will love the wooden play equipment and swings at the Hanover Gate playground.

🍽️ Where to eat

Dishoom Covent Garden

Order the delicious bacon naan roll for breakfast or the creamy, mild black daal, which kids absolutely love.

Poppies Fish & Chips (Spitalfields)

Feast on traditional, crispy cod and chunky chips served in a nostalgic retro environment that welcomes kids with open arms.

Homeslice Neal's Yard

Grab a massive, wood-fired 20-inch pizza to share, with simple Margherita options that are perfect for picky toddlers.

Giraffe (Southbank)

Enjoy a globally-inspired menu with an excellent kids' menu, coloring packs, and a highly welcoming, family-first atmosphere.

Fortnum & Mason (The Parlour)

Indulge in decadent, build-your-own ice cream sundaes in a bright, whimsical setting that feels like a special treat.
