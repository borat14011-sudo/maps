What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Kensington Gardens

Enjoy a peaceful morning walk along the wide, paved paths. The lush green lawn near the Diana Memorial Playground offers quiet shade under ancient trees, making it perfect for feeding and gentle sensory play with baby Mary Claire.

Late morning 10:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew

Explore the expansive, stroller-friendly glasshouses and flat gravel pathways. The quiet Temperate House provides a warm, wind-free environment perfect for an infant, with numerous peaceful benches for feeding.

Early afternoon 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM

The British Museum

The spectacular Great Court is fully climate-controlled, exceptionally spacious, and completely step-free. The gentle, ambient acoustics of the grand museum are highly effective at lulling infants into a restful nap.

Mid-afternoon 2:30 PM - 4:00 PM

Hyde Park Serpentine Boat Ride

Rent a family-friendly pedal boat or take a gentle stroll along the water's edge. The paved lakeside paths are flat, wide, and lined with cafes where you can quickly grab a warm drink while keeping the stroller moving.

Late morning 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Tate Modern

The massive, flat Turbine Hall offers excellent sensory stimulation with its scale and soft lighting. Elevators provide easy access to all floors, and the museum boasts some of the cleanest, most spacious baby-changing facilities in Central London.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Battersea Park and Children's Zoo

A quieter, less crowded alternative to the main London Zoo. This charming park features wide, paved avenues and a small, gentle zoo designed for toddlers and infants to see animals up close without overwhelming noise.

Early morning 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM

Regent's Park Queen Mary's Rose Garden

Stroll through London's most fragrant rose garden, featuring wide gravel paths and plenty of quiet, secluded benches. It is an idyllic, visually stimulating spot for baby Mary Claire to observe colorful blooms in a calm setting.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Natural History Museum Hintze Hall

Walk beneath the breathtaking, suspended blue whale skeleton in the grand entrance hall. The visual scale is mesmerising for infants, and the museum features excellent ramped side-access for seamless stroller entry.

Mid-afternoon 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM

Victoria and Albert Museum John Madejski Garden

Relax in the peaceful, elegant courtyard featuring a shallow, paved paddling pool where baby can dip her feet on a warm day. The surrounding arcade offers cool shade, upscale outdoor seating, and a highly tranquil atmosphere.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Wolseley

This iconic, grand Piccadilly institution is exceptionally welcoming to families during their early breakfast service. The vibrant morning atmosphere easily masks any baby babble, highchairs are readily available, and the soft, fresh pastries are world-class.

The Diamond Jubilee Tea Salon at Fortnum & Mason

Enjoy an ultra-luxurious traditional afternoon tea in a refined space that warmly accommodates young families. Booking an early afternoon slot ensures a quieter room, where the attentive staff will gladly provide a highchair and space for your stroller.

Dishoom Covent Garden

Known for its spacious booths and incredibly warm, welcoming hospitality. Arriving for an early lunch or their famous breakfast naan rolls allows you to beat the crowds, leaving plenty of room for a stroller right next to your dining table.

Petersham Nurseries Cafe

A gorgeous, plant-filled glasshouse restaurant in Richmond that offers a tranquil, nature-infused dining experience. The spacious, dirt-floor layout easily accommodates high-end strollers, and the fresh, seasonal Italian dishes are spectacular.

Spring at Somerset House

Chef Skye Gyngell's elegant restaurant is flooded with natural light and features a spacious, calm layout. Opt for an early dinner reservation to enjoy exquisite, seasonal dining in a relaxed, sophisticated environment that welcomes children.
