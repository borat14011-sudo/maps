Dubrovnik

← back to travel board
Dubrovnik offers a dramatic Adriatic backdrop where limestone streets meet high-end seaside luxury, perfect for a finance dad seeking a seamless family getaway. While the medieval Old Town is famously stepped, basing yourselves in the resort-heavy Lapad Peninsula provides stroller-friendly promenades, private beach clubs, and calm turquoise waters. It is a destination that balances sophisticated coastal dining and deep history with the logistics of traveling with an infant, ensuring both parent relaxation and toddler safety.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min private Mercedes V-Class transfer from Čilipi Airport (DBV) directly to your Babin Kuk or Lapad hotel, avoiding the steep pedestrian stairs of the Old Town.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Dubrovnik Cable Car

Glide up to Mount Srđ for panoramic views of the red-roofed city and the Adriatic without the grueling hike. The lower and upper stations are fully stroller-accessible, and the indoor museum at the top offers a cool, air-conditioned escape for a mid-morning nap.

Morning / 1-2h

Lapad Forest Park (Velika i Mala Petka)

This shaded pine forest on the Lapad Peninsula features paved, stroller-friendly paths away from the midday heat and Old Town crowds. It offers stunning coastal viewpoints and plenty of flat, grassy areas for an infant to crawl safely on a blanket.

Morning / 3-4h

Lokrum Island Botanical Garden

A 15-minute taxi boat ride from the Old Harbor brings you to a vehicle-free nature reserve populated by friendly, free-roaming peacocks. The gravel paths are flat and easily navigable with a sturdy stroller, and the giant pine trees provide excellent shade for nursing.

Early Morning / 1h

Gundulić Square Market

This lively morning open-air market in the heart of the Old Town is perfect for picking up fresh, local organic figs and peaches for baby snacks. It is flat and vibrant, making it easy to navigate before the heavy cruise-ship crowds arrive around 10:00 AM.

Midday / 1h

Dubrovnik Aquarium

Housed inside the cool, stone walls of St. John's Fortress, this quiet aquarium showcases local Adriatic marine life in a low-light environment that is incredibly soothing for overstimulated toddlers. The flat, stone floors are stroller-friendly, and the thick fortress walls keep the interior naturally cool on hot days.

Morning / 2-3h

Copacabana Beach Dubrovnik

Located in Babin Kuk, this upscale beach club offers luxury concrete and pebble shores with cabanas, making it highly stroller-accessible compared to rocky cliffs. The shallow, calm waters of the bay are ideal for dipping a toddler's toes, and there is plenty of space to park a stroller next to your lounger.

Morning / 1h

Rector's Palace

This grand Gothic-Renaissance palace features a flat, shaded atrium perfect for a slow, cool walk with an infant. While the upper floor has stairs, the ground level is easy to navigate and provides a quiet, awe-inspiring break from the bustling, sunlit streets outside.

Evening / 1-2h

Uvala Lapad Promenade

A completely pedestrianized, flat tree-lined pathway that stretches along the Lapad bay, packed with cafes and playgrounds. It is the absolute best spot in the city for an uninterrupted sunset stroller push while grabbing a high-quality espresso or artisanal gelato.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Cavtat Harbor Promenade

A scenic 30-minute water taxi ride from Dubrovnik's Old Harbor brings you to this quiet, ultra-flat seaside town. It is incredibly stroller-friendly, lined with palm trees and calm waters, offering a peaceful, luxury-village vibe far away from the city's crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pantarul

Located in Lapad, this highly rated modern bistro is famously welcoming to families, offering high chairs and early 6:00 PM dinner service. Order the slow-cooked beef cheeks; the spacious, air-conditioned indoor seating is a comfortable refuge for a dining baby.

Grano Duro Pizza & Pasta

Situated in the pedestrian-only, family-friendly Mali Stradun square in Babin Kuk, this restaurant offers exceptional hand-stretched pizzas. The wide terrace has zero stairs, making stroller parking alongside your table completely effortless while your toddler babbles freely.

Cave Bar More

The exterior seaside terrace offers stunning sunset views and a relaxed, breezy atmosphere where the crashing waves naturally mask baby noises. Go early for a high-end cocktail and a club sandwich while your toddler naps in the stroller right next to your table.

Konoba Dubrava

Located up on Bosanka, this rustic, open-air restaurant is famous for 'peka' (traditional slow-roasted meat under an iron bell). The spacious outdoor seating under oak trees is perfect for families, with plenty of room to roam and high tolerance for noisy little ones.

Lacroma Restaurant

This modern oasis on Lokrum Island features a massive green lawn with outdoor lounge seating where your baby can stretch out on a blanket. Enjoy fresh local sea bass while utilizing the breezy, shaded tables that easily accommodate large strollers.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sun Gardens Dubrovnik

Located just outside the city in Orašac, this world-class resort offers massive multi-bedroom residences with full kitchens, a dedicated babysitting service, and multiple zero-entry pools perfect for splashing with an infant.

Valamar Collection Dubrovnik President Hotel

Situated on the tip of Babin Kuk, every room faces the sea with a spacious terrace, and the hotel features a calm, private pebble beach. It is highly accessible via spacious elevators, and they provide top-tier wooden cribs and baby bottle warmers upon request.

Hotel More

A boutique luxury option in Lapad featuring spacious family suites and a beautiful pool overlooking the bay. Its direct access to the flat Lapad Promenade allows you to roll your stroller straight out of the hotel lobby and onto a scenic, car-free coastal walk.

👨‍👩‍👧 Avoid pushing strollers up the Old Town walls or the steep side-alleys off Placa (Stradun); instead, stick to the completely flat main limestone thoroughfare. Ditch the heavy stroller for a high-quality structured baby carrier when exploring the historic fortresses, as historic steps are ubiquitous and unforgiving. Tap water in Dubrovnik is exceptionally clean and safe for mixing baby formula, and you can easily stock up on European organic baby brands like Hipp at the DM drogerie markt locations in Lapad.
