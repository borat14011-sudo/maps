Dubrovnik

← back to travel board
Dubrovnik’s medieval stone walls and shimmering Adriatic waters offer an enchanting Mediterranean escape tailored for refined family travel. While the historic Old Town features limestone streets that can be challenging for strollers, the surrounding coastal properties provide seamless waterfront access and calm, shallow bays. Sophisticated parents can enjoy private boat charters to the nearby Elafiti Islands, ensuring baby Mary Claire stays comfortable in the shaded, sea-breezed cabin. This tailored itinerary balances rich Croatian history with elite, baby-friendly leisure.
🚕 Getting there: Dubrovnik Airport (DBV) to the Lapad Peninsula or Ploče hotel strip takes approximately 30 minutes via a pre-arranged private Mercedes V-Class transfer equipped with a premium Cybex infant car seat and ample space for a Bugaboo stroller.

What to do

Morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Lokrum Island Botanical Garden

A lush, car-free nature reserve just a 15-minute ferry ride from the Old Town. The botanical gardens offer deep pine shade, flat dirt paths perfect for walking, and free-roaming peacocks to delight baby Mary Claire. Avoid the steep hike to Fort Royal and stick to the paved paths near the monastery.

Early morning 9-10am / 1h

Dubrovnik Cable Car

Ascend Mount Srđ in a spacious, fully enclosed cabin that accommodates strollers and keeps baby protected from high winds. The ride is under four minutes, minimizing any potential fussiness while delivering sweeping panoramas of the Adriatic sea.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Lapad Bay Promenade

A beautifully paved, completely stroller-friendly seaside walk lined with fragrant pine trees. It features excellent shade, quiet park benches for feeding, and a gentle ocean breeze, making it the perfect spot for an easy afternoon walk with Mary Claire.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Cavtat Harbor Promenade

Located 25 minutes south of Dubrovnik, this charming, flat harbor walk is blissfully quiet compared to the Old Town. Paved paths wind under palm trees, offering a peaceful, low-sensory environment for a stroller nap.

Morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Copacabana Beach

A high-end beach club on the Lapad peninsula offering luxury canopy daybeds that provide excellent sun protection. The beach features a gentle, shallow pebble shoreline perfect for dipping baby Mary Claire's toes into the calm water.

Mid-day 12-1:30pm / 1.5h

Dubrovnik Maritime Museum

Housed inside the cool stone walls of St. John's Fortress, this museum offers wide, stroller-accessible ground floors. It serves as an excellent mid-day air-conditioned escape from the intense Mediterranean sun while keeping the family culturally engaged.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Rector's Palace

A stunning Gothic-Renaissance palace featuring a wide, flat, and shaded central atrium. The stone architecture keeps the interior naturally cool, making it a comfortable spot for a quiet historical walk with an infant in a carrier.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Trsteno Arboretum

A historic Renaissance garden located just north of the city, offering towering plane trees and lush exotic plants that cast deep, cool shade. The main pathways are wide and manageable with an all-terrain stroller, culminating in a gentle fountain.

Sunset 6-7:30pm / 1.5h

Mount Srd Viewpoint

Skip the cable car line and hire a private driver to take you to the summit in an air-conditioned luxury SUV. This allows you to experience the breathtaking high-altitude sunset views without exposing the baby to crowds or long standing wait times.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant 360

This Michelin-starred landmark offers a breathtaking setting atop the historic city walls. Book the earliest 6:30 PM seating on the spacious outdoor terrace, which provides a quieter atmosphere and stunning harbor views before the late-night dining crowd arrives.

Proto Fish Restaurant

A legendary seafood institution in the heart of Old Town featuring a quiet, elegant rooftop terrace. Highly accommodating to families during early lunch service, they offer comfortable seating and fresh, simply prepared fish that is easy for a toddler to sample.

Nautika Restaurant

Arguably the most famous view in Croatia, situated right on the water edge outside Pile Gate. Request a spacious terrace corner table during early dining hours to comfortably park a stroller and enjoy the gentle, soothing sound of the waves.

Banje Beach Restaurant

A chic, beachfront dining spot offering easy stroller ramp access directly from the road. The open-air terrace serves high-end Mediterranean dishes, and the soft ambient music and ocean breeze make it highly forgiving of any baby noises.

Panorama Restaurant

Located at the upper station of the Cable Car, this spot offers unparalleled views. The outdoor terrace is spacious, equipped with sturdy highchairs, and the constant light breeze keeps the temperature perfect for an early outdoor lunch with the family.
