What to do

Early Morning / 2h

Dubrovnik City Walls

Walk the historic medieval ramparts for breathtaking views of the sea and terracotta rooftops. Toddlers will need to be in a carrier due to steep stone steps and narrow paths.

Half-Day / 3-4h

Lokrum Island

Take a short, exciting ferry ride to see wild peacocks and swim in the shallow, salty "Dead Sea" lake. It is a fantastic shaded escape where toddlers can splash safely.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Dubrovnik Cable Car

Ride up to Mount Srd for a spectacular bird's-eye view of the entire Old Town. The quick ride is thrilling for little ones, and the summit station is stroller-accessible.

Morning / 3h

Copacabana Beach

A pebble-and-sand beach in Lapad featuring shallow, calm water and comfortable sun lounger rentals. Toddlers will love playing in the gentle surf and the playground nearby.

Morning / 1h

Gundulić Square Morning Market

Explore this bustling open-air market in the heart of the Old Town filled with fresh seasonal fruits, honey, and lavender. It is a sensory delight for toddlers and a great spot to grab a healthy snack.

Midday / 1-2h

Maritime Museum

Located inside the historic St. John's Fort, this museum features model ships and fascinating maritime artifacts. The cool stone interior offers a quiet, air-conditioned refuge from the midday heat.

Evening / 2h

Uvala Lapad Sunset Promenade

A completely pedestrianized walkway lined with playgrounds, cafes, and ice cream shops. It is the ultimate hassle-free spot to let toddlers run free while parents relax with a drink.

Morning / 2-3h

Trsteno Arboretum

Wander through lush botanical gardens featuring massive ancient plane trees and a beautiful fountain with goldfish. The wide, quiet gravel paths are stroller-friendly and perfect for a peaceful family walk.

Afternoon / 1h

Dubrovnik Aquarium

Housed inside the thick fortress walls, this small aquarium showcases local Adriatic marine life. It is the perfect size for toddlers with short attention spans to see sea stars and fish up close.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pantarul

Order the homemade pasta with local beef ragout or the fresh catch of the day at this highly welcoming, family-friendly neighborhood bistro.

Lacroma Restaurant

Sit outdoors beneath the pine trees on Lokrum Island and enjoy a kid-approved wood-fired pizza or a light Mediterranean risotto.

Gradska Kavana Arsenal

Grab a table overlooking the historic old harbor and order the traditional Dubrovačka Rozata, a delicious local custard pudding kids love.

Pizzeria Castro

Located in a quiet square near the cathedral, this is the best spot to get a classic, thin-crust Margherita pizza for a quick and easy family lunch.

Dolce Vita

Tucked down a quiet Old Town alley, this cozy dessert spot is the ultimate place to get artisanal gelato and chocolate-filled crepes.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller for a sturdy baby carrier when visiting the Old Town, as the historic streets are filled with steep stone stairs. Always carry cash for public restrooms and buy a Dubrovnik Pass to save on top family attractions.
