Cinque Terre

← back to travel board
Cinque Terre welcomes families with its pastel-colored villages, sparkling turquoise waters, and car-free streets perfect for wandering. While the dramatic cliffs demand some caution, the gentle sea breezes and slow-paced Italian charm create an unforgettable coastal playground. Pack your sturdiest baby carrier and prepare for days filled with beach play, sweet gelato, and scenic train rides.
🚕 Getting there: ~1.5 hour private transfer from Pisa Airport to Monterosso al Mare, or a 1.25 hour train ride via La Spezia

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Fegina Beach in Monterosso

The largest and only true sandy beach in Cinque Terre, perfect for splashing in the calm, shallow waters. Toddlers will love building sandcastles while parents relax under the iconic orange and blue umbrellas.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Cinque Terre Express Train Ride

Hop between the five cliffside villages on this quick, scenic train that tunnels through the mountains. Toddlers will be thrilled by the dark tunnels and sudden bursts of glittering ocean views.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Piazza Garibaldi Playground

A charming public square in Monterosso's old town featuring a small, fenced-in play area with swings and slides. It is a fantastic spot for toddlers to burn off energy safely while parents enjoy a nearby espresso.

Morning / 1-2h

Vernazza Harbor and Sandy Cove

The natural harbor of Vernazza features a small, protected sandy area where toddlers can dip their toes safely away from open waves. Sit on the stone steps with a slice of focaccia and watch the colorful wooden boats bobbing in the water.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Manarola Scenic Waterfront Walk

A paved, relatively flat pathway leading from the harbor to the playground at Punta Bonfiglio. It offers stunning, postcard-perfect views of the colorful town without the strenuous stairs, making it highly toddler-friendly.

Midday / 1.5h

Pesto Making Experience at Nessun Dorma

Learn the traditional way to make authentic Ligurian pesto using a mortar and pestle high above Manarola. Children love the tactile experience of mashing fresh basil leaves, and the panoramic views are unbeatable.

Afternoon / 1h

Ferry Boat Ride between Villages

Trade the train for a public ferry ride to see the dramatic cliffs and pastel towns from the water. Toddlers will love the wind in their hair and the boat's gentle rocking motion on this scenic voyage.

Morning / 2h

Corniglia Village Shuttle and Alleys

Skip the 382 steps of the Lardarina by taking the green shuttle bus from the station straight into this quiet, car-free village. Its flat, narrow stone alleys are peaceful and perfect for a slow-paced toddler stroll.

Afternoon / 1h

Gelato Walk in Riomaggiore

Stroll down the lively main street of Riomaggiore with a melting cone of artisanal gelato. It is the ultimate toddler reward and a delicious way to cool down after exploring the steep town.

🍽️ Where to eat

Cantina di Miky

Order the fresh hand-cut trofie pasta with local basil pesto and the kid-friendly handmade potato gnocchi.

Ristorante Belforte

Request a terrace table overlooking the sea and order the fresh seafood spaghetti or the simple, kid-approved pasta al pomodoro.

Nessun Dorma

Download their app to queue for a table and order the massive local charcuterie board loaded with focaccia, cheeses, and fresh melon.

Pizzeria La Lampara

Enjoy a relaxed family dinner featuring their authentic wood-fired Margherita pizzas and freshly fried local seafood cones.

Il Frantoio

Grab a variety of warm, freshly baked focaccia bread—especially the classic olive oil and rosemary version that toddlers adore.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller for a high-quality carrier, as the endless steps, trains, and cobblestones across all five villages make wheels highly impractical. Purchase the Cinque Terre Card family option for unlimited train rides and easy access to station restrooms.
