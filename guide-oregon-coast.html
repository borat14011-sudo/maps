Oregon Coast

← back to travel board
The Oregon Coast around Cannon Beach is a magical playground where dramatic sea stacks rise from the mist and ancient rainforests meet the Pacific. Kids can chase low tides to discover neon-colored sea stars, while parents soak in the rugged beauty of Haystack Rock. It is the ultimate laid-back coastal escape for families seeking sandcastles, short hikes, and cozy seaside eats.
🚕 Getting there: ~1.5 to 2 hours drive from Portland International Airport (PDX) via rental car, which is highly recommended for exploring the scenic coastline.

What to do

Morning at low tide / 2 hours

Haystack Rock Tide Pools

Explore the base of this massive 235-foot sea stack at low tide to find colorful starfish, anemones, and crabs. It's flat and sandy, making it incredibly easy for toddlers to toddle right up to the marine life.

Afternoon / 1-2 hours

Ecola State Park Overlook

Take in the breathtaking, postcard-perfect views of the coastline and lighthouse from easy paved pathways. The grassy viewpoint areas are perfect for toddlers to stretch their legs safely.

Morning at low tide / 2 hours

Hug Point State Recreation Site

Walk along the beach to discover seasonal waterfalls, sea caves, and old stagecoach wheel ruts carved into the rock. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, freshwater creek that empties onto the sand.

Late Afternoon / 1.5 hours

Arcadia Beach Tidepools

A quieter alternative to Haystack Rock, offering excellent rock formations and arches to explore. The soft sand and gentle slope make it a wonderful spot for building sandcastles with little ones.

Friday Evening (Summer) / 1-2 hours

Manzanita Farmers Market

Stroll through this lively seasonal market to sample fresh berries, local pastries, and listen to live music. It is highly stroller-friendly and a great way to pick up picnic supplies.

Morning / 2-3 hours

Short Sand Beach Trail

Walk through a lush, stroller-accessible rainforest canopy down to a sheltered cove popular with surfers. The stroller-friendly gravel path means even the youngest family members can enjoy the deep green forest.

Midday / 1 hour

Cannon Beach City Park Playground

Let the kids burn off energy on this wonderful wooden play structure equipped with swings, slides, and a climbing wall. It's a perfect, toddler-safe harbor when you need a quick break from the sand.

Morning / 1-2 hours

Seaside Aquarium

One of the oldest aquariums on the West Coast, where kids can personally feed pieces of fish to noisy, playful harbor seals. It is small, completely indoor, and highly engaging for toddlers.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Bruce's Candy Kitchen

Step into this pink-and-white striped wonderland to watch saltwater taffy being pulled and wrapped on vintage machines. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the colors, and you can grab a bag of legendary local sweets.

🍽️ Where to eat

Pelican Brewing Company

Order the famous fish and chips alongside a flight of craft beers in their highly spacious, family-friendly dining room.

Sleepy Monk Coffee Roasters

Grab a morning scoop of scratch-made marionberry scones and a smooth salted caramel latte to enjoy on the beach.

Mo's Restaurant Cannon Beach

Get a hot, comforting bowl of their legendary creamy clam chowder served in a sourdough bread bowl right next to the beach.

Pizza a' ffettta

Grab giant, kid-pleasing slices of fresh New York-style pepperoni pizza to go for an easy, delicious beach picnic.

Lazy Susan Cafe

Enjoy a cozy, cottage-style family breakfast featuring their famous sourdough waffles topped with fresh local berries.
