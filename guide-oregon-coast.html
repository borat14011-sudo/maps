Oregon Coast luxury with baby-friendly oceanfront

Oregon Coast

← back to travel board
Cannon Beach offers a dramatic coastline of towering sea stacks and soft sands, perfect for a serene family escape. The iconic Haystack Rock rises from the shore, providing a stunning backdrop for stroller walks along the beach. For infants, the gentle waves and wide, hard-packed sand make beach access easy with a baby carrier or stroller. The nearby Ecola State Park features a calm, protected lagoon ideal for splashing with little ones.
🚕 Getting there: From Portland International Airport (PDX) to Cannon Beach, expect a 1.5-hour drive via US-26. Book a private transfer with infant car seat and stroller storage, such as Oregon Coast Car Service or Prestige Ride for seamless door-to-door luxury.

What to do

Late morning 10am-12pm (low tide)

Haystack Rock

This iconic sea stack reveals tide pools at low tide, fascinating for all ages. Baby-friendly: hard-packed sand, stroller accessible, and shallow water safe for supervised splashing. Check tide charts for best exposure.

Early morning 9-11am

Ecola State Park – Indian Beach

A crescent cove with calm waters, driftwood, and forested trails. Stroller-accessible via the main path from the parking lot. Plenty of shaded spots for nursing or naps under the pines.

1-2 hours flexible

Cannon Beach Promenade

A paved, stroller-friendly walkway along the beachfront with benches and ocean views. Perfect for a morning stroll with coffee and a baby in the buggy. No stairs or cobblestones.

2 hours (drive 40 min from Cannon Beach)

Tillamook Cheese Factory

Interactive cheese-making tour and self-guided viewing gallery. Baby-friendly: highchairs in the café, stroller parking, and a calm indoor environment with air conditioning. Try the squeaky cheese curds.

1.5 hours

Seaside Carousel & Promenade

Historic 1900s carousel with hand-painted animals (infant can sit in your lap). Adjacent paved promenade with gentle breeze and shade. Nearby aquarium and candy shop for older toddlers.

Late afternoon 2-4pm (low tide)

Hug Point State Recreation Site

A small beach with tide pools, a waterfall, and sea caves. Low tide reveals a historic stagecoach route carved into the cliff. Stroller-friendly only on the sand; carriers recommended for exploring caves.

1 hour (drive 30 min from Cannon Beach)

Cape Meares Lighthouse & Octopus Tree

Short, paved path to the lighthouse with stunning coastal views. The Octopus Tree is a unique Sitka spruce. Stroller-friendly trail, shaded benches, and a quiet atmosphere – skip if windy or foggy.

45 min (drive 30 min)

Astoria Column

Climb the spiral staircase for panoramic views (best for parents; baby carrier works, but stairs are narrow). Base area has picnic tables and a grassy lawn for tummy time. Quiet morning visit avoids crowds.

1.5-2 hours (drive 40 min from Cannon Beach)

Fort Clatsop (Lewis & Clark National Historical Park)

Reconstructed fort with a gentle woodland boardwalk, perfect for strollers. Visitor center with baby changing facilities and climate control. Rangers offer family-friendly talks. Calm, shaded environment ideal for a midday break.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Stephanie Inn Dining Room

A refined oceanfront restaurant with an early seating (5pm) and highchairs. Signature Dungeness crab cake and Northwest salmon. Quiet, elegant atmosphere with blackout curtains for sleeping infants.

Newmans at 988

Upscale farm-to-table venue in a cozy historic house. Highchairs available, outdoor terrace in summer, and a kids' menu with fresh local ingredients. Try the Oregon albacore tuna with seasonal vegetables.

Pelican Brewing Company (Cannon Beach)

Beachfront brewpub with a wide patio, highchairs, and a relaxed vibe. Signature Pelican Pub Pale Ale and fish & chips. Early lunch (11:30am) is quiet; baby-friendly noise level moderate.

Ecola Seafoods Restaurant & Market

Casual, family-owned seafood spot with a market for takeout. Highchairs, no reservations, but friendly for infants. Order the clam chowder bread bowl and grilled halibut. Outdoor picnic tables with ocean view.

Driftwood Inn & Restaurant

A Cannon Beach institution with traditional seafood and a cozy fireplace. Highchairs, early bird dinner (4-6pm), and a kids' menu. The Driftwood salmon is a must. Quiet weeknight dining recommended.
