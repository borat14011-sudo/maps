San Salvador

← back to travel board
San Salvador surprises families with its lush volcanic valleys, vibrant public plazas, and warm Salvadoran hospitality. Navigating the capital reveals a treasure trove of interactive museums, stroller-friendly parks, and colorful markets. It is an unexpected, sensory-rich playground where toddler-friendly pockets balance rich cultural history.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Saburo Hirao

A lush, terraced park featuring Japanese-style gardens, multiple play areas, and a small natural history museum. Toddlers will love chasing leaves along the paved paths and exploring the whimsical, retro playground structures.

Morning / 2h

El Boquerón National Park

Hike along the crater rim of the San Salvador Volcano on flat, pine-scented walking trails with dramatic views. The cool mountain air is refreshing, and the main trail is easy enough for toddlers to walk or be carried in a backpack.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Tin Marín Museo de los Niños

An interactive, hands-on science and sensory museum featuring a giant model airplane, a butterfly garden, and a pretend supermarket. It has a dedicated toddler sensory zone designed specifically for safe, soft-play exploration.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Libertad and Centro Histórico

This vibrant public square is framed by the stunning El Rosario Church and filled with lively street musicians. The wide pedestrian plaza is perfect for toddlers to watch pigeons, though you should keep them close due to the bustling crowds.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

A bustling, multi-block market exploding with colorful tropical fruits, handcrafted toys, and local textiles. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the sights and smells, but a baby carrier is highly recommended here instead of a stroller.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Parque Cuscatlán

This newly renovated, modern urban park offers vast lawns, dedicated play zones, and wide, stroller-friendly concrete walkways shaded by giant trees. It is an ideal spot for a relaxing family picnic and letting toddlers run wild on the manicured grass.

Morning / 1-2h

Jardín Botánico La Laguna

Built inside a dormant volcanic crater, this lush botanical garden features peaceful ponds filled with giant koi fish and turtles. Toddlers will love feeding the fish and spotting iguanas lounging on the tropical plants along the paved loop.

Morning / 1h

Palacio Nacional

Explore the grand, colorful rooms of this historic palace, which surround a central courtyard filled with towering palm trees. The open-air courtyard is a peaceful, enclosed space where toddlers can stretch their legs away from busy city traffic.

Evening / 2-3h

Planes de Renderos

A breezy hilltop region famous for panoramic city views, weekend puppet shows, and the absolute best pupuserías. Toddlers will enjoy the festive atmosphere and the soft, easy-to-chew cheese pupusas.

Where to eat

  • Pupusería El Típico Margoth — A beloved, spacious local chain offering high-quality, authentic pupusas that are perfectly sized and mild enough for toddler hands.
  • El Mercadito at Multiplaza — A clean, upscale food hall featuring a wide variety of local eats, offering high chairs, air conditioning, and plenty of space for strollers.
  • Cadejo Brewing Company — This craft brewery has a surprisingly family-friendly outdoor garden patio, an excellent kids' menu, and a relaxed vibe where toddler giggles blend in easily.
  • Sorbetes Sin Rival — Introduce your little one to traditional Salvadoran artisanal ice cream served from classic wooden carts, featuring simple, sweet fruit flavors.
👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a sturdy baby carrier rather than a stroller, as many historic sidewalks are uneven, steep, or lack curb cuts. Use rideshare apps like Uber for safe, hassle-free door-to-door transit, especially during the midday heat when toddlers need air conditioning.
