Ditch the crowded Algarve for the Alentejo Coast’s raw, pine-scented beauty where wide-open Atlantic beaches meet slow-paced Portuguese farm life. It is an ideal refuge for a young family, offering chic but incredibly relaxed design hotels, stroller-accessible boardwalks, and a welcoming, child-friendly dining culture. The rhythm here is dictated by the tides and long, lazy lunches, providing a stress-free environment where your toddler can run wild in the sand while you sip crisp Alentejo white wine.
🚕 Getting there: Fly into Lisbon Humberto Delgado Airport (LIS), rent an SUV, and make the scenic 1-hour and 20-minute drive south to Comporta, or continue another hour to Vila Nova de Milfontes.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Praia da Franquia

This calm river beach in Vila Nova de Milfontes is sheltered from the heavy Atlantic waves, making it incredibly safe for toddler wading. The paved town-front promenade allows you to wheel a stroller right up to the sand's edge with ease.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Cais Palafítico da Carrasqueira

Walk along this masterpiece of popular architecture, a unique network of irregular wooden stilt piers built by local fishermen. You will want to leave the stroller in the car and use a baby carrier to safely navigate the uneven planks while enjoying the stunning estuary views.

Morning / 3h

Praia do Carvalhal

This expansive beach features excellent wooden boardwalks that make navigating the dunes with a stroller completely hassle-free. It is nestled in a valley that offers slightly calmer water than neighboring beaches, perfect for dipping tiny toes in the ocean.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Badoca Safari Park

Located just inland from the coast, this open-air wildlife park lets toddlers see giraffes, zebras, and lemurs up close on a tractor-led safari. The main pathways are wide, unpaved dirt tracks that are highly manageable with a sturdy all-terrain stroller.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Vila Nova de Milfontes

This vibrant covered market is the perfect spot to pick up fresh local berries, figs, and artisanal cheeses for easy toddler snacks. The building is entirely flat and spacious, making it a quick, sensory-rich morning stroll for a family.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Herdade da Comporta Wine Boutique

Parents can indulge in a tasting of crisp local whites while relaxing in the shaded, expansive garden area. The winery's outdoor spaces are extremely welcoming to families, allowing toddlers plenty of room to stretch their legs on the grass.

Late Afternoon / 1.5h

Praia do Malhão

This raw, breathtaking beach is famous for its dramatic cliffs and beautifully maintained wooden eco-boardwalks running along the cliffside. It is a fantastic spot for a scenic coastal walk with a baby carrier to watch the local surfers below.

Morning / 2-3h

Praia da Comporta

This iconic beach offers wide, pristine white sands and exceptionally wide wooden boardwalks leading directly from the parking lot. The stroller roll is seamless, and the on-site amenities make diaper changes and beachside logistics incredibly simple.

Morning / 2h

Ruínas Romanas de Miróbriga

Located just a short drive inland, these remarkably preserved Roman ruins feature ancient paved streets, a forum, and the only Roman hippodrome in Portugal. The dirt pathways are shaded by beautiful pine trees, offering a peaceful, low-stress walk with a baby carrier.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sublime Comporta Beach Club

Order the grilled sea bass with coriander rice. The open-air, upscale beachside pavilion is highly spacious, and the roaring Atlantic surf easily drowns out any toddler noise.

A Tasca do Celso

Order the 'Secretos de Porco Preto' (Iberian black pork). The warm, professional staff treats children like royalty, and their early 7:00 PM opening slot is perfect for beating the late-night European dinner rush.

O Dinis - Restaurante dos Pescadores

Order the ultra-fresh grilled sardines. This rustic, beloved seafood shack sits directly on the sand, meaning your toddler can play happily with a bucket and spade right next to your table.

Restaurante Sal

Order the signature black rice with cuttlefish and shrimp. It offers a lively, buzzy atmosphere on the dunes where the outdoor deck easily accommodates large strollers and high chairs.

Padaria da Barbosa

Order a traditional 'Pão de Ló' sponge cake and fresh croissants. This cozy café features a quiet, sun-dappled backyard patio where your baby can safely crawl around while you enjoy your morning espresso.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Sublime Comporta Country Retreat & Spa

Tucked away in a stunning pine forest, this luxury resort offers multi-bedroom cabana villas with private pools, a dedicated kids' club, and top-tier complimentary baby gear.

Quinta da Comporta - Wellness Boutique Resort

This chic resort overlooking the rice fields features spacious family suites, a gorgeous heated infinity pool, and a remarkably calm environment that warmly welcomes young children.

Herdade do Toural

Located just south of Milfontes, this peaceful country estate offers cozy self-catering houses, a gated and secured swimming pool, and friendly resident farm animals that toddlers will adore visiting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a sturdy SUV with robust suspension because many of the access roads to the best beaches and coastal estates are unpaved, sandy dirt tracks. Pack a high-quality baby carrier alongside your stroller, as the iconic sand dunes and historic stilt piers are not wheel-friendly. Alentejo locals are exceptionally warm toward families, so do not hesitate to book dinner tables right when restaurants open at 7:00 PM to align with toddler bedtimes.
