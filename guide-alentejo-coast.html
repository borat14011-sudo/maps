Alentejo Coast

← back to travel board
Where pine forests meet dramatic Atlantic cliffs and calm river estuaries, the Alentejo Coast offers a blissful, slow-paced family escape. Children will love running on wide golden sands, while parents savor the freshest seafood and chic, laid-back vibes of Comporta and Milfontes. It is Portugal's best-kept seaside secret, perfectly tailored for unstructured, sunny days together.
🚕 Getting there: ~1.5 to 2 hours drive via rental car from Lisbon Portela Airport down the scenic highway to the coastal towns of Comporta and Vila Nova de Milfontes.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Praia da Comporta

A gorgeous stretch of golden sand with easy boardwalk access, making it incredibly simple to navigate with a stroller. Toddlers will love splashing in the gentle shoreline tide pools during low tide.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Cais Palafítico da Carrasqueira

Walk along this unique, rustic wooden stilt pier built by local fishermen over the mudflats. Keep toddlers close or in a carrier, as they will be fascinated by the little wooden boats bobbing in the tide.

Morning / 3-4h

Badoca Safari Park

Embark on an open-air safari truck to spot giraffes, zebras, and exotic birds in a spacious, natural environment. It is highly engaging for toddlers, who will also love the petting zoo and green play areas.

Morning / 2-3h

Praia do Farol

Located where the Mira River meets the Atlantic, this beach offers incredibly calm, warm waters with no big waves. It is the ultimate safe-haven for toddlers to paddle and build sandcastles.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Municipal de Vila Nova de Milfontes

A vibrant covered market filled with local farmers selling sweet, fresh berries, local cheeses, and baked goods. Toddlers will enjoy the lively sights and smells, and it is the perfect spot to stock up on healthy beach snacks.

Morning / 2-3h

Dolphin Watching in the Sado Estuary

Board a spacious catamaran from nearby Tróia to spot the resident pod of friendly bottlenose dolphins. The boat is stroller-accessible and the calm estuary waters ensure a smooth ride for sensitive toddler tummies.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Herdade da Comporta Rice Fields

Explore the flat, vibrant green rice paddies on foot or by renting an e-bike with a child seat. Toddlers will love watching the storks nesting high on the wooden posts along the peaceful paths.

Morning / 2h

Ruins of Miróbriga

Step back in time at these ancient Roman ruins featuring the only intact Roman hippodrome in Portugal. The wide-open grassy pathways are perfect for toddlers to run around safely away from cars.

Morning or Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Fishermen's Trail Dune Walks

Walk a stroller-friendly section of the Rota Vicentina wooden boardwalks winding through coastal scrub and dunes. It offers breathtaking ocean views while keeping little feet safe and sand-free.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sublime Comporta Beach Club

Order the exquisite grilled sea bass served beachside while kids play in the soft sand right next to your table.

Tasquita do Amado

Feast on a steaming pot of fresh clams cooked with garlic and cilantro in a highly welcoming, family-friendly setting.

O Dinis - Restaurante dos Pescadores

Grab a table directly on the sand at Carvalhal beach and order the charcoal-grilled sea bream with local sweet potatoes.

A Choupana

Indulge in a rich, traditional seafood rice while overlooking the dramatic cliffs of Vila Nova de Milfontes.

Pastelaria Mabi

Treat the family to their legendary, warm, cream-filled croissants and artisanal fruit ice creams, a beloved local tradition.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a sturdy stroller with large wheels or bring an ergonomic carrier, as many beach accesses use wooden boardwalks that can be sandy. Always pack windbreakers and light layers for toddlers, as the Atlantic breeze can pick up quickly in the late afternoon despite the warm daytime sun.
