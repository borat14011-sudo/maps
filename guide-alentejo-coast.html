Alentejo Coast

← back to travel board
The Alentejo Coast invites families into a world of endless golden sand dunes, storks nesting on dramatic cliffs, and whitewashed villages. Between the chic, pine-scented shores of Comporta and the calm river estuaries of Vila Nova de Milfontes, time slows down to a gentle, toddler-friendly pace. It is a playground of wild Atlantic beauty, safe shallow lagoons, and rustic-chic charm designed for easy memory-making.
🚕 Getting there: ~1 hour 15 min drive from Lisbon Airport to Comporta, or ~2 hours to Vila Nova de Milfontes via rental car, which is highly recommended for exploring this region.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Praia da Franquia

Located on the calm Mira River estuary in Vila Nova de Milfontes, this beach offers wave-free, shallow waters that are incredibly safe for toddlers to splash in. Parents can relax on the soft sand while watching paddleboards glide by.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Cais Palafítico da Carrasqueira

Wander along this unique, rustic wooden stilt pier built by local fishermen, offering a surreal walk over the tranquil mudflats. Keep toddlers close or in a carrier as there are no safety railings over the water.

Morning / 4-5h

Badoca Safari Park

Take a tractor-led safari to see giraffes, zebras, and lemurs roaming freely in an open landscape just inland from the coast. Toddlers will love the petting zoo and the shady picnic areas under the giant cork oaks.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Praia do Carvalhal

This wide, golden-sand beach features wooden boardwalks for easy stroller access and a relatively sheltered shoreline. It is perfect for building sandcastles and has excellent family-friendly beachside dining options.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal de Vila Nova de Milfontes

Immerse yourselves in local life at this vibrant indoor market filled with fresh Atlantic fish, sweet local berries, and colorful vegetables. Toddlers will enjoy picking out fresh figs and sweet bread for an easy afternoon picnic.

Morning / 2-3h

Sado Estuary Dolphin Watching

Embark on a calm boat tour from nearby Setúbal or Troia to spot the resident pod of bottlenose dolphins playing in the estuary. Most boats are family-friendly with plenty of shade, seating, and safety vests for little ones.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Cabo Sardão Lighthouse and Cliffs

Marvel at the dramatic ocean views and look for white storks nesting on the sheer cliff faces—the only place in the world where they do this. The walking paths are wide and stroller-accessible, though keeping a close eye on wandering toddlers is a must.

Morning / 2-3h

Praia do Malhão Low Tide Pools

Explore the dramatic rock formations and gentle, crystal-clear tide pools that appear at low tide, perfect for toddlers to spot tiny crabs and sea anemones. The dramatic dune backdrop makes for incredible family photos.

Early Morning / 1-2h

Herdade da Comporta Rice Fields

Stroll or rent a family bike to cruise along the flat, scenic dirt paths winding through vibrant green rice paddies. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the numerous storks and herons wading in the shallow water.

🍽️ Where to eat

Sublime Comporta Beach Club

Order the grilled sea bass or the fresh seafood rice while the kids play in the sand right next to your outdoor deck table.

Restaurant A Choupana

Sit directly overlooking the Atlantic beach in Vila Nova de Milfontes and order the freshly grilled sardines or sea bream.

Museu do Arroz

Savor their legendary, rich duck rice (Arroz de Pato) in this converted historic rice factory overlooking the peaceful green fields.

Tasquita do Mar

Try the fresh clams cooked in olive oil and garlic alongside octopus salad in this welcoming, casual spot that makes families feel right at home.

Pastelaria Mabi

Treat the family to their legendary homemade ice cream—especially the dark chocolate or strawberry flavors—and a warm, flaky pastel de nata.

👨‍👩‍👧 Renting a car is essential for navigating between the spread-out beaches and towns of the Alentejo, and always pack high-quality mosquito repellent for the Comporta rice field areas. Opt for calm river beaches like Praia da Franquia for wave-free paddling, and bring a sturdy baby carrier as deep beach sand makes strollers difficult to push.
