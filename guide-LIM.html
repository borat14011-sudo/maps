Lima

← back to travel board
Perched on dramatic Pacific cliffs, Lima blends ancient history with vibrant coastal energy that delights travelers of all ages. From splashing in synchronized light fountains to tasting sweet churros in historic plazas, the city offers a sensory playground for families. With its breezy parks and stroller-friendly boardwalks, it is an unexpectedly easy South American destination to explore with little ones.

What to do

Evening / 2h

Circuito Mágico del Agua (Magic Water Circuit)

Marvel at the world's largest fountain complex in a public park, featuring spectacular light shows and interactive water tunnels. Toddlers will love running through the splash-friendly 'Tunnel of Surprises'—just remember to bring a change of dry clothes!

Morning / 3-4h

Parque de las Leyendas (Legends Park Zoo)

This massive zoo built around ancient pre-Inca ruins lets families spot Andean condors, penguins, and jaguars. It is highly stroller-accessible and features wide grassy areas for toddlers to stretch their legs.

Afternoon / 2h

El Malecón and Parque del Amor

Stroll along the clifftop boardwalk, taking in sweeping ocean views and the vibrant mosaic walls of Love Park. Toddlers can burn off energy at the excellent, fenced-in 'Parque del Faro' playground nearby.

Morning / 1.5h

Huaca Pucllana Archaeological Site

Explore a towering adobe pyramid built right in the middle of modern Miraflores. The flat, sandy paths are easy for toddlers to walk, and they will adore meeting the resident llamas and alpacas at the end of the tour.

Morning / 1h

Mercado Surquillo No. 1

Immerse your senses in a bustling local market filled with colorful mounds of exotic Amazonian fruits, native potatoes, and fresh juices. Toddlers will love tasting sweet granadilla fruit cracked open right in front of them.

Morning / 1h

Chorrillos Boat Ride at Pescadores

Take a short, traditional wooden boat ride from the artisanal fishing port of Chorrillos to see pelicans up close and view the coastline. Life jackets are provided, but keep a firm grip on toddlers as the wooden docks can be slippery.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Barranco Street Art and Bridge of Sighs

Wander through Lima’s colorful bohemian district to admire whimsical murals and cross the famous wooden Puente de los Suspiros. Toddlers will enjoy the pedestrian-only pathways and the sweet treats sold by vendors near the main plaza.

Afternoon / 1h

LUM Gardens & Clifftop Walk

While the museum inside is for older visitors, the surrounding architectural gardens and clifftop paths offer peaceful, car-free spaces to run. Toddlers can watch paragliders launch into the Pacific breeze from the cliffs above.

Morning / 2h

Plaza Mayor (Plaza de Armas) and Cathedral

Lima's historic heart features grand colonial architecture, a beautiful central fountain, and the changing of the guard. The wide, pedestrianized plaza is perfect for toddlers to chase pigeons while parents admire the yellow facades.

Where to eat

  • Churros at Manolo — A classic Miraflores café famous for its freshly fried, sugar-dusted churros filled with dulce de leche that kids will absolutely devour.
  • Causa Rellena — This cold, layered potato dish mashed with lime and mild yellow chili, stuffed with avocado and chicken salad, is soft, non-spicy, and a major hit with toddlers.
  • La Lucha Sanguchería Criolla — A lively, family-friendly sandwich spot serving delicious roasted pork sandwiches alongside the best thick-cut, native Peruvian french fries in the city.
  • Lucuma Gelato at Laritza D' — An iconic local parlor offering ice cream made from lucuma, a native fruit that tastes like maple and sweet potato, providing a refreshing treat for little ones.
👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to official ride-hailing apps like Uber or Cabify to navigate Lima's chaotic traffic safely with a toddler in tow. Keep in mind that while Miraflores and Barranco are highly walkable, historic downtown sidewalks can be narrow and bumpy, making a lightweight, sturdy umbrella stroller your best companion.
