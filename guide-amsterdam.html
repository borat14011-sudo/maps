Amsterdam

← back to travel board
Amsterdam is incredibly flat, compact, and navigable, making it an elite destination for a young family transitioning from the car-centric lifestyle of Houston. Broad canal-side paths, pristine parks, and a highly efficient tram system ensure stress-free transit with a stroller in tow. You get world-class culture, design, and dining without the rigid, high-stress vibe of other European capitals.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 minute taxi ride from Schiphol Airport to central hotels, easily arranged via the official airport taxi queue or by pre-booking a private transfer with a guaranteed toddler car seat.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Groot Melkhuis inside Vondelpark

A leafy oasis in the city's main park featuring a dedicated toddler playground and a canal-side terrace. It is perfect for letting your toddler run wild while you enjoy a local craft beer or coffee.

Morning / 3-4h

ARTIS Amsterdam Royal Zoo

One of Europe's oldest zoos, featuring wide paved paths, historic architecture, and a fantastic indoor butterfly pavilion. Strollers are easily pushed throughout, and there are plenty of clean family changing facilities.

Afternoon / 1-2h

NEMO Science Museum Rooftop

An iconic giant green hull building with a massive, free-to-access stepped roof plaza featuring interactive water play installations. Take the elevator to the top for panoramic city views while your little one splashes around.

Morning / 2-3h

Amstelpark Train (Amstelpark Trein)

A beautifully manicured park away from the tourist core that features a vintage miniature railway ride. The park is highly stroller-accessible and hosts a petting zoo and massive playground.

Morning / 2h

Rijksmuseum Gardens

The museum's manicured public gardens are completely free and feature fountains, sculptures, and lawns perfect for a toddler crawl break. Inside, the massive museum has excellent elevator access for strollers, though booking early morning slots is crucial to avoid crowds.

Morning / 1-2h

Albert Cuyp Market

Europe's largest outdoor market, ideal for sensory exploration and tasting hot, freshly made stroopwafels from the Rudy's Stroopwafels stand. Go right at 9:00 AM when the stalls open to easily maneuver your stroller before the crowds pack the lanes.

Afternoon / 2h

The National Maritime Museum (Het Scheepvaartmuseum)

Features a spectacular, glass-roofed central courtyard that is completely weather-proof and perfect for stroller walks on rainy days. Kids love looking at the life-sized replica of the 18th-century merchant ship docked outside.

Morning / 1-2h

Hortus Botanicus Amsterdam

One of the oldest botanical gardens in the world, offering a peaceful, warm escape inside its giant three-climate greenhouse. The paved, flat paths make stroller navigation seamless while your toddler gazes at giant water lilies.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Westerpark Paddling Pool (Westergasfabriek)

A sprawling creative park complex featuring a shallow, clean concrete wading pool that is a hotspot for local toddlers in the summer. Grab an espresso from nearby Espressofabriek while your daughter splashes around.

🍽️ Where to eat

Dignita Hoftuin

Order the classic Benny Boy poached eggs on sourdough while sitting in their glass pavilion overlooking a quiet, hidden courtyard garden. It is exceptionally spacious with ramp access, high chairs, and plenty of room for parked strollers.

Pllek

Take the free, stroller-friendly ferry to NDSM Wharf and order their organic beef burger alongside a local draft beer. The restaurant is housed in a massive industrial hangar with a private sandy beach where your toddler can play in the sand while you dine.

Bakers & Roasters (De Pijp)

Order the legendary Blueberry Pancakes or New Zealand-style savory breakfast plates. It is highly vibrant and loud enough to easily mask any toddler tantrums, featuring incredibly friendly staff, high chairs, and baby-changing facilities.

Restaurant De Kas

Indulge in a world-class, farm-to-table lunch menu served inside a magnificent, light-filled 1920s greenhouse. Booking an early lunch sitting is highly welcoming to families, offering a peaceful garden setting and plenty of space between tables.

The Pancake Bakery

Savor a traditional Dutch savory pancake loaded with bacon and cheese inside this historic 17th-century warehouse. Arrive right at their 11:00 AM opening to secure a spacious ground-floor table that easily accommodates a high chair.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Conservatorium Hotel

A luxury masterpiece in the Museum Quarter offering gorgeous, spacious multi-level suites and top-tier wooden cribs. The indoor lap pool is highly family-friendly during designated hours, and you are steps away from Vondelpark.

Hotel Jakarta Amsterdam

A spectacular, eco-luxury hotel featuring a central subtropical indoor garden that feels like a rainforest. It offers incredibly spacious family rooms, a warm indoor pool, and direct, elevator-accessible waterfront views.

Pulitzer Amsterdam

Set within 25 restored canal houses, this luxury property features a peaceful, enclosed private inner courtyard garden where your toddler can safely toddle. They provide exceptional, personalized family amenities including custom baby bath kits, high-end cribs, and coloring books.

👨‍👩‍👧 Never step or stop in the red-asphalt bike lanes, as cyclists move fast and expect pedestrians to stay on the sidewalk. Utilize the highly accessible tram system by boarding through the designated double doors marked with a stroller icon, where conductors will assist you. Pre-book timed entry tickets for major sights like the Rijksmuseum weeks in advance, selecting the first slot of the day to beat both the crowds and naptime.
