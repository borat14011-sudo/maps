Amsterdam

← back to travel board
Amsterdam is a fairytale landscape of winding canals, arched bridges, and vibrant green parks that welcomes little explorers with open arms. Easily navigated by cargo bike or stroller, this flat city turns history and culture into a playground of open-air wonders. From warm pancake feasts to sensory-friendly museums, it is the ultimate stress-free European destination for families.
🚕 Getting there: ~20 min train ride from Schiphol Airport to Amsterdam Centraal, or ~25-35 min taxi directly to city center hotels

What to do

Morning / 2h

Vondelpark

Let the kids run wild at the Groot Melkhuis playground or splash in the dedicated paddling pool while you enjoy a lakeside coffee. Toddlers will love the wooden climbing structures and spotting the park's famous wild green parakeets.

Morning / 3h

ARTIS Amsterdam Royal Zoo

One of Europe's oldest zoos features historic architecture, shady stroller-friendly paths, and a spectacular indoor aquarium. Toddlers will love getting up close to the free-roaming pelicans and feeding the goats at the petting zoo.

Afternoon / 3h

NEMO Science Museum

A giant green ship-shaped building packed with interactive, hands-on exhibits tailored for curious young minds. Toddlers can safely play with giant soap bubbles and sensory water experiments on the dedicated lower levels.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Albert Cuyp Market

Experience Amsterdam's largest outdoor market, packed with sensory delights, colorful flower stalls, and delicious street food. Grab a warm, freshly pressed stroopwafel for your toddler to munch on while riding in the stroller.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Canal Cruise with Blue Boat Company

See the city's historic sights from the water on a family-friendly boat complete with dedicated kid audio guides and activity packs. Toddlers will be mesmerized by passing under low stone bridges and spotting ducks along the canal walls.

Morning / 3h

Goat Farm Ridammerhoeve (Amsterdamse Bos)

Escape to this massive forest park to visit a working organic goat farm where kids can run free in the hay. Toddlers can bottle-feed baby goats, climb on wooden play tractors, and enjoy fresh goat-milk ice cream.

Morning / 2h

Het Scheepvaartmuseum (National Maritime Museum)

Climb aboard a magnificent replica 18th-century cargo ship and explore interactive maritime exhibits designed for young sailors. There is a peaceful, sensory-rich indoor play area perfect for toddlers to crawl and explore.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Hortus Botanicus

One of the oldest botanical gardens in the world offers a peaceful green escape with giant water lilies and a warm butterfly greenhouse. Toddlers will be spellbound watching hundreds of colorful butterflies flutter right past their faces.

Afternoon / 2h

Jeugdland

A massive, hidden outdoor nature playground on the edge of the city where kids can construct wooden forts and interact with farm animals. Toddlers have their own fenced-in sand and water play zone safely separated from older kids.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Pancake Bakery

Order the traditional Dutch mini-pancakes (poffertjes) smothered in powdered sugar and butter, served in a cozy 17th-century canal warehouse.

Foodhallen

Head to this trendy indoor food market where parents can enjoy craft beers and gourmet tacos while kids happily munch on wood-fired pizza slices from Renato's.

Restaurant Moeders

Enjoy hearty, traditional Dutch comfort food like 'stamppot' (mashed potatoes with vegetables and sausage) in a quirky, family-friendly space lined with photos of mothers.

Winkel 43

Treat the family to Amsterdam's legendary, deep-dish Dutch apple pie topped with a mountain of fresh whipped cream in a casual cafe setting.

Kinderkookkafé

Visit this unique cafe near Vondelpark where young children can bake their own pizzas and decorate cookies while parents relax with a coffee.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a 'bakfiets' (cargo bike) with integrated child seats to zip through the city like a local, but always watch out for the fast-moving tram tracks. Most historic cafes lack changing tables, so carry a portable changing mat and utilize larger museum restrooms whenever possible.
