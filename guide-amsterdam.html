What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Vondelpark

This sprawling English-style park features wide, paved, stroller-friendly paths and quiet shaded lawns. It is the perfect place for a gentle morning walk with Mary Claire, away from the bustling city traffic.

Morning 10am-12:30pm / 2.5h

Rijksmuseum

Fully stroller-accessible with spacious elevators and quiet nursing rooms, this world-class museum lets you admire Dutch masterpieces in a highly accommodating environment. Booking the earliest time slot ensures a peaceful, crowd-free visit.

Afternoon 2-5pm / 3h

ARTIS Amsterdam Royal Zoo

One of the oldest zoos in Europe, ARTIS features shaded botanical gardens, wide walking paths, and a tranquil aquarium. It offers a incredibly relaxed environment that is perfect for a slow-paced afternoon with an infant.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Van Gogh Museum

Boasting excellent accessibility, wide corridors, and smooth elevator access, this museum makes viewing iconic art effortless with a stroller. The climate-controlled galleries provide a comfortable temperature for babies year-round.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

NEMO Science Museum Rooftop

The stepped roof terrace of this striking building offers panoramic city views, accessible via an elevator. It features a relaxed outdoor space with gentle water installations, perfect for a breezy rest while Mary Claire naps.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Albert Cuyp Market

Stroll through this famous outdoor market during the quieter morning hours when the wide street is easy to navigate with a stroller. Kathryn and Will can sample warm, fresh stroopwafels while Mary Claire enjoys the sights and sounds from her seat.

Afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Hortus Botanicus Amsterdam

One of the oldest botanical gardens in the world, this oasis features quiet greenhouse pavilions and smooth, flat pathways. It is an exceptionally calm, warm, and peaceful sanctuary for nursing or soothing a baby.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Pure Boats Private Canal Cruise

A private, high-end salon boat charter offers a luxurious, fully enclosed cabin with central heating and plush seating. It provides a highly comfortable, private setting to nurse Mary Claire while gliding along the historic canals.

Afternoon 3-5:30pm / 2.5h

Westergasfabriek (Westerpark)

This expansive, car-free cultural park features lush green spaces, quiet paved paths, and a shallow paddling pool perfect for splashing. It provides a peaceful, local atmosphere away from the main tourist hubs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant De Kas

Housed in a stunning 1920s greenhouse in Frankendael Park, this Michelin-starred, farm-to-table destination offers spacious seating and a lovely outdoor terrace. Their early 6:00 PM dinner seating is incredibly accommodating for families with infants.

RIJKS

Located adjacent to the Rijksmuseum, this sophisticated restaurant offers a refined, modern Dutch lunch menu. The dining room is spacious enough for strollers, and the staff is highly accommodating with highchairs and early seatings.

Buffet van Odette

A chic, light-filled canal-side bistro on the Prinsengracht offering classic Dutch and French comfort food. Their beautiful outdoor terrace overlooking the water is perfect for a relaxed, stylish lunch with a sleeping baby.

Café George

This lively, French-style bistro on the Leidsegracht canal offers a vibrant yet upscale atmosphere where gentle ambient noise easily masks baby sounds. They offer comfortable booth seating, highchairs, and excellent service.

Pllek

Located a short, scenic ferry ride away in Amsterdam Noord, this spacious waterfront restaurant features an expansive sandy beach area. It is the ultimate spot for parents to enjoy a drink and casual bites while Mary Claire plays on a blanket.
