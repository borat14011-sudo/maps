What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

La Seu (Palma Cathedral)

A masterpiece of Gothic architecture with stunning rose windows. The wide, flat interior aisles are highly stroller-accessible, and the cool stone interior offers a welcome escape from the midday Mallorcan heat.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Parc de la Mar

A beautiful lakeside park situated directly below the cathedral. Its wide, paved paths are perfect for stroller cruising, and the gentle sea breeze keeps baby Mary Claire cool during afternoon naps.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Passeig del Born

Palma's most elegant boulevard, shaded by massive plane trees and lined with luxury boutiques. Perfect for a relaxing stroll with a stroller, offering plenty of stone benches for quick bottle feedings.

Afternoon 2-3pm / 1h

Jardines de S'Hort del Rei

Tranquil medieval gardens featuring beautiful fountains, shaded archways, and fragrant orange trees. It is an ideal, quiet oasis right in the city center to soothe a fussy baby away from the crowds.

Early morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Playa de Cala Mayor

A beautiful, sheltered sandy beach with calm, shallow waters and minimal waves. Excellent for dipping baby Mary Claire's toes in the Mediterranean, with easy ramp access for strollers and nearby sun loungers for rent.

Morning 10am-1pm / 3h

Ferrocarril de Sóller (Palma Station)

The historic wooden train ride offering breathtaking mountain views. Skip the full journey if baby is restless, but a short ride to Bunyola with baby safely in a carrier is an unforgettable family experience.

Morning 9:30-11:30am / 2h

Castell de Bellver

A unique circular castle offering 360-degree panoramic views of Palma Bay. While the castle interior has stairs, the surrounding shaded pine forest paths are wonderful for a carrier-assisted scenic walk.

Late afternoon 5-6:30pm / 1.5h

Plaza Mayor

Palma's central car-free square, surrounded by beautiful porticoed buildings. The open, flat terrain is perfect for pushing a stroller while enjoying street musicians whose soft melodies will entertain baby.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Es Baluard Museu d'Art Modern i Contemporani (Outdoor Terraces)

A contemporary art museum integrated into the old Renaissance city walls. The outdoor terraces and ramped walkways are completely stroller-friendly, offering stunning, breezy viewpoints of the harbor.

🍽️ Where to eat

Restaurant Pesquero

Seared wild sea bass and traditional paella. The spacious outdoor harbor-front terrace easily accommodates strollers, and the gentle ambient hum of the marina naturally soothes baby.

Es Baluard Restaurant & Lounge

Mediterranean fusion and fresh local grilled fish. Located on the historic ramparts, this venue offers a massive, breezy outdoor terrace with ramp access and early dinner service starting at 7:30 PM.

Can Joan de S'Aigo (Calle Sans)

Classic ensaimadas pastry and homemade almond ice cream. This legendary 18th-century cafe features spacious, plush booths perfect for nursing, and a warm, family-welcoming atmosphere.

Emilio Innobar

Innobar style fine dining featuring Mexican-Asian-Mediterranean fusion sashimis and tatakis. Perfect for an upscale lunch at 1:00 PM when the atmosphere is calm, sophisticated, yet accommodating to quiet infants.

Sadrassana Restaurant Coctelería

Elevated Mallorcan classic dishes like slow-cooked suckling pig. Set in a gorgeous 19th-century manor, they offer early evening dining slots and spacious private dining alcoves that provide privacy for families.
