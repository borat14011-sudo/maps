Palma de Mallorca

Palma de Mallorca blends majestic Gothic architecture with the relaxed, sun-kissed rhythm of the Balearics, making it an effortless Mediterranean playground for families. Golden-stone castles overlook sparkling turquoise bays, while pedestrian-friendly old town lanes invite slow strolls with a stroller in hand. It is a place where historic charm meets gentle beaches, keeping both parents and toddlers thoroughly enchanted.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi from Palma de Mallorca Airport (PMI) to central city hotels, or ~25 min to nearby beach resorts

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

La Seu (Palma Cathedral)

This awe-inspiring Gothic cathedral stands majestically over the sea, featuring dazzling rose windows that create a magical light show inside. The spacious pedestrian plaza outside is perfect for toddlers to safely run around and watch the pigeons.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Castell de Bellver

A rare circular castle offering breathtaking 360-degree views of the bay and a pine forest surrounding it. The outdoor playground nestled in the shaded woods just below the castle is a perfect spot for toddlers to burn off energy.

Morning / Half-day

Ferrocarril de Sóller (Vintage Train)

Ride a historic wooden train from 1912 as it clatters through the dramatic Tramuntana mountains and orange groves to the town of Sóller. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the open-air breeze, though it's best to fold up strollers before boarding.

Morning / 2-3h

Playa de Ca'n Pere Antoni

Palma's closest city beach features soft golden sand and calm, shallow waters ideal for little splashers. There is a paved promenade running alongside it, making it incredibly easy to push a stroller right up to the sand.

Mid-day / 1-2h

Mercat de l'Olivar

A bustling indoor market filled with vibrant fruit stalls, fresh cheese, and local delicacies that excite all the senses. It's a great spot to grab fresh berries and Spanish pastries for a toddler-friendly picnic lunch.

Morning / 3-4h

Palma Aquarium

Home to Europe's deepest shark tank, this world-class aquarium features interactive touch pools and an indoor jungle. The fantastic pirate-ship play area outside provides the ultimate break for toddlers to slide and climb.

Evening / 1-2h

Parc de la Mar

Set directly below the cathedral, this expansive park features a giant saltwater lake, open grassy lawns, and a fantastic gated playground. It is the ultimate spot for parents to relax with cathedral views while toddlers enjoy the swings.

Morning / 2h

Jardines de Alfabia

Located just north of Palma, these historic Moorish gardens feature enchanting water fountains, lily ponds, and lush palm trees. Toddlers will love pressing the secret buttons that activate playful water jets along the stone paths.

Afternoon / 1h

Passeig des Born

A beautiful, tree-lined pedestrian avenue in the heart of Palma, perfect for a shady stroll away from the midday sun. Grab a bench under the leafy canopy while your toddler spots the stone lion statues guarding the promenade.

🍽️ Where to eat

Ca'n Joan de S'Aigo

Visit this 300-year-old cafe to try their legendary ensaimada pastry dusted with powdered sugar alongside a cup of thick hot chocolate.

Es Baluard Restaurant & Lounge

Order the fresh Mediterranean seafood tapas and let your toddler stretch their legs on the spacious, car-free terrace set inside the old city walls.

El Camino

Arrive early at opening to share delicious local tomato bread and grilled baby squid in a chic yet highly welcoming atmosphere.

Sadrassana Restaurant Coctelería

Indulge in traditional Mallorcan slow-cooked suckling pig inside a beautiful, spacious 19th-century manor house.

La Rosa Vermutería

Feast on family-friendly classics like crispy patatas bravas and Iberico ham croquetas in a bustling, lively environment where toddler noise easily blends in.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to the pedestrianized Old Town streets when exploring with a stroller, as many historic side alleys have narrow, bumpy cobblestone sidewalks. Spanish dining hours skew quite late, so plan ahead by stocking up on fresh market fruits and snacks before restaurants close for afternoon siestas.
