Vancouver

← back to travel board
Where glassy skyscrapers meet towering ancient rainforests, Vancouver offers an effortless coastal playground for families. From gentle sandy beaches to interactive indoor spaces, the city transitions seamlessly from outdoor exploration to urban comfort. It is an exceptionally stroller-friendly destination where nature is always just a few steps away.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Stanley Park Seawall & Second Beach Pool

Walk or rent a bike with a trailer to cruise this scenic paved path, stopping at Second Beach's heated outdoor pool. Toddlers will love the sandy playground and watching giant cargo ships glide by.

Midday / 2-3h

Granville Island Kids Market

Explore a literal warehouse dedicated to kids' toys, books, and indoor play, then grab lunch at the bustling public market nearby. The outdoor duck pond and old caboose are perfect for toddler-scale exploration.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Capilano Suspension Bridge Park

Cross the thrilling giant suspension bridge and walk among the treetops on elevated wooden boardwalks. Toddlers can easily tackle the Cliffwalk in a carrier and will love the magical seasonal light displays.

Morning / 3h

Science World at TELUS World of Science

This iconic geodesic dome features hands-on science galleries, including the Wonder gallery designed specifically for kids under five. It is the ultimate rainy-day sanctuary for energetic toddlers to splash, climb, and build.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Bloedel Conservatory

Step into a warm, domed paradise filled with colorful free-flying exotic birds and lush tropical plants. It is a sensory-rich, calm environment that is completely flat and easy for toddler steps.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Kitsilano Beach Playground

Dig in the sand at this legendary beach and play on the massive, fully accessible playground. Toddlers can swing while staring at the stunning North Shore mountains across the water.

Morning / 1h

Aquabus Ferry to Olympic Village

Hop on these cute, rainbow-colored mini ferry boats to cruise across False Creek. Toddlers will find the short boat ride thrilling, and you can hop off at Olympic Village to see the giant beaver sculptures.

Morning / 2h

Maplewood Farm

Just a short drive across the bridge to North Vancouver, this historic five-acre farm lets kids get up close with goats, sheep, and rabbits. It is a peaceful, slow-paced destination perfect for animal-loving toddlers.

Evening / 2-3h

Richmond Night Market

Immerse your senses in North America’s largest night market, packed with colorful lights, giant inflatables, and endless street food. Go early right when it opens to beat the heavy crowds with a toddler in tow.

Where to eat

  • Rocky Point Ice Cream — Grab a scoop of handcrafted, locally sourced ice cream in family-friendly flavours, located right near the water in Port Moody.
  • Lee's Donuts at Granville Island — Treat the family to warm, classic honey-dipped donuts made fresh daily, a legendary sweet treat that is perfectly sized for little hands to share.
  • White Spot — A beloved BC institution, this casual spot serves legendary burgers and kids' meals in cardboard "Pirate Ships" that guarantee toddler mealtime success.
  • Tacofino Oasis — Enjoy fresh, kid-friendly fish tacos and quesadillas on a spacious, casual outdoor patio that welcomes messy eaters and loud voices.
