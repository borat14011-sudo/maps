Cascading pastel houses cling to dramatic cliffs, meeting the sparkling blue of the Tyrrhenian Sea in Positano. While famous for its vertical stairs, this Amalfi Coast jewel offers gentle beach days, boat adventures, and sweet lemon treats that captivate children. It is a sensory playground where family memories are painted in vibrant coastal hues.
🚕 Getting there: ~75-90 min private car transfer from Naples Capodichino Airport (NAP) directly to your Positano accommodation.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Spiaggia Grande Beach Day

Enjoy the iconic dark volcanic sand and calm waters by renting a sunbed for maximum comfort. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow shoreline while parents soak in the vertical town views.

Mid-day / 2h

Boat Charter to Emerald Grotto

Hop on a private or small-group boat tour to witness the magical green light reflecting inside this marine cave. Kids will be spellbound by the glowing water, and the gentle boat ride is perfect for toddler naptime.

Afternoon / 1h

Gelato and Walk at Piazza di Mulini

Stroll through this pedestrian-only square lined with cascading bougainvillea, stopping for artisan gelato. Toddlers can safely wander the car-free zone without the worry of busy traffic.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Fornillo Beach Coastal Walk

Take the scenic, relatively flat coastal path from Spiaggia Grande to the quieter Fornillo Beach. It is a stroller-friendly path in parts that offers stunning sea views and a calmer beach for kids to play.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Lemon Granita at Collina Positano Bakery

Treat the family to a refreshing, freshly squeezed lemon slushy served inside a giant hollowed-out Amalfi lemon. It is the ultimate cool-down treat for toddlers on a warm afternoon.

Morning / 1-2h

Ceramic Painting Watching on Via Pasitea

Browse the vibrant ceramic shops along Via Pasitea, watching local artisans paint intricate lemon patterns. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the bright colors, and many shops offer durable, kid-safe small ceramic souvenirs.

Full Day / 6-8h

Day Trip to Ravello Gardens

Take a short ferry to Amalfi, then a taxi up to Ravello's Villa Rufolo to run around the lush, wide-open gardens. Toddlers can freely explore the grassy terraces overlooking the sea far from cliff edges.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Arienzo Beach Club Ferry Ride

Take a short, exciting 5-minute orange shuttle boat ride from the main pier to this family-friendly private beach. Toddlers love the mini boat ride, and the beach club provides clean changing facilities and shallow waters.

Morning / 2-3h

Montepertuso Playground and Village Visit

Ride the local bus up to the mountain hamlet of Montepertuso to see the giant 'hole in the mountain' rock formation. There is a peaceful local playground where children can play with local kids away from the tourist crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Black

Order the famous kid-friendly heart-shaped Margherita pizza and the local sea urchin pasta right on the beachfront.

Ristorante Max

Try the hand-rolled scialatielli pasta with fresh seafood or simple butter and cheese for kids in a charming, art-filled courtyard.

La Sponda

Book an early dinner to enjoy homemade ravioli Caprese under the glow of 400 candles, offering a magical, fairy-tale atmosphere for toddlers.

Ristorante Bruno

Get the paccheri pasta with fresh tomatoes and basil while sitting at outdoor cliffside tables overlooking the sea.

Da Vincenzo

Get the slow-cooked beef Genovese ziti pasta and the exceptionally creamy local lemon cake in a welcoming, family-run setting.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the stroller in favor of a sturdy baby carrier to navigate Positano's steep, endless staircases with ease. Book scenic ferry rides instead of winding buses to travel between coastal towns, as the sea route prevents motion sickness and delights young children.
