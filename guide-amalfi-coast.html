What to do

Morning / 3-4h

L'Incanto Beach Club on Spiaggia Grande

Rent a front-row luxury sun lounger with a large umbrella on Positano's main beach to let your toddler play in the dark volcanic sand. The club is easily accessed via the flat pedestrian boardwalk, avoiding the town's notorious cliffside staircases entirely.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Via Positanesi d'America Coastal Walk

This fully paved, completely flat pedestrian cliffside path connects Spiaggia Grande to Fornillo Beach without a single stair. It is the absolute best route in town for a scenic, stress-free stroller walk while your baby naps to the sound of crashing waves.

Morning / 4h

Private Wooden Gozzo Charter with Lucibello Positano

Charter a classic, wide-beam wooden boat equipped with a large sun-shading bimini canopy and padded lounging areas perfect for a baby nap. You can cruise the dramatic coastline and swim in calm coves without carrying any heavy gear up and down cliff stairs.

Morning / 2-3h

Villa Rufolo Gardens in Ravello

Take an easy taxi ride up to Ravello to stroll the wide, gravel paths of these historic cliffside gardens. The lush, manicured lawns and vibrant floral displays offer a calm, shaded sensory environment that is highly stroller-friendly.

Morning / 2h

Amalfi Lemon Experience (Salvatore Aceto)

Walk through a historic, shaded lemon grove in neighboring Amalfi where family golf carts are available to help navigate the lower terraced paths. Your toddler will love the fragrant, oversized sfusato lemons, and the tour ends with a refreshing, family-friendly lemon sorbet.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Piazza Duomo Gelato Stop at Pasticceria Andrea Pansa

Take a smooth ferry ride to Amalfi and head straight to this historic pastry shop right on the flat main square. Sit at an outdoor table where your toddler can safely watch the bustling piazza while you enjoy world-class gelato and Sfogliatella.

Morning / 6h

SNAV High-Speed Ferry to Capri

Board a spacious, smooth-sailing high-speed ferry from the Positano pier for a quick day trip to Capri. The gentle motion of the boat is incredibly soothing for toddlers, and the large indoor cabin provides air-conditioned relief from the midday heat.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Piazza dei Mulini Pedestrian Zone

Stroll the flat, car-free lower section of Positano lined with beautiful linen shops, fragrant wisteria, and open-air cafes. It is a completely safe space to let your toddler walk and explore without worrying about active scooter or car traffic.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Amalfi Paper Museum (Museo della Carta)

Located in a flat, cool stone valley in Amalfi, this indoor museum features ancient, water-powered wooden mills that are highly tactile and visually engaging for children. The cool indoor climate provides an excellent midday escape from the Mediterranean sun.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Black

Order the famous Spaghetti con Polpettine (spaghetti with meatballs) at this iconic beachfront institution. Located right on the flat boardwalk, its bustling, high-energy outdoor seating naturally drowns out any baby noise, and the staff love entertaining toddlers.

Ristorante Max

Order the handmade Scialatielli pasta with fresh local tomatoes in this quiet, spacious art-gallery courtyard. Tucked away from the main streets, it opens early for dinner at 7:00 PM, offering a calm, wind-free environment with plenty of room for a stroller beside your table.

Da Adolfo

Board their private, orange-flagged shuttle boat from the Positano pier to reach this casual beachfront restaurant for grilled mozzarella on lemon leaves. The direct sand-side dining is incredibly relaxed, letting your toddler play in the sand right next to your dining table.

Lo Guarracino

Savor the fresh local sea bass baked in a herb crust at this cliffside restaurant perched along the flat Via Positanesi d'America path. It offers some of the best views in town with a spacious, quiet terrace that is easily accessible by stroller without negotiating steps.

Ristorante Bruno

Enjoy the classic Spaghetti alle Vongole on their spectacular roadside terrace overlooking the vertical town. Since it sits directly on Via Cristoforo Colombo, you can take a taxi straight to the entrance, eliminating all stairs for a seamless upscale family dinner.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Palazzo Murat

This historic oasis is located in the flat pedestrian center of Positano, featuring a beautiful botanical garden, swimming pool, and zero-stair access from the main boardwalk. They provide high-end wooden cribs, spacious suites, and a highly professional babysitting service.

Covo dei Saraceni

Situated directly on the harbor of Spiaggia Grande, this luxury hotel offers elevator access straight to the beach and ferry docks, completely bypassing the town's stairs. Families will love the large rooms, stunning rooftop pool, and close proximity to beachfront restaurants.

Hotel Poseidon

A family-run boutique hotel that sits on the scenic upper road, accessible directly by car or taxi to avoid walking. It features an expansive, elevator-accessible pool terrace, incredibly spacious family rooms, and a warm staff that goes out of their way to accommodate infants.

👨‍👩‍👧 Ditch the heavy stroller for a premium baby carrier like an Ergobaby Omni 360, as Positano's steep, cobbled staircases are virtually impassable with wheels. Utilize the internal Positano town bus (Mobility Amalfi Coast) which has designated street-level stops, allowing you to easily traverse up and down the cliffs without walking. Always book beach club loungers and private boat charters weeks in advance to secure shaded spots that keep your toddler safe from the intense Mediterranean midday sun.
