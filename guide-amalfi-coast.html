Amalfi Coast

The Amalfi Coast sparkles as a vertical paradise of pastel-colored villas cascading down to the shimmering Tyrrhenian Sea. For high-flying couples traveling with an infant, navigating this dramatic landscape requires curated luxury and careful planning. While Positano's steep stairs are famous, Spiaggia Grande offers a surprisingly accessible beachfront boardwalk ideal for evening strolls with a premium stroller. Enjoying private boat charters from Marina Grande ensures Mary Claire stays cool and shaded under a luxury canopy while you soak in the Mediterranean sun.
🚕 Getting there: Naples International Airport (NAP) to Positano: 1 hour and 20 minutes via a private Mercedes V-Class transfer booked through Positano Car Service, equipped with a pre-installed, sanitised rear-facing infant car seat and ample room for a premium stroller.

What to do

Early morning 8:30-10:00am / 1.5h

Spiaggia Grande Boardwalk

Enjoy a scenic morning stroll along Positano's main beachfront boardwalk; it is completely flat, stroller-friendly, and offers spectacular cliffside views before the midday heat and crowds arrive.

Morning 10:30am-12:30pm / 2h

Villa Rufolo Gardens

Escape the Positano crowds in Ravello where the paved, shaded garden paths of this historic villa are highly accessible for strollers and offer cool breezes for baby Mary Claire.

Full day 9:00am-4:00pm / 7h

Private Yacht Charter to Capri

Board a private Luano Charter yacht from Positano; the spacious cabin provides full air-conditioned shade and a quiet place for infant naps while cruising past the Faraglioni rocks.

Midday 12:30-1:30pm / 1h

Giardini di Augusto

Stroll through these beautifully manicured, paved botanical gardens in Capri which feature wide, shaded pathways and breathtaking sea vistas without any strenuous climbing.

Afternoon 2:00-3:30pm / 1.5h

Museo della Carta

Located on the flat valley floor of Amalfi, this historic paper museum offers a cool, indoor escape from the sun with stroller-friendly exhibition spaces.

Late afternoon 4:00-5:30pm / 1.5h

Piazza Duomo Amalfi

Relax at a shaded outdoor café in Amalfi's central pedestrian square; it is highly accessible, lively enough to mask baby noises, and perfect for a slow afternoon gelato.

Sunset 6:00-7:00pm / 1h

Marina Grande of Positano

Walk along the flat harbor pier to watch the colorful boats slip away; the sea breeze is incredibly refreshing for infants and there are zero stairs to navigate.

Morning 9:00-11:00am / 2h

Valle delle Ferriere Lower Trail

Explore the very beginning of this lush nature reserve starting from Amalfi; the lower gravel path is flat, shaded by tall ferns, and cooled by rushing waterfalls.

Morning 11:00am-12:00pm / 1h

Duomo di Ravello

Visit this peaceful 11th-century cathedral located on Ravello's flat central square, featuring cool marble interiors, quiet atmospheres, and an easy-access side ramp.

Indulge in their famous spaghetti with sea urchins right on the beach boardwalk; the lively, buzzing atmosphere is highly welcoming to infants and easily masks occasional crying.

Zass

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Il San Pietro di Positano

$1,200-2,500/night

The pinnacle of coastal luxury, offering a private elevator carved directly into the cliff for seamless stroller access to the pool and beach. They provide premium wooden cribs, customized baby purees from the Michelin-starred kitchen, and spacious suites with blackout curtains.

Le Sirenuse

$1,100-2,300/night

An iconic family-run oasis in the heart of Positano featuring hand-painted tiles, breathtaking pool deck views, and on-call English-speaking babysitting services. Suites are equipped with luxury cribs, bottle warmers, and private terraces with shade-giving lemon trees.

Hotel Santa Caterina

$950-1,800/night

Located in Amalfi, this late 19th-century liberty-style villa features split-level gardens and a private beach club accessed via a spacious glass elevator. Offers generous family suites, heated saltwater pool, professional nanny services, and baby-proofing kits on request.
