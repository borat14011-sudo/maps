Sun-soaked family adventures on Aruba's gentle white sands.

Aruba

← back to travel board
With its perpetually calm, turquoise waters and powder-soft beaches, Aruba is a Caribbean paradise tailor-made for stress-free family travel. Beyond the shore, easy nature trails, colorful animal encounters, and a warm, welcoming local culture await travelers of all ages. It is a breezy island haven where toddler-friendly convenience meets genuine tropical wonder.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Baby Beach

This shallow, man-made lagoon is incredibly calm and behaves like a giant wading pool for tiny swimmers. Toddlers can safely splash in the waist-deep water while older kids spot colorful fish near the breakwater.

Morning / 1-2h

Aruba Butterfly Farm

Walk through a lush tropical garden filled with thousands of colorful, free-flying butterflies that might land right on your shoulder. The flat, paved paths are entirely stroller-friendly, and toddlers will be mesmerized by the fluttering wings.

Morning / 2h

Philip's Animal Garden

This interactive rescue shelter allows kids to feed friendly goats, pigs, and birds directly from a cup of food provided at the entrance. It is fully outdoors and features wide, flat dirt paths ideal for strollers, plus plenty of shaded rest spots.

Afternoon / 1h

Oranjestad Downtown Trolley

Hop onto this free, open-air double-decker streetcar to cruise past Oranjestad’s colorful Dutch colonial architecture. It is an effortless way to sightsee without tiring out little legs, and toddlers love the novelty of the bell-clanging ride.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Donkey Sanctuary Aruba

Help feed and pet the island's friendly, rescued wild donkeys in a safe, fenced environment. Toddlers will love getting up close to these gentle animals from the safety of the shaded viewing porch.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Eagle Beach

Famous for its iconic, wind-bent fofoti trees, this expansive beach offers plenty of room for building sandcastles. The wide shoreline is great for stroller walks during sunset, and shade is easy to find under the palapas.

Morning / 2-3h

Arikok National Park Visitor Center and Dunes

Explore the rugged beauty of Aruba's desert landscape with easy walks around the modern visitor center and the adjacent Boca Prins sand dunes. While the rocky natural pools are too rough for toddlers, they will love playing in the soft, wind-swept sand hills.

Afternoon / 1h

Aruba Aloe Factory Museum and Store

Take a short, engaging tour to see how soothing aloe gel is harvested directly from the plants. The breezy walking paths are stroller-friendly, and kids love the gooey plant demonstrations.

Evening / 2h

Local Market at Harbor Arena

Stroll through this vibrant open-air market to browse handmade crafts and listen to upbeat local music. It is a lively, sensory experience with plenty of space for toddlers to stretch their legs safely.

Where to eat

  • The Dutch Pancakehouse — Kids will love choosing from dozens of sweet or savory Dutch pancakes, served in a vibrant, family-friendly plaza in Oranjestad.
  • Eduardo's Beach Shack — Grab refreshing, colorful acai bowls, fresh fruit smoothies, and dairy-free coco whips right on the sand at Palm Beach.
  • Zeerovers — A rustic, overwater dock restaurant in Savaneta where you eat fresh fried fish and plantains with your hands while watching pelicans dive.
  • Pastechi House — Try warm, flaky, crescent-shaped local pastries stuffed with cheese or chicken, a perfect, cheap, toddler-approved handheld snack in Oranjestad.
