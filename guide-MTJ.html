Telluride

← back to travel board
Tucked away in a dramatic box canyon, Telluride charms families with its colorful Victorian-era main street and soaring Rocky Mountain peaks. Getting around is half the fun thanks to a free, breathtaking gondola ride that connects the historic town to the resort village above. Whether splashing in mountain creeks or exploring scenic trails, this alpine wonderland offers a perfect playground for parents and little adventurers alike.

What to do

Morning or Sunset / 1h

The Telluride-Mountain Village Gondola

This free 13-minute aerial ride offers jaw-dropping 360-degree mountain views and connects the two main base areas. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the quiet, soaring ride, and it is completely stroller-accessible.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Telluride Town Park

Located at the east end of town, this sprawling park features a fantastic playground, a toddler-friendly wading pool, and scenic paved paths. It is the perfect spot for kids to burn off energy while parents soak in the canyon views.

Morning / 2-3h

Bear Creek Trail to the Falls

A well-graded, wide dirt trail winding through groves of aspen trees up to a spectacular, roaring waterfall. While the full hike might tire little legs, the lower portion is very stroller-friendly and perfect for a slow-paced toddler stroll.

Morning / 1-2h

Telluride Historical Museum

Housed in a historic 1896 hospital, this museum offers interactive exhibits on mining, skiing, and Ute Native American history. Little ones will love the outdoor gold panning station where they can search for real treasures.

Friday Morning / 1-2h

Telluride Farmers' Market

Held on Fridays along South Oak Street, this vibrant market showcases organic Colorado produce, baked goods, and local crafts. It is a festive, sensory-rich environment for toddlers to sample fresh fruit and enjoy live street music.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Bridal Veil Falls Overlook

At 365 feet, Colorado's tallest free-falling waterfall is a breathtaking sight at the end of the canyon. Families can easily drive right up to the viewing area, making it highly accessible for families with toddlers in carriers.

Late Afternoon / 1h

San Miguel River Trail

This flat, gravel-covered path hugs the rushing river right through the heart of town. It is ideal for all-terrain strollers, allowing toddlers to safely throw pebbles into the shallow water along the banks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Mountain Village Plaza & Splash Pad

The pedestrian-only plaza features a delightful pop-up splash pad in the summer and cozy fire pits for roasting s'mores in the winter. Toddlers can safely run wild in this car-free zone while parents grab a coffee nearby.

Evening / 1h

Elks Park and Main Street Stroll

Located right in the center of the historic district, this green lawn is perfect for a family picnic or a toddler runaround. Stop by the adjacent historic buildings for a scoop of ice cream after your stroll.

Where to eat

  • Brown Dog Pizza — Famous for its award-winning Detroit-style square pizzas, this lively, family-friendly spot offers a welcoming atmosphere and a great kids' menu.
  • Baked in Telluride — This beloved local bakery serves up giant fresh-baked cookies, delicious pastries, and kid-approved bagels in a casual, quick-service setting.
  • The Steaming Bean — A cozy coffee shop offering excellent espresso for parents and delicious hot cocoa and smoothies for the little ones to enjoy on the outdoor patio.
  • La Cocina de Luz — This casual eatery serves up healthy, organic Mexican fare with plenty of mild options like quesadillas and bean bowls that toddlers love.
