High-Altitude Wonders and Equatorial Magic for All Ages

Quito

← back to travel board
Nestled high in the Andes, Quito wraps families in a tapestry of colonial history, lush volcanic parks, and interactive wonders. From standing on the middle of the world to soaring above the clouds, this vibrant capital offers an unforgettable high-altitude playground. It is a captivating blend of rich culture and outdoor thrills tailored perfectly for curious little travelers.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

TelefériQo

Soar up the slopes of Pichincha Volcano in a gondola for breathtaking views of the Andean peaks. Toddlers will love the cable car ride, but keep them bundled up as it gets very windy and chilly at the top.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Mitad del Mundo & Intiñan Site Museum

Stand with one foot in each hemisphere and try quirky gravity experiments like balancing an egg on a nail. The hands-on museum is perfect for keeping little hands busy, though a stroller is recommended for the paved walking paths.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque La Carolina

This massive urban oasis features a paddleboat lagoon, a botanical garden, and a dinosaur park. It is the ultimate spot for toddlers to burn off energy on the playground while parents enjoy the mountain backdrop.

Morning / 1-2h

Quito Botanical Garden (Jardín Botánico)

Wander through stunning glasshouses filled with thousands of orchids and a magical butterfly house where colorful wings flutter all around. The smooth, flat pathways are incredibly easy for strollers and toddlers to navigate safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Central

Immerse your senses in a bustling local market packed with colorful exotic fruits, fresh juices, and flower stalls. Toddlers will love sampling sweet granadillas, but hold their hands tightly as the aisles can get crowded.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza Grande (Plaza de la Independencia)

The heart of the Old Town is perfect for people-watching, chasing pigeons, and admiring the grand presidential palace. It is entirely pedestrianized, giving toddlers plenty of space to toddle safely away from traffic.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Yaku Water Museum (Yaku Parque Museo del Agua)

An interactive, water-themed museum with sensory exhibits, bubble-making stations, and beautiful views of the city below. Toddlers can splash in the outdoor water play areas, so bring a change of clothes just in case.

Morning / 2-3h

Parque Metropolitano Guangüiltagua

This sprawling eucalyptus forest offers hiking trails, wild llamas, and giant wooden swings with volcano views. It is a peaceful escape where toddlers can spot local birds and enjoy a rustic family picnic.

Morning / 1-2h

Casa Museo María Augusta Urrutia

Step back in time inside this beautifully preserved 19th-century home featuring a quiet, sunny interior courtyard. The peaceful fountain and spacious rooms offer a calm, slow-paced sensory break from the busy Old Town streets.

Where to eat

  • Locro de Papa at Hasta la Vuelta, Señor — Cozy up with a bowl of traditional, creamy potato soup loaded with cheese and avocado, served in an open-air courtyard that is great for families.
  • Empanadas de Viento at Colaciones del Cruz Verde — Kids will love these giant, airy, sugar-dusted fried cheese empanadas that melt in your mouth.
  • Artisanal Ice Cream at Heladería San Agustín — Enjoy historic pail-style ice cream in traditional fruit flavors at this family-friendly shop operating since 1858.
  • Chocolate Tasting at Yumbos Chocolate — While parents savor premium organic Ecuadorian dark chocolate, kids can enjoy sweet milk chocolate samples and watch the chocolate-making process.
👨‍👩‍👧 To ease toddler acclimatization to Quito’s high altitude, plan low-energy activities for your first 24 hours and keep everyone well-hydrated. Opt for a sturdy baby carrier over a stroller in the Historic Center, as the cobblestone streets and steep hills can be challenging to navigate with wheels.
