Bocas del Toro

← back to travel board
Bocas del Toro offers a rare blend of untamed Caribbean nature and laid-back island life that is remarkably manageable with a toddler. Isla Colón's main town is flat and highly walkable, while the calm, wave-free waters of child-friendly beaches are just a short water taxi or private shuttle ride away. For a finance dad who values seamless logistics and comfort, staying just outside the bustling town center provides a quiet, high-end sanctuary perfect for matching your daughter's nap schedule.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min private shuttle or open-air taxi to the main beach resorts, or a 5-minute walk to town hotels from Bocas del Toro International Airport (BOC).

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Playa Starfish (Playa Estrella)

This world-famous beach features crystal-clear, wave-free water that acts like a giant swimming pool for toddlers to wade in safely while looking at giant red starfish. Skip the bumpy public bus and hire a private boat from town to land directly on the sand, avoiding the 15-minute dirt path walk with baby gear.

Morning / 2h

Finca Los Monos Botanical Garden

A lush, beautifully maintained tropical garden offering guided tours where you can spot wild howler monkeys, sloths, and toucans in a controlled environment. The shaded gravel paths are excellent for a structured baby carrier walk while keeping out of the direct midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Playa Paunch Stroller Walk

While the beach itself is rocky and famous for surfing, the newly paved coastal road running alongside Paunch is flat, smooth, and perfect for an ocean-side stroller walk. It is a scenic way to get the baby to nap while dad enjoys watching the professional surfers tackle the reef breaks.

Morning / 3h

Playa Boca del Drago

Located on the quiet northwestern tip of the island, this beach offers gentle waves, swaying palms, and plenty of natural shade. It is much quieter than Starfish Beach, making it easy to set up a pop-up baby beach tent for a peaceful afternoon nap.

Morning / 3-4h

Bocas Yacht Club and Marina

A calm, secure marina environment where you can arrange private, captained boat charters customized to your family's exact schedule. Private boats here have sturdy sun-canopies to protect sensitive toddler skin from the intense Panamanian sun.

Afternoon / 30m

Supermercado Isla Colon

The largest and best-stocked grocery store on the island, perfect for stocking up on trusted diaper brands, wipes, and fresh tropical fruit. It features wider, air-conditioned aisles that easily accommodate a compact travel stroller compared to smaller corner markets.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa Bluff Beachcombing

This dramatic, golden-sand beach is too dangerous for toddler swimming due to powerful shore breaks, but it is spectacular for a beach walk. Bring a baby carrier to walk along the tree line where the dense jungle meets the ocean, offering incredible photo opportunities.

Morning / 4h

Simon's Seahorse Private Tours

A premium, family-friendly private boat charter service that will tailor a custom itinerary to Dolphin Bay and peaceful mangrove channels. Booking a private tour ensures you can immediately head back to the hotel dock if the baby gets fussy or needs an emergency change.

Early Evening / 1h

Parque Simón Bolívar

The central plaza in Bocas Town features a clean, fenced-in playground, mature shade trees, and smooth paved walkways. It is the perfect safe spot in the late afternoon for your toddler to burn off some energy and interact with local children.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Coralina Beach Club Restaurant

Order the wood-fired octopus or the fresh daily catch in this ultra-stylish, spacious open-air restaurant right on the sand. It offers plenty of room for strollers, high-end high chairs, and a highly professional staff accustomed to upscale family travelers.

El Ultimo Refugio

Order the double-mustard pork chop or the coconut seafood curry at this famous overwater restaurant. Go right when they open at 4:30 PM to secure a deck-side table where the soothing sound of water under the floorboards keeps babies calm.

Amaranto

Order the fresh pitaya smoothie bowl and an artisanal flat white coffee for an energized start to your morning. This quiet café has a peaceful, shaded back-garden patio that is incredibly welcoming to young families needing an early-morning breakfast spot.

Octo

Order the signature charred octopus or pork belly bao buns at this open-air, garden-style Caribbean kitchen. The casual outdoor seating and early opening hours make it a low-stress dining experience where minor toddler noises are absorbed by the ambient breeze.

Skully's

Order the classic double cheeseburger and a cold local Balboa beer. This beachfront spot is wildly family-friendly during the day, featuring a massive sandy yard, a giant pirate ship play structure, and a fenced-in pool where kids can splash safely.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

La Coralina Island House

An ultra-luxury, Bali-inspired resort near Paunch Beach featuring stunning zero-entry pools, expansive family villas, and pristine cribs upon request. It offers a tranquil, secure, and shaded sanctuary away from the town's noise while maintaining five-star service standards.

Island Plantation

An eco-chic, upscale resort located at Bluff Beach that offers peaceful, spacious rooms with private terraces perfect for relaxing during your baby's nap. The property features a beautiful pool surrounded by lush jungle, offering a quiet, nature-focused family escape.

Divers Paradise Boutique Hotel

Located right on the water in Bocas Town, this modern hotel features spacious, fully air-conditioned rooms and rare elevator access making stroller transport effortless. It is ideal for families who value being steps away from top restaurants and private water taxi docks.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry US cash in small bills ($1, $5, $10) because card readers frequently fail on the island and local water/land taxis only accept cash. Opt for a high-quality, lightweight baby carrier instead of a bulky stroller, as most beaches and wooden docks lack ramps and paved access. Use bottled water for mixing baby formula and rely on high-SPF, reef-safe sunscreen and toddler-friendly mosquito repellent when visiting forested coastal areas like Bluff or Drago.
