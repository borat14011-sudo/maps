Bocas del Toro

← back to travel board
Where turquoise Caribbean waters meet lush tropical rainforests, Isla Colón offers a laid-back paradise perfect for family exploration. Children will marvel at lazy sloths in the canopy and colorful starfish in the shallow waves, while parents soak in the vibrant, eco-friendly island culture. With its gentle waters and easy boat transfers, it's an accessible tropical playground for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~5-10 min taxi from Bocas del Toro International Airport (BOC) to Bocas Town, or ~20 min taxi to Bluff Beach hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Starfish Beach (Playa Estrella)

A calm, wave-free beach filled with giant, colorful starfish that kids can easily view from the shore. Toddlers will love wading safely in this giant natural pool.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute

An educational marine station where families can learn about local ecosystems and spot frogs on the nature trails. Toddlers will enjoy the interactive displays and flat walking paths.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Bluff Beach Play

A stunning golden-sand beach bordered by jungle, ideal for building sandcastles and spotting monkeys. Note that the shorebreak is too strong for toddlers to swim, but the beach play is unmatched.

Morning / 1-2h

Plastic Bottle Village

A quirky and educational eco-residential community featuring a castle built entirely of recycled plastic bottles. It's a fun, visual way to teach kids about recycling and creative building.

Morning / 1-2h

Bocas Butterfly Garden

Walk through a lush enclosure filled with hundreds of vibrant, fluttering tropical butterflies, including the giant blue morpho. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the colorful insects landing nearby.

Afternoon / 2h

Paki Point

A relaxed beachfront hang-out with giant beanbags on the grass, perfect for watching surfers while kids play in the sand. Toddlers will love exploring the shallow, rocky tide pools at low tide.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Bocas Town Central Park

A shady, vibrant town square with a playground where local kids play and vendors sell fresh coconut water. It's the perfect, low-key spot for toddlers to burn off energy in the late afternoon.

Morning / 2-3h

Sloth Island Boat Tour

A gentle boat ride through the mangroves to spot sleepy sloths hanging low in the tree canopy. Toddlers can easily spot these slow-moving creatures from the safety of the boat.

Morning / 30m

Super Mercado Isla Colon

A bustling local market hub where families can pick up exotic tropical fruits like rambutan and fresh mangoes. Toddlers will love picking out new, colorful fruits to taste-test.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Leaf Eatery

Grab the fresh fruit smoothies and vegan wraps in a tranquil, treehouse-like garden setting where you might spot a sloth.

El Último Refugio

Enjoy the coconut-crusted fish or fresh tropical fruit bowls on a beautiful overwater deck with a relaxed family-friendly vibe.

Skully's House

Feast on classic wood-fired pizzas and burgers while the kids play in the beachfront pirate-themed sandbox and family-friendly pool.

Raw Love Bocas

Indulge in vibrant, nutrient-packed acai bowls and raw treats right by the water, perfect for picky toddler palates.

La Buga Dive & Surf Kitchen

Sit over the water and enjoy delicious fish tacos and kid-friendly chicken tenders while watching boats pass by.

👨‍👩‍👧 Always carry US dollar cash in small denominations, as many water taxis, fruit stands, and local eateries on Isla Colón do not accept credit cards. Bring plenty of reef-safe sunscreen and high-quality mosquito repellent, as sandflies (chitras) can be active on the beaches during calm, windless afternoons.
