Huatulco

← back to travel board
Nestled on Oaxaca’s dramatic coast, Huatulco delights families with its nine pristine bays, calm emerald waters, and protected national parklands. It’s an eco-conscious paradise where toddlers can safely splash in gentle shallows while parents soak in the laid-back Pacific breeze. With its modern infrastructure, clean streets, and warm hospitality, this tropical escape offers the perfect balance of wild nature and easy comfort.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-25 min by pre-arranged private shuttle or official airport taxi to the beach resorts in Tangolunda or Santa Cruz.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Bahía de Santa Cruz

The most family-friendly bay in Huatulco, offering virtually wave-free waters that act like a giant wading pool. Toddlers can safely splash for hours while you rent a shaded beach umbrella and relax on the soft sand.

Morning to Afternoon / 4-5h

Huatulco Bays Boat Tour

Embark on a catamaran or private boat to explore the untouched, undeveloped bays of the National Park. Toddlers will love spotting wild sea turtles and dolphins jumping in the boat's wake.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Playa La Bocana

Where the Copalita River meets the ocean, offering a vast expanse of sand perfect for building castles. While the ocean waves here are strong, toddlers can safely paddle in the calm, warm river estuary.

Morning / 2h

Copalita Archaeological Zone

Wander through shady jungle trails to discover ancient ruins dating back over 2,000 years. The flat, paved paths are stroller-friendly, and kids will love spotting iguanas sunbathing on the stone structures.

Evening / 1-2h

La Crucecita Zócalo

The vibrant heart of Huatulco, perfect for an evening stroll around the gazebo with a refreshing popsicle. Toddlers will enjoy chasing pigeons in the plaza while parents admire the nearby church's massive Virgin of Guadalupe painting.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Municipal El Guamilito

Introduce your little ones to colorful Mexican culture through sights of handwoven baskets and smells of fresh Oaxacan cheese. It is an indoor, sensory-rich market where toddlers can snack on sweet, freshly baked pan dulce.

Morning / 3h

Playa La Entrega

Famous for its crystal-clear waters and thriving coral reef situated remarkably close to the shore. Rent a life vest for your toddler so they can peer through a window-bottom float at the colorful tropical fish.

Morning / 1-2h

Sendero El Llorón Nature Walk

An easy, flat nature trail winding through low forest within the national park, ideal for little legs to stretch. Keep an eye out for colorful butterflies and listen for the calls of native birds.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Playa Tangolunda

A wide, golden-sand beach bordered by luxury resorts, perfect for a relaxed family afternoon. The spacious shoreline gives toddlers endless room to run, play, and watch the spectacular Pacific sunset.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Sabor de Oaxaca

Order the Oaxacan sampler platter with stringy quesillo cheese, mild tlayudas, and delicious chicken tamales that toddlers will devour.

Ocean Blue Huatulco

Get the fresh garlic shrimp or grilled fish fillet while sitting right on the sand of Santa Cruz beach, allowing kids to play in the sand between bites.

Ve El Mar

Try the fresh coconut shrimp and homemade lemonade, served directly on the beach under a shady palapa.

Giordana's Pastificio

Order the homemade butter-and-parmesan fettuccine or a fresh margherita pizza, a guaranteed hit for tired toddlers craving familiar comfort food.

Tacos El Chino

Order the melted cheese and pastor tacos (gringas), which are cut into perfect bite-sized pieces for toddler hands.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to bottled water for everything, including brushing your toddler's teeth, and pack high-SPF reef-safe sunscreen since Huatulco's tropical sun is exceptionally strong. Opt for a lightweight, breathable baby carrier over a heavy stroller, as cobblestone streets in town and soft beach sand can make wheels difficult to navigate.
