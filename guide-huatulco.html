Huatulco welcomes discerning travelers with its nine pristine bays and commitment to eco-luxury. Will and Kathryn will appreciate the calm, shallow waters of Tangolunda Bay, perfect for introducing Mary Claire to the ocean. The destination offers a rare combination of unspoiled Oaxacan nature and highly accessible, modern infrastructure. With its paved, stroller-friendly paths lining the Santa Cruz marina, it is an effortless tropical haven for parents navigating with an infant.
🚕 Getting there: Bahías de Huatulco International Airport (HUX) is a seamless 20-minute drive to the main hotel zones of Tangolunda and Conejos. The best luxury family option is a pre-arranged private Suburban transfer through Huatulco Prime Transfers, which includes a sanitized infant car seat and ample cargo space for a full-size stroller.

What to do

Early morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Bahía de Tangolunda

The crown jewel of Huatulco's bays offers exceptionally calm waters and golden sand. It is ideal for a gentle morning dip with Mary Claire before the midday sun, with plenty of shaded palapas to keep her cool.

Morning 9:00-11:00am / 2h

Zona Arqueológica Bocana del Río Copalita

An ancient civic-ceremonial center featuring flat, heavily shaded walking trails winding through lush jungle. The on-site museum is air-conditioned, offering a perfect, cool sanctuary for baby's mid-morning nap.

Late afternoon 5:00-6:30pm / 1.5h

La Crucecita Plaza Principal

The charming town center features wide, paved walkways that are highly stroller-accessible. Parents can admire the stunning Virgin of Guadalupe painting inside the church while baby enjoys the gentle breeze under the shaded plaza trees.

Morning 10:00-11:30am / 1.5h

Bahía de Santa Cruz Marina & Boardwalk

A pristine, paved waterfront promenade that is perfect for a smooth stroller walk. Kathryn and Will can watch the yachts go by and enjoy the cool sea breeze without worrying about bumpy terrain.

Mid-day 11:00am-1:00pm / 2h

Bahía de San Agustín

A stunning, protected bay with shallow tidal pools and a gentle reef. While one parent snorkels the crystal-clear waters, the other can relax with Mary Claire on a beach lounger sheltered from the sun.

Afternoon 3:00-5:00pm / 2h

Bahía de Chahué

A clean, quiet, and expansive beach that holds a prestigious Blue Flag certification. The nearby beach clubs offer clean restroom facilities and shaded lounge areas, making diaper changes stress-free.

Sunset 5:30-6:30pm / 1h

Mirador El Faro

A scenic lighthouse viewpoint overlooking the dramatic coastline and a natural blowhole. The paved viewing platform is easy to navigate with a stroller, offering breathtaking family photo opportunities.

Sunrise 7:30-9:00am / 1.5h

Bahía de Conejos

An undeveloped, peaceful bay perfect for a quiet morning stroll using a baby carrier. The sound of the gentle waves provides a natural white-noise machine for Mary Claire to rest comfortably.

Early morning 8:00-9:30am / 1.5h

Huatulco National Park (El Limoncito Trail)

A low-impact nature trail through dry deciduous forest. The dense canopy provides excellent natural shade, making it a comfortable hike for parents using a structured baby backpack carrier.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Sabor de Oaxaca

Indulge in authentic Oaxacan mole and tlayudas in a vibrant, family-friendly setting in La Crucecita. They offer highchairs, a very welcoming staff, and an early dinner service that fits perfectly with baby's bedtime routine.

Terra-Cotta

This air-conditioned oasis in La Crucecita serves excellent international and Oaxacan cuisine. The spacious interior accommodates strollers easily, and the quiet atmosphere makes it perfect for a peaceful family breakfast.

Ocean Restaurant at Celeste Beach Residences

An elegant beachfront restaurant on Playa Tejoncito serving exquisite fresh seafood. The open-air luxury terrace is breezy and spacious, allowing parents to enjoy fine dining while baby relaxes in a stroller alongside the table.

Mercader

An upscale, highly acclaimed fusion restaurant in Tangolunda. Its beautiful, quiet outdoor garden terrace offers generous spacing between tables, providing privacy and a serene ambiance that is highly accommodating for families.

Ve El Mar

Set directly on the beach in Santa Cruz, this beloved spot allows you to dine with your feet in the sand. Kathryn and Will can enjoy fresh fish tacos under a shaded palapa while Mary Claire plays happily on a beach blanket next to them.
