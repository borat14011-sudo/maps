Vail

← back to travel board
Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Vail welcomes families with its storybook Bavarian-style pedestrian villages and majestic snow-capped peaks. Whether you are splashing in pristine mountain creeks in summer or gliding down gentle slopes in winter, this alpine paradise offers endless outdoor wonder. It is a place where cobblestone streets, scenic gondola rides, and pristine nature trails create unforgettable family memories.
🚕 Getting there: ~2 hours from Denver International Airport (DEN) via mountain shuttle or rental car, or ~40 minutes from Eagle County Regional Airport (EGE) via local taxi.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Gondola One to Mid-Vail

Ride up the mountain in a scenic, enclosed gondola cabin for breathtaking panoramic views of the Gore Range. Toddlers will love the smooth, exciting ride, and the fully enclosed cabins make it completely safe and stress-free for strollers.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Betty Ford Alpine Gardens

Explore the world's highest botanical garden, featuring stunning alpine wildflowers, peaceful waterfalls, and a dedicated children's garden. Toddlers can safely wander the paved, stroller-friendly paths and play in the sand and water educational areas.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Vail Farmers' Market & Art Show

Stroll through a vibrant summer market filled with local crafts, fresh Colorado produce, and live music. It's a great spot to let toddlers sample sweet pastries and listen to street performers in a lively, car-free environment.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Epic Discovery on Vail Mountain

An on-mountain summer playground offering alpine coasters, climbing walls, and tubing hills. Toddlers will love the dedicated Little Eagle Zip Line and the Marmot Mini-Gondola designed just for smaller adventurers.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Pirate Ship Park

This iconic wooden pirate ship playground located at the base of Vail Mountain is a dream for imaginative play. Toddlers will spend hours in the giant sandbox, climbing the low decks, and sliding down kid-friendly slides.

Morning / 1-2h

Vail Nature Center

A peaceful 7-acre oasis with easy walking trails and a historic log cabin filled with hands-on nature exhibits. Toddlers can touch animal pelts, see local birds, and participate in gentle, kid-focused nature walks.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Piney River Ranch

A stunning high-alpine ranch offering canoe rentals, hiking, and spectacular lake views. Toddlers can easily walk along the flat shoreline, throw pebbles into the water, and spot friendly horses roaming the property.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Imagination Station

A thoughtful indoor play space featuring a community art studio, a sensory room, and virtual reality games. It is perfect for a rainy afternoon, offering a safe, padded play area designed specifically for toddlers.

Evening / 1h

Gore Creek Path

A beautifully paved, flat trail that runs alongside the rushing waters of Gore Creek. It is the perfect scenic route for a smooth stroller ride or letting toddlers watch the ducks swim by.

🍽️ Where to eat

Mountain Standard

Get the wood-fired rotisserie chicken and a side of their famous, family-friendly pimento cheese dip.

Blue Moose Pizza

Order a classic New York-style cheese pizza and let the kids draw on the paper-covered tables with crayons.

The Little Diner

Go for the giant, fluffy Dutch Baby pancake dusted with powdered sugar, which is perfect for sharing.

Bully Ranch

Order the famous Bully Burger and a giant warm Bavarian pretzel served with sweet mustard.

Rimini Gelato

Treat the family to a scoop of authentic, artisanal dark chocolate or sweet strawberry gelato after dinner.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep your family hydrated and apply sunscreen frequently, as Vail's high altitude means stronger UV rays and quicker dehydration. Take full advantage of the free, stroller-friendly in-town bus system to easily hop between Vail Village and Lionshead without having to fold up your stroller.
