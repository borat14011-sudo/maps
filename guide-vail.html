Alpine adventures and cozy village charm for all ages

Vail

← back to travel board
Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Vail offers a picture-book European-style village experience paired with majestic wilderness. Families can explore wildflower-filled meadows, cobblestone pedestrian streets, and thrilling mountain-top play areas. It is an alpine wonderland where outdoor magic meets effortless, stroller-friendly convenience.
🚕 Getting there: ~2 hours by shuttle or rental car from Denver International Airport, or ~40 minutes from Eagle County Regional Airport.

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Betty Ford Alpine Gardens

The world's highest botanical garden features peaceful paths, babbling streams, and a dedicated children's garden. Toddlers will love the sensory stepping stones, educational play structures, and treasure hunts.

Morning / 2h

Vail Scenic Gondola Ride

Take Gondola One up the mountain for breathtaking, panoramic views of the Gore Range. Strollers fit easily on board, making it an absolute breeze to reach the scenic summit paths with toddlers.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Epic Discovery at Vail Mountain

This mountaintop adventure park features alpine coasters, tubing hills, and a kid-friendly challenge course. The Little Eagle Aqua Tubing and sandbox areas are ideal for toddlers while older kids tackle the ziplines.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Vail Farmers' Market & Art Show

Stroll past over 100 tents filled with local Colorado treats, fresh pastries, and handmade crafts. It is a lively, sensory-rich morning outing with wide, flat pavement perfect for strollers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Pirate Ship Park

Located right next to Vail Village, this iconic wooden pirate ship playground features slides, climbing nets, and secret cabins. It is the ultimate spot for toddlers to burn off energy while parents enjoy a coffee nearby.

Morning / 2h

Vail Nature Center

A historic homestead cabin sitting on a beautiful mountain meadow offering easy, flat walking trails. Toddlers can join the guided beaver pond walks or explore the indoor hands-on nature exhibits.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Piney River Ranch

A stunning mountain retreat featuring a pristine lake, towering peaks, and easy canoe rentals. Toddlers will love watching the resident horses and splashing safely along the shallow, grassy lake edges.

Morning / 1-2h

Gore Valley Trail

This paved, multi-use path winds alongside the scenic, rushing Gore Creek, ideal for strollers or balance-bike rides. Toddlers can spot ducks in the water while parents enjoy the flat, shaded mountain walkway.

Morning / 1h

Vail Public Library Children's Room

A cozy, light-filled library overlooking the creek with an outstanding kids' corner filled with toys, puzzles, and books. It is the perfect indoor refuge for toddlers on a rainy day or during a midday wind-down.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Little Diner

Order the world-famous homemade Dutch Baby pancake, a giant, fluffy golden masterpiece that kids and adults love to share.

Pazzo's Pizzeria

Get a custom thin-crust cheese pizza in a casual, highly family-friendly atmosphere where kids can play with real pizza dough.

Garfinkel's

Enjoy classic American burgers and crispy chicken tenders on the massive, sunny deck overlooking the Lionshead gondola.

Swiss Chalet Restaurant

Indulge in authentic, interactive Swiss cheese fondue that makes dinner feel like a fun alpine adventure for the whole family.

Rimini Vail

Treat the little ones to decadent artisan gelato, hot chocolate, or fresh sweet crepes in a cozy European-style dessert parlor.

👨‍👩‍👧 Keep kids hydrated and apply plenty of sunscreen, as Vail's high elevation makes sunburns and dehydration happen much quicker than at sea level. Utilize the free, stroller-friendly in-town shuttle buses to easily navigate between Vail Village and Lionshead without tiring out toddler legs.
