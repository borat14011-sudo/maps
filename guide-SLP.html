San Luis Potosí

← back to travel board
Nestled in Mexico's high plateau, San Luis Potosí blends majestic pink-stone colonial plazas with breathtaking natural wonders. Families can wander through vibrant pedestrian streets, splash in tranquil springs, and explore surrealist rainforest gardens. It is a captivating, stroller-friendly destination where cultural heritage meets effortless outdoor play.

What to do

Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de Aranzazú

This beautiful, pedestrian-only plaza features iconic yellow arches and a bright pink temple, making it perfect for family photos. Toddlers will love chasing pigeons in the open space while parents admire the colonial architecture.

Morning / 3-4h

Tangamanga I Park

One of Mexico's largest urban parks, this green oasis offers paddleboats, duck ponds, and shaded playgrounds. It is highly stroller-friendly, with paved paths perfect for a relaxed toddler stroll.

Morning / 2-3h

Laberinto de las Ciencias y las Artes

A stunningly designed interactive science museum with hands-on exhibits that spark curiosity in kids of all ages. There is a dedicated, sensory-rich early childhood pavilion perfect for toddlers to explore safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Leonora Carrington Museum

Housed in a beautifully converted historic prison, this museum showcases whimsical, dreamlike bronze sculptures that captivate young imaginations. The wide, outdoor courtyards allow toddlers to walk freely between the outdoor art installations.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Hidalgo

A bustling, colorful market filled with sweet smells, fresh fruits, and local crafts. Keep toddlers close in a carrier here to navigate the lively, narrow aisles safely while sampling sweet treats.

Full Day / 5-6h

Cascadas de Tamasopo

These gentle, park-like waterfalls feature manicured pathways and shallow, calm pools ideal for a family dip. It's highly accessible for toddlers, though a life jacket is recommended for water play.

Afternoon / 1h

Tranvía San Luis Rey

Hop on this open-air replica trolley for a fun, breezy tour of the city's historic center without tiring out little legs. Toddlers will be enchanted by the novelty of the tram ride while you soak in the history.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Cerro de San Pedro

A quiet, cobblestone historic mining town just outside the city, perfect for a peaceful family escape. Toddlers will enjoy the open, car-free streets, but a sturdy carrier is best for the uneven terrain.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza del Carmen

This expansive pedestrian plaza is dominated by the gorgeous Templo del Carmen and comes alive with street performers and balloon vendors. It is the ultimate spot for toddlers to stretch their legs in the evening while enjoying a local ice cream.

Where to eat

  • Enchiladas Potosinas — These iconic, orange-hued corn tortillas are infused with chili, stuffed with cheese, and fried to perfection, offering a mild and savory flavor that kids love.
  • El Rincón Huasteco — A colorful, family-friendly restaurant serving authentic regional dishes like giant tamales in a lively atmosphere with high chairs available.
  • La Parroquia — Famous for its traditional café con leche and sweet concha breads, this bustling diner-style spot is highly welcoming to families with toddlers.
  • Chocolates Costanzo — A legendary local candy shop where families can stock up on sweet chocolate-covered marshmallows and fruit jellies.
👨‍👩‍👧 While the historic center is highly pedestrianized, the historic cobblestones can be rough, so opt for a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier. Always carry cash for market snacks and trolley tickets, as small vendors rarely accept credit cards.
