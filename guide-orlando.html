What to do

Full Day / Morning start

Magic Kingdom Park

Experience classic fairy tales with gentle rides like Peter Pan's Flight and spectacular fireworks. Toddlers will love meeting Mickey Mouse and exploring the soft, air-conditioned play areas in Dumbo's queue.

Morning / 2 hours

Harry P. Leu Gardens

Stroll along paved, stroller-friendly paths winding through 50 acres of beautiful tropical plants and giant camphor trees. Toddlers can safely run on the wide lawns and spot butterflies in the dedicated garden.

Afternoon / 1-2h

East End Market

This vibrant neighborhood food hall offers artisanal treats, local coffee, and a lovely courtyard to relax in. It's a low-stress spot for toddlers to enjoy a giant chocolate chip cookie while parents sip craft brews.

Evening / 3h

Disney Springs

Walk around this bustling outdoor waterfront complex filled with massive Disney shops, LEGO building stations, and a vintage carousel. Toddlers love riding the free water taxis and watching the hot air balloon launch.

Morning / 2h

Lake Eola Park

Walk the paved loop in the heart of downtown to see the famous swan boats and feed the resident birds. There is a fantastic gated playground perfect for toddlers to burn off some energy.

Morning / 3-4h

Orlando Science Center

Four floors of hands-on exhibits offer endless interactive fun, especially in the KidsTown zone designed specifically for little ones. Toddlers can splash in the water play table and climb the miniature orange grove.

Morning / 1h

Scenic Boat Tour of Winter Park

Take a relaxing, open-air pontoon boat cruise through three of Winter Park's pristine lakes and historic canals. It is quiet, smooth, and short enough to keep toddlers captivated by passing birds and grand estates.

Afternoon / 3h

Crayola Experience Orlando

Dive into a colorful wonderland of hands-on creative play where kids can melt crayons into shapes and star in their own coloring pages. The Toddler Town section features a safe, soft play structure and giant pegboards.

Morning / 3h

Blue Spring State Park

Located just north of Orlando, this crystalline spring is a designated refuge where families can view hundreds of wild manatees from wooden boardwalks. The boardwalks are completely stroller-accessible, making it easy for toddlers to spot the gentle giants.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gideon's Bakehouse

Get the legendary, nearly half-pound Chocolate Chip Cookie paired with their signature Peanut Butter Iced Coffee.

Yellow Dog Eats

Try the Club Elvis sandwich or their famous pulled pork in a quirky, ultra-casual, and highly family-friendly atmosphere.

Seito Sushi (Baldwin Park)

Order the beautifully fresh sushi rolls and a comforting bowl of classic ramen while enjoying the peaceful lakeside view.

Kelly's Homemade Ice Cream

Treat the family to a scoop of their signature Ore-Dough ice cream or a seasonal flight of unique, handcrafted flavors.

Teak Neighborhood Grill

Dig into one of their award-winning, creative burgers on a spacious, casual outdoor patio that welcomes busy families.
