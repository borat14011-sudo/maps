European charm, vibrant culture, and endless toddler-friendly parks

Buenos Aires

← back to travel board
Buenos Aires welcomes families with its wide, stroller-friendly boulevards, lush green spaces, and a warm, child-loving culture. From colorful artistic streets to interactive museums, the city moves at a relaxed pace perfect for little feet. Enjoy world-class dining where kids are always greeted with a welcoming smile.

What to do

Morning / 1.5-2h

Jardín Japonés

This tranquil oasis features gravel paths, red bridges, and massive koi fish that toddlers love to feed. It is entirely fenced, making it a safe place for little ones to wander freely.

Afternoon / 1h

El Ateneo Grand Splendid

A majestic theater converted into a stunning bookstore with a dedicated, cozy children's section in the basement. It's a sensory wonder, though you'll need to keep active toddlers close near the stage ramp.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Bosques de Palermo

This massive park offers paddle boats, a beautiful rose garden, and plenty of paved paths for strollers. Toddlers will love watching the ducks and running on the expansive lawns.

Morning / 2h

Museo Participativo de Ciencias

Located in the Recoleta Cultural Center, this hands-on science museum is designed specifically for kids to touch and explore. Even toddlers will enjoy the sensory rooms, water play, and colorful lights.

Morning / 1.5h

Caminito Street Museum

A colorful, open-air street museum filled with bright tin houses, tango dancers, and street art. Go early in the morning to beat the crowds and let your toddler admire the giant paper-mache puppets.

Morning / 1.5h

Mercado de San Telmo

A historic indoor market packed with antiques, fresh produce, and delicious food stalls. It's best visited on a weekday morning when it's less crowded and easier to navigate with a stroller.

Morning / 1h

Plaza de Mayo & Casa Rosada

The historic heart of the city features the iconic pink presidential palace and a wide open plaza. Toddlers will be delighted chasing the hundreds of pigeons that gather around the central fountain.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Puerto Madero Waterfront

A modern, completely pedestrianized dockside area perfect for stroller walks away from city traffic. Kids will love watching the passing boats and crossing the futuristic suspension bridge.

Morning / 2h

Eco-Parque Buenos Aires

A former zoo transformed into an educational eco-park where peacocks, maras, and ducks roam freely alongside the paths. It is stroller-friendly, free to enter, and teaches conservation in a hands-on way.

Where to eat

  • Steak and Papas Fritas at a Local Parrilla — Argentine steakhouses are incredibly welcoming to families, often providing high chairs and simple, delicious french fries that toddlers devour.
  • Heladería Lucciano's — This famous gelato spot serves artisanal ice cream shaped like funny animals and minion characters that toddlers will love.
  • Baked Empanadas at El Sanjuanino — These pockets of dough filled with beef, cheese, or corn are the perfect, mess-free, kid-friendly finger food for on-the-go fueling.
  • Medialunas at Café Tortoni — Treat your little ones to sweet, buttery Argentine croissants dipped in thick, warm chocolate at the city's most historic cafe.
👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for a lightweight, sturdy stroller as many sidewalks in historic neighborhoods like San Telmo are uneven and cobbled. Keep in mind that locals eat dinner very late, so plan to visit kid-friendly cafés for a 'merienda' afternoon snack around 5 PM to keep toddlers fed and happy.
