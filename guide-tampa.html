Families will love the mix of lush wildlife encounters, interactive museums, and historic streets perfect for wandering with a stroller. It is a sunny playground where pirate legends come to life and outdoor adventures await around every corner.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or rideshare to downtown Tampa hotels, or ~35-40 min to the Gulf beaches

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Florida Aquarium

Pet stingrays, walk through a massive wetlands dome, and explore a thriving coral reef habitat. Toddlers can cool off at the outdoor, pirate-themed Splash Pad play area.

Full Day / 6-8h

Busch Gardens Tampa Bay

Experience world-class rollercoasters alongside a sprawling, accredited African-themed zoo. Younger kids will love the Sesame Street Safari of Fun featuring toddler-friendly rides and water play.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Tampa Riverwalk

Walk, bike, or stroll along this scenic 2.6-mile pedestrian trail connecting major parks, museums, and restaurants. Toddlers will love chasing the water fountains at the Water Works Park playground along the way.

Morning / 2-3h

Glazer Children's Museum

A highly interactive, colorful indoor playground where kids can roleplay as veterinarians, grocery shoppers, or builders. It is fully stroller-accessible and perfect for escaping the afternoon heat.

Morning / 3-4h

ZooTampa at Lowry Park

Voted one of the best family zoos in the country, offering up-close encounters with manatees, koalas, and elephants. Take toddlers to the Wallaroo Station area for pint-sized rides and a splash pad.

Afternoon / 2h

Ybor City & Historic Streetcar

Ride the free, air-conditioned historic yellow streetcar into Tampa's historic Latin quarter to spot wild chickens roaming the brick streets. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the clanging streetcar bells and colorful historic buildings.

Morning / 1-2h

TECO Manatee Viewing Center

Walk along safe, elevated boardwalks to spot wild manatees seeking warmth in the power station's clean discharge canal. Toddlers can easily peer over the safety rails to see these gentle giants floating below.

Lunch / 1-2h

Armature Works & Heights Public Market

A historic, converted streetcar barn filled with local food stalls, offering an outdoor riverfront lawn for kids to run around. Toddlers can snack on artisanal wood-fired pizza or local gourmet ice cream.

Morning / 2h

Lettuce Lake Park

Walk along a scenic 3,500-foot boardwalk winding through a quiet cypress swamp where you can spot wild alligators and herons. The entire loop is flat, completely stroller-friendly, and features a large shaded playground.

🍽️ Where to eat

Columbia Restaurant

Order the legendary '1905' Salad prepared tableside, traditional Cuban sandwiches, and warm Cuban bread in Florida's oldest historic restaurant.

Ulele

Get the famous chargrilled oysters and native Florida-inspired alligator hushpuppies while the kids play safely on the adjacent riverfront lawn.

Oxford Exchange

Order the fluffy pancakes, whipped feta, and fresh green juices inside a breathtaking, historic brick building with a European-style glass conservatory.

Goody Goody Burger

Try the famous 'POX' burger with a side of sweet potato fries and a giant slice of butterscotch pie in this retro, highly kid-friendly Hyde Park diner.

Daily Eats

Order the massive, fully customizable breakfast bowls or giant, thick milkshakes in a bustling, casual atmosphere that happily welcomes noisy toddlers.

👨‍👩‍👧 Beat the intense Florida heat by planning outdoor zoo and park visits for early morning, then retreating to air-conditioned indoor museums during the peak afternoon sun. Utilize the free, air-conditioned TECO Line Streetcar to travel easily between downtown, the Channel District, and Ybor City without having to fold and unfold strollers.
