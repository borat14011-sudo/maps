Anchorage

← back to travel board
Anchorage blends raw Alaskan wilderness with easy, family-friendly urban comforts where glaciers meet city streets. From towering moose wandering local parks to touch-tanks filled with marine life, it’s an outdoor playground designed for curious little eyes. Here, grand mountain backdrops frame everyday strolls, making every moment feel like a giant, safe-to-explore storybook.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Alaska Zoo

Nestled in the boreal forest, this unique zoo focuses on orphaned and injured Arctic and sub-Arctic wildlife like polar bears and muskoxen. Toddlers will love the stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks and up-close views of gentle reindeer.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Alaska Museum of Science and Nature

Perfect for rainy days, this hands-on museum lets kids touch real dinosaur bones and learn about Alaska's unique geology. There is a dedicated, carpeted play area designed specifically for toddlers to explore safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Tony Knowles Coastal Trail

This flat, paved path hugs the coastline, offering stunning views of Cook Inlet and the chance to spot wild moose. It is completely stroller-accessible, making it easy to turn around whenever little legs get tired.

Weekend Midday / 1-2h

Anchorage Market

Anchorage's largest open-air market features local crafts, live music, and delicious street food. It’s an energetic spot where toddlers can groove to the music and snack on fresh, sweet Alaskan berries.

Morning / 1h

Alaska Public Lands Information Center

Located downtown, this free center offers interactive exhibits about Alaska's wild spaces and native culture. Toddlers will enjoy the realistic animal displays and the cozy corner filled with nature-themed children's books.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Potter Marsh Bird Sanctuary

Walk along an elevated wooden boardwalk over scenic wetlands to spot nesting eagles, spawning salmon, and occasional moose. The boardwalk has secure railings all the way, keeping enthusiastic toddlers safe from the water.

Afternoon / 2-3h

H2Oasis Indoor Waterpark

Escape the chilly weather at this massive indoor tropical paradise featuring a lazy river and warm water slides. A dedicated, shallow pirate splash zone keeps toddlers safely entertained for hours.

Afternoon / 2h

Kincaid Park

A massive, forested park offering scenic vistas of Cook Inlet, sandy hills, and frequent wildlife sightings. The playground near the chalet is excellent for toddlers to burn off energy while parents soak in the mountain views.

Morning / 2-3h

Anchorage Museum

This world-class museum features interactive spaces designed for hands-on, creative play. Toddlers will love the sensory water tables, giant building blocks, and light displays in the dedicated kid zones.

Where to eat

  • Moose's Tooth Pub & Pizzeria — Famous for its creative gourmet pizzas, this bustling spot has a loud, family-friendly vibe where toddler tantrums easily blend into the background noise.
  • Snow City Cafe — A cheerful, colorfully decorated breakfast spot serving up fluffy reindeer sausage scrambles and kid-approved giant pancakes.
  • Wild Scoops Ice Cream — An artisanal micro-creamery offering unique Alaskan flavors like 'Sitka Swirl' alongside toddler-friendly classic vanilla in fresh waffle cones.
  • Fire Island Rustic Bakeshop — A cozy local bakery perfect for grab-and-go morning pastries, soft cookies, and fresh breads that toddlers can easily snack on during strolls.
👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and a high-quality rain cover for your stroller, as Anchorage weather can shift rapidly from sunny to chilly. Always maintain a safe distance from urban moose, which are common even in city parks, and teach toddlers to look but never approach them.
