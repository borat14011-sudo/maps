Rio de Janeiro

← back to travel board
Framed by dramatic granite peaks and the sparkling Atlantic, Rio de Janeiro is a vibrant playground where rainforest meets the sea. Families will find a warm, child-loving culture, golden beaches with dedicated play areas, and gentle adventures perfect for all ages. It is a city where every day feels like a celebration of nature and life.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 to 45 min prepaid taxi or Uber from Tom Jobim International Airport (GIG) to Copacabana or Ipanema beach hotels.

What to do

Early Morning / 2-3h

Christ the Redeemer via Corcovado Train

Ride the historic cog train up through the lush Tijuca forest to see the iconic statue up close. Toddlers will love the train ride, but bring a carrier as the summit can get crowded and stroller-unfriendly.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Sugarloaf Mountain Cable Car

Take the two-stage glass cable cars up to the summit for breathtaking 360-degree views of the bay. There are wide, paved pathways at the top making it highly stroller-accessible, plus grassy areas to rest.

Morning / 2-3h

Copacabana Beach Posto 6

Play in the calmer, gentler waters of Posto 6, which is favored by local families and paddleboarders. Toddlers will love building sandcastles here, and you can rent umbrellas and chairs directly on the sand.

Morning / 2-3h

Jardim Botânico

Stroll down paths lined with towering imperial palms, spot wild monkeys, and visit the sensory garden. It is a tranquil, stroller-friendly oasis with a great playground and a dedicated sensory space for little hands.

Midday / 2h

AquaRio Marine Aquarium

Explore the largest marine aquarium in South America, featuring an impressive underwater tunnel with sharks and rays. The entire facility is air-conditioned and fully stroller-accessible, making it the perfect escape from the midday heat.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas

Rent a swan-shaped pedal boat or bike along the paved path surrounding this beautiful lagoon. There are multiple lakeside playgrounds with soft sand perfect for toddlers to explore safely.

Morning / 1-2h

Parque Lage

Explore a beautiful public park featuring a mansion wrapped around a pool, walking trails, and small stone caves. Toddlers will love exploring the aquarium cave and running on the grassy lawns under the shadow of Corcovado.

Sunday Morning / 2h

Feira Hippie de Ipanema

Browse this colorful Sunday open-air market filled with local crafts, art, musical instruments, and delicious street food. It is bustling, so keep toddlers in a carrier or stroller as you sample tapioca crepes together.

Morning / 2h

Bosque da Barra

Visit this peaceful nature reserve to spot capybaras, caimans, and exotic birds along flat, easy walking trails. There is a great wooden playground and plenty of shade, making it a wonderful, low-stress spot for toddlers to stretch their legs.

🍽️ Where to eat

Confeitaria Colombo

Order the traditional coxinha and a selection of delicate Portuguese egg tarts in this stunning, historic Belle Époque cafe.

Churrascaria Palace

Indulge in an upscale rodízio experience with prime cuts of beef, grilled cheese, and an extensive buffet that is free for children under five.

Garota de Ipanema

Try the famous, sizzling picanha na chapa served with rice, beans, and farofa at the birthplace of the famous bossa nova song.

Bibi Sucos

Grab a refreshing açaí bowl topped with bananas and granola, paired with a freshly squeezed tropical juice and hot pão de queijo.

Tacacá do Norte

Savor authentic Amazonian flavors with a cold bowl of premium unsweetened açaí or a quick, delicious crab pastel.

👨‍👩‍👧 Look for 'Baixo Bebê' areas on Copacabana and Ipanema beaches, which are designated family zones featuring beach playgrounds, toy rentals, and toddler-friendly amenities. Always pack a lightweight stroller for paved boardwalks and a sturdy baby carrier for historic, cobblestoned sites or crowded tourist spots.
