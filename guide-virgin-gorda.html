Virgin Gorda

← back to travel board
With its dramatic granite formations, quiet turquoise coves, and laid-back Caribbean rhythm, Virgin Gorda is a paradise designed for effortless family exploration. Here, gentle waves lap against soft white sands, making it incredibly safe and welcoming for your littlest travelers. From climbing through mystical sea caves to dining with your toes in the sand, every day feels like a tropical storybook come to life.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min open-air safari taxi from Virgin Gorda Airport to Spanish Town hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Baths National Park

Explore a labyrinth of giant granite boulders forming secret tidal pools and grottoes. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow, shaded pockets of water, but be sure to use a baby carrier as strollers cannot navigate the narrow rocky passages.

Morning / 2h

Spring Bay Beach

Located just north of The Baths, this beach features incredibly calm, crystal-clear water and a natural wading pool sheltered by boulders. It is the ultimate worry-free swimming spot for parents with toddlers.

Morning / 1.5h

Gorda Peak National Park

Take an easy, shaded hike through a lush semi-rainforest to an observation tower with panoramic views of the surrounding islands. The lower portion of the trail is flat and well-cleared, making it highly manageable for little legs.

Afternoon / 2h

Devil's Bay Beach

Arrive at this pristine, horseshoe-shaped beach via a scenic walk through the boulders from The Baths. The soft, powdery sand is perfect for building sandcastles, and the calm shoreline is ideal for toddler wading.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Savannah Bay

A long, quiet stretch of white sand beach protected by a reef, meaning virtually no strong waves. It is highly peaceful, spacious, and perfect for toddlers to safely practice their first ocean swims.

Evening / 3h

Leverick Bay Marina

A lively marina hub featuring a freshwater pool, shops, and a beach area. Toddlers will love dancing to the live steel pan music during the famous weekly beach barbecue nights.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Virgin Gorda Yacht Harbour

Stroll along the marina to watch the luxury yachts, grab a fresh fruit smoothie, and let toddlers wander the grassy, car-free promenade. It is a relaxed, low-key spot for an afternoon walk.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Copper Mine National Park

Walk through the dramatic, windy ruins of a 19th-century copper mine set against the crashing Atlantic ocean. Keep toddlers closely in hand or in a carrier as you explore the historic stone structures.

Morning / 30m

Buck's Food Market

Visit this centrally located local market in Spanish Town to stock up on fresh tropical fruits, organic snacks, and toddler essentials. It is a colorful and easy stop to get a taste of local island life.

🍽️ Where to eat

CocoMaya Restaurant

Get the coconut-crusted calamari and house-made sushi rolls while the kids play safely in the white sand dining area right at your feet.

The Bath and Turtle

Get the classic, crispy fish and chips in a highly casual, family-welcoming courtyard setting right in the heart of Spanish Town.

Hog Heaven

Get the slow-cooked pulled pork platter with a side of cheesy baked macaroni that toddlers will absolutely love, accompanied by world-class hilltop views.

Sugarcane Restaurant

Get the local lobster flatbread and let the kids splash in the adjacent freshwater pool while you wait for your meal to arrive.

Top of the Baths

Get a refreshing mango smoothie and Caribbean chicken roti while enjoying the panoramic pool deck right at the entrance to the national park.
