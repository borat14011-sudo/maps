Virgin Gorda

← back to travel board
Virgin Gorda offers an exclusive Caribbean sanctuary where dramatic geological wonders meet tranquil, untouched shores. The island's signature gentle breezes and slow-paced lifestyle provide the perfect upscale environment for a luxury family getaway. Parents can easily navigate the soft sands of Savannah Bay or relax under the shaded palms with baby Mary Claire. The calm, shallow waters of the island's premier beachfront resorts offer a safe, warm environment for an infant's first dip in the sea.
🚕 Getting there: Fly into Beef Island Airport (EIS), then take a private 30-minute speed charter directly to Spanish Town where a private open-air safari vehicle with a pre-arranged infant car seat awaits.

What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

The Baths National Park

Explore the iconic pocket beaches and giant granite boulders; use a supportive baby carrier rather than a stroller and visit early in the morning to beat the tropical heat.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Savannah Bay

A pristine stretch of white sand with incredibly calm, shallow waters that are ideal for wading with baby Mary Claire, though you should bring a pop-up UV tent for shade.

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Devil's Bay National Park

Reach this secluded paradise via a scenic, flat trail using a baby carrier to enjoy the quietest white sands and gentlest surf on the southern tip of the island.

Early morning 8-9:30am / 1.5h

Gorda Peak National Park

Hike the heavily shaded lower loop trail under a canopy of lush mahogany trees, offering a cool climate and a peaceful morning nap environment for an infant in a carrier.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Spring Bay

A stunning beach featuring giant boulders that form natural, wave-free wading pools, acting as a safe and tranquil natural baby bath for Mary Claire.

Late afternoon 4:30-5:30pm / 1h

Copper Mine Ruins

Stroll along a completely flat, stroller-accessible gravel path to view the historic brick engine house standing dramatically against the breezy, scenic Atlantic cliffs.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Leverick Bay Marina

Walk along the wide, stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks to view yachts, browse local boutiques, and enjoy soft steel pan music during the gentle sunset hours.

Midday 11:30am-1:30pm / 2h

Saba Rock

Take a smooth, five-minute resort ferry over calm waters to this tiny island attraction where baby can watch giant tarpon feeding from the safe, shaded boardwalk decks.

Morning 9am-1pm / 4h

Prickly Pear Island

Charter a short private water taxi to Vixen Point where you will find exceptionally flat waters, soft sand, and spacious, shady rental cabanas perfect for infant naptime.

🍽️ Where to eat

CocoMaya Restaurant

Enjoy signature coconut wok-tossed calamari and fresh sushi at early seating while baby Mary Claire plays safely in the soft sand right beside your low lounge table.

The Sugar Mill at Rosewood Little Dix Bay

Savor pan-roasted local snapper within a historic stone mill featuring quiet outdoor terrace dining, premium highchairs, and an early dinner service that respects infant bedtimes.

The Restaurant at Leverick Bay

Taste delicious local lobster thermidor on a breezy, open-air waterfront deck that provides highchairs and a relaxed, ambient harbor noise level to mask any infant fussiness.

Saba Rock Restaurant

Dine on grilled mahi-mahi tacos at this open-air overwater pavilion, easily reached via a flat ferry dock and offering a breezy, vibrant, and highly family-friendly midday atmosphere.

Hog Heaven

Relish slow-cooked BBQ ribs at this casual hilltop spot with cool mountain breezes, spacious outdoor picnic tables, and a highly welcoming, low-key environment for families.
