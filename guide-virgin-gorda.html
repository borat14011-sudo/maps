Virgin Gorda captivates with its dramatic granite boulder formations, pristine white sands, and a laid-back yachting lifestyle that defines Caribbean luxury. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the island offers a tranquil escape far removed from the bustling crowds of larger resorts. The shallow, crystal-clear tide pools of Spring Bay provide a perfectly safe, natural paddling pool for an infant's first dip. Accessible boardwalks at Leverick Bay Marina allow for seamless stroller walks alongside sleek catamarans under the warm tropical sun.
🚕 Getting there: Fly into Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport on Beef Island, Tortola. Take a 30-minute scenic private water taxi to Spanish Town, Virgin Gorda, followed by a 10-minute private air-conditioned SUV transfer directly to your resort, totaling roughly 45 minutes of travel time with seamless luggage and stroller handling.

What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Spring Bay National Park

Calm, shallow waters nestled between giant granite boulders make this a natural, shaded paddling pool perfect for baby Mary Claire. Stroller access is limited, so use a front carrier for the short walk down from the parking area.

Morning 8-10am / 2h

The Baths National Park

Marvel at the world-famous colossal tide pools. Visit early to avoid cruise crowds, and stick to the main sandy entrance of the beach area with a baby carrier, skipping the tight crevices of Devil's Bay Trail which are too steep for an infant.

Late morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Savannah Bay

A breathtaking, sweeping crescent of white sand with exceptionally calm, shallow water. Perfect for laying out a UV beach tent for Mary Claire to nap while Will and Kathryn enjoy the gentle, cooling trade winds and pristine shoreline.

Morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

Leverick Bay Marina Boardwalk

Enjoy a leisurely morning stroller walk along the wooden boardwalk. Watch the luxury yachts dock and grab a fresh juice, benefiting from flat, easy-to-navigate paths and plenty of harbor shade.

Afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Saba Rock Islet

Take a quick, fun 5-minute ferry ride from Gun Creek to this iconic islet. Mary Claire will love watching the daily tarpon feeding from the safety of the shaded, breezy observation deck while parents enjoy a refreshing cocktail.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Copper Mine National Park

Explore the historic 19th-century chimney ruins set against dramatic coastal cliffs. The gravel path is stroller-friendly with care, offering spectacular ocean views and a refreshing sea breeze for the family.

Early morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Gorda Peak National Park

Hike the lush canopy trails for panoramic views of the archipelago. Keep baby in a cool, breathable carrier to navigate the dirt stairs, enjoying the natural shade and cooler temperatures at the island's highest point.

Mid-day 10am-2pm / 4h

Prickly Pear Island

Charter a water taxi to this uninhabited national park. The calm, shallow shoreline of Sandbox Beach offers excellent shade trees and a peaceful setting for an exclusive, quiet family beach day.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Nail Bay Beach

A quiet, secluded escape sheltered by a coral reef. The calm waters are ideal for gentle baby wading, and the nearby ruins of the sugar plantation offer a shady, picturesque backdrop for family photos.

🍽️ Where to eat

CocoMaya Restaurant

Try the signature CocoMaya House Ribs and fresh sushi. This beachfront chic venue features a sandy lounge area perfect for early dinner seatings where Mary Claire can play safely while parents enjoy sunset cocktails.

The Restaurant at Leverick Bay

Savor the local fresh catch of the day or a prime steak. The spacious open-air terrace easily accommodates highchairs, and the lively but relaxed atmosphere easily masks any baby babble.

The Bath and Turtle

Indulge in their famous stone-baked pizzas and local rotis. Located in Spanish Town, this casual courtyard venue is incredibly welcoming to young families, offering early dinner service and plenty of highchairs.

Saba Rock Restaurant

Enjoy a grilled lobster tail right on the water. The open-air, breezy island pavilion is accessible via a short boat ride, providing a stimulating but relaxed environment for an early family dinner.

Hog Heaven

Savor slow-cooked pulled pork with panoramic views of the North Sound. This highly casual, open-air ridge-top spot has picnic tables and a relaxed vibe that is ideal for a quick, stress-free lunch with a baby.
