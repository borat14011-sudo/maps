What to do

Early morning 8-11am

Xunantunich Maya Ruins

Climb the towering El Castillo for panoramic jungle views; stroller-accessible pathways near the base and ample shade make it manageable with a baby. Avoid midday heat — go early morning.

Morning 9am-12pm

Cahal Pech Maya Ruins

Closer to town, this smaller site offers quiet paths under dense canopy. Stroller-friendly main plaza, and the museum has a cool interior for a break. Perfect half-day trip.

2-3 hours, any time

Belize Botanic Gardens

Gorgeous gardens along the Macal River with labeled plants, a butterfly house, and shaded trails. Stroller-friendly wide paths and a picnic area; calm setting for baby. Skip if heavy rain.

45 minutes to 1 hour

Green Hills Butterfly Ranch

An enclosed butterfly exhibit with hundreds of species; cool and calm, perfect for infants. Stroller accessible, but carry baby for best views. Short visit ideal.

N/A (not recommended for baby)

Actun Tunichil Muknal (ATM) Cave

World-class cave with Maya artifacts — but NOT suitable for infants due to long swims and tight passages. Skip unless you have childcare. Note for parents: consider a private sitter through your hotel.

2 hours, morning preferred

Barton Creek Cave

Canoe through a peaceful cave system with stalactites; calm water, no hiking. Baby can sit on a parent's lap in the canoe (life vest provided). Shaded and cool.

2-3 hours midday

Rio on Pools

Natural granite pools in the Mountain Pine Ridge; gentle cascades and cool water. Easy access with a short walk — baby can splash in shallow areas. Bring sun protection.

1 hour

Big Rock Falls

A stunning 150-foot waterfall with a pool at the base. The trail is steep but short — not stroller-friendly (carry baby in carrier). Worth a quick visit for photos and cool mist.

Morning 7-10am

San Ignacio Market

Vibrant local market with fresh fruit, crafts, and lively atmosphere. Stroller may be tough on rough ground; use a baby carrier. Great for grabbing snacks and souvenirs. Go early to avoid crowds.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Running W Steakhouse

Upscale steakhouse with a covered outdoor terrace overlooking the river. Highchair available, early seating at 5pm for families. Their Belizean ribeye and grilled lobster are signature dishes.

Guava Limb

Creative farm-to-table dining in a restored colonial house. Quiet courtyard seating, kids menu, and highchairs. Try the coconut curry grouper or the Belizean tapas platter. Book ahead.

Erva's Restaurant

Beloved local spot serving traditional Belizean dishes like stew chicken and rice & beans. Casual, family-friendly with outdoor picnic tables. Highchair available; baby may enjoy the no-fuss vibe.

Ko-Ox Han Nah

Authentic Maya cuisine in a rustic setting. Try the pibil (slow-cooked pork) or tamales. Shaded garden seating; no highchair but staff happy to accommodate. Calm atmosphere, good for early dinner.

Pop's Restaurant

Airy, modern restaurant with river views and a playground for older kids (baby can watch). Highchairs, outdoor terrace, and a menu with burgers, seafood, and Belizean classics. Family favorite.

$300-400/night

Eco-luxury lodge on a private nature reserve. Family cottages with separate sleeping areas, crib provided, and an infinity pool overlooking the Macal River. On-site naturalist for baby-friendly nature walks.

San Ignacio Resort Hotel

$200-300/night

Classic luxury in the heart of town with an outdoor pool, tropical gardens, and a restaurant with highchairs. Family suites available, crib on request, and blackout curtains. Easy access to shops and ruins.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stock up on diapers, wipes, and formula at Farmacia San Ignacio (downtown) — limited selection, so bring essentials from home. Stroller terrain varies: paved riverwalk and hotel grounds are fine, but cobblestone streets and ruin paths require a baby carrier for bumpy spots. For heat, use a rechargeable clip-on fan, dress baby in breathable cotton, and plan outdoor activities before 10am or after 4pm. Nearest pediatric care is at Cayo Medical Center (a 5-minute drive from town).
