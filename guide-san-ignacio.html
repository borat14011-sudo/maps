San Ignacio

← back to travel board
Nestled in the lush Cayo District, San Ignacio is Belize's beating heart of adventure, where emerald rainforests meet ancient Mayan ruins. Families can explore winding river paths, encounter vibrant wildlife, and dive into a rich cultural tapestry together. It is a welcoming, laid-back haven where slow travel and wild discoveries perfectly align for parents and little ones alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~2 hours via private shuttle or rental car from Philip S.W. Goldson International Airport (BZE) to San Ignacio

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Cahal Pech Mayan Ruins

An incredibly accessible and shaded Mayan site perched on a hill overlooking the town, featuring grassy plazas perfect for running. Toddlers will love exploring the low, grassy pathways without the massive climbs of larger ruins.

Afternoon / 1h

Green Iguana Conservation Project

Located inside the San Ignacio Resort Hotel, this hands-on exhibit allows kids to meet, hold, and feed friendly green iguanas. It is highly interactive and perfectly safe for toddlers under close supervision.

Saturday Morning / 1-2h

San Ignacio Market

A bustling, colorful hub of local life especially on Saturdays, packed with fresh tropical fruits, handmade crafts, and local snacks. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the vibrant colors, and it is a great spot to grab a sweet, fresh banana or coconut water.

Morning / 3h

Xunantunich Mayan Ruins

Take a hand-cranked ferry across the Mopan River to reach this majestic ancient city surrounded by jungle. While the climb up El Castillo is for older kids, toddlers will enjoy the ferry ride and spotting howler monkeys in the trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Belize Botanic Gardens

Spread over 45 acres, these beautiful gardens showcase Belize's incredible native flora, orchid houses, and walking trails. It is a peaceful, stroller-friendly escape where toddlers can safely toddle along the grassy paths.

Afternoon / 2h

Macal River Tubing and Canoeing

A gentle paddle or tube ride down the calm Macal River offers a relaxing way to see local birds and lush riverbanks. Toddlers can safely sit in a double kayak or canoe with parents wearing a toddler-sized life jacket.

Evening / 1h

Cayo Welcome Center and Plaza

A pedestrian-friendly town square in the heart of San Ignacio with green spaces, benches, and occasional live music. It is a stress-free spot for toddlers to stretch their legs and eat an ice cream away from vehicle traffic.

Midday / 30m

A&R Enterprise Store

A local department-style store in town that is a treasure trove for inexpensive beach toys, coloring books, and snacks. It's a lifesaver for parents needing to restock on distraction items for long car rides or quiet hotel afternoons.

Afternoon / 1h

Ajaw Chocolate Making Workshop

Learn how the ancient Maya made chocolate from cacao beans, complete with grinding the beans on a traditional stone metate. Toddlers will love the sensory experience of smelling the cacao and tasting the child-friendly sweet versions.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Guava Limb Restaurant & Cafe

Order the wood-fired artisanal pizzas and fresh, colorful salads in their beautiful, air-conditioned garden-style cafe.

Ko-Ox Han-nah (Let's Go Eat)

Try the traditional Belizean stew chicken with rice and beans, which is mild, tender, and incredibly kid-friendly.

Erva's Restaurant

Savor the freshly made quesadillas and local Belizean salbutes in a relaxed, family-welcoming atmosphere.

Pop's Restaurant

Go for breakfast and order their famous fluffy fry jacks served with eggs, refried beans, and cheese.

Cenaida's Belizean Cuisine

Grab a plate of their delicious, home-style fish fillet in coconut sauce or tender chicken fajitas.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a lightweight, sturdy baby carrier instead of a stroller, as Mayan ruins and jungle paths are uneven and rocky. Always keep a high-quality insect repellent and kid-safe sunscreen handy to protect against the tropical sun and forest bugs.
