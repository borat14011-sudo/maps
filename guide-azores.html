Azores

The Azores offer a breathtaking playground of emerald crater lakes, warm thermal springs, and marine adventures in the heart of the Atlantic. It is an incredibly safe, uncrowded paradise where families can explore dramatic volcanic landscapes at a relaxed, toddler-friendly pace.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi from Ponta Delgada Airport (PDL) to downtown Ponta Delgada hotels

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Sete Cidades Vista do Rei

Peer into the massive twin volcanic lakes, one blue and one green, using the stroller-accessible lookout paths. Keep toddlers close to the fenced railings and bring a jacket for the summit wind.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Terra Nostra Park

Explore a botanical wonderland of giant ferns and winding paths, ending at a warm geothermal mineral pool. The iron-rich water stains fabrics, so dress your toddler in an old swimsuit.

Morning / 2h

Caldeira Velha

Soak in jungle-like warm springs surrounded by a lush forest canopy and a cascading waterfall. The lower pools are shallow and lukewarm, making them perfect for gentle toddler wading.

Morning / 1h

Mercado da Graça

Stroll through this vibrant market to sample sweet local pineapples and rich Azorean cheeses. It is flat, lively, and offers an easy, sensory-rich morning walk for little ones.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Gorreana Tea Plantation

Wander through Europe's oldest tea plantation featuring rolling green hedges and a vintage factory. Toddlers will love running along the wide, maze-like paths of the tea fields.

Morning / 3h

Whale and Dolphin Watching

Take a boat tour from the harbor to see wild dolphins and whales leaping in the Atlantic. Book a large, stable catamaran rather than a zodiac for a smooth and safe toddler ride.

Afternoon / 1h

Plantação de Ananases A. Arruda

Walk inside unique glass greenhouses to see how sweet Azorean pineapples are grown in soil beds. It is a quick, free stop with a lovely garden and delicious pineapple ice cream.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Praia das Milícias

Build sandcastles on a wide, striking beach covered in soft, dark volcanic sand. The sand gets hot under the sun, so put water shoes on your toddler's feet.

Morning / 1-2h

Lagoa das Furnas Fumaroles

Watch dramatic volcanic steam rise from the ground and see chefs dig up pots of slow-cooked stew. Secure wooden boardwalks make it easy and safe to keep toddlers away from the heat.

🍽️ Where to eat

A Tasca

Order the famous grilled limpets and a slice of pineapple cake in this bustling, highly welcoming tavern.

Restaurante Tony's

Try the authentic Cozido das Furnas, a rich meat and vegetable stew slow-cooked underground by volcanic steam.

O Barbosa

Feast on incredibly fresh grilled local fish or tender steak in a relaxed, family-friendly space near the marina.

Associação Agrícola de São Miguel

Savor the absolute best local beef steaks in a spacious, lively venue equipped with high chairs and fast service.

Tasca Morrison

Grab excellent artisanal burgers topped with regional cheeses and served alongside crispy sweet potato fries.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack layers and rain gear for everyone, as the Azores are known for experiencing four seasons in a single day. Renting a car with a pre-booked car seat is essential for reaching the island's remote volcanic sights and parks at your toddler's pace.
