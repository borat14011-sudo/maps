What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Sete Cidades Twin Lakes Vista

Gaze down at the breathtaking blue and green twin crater lakes from the Vista do Rei viewpoint. It is easily accessible with a stroller directly from the parking lot, making it stress-free for families.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Terra Nostra Park

Explore a whimsical botanical garden filled with hidden pathways and a giant, warm geothermal pool. The iron-rich orange water is great for a family float, but pack old swimsuits as it can stain.

Late Morning / 1-2h

Gorreana Tea Plantation

Walk through Europe's oldest tea plantation and watch the vintage machinery process the leaves. The wide, terraced green fields are safe and perfect for toddlers to burn off some energy.

Morning / 3h

Whale and Dolphin Watching

Embark on a boat tour to spot resident sperm whales and playful dolphins jumping in the Atlantic. Booking a larger catamaran ensures a smoother, more stable ride for little ones prone to seasickness.

Morning / 1h

Mercado da Graca

Immerse your senses in Ponta Delgada's bustling farmers market filled with local pineapples, fresh cheeses, and flowers. Toddlers will love tasting the sweet, local baby pineapples sliced fresh on the spot.

Morning / 2h

Caldeira Velha

Bathe in warm, jungle-like thermal pools fed by a cascading waterfall. Use the shallow, cooler lower pools which are much safer and more comfortable for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Pinhal da Paz Forest Reserve

Discover a peaceful forest reserve featuring manicured gardens, picnic areas, and free-roaming peacocks. The massive, well-maintained playground is an absolute paradise for active toddlers.

Morning / 1.5h

Lagoa do Congro

Hike down to a mystical, neon-green lake hidden deep within a dense, mossy forest. The trail is short but can be muddy and steep, so swap the stroller for a sturdy baby carrier.

Afternoon / 1h

Ceramica Vieira

Visit a historic pottery factory to watch artisans hand-paint traditional blue-and-white ceramics. Children will be mesmerized by the potters spinning clay wheels in the open workshop.

🍽️ Where to eat

A Tasca

Order the famous grilled limpets with garlic butter and follow it up with a slice of their legendary local sweet pineapple cake.

Restaurante Tony's

Try the iconic Cozido das Furnas, a rich meat and vegetable stew slow-cooked underground by natural volcanic steam.

Associacao Agricola de Sao Miguel

Indulge in the Bife a Associacao, a legendary, tender local steak topped with a fried egg and regional red peppers.

Oves e Alho

Grab a table at this cozy spot and order the local Queijo da Josefa baked cheese with honey and a side of sweet potato fries.

Quinta dos Acores

Enjoy delicious burgers and treat the kids to artisanal ice cream made from the milk of grass-fed Azorean cows.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and light rain jackets, as Azorean weather can change from sunshine to rain in minutes. Rent a car early and ensure you reserve a child car seat in advance, as driving is the only practical way to explore the island's remote nature spots.
