Bruges enchants with cobblestone lanes, serene canals, and fairy-tale gabled houses — a dream for art lovers and romantics. The Minnewater Park offers a stroller-friendly lakeside promenade where baby Mary Claire can gaze at swans. Many canal boat tours welcome infants with life jackets, providing calm, shaded views of the city. Luxury hotels here provide blackout curtains and cribs, ensuring restful nights after days of gentle exploration.
🚕 Getting there: Brussels Airport to Bruges: 1 hour 15 minutes by private transfer (car seat and stroller included) or 1 hour via InterCity train from Brussels-Midi to Bruges station (elevators and luggage areas available; book a first-class compartment for extra space).

What to do

Late morning 10:30am-12pm

Belfry of Bruges

Climb 366 steps for panoramic views — but skip the climb with an infant; instead, admire the carillon from the Markt square below, which is stroller-friendly and shaded by umbrellas.

Early morning 9-10am

Church of Our Lady Bruges

Home to Michelangelo's Madonna and Child — stroller-accessible nave with quiet, cool interior perfect for a short visit. The adjacent gardens offer a calm spot for nursing.

Morning 9-11am

Minnewater Lake and Park

A peaceful, flat lakeside park with wide paths ideal for strollers. Swans, bridges, and a castle backdrop — perfect for baby's first photo and a gentle walk.

Afternoon 1-2pm

Basilica of the Holy Blood

A small, ornate chapel on Burg square housing a relic. The short visit is doable with a baby in a carrier — low noise, dim light, and no steps inside.

Midday 11am-1pm

Groeningemuseum

Flemish masterpieces in a stroller-friendly museum (elevators available). The Baby Room on site has changing facilities, and the café terrace allows feeding.

Afternoon 2-2:30pm

Bruges Canals Boat Tour

A 30-minute quiet canal ride under low bridges — life jackets provided for infants, shaded seats, and soft lapping water. Best early afternoon when crowds thin.

Late morning 10:30-11:30am

Begijnhof Ten Wijngaerde

A tranquil, white-walled courtyard with a single entrance (stroller-friendly) and a small museum. The garden is quiet, perfect for baby to crawl on grass.

Early afternoon 1-2pm

Sint-Janshuismolen

One of Bruges' last working windmills on a grassy hill. Accessible via a gentle slope (stroller possible), with a small interactive mill museum.

Afternoon 3-4pm

Chocolate Museum (Choco-Story)

Interactive exhibits with chocolate tasting — noisy at times, but the quieter upper floors and a baby-changing station make it feasible. Stroller-friendly entry ramp.

🍽️ Where to eat

Den Dyver

Michelin-starred Belgian cuisine with a tasting menu; highchairs available, quiet atmosphere, and a garden terrace. Best for an early seating with baby.

De Halve Maan

Historic brewery with a family-friendly beer hall and rooftop views. Highchairs, stroller parking, and a non-alcoholic option for parents. Noise moderate.

The Olive Tree

Authentic Italian in a cozy setting; children's menu, highchairs, and a calm back room. Perfect for early dinner with Mary Claire.

't Brugs Beertje

Famous beer café with over 300 Belgian brews; small but stroller-friendly if placed at a quiet table. No highchairs, but staff will help. Best for lunch.

Restaurant De Karmeliet

Three-Michelin-star dining (closed Sun/Mon) — only for a special evening with babysitter; no highchairs, but they can accommodate a bassinet in private room. Reserve months ahead.

$200-400/night

Charming Relais & Châteaux property along a canal; family suite with balcony, crib, and blackout curtains. Heated indoor pool, breakfast with highchairs, and a 24-hour concierge for baby needs.
