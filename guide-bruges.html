Bruges

← back to travel board
Step into a real-life storybook where winding canals reflect historic guildhouses and the scent of warm chocolate fills the air. Bruges is remarkably walkable and mostly car-free, making its medieval streets a magical playground for families. From gentle boat rides to sprawling green parks, this Belgian gem offers effortless wonder for travelers of all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~70 min direct train or private transfer from Brussels Airport (BRU) straight to the historic city center

What to do

Morning / 45 mins

Canal Boat Tour (Boottochten Brugge)

Enjoy a peaceful drift down the historic waterways, offering a unique duck's-eye view of medieval bridges and secret gardens. Toddlers will love pointing at the resident swans and waving at people on the bridges.

Morning / 1-2h

Belfry of Bruges & Markt

The bustling main square is lined with colorful step-gabled houses and features horse-drawn carriages that kids adore watching. While the 366-step climb up the tower is too steep for toddlers, the lively square has plenty of space to safely run around.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Minnewater Park (Lake of Love)

A tranquil, leafy escape on the edge of the historic center featuring scenic bridges, weeping willows, and a large population of friendly swans. Paved paths make it highly stroller-friendly for an easy afternoon walk.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Choco-Story (The Chocolate Museum)

An interactive, multi-sensory journey through the history of cocoa, featuring family-friendly exhibits and a live chocolate-making demonstration. Toddlers get to enjoy fresh chocolate samples at the end of the tour.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Koning Albert I Park Playground

A spacious green park just outside the busy shopping streets that hosts a modern, fenced-in wooden playground. It is the perfect spot to let toddlers swing, climb, and slide while parents relax on the shaded benches.

Morning / 1.5h

Historium Brugge

A sensory historical attraction that transports you back to the Golden Age of Bruges through music, special effects, and themed rooms. There are family-friendly activity booths and a panoramic terrace overlooking the main market square.

Morning or Afternoon / 3-4h

Boudewijn Seapark

A nearby amusement park featuring an extensive indoor playground, gentle vintage carousel rides, and a massive water splash park. Toddlers will find plenty of soft-play areas and low-thrill rides designed just for their size.

Afternoon / 1h

Frietmuseum (Frite Museum)

The world's only museum dedicated to Belgian fries, featuring quirky exhibits that are visually fun for small children. The tour ends in the medieval cellars where families can sample freshly fried, crispy Belgian fries.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

The Windmills of Bruges (Kruisvest)

Take a scenic walk along the old city ramparts to see four historic, majestic wooden windmills standing on grassy hills. Toddlers will love running up and rolling down the gentle grassy slopes away from city traffic.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Old Chocolate House

Go for their legendary DIY hot chocolate, served in a giant mug with a chocolate bowl filled with callets to melt yourself in a cozy upstairs tea room.

House of Waffles

Get the classic, crispy Brussels waffle topped with fresh strawberries and whipped cream in a highly kid-friendly, bright setting.

Bistro Den Amigo

Order the family-friendly Flemish beef stew (stoofvlees) which is slow-cooked to tender perfection and served with an endless supply of crispy fries.

Books & Brunch

Enjoy a healthy organic family breakfast of pancakes and fresh fruit surrounded by cozy shelves of secondhand children's books to read together.

Pasta Maria

Grab a quick, delicious cup of take-away fresh pasta with mild homemade tomato sauce that is perfect for a fussy toddler on the move.

👨‍👩‍👧 Opt for an all-terrain stroller or a sturdy baby carrier to comfortably navigate the historic cobblestone streets. Note that many public restrooms charge a small fee, so always keep some 50-cent coins handy in your diaper bag.
