Punta Cana

← back to travel board
Punta Cana welcomes families with its powdery white-sand beaches, swaying coconut palms, and exceptionally calm, shallow waters. Beyond the all-inclusive resorts lie lush ecological reserves, playful animal encounters, and rich cultural experiences designed to captivate travelers of all ages. It is a Caribbean paradise where convenience meets tropical adventure, making every day feel effortlessly magical.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Bávaro Beach

Enjoy miles of soft, footprint-free sand and incredibly calm, shallow water perfect for splashing. Toddlers will love building sandcastles under the shade of mature palm trees.

Morning / 2-3h

Hoyo Azul

Journey through a lush rainforest trail to swim in a breathtaking, hidden cenote filled with crystal-clear turquoise water. While the cenote is deep, toddlers can safely enjoy the scenic boardwalk walk and the park's petting zoo.

Morning / 2h

Indigenous Eyes Ecological Reserve

Walk through shaded, stroller-friendly dirt paths to discover twelve freshwater lagoons nestled in a protected forest. Toddlers will delight in spotting turtles and colorful fish swimming in the clear waters.

Afternoon / 3h

Monkeyland Punta Cana

Interact with friendly, inquisitive squirrel monkeys who will happily climb onto your shoulders for a photo. Toddlers will be absolutely enchanted by these gentle, pint-sized creatures in a safe environment.

Afternoon / 1-2h

ChocoMuseo Punta Cana

Learn how Dominican cacao goes from bean to bar in a free, interactive museum with sweet tastings. Kids of all ages can participate in a hands-on chocolate-making workshop that is both delicious and mess-friendly.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Macao Beach

A beautiful, undeveloped public beach known for its dramatic cliffs and local surf culture. Families can splash in the gentler shoreline areas, and toddlers will love watching the surfers ride the waves.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Galerías Punta Cana

Stroll through this vibrant, open-air shopping village featuring local crafts, ice cream shops, and a dedicated children's playground. It is a relaxed, pedestrian-only spot perfect for a safe family stroll and souvenir hunting.

Morning / 3-4h

Bávaro Adventure Park

An outdoor adventure hub featuring ziplines, a dinosaur park with animatronic figures, and a dedicated playground. The Dino World exhibit is a massive hit for toddlers who love prehistoric giants.

Morning / 4-5h

La Hacienda Park

Experience traditional Dominican countryside life with a safari ride, a visit to a coffee plantation, and gentle horseback riding. Toddlers will enjoy the slow-paced safari truck ride and meeting farm animals.

Where to eat

  • La Yola Restaurant — Perched over the water at Marina Punta Cana, this stunning restaurant serves fresh seafood with a glass-bottom floor section that kids will love peering through to spot fish.
  • Sancocho — This hearty, comforting Dominican meat and vegetable stew is naturally mild and easy for toddlers to eat when mashed.
  • Jellyfish Beach Restaurant — A gorgeous beachside eatery offering casual dining with your toes in the sand, featuring a kids' menu and plenty of beachfront space for restless little ones to play.
  • Mofongo — A delicious, savory dish of mashed fried plantains with garlic and pork cracklings that kids love for its fun shape and rich, comforting flavor.
