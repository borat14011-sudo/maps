Guadeloupe (Pointe-à-Pitre)

← back to travel board
Guadeloupe offers families a vibrant blend of lush rainforests, calm turquoise lagoons, and rich Creole culture. Centered around Pointe-à-Pitre, this butterfly-shaped archipelago is remarkably easy to navigate with little ones in tow. From gentle white-sand beaches to bustling spice markets, it is a sensory playground for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~15-20 min taxi or rental car ride from Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP) to the main beach resorts in Le Gosier

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

La Datcha Beach (Le Gosier)

This gorgeous beach features incredibly calm, shallow waters protected by a reef, making it a safe wading pool for toddlers. It is also lit up at night for a magical evening swim.

Morning / 1-2h

Marché de la Darse (Pointe-à-Pitre)

Introduce your little ones to tropical sights and scents at this bustling waterfront market filled with colorful exotic fruits, spices, and local crafts. Toddlers will love tasting sweet bananas and seeing the fresh fish.

Morning / 3h

Parc des Mamelles (Zoo de Guadeloupe)

Located in the lush national park, this beautifully maintained zoo features wooden walkways where kids can spot jaguars, monkeys, and colorful parrots. The lower paths are manageable for sturdy toddlers, though strollers aren't recommended.

Afternoon / 3-4h

Plage de Bois Jolan (Sainte-Anne)

With its knee-deep, crystal-clear water extending far out and plenty of natural shade from coconut palms, this is the ultimate toddler-friendly beach on Grande-Terre. Pack a picnic and let the little ones splash safely for hours.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Memorial ACTe Plaza

This striking architectural marvel on the waterfront teaches Caribbean history, and while the interior exhibits are for older kids, the wide, pedestrian-only harbor plaza outside is perfect for toddlers to run around safely.

Midday / 1-2h

Aquarium de la Guadeloupe (Le Gosier)

This compact and air-conditioned aquarium is the perfect size for a toddler's short attention span, featuring sea turtles, sharks, and touch pools. It offers a great, cool escape from the midday Caribbean sun.

Morning / 2-3h

Jardin Botanique de Deshaies

Meander along smooth, stroller-friendly paths through gorgeous tropical plants, lily pads, and a waterfall. The absolute highlight for toddlers is feeding the colorful, friendly lorikeets that land right on your hands.

Morning / 3h

Plage de la Caravelle (Sainte-Anne)

This postcard-perfect beach features calm, bright blue waters and soft white sand that is perfect for building sandcastles. Keep an eye out for the friendly iguanas lounging in the trees, which will delight animal-loving toddlers.

Morning / 2-3h

Valombreuse Garden (Petit-Bourg)

This magical floral park features thousands of tropical plants, a beautiful waterfall, and a dedicated play area with inflatable castles and aquatic games. It is highly stroller-accessible and designed with young families in mind.

🍽️ Where to eat

Koté Sud (Sainte-Anne)

Order the mild, crispy accras de morue (cod fritters) and fresh fruit juices that toddlers will love sipping beachside.

Le Zoo Rock Café (Le Gosier)

Grab the child-friendly gourmet burgers or a plate of fresh grilled mahi-mahi in a lively, casual setting that welcomes busy toddlers.

Le Karacoli (Deshaies)

Sit directly on the sand and enjoy fresh grilled lobster or chicken Colombo while your toddler safely plays in the sand right next to your table.

An Chon Sa (Pointe-à-Pitre)

Try the traditional Creole chicken Colombo, a mild and flavorful coconut curry dish that is highly appealing to toddler palates.

Le Baguet (Sainte-Anne)

Get the freshly baked chocolate croissants and local fruit crepes for a sweet, easy, and kid-approved breakfast right on the beach.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car at the airport with a pre-booked car seat, as public transport is irregular and having your own vehicle is crucial for navigating both flat Grande-Terre and mountainous Bas-Terre. Always pack mosquito repellent and high-SPF mineral sunscreen, as the Caribbean sun is intense and shady beach spots can still host pesky sandflies in the late afternoon.
