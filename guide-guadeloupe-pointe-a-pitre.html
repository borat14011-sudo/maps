Guadeloupe (Pointe-à-Pitre)

← back to travel board
Guadeloupe blends Parisian culinary sophistication with untouched, lush Caribbean landscapes. The butterfly-shaped archipelago offers the perfect balance of rugged rainforested peaks and serene, reef-protected beaches. For families traveling with an infant, the calm, shallow turquoise waters of Sainte-Anne's lagoons provide an idyllic, wave-free environment for baby's first dip. Exploring the island is a breeze with well-maintained French infrastructure and accessible coastal promenades perfect for stroller walks.
🚕 Getting there: Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP) to the main resort area of Le Gosier or Sainte-Anne is a seamless 15 to 30-minute drive. The best luxury family option is a pre-booked private Mercedes V-Class transfer equipped with a rear-facing infant car seat, allowing you to load your stroller directly without folding.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Plage de la Caravelle

This breathtaking white-sand beach features calm, shallow, reef-protected waters perfect for dipping baby Mary Claire's toes. Abundant shade from leaning coconut palms offers natural protection from the midday sun. Stroller-friendly paths lead right up to the sand's edge.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Jardin Botanique de Deshaies

A beautifully manicured sanctuary featuring smooth, fully paved pathways that make stroller navigation effortless. Enjoy the vibrant tropical flora and quiet shaded gazebos while Mary Claire marvels at the colorful flamingos and koi ponds.

Midday 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Aquarium de la Guadeloupe

A modern, fully air-conditioned indoor aquarium located in Gosier, providing a cool refuge from the Caribbean heat. The wide, flat viewing areas are completely stroller-accessible, allowing Kathryn and Will to stroll easily while baby watches the glowing jellyfish.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Plage de Bois Jolan

Known for its remarkably shallow and wave-free lagoon, this beach is an infant paradise where you can sit in the water with baby. The low-hanging sea grape trees provide extensive natural shade, making it a highly relaxing afternoon spot.

Morning 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Zoo de Guadeloupe au Parc des Mamelles

Nestled in the lush rainforest of Basse-Terre, this quiet park offers cooler mountain temperatures. While the canopy walkways are not stroller-ready, using a high-end baby carrier allows you to walk shaded pathways beneath majestic mahogany trees.

Afternoon 1:30 PM - 3:30 PM

Mémorial ACTe

An architectural masterpiece and world-class museum in Pointe-à-Pitre. The indoor galleries are fully stroller-accessible, quiet, and air-conditioned, offering a sophisticated historical escape while Mary Claire naps in her bassinet.

Early morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM

Marché de la Darse

An early morning sensory delight along the Pointe-à-Pitre harbor, filled with the aromas of fresh vanilla and tropical fruits. Keep baby secure in a front carrier to navigate the vibrant stalls easily before the midday crowds arrive.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Maison de la Forêt

Located in the heart of the National Park, this site features a very short, flat, and shaded discovery trail through the rainforest. It provides cool, oxygen-rich mountain air and peaceful river sounds, perfect for a soothing contact nap in the carrier.

Late Afternoon 4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Plage de Grande Anse

A dramatic, sweeping golden-sand beach in Deshaies backed by lush palms. While the waves can be strong for swimming, the wide shoreline is perfect for a scenic sunset stroller walk along the firm damp sand under the golden canopy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Château de Feuilles

An exclusive, open-air gourmet restaurant set in a lush tropical garden in Anse-Bertrand. They gladly accommodate upscale families during early lunch seatings with spacious garden tables that easily fit a luxury stroller beside your chair.

Le Karacoli

A chic beachfront venue on Grande Anse beach offering French-Creole fusion. The outdoor wooden deck allows you to dine with your feet steps from the sand, offering plenty of room for a highchair and a relaxing ocean breeze.

La Rhumerie du Pirate

Perched on the dramatic rocky coast of Saint-François, this upscale restaurant offers a covered, breezy seaside terrace. The staff is exceptionally welcoming to babies during early dinner seatings, and the sound of crashing waves easily masks any baby babble.

L'Orangerie

Located at La Créole Beach Hôtel, this sophisticated restaurant features a beautiful covered outdoor patio overlooking tropical gardens. Perfect for a quiet dinner with baby, offering highchairs on request and a gentle, baby-soothing ambient playlist.

Koté Sud

A highly-rated culinary gem in Sainte-Anne specializing in upscale local Creole gastronomy. Its intimate yet open-air garden terrace is exceptionally welcoming for early evening reservations, allowing dining with your sleeping infant in a peaceful setting.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Langley Resort Fort Royal

$200-400/night

Located on a scenic northern cove of Basse-Terre, this resort offers spacious beachfront bungalows and suites ideal for families. They provide high-quality wooden cribs upon request, a large swimming pool, and direct access to a calm beach area.

La Créole Beach Hôtel & Spa

$220-380/night

A premier upscale resort in Gosier featuring large family suites, a beautiful palm-fringed swimming pool, and a sheltered private beach cove. The hotel offers cribs, professional babysitting services, and blackout curtains to ensure Mary Claire's nap schedule stays perfectly on track.

Auberge de la Vieille Tour

$250-400/night

Built around an 18th-century windmill, this historic, upscale property offers refined luxury and a quiet atmosphere. The spacious ocean-view rooms easily accommodate a crib, and the resort features a serene private beach with calm, shallow waters ideal for infants.
