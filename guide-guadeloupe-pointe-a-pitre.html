French Caribbean luxury meets effortless toddler-friendly adventure

Guadeloupe (Pointe-à-Pitre)

← back to travel board
Guadeloupe blends sophisticated French infrastructure with laid-back Creole culture, making it an incredibly seamless destination for a young family. The calm, reef-protected lagoons of Grande-Terre offer stress-free wading for your toddler, while the lush, shaded rainforests of Basse-Terre provide cool relief from the afternoon sun. For a finance dad who values efficiency and quality, the paved coastal roads, modern supermarkets stocked with premium French baby brands, and world-class culinary scene make this an easy, high-end Caribbean escape.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 to 20 minute rental car drive from Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP) to hotels in Le Gosier, or ~30 minutes to Sainte-Anne; booking a rental car with a pre-installed car seat is highly recommended.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Plage de la Caravelle

This postcard-perfect beach features exceptionally calm, shallow, turquoise water shaded by coconut palms, making it a dream for a toddler's first ocean dip. Park near the Club Med entrance and use an all-terrain stroller to wheel your gear right onto the hard-packed sand.

Morning / 1.5-2h

Jardin Botanique de Deshaies

Stroll along beautifully paved, stroller-friendly paths winding through lush tropical flora, lily pads, and tranquil waterfalls. Your toddler will love watching the colorful, friendly lorikeets that fly down to feed right out of your hand.

Midday / 1-1.5h

Aquarium de la Guadeloupe

Located right at the marina, this modern, fully air-conditioned aquarium offers a perfect escape from the midday sun with engaging shark and turtle exhibits. The flat, smooth layout is highly stroller-friendly and easy to navigate with an infant.

Morning / 2h

Zoo de Guadeloupe au Parc des Mamelles

A spectacular rainforest zoo built into the canopy, featuring well-maintained wooden boardwalks that showcase jaguars, monkeys, and tropical birds. Opt for a baby carrier here instead of a stroller to easily navigate the occasional steps and elevated walkways.

Early Morning / 1h

Marché de la Darse

Introduce your daughter to the vibrant sights and smells of a real Creole market, brimming with colorful tropical fruits, fresh vanilla, and sweet spices. Arrive early to easily navigate the market stalls along the harbor before the crowds and daytime heat pick up.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Plage du Souffleur

A gorgeous, expansive beach in Port-Louis with almost no waves, serving as a giant, natural, crystal-clear wading pool for infants. Shade trees line the back of the beach, providing plenty of cool spots to lay out a blanket for a baby nap.

Morning / 45m

Maison de la Forêt Boardwalk Trail

An ultra-short, flat wooden loop trail set deep within the lush Parc National de la Guadeloupe rainforest. It provides an immersive jungle experience with towering ferns and cool river breezes, perfectly sized for a carrier-bound infant or a toddler's short walk.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Mémorial ACTe Plaza

While the striking museum itself is a profound historical site, the massive, flat, paved waterfront plaza outside is a premier spot for a smooth stroller walk. Enjoy the sea breeze and the impressive architectural design of this iconic harbor landmark.

Afternoon / 2h

Plage de l'Autre Bord

A fantastic family beach offering a protected swimming area, a grassy shade park, and a playground right nearby for toddlers to burn off energy. It is exceptionally well-equipped with showers and flat paths, making post-swim clean-ups and transitions back to the car a breeze.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Karacoli

Dine right on the sand of Grand Anse beach under shady sea grape trees, where you can sip a Ti' Punch while your toddler plays in the sand next to your table. Order the fresh grilled lobster or local fish tartare, and enjoy the incredibly relaxed, spacious outdoor setting.

La Route du Rhum

Located on the flat, stroller-friendly Saint-François marina, this classic French-Creole bistro offers spacious outdoor patio seating and fast service. It is excellent for an early dinner of Mahi-Mahi with passion fruit sauce while watching the yachts glide in.

Le Ti-Saka

This casual, open-air spot in Le Gosier offers incredibly welcoming service, great high chairs, and flavorful Creole classics. The lively but relaxed ambient noise easily drowns out any toddler fussiness while you enjoy their famous cod accras.

Le Spot

An upscale yet highly casual beachside venue in Sainte-Anne, perfect for grabbing fresh tropical fruit juices and light bites right off the sand. The open-air, breezy layout means you can keep your sleeping baby in the stroller right next to your table.

L'Orlando

A reliable, oceanfront Italian-Creole restaurant in Le Gosier that opens early and serves exceptional wood-fired pizzas and fresh pastas. This is a perfect dining option if your toddler needs familiar, high-quality comfort food in a spacious, breezy setting.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Club Med La Caravelle

The gold standard for families in Guadeloupe, featuring an exceptional 'Baby Club Med' program, dedicated splash pools, and baby-welcoming amenities like cribs, bottle warmers, and strollers. It sits directly on the calmest, shallowest beach on the island, meaning you can walk straight from your room to safe, toddler-friendly waters.

La Toubana Hotel & Spa

For a more upscale, private experience, this luxury resort offers spacious, multi-bedroom villas equipped with full kitchens, allowing you to easily prepare infant meals and milk. The property features a calm, private beach cove sheltered from heavy waves and a gorgeous infinity pool with sweeping views.

Langley Resort Fort Royal

Offering spacious beachfront bungalows where you can step right onto the sand, this resort provides a relaxed, spacious atmosphere ideal for families. It features a great children's wading pool, baby cot rentals, and expansive grassy lawns where your toddler can safely crawl and play.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car directly at PTP airport and pre-book a high-quality car seat, as public transit is virtually non-existent for tourists and taxis with car seats are rare. Stop at a 'Carrefour' or 'Super U' supermarket near Pointe-à-Pitre on day one; they are fully stocked with premium French baby formula, diapers, and organic purées. Pack a high-quality baby carrier alongside your stroller, as the rainforest trails of Basse-Terre and the bustling markets have uneven surfaces and high curbs.
