Guadeloupe (Pointe-à-Pitre)

← back to travel board
Guadeloupe offers a mesmerizing blend of lush rainforests, white-sand beaches, and rich cultural history, all wrapped in a warm French-Creole embrace. Pointe-à-Pitre serves as your bustling gateway to vibrant spice markets, gentle turquoise lagoons, and easily accessible natural wonders. It is an ideal, stress-free tropical playground where families can slow down and explore at their own pace.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min by rental car or pre-booked taxi from Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP) to the family-friendly beach hotels in Le Gosier.

What to do

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Plage de la Datcha

A stunning beach with calm, shallow waters and a view of the islet, perfect for evening swimming under the floodlights. Toddlers will love splashing safely in the gentle, wave-free shallows.

Morning / 1-2h

Marché de la Darse

Experience a sensory explosion at this bustling waterfront market filled with colorful tropical fruits, fresh fish, and aromatic spices. Toddlers will be captivated by the sights and the sweet taste of fresh sugarcane juice.

Morning / 2-3h

Zoo de Guadeloupe au Parc des Mamelles

Walk through the canopy on wooden suspension bridges and spot jaguars, iguanas, and monkeys in a lush, natural rainforest setting. The paved lower pathways are easy for stroller navigation and sturdy toddlers.

Morning / 2h

Jardin Botanique de Deshaies

Wander through beautifully landscaped pathways filled with vibrant orchids, giant lily pads, and a feeding station for colorful lorikeets. Toddlers will love feeding the hungry koi fish in the main pond.

Midday / 1.5h

Aquarium de la Guadeloupe

An air-conditioned haven featuring local marine life, reef sharks, and sea turtles native to the Caribbean. It is the perfect size for toddlers to walk through without getting tired or overheated.

Morning / 2h

Mémorial ACTe

This architecturally stunning museum dedicated to the history of the slave trade offers an eye-opening cultural experience. While the exhibition is heavy, the outdoor waterfront promenade is wide and perfect for pushing a stroller.

Morning / 3h

Plage de Bois Jolan

Famous for its incredibly shallow, warm turquoise waters protected by a reef, this beach feels like a giant natural swimming pool. It is arguably the best beach on the island for toddlers to wade safely.

Morning / 1h

Cascade aux Écrevisses

A beautiful waterfall located along the Route de la Traversée with a very easy, stroller-accessible paved pathway leading right to the viewing platform. Toddlers can safely dip their toes in the cool, shallow stream nearby.

Morning to Afternoon / 3-4h

Îlet du Gosier

Take a quick 5-minute local boat ride to this tiny, uninhabited island ringed by pristine white sand and shallow reefs. Toddlers can build sandcastles in the shade of the coconut palms while watching the lighthouse.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Karacoli

Get the traditional crispy Accras de Morue (cod fritters) and fresh passion fruit juice enjoyed directly on the sand.

Le Zawag

Get the signature fresh grilled local lobster served right next to the ocean waves at this scenic spot.

An Chon Sa

Get the classic, mild chicken colombo with coconut rice and fried sweet plantains that kids will love.

Les Délices de l'Anse

Get the grilled catch of the day with sweet potato puree followed by a scoop of artisanal coconut sorbet.

La Rhumerie du Pirate

Get the local Creole tapas platter and a refreshing virgin mojito for the kids while enjoying dramatic cliffside ocean views.
