Valle de Guadalupe

← back to travel board
Nestled amid rolling hills and olive groves, Valle de Guadalupe offers a rustic escape where parents can savor a world-class culinary scene while children explore wide-open spaces. From petting zoos at boutique farms to wood-fired outdoor dining, this Baja oasis blends relaxed luxury with kid-friendly adventure. It is the ultimate laid-back destination for families seeking fresh air and fabulous food.
🚕 Getting there: ~90 min rental car or private shuttle from Tijuana International Airport (TIJ) to Valle de Guadalupe hotels

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Parque del Niño Jersey

A delightful children's park and petting zoo featuring local farm animals, sprawling playgrounds, and swimming pools. Toddlers will love feeding the friendly goats and riding the miniature train.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Mirador Cuatro Cuatros

A breathtaking clifftop lookout offering panoramic ocean views and comfortable outdoor lounge seating. Toddlers can safely watch the sailboats from the wide wooden decks while parents enjoy the coastal breeze.

Midday / 1-2h

Museo de la Vid y el Vino

An interactive museum detailing the history of winemaking in the region with colorful displays and outdoor gardens. The spacious, air-conditioned indoor galleries offer a cool, stroller-friendly respite from the afternoon sun.

Afternoon / 2h

Finca Altozano Farm and Gardens

A gorgeous open-air culinary ranch where kids can visit the resident giant pigs, climb up into oversized wine barrels, and run across the lawns. It is the ultimate stress-free spot for toddlers to explore while waiting for a farm-to-table lunch.

Morning / 2h

El Cielo Winery Resort & Gardens

A stunning estate featuring manicured paths, a scenic lake filled with eager ducks, and golf cart tours of the vineyards. Toddlers will be mesmerized by feeding the birds while parents enjoy the tranquil, stroller-friendly paths.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Ensenada Malecón and Ventana al Mar

A vibrant seaside boardwalk just a short drive from the valley, featuring a massive dancing fountain and harbor boat rides. Toddlers will love watching the sea lions splash in the harbor and running along the pedestrian-only promenade.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Monte Xanic Oasis and Lake

A historic winery set against a dramatic rock cliff with a peaceful lake home to elegant white swans. Toddlers can watch the swans glide by from the lakeside picnic areas while the family relaxes in the shade.

Morning / 2-3h

Rancho Los Bandidos

A friendly ranch offering gentle horseback riding tours through the scenic desert trails of the valley. While older kids ride, toddlers can safely pet the ranch's docile horses and enjoy the dusty trail atmosphere.

Morning / 1h

La Casa de Doña Lupe

An organic farm shop famous for its homemade jams, olive oils, sweet treats, and shaded patio. Toddlers will love sampling the natural fruit preserves and running around the rustic, unpretentious backyard garden.

🍽️ Where to eat

Finca Altozano

Get the wood-fired octopus or the local organic ribeye, followed by a scoop of artisanal ice cream for the kids.

Deckman's en el Mogor

Order the freshly shucked local oysters and the slow-roasted lamb, prepared entirely on open outdoor hearths.

La Cocina de Doña Esthela

Order the world-famous machaca con huevo (shredded beef with eggs) and freshly made flour tortillas hot off the griddle.

Troika

Try the gourmet pork belly sliders and hand-cut fries served in an ultra-relaxed, pond-side dirt lot where toddlers can play freely.

Ochentos Pizza

Order the artisanal wood-fired pepperoni pizza and let the kids burn off energy in the massive outdoor playground.

👨‍👩‍👧 Valle de Guadalupe's dusty, unpaved roads can be very bumpy, so opt for an all-terrain stroller or a sturdy baby carrier. Because most top attractions and restaurants are entirely outdoors, packing wide-brimmed hats, sunscreen, and layers for the cooler evenings is essential.
