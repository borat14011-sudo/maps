What to do

Morning 9-11:30am

Kastani Beach

Famous for its lush pine trees meeting the sand, this beach offers premium sun loungers with excellent umbrellas. The sand is soft for baby's feet, and the Kastani Beach Bar provides quiet morning service with easy stroller access via a wooden boardwalk.

Mid-day 11am-2pm

Panormos Beach

A highly sheltered bay with incredibly calm, pool-like waters perfect for dipping Mary Claire's toes. Luxurious sunbeds are serviced by nearby tavernas, and the flat beachside road allows for easy stroller navigation.

Late afternoon 6-8pm

Skopelos Town Promenade

This wide, flat, paved harborfront is ideal for an effortless sunset stroller walk. Will and Kathryn can admire the luxury yachts while enjoying a gentle sea breeze, completely free of stairs or steep inclines.

Morning 9-11:30am

Milia Beach

Widely considered the island's most beautiful beach, Milia offers natural shade from mature pine trees right on the shoreline. Arrive early to secure a premium sunbed in the quiet zone, far from any loud beach-bar music.

Early morning 8-10am

Agios Ioannis Chapel Viewpoint

While the 100+ cliffside stairs to the famous 'Mamma Mia' chapel are too steep with an infant, the sea-level cafe and cove below offer breathtaking, shaded views. Will and Kathryn can enjoy a frappe while Mary Claire rests in the carrier under the cliffs.

Morning 9-11:30am

Stafylos Beach

A stunning, wind-sheltered bay close to Skopelos Town. Access involves a short walk down, so leave the stroller at the top and use an infant carrier to reach the soft sand and calm, shallow swimming area.

Morning 10am-12:30pm

Limnonari Beach

A virtually wave-free lagoon with a sandy bottom and beautiful green-turquoise water. The ultra-calm conditions make it the safest beach on the island for floating with a baby in a UV-protected canopy float.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Monastery of Evangelistria

Perched high above Chora, this active historic monastery features a peaceful, shaded stone courtyard. It is easily accessible by car with only flat walking paths, offering a cool mountain breeze and quiet reflection time.

Evening 6-8pm

Agnontas Harbor

A quiet, picturesque fishing village centered around a sheltered harbor. The flat, paved waterfront is perfect for an evening stroller walk, shaded by massive plane trees right at the water's edge.

🍽️ Where to eat

Agnanti Restaurant

Savor upscale Greek cuisine like slow-cooked pork with local plums on a stunning cliffside terrace in Glossa. They offer early dinner seatings at 6:30 PM with highchairs and a quiet, romantic atmosphere before the main crowds arrive.

Adrina Tavern

Located beachside at the Adrina Beach Hotel, this open-air luxury taverna is famous for its fresh lobster spaghetti. The staff is exceptionally welcoming to infants, the tables are stroller-accessible, and the gentle waves provide soothing white noise.

Ta Kymata (The Muses)

A historic family-run tavern right on the quiet edge of Skopelos harbor. Will and Kathryn can enjoy the legendary Skopelos goat cheese pie at a water-facing table with plenty of space to park a stroller next to the table.

Korali Seafood Restaurant

An elegant seafood spot located directly on the beach at Agnontas. Mary Claire can sleep in her stroller on the flat wooden deck while her parents enjoy impeccably grilled, locally caught sea bream and octopus.

Olivo Restaurant

A refined Mediterranean garden restaurant hidden in Skopelos Town. The spacious outdoor courtyard is quiet and shaded, offering an early 7:00 PM seating for enjoying gourmet truffle pasta in a sophisticated, baby-friendly garden setting.
