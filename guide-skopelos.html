Skopelos

← back to travel board
Skopelos blends dramatic, pine-meets-sea landscapes with a relaxed, uncommercialized Greek island charm that feels instantly welcoming to young families. Unlike busier Cycladic islands, the pace here is slow, the locals genuinely dote on babies, and the fragrant forest air makes for restorative toddler naps. With calm, sheltered bays and flat harborfront strolls, it offers the perfect balance of rugged Mediterranean beauty and practical conveniences for parents.
🚕 Getting there: No direct flights exist; fly into Skiathos (JSI), take a 5-minute taxi to the port, board a 45-minute ferry to Skopelos Chora, and then take a 15-minute taxi or rental car to your resort.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Kastani Beach

This iconic beach features a manicured lawn area behind the sunbeds that is perfect for setting up a baby play pen or pop-up sun tent. The main path from the parking lot is relatively flat, making the short walk with a stroller and beach gear highly manageable.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Agnontas Beach

A highly sheltered harbor where the water is famously calm like a swimming pool, making it ideal for dipping toddler toes safely. Parking is located just yards from the shoreline, eliminating long, hot walks with heavy baby bags.

Evening / 1-2h

Skopelos Chora Waterfront Promenade

This wide, flat paved harbor path is completely stroller-friendly and lined with shade-giving mulberry trees. It is the ultimate spot for an effortless sunset stroll while your little one naps in the carriage.

Morning / 3-4h

Limnonari Beach

One of the few beaches on the island with a sandy, shallow entry, making it exceptionally safe for toddlers to splash safely. The nearby beach taverns offer shaded grassy patches and clean changing facilities for families.

Midday / 1h

Antoniou Family Olive Press

Located in Glossa, this air-conditioned, family-run mill museum offers a fascinating, sensory-rich break from the midday sun. The indoor layout is flat and easy to navigate with a baby carrier while exploring the historic olive oil production gear.

Afternoon / 3h

Panormos Beach

A massive, pebbled bay protected from the winds where you can rent comfortable sunbeds steps away from excellent tavernas. The flat access from the coastal road means you can easily wheel a stroller right up to the edge of the beach dining tables.

Early Morning / 1.5h

Agios Ioannis Kastri

While the 100+ cliffside steps are too steep and narrow for a toddler, the dramatic seaside base offers stunning views and a small, breezy café. Strap your daughter into a secure front-carrier if you plan to make the ascent, or take turns climbing while one parent relaxes below.

Afternoon / 3h

Milian Beach

Famous for its stunning pine trees that grow right down to the shoreline, providing natural afternoon shade for sensitive baby skin. The northern side of the beach is quieter, offering a peaceful, low-noise environment for an outdoor stroller nap.

Evening / 1h

Panagitsa tou Pyrgou

Perched at the edge of Skopelos port, this iconic white church offers panoramic sea views without a grueling drive. While there are some steps to climb, the path is wide enough to safely carry a toddler to watch the ferries glide in.

🍽️ Where to eat

Agnanti Restaurant

Order the slow-cooked lamb or the traditional Skopelos goat cheese pie with honey. The terraced seating in Glossa offers breathtaking sunset views, and the staff is incredibly accommodating, gladly pureeing vegetables or providing high chairs for early diners.

Korali Fish Tavern

Order the exceptionally fresh grilled red mullet or lobster pasta right on the edge of Agnontas bay. The tables are set directly on the pebbles, allowing toddlers to play safely in the stones right next to your dining table.

Klimataria Restaurant

Try the traditional slow-baked pork with plums, a local Skopelos specialty. Located in a spacious, leafy courtyard in Chora, the ambient rustle of grapevines easily masks any toddler fussiness during the early evening hours.

Michalis Pie Shop

Grab the iconic spiral-shaped, deep-fried Skopelos cheese pie hot out of the pan. It is the perfect, mess-free finger food for a toddler snack while sitting at their casual outdoor, harbor-facing tables.

The Muses (Ta Kymata)

Order the fresh catch of the day or the stuffed zucchini blossoms. Positioned on the flat, breezy edge of the Chora port, it features plenty of space between outdoor tables to park a stroller without disrupting other diners.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Adrina Resort & Spa

This luxury resort features a funicular lift that effortlessly transports strollers down the steep hillside to the private beach and saltwater pool. They provide high-end wooden cribs, baby-proofing options, and have excellent on-site family dining.

Skopelos Village Hotel

Located on a flat, stroller-friendly path just a 10-minute walk from Chora, this hotel offers spacious multi-room suites with kitchenettes for easy baby formula prep. The lush lawns and twin swimming pools provide plenty of safe outdoor space for a toddler to run around.

Blue Green Bay

Nestled right on the calm shores of Panormos, this hotel offers direct beach access without any stairs or roads to cross. The family apartments feature convenient kitchenettes, and the shaded pool area is highly secure for young children.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a mid-size SUV rather than a compact car, as the winding pine-forested roads to northern beaches require a sturdy vehicle with enough trunk space for a heavy-duty stroller. Stock up on diapers, organic baby food pouches, and formula at the larger supermarkets like AB Vassilopoulos or Sklavenitis located just outside Skopelos Chora, as smaller village mini-markets have limited baby inventories. Because many scenic spots involve steps or historic cobblestones, bring a high-quality, breathable infant carrier alongside your stroller to navigate the winding alleyways of Chora and Glossa comfortably.
