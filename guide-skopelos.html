Skopelos

← back to travel board
Cloaked in emerald pine forests that spill into sapphire waters, Skopelos offers a tranquil Greek island escape perfect for families. Meander through the whitewashed, car-free alleys of Skopelos Town where bougainvillea cascades over quiet plazas before spending sunny afternoons on calm, shallow shores. It is a playground of natural beauty where a slow-paced lifestyle makes traveling with little ones effortless.
🚕 Getting there: Fly to Skiathos (JSI), take a ~30-45 min ferry to Skopelos Port, then a ~10-25 min taxi to town or beach hotels

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1h

Panagitsa of Pyrgos

Climb the gentle, wide stone steps to this iconic whitewashed church guarding the harbor entrance for breezy sea views. Toddlers will love watching the ferries bobbing in the water from the safe, walled lookout plaza.

Morning / 3-4h

Kastani Beach

Relax on the soft sands of this famous pine-fringed bay where the waters are exceptionally calm and clear. The grassy lawn near the beach bar is perfect for toddlers to toddle safely away from the shoreline.

Morning / 2-3h

Stafilos Beach

A sheltered bay close to town featuring shallow waters and a mix of sand and shingle that is great for paddling. Keep in mind there are wooden stairs to access the beach, so opt for a baby carrier over a stroller.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Skopelos Town Alley Wandering

Explore the car-free, maze-like streets of the main Chora filled with colorful doors, sleeping cats, and tiny squares. It is incredibly safe for toddlers to explore on foot, though a carrier is best for navigating occasional steep steps.

Morning / 3h

Milia Beach

This gorgeous stretch of coast features tiny pebbles, crystalline water, and plenty of natural shade from the overhanging pine trees. The right side of the beach is exceptionally quiet and has shallower entry points ideal for small children.

Morning / 2h

Agios Ioannis Chapel

Behold the spectacular church perched atop a sea rock made famous by the movie Mamma Mia. While the steep 100-step climb is not toddler-friendly, the small, sheltered beach at the base is perfect for a quiet splash in the shallows.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Antoniou Family Olive Oil Press

Learn about the island's liquid gold at this family-run press where kids can see traditional and modern olive oil production. Toddlers will enjoy tasting the fresh bread dipped in mild, kid-friendly olive oil varieties.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Glossa Village Sunset Stroll

Visit the mountain village known as the "balcony of Skopelos" to experience authentic island life and stunning Aegean sunsets. Let toddlers spot goats on the distant hillsides and enjoy a slow-paced stroll through traditional stone-paved streets.

Afternoon / 1h

The Vakratsa House Museum

Step back in time at this traditional 18th-century mansion featuring historical costumes, old toys, and local artifacts. The shaded, quiet courtyard offers a peaceful retreat where toddlers can rest away from the midday sun.

🍽️ Where to eat

Agnanti Restaurant

Order the traditional Skopelitiki cheese pie made with flaky twisted pastry and local goat cheese while enjoying panoramic sea views.

Klimataria Restaurant

Try the slow-cooked pork with plums, a traditional Skopelos specialty, in a lovely harbor-front setting shaded by a massive plane tree.

Nostos Taverna

Feast on fresh grilled sea bass and Greek salad right on the pebbles of Panormos beach where kids can play in the sand near your table.

Michalis Pie Shop

Grab a warm, freshly baked traditional twisted cheese pie or sweet bougatsa custard pie for an easy, toddler-approved breakfast on the go.

Perivoli Restaurant

Dine on upscale Mediterranean dishes like slow-roasted lamb in a beautiful, secluded orchard garden filled with pomegranate and lemon trees.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a sturdy baby carrier rather than a stroller, as the island's charming villages and beach paths are dominated by cobblestones and steep steps. Always pack water shoes for the whole family, as many of the most beautiful and pristine beaches feature pebbles rather than sand.
