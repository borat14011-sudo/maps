Lake Constance (Bodensee) Region

← back to travel board
Nestled where Germany, Austria, and Switzerland meet, Lake Constance offers a fairytale backdrop of sparkling alpine waters, flower-filled islands, and medieval castles. Perfect for families, the region combines gentle lakeside promenades with thrilling mountain cable cars and open-air museums. Here, days are filled with steam boat rides, fresh lake breeze, and easy cycling paths designed for little legs.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min scenic train ride or taxi from Friedrichshafen Airport (FDH) to Lindau island hotels, or ~1 hour drive from Zurich Airport (ZRH) to Konstanz.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Mainau Island

A pedestrian-only paradise of massive floral sculptures, a butterfly house, and an incredible adventure playground. Toddlers will love the petting zoo and water play areas.

Morning / 2-3h

Pfänder Cable Car

Ride the panoramic cable car to the mountain peak for sweeping three-country views and an alpine wildlife park. The path is stroller-friendly and features deer and ibex.

Afternoon / 2h

Lake Dwelling Museum Unteruhldingen

Explore reconstructed Stone and Bronze Age stilt houses suspended right over the water. It is an interactive walk through history with wide wooden boardwalks perfect for strollers.

Morning / 2h

Affenberg Salem

Walk through a forested sanctuary where over 200 Barbary macaques roam completely free. Toddlers will be mesmerized watching the monkeys eat popcorn right from visitors' hands.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Lindau Harbor and Lighthouse

Stroll the iconic harbor entrance flanked by a Bavarian Lion statue and a historic lighthouse. Little ones can watch the giant white ferries dock while enjoying a lakeside gelato.

Morning / 2-3h

Rhine Falls

Feel the mist of Europe's largest waterfall on an exciting but safe family boat tour. The paved viewing platforms are easily accessible for strollers to see the thundering water close-up.

Morning / 1-2h

Konstanz Market Hall

Sample fresh local berries, freshly baked soft pretzels, and regional cheeses at this vibrant open-air market. It is highly spacious and easy to navigate with a double stroller.

Full Day / 5-6h

Ravensburger Spieleland

A massive theme park dedicated to the beloved Ravensburger games, featuring gentle rides and giant play zones. Toddlers have their own dedicated soft-play areas and tractor rides.

Afternoon / 2h

Meersburg Castle

Step into Germany's oldest inhabited castle with medieval knights' halls, dungeons, and armor displays. Keep toddlers engaged in the grassy castle gardens with panoramic lake views.

🍽️ Where to eat

Gasthof Zum Becher

Try the traditional Kässpätzle (cheesy German noodles) which is an absolute hit with toddlers.

Wirtshaus zum Gucken

Order the classic, crispy Wiener Schnitzel served with cranberry sauce, perfect for sharing with little ones.

Seekuh

A laid-back, highly kid-friendly spot known for its legendary homemade burgers and organic sweet potato fries.

Restaurant Hafen

Enjoy fresh-caught local whitefish (Felchen) overlooking the harbor while kids devour their specialized children's fish fingers.

Seegarten

Grab a table by the water and order the wood-fired pizzas while the kids play on the adjacent lakeside lawn.

👨‍👩‍👧 Purchase the Bodensee Card Plus for all-inclusive access to ferries and key attractions, saving time and money at ticketing lines. Pack a lightweight stroller along with a carrier, as some cobblestone old towns and boat ramps can be bumpy but are overall highly accessible.
