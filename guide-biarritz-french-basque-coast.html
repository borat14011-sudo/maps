What to do

Early morning 9-10am

Rocher de la Vierge

Iconic rock formation with a footbridge and statue; views of the coastline. Stroller-accessible boardwalk leading to the viewpoint. Calm morning light, minimal crowds, good for babywearing.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Côte des Basques Beach

Famous surf beach with gentle waves at the southern end. Shallow tidal pools for splashing. Walk the promenade with a stroller; find shade under the cliff. Avoid crowded summer weekends.

Midday 11am-1pm

Aquarium de Biarritz (Musée de la Mer)

Stroller-friendly museum with shark tunnel and touch pools. Cool indoor space for hot days. Baby-changing facilities and quiet corners for nursing.

Morning 10-11am

Phare de Biarritz

Historic lighthouse with panoramic views (skip the climb with infant; enjoy the park at base). Grassy area for picnic and crawling. Stroller-friendly paths.

Morning 9-11am

Plage de la Milady

Family-friendly beach with calm, protected waters and a playground nearby. Sand is soft for baby play. Rent a parasol and chairs. Easier parking than central beaches.

Late afternoon 5-6pm

Esplanade du Rocher de la Vierge

Extends from the rock, offering a flat, stroller-wide path along the cliff. Benches for nursing breaks. Watch fishermen and boats. Sunset brings golden light for photos.

2-3 hours, late morning start

Bayonne Old Town (Petit Bayonne)

Charming medieval streets, but cobblestones require a sturdy stroller. Visit the Cathédrale Sainte-Marie (quiet interior for cooling down). Try a Basque cake at a pâtisserie.

Morning 9am-12pm

Saint-Jean-de-Luz Grand Plage

Picturesque bay with very calm, shallow water perfect for babies. Stroller-friendly boardwalk along the beach. Historic fishing port nearby for a scenic walk.

2 hours, mid-morning

Château d'Abbadia

Neo-Gothic castle overlooking the ocean. The park has gentle trails (stroller-accessible, some gravel). Quiet, uncrowded, and shaded. Book a guided tour—skip if baby is fussy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Café du Port

Harbour-side brasserie serving fresh seafood and Basque pintxos. Highchairs available, outdoor terrace with shade, early dinner seating (6pm). Noise level moderate; best for calm babies.

Les Halles de Biarritz (Biarritz Market)

Indoor market with food stalls—great for a casual lunch. Grab a table at the central bar for oysters and grilled fish. Stroller-friendly aisles, noisy but lively; good for baby-wearing.

Le Bistrot d'Hélène

Family-run bistro serving traditional Basque dishes like axoa de veau. Offers early seating (6:30pm), high chairs, and a quiet side room. Book ahead for a table near the window.

L'Imprimerie

Stylish restaurant in a former print shop, known for truffle dishes and local wines. Quiet atmosphere, no loud music. Infants welcome; request a booth for extra space.

Chez Albert

Long-established fish restaurant on the port. Signature bouillabaisse and grilled squid. Terrace seating, highchairs, early dinner (7pm). Accepts babies; avoid peak 8pm rush.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Hotel Regina Biarritz

$200-400/night

Belle Époque five-star with a heated outdoor pool and garden. Family suites available (connecting rooms), cribs on request, blackout curtains, and a calm lounge for feeding. Short walk to Grande Plage.

Sofitel Biarritz Le Miramar Thalassa Sea & Spa

$200-400/night

Luxury thalassotherapy hotel with direct beach access and an indoor/outdoor pool. Spacious family rooms with sofa beds, crib provided, blackout curtains, and a kids' club (for older ones). Quiet atmosphere.

Hotel Le Saint-Julian

$200-400/night

Boutique five-star in a restored 19th-century building with a small heated pool and lush garden. Family suites with kitchenette, crib available, and garden terrace for private relaxation. Near city center.
