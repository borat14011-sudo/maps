Biarritz & French Basque Coast

← back to travel board
Where the Pyrenees plunge into the wild Atlantic, the French Basque Coast offers elegant Belle Époque architecture alongside a laid-back surf culture. Families will find sheltered sandy coves, legendary coastal walks, and vibrant markets brimming with local treats like Gâteau Basque. It is a coastal playground where rugged nature meets effortless French sophistication, perfect for all generations.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi or Line 36 bus from Biarritz Pays Basque Airport (BIQ) to downtown Biarritz hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Grande Plage

Biarritz's iconic main beach features golden sands, striped beach tents, and plenty of space to build sandcastles. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow tide pools during low tide, though watch out for strong Atlantic waves on windy days.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Rocher de la Vierge

Walk across a metal footbridge designed by Gustave Eiffel to reach this dramatic rock formation with sweeping coastal views. It is stroller-accessible, making it an easy and breezy coastal walk for little legs.

Morning / 1-2h

Les Halles de Biarritz

This bustling covered market is a feast for the senses, packed with local cheeses, cured meats, and fresh pastries. Grab a slice of cherry-filled Gâteau Basque for the kids while you sample local tapas at the counters.

Afternoon / 2h

Aquarium de Biarritz

Housed in a beautiful Art Deco building, this aquarium features a shark cave, a seal feeding show, and a hands-on touch pool. It's the ultimate rainy-day activity and is the perfect, manageable size for toddlers.

Morning / 1h

Phare de Biarritz

Take a scenic walk up to the lighthouse's grassy cape, which offers panoramic views of the entire sandy coast. While older kids can climb the 248 steps, toddlers can safely run around the grassy headland below.

Afternoon / 2h

Plage de la Côte des Basques

Famous as the birthplace of European surfing, this stunning beach is framed by dramatic cliffs and offers great tide-watching. Keep toddlers safely on the paved promenade at high tide when the sand completely disappears.

Morning / 2h

Sentier du Littoral in Bidart

A spectacular coastal path stretching down the Basque coast with dramatic cliffside views and wild beaches. The paved sections near Bidart are ideal for a scenic, breezy stroller walk with ocean views.

Morning / 3h

Le Train de la Rhune

Ride a vintage 1924 wooden rack railway up to the summit of the Pyrenees for breathtaking 360-degree views of the coast. Toddlers will be enchanted by the slow-moving train and the wild Pottok ponies roaming the mountain slopes.

Morning / 3-4h

Plage de Socoa in Saint-Jean-de-Luz

This sheltered, sandy bay is protected by giant stone sea walls, making it the calmest swimming spot on the Basque coast. It is the absolute best beach for toddlers to paddle safely without the heavy Atlantic surf.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Albert

Get the incredibly fresh, locally caught sea bass grilled whole and served in the lively historic Port des Pêcheurs.

Le Surfing

Order the gourmet burgers and sweet potato fries at this laid-back, highly family-friendly spot right on the Côte des Basques beach.

Maison Adam

Indulge in their legendary traditional Basque macarons, which have been made using a secret family recipe since 1660.

Bar Jean

Enjoy a vibrant, casual lunch of pintxos (Basque tapas) like tortilla, croquetas, and local ham right next to the market.

Bali Bowls

Grab refreshing, kid-approved organic acai bowls, cold-pressed juices, and vegan treats for a healthy snack near the beach.

👨‍👩‍👧 The Atlantic waves and tides on the Basque coast are very powerful, so always stick to designated lifeguarded zones marked by blue flags when swimming with kids. For easy transit with a stroller, utilize the free electric shuttle buses (Navette Gratuite) that loop around Biarritz's center and beaches.
