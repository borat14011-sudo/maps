Biarritz & French Basque Coast

← back to travel board
Where rugged Pyrenees cliffs crash into the sparkling Atlantic, the French Basque Coast offers a playground of sandy coves and elegant seaside towns. Biarritz blends Belle Époque grandeur with a laid-back surf vibe that keeps both toddlers and parents enchanted. From tasting sweet gateau Basque at local markets to exploring dramatic ocean tidepools, this coastal haven is built for easy, memorable family adventures.
🚕 Getting there: ~10 min taxi or Line 3 local bus from Biarritz Pays Basque Airport (BIQ) directly to downtown beach hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Grande Plage

The main sweep of golden sand in Biarritz is perfect for building epic sandcastles and watching colorful surf schools. Toddlers will love splashing in the shallow tidal pools left behind at low tide.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Rocher de la Vierge

Walk across a dramatic metal footbridge designed by Gustave Eiffel to reach this iconic statue on a rocky outcrop. Strollers fit easily on the bridge, but hold toddlers' hands tightly as the sea breeze can get quite gusty.

Morning / 2h

Aquarium de Biarritz

An Art Deco masterpiece housing over 150 species of marine life, featuring a touch pool and a giant shark tank. Toddlers will be absolutely mesmerized by the daily seal feeding sessions on the open-air terrace.

Morning / 1-2h

Les Halles de Biarritz

A bustling indoor market packed with vibrant stalls offering local cheeses, cured meats, and fresh pastries. Go early to beat the crowds, grab a slice of local cake, and let your toddler admire the colorful fish displays.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Plage de Port Vieux

A tiny, horseshoe-shaped rocky cove that is heavily sheltered from the Atlantic's notorious waves and winds. This is the absolute safest beach in town for toddlers to swim and paddle without any surf anxiety.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Phare de Biarritz

This historic 19th-century white lighthouse stands tall on a cliff overlooking the entire Basque coast. While the climb up is too steep for toddlers, the surrounding grassy lawns are perfect for them to run around freely while parents enjoy the panoramic ocean views.

Afternoon / 2h

La Cité de l'Océan

An interactive science museum dedicated to ocean preservation with 3D virtual reality surf experiences and dynamic special effects. While older kids dive into the tech, toddlers can enjoy the sensory play areas and rolling down the grassy, wave-shaped roof outside.

Morning / 45 mins

Petit Train de Biarritz

An open-air tourist road train that winds along the scenic coastline and through the grand imperial streets of the town. It is a fantastic, low-effort way to sightsee when little legs are completely exhausted.

Afternoon / 3h

Plage de l'Uhabia in Bidart

A short drive south brings you to this wide, sandy river-mouth beach with a lovely wooden playground right on the sand. Toddlers will love playing in the calm river stream that flows gently into the ocean.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Albert

Located right in the picturesque Port des Pêcheurs, order the fresh, wild-caught grilled sea bass to share with a side of crispy French frites.

Maison Miremont

Step into this historic, elegant 1872 tea salon to enjoy a traditional cherry-filled Gateau Basque alongside a rich, velvety hot chocolate.

Le Crabe Tambour

A cozy, family-friendly seafood tavern serving excellent plancha-grilled fish and simple, delicious buttered pasta for the little ones.

Bali Bowls

Perfect for a quick, healthy breakfast or snack, grab a colorful, vitamin-packed dragon fruit acai bowl topped with fresh banana slices that toddlers will devour.

Beach House (Anglet)

Located just north of Biarritz, this ultra-relaxed venue offers a poolside garden terrace, delicious fish tacos, and a dedicated, kid-approved menu.

👨‍👩‍👧 Stick to the sheltered waters of Port Vieux for stress-free toddler swimming, as the main surf beaches have notoriously strong currents. Always keep an all-terrain stroller handy for the hilly coastal paths, but be prepared to fold it for the steps leading down to most beaches.
