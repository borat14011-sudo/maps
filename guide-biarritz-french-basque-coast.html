What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Grande Plage Promenade

This paved walkway offers sweeping views of the ocean and easy navigation for a premium stroller. Early morning strolls are peaceful and provide plenty of fresh sea air for baby Mary Claire.

Late morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Rocher de la Vierge

Walk across the footbridge designed by Gustave Eiffel for dramatic coastal vistas. The path is stroller-friendly, though it is best to visit during low-wind mornings to keep baby comfortable.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Aquarium de Biarritz

This historic Art Deco aquarium features mesmerizing seal feedings and a giant shark tank. The indoor, air-conditioned galleries provide a sensory-rich, shaded escape that is perfect for an infant.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Port Vieux Beach

This sheltered, horseshoe-shaped cove is naturally protected from strong winds and heavy waves. It is the safest spot on the coast for dipping baby Mary Claire's toes into the calm, shallow waters.

Late afternoon 4-6pm / 2h

Phare de Biarritz

The grassy gardens surrounding this historic lighthouse offer the perfect vantage point for a luxury family picnic. While the tower stairs are not suitable for a baby, the flat surrounding park is highly stroller-accessible.

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Les Halles de Biarritz

Sample local Basque specialties at this vibrant covered market. Navigating the wide aisles is easiest during the quieter early morning hours before the midday crowds arrive.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Plage de la Milady

Located on the southern edge of town, this beach features a well-maintained seaside playground and a wide promenade. It is a fantastic spot to watch the surfers while pushing a stroller along the paved paths.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Sentier du Littoral Bidart

This scenic coastal path offers breathtaking views of the Pyrenees meeting the sea. The paved portion starting near Bidart's central beach is smooth enough for a robust stroller and offers plenty of benches for rest.

Midday 12-2pm / 2h

Jardin Public de Biarritz

Located directly opposite the Gare du Midi, this elegant public park features lush green lawns and shade-giving trees. It is an ideal, quiet spot to escape the midday heat and let baby take a peaceful nap in her stroller.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chez Albert

Located in the historic Port des Pêcheurs, this iconic spot serves wild-caught turbot and fresh oysters. The spacious outdoor harbor-side terrace easily accommodates strollers and offers a lively yet family-friendly atmosphere.

IQORI

Situated inside the Regina hotel, this fine-dining venue offers Basque-inspired dishes like line-caught hake. Their outdoor terrace overlooking the lighthouse is quiet, spacious, and highly welcoming to families with infants.

Miremont Pâtisserie

This historic tea salon serves exquisite pastries and light lunches with stunning views of the Grande Plage. Request a window table during the early lunch service to enjoy a relaxed experience with baby Mary Claire.

Le Carlos

Overlooking the Côte des Basques, this chic beachfront establishment is perfect for sunset tapas and mocktails. The outdoor lounge area is casual, spacious, and features a relaxed vibe that easily absorbs any baby noise.

Le Sissinou

A highly-rated bistro known for its refined presentation of local ingredients. Booking an early evening seating ensures attentive service, highchair availability, and a quieter dining room for a peaceful family meal.
