Where modern skyline meets wild rainforest adventures

Panama City

← back to travel board
Panama City pulses with a unique energy where shimmering skyscrapers edge against lush, monkey-filled jungles and a historic colonial heart. It is an unexpectedly accessible tropical playground where families can watch massive ships transit the canal in the morning and bike along a breezy Pacific causeway by afternoon.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Miraflores Locks (Panama Canal)

Watch giant container ships rise and fall in the historic locks from the interactive visitor center. Toddlers will love the 3D IMAX movie and waving to the captains from the observation deck.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Amador Causeway

Rent a multi-pedal family surrey bike to cruise this scenic pathway linking four small islands with incredible skyline views. It is flat and paved, making it ideal for stroller pushing or letting toddlers stretch their legs near the playgrounds.

Early Morning / 2h

Metropolitan Natural Park

Hike the easy trails of this lush tropical rainforest located right within the city limits to spot wild sloths, monkeys, and colorful toucans. The Momides trail is short and flat enough for tiny, wandering toddler feet.

Midday / 2h

BioMuseo

Designed by Frank Gehry, this world-class, multi-colored museum tells the story of how the Isthmus of Panama changed the planet's biodiversity. The giant indoor aquarium screens and hands-on exhibits are highly engaging for toddlers.

Late Afternoon / 3h

Casco Viejo

Wander the vibrant, colonial cobblestone streets lined with colorful ruins, plazas, and street performers. Stop at Plaza Herrera where toddlers can run safely in the car-free square.

Morning / 1-2h

Punta Culebra Nature Center

Run by the Smithsonian, this open-air museum features touch tanks with starfish, a turtle pool, and a forest trail where wild sloths hang low in the trees. Toddlers will be thrilled by the up-close marine life encounters.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Mercado de Mariscos

Experience the bustling local culture at this lively market filled with fresh catches, energetic music, and vendors selling cheap ceviche. While loud and busy, toddlers will be fascinated by the giant crabs and colorful fish on ice.

Morning / 3h

Summit Municipal Park

Located just outside the city, this peaceful park features a botanical garden and a rescue sanctuary home to the majestic Harpy Eagle. It has wide, grassy lawns perfect for a family picnic and toddler-friendly playgrounds.

Morning / 1-2h

Mi Pueblito

Explore replica villages representing Panama’s Afro-Antillean, indigenous, and colonial heritages nestled at the foot of Ancon Hill. It is a quiet, vehicle-free space where toddlers can safely toddle around the colorful wooden houses.

Where to eat

  • El Trapiche — This legendary, family-friendly spot serves authentic Panamanian dishes like sancocho chicken soup and carimañolas yuca fritters that kids love to eat with their hands.
  • Mercado de Mariscos Ceviche — Grab a cup of fresh, tangy sea bass or shrimp ceviche served with saltine crackers, which is cheap, delicious, and easy to eat on the go.
  • Palettas (Casco Viejo) — Cool off with these artisanal gourmet ice pops packed with fresh tropical fruits like passionfruit and mango, with mini-sized options perfect for small hands.
  • Diablicos — Enjoy traditional Panamanian cuisine accompanied by high-energy folkloric dance performances featuring the famous devil masks that will mesmerize older kids.
