Sun-drenched Caribbean adventures for the whole family

St. Thomas

← back to travel board
St. Thomas welcomes families with its dramatic emerald hillsides, sparkling turquoise waters, and vibrant colonial charm. From the gentle, pool-like waves of Magens Bay to the bustling streets of Charlotte Amalie, this island paradise blends laid-back relaxation with exciting tropical discoveries. It is the ultimate hassle-free Caribbean escape where toddlers can splash safely and parents can truly unwind.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Magens Bay Beach

This world-famous bay offers incredibly calm, pool-like waters and soft white sand, making it the ultimate safe haven for swimming. Toddlers will love splashing in the virtually wave-free shallows while parents relax under the shade of towering coconut palms.

Morning / 2-3h

Coral World Ocean Park

Get up close with sea turtles, sharks, and sea lions in this beautifully maintained marine park next to Coki Beach. The interactive touch pools are at the perfect height for curious toddlers to gently touch sea stars and sea cucumbers.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Phantaseas Tropical Botanical Garden

A lush, shaded oasis filled with blooming orchids, towering palms, and colorful resident parrots. The gravel paths are mostly stroller-accessible, offering a peaceful, cool escape from the midday sun with plenty of places to sit.

Morning / 30m

Drake's Seat

This historic lookout offers breathtaking, panoramic views over Magens Bay and the surrounding green hills. It's a quick, easy roadside photo stop where kids can spot sailboats in the harbor without any long walking required.

Morning / 1h

Charlotte Amalie Market Square

Immerse your family in local culture at this historic bustling marketplace filled with vendors selling fresh tropical fruits, spices, and handmade crafts. Let your little ones try a sweet slice of local mango or fresh coconut jelly.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Skyride to Paradise Point

Ride a modern aerial tramway 700 feet above the cruise ship harbor for spectacular island vistas. The smooth, slow-moving cable cars are thrilling yet safe for toddlers, and there is delicious ice cream waiting at the top.

Morning / 2-3h

Coki Point Beach

Renowned for its crystal-clear water and abundance of friendly, colorful fish right near the shore. Buy some dog biscuits at the beach to let your toddler watch the yellowtail snapper swarm right around their feet in the shallows.

Morning / 4-5h

Honeymoon Beach on Water Island

A short, exciting 10-minute ferry ride from Crown Bay brings you to this tranquil beach where golf carts are the primary transport. The ultra-calm, shallow waters are ideal for toddler wading, and wild turtles often swim nearby.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Pirates Treasure Museum

A fun, air-conditioned museum showcasing shipwreck treasures through engaging, hands-on interactive exhibits. Toddlers will love pressing buttons, steering virtual ships, and looking at shiny gold doubloons.

Where to eat

  • Gladys' Cafe — Located in a historic stone alley in Charlotte Amalie, this welcoming spot serves authentic Caribbean dishes like conch fritters and kid-approved sweet plantains.
  • Mountain Top Dairy Bar — While parents sample the world-famous banana daiquiri, kids can indulge in delicious local fruit smoothies or milkshakes while taking in the island's best panoramic view.
  • Dinghy's Beach Bar & Grill — Dine right in the sand on Honeymoon Beach, offering casual bites like fish tacos and chicken tenders where toddlers can play in the sand while you wait.
  • Brooks Bar — Located near Magens Bay, this casual, welcoming local eatery serves hearty portions of BBQ chicken, rice and peas, and sweet johnnycakes that kids adore.
👨‍👩‍👧 Many of the island's best beaches have limited natural shade, so packing a pop-up UV tent and reef-safe sunscreen is essential for keeping toddlers cool and protected. Additionally, renting a car with a pre-booked car seat is highly recommended, as local open-air safari buses lack seatbelts and are difficult to navigate with strollers.
