Caribbean paradise tailored for tiny travelers and sun-seekers

St. Thomas

← back to travel board
Golden sand beaches meet turquoise waters in St. Thomas, where gentle waves and lush hillsides offer the perfect tropical backdrop for family adventures. From interactive marine encounters to slow-paced beach days, this island blends Caribbean charm with hassle-free American conveniences. It is an approachable, sun-drenched escape where parents can relax and toddlers can safely explore.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Magens Bay Beach

This world-famous, mile-long stretch features incredibly calm, wave-free waters protected by lush peninsulas. The zero-entry shoreline and abundant shade from coconut palms make it the island's safest natural splash zone for toddlers.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Coral World Ocean Park

Get up close with sea turtles, stingrays, and wild iguanas in a beautifully designed, conservation-focused park. The paved walkways and unique underwater observatory tower are highly stroller-accessible and perfect for curious little eyes.

Morning / 2h

Coki Point Beach

Located right next to Coral World, this lively beach features crystal-clear, shallow water teeming with friendly, colorful fish. Toddlers will love standing in the shallows as schools of sergeant majors swim right up to their feet.

Morning / 1-2h

Phantaseas Tropical Botanical Garden

Wander through a lush canopy filled with blooming orchids, towering palms, and colorful resident parrots. The gravel paths are easy to navigate, and the garden's shade provides a cool, sensory-rich respite from the midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Skyride to Paradise Point

Fly 700 feet above Charlotte Amalie in a modern aerial cable car to witness breathtaking harbor views. The smooth, gentle ride is thrilling for kids without being scary, and there is plenty of room for a folded stroller.

Morning / 1-2h

Charlotte Amalie Market Square

Immerse your family in local culture at this historic open-air market where vendors sell fresh tropical fruits and handmade crafts. It is a sensory delight for toddlers, who can munch on sweet, fresh mango slices while you browse.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Lindqvist Beach (Smith Bay Park)

Part of a protected park, this pristine, less-crowded beach offers soft pink-hued sand and calm tide pools. It is the ideal spot for a peaceful afternoon of building sandcastles followed by a quiet toddler nap under the sea grape trees.

Full Day / 4-5h

Water Island Ferry and Honeymoon Beach

Take a fun, 10-minute ferry ride to Water Island, rent a golf cart, and head to this secluded beach paradise. The ferry ride is a major novelty for kids, and the beach's exceptionally quiet waters are perfect for tiny floaties.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Drake's Seat Overlook

This iconic, easily accessible roadside lookout offers a stunning panoramic view of Magens Bay and the surrounding green hills. It is a quick, stroller-friendly photo stop to capture postcard-perfect family memories without any hiking required.

Where to eat

  • Gladys' Cafe — A cozy, family-friendly institution in a historic stone alleyway serving up mild coconut chicken, fungi, and exceptional local fruit hot sauces.
  • The Shack at Magens Bay — Conveniently located right on the sand, this spot serves quick, kid-pleasing favorites like chicken tenders, fries, and fresh-squeezed fruit smoothies.
  • Brooks Bar — A casual, welcoming local spot near the north side offering comforting Caribbean dishes like pull-apart Johnny cakes that toddlers love.
  • Duffy's Love Shack — An energetic, open-air eatery in Red Hook serving fun tropical mocktails in souvenir cups alongside high-quality burgers and fish tacos.
👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a sturdy, all-terrain stroller as many historic sidewalks in Charlotte Amalie are cobblestone and beach paths can be soft and sandy. Always pack reef-safe sunscreen and a pop-up beach tent, as the Caribbean sun is intense and natural shade can fill up quickly at popular spots.
