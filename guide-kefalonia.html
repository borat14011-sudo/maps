Emerald mountains plunge into the translucent Ionian Sea, welcoming Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire to Greece's most diverse island haven. From the Venetian-influenced harbor of Fiskardo to the dramatic white sands of Myrtos, Kefalonia pairs rugged natural beauty with exclusive Mediterranean living. Spend your afternoons sipping local Robola wine on a shaded terrace while Mary Claire naps soundly under the cool sea breeze.

What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Melissani Cave

A breathtaking underground lake where sunlight creates a surreal blue glow. Will and Kathryn can enjoy a serene boat ride; baby Mary Claire must be worn in a secure front carrier as strollers are not permitted down the access tunnel.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM / 2h

Fiskardo Promenade

A pastel-colored, Venetian-style harbor town that was untouched by the 1953 earthquake. It offers a completely flat, stroller-friendly paved promenade right along the yacht-filled water with plenty of shade from restaurant awnings.

Sunset 6:30 PM - 7:30 PM / 1h

De Bosset Bridge

The world's longest stone bridge across a sea, spanning the Argostoli Gulf. It is entirely pedestrianized, perfectly flat, and stroller-accessible, making it the ideal spot for an easy sunset walk to spot giant Loggerhead sea turtles swimming in the shallows.

Morning 9:30 AM - 12:30 PM / 3h

Antisamos Beach

A lush, green-framed bay with exceptionally calm, wave-free waters. Rent a luxury double daybed with a wide umbrella at one of the upscale beach clubs to keep Mary Claire fully shaded and comfortable while dipping her toes in the warm water.

Late morning 11:00 AM - 11:45 AM / 45m

Myrtos Beach Viewpoint

Kefalonia's most iconic dramatic cliffside vista. Skip the steep, bumpy drive and hot pebble beach below with an infant; instead, stop at the paved, stroller-safe cliffside viewing platforms for stunning, hassle-free family photos.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Assos Village

A picture-perfect, sleepy peninsula village painted in soft pinks and blues. While the path up to the castle is too steep and rugged for a stroller, the lower village square is flat, shady, and offers a calm, shallow beach cove perfect for splashing.

Midday 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM / 1h

Drogarati Cave

An awe-inspiring cave featuring magnificent stalactites and natural acoustics. The cave interior maintains a cool, constant 64°F (18°C) temperature, providing excellent midday heat relief, but you must carry baby Mary Claire down the steps in a carrier.

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM / 1.5h

Castle of Saint George

A ruined Venetian castle standing high above the island. Avoid pushing a stroller over the ruins inside; instead, enjoy a peaceful, stroller-friendly walk through the beautiful, quiet historic village of Kastro just outside the main gate.

Late afternoon 5:00 PM - 7:30 PM / 2.5h

Petani Beach

A beautiful western bay known for its incredible sunset views. Reserve an umbrella and a sun lounger early to enjoy the gentle sea breeze, which provides excellent natural white noise for Mary Claire's late afternoon nap.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tassia Restaurant

The island's premier luxury seafood institution located right on the Fiskardo harbor. Famous for its lobster pasta, it offers a spacious outdoor dockside terrace, easy stroller access, early dining starting at 6 PM, and highchairs upon request.

Vassilis Restaurant

An upscale, traditional Greek taverna in Fiskardo offering refined local dishes like Kefalonian meat pie. The broad, garden-lined courtyard accommodates strollers easily, and the early evening service is peaceful and welcoming to families.

Aristofanis Tavern

An excellent waterfront seafood spot in Argostoli. It is highly accommodating for families, offers highchairs, and provides a front-row seat to watch the local Caretta-Caretta turtles in the harbor while you dine on fresh, simply grilled sea bass.

Platanos Tree Restaurant

A peaceful tavern set under a massive, cooling plane tree in the heart of Assos. The shaded, spacious outdoor layout is ideal for parking a premium stroller table-side, allowing Mary Claire to sleep in the cool shade while you enjoy hand-rolled dolmades.

Ampelaki Restaurant

A refined, family-friendly Greek bistro in Argostoli near the port. Highly rated for its modern takes on traditional Greek plates, it features a spacious, flat outdoor terrace, accommodating staff who love infants, and clean baby-changing facilities.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Emelisse Nature Resort

$300-400/night

Nestled near Fiskardo, this luxury resort is exceptionally family-friendly. It offers spacious family suites, baby cribs on request, flat paved resort pathways, a designated children's wading pool, and high-quality blackout curtains to ensure Mary Claire's nap schedule is uninterrupted.

Eliamos Villa Resort

$350-400/night

A luxury 5-star design resort in Spartia offering private pool villas with sweeping Ionian views. Ideal for a finance couple seeking seclusion; you can request a wooden crib, bottle sterilizers, and enjoy your own private plunge pool with safety barriers available on demand.

White Rocks Hotel Kefalonia

$250-380/night

A stylish, upscale cliffside retreat in Lassi offering direct lift access to Platis Gialos Beach. Perfect for families, with modern bungalows, high-end cribs, calm beach coves for baby dips, and lush, manicured grounds that are easy to navigate with a stroller.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pharmacies in Argostoli and Lassi are fully stocked with premium European formula, diapers, and baby supplies. Opt for a sturdy stroller with large wheels as historic spots like Assos feature cobblestones, though harbor promenades are universally flat and stroller-friendly. For peace of mind, the General Hospital of Argostoli 'Agios Gerasimos' is the island's primary medical facility equipped for pediatric emergency services.
