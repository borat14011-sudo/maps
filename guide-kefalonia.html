Kefalonia

← back to travel board
Kefalonia enchants families with its dramatic mountain landscapes, subterranean wonders, and some of the world's most breathtaking turquoise beaches. Kids will love searching for loggerhead turtles in the harbor, while parents will appreciate the island's laid-back, welcoming Greek hospitality. It is a playground of natural beauty perfect for creating lifelong family memories.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi or rental car drive from Kefalonia Airport (EFL) to the family-friendly beach hotels of Lassi

What to do

Morning / 1-2h

Melissani Cave

Take a magical rowboat ride inside a semi-underground lake where the midday sun turns the water an electric blue. Lifejackets are provided, and the boat ride is short enough to keep toddlers captivated without getting restless.

Late Afternoon / 30m

Myrtos Beach Viewpoint

Stop at the clifftop viewing platform for a breathtaking family photo overlooking one of the world's most famous beaches. Keep toddlers securely in a carrier or stroller as the viewpoint edge has steep, dramatic drops.

Morning / 1h

Argostoli Harbor Turtle Spotting

Walk along the harbor in the morning to watch giant Caretta Caretta sea turtles swimming near the returning fishing boats. Toddlers can safely watch the action from the wide, stroller-friendly pedestrian promenade.

Midday / 1h

Drogarati Cave

Explore a cool, ancient cavern filled with impressive stalactites and stalagmites that feel like a subterranean palace. The steps down can be slippery, so carry toddlers or hold hands closely during the descent.

Morning / 3-4h

Makris Gialos Beach

Spend a relaxing day on golden sands with incredibly clear, shallow, and calm waters ideal for paddling. Excellent facilities with sun loungers and beachside tavernas make diaper changes and snack breaks easy.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Assos Village Exploration

Wander through the pastel-colored streets of this picture-perfect peninsula village and dip your toes in the shallow harbor. The lower village is highly stroller-accessible, though you should skip the steep hike up to the castle ruins with a heavy stroller.

Evening / 1-2h

Vallianou Square Playground

Let the kids burn off energy in Argostoli's central pedestrianized plaza, which features a dedicated, gated playground. Parents can sit back and enjoy a cold Greek frappe from a neighboring sidewalk cafe while keeping an eye on the little ones.

Morning / 2-3h

Foki Beach

This fjord-like, emerald cove is surrounded by ancient olive trees that provide abundant natural shade right up to the water's edge. The extremely calm waters are perfect for a toddler's first swim, and the shady grove is ideal for outdoor naps.

Morning / 1h

Argostoli Municipal Market

Stroll down the lively pedestrian market street to see colorful stalls loaded with local fruits, honey, and fresh fish. Grab a fresh, warm sweet pastry like bougatsa from a local bakery to keep little ones happily snacking as you explore.

🍽️ Where to eat

Tassia Restaurant

Sit harbor-front in Fiskardo and order the traditional, savory Kefalonian meat pie packed with local herbs.

Il Borgo

Dine near the castle ruins and enjoy the tender, slow-cooked lamb kleftiko while taking in sweeping panoramic views of the island.

Ladokolla tis Pareas

Try the exceptionally juicy, kid-friendly pork souvlaki skewers served casually on greaseproof paper at this lively grill house.

Waterfront Taverna

Get the fresh, locally caught grilled sea bream paired with crispy, golden zucchini fries that toddlers will love.

Aristofanis

Order the light, crispy calamari and a fresh Greek salad while sitting right next to the historic sea mills.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a sturdy car with a high-quality car seat to navigate the island's winding, mountainous roads safely. Opt for a lightweight, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier, as many of the most charming coastal villages feature cobblestones and steps.
