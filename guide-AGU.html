Aguascalientes

← back to travel board
Aguascalientes welcomes families with its pedestrian-friendly historic plazas, lush green parks, and rich railway heritage. Parents can soak in thermal waters while kids delight in interactive museums and sprawling play spaces. It is a beautifully compact, safe, and sunny Mexican escape tailored for stress-free exploration.

What to do

Afternoon / 2-3h

Plaza de las Tres Centurias

Explore this historic railway station featuring vintage locomotives, dancing fountains, and a miniature train ride. Toddlers will love chasing the water fountains and watching the old steam engines roll by.

Morning / 3h

Museo Descubre

A fantastic hands-on science museum featuring a planetarium, a dinosaur area, and beautiful outdoor gardens. The dedicated early-childhood zone is perfect for toddlers to touch, play, and explore safely.

Late Afternoon / 2h

Isla San Marcos

This massive, peaceful park features a scenic lake with boat rentals, the world's largest mechanical clock, and plenty of green grass. It is incredibly stroller-friendly and perfect for a relaxed family picnic.

Morning / 2h

Baños de Ojocaliente

Relax in the historic, warm thermal mineral baths that gave the city its name, dating back to the 19th century. Families can book private family pools, making it easy to manage splashy toddlers in a controlled environment.

Morning / 1-2h

Museo José Guadalupe Posada

Located in the charming Barrio de Triana, this museum showcases the famous printmaker behind the iconic 'La Catrina' skeleton. While older kids appreciate the art, toddlers will enjoy the spacious, vehicle-free courtyard outside.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado Terán

Dive into this bustling traditional market to experience local life, vibrant colors, and sweet treats. Keep toddlers close in a carrier as the narrow aisles are packed with interesting sights and sensory delights.

Morning / 3h

Parque Rodolfo Landeros

A sprawling environmental park featuring a mini-zoo, an artificial lake, playgrounds, and bicycle rentals. Toddlers will love seeing the rescued animals and riding in the shaded pedal carts.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Tranvía Turístico

Hop on this open-air, double-decker trolley for a scenic tour of the city's historic neighborhoods without tiring out little legs. It is a savior for toddlers who need a breezy, seated break while still taking in the sights.

Evening / 1-2h

Plaza de la Patria

The heart of the city, dominated by a majestic cathedral and beautifully manicured gardens. Toddlers will love chasing pigeons in the wide pedestrian plaza while parents enjoy a cup of local coffee nearby.

Where to eat

  • Chascas — A beloved local street snack of sweet corn kernels mixed with mayonnaise, lime, and cheese, which can be customized completely spice-free for toddlers.
  • La Estación Restaurant — Located near the historic railway, this family-friendly spot serves delicious traditional breakfasts like chilaquiles in a spacious setting that easily accommodates high chairs.
  • Guava Desserts at Mercado Juárez — Treat the family to local guava-based sweets, cajeta rolls, and ice creams that are perfectly portioned for sweet-toothed kids.
  • Cenaduría Esthela — A cozy local diner serving authentic, non-spicy flautas, pozole, and sopes that are easy for toddlers to finger-feed on.
👨‍👩‍👧 Aguascalientes is highly pedestrian-friendly in the center, but bring a compact stroller with sturdy wheels for navigating the colonial cobblestone streets. Many restaurants are incredibly welcoming to families, so do not hesitate to ask for 'media ración' (half portions) or kid-friendly, non-spicy adjustments.
