Halifax

← back to travel board
With its bustling boardwalk, historic fortresses, and salty sea breezes, Halifax welcomes families with open arms and maritime charm. From watching tall ships on the harbor to exploring lush public gardens, this compact coastal city is exceptionally easy to navigate with little ones in tow. Prepare for friendly locals, fresh seafood, and endless ocean views that will captivate both parents and toddlers alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min drive via taxi, rideshare, or the Halifax Transit MetroX bus directly from the airport to downtown hotels

What to do

Morning / 2h

Halifax Harborwalk

Walk the scenic 4km wooden boardwalk to watch ferries, tugboats, and tall ships glinting on the water. Toddlers will love climbing on the famous giant blue 'Wave' sculpture or playing at the Submarine-themed playground along the route.

Late Morning / 2-3h

Halifax Citadel National Historic Site

Step back in time to 1869 at this star-shaped hill fort featuring historic re-enactors, bagpipe performances, and the daily noon cannon blast. The wide, grassy ramparts are perfect for energetic toddlers to run around, though stroller pushing requires a bit of effort on the gravel paths.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Halifax Public Gardens

This stunning Victorian oasis in the heart of the city features paved paths, ornate fountains, and a beautiful wooden bandstand. Toddlers will be delighted to feed the resident ducks in the pond or enjoy a quiet family stroll under the shaded weeping trees.

Morning / 3h (including drive)

Peggy's Cove Lighthouse

Take a short drive out of the city to explore rugged granite cliffs and see Canada's most famous, photogenic lighthouse. For safety, keep toddlers close and stick to the newly built wooden viewing platform, avoiding the wet, slippery black rocks near the waves.

Morning / 1-2h

Halifax Seaport Farmers' Market

Sample local treats, fresh pastries, and artisanal crafts at North America's oldest continuously operating market. Visit early in the morning with a stroller to beat the crowds and secure a sweet treat for the kids without the long lines.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Discovery Centre

This state-of-the-art hands-on science museum offers four floors of interactive exhibits on flight, oceans, and energy. Toddlers have their own dedicated, safe play space in the 'Little Kids Society' gallery, which features soft play areas and sensory activities.

Morning / 2h

Museum of Natural History

Discover local wildlife, Mi'kmaq culture, and impressive whale skeletons at this highly engaging museum. Young children will love meeting Gus the Gopher Tortoise, who has lived at the museum for over 70 years and goes for daily walks.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Point Pleasant Park

Located at the southern tip of the peninsula, this massive forested park features gravel trails, historic military ruins, and gorgeous coastal views. The wide, flat paths make stroller navigation incredibly easy while the toddler explores the pine-scented woods.

Midday / 1h round trip

Halifax Transit Ferry to Dartmouth

Enjoy a cheap and cheerful 12-minute harbor crossing that offers the absolute best views of the Halifax skyline. Toddlers will love the sensory thrill of the windy top deck, while parents will appreciate the budget-friendly price tag.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bicycle Thief

Snag a table on the heated waterfront patio and order the legendary handmade lobster ravioli while the kids enjoy simple, fresh buttered pasta.

Cow's Ice Cream

Treat the family to a scoop of 'Gooey Mooey' or 'Wavy Gravy' served in freshly baked waffle cones right on the boardwalk.

King of Donair

Introduce your tastebuds to Halifax's official food by ordering a classic, sweet-sauced donair wrapped in warm pita bread.

The Wooden Monkey

Enjoy locally sourced, organic meals like the roasted chickpea salad or a toddler-friendly, thin-crust cheese pizza in a relaxed setting.

The Stubborn Goat Gastropub

Dig into their famously creamy, gourmet mac and cheese in a lively, kid-welcoming environment where minor toddler noise blends right in.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack plenty of layers and windbreakers even in the summer, as the maritime harbor breeze can cause the temperature to drop rapidly. While the wooden waterfront boardwalk is exceptionally flat and stroller-friendly, keep in mind that the historic streets leading up to the Citadel are quite steep.
