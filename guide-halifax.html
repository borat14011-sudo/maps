What to do

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Halifax Waterfront Boardwalk

This completely flat, three-kilometer wooden boardwalk offers sweeping ocean views and gentle sea breezes. It is perfectly stroller-accessible, featuring wide paths and plenty of shaded benches for outdoor bottle feedings.

Mid-day 11:30am-1:30pm / 2h

Halifax Public Gardens

A pristine, Victorian-era oasis right in the heart of the city. The paved, level pathways are ideal for a smooth stroller ride, while the weeping willows and duck ponds offer a quiet, shaded environment for an outdoor nap.

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Peggy's Cove Lighthouse

An iconic maritime landmark just a short drive from the city. Stick to the newly constructed, fully accessible wooden viewing deck for safe, stroller-friendly sight-seeing away from the slippery rocks.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Discovery Centre

This modern, multi-level science centre features the 'Little Learners' gallery, a dedicated sensory play space designed specifically for toddlers and crawlers to explore safely.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

Maritime Museum of the Atlantic

An indoor, climate-controlled sanctuary that is incredibly stroller-friendly. The spacious galleries feature fascinating exhibits, offering a peaceful and quiet indoor stroll if the coastal weather gets too brisk.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Point Pleasant Park

Located at the southern tip of the peninsula, this park features wide, crushed-gravel paths beneath a canopy of towering pines. It offers fresh ocean air and a serene, quiet atmosphere for a family walk.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Halifax Citadel National Historic Site

Stroll the historic fort's paved exterior pathways for stunning panoramic views of the harbor. Be sure to skip the loud noon-gun demonstration to protect baby Mary Claire's sensitive ears.

Afternoon 3-5pm / 2h

Fisherman's Cove

A charming, 200-year-old restored fishing village in Eastern Passage. The flat, stroller-friendly wooden boardwalk offers a colorful and quiet seaside walk away from the bustle of the downtown core.

Afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Shubie Park

Located a short drive away in Dartmouth, this beautiful park offers shaded, level canal-side trails. The tranquil sound of flowing water and rustling leaves provides the ultimate natural white noise for an afternoon stroller nap.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bicycle Thief

Serving upscale Italian 'North American style' right on the waterfront. Book an early 5:00 PM seating on the heated patio to enjoy world-class lobster ravioli while baby is relaxed in her stroller.

Drift

Located in the Muir Hotel, this gorgeous venue offers refined modern Atlantic Canadian cuisine. The spacious booth seating easily accommodates highchairs, and the soft ambient morning light makes it perfect for a luxury family breakfast.

The Press Gang Oyster House

Housed in one of Halifax's oldest stone structures, this upscale spot offers premium local seafood. Opt for an exceptionally early evening reservation to beat the crowds and enjoy the quiet, intimate atmosphere with your baby.

Ristorante a Mano

An elegant, high-end Italian eatery on the waterfront. The spacious outdoor terrace is perfect for a lively family lunch, offering excellent wood-fired pizzas, highchairs, and a forgiving level of ambient background noise.

Obladee, A Wine Bar

This chic spot offers elevated charcuterie boards and local Nova Scotian wines. Arrive for an early-evening glass on the patio where the casual yet luxury atmosphere is highly accommodating to parents with a sleeping infant.
