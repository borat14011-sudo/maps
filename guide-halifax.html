Historic maritime charm meets easy-paced waterfront luxury

Halifax

← back to travel board
Halifax pairs rugged Atlantic beauty with a compact, highly walkable downtown, making it an effortless getaway for a young family. For a finance dad seeking top-tier dining and premium stays without the pretension, the city’s vibrant waterfront offers high-end seafood and seamless stroller access. From breezy seaside parks to harbor ferries, you’ll find a welcoming, laid-back maritime pace that accommodates early toddler bedtimes perfectly.
🚕 Getting there: ~35 min private SUV transfer from Halifax Stanfield International Airport to downtown waterfront hotels

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

Halifax Harbourwalk

Walk the flat, wooden 4-kilometer boardwalk right along the water, stopping at the maritime-themed playground near Sands at Salter. It's completely stroller-accessible with wide paths and plenty of benches for feeding breaks.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Halifax Public Gardens

This pristine 17-acre Victorian garden features wide paved paths, historic fountains, and a duck pond that will keep your toddler mesmerized. It is entirely flat for strollers, and the on-site Uncommon Grounds cafe is perfect for an afternoon iced coffee.

Morning / 2h

Discovery Centre

An ultra-modern, interactive science museum featuring an outstanding 'Brave New Waves' water gallery and a dedicated toddler zone on the second floor. It's fully stroller-accessible with elevators, spacious family washrooms, and private nursing rooms.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Museum of Natural History

Home to Gus, a 101-year-old Gopher Tortoise who goes for daily walks, plus fascinating ocean gallery displays. The museum is compact, quiet, and highly stroller-friendly, offering an easy indoor escape if the Atlantic weather turns chilly.

Morning / 2h

Point Pleasant Park

Located at the southern tip of the peninsula, this park offers towering pines and wide, crushed-gravel carriage paths perfect for jogging strollers. Your toddler will love watching the massive container ships pass by from the shoreline benches.

Weekend Morning / 1-2h

Halifax Seaport Farmers' Market

Browse local ocean-fresh treats and crafts in this bright, airy indoor market right on the pier. The wide aisles easily accommodate strollers, and the lively weekend morning bustle naturally masks any sudden toddler tantrums.

Morning / 3h

Peggy's Cove Lighthouse

An iconic coastal drive just 45 minutes from town, featuring a stunning, fully accessible wooden viewing deck over the rocks. Skip the stroller for the rocky terrain and use a baby carrier so you can safely explore the dramatic granite shoreline.

Afternoon / 1h

Halifax Transit Ferry to Dartmouth

For the price of a transit ticket, take a smooth 15-minute harbor crossing to Dartmouth for spectacular views of the Halifax skyline. The ferry terminals are completely step-free and ramp-accessible, allowing you to roll your stroller right onboard.

Morning / 2h

Halifax Citadel National Historic Site

Explore this 19th-century star-shaped hilltop fortress with sweeping harbor views and live bagpipe performances. While the gravel parade grounds can be slightly bumpy, the main pathways are stroller-accessible, though sensitive toddlers may want to skip the loud noon-cannon blast.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Bicycle Thief

Order the local lobster roll or the pistachio-crusted halibut on the heated, harbor-facing patio. The upscale-casual Italian atmosphere is bustling and loud enough to easily drown out any toddler noise while you enjoy a premium glass of wine.

The Canteen on Portland

Take the ferry to Dartmouth and order the legendary 'Crobster' roll loaded with fresh lobster and snow crab. The bright, energetic dining room is highly welcoming to families, with high chairs and space for stroller parking.

Drift

Indulge in elevated Atlantic Canadian cuisine like the classic Hodge Podge or pan-seared scallops inside the luxurious Queen's Marque district. Book an early 5:00 PM reservation when the spacious dining room is quiet and the staff can easily accommodate an infant carrier at your table.

Rinaldo's Italian American Specialties

Savor their incredible Detroit-style deep dish pizzas and hand-rolled garlic knots in a relaxed neighborhood setting. It is highly family-oriented with a warm, noisy vibe that makes dining with an infant completely stress-free.

2 Crows Brewing Co.

Grab a flight of top-tier local sour beers for dad while relaxing in their spacious, family-friendly taproom. They welcome kids and babies, feature changing tables in the restrooms, and allow you to bring in outside food to keep things easy.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Muir, Autograph Collection

Halifax's premier luxury waterfront hotel offers ultra-spacious, contemporary rooms with sweeping harbor views, top-tier cribs upon request, and direct boardwalk access perfect for early morning stroller walks.

The Westin Nova Scotian

An iconic, historic property situated right next to the Seaport Farmers' Market, featuring a heated indoor pool and exceptionally spacious family suites equipped with complimentary cribs.

The Prince George Hotel

Offers upscale comfort in the heart of downtown with an indoor pool, spacious double-queen rooms, and an indoor pedway connection that lets you navigate downtown with a stroller without facing wind or rain.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a high-quality baby carrier alongside your stroller, as some historic sites like the Citadel or the rocky coast of Peggy's Cove have uneven gravel or stone terrain. Halifax is famously hilly when walking away from the water, so plan your routes to walk along the flat Harbourwalk boardwalk to keep stroller pushing effortless. Atlantic weather can shift rapidly from sunny to foggy in minutes, so dress your toddler in layers and always pack a windproof rain cover for your stroller.
