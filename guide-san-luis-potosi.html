What to do

Early morning 9-11am / 2h

Jardín Japonés in Parque Tangamanga I

Stroll the serene, paved pathways of the Japanese Garden inside Mexico's second-largest urban park. The peaceful ponds and gentle shade are perfect for Mary Claire's morning nap away from city noise.

Morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Centro de las Artes de San Luis Potosí

Explore this converted 19th-century fortress, now a stunning cultural center. The wide, flat, stroller-accessible courtyards allow you to admire the impressive stone architecture effortlessly.

Late morning 11am-1pm / 2h

Museo Leonora Carrington

Browse surrealist bronze sculptures in a spacious, quiet museum setting. The expansive outdoor courtyards are highly tranquil, cool, and easily navigated with a premium stroller.

Afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Plaza de Aranzazú

Relax in this iconic, beautifully preserved baroque plaza. The flat stone courtyard is perfect for a gentle afternoon walk while Mary Claire watches the shimmering fountains.

Midday 1-2:30pm / 1.5h

Museo Nacional de la Máscara

Discover vibrant traditional masks housed in a majestic neoclassical palace. The indoor galleries are air-conditioned, quiet, and offer a cool respite from the midday heat.

Early morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Parque de Morales

A lush, tree-filled local park ideal for an early morning walk. Enjoy the flat, paved trails under a thick canopy of ash trees and watch the calm ducks on the lake.

Sunset 5:30-7pm / 1.5h

Catedral Metropolitana de San Luis Potosí

Admire the stunning pink quarry stone facade from the pedestrian-only Plaza de Armas. The flat, wide plaza is entirely stroller-friendly, perfect for a slow sunset stroll.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Jardín de San Francisco

Unwind under the shade of massive jacaranda and ash trees in this peaceful garden plaza. It is a quiet oasis with flat stone walkways, ideal for a calm baby carrier walk.

Morning 10:30am-12pm / 1.5h

Museo Federico Silva

Marvel at contemporary sculptures inside an elegant, sun-drenched historic building. The spacious hallways and interior courtyard provide a quiet, low-sensory environment for a sleeping baby.

🍽️ Where to eat

Trashumante

Upscale contemporary Mexican dining with a sophisticated yet relaxed terrace. The spacious outdoor seating, attentive staff, and early 1:30 PM lunch service make it incredibly welcoming for families with a baby.

La Gran Vía

A legendary fine-dining institution serving impeccable Spanish and Mexican classics. Request a table on the beautiful, plant-lined terrace during the quieter early evening hours for a seamless experience with Mary Claire.

Almacén de Especias

Stylish Mediterranean-Mexican fusion located in the upscale Lomas neighborhood. The restaurant offers highchairs, spacious booth seating, and a modern, ambient noise level that easily masks baby babbles.

Rincón Huasteco

Experience authentic regional Huastecan cuisine in a lively, upscale-casual environment. They feature spacious family tables, friendly staff who adore infants, and outstanding, tender cecina.

La Virreina

Housed in an exquisite historic mansion, this classic restaurant serves elegant traditional Mexican dishes. The quiet, high-ceilinged dining rooms and accommodating staff ensure a relaxed, low-stress lunch for parents.
