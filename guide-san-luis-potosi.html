San Luis Potosí

← back to travel board
Nestled in north-central Mexico, San Luis Potosí blends majestic pink-stone colonial architecture with nearby lush, surrealist landscapes. Families can wander pedestrian-only cobblestone plazas, dip into chocolate-making history, and explore tranquil, duck-filled parks. It is an underrated, highly walkable gem that captivates both parents and little ones.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or private shuttle from San Luis Potosí International Airport (SLP) to the historic downtown hotels

What to do

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Plaza de Aranzazú

This iconic plaza features stunning yellow arches and a spacious, vehicle-free courtyard. Toddlers will love chasing the gentle pigeons while parents admire the Baroque architecture.

Morning / 3-4h

Parque de Tangamanga I

One of Mexico's largest urban parks, offering shaded playgrounds, family rental bikes, and scenic lakes. Toddlers will especially enjoy the Splash Park area and the resident deer.

Morning / 2h

Museo Leonora Carrington

Set inside a beautifully restored historic penitentiary, this museum showcases whimsical, dreamlike bronze sculptures that spark kids' imaginations. The wide open-air courtyards are highly stroller-friendly.

Morning / 1-2h

Mercado República

A sensory-rich traditional market filled with colorful tropical fruits, local crafts, and bustling energy. Strap your toddler into a baby carrier to navigate the narrow, vibrant aisles safely.

Morning / 2h

Museo del Ferrocarril

Housed in a grand Art Deco station, this museum features real, historic train cars that kids can marvel at. Toddlers will love seeing the massive old locomotives and playing in the outdoor garden.

Afternoon / 30 mins

Chocolates Costanzo Factory Shop

Visit the flagship store of this legendary local chocolate maker to stock up on sweet treats. Toddlers will love the colorful 'duquesas' (marshmallows covered in chocolate) and soft fruit jellies.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Centro de las Artes de San Luis Potosí

An impressive, fortress-like cultural center featuring high stone walls and peaceful, grassy outdoor spaces. It is a quiet, enclosed area where toddlers can toddle safely away from busy streets.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Tranvía Turístico

A charming, open-air replica trolley that takes you on a relaxed tour of the city's historic center. It's the perfect way for tired toddlers to sightsee without having to walk.

Evening / 1h

Catedral Metropolitana & Plaza de Armas

The heart of the city features a grand pink-stone cathedral and a central bandstand that occasionally hosts live music. Toddlers will love dancing to the tunes and watching the balloon vendors.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Rincón Huasteco

Order the authentic Zacahuil, a giant traditional Huastecan tamal cooked in a banana leaf, which is soft and mild enough for toddlers to share.

La Gran Vía

Savor upscale Spanish-Mexican fusion like their famous suckling pig, while kids enjoy fresh artisanal breads and mild potato croquettes.

Café Cortés

Get a traditional café con leche and sweet concha pastries to enjoy on the patio while kids watch the lively plaza.

Los Arcos

Feast on fresh, family-friendly seafood dishes like shrimp tacos and mild fish fillets, served in a spacious, highly accommodating environment.

Rincón de San Francisco

Try the classic Enchiladas Potosinas—corn tortillas infused with mild chili, stuffed with cheese, and served with a side of refried beans that kids adore.

👨‍👩‍👧 Many of the historic center's streets are pedestrian-only cobblestone, so a sturdy, all-terrain stroller or a baby carrier is highly recommended. High chairs are common in family restaurants, but carry your own changing mat as restrooms in older buildings may lack dedicated changing tables.
