What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Villa Comunale Park

Located in Sorrento, this beautifully manicured public park offers stunning, panoramic views of the Gulf of Naples and Mount Vesuvius. The paved pathways are perfectly flat and stroller-accessible, while the soaring palm trees and historic statues provide ample shade for Mary Claire's morning nap.

Morning 9:30 AM - 1:30 PM

Private Gozzo Boat Charter

Embark on a private boat excursion along the coast departing from Amalfi Harbor, tailored to baby Mary Claire's feeding schedule. Luxury gozzo boats feature spacious shaded cabins, cushioned sundecks, and gentle speeds, allowing Will and Kathryn to admire the cliffs of Positano while the baby lulls to sleep on the calm water.

Afternoon 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Piazza Vescovado

Spend a relaxed afternoon in Ravello's main pedestrian-only square, which is entirely flat and highly stroller-friendly compared to the vertical streets of Positano. Sit back under a café umbrella, enjoy an espresso, and let Mary Claire watch the gentle village life unfold in a peaceful, car-free environment.

Late Morning 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Villa Rufolo Gardens

Explore these historic, multi-level cliffside gardens in Ravello, famous for their cascading floral displays and dramatic sea vistas. The upper terrace features wide, stroller-friendly gravel paths lined with towering umbrella pines that block the midday Mediterranean sun.

Morning 10:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Bagni Salvatore Beach Club

Nestled beneath Sorrento's cliffs, this beach club features clean, shallow coastal pools protected from rough waves, making it ideal for Mary Claire's first dip in the sea. A private cliffside elevator takes you directly down to the water, bypassing the steep stairs entirely.

Morning 8:30 AM - 10:00 AM

Piazza del Duomo

Stroll through Amalfi's historic center and settle in the vibrant town square outside the Cathedral of St. Andrew. The square is lively but spacious, offering a wonderful spot for parents to enjoy a fresh pastry while the baby is entertained by the ambient sounds of the local fountain.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Valle delle Ferriere Lower Path

Take a tranquil, shaded nature walk through the lower portion of this lush nature reserve, which follows the calm Canneto river. The flat dirt path is best navigated with a premium baby carrier, allowing you to walk under giant ferns and waterfalls in a remarkably cool microclimate.

Early Morning 8:30 AM - 11:00 AM

Pompeii Archaeological Park Lower Loop

Experience world-famous history by entering Pompeii through the Piazza Anfiteatro, where a specially designed, barrier-free path offers stroller access. The route is highly navigable, allowing you to view ancient villas while seeking shade under historic cypress trees during quieter morning hours.

Afternoon 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Lungomare Caracciolo

For a city day trip to Naples, walk along this expansive, completely flat, and pedestrian-only coastal promenade. It is ideal for an uninterrupted stroller walk with panoramic views of Castel dell'Ovo and the sea breeze keeping the baby comfortable.

🍽️ Where to eat

This legendary, Michelin-starred fine dining institution in Sant'Agata offers an elegant, quiet garden terrace perfect for an early evening seating. The highly accommodating staff welcomes families warmly, easily placing a luxury highchair beside your table while you savor their signature organic Mediterranean dishes.

Ristorante Marina Grande

Enjoy refined coastal cuisine and freshly caught fish right on the beach in Amalfi, featuring a stroller-accessible entry ramp. The spacious outdoor deck sits directly over the water, providing a soothing ambient soundtrack of waves that keeps Mary Claire relaxed during lunch.

La Sponda

Located inside Le Sirenuse in Positano, this breathtaking restaurant lights up with hundreds of candles at night. Book the earliest dinner seating at 7:30 PM to enjoy their famous Neapolitan cuisine on the open-air terrace, where the ocean breeze and generous table spacing accommodate a sleeping infant in a stroller.

Terrazza Bosquet

Set on an orange-grove terrace overlooking the Bay of Naples in Sorrento, this Michelin-starred restaurant offers a serene, spacious layout. The professional staff is highly attentive to families, providing quiet, comfortable corner seating that allows you to enjoy world-class dining with a baby.

Ristorante Max

Tucked away in a quiet, art-filled courtyard in Positano, this refined restaurant offers a peaceful escape from the main beach crowds. The enclosed garden patio is sheltered from the wind and sun, making it an exceptionally comfortable spot for an upscale family lunch with a highchair.
