Swedish West Coast (Gothenburg & Archipelago)

← back to travel board
Sweden’s wind-swept west coast blends cosmopolitan Gothenburg charm with granite-cliffed islands where car-free ferries rule the sea. Here, children can splash in shallow tide pools, ride vintage wooden rollercoasters, and indulge in daily fika breaks. It’s an effortless maritime playground designed for relaxed paces and tiny explorers.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi from Landvetter Airport to city center hotels, with simple tram connections to the archipelago ferries

What to do

Afternoon / 4-5h

Liseberg Amusement Park

Scandinavia's largest amusement park features lush gardens, vintage carousels, and Kaninlandet (Rabbit Land), which is a dream world specifically designed for toddlers. Toddlers will love meeting the green rabbit mascots and riding the gentle outdoor tracks.

Morning / 3-4h

Slottsskogen Park & Plikta Playground

This massive city park features a free zoo home to moose, seals, and penguins, alongside the sprawling Plikta adventure playground. Toddlers can safely climb on giant wooden sea monsters and enjoy the dedicated soft-play areas.

Morning to Afternoon / 5-6h

Southern Gothenburg Archipelago (Vrångö Island)

Hop on a car-free ferry to Vrångö, where stroller-friendly wooden boardwalks wind past red fishing shacks to sandy beaches. Toddlers can hunt for tiny crabs in the shallow, calm tide pools.

Morning / 3h

Universeum Science Center

An immersive, multi-story indoor science museum featuring an actual indoor rainforest canopy, giant ocean tanks with sharks, and hands-on toddler zones. The preschooler-focused play-labs are perfect for energetic little ones on rainy days.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Stora Saluhallen Food Market

This historic indoor food hall is packed with vibrant stalls offering Swedish cheeses, fresh berries, and gourmet treats in a bustling, sensory-rich environment. It is an easy, casual spot for toddlers to sample mild local cheeses and soft kardemummabullar.

Afternoon / 1h

Paddan Canal Boat Tour

Hop onto an open-air canal boat to glide under low stone bridges while taking in Gothenburg's historic harbor sights. Toddlers love the excitement of ducking down when the guide shouts to clear the lowest bridges.

Afternoon / 2h

Haga District Historic Walk

Stroll down Haga Nygata, a pedestrian-only cobblestone street lined with beautifully preserved 19th-century wooden houses and sweet shops. It is a car-free, safe zone where toddlers can wander freely between cozy storefronts.

Morning / 2-3h

World of Volvo

A striking, newly built interactive experience showcasing iconic Swedish vehicles with kid-friendly climbing zones and hands-on digital driving simulators. Toddlers will enjoy pushing miniature wooden trucks around the dedicated play tables.

Morning / 2h

The Garden Society (Trädgårdsföreningen)

A tranquil city-center park containing a spectacular 19th-century glass Palm House, rose gardens, and a whimsical playground. The heated glasshouse is a warm, quiet sanctuary for a stroller nap on cooler afternoons.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café Husaren

Order the legendary 'Hagabullen,' a giant cinnamon bun larger than a dinner plate that is perfect for a family to share.

Sjöbaren i Haga

Get the classic Swedish fish soup loaded with fresh seafood or a traditional open-faced shrimp sandwich in their cozy garden.

Villa Belparc

Grab an outdoor table by the pond in Slottsskogen and order their child-friendly wood-fired margherita pizzas.

Taverna Averna

Try the classic Swedish meatballs with lingonberries and cream sauce in a highly welcoming, family-friendly Italian-bistro setting.

Café Öbergska (Styrsö Island)

Enjoy fresh local herring, Swedish pancakes with jam, and artisan ice cream in a beautiful, enclosed herb garden.
