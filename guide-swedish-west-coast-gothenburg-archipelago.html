🚕 Getting there: ~25 min taxi or Flygbussarna airport coach to Gothenburg city center

What to do

Afternoon / 4-5h

Liseberg Amusement Park

Scandinavia’s largest amusement park offers thrilling rides and Kaninlandet (Rabbit Land), a magical green-themed area designed entirely for toddlers.

Morning / 3h

Slottsskogen Park

A massive green oasis featuring a free zoo with moose and penguins, plus the fantastic Plikta adventure playground. Toddlers will love the petting zoo and wide grassy spaces to run free.

Morning / 3-4h

Universeum

This massive science center features an indoor rainforest, a huge ocean zone with sharks, and a specialized Miniverseum play area designed for toddlers.

Full day / 5-6h

Southern Archipelago Ferry to Vrångö

Take a scenic ferry ride to a car-free island featuring sandy beaches and shallow swimming coves. Strollers are easy to roll along the paved coastal paths.

Afternoon / 2h

Haga Historic District

Stroll through Gothenburg's oldest neighborhood, famous for its preserved wooden houses and cobblestone streets. Toddlers will love snacking on giant pastries during a traditional 'fika' break.

Morning / 2-3h

Alfons Åbergs Kulturhus

An indoor creative play center based on the beloved Swedish children's book character Alfie Atkins. It's an absolute paradise for toddlers with climbing areas, storytime, and daily theater.

Afternoon / 1h

Paddan Canal Boat Tour

See the city from the water on an open-top boat that glides under extremely low bridges. It is highly engaging for young kids, and toddlers can sit comfortably on your lap.

Morning / 2h

Gothenburg Botanical Garden

One of Europe's largest botanical gardens, featuring winding paths, rock gardens, and peaceful glades. Toddlers can safely search for frogs in the ponds.

Lunch / 1-2h

Stora Saluhallen

A bustling historic food hall filled with local Swedish delicacies, cheeses, and fresh seafood. It's a great spot to grab quick, high-quality snacks that even picky toddlers will enjoy.

🍽️ Where to eat

Café Husaren

Order the legendary 'Hagabullen,' a plate-sized cinnamon bun that is perfect for sharing with the whole family.

Villa Belparc

Enjoy delicious wood-fired pizzas and classic Swedish dishes on a spacious outdoor terrace right next to the Slottsskogen lake.

Kafé Magasinet

Grab a casual lunch of fresh sourdough pizzas and bagels inside a hip, stroller-friendly greenhouse-style cafe.

Kåges Hörna

Try the traditional Swedish meatballs with mashed potatoes, lingonberries, and pickled cucumbers inside the busy historic food hall.

Saltholmens Kaffekopp

Stop by for fresh shrimp sandwiches and organic ice cream right before boarding the archipelago ferries.

👨‍👩‍👧 Buy a public transport day pass, which covers all city trams, buses, and even the archipelago ferries for seamless family travel. Most trams have a designated low-floor middle section making stroller boarding incredibly easy and stress-free.
