Swedish West Coast (Gothenburg & Archipelago)

← back to travel board
Gothenburg and its surrounding archipelago offer an incredibly clean, safe, and sophisticated coastal getaway that is perfectly optimized for young families. This region seamlessly combines world-class seafood dining and rich maritime culture with the world's most progressive, stroller-friendly infrastructure. From pristine car-free islands to expansive city parks, you can navigate the entire West Coast with ease, ensuring both parent relaxation and toddler wonder.
🚕 Getting there: A seamless ~25-minute taxi or private Mercedes V-Class transfer from Gothenburg Landvetter Airport (GOT) directly to downtown hotels.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Slottsskogen Park

This massive, historic park features a free open-air zoo with majestic Nordic animals like moose, harbor seals, and Humboldt penguins. Paved, wide pathways make pushing a stroller entirely effortless, and there are numerous manicured grassy lawns perfect for a relaxed family picnic.

Afternoon / 4-5h

Kaninlandet at Liseberg

Northern Europe's premier amusement park features 'Rabbit Land,' a highly-curated zone designed specifically for toddlers with gentle rides and clean, spacious nursing facilities. The entire park is impeccably clean, highly stroller-accessible, and features premium dining options for parents.

Morning / 4-5h

Styrsö Island (Southern Archipelago)

Take the stroller-accessible public ferry from Saltholmen to this car-free island paradise for peaceful coastal walks and sandy coves. The paved, winding pathways are completely free of cars, making it safe and stress-free to push a stroller while soaking in views of classic red Swedish cottages.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Trädgårdsföreningen & Palmhuset

A beautifully preserved 19th-century park in the city center featuring a giant glass Palm House filled with exotic plants and tranquil water features. It is incredibly stroller-friendly with wide gravel paths, a fantastic modern playground, and a quiet cafe for a mid-day nap window.

Morning / 3h

Universeum

Located right next to Liseberg, this massive science center features an indoor multi-story rainforest dome, giant ocean tanks, and a dedicated toddler play zone. Excellent elevators make navigating between floors with a stroller seamless, and it serves as a perfect rainy-day refuge.

Afternoon / 2-3h

World of Volvo

Opened in 2024, this architecturally stunning, Scandinavian-designed exhibition hub showcases classic vehicles and highly interactive, kid-friendly safety displays. The entire facility is ultra-modern, spacious, and features pristine stroller infrastructure alongside premium dining options.

Morning / 4h

Marstrand Island & Carlsten Fortress

Cross over on the brief, stroller-friendly ferry to explore this historic, car-free island dominated by a massive 17th-century stone fortress. While the climb to the fortress is steep and cobblestoned, the lower coastal path is smooth and perfect for a scenic stroller walk along the harbor.

Lunchtime / 1-2h

Stora Saluhallen (The Market Hall)

A bustling indoor food hall dating back to 1889, offering gourmet Swedish delicacies, seafood, and cheeses. The wide aisles make it easy to navigate with a stroller during off-peak morning hours, allowing you to sample local flavors without booking formal seating.

Morning / 4-5h

Nordens Ark

Located about 1.5 hours north of Gothenburg, this renowned wildlife sanctuary is dedicated to protecting endangered species like snow leopards and wolves. The entire park features a beautifully maintained wooden boardwalk trail that is fully stroller-accessible and winds through gorgeous pine forests.

🍽️ Where to eat

Villa Belparc

Order the classic wood-fired pizza or a fresh shrimp sandwich while sitting on the sprawling lakeside patio in Slottsskogen. It is highly accommodating for families, offering tons of outdoor stroller parking, high chairs, and room for a toddler to toddle on the grass.

Café Husaren

Stop here in the historic Haga district to share their legendary, plate-sized 'Hagabullen' cinnamon bun. The casual counter-service setup and bustling environment make it highly baby-friendly, and you can easily park your stroller outside on the pedestrian street.

Långedrags Värdshus

Experience premium dining with panoramic sea views and order the classic Butter-Poached Cod with horseradish and prawns. It is exceptionally spacious, noise-tolerant, and features excellent high-chairs and ramp access for strollers right by the water.

Taverna Averna

Located on the trendy Tredje Långgatan, this high-end Italian brasserie serves spectacular wood-fired pizzas and local seafood. Its lively, bustling atmosphere easily absorbs toddler noises, and they have plenty of room for stroller parking near the tables.

Sjömagasinet

For a high-end seafood experience, order the signature classic Swedish fish soup or tasting menu. Book an early dinner on their beautiful wooden deck overlooking the Göta River, which is highly spacious and welcoming to well-behaved little ones in strollers.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Liseberg Grand Curiosa Hotel

A premium, highly themed hotel right next to Liseberg with a slide in the lobby, incredibly spacious family suites, and excellent crib options. The location allows for instant access to the park and easy stroller-accessible tram connections.

Clarion Hotel Post

Housed in a historic post office next to the Central Station, it offers premium quiet rooms, a heated rooftop pool with family hours, and easy elevator access. Strollers roll right out into the city’s heart without navigating stairs.

Marstrands Havshotell

Located right on the harbor, this hotel offers spacious seaside family rooms, a world-class spa with dedicated family swim hours, and direct access to the stroller-friendly pedestrian ferry. It's the perfect high-end base for exploring the northern archipelago.

👨‍👩‍👧 Sweden is incredibly stroller-friendly; all public buses, city trams, and archipelago ferries allow strollers to roll directly on without folding, and tram boarding is free for the parent pushing the pram. Look for the universally clean 'Skötrum' (baby changing rooms) found in almost all public buildings, museums, and restaurants, which are exceptionally spacious and private. Adopt the local 'Fika' culture for easy mid-day breaks, and aim for early dinner reservations at 5:00 PM when restaurants are quietest and most accommodating with high chairs.
