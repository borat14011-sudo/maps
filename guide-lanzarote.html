Lanzarote

← back to travel board
Lanzarote captivates families with its dramatic, otherworldly volcanic landscapes, calm turquoise waters, and Cesar Manrique's whimsical art. Children will feel like space explorers traversing Mars-like lava fields before splashing in sheltered, shallow lagoons. It is a safe, sun-drenched sanctuary where natural wonders and easy-going island life seamlessly blend.
🚕 Getting there: ~15 min taxi to Puerto del Carmen, or ~30 min to Playa Blanca resorts.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Timanfaya National Park

Witness geothermal demonstrations where water turns to steam and ride a coach through dramatic, Martian-like volcanic craters. Toddlers will love the bubbling geyser show, though the bus ride requires sitting still.

Late Morning / 1.5h

Jameos del Agua

Explore a stunning volcanic tunnel transformed by artist César Manrique, featuring a natural lake home to tiny, blind white crabs. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glowing pool, but leave the stroller behind due to steep stone steps.

Morning / 3h

Playa Chica in Puerto del Carmen

A sheltered, horseshoe-shaped cove with crystal-clear, calm waters perfect for toddlers to splash safely and spot colorful fish. Bring a bucket and spade for the soft golden sand.

Afternoon / 1.5h

Cueva de los Verdes

Walk through a spectacular, illuminated lava tube that feels like an underground fantasy world. It is a magical walk for older kids, but toddlers are best carried in a baby carrier as it is not stroller-friendly.

Sunday Morning / 2-3h

Teguise Sunday Market

Wander the cobblestone streets of the island's historic capital, filled with colorful stalls, live music, and local crafts. Toddlers will enjoy the street performers and sweet churros, but arrive early to beat the crowds.

Full Day / 4-5h

Rancho Texas Lanzarote Park

A wild-west themed animal park featuring splash zones, sea lion shows, and gold panning activities. It is incredibly stroller-friendly and has excellent toddler play areas to cool off.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Jardín de Cactus

Walk through a giant, amphitheater-like garden housing thousands of cacti species under a picturesque traditional windmill. Toddlers will love the giant green shapes, but keep them close to avoid prickly encounters.

Afternoon / 1h

Mirador del Río

Take in jaw-dropping panoramic views of the neighboring island of La Graciosa from a spectacular lookout integrated into the high cliffs. The glass-walled indoor cafe is safe for toddlers to watch the boats below.

Morning / 3h

Playa de Papagayo

A breathtaking, shell-shaped beach tucked between cliffs with calm, emerald waters ideal for swimming. The descent from the parking lot is steep, so use a baby carrier rather than a stroller to reach the sand.

🍽️ Where to eat

El Diablo Restaurant

Order the volcanic-grilled chicken cooked directly over the geothermal heat of an open volcanic pit.

Restaurante El Risco

Try the fresh local fish of the day served with traditional Canarian wrinkly potatoes and mojo sauce.

La Cantina Teguise

Savor a selection of local cheeses, Iberian ham croquettes, and family-friendly tapas in a charming rustic courtyard.

Chiringuito Tropical

Enjoy a relaxed beachside seafood paella while the kids play safely in the sand right next to your table.

Restaurante Costa Azul

Feast on fresh grilled octopus and garlic prawns on the outdoor terrace overlooking the crashing waves.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a car with pre-installed car seats to easily navigate the island's volcanic terrain at your toddler's pace. Always pack a sturdy baby carrier alongside your stroller, as many natural attractions like caves and volcanic paths have uneven, rocky steps.
