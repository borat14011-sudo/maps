Nestled along Guerrero’s dramatic coastline, Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo offers a dual paradise of modern resort luxury and charming fishing-village soul. The sweeping curve of Playa La Ropa provides incredibly calm, wave-protected waters that are ideal for dipping tiny Mary Claire's toes into the warm Pacific. Will and Kathryn can easily navigate the flat, beautifully paved Paseo del Pescador boardwalk with a stroller while enjoying fresh ocean breezes. This tropical haven seamlessly blends high-end amenities with a tranquil, slow-paced atmosphere perfect for a rejuvenating first family getaway.

What to do

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM / 2.5h

Playa La Ropa

The crown jewel beach of Zihuatanejo offers ultra-calm waters with almost no undertow, making it exceptionally safe for baby Mary Claire to splash in the shallows. The soft, powdery sand is lined with upscale palapa restaurants offering shade, comfortable loungers, and beachside service. Best visited in the gentle morning light.

Early morning 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM / 1.5h

Paseo del Pescador (Fisherman's Walk)

This flat, beautifully paved waterfront boardwalk runs along the municipal beach and is completely stroller-accessible. It offers gorgeous views of the bay, shaded spots under towering palms, and a quiet morning atmosphere to watch local fishermen bring in their daily catch without heavy crowds.

Morning 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM / 1h

El Cocodrilario at Playa Linda

A protected, fenced crocodile sanctuary located right at the entrance of Playa Linda. Will and Kathryn can push the stroller along the flat viewing bridge to safely look down at native crocodiles, iguanas, and roseate spoonbills in their natural mangrove habitat, offering a quiet, sensory experience for baby Mary Claire.

Morning to Early Afternoon 9:30 AM - 1:30 PM / 4h

Playa Barra de Potosí Lagoon

A short, scenic drive south leads to a spectacular natural lagoon where the fresh water meets the ocean. Hire a private, shaded panga boat for a calm, slow-speed cruise through the mangroves to spot gentle birds, followed by a relaxing lunch under a shady beach palapa with perfectly flat wading water.

Early morning 7:30 AM - 9:00 AM / 1.5h

Ixtapa Ciclopista (Parque Aztlán Section)

A premier, paved ecological path winding through lush tropical jungle and wetlands. The path is entirely flat, smooth, and closed to motorized traffic, making it an idyllic setting for an early morning stroller run or walk where you can spot coatis, exotic birds, and turtles in complete peace.

Morning 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM / 4h

Isla Ixtapa (Playa Cuachalalate)

Take a brief, 5-minute private water taxi from Playa Linda pier to this quiet island. Head directly to Playa Cuachalalate, which features incredibly tranquil, pool-like water shielded from Pacific waves, perfect for floating with an infant in a canopy float.

Late Afternoon 5:30 PM - 6:30 PM / 1h

Zihuatanejo Plaza Principal

The cultural heart of the town, featuring a flat, open plaza that is highly stroller-friendly. In the late afternoon, it serves as a breezy gathering space for local families, offering a lovely, low-stimulation cultural walk under the shade of mature trees before dinner.

Morning 10:30 AM - 12:00 PM / 1.5h

Zihuatanejo Artisan Market (Mercado de Artesanías)

A covered, open-air market showcasing vibrant local crafts, hand-woven textiles, and folk art. The shaded stalls keep the midday heat at bay, and the gentle, colorful visual stimulation is delightful for a baby in a carrier while parents shop for high-end Mexican home decor.

Morning 9:00 AM - 12:30 PM / 3.5h

Playa Las Gatas

Accessible via a scenic, ultra-short 10-minute water taxi from the Zihuatanejo municipal pier. This beach is protected by a prehistoric coral reef barrier, creating a giant, crystal-clear saltwater swimming pool with absolutely zero waves, ideal for sensory baby water play under rented shade umbrellas.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Perla Restaurant

An iconic beachfront institution on Playa La Ropa serving legendary local tiritas de pescado (lime-cured fish). The restaurant is incredibly welcoming to families, offering comfortable high chairs, a spacious sand-floor layout so Mary Claire can play safely at your feet, and gorgeous ocean views.

Beccofino

An upscale Italian restaurant situated right on the Marina Ixtapa boardwalk. The outdoor wooden deck is quiet, stroller-accessible, and overlooks luxury yachts; they offer high chairs, premium service, and an excellent early dining window starting at 5:00 PM to beat the bedtime rush.

LOOT Surf & Lifestyle Store

A chic, architecturally stunning cafe and lifestyle concept serving artisan espresso, fresh juices, and incredible avocado toast. The breezy, open-air second-floor terrace is hip yet casual enough that parents won't worry about baby vocalizations, and there is plenty of room for stroller parking.

Angustina Mezcal y Cocina

An upscale, contemporary Mexican restaurant in downtown Zihuatanejo featuring elevated coastal cuisine. They offer comfortable outdoor terrace seating, a highly accommodating staff who will happily puree simple vegetables for the baby, and a peaceful early evening ambiance.

Bandidos Restaurant & Bar

A spacious, lively, and open-air palapa-style restaurant in Zihuatanejo famous for its sizzling fajitas and tacos. The upbeat, ambient hum easily masks any infant fussiness, and the exceptionally warm staff makes dining with a baby completely stress-free.
