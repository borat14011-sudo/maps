Alsace

← back to travel board
Nestled between the Vosges Mountains and the Rhine, the Alsace Wine Route looks like a storybook brought to life with half-timbered houses and cobblestone streets. It is an incredibly welcoming region for families, offering easy-paced exploration, castle ruins, and stork parks that enchant all ages. From gentle vineyard walks to cozy pastry stops, this is a slow-travel paradise for parents and little ones alike.
🚕 Getting there: ~45 min drive via rental car or taxi from EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg to Colmar hotels

What to do

Morning / 2h

La Montagne des Singes (Kintzheim)

Walk among 200 free-roaming Barbary macaques in a beautiful forest setting. Toddlers will love watching the monkeys up close, and the flat paths are highly stroller-friendly.

Morning / 2-3h

Château du Haut-Koenigsbourg (Orschwiller)

Explore a fully restored medieval fortress with sweeping views of the Alsatian plains. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the drawbridges and cannons, though a baby carrier is recommended for the stairs.

Afternoon / 1h

Little Venice Boat Tour (Colmar)

Drift down the peaceful Lauch River in a flat-bottomed boat to view the colorful half-timbered houses from the water. It is a relaxing, low-energy ride that keeps toddlers entertained without tiring their little legs.

Afternoon / 2-3h

NaturOparC (Hunawihr)

Meet Alsace's signature white storks, European otters, and coypus in a lush, conservation-focused park. Toddlers will adore the aquatic animal feedings and the gentle, flat walking trails.

Morning / 3-4h

Ecomusée d’Alsace (Ungersheim)

Step back in time at France's largest open-air museum, featuring authentic historical Alsatian buildings, crafts, and farm animals. Toddlers can pet the sheep and enjoy tractor-drawn carriage rides through the village.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Toy Museum / Musée du Jouet (Colmar)

Discover three floors of vintage and modern toys, from giant train sets to retro board games and life-sized Lego displays. There are several interactive play areas where toddlers can get hands-on.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Sentier Viticole de Riquewihr (Riquewihr)

Take a gentle, stroller-friendly walk through the scenic vineyards surrounding one of France's most beautiful villages. Toddlers can run safely along the paved paths while parents enjoy the panoramic valley views.

Morning / 1-2h

Colmar Covered Market / Marché Couvert (Colmar)

Taste your way through this historic brick market hall packed with fresh local cheeses, giant pretzels, and colorful fruits. It is an easy, casual spot to grab a quick, toddler-approved lunch or snack.

Morning / 3-4h

Cigoland (Kintzheim)

Enjoy a gentle amusement park designed specifically for families with younger kids, featuring small rides, a monorail, and stork spotting. It is the perfect size for toddlers to explore without getting overwhelmed.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Table du Brocanteur (Colmar)

Order the traditional Tarte Flambée topped with cream, onions, and bacon in this cozy, family-friendly bistro.

Wistub Brenner (Colmar)

Try the slow-cooked choucroute garnie or the kid-friendly, comforting plate of handmade spaetzle pasta.

La Table du Gourmet (Riquewihr)

Enjoy a refined lunch featuring fresh, seasonal Alsatian ingredients, with highly accommodating staff who can adapt dishes for children.

Restaurant S’Kaechele (Colmar)

Treat the family to a comforting bowl of traditional Fleischnacka, which are delicious meat-stuffed pasta rolls.

Au Trotthus (Riquewihr)

Savor an upscale but welcoming dinner featuring local trout and creative seasonal desserts in a beautiful 16th-century building.

👨‍👩‍👧 Bring a high-quality baby carrier for exploring the cobblestone streets of medieval villages like Riquewihr and Eguisheim, as strollers can be very bumpy on these ancient roads. Additionally, plan to dine early as many traditional winstubs open for dinner at 6:30 PM or 7:00 PM and fill up quickly, making reservations highly recommended.
