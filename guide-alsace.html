Alsace

Alsace blends storybook medieval charm with world-class gastronomic refinement along its sun-drenched Wine Route. Cobblestone villages decorated with bright geraniums sit nestled against rolling, vineyard-covered hillsides. For Will, Kathryn, and baby Mary Claire, the region offers a remarkably relaxed luxury escape filled with quiet strolls and exquisite tastings. The stroller-friendly paved pathways of the Parc de l'Orangerie and flat canal banks of Colmar make navigating with an infant effortlessly elegant.

What to do

Early morning 9-10:30am / 1.5h

La Petite Venise

Explore Colmar's most picturesque canal district. Use a carrier or a stroller with good suspension to navigate the flat cobblestone pathways alongside the scenic Lauch River.

Mid-morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Parc de l'Orangerie

Strasbourg's oldest park features a beautiful lake, a mini-zoo, and wide, paved, stroller-ready avenues perfect for Mary Claire's morning nap under shaded oak trees.

Early morning 9-11:30am / 2.5h

Château du Haut-Koenigsbourg

A magnificent mountain fortress with sweeping valley views. Opt for an Ergobaby carrier here as the historic stone steps are not stroller-accessible.

Early morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Vieille Ville de Riquewihr

Stroll through this perfectly preserved medieval wine village. Arrive early to enjoy quiet, uncrowded streets and cool morning air before the afternoon warmth.

Afternoon 3-4:30pm / 1.5h

Rue du Rempart in Eguisheim

A scenic, circular pedestrian walk showcasing colorful half-timbered houses. The path is narrow but paved, making it ideal for a peaceful slow-paced stroller walk.

Afternoon 2-4pm / 2h

NaturOparC

A tranquil wildlife park dedicated to Alsace's wild storks and otters. Features wide, flat gravel paths and plenty of shaded benches for nursing or resting.

Mid-morning 10am-12pm / 2h

La Montagne des Singes

Walk through a lush forest sanctuary where Barbary macaques roam freely. Highly shaded and peaceful, though a front carrier is required to keep baby close.

Early afternoon 1-3pm / 2h

Unterlinden Museum

Colmar's premier art museum housed in a historic convent. Fully air-conditioned with seamless elevator access for strollers, offering a quiet, temperature-controlled environment.

Afternoon 2-4:30pm / 2.5h

Parc de Wesserling

Incredible textile museum and expansive, manicured heritage gardens. The wide lawns are perfect for spreading a blanket for Mary Claire to enjoy supervised crawl time.

🍽️ Where to eat

La Maison des Têtes

Savor refined Alsatian gastronomy in an elegant courtyard. The spacious outdoor terrace easily accommodates strollers, and early 7 PM seatings are perfect for baby's schedule.

Le Théâtre

A stylish bistro serving classic French fare. Offers highchairs, ample room between tables for your stroller, and a warm, accommodating staff.

Wistub Brenner

Indulge in traditional choucroute on a lovely canal-side terrace. The ambient outdoor noise of Little Venice easily masks any infant babble or fussiness.

Winstub du Chambard

High-end traditional dining with a relaxed family atmosphere. They gladly provide sturdy wooden highchairs and are highly accommodating to young families.

Restaurant S'Kaechele

Cozy winstub offering exceptional local specialties. Book an early dinner slot; the intimate but welcoming team makes dining with an infant seamless.
