Lake Tahoe

← back to travel board
Surrounded by the majestic Sierra Nevada mountains, Lake Tahoe is a year-round playground of stunning sapphire waters and towering pines. Families can easily transition from sandy shores to scenic mountain trails, creating unforgettable memories in the crisp alpine air.
🚕 Getting there: ~1 hour drive from Reno-Tahoe International Airport (RNO) to North or South Lake Tahoe via rental car or the North Lake Tahoe Express shuttle.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Sand Harbor State Park

Swim in the calm, shallow coves and climb giant granite boulders. Toddlers will love playing in the soft, fine sand at the water's edge.

Morning / 1-2h

Emerald Bay State Park Lookout

Enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of Tahoe's only island from an easy-to-access paved overlook. It is stroller-accessible, making it effortless for families with toddlers.

Afternoon / 2h

Heavenly Mountain Gondola

Ride a scenic gondola high above the lake for stunning aerial views and access to mountaintop play areas. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the smooth, high-flying journey.

Morning / 1-2h

Taylor Creek Visitor Center & Rainbow Trail

Walk the flat, paved loop trail to spot native plants and look through the underground stream profile chamber to watch trout swim. Perfect for strollers and curious toddlers.

Afternoon / 3h

Kings Beach State Recreation Area

A sprawling sandy beach with shallow, warm waters and a great lakeside playground. Toddlers can safely splash in the gentle shallows while parents relax on the shore.

Tuesday Morning / 1-2h

South Lake Tahoe Farmers Market

Stroll through vibrant stalls filled with fresh local berries, baked goods, and live music. Toddlers will love sampling sweet seasonal fruits and dancing to the tunes.

Afternoon / 2h

Tallac Historic Site

Wander through historic estates and shaded forest paths along the lake shore. There are plenty of flat, grassy areas for toddlers to run around safely away from lake drop-offs.

Morning / 2-3h

Commons Beach

This popular park features a massive, modern playground right next to a gentle beach. Toddlers can alternate between swinging and dipping their toes in the calm lake.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Eagle Rock Trail

A short, 20-minute hike up an ancient volcanic plug rewards you with massive panoramic lake views. Toddlers can easily handle the gentle beginning of the trail or be carried to the top.

🍽️ Where to eat

Fire Sign Cafe

Order the homemade marionberry cobbler French toast and scratch-made biscuits for a hearty family breakfast.

Base Camp Pizza Co.

Get the Thai chicken curry pizza or a classic pepperoni pie while enjoying the live outdoor music and family-friendly patio.

Sunnyside Restaurant & Lodge

Grab a table on the massive lakefront deck and order the famous fried zucchini baskets and fresh fish tacos.

Sprouts Cafe

Try the wholesome, toddler-approved real fruit smoothies and the massive, fresh turkey milestone sandwich.

Jax At The Tracks

Feast on gourmet diner comfort foods like the famous Kobe beef meatloaf or decadent mac and cheese inside an authentic 1940s diner car.

👨‍👩‍👧 Lake Tahoe's high altitude and alpine sun can quickly dehydrate toddlers, so pack double the water and high-SPF sunscreen. To secure parking at popular spots like Sand Harbor or Emerald Bay, always aim to arrive before 8:30 AM.
