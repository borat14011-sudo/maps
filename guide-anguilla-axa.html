Anguilla welcomes sophisticated families to a pristine paradise defined by thirty-three world-class beaches and unmatched culinary refinement. The island's signature powdery white sand, particularly along the tranquil crescent of Shoal Bay East, provides the ultimate soft playground for little Mary Claire. Parents can unwind knowing that the gentle, tide-free shallows of Maundays Bay offer a perfectly safe and calm environment for an infant's first dip in the ocean. From stroller-friendly beachfront boardwalks to private open-air villas, Anguilla seamlessly pairs high-end relaxation with stress-free baby care.

What to do

Early morning 8-10am / 2h

Shoal Bay East

Renowned for its blindingly white sand, this beach is perfect for an early morning stroll with Mary Claire in a baby carrier. The calm, shallow shoreline offers safe splashing opportunities, while abundant palapas provide essential sun protection.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Maundays Bay

A protected crescent bay with virtually no waves, making it the absolute safest natural saltwater wading pool for an infant. The fine sand is free of rocks, allowing for comfortable barefoot exploration with your baby.

Morning 8:30-10:30am / 2h

Rendezvous Bay

This expansive beach offers a flat, hard-packed sand shoreline near the water's edge, ideal for a scenic morning stroll. Enjoy spectacular views of neighboring St. Martin while keeping Mary Claire comfortable under the shade of sea grape trees.

Early morning 7:30-9am / 1.5h

Meads Bay Pond

A peaceful wetland sanctuary perfect for a quiet morning stroll. Paved pathways nearby allow for easy stroller access, offering a serene environment to spot local wading birds without any disturbing noise.

Mid-morning 10am-12pm / 2h

Little Bay

Accessible via a calm, private 10-minute motorboat charter from Crocus Bay, avoiding the steep cliff path. This secluded cove is highly sheltered from wind and waves, providing a private oasis for a peaceful family picnic.

Afternoon 3-3:30pm / 30m

Anguilla Arch

A dramatic natural limestone arch carved by the sea. The viewing area is easily accessible via a flat dirt turnout, making it a quick and convenient scenic photo opportunity that requires zero strenuous hiking with a baby.

Evening 5-6:30pm / 1.5h

Sandy Ground Beach

A lively yet spacious harbor beach perfect for an early evening walk. The stroller-friendly flat terrain along the road allows parents to enjoy the sunset while Mary Claire is lulled to sleep by the gentle lapping waves.

Lunchtime 12-2:30pm / 2.5h

Scilly Cay

A tiny, private island destination reached via a ultra-short, flat two-minute boat ride from Island Harbour. The island features calm, shallow wading areas and spacious shaded dining palapas that keep infants cool and entertained.

Morning 9-10am / 1h

Island Harbour

A charming, authentic fishing village featuring a fully paved pier that is highly stroller-accessible. It is an ideal spot for a quiet morning walk to watch the colorful local sailboats glide across the calm water.

🍽️ Where to eat

Blanchards Restaurant and Beach Shack

Enjoy upmarket Caribbean fare on a spacious open-air terrace. The early 5:30 PM dinner seating is highly welcoming to families, providing highchairs and a relaxed noise level that easily masks infant sounds.

Straw Hat

Perched directly over Meads Bay, this local favorite offers highchairs and a gentle, cooling ocean breeze. The early dinner service is quiet and features exceptionally accommodating staff who prioritize comfort for young families.

Jacala Beach Restaurant

An upscale French-Caribbean bistro offering beachfront tables directly on the sand. Perfect for an early lunch, the intimate yet relaxed setting allows parents to savor fine cuisine while baby rests comfortably in a stroller alongside the table.

Veya Restaurant

Set in a lush garden canopy, this high-end eatery offers a spacious, open-air layout that keeps families comfortable. Booking the first seating of the evening ensures a peaceful dining experience before the live music volume increases.

Picante

A chic, upscale Mexican tavern located in the West End. The vibrant, open-air dining room is spacious enough for strollers, and the early evening service is lively but not loud, making it highly suitable for an infant.
