Corsica

← back to travel board
Corsica blends dramatic, pine-scented mountains with shallow, turquoise waters that rival the Caribbean. Famously known as the Isle of Beauty, this Mediterranean gem offers historic citadel towns and slow-paced coastal life perfect for family exploration. Here, days are filled with gentle beach paddles, wild nature encounters, and delicious French-Italian fusion treats.
🚕 Getting there: ~25 min rental car drive from Figari-Sud Corse Airport to Porto-Vecchio beach resorts; having a rental car is essential for exploring Corsica with kids.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Santa Giulia Beach

This sheltered horseshoe bay offers incredibly calm, knee-deep turquoise waters and soft white sand that acts as a giant natural wading pool. Toddlers can safely splash for hours while parents relax under rental umbrellas.

Morning / 2h

A Cupulatta Tortoise Sanctuary

Known as the city of turtles, this unique conservation park is home to over 3,000 tortoises and turtles from around the world. The paved, shaded pathways are highly stroller-friendly, and toddlers will love watching the giant tortoises feed.

Late Afternoon / 2-3h

Bonifacio Citadel and Petit Train

Ride the colorful motorized tourist train up the steep limestone cliffs to explore this ancient, wind-swept fortress town. It saves little legs the steep climb, and the clifftop views of the sea are absolutely spectacular.

Morning / 3h

Rondinara Beach

A near-perfect circle of fine sand keeps the waves exceptionally gentle, making this bay one of Europe's best natural swimming pools. Toddlers will love looking for tiny seashells along the shoreline of this pristine nature reserve.

Morning / 1-2h

Marché d'Ajaccio

Immerse your senses in this vibrant daily market filled with local Corsican cheeses, charcuterie, and fresh pastries. Grab some soft fiadone, a local cheesecake, for a sweet, toddler-approved snack on the go.

Afternoon / 2-3h

L'Île-Rousse Town Beach

This beautiful white-sand beach is located right in the heart of town, meaning cafes and restrooms are just steps away. A paved promenade is ideal for strollers, and the bright red granite rocks nearby offer a gorgeous backdrop.

Morning / 3h

Calvi Beach and Pine Forest

Bordered by a lush pine forest that provides excellent natural shade, this long beach features calm, shallow waters and views of the historic Calvi Citadel. It is perfect for building sandcastles while staying out of the direct midday sun.

Late Morning / 3h

Fango Valley Rock Pools

Swap the salty sea for crystal-clear, warm river pools surrounded by smooth, flat granite boulders. Toddlers can safely splash in the shallow, slow-moving edges of the river while older kids slide down gentle rock faces.

Morning / 2h

Scandola Reserve Family Boat Tour

Take a family-friendly catamaran cruise to see towering red volcanic cliffs and hidden sea caves. Opt for a shorter morning tour to keep toddlers entertained with dolphin-spotting without pushing nap times.

🍽️ Where to eat

Le Glacier de la Place

Grab an outdoor table on the pedestrian-only square in Porto-Vecchio and order their famous artisanal Corsican chestnut ice cream.

Restaurant U Fanale

Enjoy fresh local seafood and wood-fired pizzas on a spacious outdoor terrace in Calvi that overlooks the sea and offers plenty of room for kids.

Le Tahiti Beach Cafe

Sit directly on the sand of Santa Giulia beach and enjoy fresh grilled fish while your toddler plays safely with their bucket and spade right at your feet.

A Cantina di l'Orsu

Try their kid-friendly local platters featuring mild brocciu cheese and artisanal charcuterie in a very warm, casual Ajaccio setting.

Le Cactus Bonifacio

Located right on the bustling harbor of Bonifacio, this casual spot is perfect for enjoying sweet and savory crepes while watching the yachts pass by.

👨‍👩‍👧 Rent a compact but sturdy car, as Corsica's winding mountain roads are famously narrow, and bring a high-quality carrier since the historic citadels are paved with steep, stroller-unfriendly cobblestones. Pack plenty of sunscreen and UV-protection swim shirts, as natural shade can be scarce on the wilder beaches.
