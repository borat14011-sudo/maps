Corsica

← back to travel board
Corsica offers a dramatic blend of French sophistication and raw Italian-influenced beauty, making it a spectacular yet highly manageable escape for a young family. The calm, shallow gulfs of the southern coast act as giant natural wading pools for your toddler, while the rugged mountain backdrops keep the scenery world-class. It's a destination where you can enjoy Michelin-adjacent dining and pristine beaches without the chaotic, packed crowds of the Côte d'Azur. This itinerary balances relaxing beach coves, gentle stroller-accessible citadel walks, and premium comforts that keep both parents and baby happy.
🚕 Getting there: ~30 min private SUV transfer or rental car from Figari South Corsica Airport (FSC) to Porto-Vecchio.

What to do

Morning / 3-4h

Plage de Santa Giulia

This world-famous bay features exceptionally calm, crystal-clear water that stays ankle-deep for yards, acting as a perfect natural pool for your toddler. The pine-shaded shoreline offers excellent sun protection, and there are flat wooden boardwalks accessible for strollers.

Morning / 1-2h

Le Petit Train de Bonifacio

Ride this open-air mini train from the marina up to the high medieval citadel to avoid pushing a stroller up the steep, cobbled incline. Your toddler will love the breezy ride, and it lands you right at the historic cliffs with minimal effort.

Afternoon / 2h

Domaine de Torraccia

Enjoy an organic wine tasting at this historic estate while your toddler explores the wide, flat grassy lawns shaded by old oak trees. They offer casual horse-drawn carriage tours of the vineyards, which is incredibly peaceful for an infant's nap.

Morning / 3h

Plage de Rondinara

This near-perfect circular bay shields the beach from open-sea waves, creating a tranquil, wave-free paradise for your little one. A flat parking lot sits right behind the sand, making it easy to haul a diaper bag and beach shade tent without a long trek.

Morning / 2-3h

A Cupulatta

This dedicated turtle and tortoise sanctuary features shaded, paved pathways that are completely stroller-friendly. Your toddler will be fascinated by over 3,000 tortoises of all sizes, and the dense oak forest canopy provides excellent midday heat protection.

Afternoon / 2h

Lac de l'Ospedale

Escape the coastal heat with a drive up to this serene mountain lake surrounded by fragrant pine forests. The path along the water is wide, flat, and easy to navigate with an all-terrain stroller or baby carrier.

Sunday Morning / 1-2h

Marché de Porto-Vecchio

Browse stalls filled with Corsican cheeses, charcuterie, and fresh beignets au brocciu in a lively but relaxed morning atmosphere. The market is spacious enough for a stroller if you arrive early, and the sensory colors and music will keep your baby highly entertained.

Morning / 3-4h

Plage de Palombaggia

Famous for its pink granite rocks and umbrella pines, this beach features a wooden walkway near the upscale restaurants for easy stroller access. Arrive by 9:00 AM to secure a front-row shaded sunbed at one of the family-friendly beach clubs.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Place de la Libération in Sartène

This spacious, flat main square is the heart of Sartène, surrounded by cafes where you can enjoy an espresso while your toddler safely toddles around the car-free plaza. It’s the perfect, low-stress spot to experience authentic Corsican culture without navigating steep stairs.

🍽️ Where to eat

U Santa Marina

Order the local wild boar or freshly caught sea bass while sitting on the shaded outdoor terrace right over the water. The gentle lap of the waves provides natural white noise for a napping baby, and there is plenty of space between tables for a stroller.

Le Glacier de la Place

Grab a scoop of local myrtle or chestnut-flavored ice cream and sit at their expansive outdoor terrace on the flat main square. It's highly casual, bustling enough to mask any toddler fussiness, and steps away from car-free walking zones.

Da Passano

Sip Corsican craft beer and share modern tapas like Corsican cheese fritters and slow-cooked pork. Located on the flat Bonifacio marina, it offers wide outdoor seating, a highly welcoming staff for children, and opens early for dinner at 7:00 PM.

Playa Baggia

Dine right on the sand with your feet in the water, ordering wood-fired pizzas and fresh local lobster. This spot is perfect for families because your toddler can play in the clean sand right next to your table while you wait for the food.

Le Belvédère

Treat yourselves to refined Corsican gastronomy with breathtaking views of the Porto-Vecchio gulf on their spacious, breezy outdoor deck. The early evening reservation slots are highly accommodating to families, and the open-air layout keeps the atmosphere relaxed.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Grand Hôtel de Cala Rossa & Spa

This luxury estate features a private, calm beach with complimentary baby-friendly gear, custom pureed baby meals upon request, and spacious family suites with private gardens.

Hôtel Casadelmar

Offers stunning gulf views, sleek family-sized villas, a heated pool with shallow entry areas, and top-tier babysitting services so you can enjoy their Michelin-starred dining.

Les Bergeries de Palombaggia

Nestled in wild nature near the beach, this hotel provides high-end baby cots, stroller rentals, and a heated infinity pool surrounded by soft grass perfect for a crawling infant.
