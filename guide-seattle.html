What to do

Early morning 9-11am

Space Needle

Iconic observation deck with panoramic views; stroller-accessible elevator and smooth flooring. Best visited early morning to avoid crowds and noise.

2 hours

Chihuly Garden and Glass

Mesmerizing glass art in a climate-controlled indoor gallery; stroller-friendly paths and shaded garden. The vibrant colours captivate infants.

2-3 hours

Seattle Aquarium

Touch pools and calm underwater domes; stroller-accessible with quiet corners for nursing. The gentle water sounds soothe babies.

10am-12pm

Pike Place Market

Historic public market with stroller-friendly main arcade; avoid the fish-throwing area if too loud. Visit early for flower stands and less bustle.

3-4 hours

Woodland Park Zoo

Spacious zoo with wide stroller paths, shaded areas, and quiet animal exhibits. The temperate indoor rainforest is perfect for a midday break.

Half-day including island stroll

Bainbridge Island Ferry

Scenic ferry ride with stroller-friendly boarding; calm water views and sea breeze. Infant can rest in carrier while you enjoy the skyline.

Late afternoon 4-6pm

Gas Works Park

Unique park with gentle grassy slopes and waterfront views; great for a picnic and baby's first crawl on soft turf. Avoid the play area if too crowded.

Morning 9-11am

Alki Beach

Sandy beach with calm waves, stroller-friendly boardwalk, and shaded picnic spots. Ideal for a morning walk with infant in carrier.

2 hours

Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP)

Interactive exhibits with sound-dampened areas; stroller accessible. The Sky Church is a quiet, visually stunning spot for nursing.
