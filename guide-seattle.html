Seattle

← back to travel board
Nestled between the saltwater of Puget Sound and the freshwater of Lake Union, Seattle offers sophisticated families an elegant blend of maritime heritage and urban refinement. The city's stroller-ready Elliott Bay Trail provides miles of flat, paved waterfront pathways perfect for scenic morning strolls with a premium pram. Parents can sip artisanal espresso from local micro-roasteries while taking in dramatic views of the Olympic Mountains and watching ferries glide across the water. This pristine Pacific Northwest setting makes it incredibly easy to balance high-end dining and cultural sights with baby's nap schedule.
🚕 Getting there: From Seattle-Tacoma International Airport to the downtown hotel strip is a 20-to-25-minute drive, best navigated via a pre-arranged private black car service equipped with a premium infant car seat to ensure baby Mary Claire's safety and comfort.

What to do

Early morning 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Chihuly Garden and Glass

The vibrant, colorful glass installations are visually mesmerizing for infants, and the entire exhibition features wide, completely flat, stroller-accessible pathways and climate-controlled galleries.

Late morning 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Seattle Aquarium

Located right on the waterfront at Pier 59, the underwater viewing dome offers a serene sensory experience for baby Mary Claire with gentle blue lighting and floating sea otters.

Afternoon 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM

Olympic Sculpture Park

A free, nine-acre outdoor park created by the Seattle Art Museum featuring paved zig-zagging paths, gentle breezes from Elliott Bay, and plenty of shaded spots for an outdoor bottle feeding.

Early morning 8:30 AM - 9:30 AM

Pike Place Market

Arrive early before the crowds arrive to easily wheel a luxury stroller through the historic main arcade, letting baby marvel at the bright flower bouquets and fresh fruit displays.

Morning 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Washington Park Arboretum

Stroll along the beautifully paved Azalea Way, a wide and flat path winding through historic tree collections that provides a peaceful canopy of shade for an on-the-go morning nap.

Early afternoon 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM

Museum of History & Industry (MOHAI)

Set within Lake Union Park, this spacious museum offers wide elevators, highly accessible restrooms with changing tables, and a quiet, air-conditioned environment to explore regional history.

Late afternoon 4:00 PM - 4:45 PM

Seattle Great Wheel

Board a fully enclosed, climate-controlled private gondola that is incredibly quiet, allowing the family to enjoy panoramic views of Puget Sound while keeping baby protected from the elements.

Sunset 5:30 PM - 6:15 PM

Kerry Park

This iconic viewpoint on Queen Anne Hill offers a flat paved viewing platform where parents can capture a quintessential family portrait with the Space Needle and Mount Rainier as the backdrop.

Morning 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Discovery Park Loop Trail

Take the stroller-friendly paved portion of the loop trail through the quiet forest to the bluff overlook, offering crisp marine air and scenic views without challenging terrain.

🍽️ Where to eat

The Pink Door

Indulge in refined Italian classics on the breezy, covered outdoor terrace overlooking Elliott Bay; booking the earliest evening seating ensures a quiet dining environment with room for a stroller.

Ray's Boathouse

Savor high-end, locally caught wild salmon right on the water in Ballard, where the ambient chatter and gentle sound of waves easily mask any infant fussiness during an early dinner.

Westward

This chic Lake Union destination offers wood-fired Northwest seafood and outdoor deck seating with fire pits, providing a relaxed yet luxury atmosphere where baby can sit comfortably in a highchair.

Taylor Shellfish Oyster Bar

The Pioneer Square location features a spacious layout and sidewalk seating, allowing parents to enjoy fresh Puget Sound oysters and geoduck while baby rests comfortably in a pram alongside the table.

Lark
