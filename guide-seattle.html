Seattle

← back to travel board
Nestled between Puget Sound and majestic mountain ranges, Seattle blends vibrant urban energy with breathtaking Pacific Northwest nature. Families will love the mix of hands-on museums, historic waterfront docks, and lush green parks perfect for burning off toddler energy. From soaring views to ferry rides, the Emerald City offers a captivating playground for all ages.
🚕 Getting there: ~20-30 min taxi or rideshare to downtown hotels, or a 40-minute ride on the Link Light Rail for a budget-friendly, stroller-accessible option.

What to do

Morning / 2h

Pike Place Market

Watch fishmongers throw fish, smell fresh flowers, and explore endless levels of unique shops. Toddlers will love the giant bronze piggy bank 'Rachel' and tasting fresh mini donuts.

Morning / 1-2h

Space Needle

Take a thrilling 41-second elevator ride to the top for 360-degree views of the mountains and water. Toddlers will be mesmerized by the glass-bottom floor lookouts.

Afternoon / 2-3h

Seattle Children's Museum

Located right below the Space Needle, this massive hands-on museum features interactive exhibits designed for imaginative play. Toddlers will love the dedicated 'Discovery Bay' soft play area.

Afternoon / 1-2h

Chihuly Garden and Glass

Marvel at breathtaking, colorful blown-glass sculptures arranged in both indoor galleries and a beautiful outdoor garden. Toddlers will be dazzled by the bright colors and whimsical glass shapes.

Morning / 3h (including island time)

Bainbridge Island Ferry Ride

Hop aboard the double-decker ferry from downtown for a scenic, breezy ride across Puget Sound. Toddlers will love watching the wake from the deck and spotting seagulls.

Morning / 3h

Woodland Park Zoo

Explore award-winning naturalistic exhibits featuring penguins, gorillas, and grizzly bears. Toddlers can burn off steam at the Zoomazium, an indoor nature-themed play space.

Morning / 2-3h

Pacific Science Center

Ignite curiosity with hands-on science experiments, a tropical butterfly house, and laser shows. Toddlers will love splashing in the water play area and chasing butterflies.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Olympic Sculpture Park

Walk along the waterfront in this free, open-air park featuring giant, colorful modern art installations. Toddlers have plenty of room to run on the paved paths with gorgeous mountain backdrops.

Afternoon / 2h

Ballard Locks and Fish Ladder

Watch large boats and yachts rise and lower as they transition between the lakes and Puget Sound. Toddlers will be fascinated by the underwater viewing windows where they can spot migrating salmon.

🍽️ Where to eat

Beecher's Handmade Cheese

Grab a piping hot cup of their World's Best Mac & Cheese and watch cheese being made through the glass windows.

Daily Dozen Doughnut Company

Get a bag of warm, freshly made cinnamon sugar mini donuts served in a classic paper bag.

Ivar's Acres of Clams

Order the classic kid-friendly fish and chips and feed a few leftover french fries to the seagulls on the outdoor patio.

Portage Bay Cafe

Indulge in seasonal French toast or pancakes and let the kids load up on fresh berries and whipped cream at the famous toppings bar.

Dick's Drive-In

Savor a cheap, iconic 'Dick's Deluxe' burger and hand-cut fries at this classic, fast-moving walk-up counter.

👨‍👩‍👧 Pack a reliable rain cover for your stroller and dress in layers, as Seattle weather can shift quickly from cool drizzle to bright sunshine.
