Go slow on sandy streets with your little one

Caye Caulker

← back to travel board
With its famous "Go Slow" motto, Caye Caulker is the ultimate low-stress Caribbean escape for a high-intensity finance family looking to disconnect. The island has no cars—only golf carts and sandy lanes—meaning you can trade traffic noise for the rustle of palms and the soft lapping of the reef. It is incredibly safe and walkable, allowing you to easily carry a toddler or push an all-terrain stroller between seaside cafes. Here, the lack of mega-resorts means genuine local warmth, shallow wading spots, and a rhythm that naturally aligns with early wake-ups and afternoon naps.
🚕 Getting there: From BZE Airport, take a 15-minute scenic puddle-jumper flight via Tropic Air directly to Caye Caulker Airstrip, followed by a 5-minute open-air golf cart taxi to your hotel.

What to do

Morning / 2-3h

The Split

This narrow channel at the island's northern tip features a shallow, calm wading area right by the seawall where toddlers can safely splash in clear water without waves. It is highly stroller-accessible via the paved path, though you will want to grab a shaded spot early to avoid the midday sun.

Late Afternoon / 1h

Stingray Feeding at Iguana Reef Inn

Every afternoon, wild stingrays glide right up to the shallow shoreline here to feed, allowing toddlers to watch them safely from the dry sand or your arms. The property's beach is wide, flat, and perfect for parking a stroller while enjoying a quiet sunset.

Morning / 3h

Private Charter with Caveman Snorkeling Tours

Booking a private boat allows you to customize the itinerary to visit the calmest shallow reefs, keeping your toddler safe and comfortable under the boat's canopy. They provide infant-sized life jackets so your little one can float securely in the calm, warm water of the reef.

Morning / 1h

Caye Caulker Seahorse Reserve

This small dockside sanctuary features submerged netting where toddlers can peer down to spot tiny wild seahorses clinging to the ropes. It is completely free, easy to access with a stroller, and takes just fifteen minutes of quiet observation.

Afternoon / 1h

Tarpon Feeding Dock

Buy a small bowl of bait fish at the dock so you can watch massive tarpon leap out of the calm water to grab them. It's a thrilling, fast-paced spectacle that will mesmerize a toddler from the safety of a fenced wooden pier.

Morning / 1-2h

Ice and Beans Café

Grab freshly made mini-donuts and iced coffee right on the beach where your toddler can play in the soft sand right at your feet. There are plenty of hammocks and picnic tables under the sea grape trees, making it a highly relaxed spot for a morning snack.

Late Afternoon / 1-2h

Caye Caulker Playground at Central Park

Located right on the beach, this fenced-in playground features swings and slides with a gorgeous ocean view for the parents. It is a great spot for your toddler to burn off energy on real grass and sand while interacting with local kids.

Morning / 3h

Swallow Caye Wildlife Sanctuary

Take a short, gentle boat ride to this protected mangrove area to spot West Indian manatees poking their noses out of the calm water. The boat moves slowly and quietly, ensuring a peaceful experience that won't frighten a baby.

Afternoon / 2h

Split Ferry to North Caye Caulker

Take a 2-minute open-air ferry ride across the Split to the quieter north island to explore the car-free, peaceful dirt paths. It is an easy micro-adventure that toddlers find exciting, leading to virtually empty beaches perfect for shell collecting.

🍽️ Where to eat

Chef Juan's Kitchen and Pastries

Order the coconut curry snapper and a slice of key lime pie at this cozy, open-air spot that welcomes families. The service is incredibly fast, and the casual outdoor seating means you don't have to worry if your toddler gets wiggly.

Namaste Cafe

Perfect for early-riser breakfast, this spacious, airy cafe offers fresh fruit smoothies and avocado toast starting at 7:00 AM. There is plenty of room to park a stroller, and the laid-back vibe is highly forgiving of baby noises.

Happy Lobster Restaurant

Famous for its Belizean fry jacks for breakfast and fresh lobster dinners served in a roomy, covered outdoor patio. It is centrally located on Front Street, highly accessible, and the staff is famously sweet with young children.

Hibisca Restaurant

Offers high-quality, creatively prepared seafood and steaks in a beautiful garden setting with plenty of space between tables. The lush garden environment provides great sensory distraction for a baby while you enjoy a high-end meal.

Maggie's Sunset Kitchen

Sit on the back deck right over the water to watch the sunset while eating fresh grilled snapper and lobster. The early opening hours (starting dinner at 5 PM) align perfectly with toddler bedtime schedules.

🏨 Family-friendly hotels

Iguana Reef Inn

Offering spacious, air-conditioned suites on the quieter sunset side of the island, this hotel features a private flat beach and a lovely pool. It is highly toddler-friendly with on-site stingray viewings and close proximity to calm walking paths.

Colinda Cabanas

These beachfront cabanas offer fully equipped kitchens to prep baby meals and private porches with hammocks for nap-time relaxing. They provide complimentary cribs and have a private, quiet dock perfect for morning strolls with an infant.

Weezie's Ocean Front Hotel & Garden Cottages

Located on the quiet southern tip, this property offers spacious multi-bedroom cottages with kitchens and multiple pools, including a shallow-entry pool perfect for toddlers. It provides a peaceful, luxurious retreat away from the center of town.

👨‍👩‍👧 Leave the standard umbrella stroller at home; the unpaved sandy streets of Caye Caulker demand an all-terrain stroller with large rubber tires or a high-quality baby carrier. Pack all your baby essentials like specific formula, diapers, and swim pants from Houston, as the local supermarkets have limited, rotating stock. Rent a golf cart with a rear-facing seat and strap your baby carrier to it, or simply walk, as the island is small enough to navigate entirely on foot if you stay centrally located.
