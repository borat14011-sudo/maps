What to do

Morning 8:30-10am / 1.5h

Paseo de Montejo

Stroller-friendly tree-lined boulevard featuring iconic 19th-century mansions. Best explored in the cool early morning; paved sidewalks are highly accessible for high-end strollers.

Midday 11:30am-1:30pm / 2h

Gran Museo del Mundo Maya

A world-class museum celebrating Mayan culture. Fully air-conditioned, spacious, and 100% stroller-accessible with elevators, making it a perfect mid-day escape from the heat.

Late afternoon 4-5:30pm / 1.5h

Plaza Grande

The historic main square, ideal for people-watching. Paved pathways, shaded stone benches, and vibrant green spaces allow Mary Claire to watch the birds under the shade of giant laurel trees.

Morning 10-11:30am / 1.5h

Quinta Montes Molina

A beautifully preserved Porfirian-era mansion on Paseo de Montejo. The lush, manicured gardens offer a quiet, shaded sanctuary perfect for an outdoor stroll while baby naps in the carrier.

Evening 6-7:30pm / 1.5h

Parque de Santa Lucía

A lively square known for its paved courtyard and giant white kissing chairs. Visit during the day for a peaceful walk, or early evening to enjoy gentle, open-air ambient music.

Morning 9am-12pm / 3h

Cenote San Ignacio

A highly developed, family-friendly cenote complex just outside the city. It features flat pathways, a shaded restaurant, and a shallow kid-friendly pool where Mary Claire can safely splash.

Morning 9-11am / 2h

Parque El Centenario

Mérida's historic public park featuring lush botanical elements and a quiet mini-train. Shaded by massive trees, it offers a gentle sensory experience with plenty of smooth walking paths.

Afternoon 2-3pm / 1h

Palacio de Gobierno

An elegant government palace showcasing grand historic murals. It features ramp access, wide hallways, and a quiet, shaded central courtyard that offers a peaceful retreat.

Afternoon 3-4pm / 1h

Fundación de Artistas

A serene art gallery housed in a beautifully restored 19th-century colonial home. The quiet, plant-filled inner courtyard is exceptionally peaceful for a sleeping baby.

🍽️ Where to eat

K'u'uk

Avant-garde Yucatecan fine dining. Book an early 6:00 PM seating in their spacious, climate-controlled historic villa; request a quiet corner table suitable for a stroller.

Museo de la Gastronomía Yucateca (MUGY)

Authentic regional cuisine set in an open-air courtyard. The bustling, lively atmosphere easily masks any baby noises, and they readily provide clean highchairs.

Apoala

Upscale Mexican-Oaxacan fusion in Plaza Santa Lucía. Reserve a spot on the spacious outdoor terrace, allowing ample room to park a stroller next to your table under the cooling fans.

Oliva Enoteca

High-end, sophisticated Italian dining in a chic industrial-modern space. Go for an early dinner; the staff is incredibly accommodating, and the gentle ambient music is baby-friendly.

Hacienda Teya

Exquisite traditional Yucatecan dining located on a historic hacienda. The expansive, manicured gardens are perfect for walking Mary Claire in her stroller if she gets fussy during lunch.
